First Avenue

corridor_first-avenue-manhattan-2010

First Avenue is the northbound side of the First Avenue/Second Avenue Select Bus Service corridor, whose route diagram showed SBS running between South Ferry and 125th Street with paired First and Second Avenue station locations First/Second Avenue SBS route diagram. The September 2010 CAC deck states that M15 Select Bus Service would begin on October 10, 2010, and its markings plan listed First Avenue bus-lane paint from Houston Street to 72nd Street alongside protected bike-lane paint from Houston Street to 34th Street M15 SBS begins October 10, 2010 First Avenue markings and paint plan.

The corridor used both offset and curbside bus-lane treatments: the CAC materials describe offset bus lanes as in effect at all times, while curbside bus lanes were paired with morning and afternoon bus-lane hours, midday commercial loading, and overnight parking offset bus lane heading offset bus lane in effect at all times curbside bus lane hours and curb use. Later CB6 materials added bus bulbs, with early action implementation in 2011, remaining bulbs in 2012, and stormwater management where feasible early action bus bulbs in 2011 remainder in 2012 stormwater component where feasible.

The May 2011 CB6 deck reported NYPD bus-lane summonses on First Avenue, including 2,009 parking-in-bus-lane summonses, 72 driving-in-bus-lane summonses, and 2,081 total First Avenue summonses NYPD summons heading First Avenue summons table. The same deck reported 5,800 bus-lane camera summonses through April 5, 2011, and recorded First Avenue cycling volume at 50th Street 828 cyclists_per_12_hours as 828 riders, based on three single-day 7am-7pm counts in May, August, and September 2010 5,800 camera summonses through April 5, 2011 First Avenue 828 cycling count three single-day counts note.
