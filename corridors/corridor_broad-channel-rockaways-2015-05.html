Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevard (Q52/53) Presentation to Com

This segment covers the Broad Channel / Rockaways portion of the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevards Select Bus Service corridor in Queens Broad Channel / Rockaways. It spans approximately the southern end of the larger Broad Channel / Rockaways segment, which runs about 14 miles from Woodside to the Rockaways and carries roughly 30,000 daily bus riders 30000 riders/day 30,000 daily bus riders, 14 miles long. Unlike the Woodhaven Boulevard mainline section to the north, the Broad Channel / Rockaways segment was proposed to have no bus lanes, instead receiving targeted transit priority treatments and improved curbside bus stops No bus lanes, targeted transit priority treatments, improved curbside bus stops. The proposed design for the full corridor was presented to Community Board 6 on May 13, 2015, as part of the outreach process for the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevards SBS project Presentation to Community Board 6 | May 13, 2015. At the time of the presentation, the corridor was identified as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with over 3,000 injuries and 22 fatalities between 2009 and 2013, including 8 fatalities within Community Board 6 Vision Zero – 3,000 injuries, 22 fatalities, 8 in CB6. The Q53 Ltd route, which served this segment, experienced one-way travel times varying between 55 and 85 minutes with buses stopped nearly half the time One-way travel time varies between 55 and 85 minutes; northbound trips spent only 57% of time in motion, with 25% at red lights and 18% at bus stops 57% in motion, 25% red lights, 18% bus stops.
