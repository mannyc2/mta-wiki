116th Street, Morningside Av. to Pleasant Av.: Bus Priority

The Manhattan Avenue (110th to 113th Streets) corridor is part of the NYC DOT BETTERBUSES program and carries over 40,000 daily bus passengers across the M3, M7, and M116 routes, serving as a critical crosstown connection to the B and C subway lines corridor description. The study area covers Manhattan Avenue between 110th and 113th Streets, and Morningside Avenue between 113th and 114th Streets CB9 study area. Roughly 10,400 residents live within 1/8 mile of the corridor 10,400 residents. Of those households, 76% are car-free, far exceeding the city average of 55%, and 86% of residents commute via transit, walking, or biking, compared to 71% citywide car-free and commute statistics. Travel mode split is 69% transit, 14% walking, 12% car, and 3% biking travel mode split.

Bus service along the corridor experiences severe delays, with buses moving at less than 5 mph in some segments, driven by traffic congestion and double-parking slow bus speeds slow speeds near 116th Street. Between 2019 and 2023, 32 people were injured in crashes in the study area, including 7 pedestrians, 10 bicyclists (2 severe), and 14 motor vehicle occupants (1 severe) crash injuries injury summary table.

A prior Morningside Avenue Vision Zero Pedestrian Project completed in 2014 achieved a 40% reduction in pedestrian injuries through pedestrian islands, left-turn lanes, stop signs, signal infrastructure, and crosswalks Vision Zero project. The stated vision for the corridor includes making bus service fast, reliable, and on-time; improving safety; ensuring residential access; adding commercial vehicle loading; and exploring programmable space, while challenges cited include traffic congestion, double-parking, and circulation needs vision challenges. Potential treatments under consideration include curbside bus lanes, improved stop spacing, and queue jump signals curbside bus lane stop spacing queue jump signal. The project timeline targets continued outreach through Winter/Spring 2025 with implementation anticipated in Summer/Fall 2025 implementation timeline.
