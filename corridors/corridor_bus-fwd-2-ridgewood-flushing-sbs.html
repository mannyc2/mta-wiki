Ridgewood to Flushing (SBS candidate M)

corridor_bus-fwd-2-ridgewood-flushing-sbs

Ridgewood to Flushing was identified as SBS candidate corridor M, one of 21 candidate corridors across the city for the next phase of Select Bus Service expansion Queens SBS candidate corridors. Located in Queens, the corridor was identified by NYC DOT and the MTA through data analysis and public feedback including workshops and on-street surveying 21 candidate SBS corridors identified. The existing SBS network served over 309,000 daily riders at the time of the Bus Forward plan, and with proposed additions like this corridor, over 800,000 bus riders would be on routes with fast and reliable service SBS network ridership. Other Queens SBS candidates in the same round included Flushing to Springfield Boulevard, Hillside Avenue, Union Turnpike, Northern Boulevard, and Jamaica to Flushing via Kissena Boulevard Queens SBS candidate corridors. The corridor is among the candidates NYCDOT and the MTA identified to address four key analyses: access to rapid transit, long and slow bus trips, slow bus speeds, and bus reliability Four key analyses. The Bus Forward plan, published by NYC DOT in 2017, grew out of 2015 and May 2017 City Council legislation calling for an updated SBS implementation plan and broader transit improvements 2015 legislation 2017 legislation.
