M79 Corridor (79th Street Crosstown)

corridor_m79-crosstown-street

The M79 Corridor (79th Street Crosstown) runs approximately 2 miles across Manhattan on 79th Street, connecting the Upper West Side and Upper East Side. It was identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the Bus Rapid Transit: Phase II Study in 2009 BRT Phase II Study. The corridor carries over 18,000 passengers per day over about 2 miles and serves as a crucial connection to the 1, 6, B, and C subway lines as well as 16 bus routes including the M15 SBS M79 corridor description.

At the time of outreach in early 2017, the M79 spent only 43% of its trip in motion, with 26% spent at bus stops, 21% in delays, and 9% crawling below 2.5 mph M79 time breakdown table. Overall, over 50% of each trip was spent delayed, crawling, or stopped at a bus stop, and customers waited in long lines to pay on board over 50% delayed, crawling, or stopped long lines to pay on board. Five intersections along the corridor — at Broadway, Lexington Avenue, 3rd Avenue, 2nd Avenue, and 1st Avenue — were identified as high-crash Vision Zero locations Vision Zero intersections.

The SBS program has historically delivered consistent benefits: 10-30% faster bus speeds, about a 10% increase in ridership, more reliable service, 95% customer satisfaction, and safety improvements with crash reductions SBS benefits summary. The M79 SBS project followed a sequenced implementation schedule in spring 2017: finalizing street designs and continuing bus stop construction (fare machines, wayfinding, real-time passenger info) from March to early April, beginning roadway and signage work in April, and launching M79 SBS service in late spring 2017 March to early April tasks April roadway and signage work late spring launch.

Treatment components addressed several intersections along the route, including bus lane additions at W 81 St and Central Park West, a relocated bus layover at W 79 St and Riverside Drive, a dedicated left-turn bay and protected phase at W 79 St and Broadway, signal and turn improvements at W 79 St and Columbus Avenue, and visibility and congestion changes at W 81 St and Columbus Avenue W 79-Broadway treatment W 79-Riverside treatment W 79-Columbus treatment W 81-Columbus visibility and congestion change W 81-CPW bus lane treatment.
