The Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd Corridor is a bus corridor in Queens identified as a candidate for future Select Bus Service (SBS) improvements as part of the Flushing-Jamaica BRT study Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd listed as study corridor. The corridor serves two bus routes: the Q25 running from Jamaica to College Point and the Q34 running from Jamaica to Whitestone Q25 and Q34 routes on corridor. According to a June 2015 presentation to Community Board 8, the corridor carried 26,000 daily riders 26000 riders on the Q25 and Q34 26,000 daily riders on Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd. By comparison, the parallel Main Street corridor—which was prioritized ahead of Parsons/Kissena for SBS upgrade—carried 42,000 daily riders 42000 riders on the Q44 and Q20 routes 42,000 daily riders on Main Street.

The 2014 public workshop presentation identified Parsons Boulevard as a congestion hot spot and noted that overall bus speeds in the study area were under 10 mph Parsons Blvd listed as congestion hot spotBus speeds under 10 mph overall. The Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica corridor, within which this corridor sits, was initially identified in the 2006 NYC BRT study and the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II report, and was characterized by "long and slow bus trips" Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica corridor identified in earlier BRT studiesCharacterized by long and slow bus trips.

The records describe this corridor as a future SBS implementation goal candidate, but the Q44 on Main Street was prioritized for immediate SBS upgrade based on higher ridership—over 29,000 per day on the Q44 alone—and greater opportunity for on-street bus priority on Main Street Q44 prioritized based on ridership, key connection, and on-street bus priority opportunity.
