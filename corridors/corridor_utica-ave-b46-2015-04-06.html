The Utica Avenue B46 corridor was first identified as a candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study and later appeared on the short list of 16 potential future BRT corridors 2009 BRT Phase II Study identification short-list ranking #5. The B46 is the second-busiest bus route in New York City, carrying nearly 50,000 passengers per day and providing crucial connections to the 3, 4, A, and C subway lines along with 30 bus routes second-busiest route, 50k daily riders subway and bus connections. The corridor is also designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor Vision Zero Priority Corridor.

Early bus lane treatments on Utica Avenue produced measurable improvements. In summer 2014, bus lanes, left-turn bays, and turn restrictions were installed from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue, resulting in a B46 Limited/local travel time decrease of 8% to 15% decreased 8% to 15% percent during peak periods, along with a 20% to 25% reduction in general vehicle travel times in the peak direction 2014 bus lane treatments and travel time results.

The corridor later became part of the Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave transit improvements identified as a 2019 Better Buses Action Plan priority project 2019 priority project. A southbound curbside bus lane from Fulton Street to Atlantic Avenue was implemented in 2019, producing substantial bus speed improvements of 54% in the AM peak and 106% in the PM peak Fulton to Atlantic bus lane results. A further extension of the southbound bus lane from Chauncey Street to Fulton Street was proposed for spring 2020 Chauncey to Fulton proposed bus lane implementation timeline.
