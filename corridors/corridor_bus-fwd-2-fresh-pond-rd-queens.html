Fresh Pond Road, Metropolitan Avenue to Putnam Avenue was identified as a 2019 NYC DOT bus-priority project because the corridor had slow PM-peak bus speeds of 3.0 mph compared with a 6.4 mph Queens peak average, about 30,000 daily riders, and a high volume of buses Fresh Pond Road 2019 priority project reasons. The project materials describe the corridor as a 1/2-mile segment with continuous southbound traffic backup in the afternoon and evening Metropolitan Avenue to Putnam Avenue corridor conditions.

The core proposal was a curbside southbound bus lane from Bleecker Street to Putnam Avenue, active only during PM peak hours with parking and loading allowed at other times southbound PM peak bus lane proposal. NYC DOT also proposed consolidating stops to four stop pairs roughly three blocks apart, retaining three pairs, moving one pair for even spacing, and eliminating one pair four proposed stop pairs three pairs retained one pair moved one pair eliminated.

The curb-management plan said the bus lane would repurpose 70 spaces during the PM peak, including 55 metered and 15 alternate-side-parking spaces, while adding 61 new metered spaces on spurs and loading-zone changes at key locations 70 PM-peak spaces and 61 new metered spaces loading zones and bus stop consolidation. The June 2019 timeline shows continuing community outreach after the May 21 CB 5 Transportation Committee meeting, including a May 30 Farmers Oval Civic Association meeting and a June 6 Ridgewood Property Owners and Civic Association meeting Fresh Pond Road outreach timeline.
