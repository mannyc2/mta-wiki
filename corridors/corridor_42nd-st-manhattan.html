42nd Street Corridor

corridor_42nd-st-manhattan

The 42nd Street Corridor is a major commercial crosstown corridor in midtown Manhattan, running from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive, serving transit hubs including Port Authority Bus Terminal, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal 42nd Street Corridor description. In 2017, it was identified as a Bus Forward priority corridor due to its slow bus speeds 42nd St Bus Forward priority, and in April 2019 it was named as one of 24 priority projects in NYC DOT's Better Buses Action Plan BBAP 24 priority projects.

Average bus speeds along the corridor were 4.0 mph during peak hours, compared to the citywide average of 7.7 mph Area bus speeds in context. Eight MTA bus routes across three boroughs use 42nd Street, carrying approximately 16,000 daily passengers with up to 210 buses per hour Importance to bus network. Despite using only 33% of the street space, buses carry 79% of the people traveling along the corridor Allocating street space. The Buses carry 79% of people on 42nd St using 33% of street space 79 percent metric captures this imbalance. The M42 local route showed eastbound weekday running times of 18.8 minutes overnight (12-6am) 18.8 minutes, rising to 33.1 minutes in the 6-10am period 33.1 minutes and 34.4 minutes from 10am-3pm 34.4 minutes, while westbound weekday trips took 35.2 minutes in the 3-7pm period 35.2 minutes M42 running time table.

The project, jointly presented by NYC DOT and MTA under the Better Buses banner, was presented to Community Boards 4, 5, and 6 in June 2019 and to CB 6 again in September 2019 CB6 Sep 4 2019 CB4 June 19 2019. Proposed treatments included offset bus lanes (24/7), bus queue jump signals (7-second phases), turn bays, quick kurb installations, turn restrictions, and curb management changes WB bus queue jump signal Offset bus lanes Lexington Ave design details. The routes served by this corridor include the M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, SIM26, and SIM30 42nd St routes served.
