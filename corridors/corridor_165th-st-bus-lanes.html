165th Street

corridor_165th-st-bus-lanes

The 165th Street bus lane corridor in Jamaica, Queens, was proposed as part of the Jamaica Bus Improvement Study led by NYC DOT NYC DOT study, spanning from Hillside Avenue to 89th Avenue limits in CAC slides limits in CB12 slides limits in open house poster. The primary purpose of the proposed bus lane was to prioritize bus movements exiting from the 165th Street Bus Terminal, a major hub for bus routes serving southeast Queens prioritize bus movements prioritize bus movements CB12.

The corridor was presented in community outreach materials from spring 2011, including a Community Advisory Committee meeting on March 25, 2011, and a Queens Community Board 12 meeting on June 15, 2011 CAC meeting date CB12 meeting date. The proposed treatment was part of a broader package of bus lane improvements along Merrick Boulevard, Archer Avenue, and Jamaica Avenue in the Jamaica study area Merrick and 165th poster. Source materials do not indicate whether the 165th Street bus lane was ultimately implemented.
