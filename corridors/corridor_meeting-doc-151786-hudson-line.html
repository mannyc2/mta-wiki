Staff Summary - October 6, 2024 Schedule ChangeJuly Financial Plan - 2025 Mid-Year Forecast

The Hudson Line is a Metro-North Railroad commuter rail line serving the Hudson Valley between Grand Central Terminal and Poughkeepsie. Effective Sunday, October 6, 2024, Metro-North implemented a schedule change on the Hudson Line to support major construction and maintenance-of-way projects, including the continuation of Park Avenue Viaduct span renewal and Walk Bridge replacement October 6, 2024 Schedule Change Projects supported by schedule changes. As part of that change, the Hudson Line received minor adjustments to weekday outbound trains later in the PM-Peak for better spacing during Park Avenue Viaduct two-track outages, and weekend trains saw a 1-to-2 minute reduction in run time Hudson Line schedule adjustments.

The MTA Metro-North Railroad July Financial Plan - 2025 Mid-Year Forecast reports the Hudson Line Total Farebox Revenue 146.359 $ in millions at $146.4 million for the 12-month period (January through December forecast), combining $41.3 million in commutation revenue and $105.1 million in non-commutation revenue Hudson Line farebox revenue table. The same forecast shows Hudson Line Total Ridership 14.624 millions of 14.6 million riders, comprising 5.6 million commutation trips and 9.0 million non-commutation trips Hudson Line ridership table.

The Park Avenue Viaduct repair project, which drives the PM-Peak schedule adjustments on the Hudson Line, is part of the 2020-2024 Capital Program and is a state-of-good-repair effort that carries all of Metro-North's Grand Central Terminal trains Viaduct outage impact on Hudson Line Viaduct carries 98% of GCT trains. The October 2024 schedule change also supported additional construction such as switch replacements, track and tie renewals, and CDOT projects Projects supported by schedule changes.
