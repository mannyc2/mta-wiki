BUSFORWARD 2017

The Southeast Queens corridor was designated SBS candidate corridor "R" as part of the 21 candidate corridors identified by NYC DOT and the MTA for the next phase of Select Bus Service expansion SBS candidate corridors listing. The corridor is located in the borough of Queens and is listed as "Southeast Queens (multiple)," indicating it encompasses multiple route segments in the southeastern portion of the borough Southeast Queens (multiple). The SBS program, a joint effort between NYC DOT and the MTA, was developed in response to 2015 and May 2017 City Council legislation that called for an updated transit plan and strategies for improving transit access in neighborhoods not well served by the subway 2015 legislation 2017 legislation.

Existing SBS routes across the city have demonstrated 10-30% faster bus speeds, approximately 10% ridership increases, and 95% customer satisfaction SBS performance metrics. At the time of the Bus Forward plan, the existing SBS network carried over 309,000 daily riders (12% of city bus ridership); with the addition of proposed SBS routes including Southeast Queens, coverage could expand to over 800,000 riders (over 32% of city bus ridership) SBS ridership totals. Average bus speed citywide was 7.4 mph at the time, and in busy commercial districts the average was less than 4 mph average bus speed. This candidate corridor remains in the proposed phase with no known implementation date from the Bus Forward document.
