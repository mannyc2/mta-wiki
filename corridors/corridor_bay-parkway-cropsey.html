This corridor in Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue, Brooklyn comprises Bay Parkway from Avenue J to Shore Parkway (2.4 miles) and Cropsey Avenue from Bay Parkway to 26th Avenue (0.6 miles), serving approximately 35,000 daily bus riders across the B6, B82, and X28/X38 routes with connections to the F, N, and D subway lines Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue study areas. Bus speeds on Bay Parkway have been recorded as low as 3 miles per hour, and the corridor is designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor bus speeds and safety data. Between 2020 and 2024, 22 people were killed or seriously injured along the corridor, with 2 additional deaths reported in 2025 KSI statistics.

NYC DOT and MTA have been studying the corridor since at least 2024, with community board presentations to CB 11 in November 2024, CB 12 in January 2025, and both boards again in June 2025 CB 11 November 2024 CB 12 January 2025 CB 12 June 2025 CB 11 June 2025. A draft proposal presented in June 2025 calls for offset bus lanes on Bay Parkway between Avenue J and Cropsey Avenue and on Cropsey Avenue between Bay Parkway and 26th Avenue, with bus lanes shifting to curbside at some intersections to accommodate left-turn lanes, plus optimized traffic signal timing draft proposal offset bus lanes.

The B82 Select Bus Service project previously identified Bay Parkway between Cropsey Avenue and Kings Highway as part of its corridor, with planned bus lane segments on Kings Highway and stop relocations on Bay Parkway at Bath Avenue and Kings Highway B82 corridor map stop relocation at Bath Avenue stop relocation at Kings Highway.
