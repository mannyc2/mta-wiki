Bus Rapid Transit Phase II: Public Input

The Staten Island North Shore Corridor was identified as a priority corridor during BRT Phase II public workshops held in spring 2009 North Shore Corridor heading. Workshop participants were interested in using a BRT route to stimulate economic development in this area, specifically naming Richmond Terrace and Snug Harbor as growth areas for development and tourism North Shore Corridor participant comments. Participants suggested extending the corridor south to the Teleport or Fresh Kills, and also proposed connections to NJ Transit's Hudson Bergen Light Rail (HBLR) in Bayonne North Shore Corridor participant comments.

At the Staten Island workshop held on June 16, 2009 in New Dorp, 39 participants took part, one of the smaller turnouts across the seven borough workshops Workshop attendance table. More broadly, Staten Island workshop participants emphasized the need for better inter-borough transit connections and suggested a transit hub at Grasmere linking the North Shore corridor with the Staten Island Railroad Staten Island overall trends. The corridor was discussed alongside Hylan Boulevard and a proposed Bayonne Connection as Staten Island's most-discussed transit needs Hylan Boulevard headingBayonne Connection heading.
