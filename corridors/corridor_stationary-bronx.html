Bronx stationary camera corridors

corridor_stationary-bronx

This corridor groups six Bronx streets where the New York City Department of Transportation installed stationary bus lane cameras through 2023 as part of the DOT stationary camera program Bronx headerBronx corridor list. The six streets are 149th Street, Bronx River Avenue, East 161st Street, East 163rd Street, Fordham Road, and Webster Avenue Bronx corridor list. Three of these corridors (149th Street, Fordham Road, and Webster Avenue, marked with asterisks in the source) also had mobile ABLE cameras in use through 2023, operated by the MTA ABLE cameras also in use.

Citywide, stationary bus lane cameras were installed at 188 locations across all five boroughs through 2023 188 locations. The stationary camera system, implemented by NYC DOT, uses fixed cameras mounted above bus lanes that capture primarily driving-in-the-bus-lane violations, as distinct from the MTA's ABLE mobile cameras which capture no-standing and no-parking violations stationary vs ABLE violation types. The Bronx stationary camera corridors are one of six borough-level corridor groups listed in the 2024 NYC Bus Lane Camera Enforcement report covering 2022-2023 report scope. During that period the DOT spent $8,773,020 in 2022 (DOT stationary camera operating cost 2022 8773020 dollars) and $8,761,035 in 2023 (DOT stationary camera operating cost 2023 8761035 dollars) on stationary camera operating costs DOT operating costs.

The report notes that the graduated fine structure applies to all stationary camera violations: a first-offense base fine of $50, escalating to $100 for the second, $150 for the third, $200 for the fourth, and $250 for subsequent offenses within a 12-month period graduated fine structure. Stationary violations had a 92% not-challenged rate in 2022-2023 (Stationary violations not challenged percentage 92 percent), with 6% challenged and ruled guilty stationary violation outcomes.
