The corridor Fifth Avenue in Manhattan was identified as a Bus Priority/Hot Spot location within the NYC BRT Program, paired with Madison Avenue under the "Fifth Avenue/Madison Avenue Bus Priority" initiative BRT Program map showing Bus Priority locations. At the time of an April 2008 presentation to Manhattan Community Board 5, Fifth Avenue had an existing single bus lane from 86th Street to 34th Street Existing conditions list item. The proposed treatment called for converting Fifth Avenue to a double bus lane extended south to 23rd Street, painting bus lanes red, and adding overhead signage Key features list item.

The corridor served 6 local bus routes with over 100,000 combined daily riders 6 local routes 6 routes over 100,000 daily riders 100000 riders_per_day Overall benefits list item. Projected travel time improvements of up to 20% were anticipated through the corridor up to 20% travel time improvement 20 percent Overall benefits list item. The double-lane design was intended to let limited and express buses pass stopped local buses without entering general traffic, and reliability was expected to be substantially improved Overall benefits list item. Express bus routes from all outer boroughs also used the corridor Overall benefits list item.
