The Main St/Cross Bronx Service Road (Flushing to Jamaica) corridor was first identified in the initial NYC BRT study of 2006 and the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II report of 2009, and was characterized by "long and slow bus trips" Project Background Long and slow bus trips. The Q44 route, running from Jamaica to the Bronx Zoo along Main St/Cross Bronx Service Road, carries 28,000 daily riders 28000 riders per day and functions as a vital Bronx-Queens inter-borough link; 29% of all Q44 trips start or end in the Bronx 29 percent and 75% of Bronx trips are bound for Queens 75 percent Q44: Jamaica to Bronx Zoo Main St/Cross Bronx Service Road Corridor overview facts.

Bus speeds on the corridor are low: Q44 average bus speed 5-10 mph in the Bronx Average bus speed 5-10 miles per hour in the Bronx miles per hour, and Q44 less than 5 mph on Main Street and Hillside Avenue/Sutphin Boulevard 5 mph Average bus speed Less than 5 mph Main Street, Hillside Avenue/Sutphin Boulevard. A Q44 LTD bus delay analysis found that buses spend 51% of travel time in motion, 29% delayed by red lights or traffic, 18% at bus stops, and 2% by other causes Q44 LTD Bus Delay pie chart. Bronx rider destinations split across Flushing (34%), the Bronx itself (25%), Jamaica (15%), Whitestone (15%), and points between Flushing and Jamaica (11%) Bronx rider destination table.
