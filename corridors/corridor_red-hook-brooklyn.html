Red Hook Corridor

corridor_red-hook-brooklyn

The Red Hook corridor was identified through BRT Phase II public workshops as an underserved area in Brooklyn that is difficult to reach without a car--four out of five participant tables at the Downtown Brooklyn workshop raised it, and it was also frequently discussed at the Flatbush workshop Red Hook workshop discussion. The most common participant suggestion was for a BRT route between Red Hook and Downtown Brooklyn; participants also proposed routes from Red Hook to Long Island City, Greenpoint, and the Williamsburg East River waterfront Red Hook suggested routes. The corridor was frequently added as an extension to other proposed corridors, including the Bushwick to Downtown Brooklyn Corridor and the Williamsburg East River Waterfront Corridor Red Hook corridor extensions. The corridor map included in the BRT Phase II outreach report identifies the Red Hook area as a participant-identified underserved area warranting service need evaluation Brooklyn service needs map. The Red Hook Corridor remains a concept identified during this 2009 outreach phase; no specific route or implementation timeline was established from these workshops.
