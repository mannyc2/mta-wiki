The Second Avenue corridor runs through Manhattan and is paired with First Avenue as the route for M15 Select Bus Service (SBS), a joint project of MTA New York City Transit and the New York City Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) launched on October 10, 2010 M15 SBS begins October 10, 2010. The SBS route extends from South Ferry to 125th Street, with dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare payment, and revised station spacing SBS features SBS route map. After six months of service, M15 SBS recorded a 30% average daily ridership increase from the Limited service it replaced, carrying approximately 34,000 weekday SBS riders alongside 22,000 Local riders in March 2011, while overall Manhattan bus ridership declined 5% over the same period Avg. Daily Ridership chart 30% increase from Limited to SBS. SBS running time was 12 minutes (15%) faster than the Limited service it replaced SBS 12 minutes faster than Limited.

Bus lane enforcement on the corridor relied on both NYPD summonses and automated bus lane cameras. Between January 1 and March 31, 2011, NYPD issued 2,081 summonses on First Avenue and 2,614 on Second Avenue for parking or driving in the bus lane NYPD summons table. The Bus Lane Camera program issued 5,800 summonses through April 5, 2011 Camera summons: 5,800. Bus Lane Camera program summons (through April 5, 2011) 5800 summons and Bus Lane Camera program summonses 5800 summons both record the same 5,800 figure.

Cycling volumes documented at 50th Street in 2010 averaged 828 cyclists per 12-hour weekday on First Avenue and 1,118 on Second Avenue First Ave: 828 Second Ave: 1,118 Average of 3 single day counts 7am-7pm. Infrastructure improvements included a limited number of bus bulbs implemented as early actions in 2011 with the remainder in 2012, incorporating stormwater management where feasible Early action bus bulbs 2011 Remainder 2012 Stormwater management component. Protected bicycle path segments with mixing zones and pedestrian islands were proposed from E 34th St to E 49th St, repurposing 16 parking spaces E 34th to E 49th protected bike path 16 parking spaces repurposed. In September 2013, NYC DOT proposed upgrading the Second Avenue curbside bike lane (installed June 2010) to a parking-protected bike lane with a widened buffer, presented to Manhattan Community Board 6 June 2010 curbside bike lane / Sep 2013 upgrade proposal Parking protected bike lane with widened buffer.
