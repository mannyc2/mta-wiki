Bus Rapid Transit Phase II: Public Input

The Soundview Corridor was identified as a priority corridor by Bronx participants during the BRT Phase II public workshops held in May and June 2009 p016_c0011. Multiple participant tables characterized the area's existing transit options as unreliable and insufficient, and noted difficulty for pedestrians to reach subway stops and other parts of the Bronx p016_c0012. Participants expressed a desire for better connectivity both within Soundview and to other areas of the Bronx p016_c0012.

The most prominent recommendation from the Bronx workshop was that four out of five participant tables wanted to extend the Soundview corridor east-west, potentially along 161st Street p016_c0012. This desire for improved east-west connections was a recurring theme among Bronx participants more broadly, who noted that most subway service in the borough runs north-south p016_c0005.

The Bronx workshop, held May 28, 2009 at The Hub, drew 29 participants and was one of seven citywide BRT Phase II workshops p010_c0005. Bronx participants who completed the questionnaire showed strong BRT support, with 74% supporting expansion outright, 24% supporting with reservations, and 2% opposed p014_c0007.

The Soundview Corridor was one of three Bronx priority corridors to emerge from the workshops, alongside the Webster Avenue/Third Avenue Corridor and Hunts Point Peninsula p016_c0007p016_c0009. A separate press release from 2021 documents a Soundview Bus Priority project, but the relationship between that project and the 2009 corridor identification is not yet clear from available evidence soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021.
