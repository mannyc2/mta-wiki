Corridor Role

Nostrand Avenue is the southbound spine of the B44 Select Bus Service corridor, paired with Rogers Avenue for northbound SBS service. The B44 corridor is described as a major Brooklyn north-south bus route connecting residential neighborhoods, retail districts, subway transfers, Brooklyn College, Kings County Medical Center, SUNY Downstate, and Sheepshead Bay-area destinations. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p007_c0003]

Earlier project materials framed the corridor as Nostrand/Rogers/Bedford Avenues SBS, with B44 SBS replacing the B44 Limited while the B44 local remained in service. The fall 2011 handout says northbound SBS would shift from New York Avenue to Rogers Avenue for a faster and more direct path, while the corridor map shows southbound service on Nostrand Avenue and northbound service on Rogers Avenue. [201011_nostrand_handout#p001_c0004] [201011_nostrand_handout#p001_c0006] [201011_nostrand_handout#p004_c0004]

Design And Implementation

The project combined route changes with street treatments: offset bus lanes with bus bulbs, transit signal priority from Flatbush Avenue to Sheepshead Bay, new low-floor buses, next-generation fare collection, and station/bus branding. [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0002] [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0003] [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0004] [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0006]

By June 2012, the design scope was described as running from Flushing Avenue to Eastern Parkway and from Empire Boulevard to Farragut Road, with curbside bus lanes on Nostrand/Rogers between Eastern Parkway and Empire Boulevard and on Bedford Avenue between DeKalb and Flushing. Separate DDC work rebuilt Nostrand Avenue from Flushing to Atlantic, including SBS bus bulb stations at DeKalb Avenue and Fulton Street, while another DDC package built SBS features on Rogers, Bedford, and Nostrand south of Atlantic Avenue. [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p004_c0002] [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p004_c0004] [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p011_c0002] [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p011_c0004] [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p012_c0002] [2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p012_c0004]

B44 SBS launched in November 2013. The 2016 progress report says construction south of Atlantic Avenue occurred between spring and fall 2013, while unrelated utility work and remaining surface work between Flushing and Atlantic continued to affect southbound SBS until final completion in October 2015. [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p001_c0001] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p022_c0004] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p022_c0006]

Results And Follow-Up

The 2016 progress report found faster B44 travel times after SBS: northbound trips were 31% faster in the AM and 20% faster in the PM, while southbound trips were 19% faster in the AM and 15% faster in the PM. The report translates those changes into roughly 15 to 28 minutes saved for riders traveling most of the route. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0004] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0005] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0006]

The same report attributes the travel-time gains to the combined package of stop consolidation, bus lanes, off-board fare collection, and the northbound reroute to Rogers Avenue. Bus delay data shows total travel time falling from 83.2 minutes for the B44 Limited to 65.2 minutes for B44 SBS, with less time stopped at bus stops and in traffic. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0004] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0011] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0012] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0013]

Ridership took time to recover during construction and early SBS operation, but by 2015 the report shows B44 total average weekday ridership at 36,989, up 2.7% from 2014. It also reports B44 SBS ridership increasing 10% from 2014 to 2015 while B44 local ridership fell 4%. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p026_c0007] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p026_c0008] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0002] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0008] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0010]

The corridor also appears in the progress report as a safety corridor. Nostrand and Rogers were identified as Vision Zero priority corridors, and the report says the pre-implementation period averaged 583 traffic injuries per year between Atlantic and Flatbush. After SBS, injuries fell 2% in the bus-lane section and 37% at intersections where bus bulbs or neckdowns shortened pedestrian crossings. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p008_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p034_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p034_c0007] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p034_c0008]

Post-launch adjustments continued after service began. Gates Avenue and Avenue L SBS stations were added in response to public feedback, B44 local service was increased, and an Avenue R SBS stop request was evaluated but not implemented. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0008]
