The 21st Street in Long Island City, Queens corridor was the focus of a bus priority project led by the NYC Department of Transportation in partnership with MTA Bus Company and NYC Transit bus priority project on 21st Street. NYC DOT planned to convert one of two general travel lanes in each direction into a bus lane between Queens Plaza South and Hoyt Avenue, leaving one general travel lane, one bus lane, and one parking lane per direction bus lane conversion details. As of September 2022, over five miles of new bus lanes had been added along three corridors citywide including 21st Street in Queens new bus lane corridors. These new bus lanes on 21st Street, running from Long Island City to Astoria, served the Q66, Q69 and Q100 routes and were expected to improve service for 29,000 riders 21st Street bus lanes routes and ridership.

As part of the bus priority improvements, NYC DOT prohibited left turns from northbound 21st Street onto 41st Avenue and installed a left turn bay on northbound 21st Street at 40th Avenue left turn adjustments on 21st Street. These traffic changes required revising the Roosevelt Island-bound Q102 travel path: instead of turning left onto 41st Avenue, buses would continue one block north to 40th Avenue, turn west, then north onto Vernon Boulevard Q102 travel path revision. The revision relocated three Roosevelt Island-bound Q102 bus stops from 41st Avenue to 40th Avenue, where the Q102 would use existing Q103 bus stops, affecting approximately 180 weekday passengers who would walk 600 to 1,040 feet to the closest alternative stop Q102 bus stop impacts. The net travel distance and travel time for the Q102 remained unchanged no change in Q102 travel distance or time. The change was designed to be compatible with the Draft Queens Bus Network Redesign released March 29, 2022 compatible with QBNR draft plan.

The 21st Street Bus Priority project also included bus stop balancing on the Q69 (discontinuing nine stops) and Q19 (combining two stops), affecting approximately 1,395 weekday passengers Q69 and Q19 bus stop changes. The Q102 carries approximately 3,500 passengers per weekday over a one-way distance of approximately 6 miles, with service operating 5am to 1am Q102 service details. Public outreach for the overall 21st Street Priority project included two public workshops and a presentation to the Transportation Committee of Queens Community Board 1 outreach for 21st Street project. The revised traffic patterns and Q102 travel path were targeted for implementation in Spring/Summer 2022 implementation timeline.
