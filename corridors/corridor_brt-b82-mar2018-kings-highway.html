Kings Highway (Southern Brooklyn)

corridor_brt-b82-mar2018-kings-highway

Kings Highway (Southern Brooklyn) is a major east-west transit corridor in Brooklyn with two distinct segments. The eastern segment, between E 23 St and Ave K, is described as a wide, boulevard-style portion of Kings Highway with narrow, deteriorating medians that serve as bus stops and large, complex intersections Existing: Kings Highway (E 23 St to Ave K). The western segment, from Bay Pkwy to Ocean Ave, is a narrower roadway with one lane in each direction, a local truck route with frequent double-parking and truck-loading issues Kings Highway (Bay Pkwy to Ocean Ave).

During AM and PM rush hours, buses come once per minute along Kings Hwy, used by the B82 Limited and B82 Local 28000 riders per day (serving 28,000 daily riders), the B7 Local, school buses, and coach buses Wide boulevard section description. Within Community Board 15 alone, 12,500 people use the B82 Limited, B82 Local, or B7 service each day 12,500 people in CB15.

The Southern Brooklyn B82 corridor was identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study 2009 BRT Phase II Study. The proposed SBS features include dedicated bus lanes (active 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM weekdays), transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, and improved bus stop amenities such as larger lighted shelters, wider medians, level landing platforms, and ADA-accessible stops with subway-style edge strips Dedicated Bus LanesSignal Priority for BusesReal-time passenger information screens and off-board fare paymentBus lanes in effect 7-10 AM & 4-7 PM.
