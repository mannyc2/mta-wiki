Livingston St / Boerum Pl / Joralemon St

corridor_livingston-street-2023

Livingston St / Boerum Pl / Joralemon St was identified as one of 17 top-ranked corridors among 49 Brooklyn corridors studied for potential bus priority street improvements as part of the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign 49 corridors studied, 17 top-ranked. NYC DOT is pursuing options for upgrading the existing bus priority on Livingston Street and improving bus speeds and reliability on this corridor NYC DOT pursuing bus priority upgrades on Livingston Street. The corridor currently has westbound curbside bus lanes and eastbound offset bus lanes, but illegal placard parking and a proliferation of building sites that impinge on the street have led to deteriorating effectiveness of the bus lanes over time, with bus speeds well below boroughwide averages for much of the day bus lane effectiveness deteriorating, speeds below average. NYC DOT is exploring options that will address current enforcement challenges exploring enforcement solutions.

An eastbound busway on Livingston Street opened in downtown Brooklyn, benefiting over 50,000 bus customers daily eastbound busway opened, over 50,000 customers.
