The 14th Street TTP Corridor is a Transit & Truck Priority pilot corridor on 14th Street in Manhattan, implemented by the New York City Department of Transportation in October 2019 as an 18-month pilot project to improve operations of the M14 A/D Select Bus Service while maintaining the street as an important truck route and increasing safety on this Vision Zero Priority Corridor TTP pilot project description pilot start date and limits.

Between 6 AM and 10 PM, seven days a week, only buses, trucks, and emergency vehicles may travel through the corridor between 9th Avenue and 3rd Avenue (eastbound) or between 3rd Avenue and 8th Avenue (westbound) operating rules. Other vehicles may enter for local trips, pick-up/drop-off, or garage access but must take the next available right turn off 14th Street, and all left turns are restricted except for MTA buses at signed locations restrictions and local access left turn restriction. Between 10 PM and 6 AM, all vehicles may make through trips along the corridor overnight through trips.

Performance monitoring by Sam Schwartz found a 24% improvement in bus travel times and 2.9 minutes saved, combined for both directions between 3rd Avenue and 8th Avenue from January 2018 to January 2020 24% travel time improvement 2.9 minutes faster measurement period and direction monitoring by Sam Schwartz.

The corridor was also identified as a candidate during BRT Phase II planning, listed as the "14th Street Crosstown Corridor" among 16 potential future BRT corridors evaluated by NYCT and DOT table listing 14th Street Crosstown Corridor difficult trip corridor noted in outreach. Existing M14 bus service on the corridor carried approximately 39,000 trips per day at the time of the BRT Phase II analysis M14 ridership figure Manhattan survey context.
