Story Avenue Corridor

corridor_story-ave

The Story Avenue Corridor is a 1.4-mile bus priority corridor in the Soundview neighborhood of the South Bronx, running from Bronx River Avenue to White Plains Road and classified as "Present Implementation" in the Bronx Bus Network Redesign corridor metrics status table. In December 2021, NYC DOT completed the conversion of Story Avenue and adjacent streets into bus-priority corridors, installing the first dedicated bus lanes in Soundview covering three miles of roadway, curbside bus lanes on Story Avenue in effect weekdays 7 AM to 7 PM, transit signal priority, and Vision Zero pedestrian safety improvements press release.

The corridor is served by the Bx5 (existing) and the Bx6 SBS (after redesign), with a combined daily route ridership of 32,000 corridor metrics. Under the redesign, with both the Bx5 and Bx6 SBS combined, Story Avenue was planned to have a bus scheduled every 3 minutes in the weekday AM peak, every 6 minutes in the midday, and every 4 minutes in the PM peak combined frequency claim. The Bx6 SBS implementation and associated Bx5 and Bx6 Local schedule changes were postponed until 2023 due to the 18-month COVID-19 pandemic pause and the planned full deployment of OMNY postponement event.

The press release from December 2021 noted that prior to the bus lane conversion, peak-period bus speeds on these Soundview streets averaged about 5 MPH press release. The existing average bus stop spacing across the Bronx is 882 feet, which the redesign aimed to increase toward 1,092 feet through stop balancing stop spacing metric.
