May 11, 2022: Presentation to Bronx Community Board 2 - EconBx6 Select Bus Service Project Update - Manhattan CB 12 Tran

The Bx6 Corridor is served by the Bx6 Select Bus Service (SBS) and Bx6 local routes, running from the Riverside Dr W Terminal in Washington Heights to the Food Center Dr Terminal in Hunts Point, traversing both Manhattan and the Bronx route map with terminals. The Bx6 SBS launched on September 3, 2017 launch date.

After SBS launch, end-to-end bus travel times improved by 11% to 16% post-launch speed improvement. On 163rd Street, where an eastbound bus lane was implemented, weekday travel time improvements ranged from -6% (6-10AM), to -12% (10AM-3PM), to -23% (3-7PM) as measured from October 2016 to October 2019 163rd St EB travel time table. The 163rd St EB 6-10AM travel time improvement of -6% -6 percent, 163rd St EB 10AM-3PM improvement of -12% -12 percent, and 163rd St EB 3-7PM improvement of -23% -23 percent all derive from this data.

Bus reliability also improved, with fewer late arrivals and less bus bunching reliability improvement statement. Peak-period bus bunching on the eastbound 6-10AM direction dropped from 22.5% in 2016 (Bx6 Local) to 1.0% in 2019 (Bx6 SBS) bunching data table. Bus speeds spiked during the height of the pandemic but have been trending back toward pre-pandemic levels pandemic speed trend. Ridership along the Bx6 corridor has been declining, but at a slower rate than the Bronx borough average ridership decline comparison.

Implemented improvements include bus lanes, off-board fare machines, boarding islands, and the 161st Street Bus Only Tunnel (eastbound) Implemented Improvements section. Proposed capital project treatments include pedestrian islands, curb extensions, bus pads, and bus bulbs Bx6 SBS Capital Project Treatments section. The project timeline envisioned design finalization in Winter/Spring 2023, procurement in Summer/Fall 2023, and construction start in Winter/Spring 2024 project timeline.

The Bx6 SBS project was presented as a joint effort by MTA New York City Transit, NYC Department of Design and Construction, and NYC Department of Transportation MTA logo DDC logo NYC DOT logo. The corridor spans multiple community boards, with presentations made to Manhattan CB 12, CB 10, CB 9 and Bronx CB 2, CB 4, and CB 3 in Spring 2022 community board outreach schedule.
