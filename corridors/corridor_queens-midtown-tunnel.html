Queens Midtown Tunnel

corridor_queens-midtown-tunnel

The Queens Midtown Tunnel is an MTA TBTA tunnel connecting Manhattan and Queens QMT traffic data in May 2023 facility table. During the 12-month period ending May 2023, the tunnel recorded 1.68 total collisions per million vehicles, down from 3.54 in the prior June 2021-May 2022 period, and 0.20 injury collisions per million vehicles, down from 0.35 total collision rates by facility tableinjury collision rates by facility table.

The tunnel is protected by 20-ton steel portal flood doors measuring approximately 29 feet wide by 14 feet high and nearly 2 feet thick, tested annually by TBTA Central Maintenance crews working with MTA Construction & Development partners flood door specifications and testing. In the lead-up to hurricane season, flood door testing is conducted at each of the eight tunnel portals at the Queens Midtown and Hugh L. Carey tunnels flood door testing at QMT and HLC portals. Additionally, a stop log system at the Queens Midtown Tunnel provides permanently deployable flood protection designed to a flood elevation 4 feet higher than the levels experienced during Superstorm Sandy in 2012, and is tested annually in May stop log system at QMT.
