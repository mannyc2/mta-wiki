Dekalb & Lafayette Avenues, Brooklyn

corridor_dekalb-lafayette

NYC DOT and MTA are studying bus priority and safety treatments on Dekalb and Lafayette Avenues, the core corridor for the B38 route connecting Downtown Brooklyn, Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Bed-Stuy, Bushwick, and Ridgewood into Queens Better Buses Action Plan listing for Dekalb & Lafayette Avenues B38 Route Profile. The B38 carries 18,000+ average daily riders with both local and Limited-stop service (Limited runs weekdays 6:30 AM – 7:30 PM) and arrives every 5-10 minutes during peak hours Why Dekalb Av and Lafayette Av? B38 Route Profile.

Bus speeds on the corridor are slow, ranging from approximately 4-8 MPH, with buses slowest in the highest-ridership areas around Vanderbilt, Nostrand, and Broadway B38 Route Profile AM peak speed map annotation. Large sections of the route fall within Tier 1 Priority Investment Areas in the NYC Streets Plan Why Dekalb Av and Lafayette Av?. A temporary bus lane project implemented for G Train shuttles during summer 2024 demonstrated that bus priority treatments can improve B38 speeds and reliability Why Dekalb Av and Lafayette Av?.

Safety is a major concern on the corridor: there were 858 injuries in the past five years (2019-2023), including 51 severe injuries or deaths Why Dekalb Av and Lafayette Av?. Lafayette Avenue is designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, and the entire study area falls within a Vision Zero priority area Crash History.

Demographically, the study area (within a quarter mile of the B38 route) has 215,000 residents, of whom 63% are Hispanic, Latino, or non-white, 18% live below the poverty threshold, and 9% have a disability Demographics. Nearly two-thirds of households do not own a vehicle, and over 80% commute by public transit, walking, or biking Demographics.

Community outreach began with existing-conditions presentations to Brooklyn Community Board 2 and Community Board 3 in December 2024 CB 2 Existing Conditions Meeting date CB 3 Existing Conditions Meeting date.
