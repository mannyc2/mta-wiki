Port Jefferson Branch

corridor_port-jefferson-branch

The Port Jefferson Branch is a Long Island Rail Road corridor between Hicksville and Cold Spring Harbor Port Jefferson Branch Crossing Renewal description. A single source is available so far: a staff summary dated October 30, 2024, that documents a crossing renewal program and a Thanksgiving Day service program Staff summary header.

The Syosset-Woodbury Road railroad crossing on the branch was scheduled for rehabilitation during the weekends of December 7-8 and December 14-15, 2024 Crossing renewal dates. During those weekends, one of two main tracks was taken out of service between Hicksville and Cold Spring Harbor Track out of service detail. The resulting temporary service adjustments required schedule changes on the Huntington, Port Jefferson, and Ronkonkoma Branches, with connecting Greenport service also adjusted Temporary service adjustments. Funding for these projects was drawn from the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets Funding source. The current evidence covers only the Hicksville-to-Cold-Spring-Harbor segment where the crossing work occurred; no additional service, ridership, or infrastructure detail for the full branch has yet been captured from this single source Port Jefferson Branch limits from evidence.
