Linden Boulevard, Fountain Avenue to Conduit Avenue, Brooklyn/Queens

corridor_linden-boulevard

The Linden Boulevard, Fountain Avenue to Conduit Avenue, Brooklyn/Queens corridor extends approximately 1.1 miles from Fountain Avenue to Conduit Avenue across Brooklyn and Queens 1.1 miles, 4 routes, 38,000 riders. In January 2025 the corridor served 38,000 daily bus passengers on 4 routes with Bus speeds as low as 6 mph 6 mph Jan 2025 corridor data. By April 2026 the corridor had grown to 6 bus routes serving 60,000 daily riders, with speeds worsening to as low as Bus speeds as low as 4 mph 4 mph Apr 2026 corridor data. Slow speeds are concentrated between Fountain Avenue and Emerald Street, caused by traffic congestion, double parking, and commercial vehicles blocking the single travel lane in the service road bus speed congestion causes.

The corridor is a designated Vision Zero Priority Corridor with a serious safety record. Between 2019 and 2023 there were 330 total injuries 2019-2023 330 injuries, 13 severe injuries 2019-2023 13 severe injuries, and 2 fatalities 2019-2023 2 fatalities, amounting to 15 people killed or severely injured safety data table 2019-2023. The intersection at Linden Boulevard and Euclid Avenue is a Vision Zero Priority Intersection safety and Vision Zero intersection. The roadway is approximately 146 feet wide with 10 car lanes, split into main lanes and local access service roads, with crosswalks up to 1,200 feet apart existing conditions 146 ft, 10 lanes.

MTA and NYC DOT presented the Linden Blvd Traffic Safety and Bus Priority Improvements project to Brooklyn Community Board 5 in January 2025 and again in April 2026, in coordination with the HPD Jewel Streets Neighborhood Plan Jan 2025 presentation date Apr 2026 presentation date. The April 2026 proposal would move buses from the service road to the main roadway, with new boarding island bus stops at Euclid Avenue, Crescent Street, Eldert Lane, and 79 Street proposed bus routing and stops. A B14 route adjustment is proposed to place service on Eldert Lane in both directions between Sutter Avenue and Stanley Avenue B14 route proposal. NYC DOT is also investigating a potential capital project to build upon the 2025 improvements capital project investigation.
