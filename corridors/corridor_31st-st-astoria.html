NYC Streets Plan Update 2026

The 31st Street Corridor (Astoria) is identified by the New York City Department of Transportation as a safety-critical corridor in Queens where the redesign process had stalled in prior years and is being restarted through the Streets Plan Update 2026 NYC Streets Plan Update 2026, p.2. NYC DOT announced it would resume important street redesign projects that had been stalled, including restarting the process to redesign the 31st Street corridor in Astoria NYC Streets Plan Update 2026, p.2. This corridor is distinct from the nearby 31st Avenue in Astoria, where NYC DOT completed a transformational Bike Boulevard as part of more than 130 projects finished in 2025 NYC Streets Plan Update 2026, p.2. The planned redesign of the 31st Street corridor was listed alongside other near-term priorities such as the McGuinness Boulevard parking-protected bike lanes in Greenpoint and the Madison Avenue double bus lane extension in Manhattan NYC Streets Plan Update 2026, p.2. No further details about the scope, timeline, or design treatments for the 31st Street corridor redesign have been published in this source.
