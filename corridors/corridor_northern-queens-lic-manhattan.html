Northern Queens-LIC-Manhattan Corridor

corridor_northern-queens-lic-manhattan

The Northern Queens-LIC-Manhattan Corridor was identified through BRT Phase II public outreach conducted by NYC DOT and MTA NYCT in spring 2009 Northern Queens-LIC-Manhattan Corridor heading. The report combined the pre-identified DOT/NYCT Queens-Manhattan corridor with a participant-identified northern Queens east-west corridor; almost all tables at the Jackson Heights workshop (the Queens I Workshop 52 participants with 52 participants) suggested extending the Queens-Manhattan corridor east via Northern Boulevard to connect with Downtown Flushing combined corridor description. Some participants suggested further extensions east to Nassau County and south to Jamaica along the proposed Jamaica to Flushing Corridor further extensions. Participants saw this corridor as filling a need for improved intra-Queens transit and as relieving congestion on the 7 train and the Long Island Expressway relieving congestion. The corridor was among several Queens priority corridors discussed at both the Jackson Heights and Jamaica workshops, where participants also identified a broader need for east-west service across northern Queens extending from Manhattan to the Nassau County border east-west service need.
