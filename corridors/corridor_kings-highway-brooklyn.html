Kings Highway Corridor

corridor_kings-highway-brooklyn

The Kings Highway Corridor runs approximately 2 miles through Brooklyn and carries both the B82 Local and B82 Select Bus Service routes, which replaced the B82 Limited when the SBS launched on October 1, 2018 2-mile corridor B82 SBS launched Oct 2018. The New York City Department of Transportation classifies Kings Highway as a street with center-running, median bus lanes, alongside Woodhaven Boulevard and 161st Street, where vehicles cannot enter the bus lane for any reason during its hours of operation center-running bus lane example.

An August 2018 curbside changes map proposed installing bus lanes along 8 block faces effective 7 AM to 7 PM all days, adding or lengthening 4 turn bays, relocating the B/Q subway taxi stand from Kings Highway to East 16th Street, installing 28 truck loading zones (averaging 2-3 spots each, active from mid-morning to early afternoon), and adding metered parking along 13 commercial blocks bus lane hours 7AM-7PM bus lanes 8 blocks, 4 turn bays 28 truck loading zones, 13 metered blocks.

At the Fraser Square traffic circle, NYC DOT planned pedestrian and traffic safety improvements on Kings Highway at East 34th Street, East 35th Street, and Avenue M, including installing new and upgraded crosswalks, upgrading traffic signals and adjusting signal timing, creating new pedestrian spaces and bus stops, and reconfiguring intersections and lanes Fraser Square safety improvements Kings Highway at E 34th, E 35th, Avenue M intersection detail.
