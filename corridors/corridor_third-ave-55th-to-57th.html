The Third Avenue: 55th Street to 57th Street corridor is Section 2 of the Third Avenue Bus Lane Improvements project in Manhattan Section 2 map label Section 2 heading. A curbside bus lane had been in effect along this corridor from 36th Street to 56th Street since 1982, operating 7am-7pm and serving 59,000 daily riders on M101, M102, M103 59000 riders as well as 12 express routes on Third Avenue 12 routes bus lane description with rider and route counts. Trucks frequently blocked that curbside lane because no loading space existed on the east side of Third Avenue, making the bus lane ineffective problem statement.

In response, NYC DOT converted the curbside lane to an offset bus lane from 36th Street to 56th Street in Fall 2014, adding daytime commercial loading space at the curb offset lane conversion description. The intersection of Third Avenue and 57th Street was identified as a Vision Zero Priority Intersection, recording 39 non-fatal pedestrian injuries at 57th Street intersection 2008-2012 39 injuries and 1 pedestrian fatality at 57th Street intersection 2008-2012 1 fatalities safety data for 57th intersection. According to a December 2014 presentation to Manhattan Community Board 6, a package of five treatments was proposed for this section, including a bus boarding island two lanes from the curb from 56th to 57th Street, banning the left-turn from 57th Street eastbound onto Third Avenue, adding green signal time for Third Avenue northbound and 57th Street westbound traffic, protected crossing time on the east crosswalk of 57th Street, and a leading bus interval five-project treatment list. Implementation of the boarding island was proposed for Spring 2015 boarding island timeline.
