116th Street, Morningside Av. to Pleasant Av.: Bus Priority 116th Street Bus and Pedestrian Priority Project - Proposal

The Morningside Avenue (113th to 114th Streets) corridor is the CB9 study area segment of the 116th Street Bus and Pedestrian Priority Project, a joint effort by NYC DOT's BETTERBUSES program and MTA NYCT presented February 2025 to CB9's Uniform Services & Transportation Committee. The corridor carries over 40,000 daily bus passengers across the M3, M7, and M116 routes, providing critical crosstown connections to the 2, 3, 6, B, and C subway lines CB9 study area description.

The M116 bus operates at less than 4 mph in some segments due to traffic congestion and double-parking bus speeds below 4 mph. Within the study area, 76% of households are car-free 76 percent and 86% of residents commute via transit, walking, or biking 86 percent, both well above city averages of 55% and 71% respectively demographic data from 2022 ACS. From 2019 to 2023, the study area recorded 7 pedestrian injuries, 10 bicyclist injuries (2 severe), and 14 motor vehicle occupant injuries (1 severe) injury summary table.

Project goals articulated at the February 2025 meeting include making bus service fast and reliable, improving safety, ensuring residential access, adding commercial vehicle loading, and exploring programmable space vision statements; identified challenges include traffic congestion, double-parking, and circulation needs challenges listed.

By June 2025, a draft proposal had been developed that included a curbside bus lane on Manhattan Avenue (7am-7pm weekdays, reverting to parking evenings and weekends) that would repurpose approximately 25 parking spaces curbside bus lane parking conversion, conversion of the Morningside Avenue slip lane into pedestrian plaza space offering more than 8,600 ft² of programmable space pedestrian plaza details, a painted curb extension at Morningside Avenue and 116th Street painted curb extension, concrete pedestrian islands, and bus stop consolidation stop removal details. Implementation was tentatively targeted for Summer 2025, pending engineering review and approval project timeline.
