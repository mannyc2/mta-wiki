The Tremont Avenue Corridor is a 6.8-mile major east-west transit corridor in the Bronx, extending from Sedgwick Avenue to Boston Road and serving routes Bx4A, Bx9, Bx18, Bx36, Bx40, and Bx42 with a combined daily ridership of approximately 69,000 2019 Bronx Bus Network Plan corridor profile. The Bronx Bus Network Redesign Addendum listed Tremont Avenue as a "Future Plan" corridor from Sedgwick Avenue to Boston Road served by Bx18, Bx36, Bx40, and Bx42 2021 Addendum corridor status table.

A dedicated half-mile busway segment is the centerpiece of bus priority treatments, running eastbound from Third Avenue to Southern Boulevard and westbound from Southern Boulevard to Belmont Avenue Tremont Avenue Busway page Busway limits. The busway is designed to give priority to buses, trucks, and emergency vehicles while maintaining local access for passenger vehicles Busway description. The Tremont Avenue Bus Priority Project has pursued a phased approach through community advisory board and community board engagement beginning in December 2022 CAB Meeting #1 study area. A bus queue jump signal was installed at Grand Concourse and Tremont Avenue in fall 2024 Proposed treatment timeline from CB5 presentation. The busway was planned for spring 2025 implementation, accompanied by a four-block eastbound offset bus lane gateway treatment from Webster Avenue to Third Avenue and pedestrian safety improvements at seven intersections requested by community stakeholders Final proposal treatment list from CB6 presentation. Curb regulation changes are planned throughout the corridor between Grand Avenue and the Bronx River Parkway Curb regulation scope.
