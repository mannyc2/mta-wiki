This corridor runs along Grand Street/Grand Avenue from Metropolitan Avenue to Queens Boulevard in Queens and Brooklyn Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements. It carries over 51,000 daily bus passengers on the Q54, Q58, and Q59, with the Q58 being the highest-ridership bus route in Queens Over 51,000 daily bus passengers. The corridor provides critical crosstown service and connects to the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot and Central Maintenance Facility, which serve ten bus routes MTA Grand Av Bus Depot & Central Maintenance Facility serve 10 routes.

The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor and ranks in the top third of the most dangerous corridors in Queens Vision Zero Priority Corridor. Between 2019 and 2023, 359 people were injured in crashes on Grand Avenue between Metropolitan Avenue and Queens Boulevard, with 25 severely injured and three fatalities 359 people injured in crashes.

Bus speeds are as low as 4 miles per hour in some sections, with slow speeds concentrated near the Grand Street L train station, the Grand Avenue Bus Depot, and the Long Island Expressway Bus speeds as low as 4 MPH. Contributing factors include traffic congestion, double parking, and high volumes of commercial vehicles Traffic congestion, double parking & business access needs.

The study area is approximately 50 feet wide along most of its length, with painted bike lanes between 74th Street and Queens Boulevard but no bike facilities in other sections Street width around 50 feet. The area lacks direct subway service, making reliable bus service especially important for transit access Area lacking direct subway service.
