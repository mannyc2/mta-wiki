Cross Bay Boulevard in Queens was the designated corridor for offset bus lanes as part of the proposed Q52 and Q53 Select Bus Service project developed by NYCDOT and the MTA Cross Bay Blvd - Offset Bus Lanes. The offset design placed dedicated bus lanes one lane away from the curb, allowing buses to avoid conflicts with turning vehicles and parking while preserving curbside access for deliveries and local traffic offset bus lane design preserves curbside access. The street cross-section on Cross Bay Boulevard was too narrow to accommodate the main-road bus lane concept chosen for Woodhaven Boulevard, leading NYCDOT and the MTA to select the offset lane design instead Concept 1 chosen for Cross Bay where right-of-way not wide enough.

NYCDOT identified Cross Bay Boulevard as a Vision Zero priority corridor based on per-mile crash data from the Queens Pedestrian Safety Action Plan 2, which reported 4.0 pedestrian KSI (killed or significantly injured) per mile over the 2009--2013 period 4.0 pedestrian KSI per mile on Cross Bay Boulevard. The corridor was part of the broader Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards SBS implementation that launched in November 2017 Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards.

Under the as-built implementation, curbside bus lanes on Cross Bay Boulevard operated 7--10 AM and 4--7 PM, Monday through Saturday, while offset lanes in the main roadway operated 24/7 Curbside on Cross Bay Blvd: 7-10am & 4-7pm, Mon-Sat. The bus lanes were installed on the Cross Bay Boulevard segment between 107th Avenue and North Conduit Avenue Cross Bay Blvd bus lanes between 107 Av and N Conduit Av. On Cross Bay, SBS buses stopped at bus bulb stations where the sidewalk was widened to meet the offset lane, while local and express buses continued to stop at the curb local buses stop at curb, SBS at bus bulb to allow passing.
