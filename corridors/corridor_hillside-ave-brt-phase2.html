Bus Rapid Transit Phase II: Future CorridorsBUS RAPID TRANSIT PHASE II: Future Corridors

The Hillside Avenue Corridor was identified as a candidate for future Select Bus Service (SBS) through the BRT Phase II planning study initiated in fall 2008 by the New York City Department of Transportation (DOT) and MTA New York City Transit (NYCT) BRT Phase II study initiated. Public outreach in spring 2009 included seven citywide workshops and an online survey, drawing over Workshop participants 325 people in person along with Online survey respondents 600 people; attendees included representatives from Community and business group representatives 47 groups and staff from the offices of Local elected officials' offices represented 23 offices public outreach metrics. NYCT and DOT compiled an initial list of Potential corridors initially identified 40 corridors and evaluated them on five criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity, narrowing the field to 16 potential future corridors evaluation and narrowing. From those 16, a short list of three candidates for the next BRT planning effort was identified: Webster Avenue/Third Avenue in the Bronx, Hillside Avenue in Queens, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst in Queens short list of three. Hillside Avenue is described as a typical transit corridor with defined limits, in contrast to the LaGuardia/East Elmhurst corridor which comprises several inter-related travel markets corridor classification. The corridor appears as Route 2 on the short-list table in the Phase II future corridors report short-list table entry. No further planning, design, or implementation milestones for this corridor have been documented in the available source material.
