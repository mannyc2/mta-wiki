The Archer Avenue Busway, Queens is an eastbound busway on Archer Avenue in Jamaica, Queens, launched in October 2021 as a one-year pilot project alongside the Jamaica Avenue Busway Jamaica and Archer Ave Busway pilot. The busway operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week 24/7 operation. Before implementation, Archer Avenue served 189,000 daily riders across 19 bus routes, with pre-busway afternoon peak speeds of 5.3 to 6.1 MPH Archer Ave pre-busway stats.

The busway restricts eastbound through trips between 153rd Street and 160th Street to buses and emergency vehicles only through trips restriction. Unlike the adjacent Jamaica Avenue Busway, there is no local access on Archer Avenue no local access. Passenger vehicles and trucks are not allowed eastbound, and there is no parking or loading access eastbound between 150th Street and 160th Street vehicle and parking restrictions. Pickups and drop-offs are permitted in the westbound direction westbound pickup/dropoff.

The Archer Avenue Busway was implemented as part of the Better Buses Restart initiative, with a November 2022 monitoring update tracking bus speeds and related metrics Better Buses Restart: Jamaica Busway. Earlier planning dates to the Jamaica Bus Improvement Study (2011), which described Archer Avenue as the busiest local bus corridor in NYC, carrying up to 180 buses per hour per direction, and proposed improved bus lane markings and signage between 150th and 160th Streets busiest corridor description. The study corridor for those improvements extended from Sutphin Boulevard to Merrick Boulevard Sutphin to ParsonsParsons to Merrick.
