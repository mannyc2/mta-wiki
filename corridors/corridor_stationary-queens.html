NYC DOT Bus Lane Camera Enforcement 2024 Report

The Queens stationary camera corridors include 14 streets where DOT installed fixed bus lane cameras as of 2023, part of 188 stationary camera locations citywide 188 stationary camera locations through 2023. The list published in the 2024 Bus Lane Camera Enforcement Report covers Archer Avenue, Beach 59th Street, Broadway, Cross Bay Boulevard, Fresh Pond Road, Hillside Avenue, Hoffman Drive, Jamaica Avenue, Main Street, Merrick Boulevard, Queens Boulevard, Rockaway Beach Boulevard, Sutphin Boulevard, and Woodhaven Boulevard Queens stationary camera corridor list. Six of these corridors also had MTA on-bus mobile (ABLE) cameras operating through 2023: Archer Avenue, Broadway, Fresh Pond Road, Hillside Avenue, Main Street, and Sutphin Boulevard, indicated by asterisks in the source report Mobile ABLE cameras asterisk note. The stationary cameras capture primarily driving-in-the-bus-lane violations, distinct from the ABLE mobile cameras which record stopping and parking violations Stationary vs ABLE violation types. DOT incurred operating costs of $8,773,020 in 2022 and $8,761,035 in 2023 for the stationary camera program citywide DOT stationary camera operating cost; Queens-specific costs are not separately reported. The stationary camera program generated $91,127,285.46 in revenue across all boroughs during 2022 and 2023 Stationary camera program revenue.
