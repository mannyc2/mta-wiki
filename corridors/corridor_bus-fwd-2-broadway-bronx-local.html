Broadway, Bronx (W 225th St to W 231st St)

corridor_bus-fwd-2-broadway-bronx-local

The Broadway, Bronx (W 225th St to W 231st St) corridor (also referenced as Broadway between 225th and 230th Streets Broadway (225-230 St) PM Peak Hour Speeds) was identified as a local bus priority corridor by NYC DOT and the MTA in the Bus Forward plan The Bronx Broadway from W 225th St to W 231rd St and as a priority project under the Better Buses Action Plan released in April 2019 Broadway identified as a priority corridor due to slow speeds and high ridership. Citywide, nearly 2.5 million daily bus riders 2500000 riders experience an average bus speed of 7.4 mph 7.4 mph, with speeds below 4 mph in busy commercial districts Nearly 2.5 million New Yorkers ride buses every day. On this corridor during the PM peak hour, northbound buses averaged 3.58 mph and southbound buses 4.56 mph, substantially below the Bronx borough average of 6.9 mph and the citywide peak average of 7.4 mph Broadway (225-230 St) PM Peak Hour Speeds.

The corridor serves approximately 38,500 weekday bus riders across routes Bx7, Bx9, Bx20, and BxM1, with Bx9 carrying the highest ridership at 23,180 daily riders Bx7: 13,257, Bx9: 23,180, Bx20: 813, BxM1: 1,278. Outreach found that most riders reported bus bunching and low schedule adherence as key concerns Most riders described issues with bus bunching and low schedule adherence. A merchant survey reported that 90% of businesses observed delivery vehicles double parking on Broadway and 67% reported difficulty accessing the curb 90% reported that delivery vehicles double parked on Broadway. An on-street observation found 56% of commercial vehicles double parked for an average of 13 minutes, with the heaviest double parking between 11am and 4pm 56% of commercial vehicles were double parked.

The proposed design included an offset bus lane on southbound Broadway from 230th Street to 225th Street, a restricted right turn from northbound Broadway onto Exterior Street with a bus queue jump signal combined with a Leading Pedestrian Interval, expanded commercial loading access on the Broadway service road, and new metered parking on 228th Street Add offset bus lane on southbound Broadway from 230 St to 225 St. The summary estimated the improvements would directly benefit 5,500 daily riders and improve reliability for 38,500 weekday bus riders Directly improve bus speeds for 5,500 daily riders. The project was presented to Community Board 8 on September 12, 2019, with street design implementation planned for late fall 2019 and post-implementation monitoring beginning in winter 2020 September 2019: Corridor design presentation to Community Board 8 and finalize plan.
