Eastern Parkway, Schenectady Avenue to Ralph Avenue, Brooklyn

corridor_eastern-parkway

Eastern Parkway, Schenectady Avenue to Ralph Avenue, Brooklyn is the focus of a NYC DOT transit priority proposal for the B14 bus route Eastern Parkway Transit Priority Proposal. The corridor runs through Community Board 8 and was presented as a draft proposal to that board on March 25, 2025 and again on April 10, 2025 Draft Proposal – Brooklyn Community Board 8March 25, 2025April 10, 2025.

Eastern Parkway itself was envisioned by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux in 1866 and was designated a New York City scenic landmark on August 22, 1978 designated as a scenic landmark in 1978Landmark Type: Scenic Landmark. Bus service along the parkway began in 1925 through the Eastern Parkway, Brownsville & East New York Transit Relief Association Bus service began in 1925. Today, more than 3,000 people board the B14 or B17 at the Eastern Parkway and Utica Avenue intersection on a typical weekday 3,000+ people board B14 or B17.

Eastbound B14 bus operators currently use the parkway's main line instead of the service road to avoid traffic congestion and double parking, particularly at Utica Avenue, and some buses then turn back onto the service road at Rochester Avenue Eastbound B14 bus operators use the mainline. While performance has improved since this informal operational change, the B14 stop signage was never moved to formalize it B14 stop signage was never moved.

The existing eastbound service road bus stop at Rochester Avenue is inaccessible due to a strip of grass between the sidewalk and curb, and ADA ramps connecting the northern pedestrian malls are missing Existing eastbound service road bus stop is inaccessible. The draft proposal would relocate B14 bus stops to the southern pedestrian malls, replace rough landscaping materials with hexagon or smooth granite pavers to create ADA-accessible stops, remove the Rochester Avenue stop, and install ADA ramps connecting the northern pedestrian malls Relocate B14 bus stops to the southern pedestrian mallsReplace rough granite blocks with hexagon pavers or smooth granite pavers. NYC DOT coordinated the proposed bus-stop removal with the MTA's Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Team DOT coordinated with MTA's Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Team.
