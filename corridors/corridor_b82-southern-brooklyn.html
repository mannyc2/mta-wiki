B82 Corridor - Kings Hwy and Flatlands Ave

corridor_b82-southern-brooklyn

The B82 Corridor - Kings Hwy and Flatlands Ave is an approximately 10-mile east-west transit corridor in Brooklyn, serving about 32,000 daily riders on the B82 Limited and Local routes 10 miles, 32,000 daily riders, 32,000 daily riders on B82 Limited and Local. The corridor was first identified as a candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study identified as potential SBS candidate in 2009 BRT Phase II Study. It connects to six subway lines (D, N, F, B, Q, L) and 21 bus routes, including existing SBS routes like the B44 Nostrand and B46 Utica connects to D N F B Q L subway lines and 21 bus routes.

The B82 SBS service launched on October 1, 2018, replacing the B82 Limited, and runs from Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street to Pennsylvania Avenue in Starrett City, operating from approximately 4:45 AM to 11:00 PM B82 SBS launched October 1, 2018. Key Select Bus Service treatments along the corridor include one-way curbside bus lanes on Kings Highway from Bay Parkway to McDonald Avenue, off-board fare collection with all-door boarding, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety improvements such as shorter crossings and improved visibility Kings Hwy one-way curbside bus lanes, Bay Pkwy to McDonald Ave, Off-Board Fare Collection, Pedestrian Safety Improvements.

Survey data collected in 2016 from 7,500 people along Kings Highway showed a high share of walking and transit use: on weekdays 51% walked, 24% used transit (11% bus, 13% subway), and 23% drove, with similar patterns on weekends Weekday mode: Walk 51%, Transit 24%, Car 23%, Weekend mode: Walk 58%, Transit 19%, Car 21%. A separate survey of 235 businesses along Kings Highway found that 67% reported double parking interferes with deliveries and 46% reported vehicles at the curb interfere with deliveries 67% of businesses report double parking interferes with deliveries, 46% of businesses report vehicles at curb interfere with deliveries.

The B82 SBS design included a proposed westbound re-route via Glenwood Road for a more direct connection to the Canarsie-Rockaway Parkway L train station, along with intersection redesigns at Flatlands Avenue and Ralph Avenue, and new pedestrian crossings on Pennsylvania Avenue Proposed re-route westbound via Glenwood Rd for more direct connection to L station, Flatlands/Ralph intersection redesign, pedestrian crossings on Pennsylvania Ave. Dedicated bus lanes along the corridor were proposed to operate during peak hours (7-10 AM and 4-7 PM, Monday through Friday), serving all buses, emergency vehicles, and right turns Bus lanes in effect 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM Mon-Fri, Bus lanes serve all buses, emergency vehicles, and right turns.
