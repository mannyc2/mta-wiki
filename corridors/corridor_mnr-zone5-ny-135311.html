MNR Zone 5 (NY)

corridor_mnr-zone5-ny-135311

MNR Zone 5 (NY) covers the Metro-North Railroad territory west of the Hudson River, encompassing Rockland and Orange Counties in New York State, as defined in a March 2024 Board procurement document for emergency and scheduled bus services Zone description table. The corridor is one of five defined MNR zones used to organize bus service procurement under Contract #426684, which carried an estimated aggregate budget of $55,000,000 55000000 USD across both MNR and LIRR territories Budget breakdown. MNR's share allocated to Zones 1 through 5 was $30,000,000 30000000 USD MNR budget table. The procurement sought qualified vendors to furnish emergency busing (immediate service due to sudden disruption) and scheduled busing (planned disruption from capital projects) on an as-needed basis throughout the Railroads' operating territories Emergency vs scheduled definitions. A total of 13 proposals 13 proposals were received by July 24, 2023, and the Selection Committee recommended 13 firms 13 firms for contract award, with territory assignments including firms serving MNR-only zones (such as Leprechaun Lines and Peter Pan Bus Lines) and firms serving both MNR and LIRR zones Proposals received 13 firms recommended Territory-bus company table. The contracts carried a five-year term 5 years Contract term listing. Zone 5 is geographically distinct from the other MNR zones in that it covers the West of Hudson service area, while Zones 1-2 cover Connecticut branches and Zones 3-4 cover the Hudson and Harlem lines within New York east of the Hudson Zone description table.
