University Avenue Corridor

corridor_university-ave

The University Avenue Corridor is a 2-mile bus priority corridor in the Bronx, running from Kingsbridge Road to the Cross Bronx Expressway and serving routes Bx3, Bx18, and Bx36 with a combined daily route ridership of approximately 39,000 Bronx Bus Network Final Plan 2019 corridor profile. The corridor is classified as a "Present Implementation" corridor under the Bronx Bus Network Redesign Bronx Bus Network Final Plan Addendum 2021 corridor table.

A separate NYC DOT presentation to Bronx Community Board 5 describes the corridor as extending from Kingsbridge Road to the Washington Bridge, serving routes Bx3 and Bx36, with a combined 55,000 weekday riders and approximately 12,700 weekday riders on University Avenue itself CB5 presentation limits CB5 presentation details. Average bus speeds along the corridor were measured at 6.3 mph during the AM peak and 5.6 mph during the PM peak, with documented issues including slow bus speeds, traffic congestion, buses frequently unable to pull to the curb, double parking, and long distances between pedestrian crossings CB5 speed and issues. Key destinations served include the VA Medical Center, Bronx Community College, and the Fordham Road Shopping District CB5 destinations.

The corridor was also identified as an SBS candidate corridor in the Bus Forward citywide transit plan Bus Forward 2 corridor list. NYC DOT studied potential street design treatments for University Avenue both north and south of Tremont Avenue, and investigated a queue jump signal at the intersection of University Avenue and Fordham Road University north of Tremont study University south of Tremont study Fordham queue jump location queue jump investigation note. The adjacent Washington Bridge corridor, listed as a Future Plan corridor in the addendum, would connect Amsterdam Avenue to University Avenue with routes including Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, and Bx36 Washington Bridge future plan row.
