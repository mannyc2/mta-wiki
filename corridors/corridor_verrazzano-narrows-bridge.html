Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge

corridor_verrazzano-narrows-bridge

The Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge is a TBTA-operated suspension bridge connecting Brooklyn and Staten Island VNB appears in B&T facility traffic table. In May 2023, the bridge carried an average of 229,397 weekday vehicles Average Weekday Traffic - VNB May 2023.

The bridge served as the final leg of the 45th TD Five-Boro Bike Tour on May 7, 2023, when nearly 30,000 cyclists crossed its lower level (closed to motorists) into Staten Island TD Five-Boro Bike Tour crosses VNB 45th iteration - lower level closure. TBTA's coordinated event preparations began months in advance, involving Maintenance, Operations, and Emergency Management teams to ensure safety and efficient restoration of traffic comprehensive event action plan.

In 2023, TBTA adjusted its Wind Restriction and Coastal and Severe Storm protocols based on structural changes at the Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge, allowing cargo trucks to safely traverse the upper level at certain wind speeds wind restriction adjustments based on VNB structural changes. Wind deflectors are installed on the upper level adjacent to the towers to diffuse wind pressure and reduce wind load on vulnerable vehicles wind deflectors on VNB upper level.

For the 12-month period ending May 2023, the bridge recorded a total collision rate of 6.42 collisions per million vehicles and a collisions-with-injuries rate of 1.83 per million vehicles, the highest among all B&T facilities in both categories total collision rate 6.42 VNB injury collision rate 1.83 VNB.
