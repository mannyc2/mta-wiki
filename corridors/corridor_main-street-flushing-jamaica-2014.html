Main Street Corridor (Flushing-Jamaica)

corridor_main-street-flushing-jamaica-2014

The Main Street Corridor (Flushing-Jamaica) was one of two corridors considered for Select Bus Service between Downtown Flushing and Jamaica, alongside Kissena/Parsons Boulevards, as part of a 2014 NYCDOT study two corridors being considered. The corridor spans roughly 8.4 miles and serves the Q20A/B and Q44 bus routes, connecting Jamaica to College Point and the Bronx Zoo 8.4 miles Q20A/B and Q44 routes.

The Q44 was prioritized for an SBS upgrade based on high ridership—over 29,000 daily riders—and its role as a key connection between Queens and the Bronx Ridership – Over 29,000 per day on Q44. Combined daily ridership on the Q44 and Q20A/B reached approximately 42,000 riders 42,000 daily riders on. The Q44 Limited was upgraded to Q44 Select Bus Service, while Q20A/B local service continued unchanged; the SBS version made 8 fewer stops in the Bronx and 5 fewer in Queens Q44 from Bronx Zoo to Jamaica via Main Street SBS will make fewer stops than Q44 LTD.

The corridor was also designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, with Main Street from Northern Boulevard to Queens Boulevard ranked among the top ten most dangerous roadways in Queens MAIN STREET VISION ZERO PRIORITY CORRIDOR top 10 most dangerous roadways in Queens. Proposed street treatments included offset bus lanes (no turns) in Flushing near Maple Avenue, preserving existing curbside parking and loading, and peak-period curbside bus lanes in Jamaica FLUSHING: OFFSET BUS LANES (NO TURNS) Existing curbside parking and loading remain JAMAICA: PEAK PERIOD CURBSIDE BUS LANES.

Bus lanes, fare machines, and curb regulation changes were implemented starting in September and October 2015, followed by a capital construction phase including bus bulbs scheduled for 2017-2018 September/October 2015: Bus Lanes, Fare Machines, Curb Regulation Changes Capital Project (including bus bulbs). The SBS package included dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare payment, transit signal priority, and improved station amenities, with stated benefits of 15-23% faster bus speeds, over 10% ridership increases in the first year, and improved street safety Buses 15-23% faster; 10%+ increases in ridership; safer streets.
