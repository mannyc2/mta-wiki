Manhattan-Northern Blvd-Flushing Corridor

corridor_manhattan-northern-blvd-flushing-brt-phase2

The Manhattan-Northern Blvd-Flushing Corridor was identified as candidate route 16 of 16 potential future BRT corridors in the BRT Phase II study initiated in fall 2008 by the New York City Department of Transportation and MTA New York City Transit table entry listing Manhattan-Northern Blvd-Flushing Corridor as candidate route 16. Its description in the study table indicates it would connect Manhattan to Flushing via Northern Boulevard, spanning the boroughs of Manhattan and Queens BRT Phase II study description. The corridor was not among the three corridors short-listed for the next BRT planning effort; the short-list comprised Webster Avenue/Third Avenue (Bronx), Hillside Avenue (Queens), and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst (Queens) short list of three candidates.

The corridor emerged from a multi-stage screening process that began with over 40 potential corridors identified through a transit needs analysis and public feedback, narrowed using five evaluation criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity evaluation methodology.
