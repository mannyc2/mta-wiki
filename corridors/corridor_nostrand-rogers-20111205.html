Nostrand / Rogers Corridor

corridor_nostrand-rogers-20111205

The Nostrand / Rogers Corridor is a 9.3-mile bus corridor in Brooklyn running from the Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay corridor-name limits. At the time of a December 2011 presentation to Community Board 3, the corridor was served by the B44 bus route, which carried 42,000 weekday riders making it the 7th busiest route in New York City, with buses averaging 7-8 mph route ridership.

Within a quarter-mile of the corridor lived approximately 300,000 residents, 62% of whom did not own a car within-a-mile demographics. The corridor was being planned for a Select Bus Service upgrade that included offset bus lanes, off-board fare collection with proof-of-payment enforcement by NYCT Eagle Teams, bus signal priority, enhanced stations with bus bulbs and new shelters, and dedicated SBS branding sbs-features offset-lane pre-payment-works enforcement station-design.

The Nostrand/Atlantic intersection had the highest number of crashes on the corridor, and the Bedford/Atlantic intersection ranked fourth-highest crashes-nostrand crashes-bedford. A traffic analysis projected that peak-direction vehicle travel would remain generally unchanged under the plan, while reverse-peak traffic would be the same or faster peak-analysis reverse-peak. The community engagement process leading up to the presentation included 19 public meetings over three years, comprising 4 Community Advisory Committee meetings and 2 open houses public-meetings cac-meetings.
