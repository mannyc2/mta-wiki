M60 Select Bus Service on 125th Street - CB11 Transportation

This corridor is the intersection of Lexington Avenue and 125th Street in Manhattan, documented through a January 6, 2015 presentation by MTA to Community Board 11's Transportation Committee CB11 Transportation Committee meeting. A diagram of the intersection shows curb extensions (bus bulbs) at all four corners with bus stop icons at each quadrant along Lexington Avenue intersection diagram with bus bulbs.

Curb extension proposals for the 125th Street and Lexington Avenue intersection were first presented to CB11 in 2013, and MTA noted that the community had an opportunity to shape the proposal while still in the design phase 125th/Lex curb extension proposals presented 2013 opportunity to shape proposal. As of the January 2015 meeting, next steps included refining the Lexington Avenue curb extension proposal and conducting additional traffic and transit monitoring refine curb extension proposal parking observations and traffic data.

The M60 Select Bus Service launched on May 25, 2014, operating along 125th Street with dedicated offset and curbside bus lanes installed between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue service launched May 25, 2014 bus lanes Lenox Av to Second Av.
