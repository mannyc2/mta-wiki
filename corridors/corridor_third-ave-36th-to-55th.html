Third Avenue: 36th Street to 55th Street

corridor_third-ave-36th-to-55th

This corridor covers the Third Avenue "Section 1" segment between 36th Street and 55th Street in Manhattan, part of a NYC DOT bus lane improvement project presented to Community Board 6 in 2014 Section 1 heading Section 1 map label.

A curbside bus lane had been in place on this stretch since 1982, operating 7am-7pm and serving approximately 59,000 daily riders 59000 riders on the M101, M102, and M103 local routes plus 12 express routes 12 routes Background bullet list. The existing configuration had no loading space on the east side of the street, causing trucks to frequently block the bus lane and rendering it ineffective for buses Issues list.

In response, NYC DOT proposed moving the bus lane to an "offset" design and adding parking and loading space at the curb Proposal list. The offset lane was implemented in Fall 2014, converting the curbside lane from 36th Street to 56th Street into an offset bus lane, which according to the DOT improved bus speeds and reliability for the M101, M102, M103, and express buses while adding daytime commercial loading with meters being installed Background update list.

Near the northern end of this corridor, Third Avenue and 57th Street was identified as a Vision Zero Priority Intersection with a high crash record for pedestrians Safety Needs list. Between 2008 and 2012 the intersection recorded 39 non-fatal pedestrian injuries 39 injuries and 1 pedestrian fatality 1 fatalities Section 2 issues list. A related package of treatments was proposed for this intersection, including a bus boarding island two lanes from the curb, a left-turn ban from 57th Street eastbound onto Third Avenue, additional green signal time, a leading pedestrian interval, and a leading bus interval at 55th Street Proposed changes list.

A timing discrepancy is present in the source records: the May 2014 presentation labels this corridor as ending at 55th Street, while the December 2014 presentation describes the offset bus lane conversion as extending from 36th Street to 56th Street Section 1 label Offset lane extent. The 56th Street terminus likely reflects the northern extent of the offset lane treatment rather than a corridor boundary change.
