Lexington Ave, 96th St to 60th St

corridor_lexington-ave-96th-to-60th

Lexington Ave, 96th St to 60th St is a 1.8-mile bus corridor on Lexington Avenue in Manhattan, running from 96th Street south to 60th Street Lexington Ave, 96th St to 60th St Corridor length 1.8 miles. The corridor serves M98, M101, M102, and M103 bus routes with a total daily ridership of 44,000 Routes served and daily ridership.

In 2017, Lexington Avenue was identified as a Bus Forward priority corridor due to slow bus speeds Identified as Bus Forward corridor in 2017. At the time, average bus speeds were 5.0 mph during the AM peak period and only 3.9 mph during the PM peak period Average bus speeds 5.0 mph AM / 3.9 mph PM. The existing curbside bus lane, which operated only from 7-10 AM, was frequently blocked by other curb demands, forcing buses to merge back into general traffic Existing curbside bus lane often blocked.

The proposed treatment involved shifting the bus lane from the west curb to an offset position along the full 96th Street to 60th Street segment, making the lane effective at all times and enabling commercial metered parking during the morning peak Shift bus lane to offset position, all-times, enables metered parking Proposal: Offset Bus Lane. Additional proposed treatments included the installation of bus boarding platforms at M101 Limited stops at 79th Street and 72nd Street (potential for 2019/2020), with 96th Street, 86th Street, and 68th Street identified as potential future locations Bus boarding platforms at M101 Limited stops 79th St, 72nd St (2019/2020); 96th St, 86th St, 68th St (future).
