Staff Summary - October 6, 2024 Schedule ChangeJuly Financial Plan - 2025 Mid-Year Forecast

The Harlem Line is operated by Metro-North Railroad as a commuter rail corridor between Grand Central Terminal and Wassaic, serving the Harlem Valley Harlem Line farebox and ridership table. Effective October 6, 2024, the Harlem Line schedule included minor adjustments to weekday outbound trains later in the PM-Peak period to improve train spacing during Park Avenue Viaduct two-track outages October 6, 2024 schedule change description. The Park Avenue Viaduct carries all Metro-North trains entering Grand Central Terminal and is a critical single point of failure; its state-of-good-repair renewal is part of the 2020-2024 Capital Program, and the October 2024 schedules continued to support that project along with other track and bridge work across all three main lines Schedule supports Park Avenue Viaduct project.

The MTA Metro-North Railroad July Financial Plan - 2025 Mid-Year Forecast reports that the Harlem Line generated $175.405 million in total farebox revenue over a 12-month period (January through December), comprising $60.237 million in commutation revenue and $115.168 million in non-commutation revenue Harlem Line Total Farebox Revenue 175.405 $ in millions. Over the same period, the Harlem Line carried 22.037 million total riders, split between 9.843 million commutation trips and 12.194 million non-commutation trips Harlem Line Total Ridership 22.037 millions. These figures represent the line's service as part of Metro-North's East of Hudson operations Harlem Line ridership data.
