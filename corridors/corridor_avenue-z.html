Avenue Z

corridor_avenue-z

Avenue Z is a Brooklyn bus corridor identified by NYC DOT, in collaboration with the MTA, as part of a corridor evaluation process for the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign listed among 17 top-ranked corridors. NYC DOT analyzed major Brooklyn corridors based on demand for bus service, bus performance, feasibility of street treatments, the corridor's role in the transit network, and neighborhood demographics and equity metrics evaluation criteria. This process identified 49 corridors to be studied for potential bus priority street improvements, with Avenue Z ranking among the top 17 49 corridors identified. The corridor is listed alphabetically alongside other top-ranked corridors such as Flatbush Av, Nostrand Av, and Utica Av corridor list.

The bus priority toolkit that may be considered for Avenue Z includes dedicated bus lanes, busways, queue jump intersections, transit signal priority, pedestrian safety elements, and bus stop accessibility upgrades toolkit of improvements. These efforts are part of the NYC Streets Plan, released in December 2021, which seeks to expand bus priority street improvements citywide NYC Streets Plan. As of the Draft Plan publication in December 2022, Avenue Z had not yet advanced to a specific design or implementation phase, remaining at the corridor identification and prioritization stage Draft Plan 12/01/22. The Draft Plan's proposed network includes 24 routes with frequency improvements 24 routes and 87% of Brooklyn residents within 1/4 mile of all-day frequent service 87 percent, though these borough-wide metrics are not specific to Avenue Z 24 routes frequency improvement 87% frequent access.
