source_soundview_bus_priority_2021

This corridor is White Plains Road, one of three Soundview streets (along with Story Avenue and Bronx River Avenue) where New York City DOT completed the first dedicated bus lanes in the neighborhood in December 2021. DOT press release Dec 3 2021 The three-mile bus lane project serves 45,000 daily riders 45000 riders per day on MTA bus routes Bx5, Bx36, and Bx39. DOT press release Prior to conversion, average peak-period speeds were about 5 MPH 5 MPH and daytime speeds averaged as low as 5 MPH 5 MPH. DOT press release

The curbside bus lanes on White Plains Road are in effect Monday-Friday, 7am to 7pm. DOT press release The project also installed transit signal priority, painted curb extensions at five intersections, updated daytime parking regulations, and angled parking on adjacent side streets. DOT press release The bus lanes are enforced by NYPD and DOT automated enforcement cameras, with a 60-day warning period followed by fines starting at $50. DOT press release The Soundview project was identified through the MTA's Bronx Bus Network Redesign, and is part of seven bus lane miles 7 miles added in the Bronx since 2020. DOT press release
