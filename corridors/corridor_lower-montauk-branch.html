Lower Montauk Branch Passenger Rail Study

The Lower Montauk Branch is a two-track, non-electrified rail corridor running 8.5 miles through Queens from Long Island City to Jamaica LIRR Lower Montauk Branch runs 8.5 miles from Long Island City to Jamaica. Formerly part of the Long Island Rail Road, infrequent passenger service at 5 stations was discontinued in 1998 due to low ridership; the corridor currently serves only freight trains previous passenger service discontinued in 1998.

In January 2018, the New York City Department of Transportation published the Lower Montauk Branch Passenger Rail Study, prepared by AECOM, which found that joint passenger-freight operations on the Branch would be feasible initial analysis shows it would be feasible to develop joint passenger-freight operations. The study proposed 10 station locations — the two termini at Long Island City and Jamaica plus eight intermediate stations roughly one mile apart recommended 10 station locations.
