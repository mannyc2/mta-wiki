Rockaway Beach Boulevard

corridor_rockaway-beach-blvd

Rockaway Beach Boulevard is a 3.7-mile primary east-west transit corridor in the Rockaways, Queens, that serves the Q22, Q52/Q53 SBS, and QM16 bus routes with approximately 36,000 daily riders primary east-west transit connections 3.7 miles, routes, ridership. The NYC DOT Better Buses Action Plan identified it as a priority 2019 project due to slow bus speeds, unreliable travel times, high ridership, and community safety requests priority 2019 project reasons. Bus speeds along the corridor range from 7.6 mph to 15.1 mph depending on segment slow bus speeds.

Q52/Q53 Select Bus Service launched in November 2017 with corridor-wide pedestrian safety and transit improvements, producing a 5-8% ridership increase, a 9-10% improvement in SBS travel times, and a 6% reduction in crashes Q52/Q53 SBS launch results. However, no street improvements were made specifically within the Rockaways beyond bus service and routing enhancements no street improvements in Rockaways.

The project area recorded 124 total traffic injuries 124 injuries between 2013 and 2017, including 25 pedestrian injuries, 4 bicyclist injuries, and 95 motor vehicle occupant injuries, with 6 killed or seriously injured (KSI) injury summary table. Three pedestrian fatalities occurred between 2013 and 2018 at Beach 59 St, Beach 73 St, and Beach 116 St pedestrian fatalities.

A 2018 Access to Opportunity Study identified numerous locations along the corridor for pedestrian and transit improvements Access to Opportunity Study. The NYC DOT presentation to Queens Community Board 14 on June 3, 2019 proposed targeted improvements at six locations: B 116 St, the B 108 St–B 102 St segment, the B 102 St–B 94 St segment, B 90 St, the B 73 St–B 67 St segment, and B 59 St at Beach Channel Drive CB14 presentation date six project locations. Proposed treatments include bus lanes (24/7 and peak-hour), concrete median extensions, painted curb extensions, pedestrian islands, signal timing changes, bus stop relocations, and lane-marking improvements.
