Utica Avenue Bus Priority and Safety Study

This corridor covers the northern segment of Utica Avenue from St. Johns Place to Carroll Street, within the broader study area the New York City Department of Transportation examined for the Utica Avenue Bus Priority and Safety Study, presented to Brooklyn Community Board 9 on September 24, 2013 CB 9 presentation. The study focused on Utica Avenue from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue, with key intersections at Eastern Parkway, Empire Boulevard/Lefferts Place, and Church Avenue study scope.

The B46 local and limited routes, the second busiest bus route in New York City, operate along this corridor, carrying over 49,000 weekday passengers B46 weekday passengers 49000 passengers and averaging only 6.8 mph, below the Brooklyn average B46 conditions B46 average speed 6.8 mph 6.8 mph. Safety conditions were a major concern: 63% of vehicles exceeded the 30 mph speed limit 63% of vehicles exceed speed limit 63 percent, and from 2006 to 2010 the corridor experienced 766 total crashes 766 total crashes 2006-2010 766 crashes, 41 severe injuries 41 severe injuries 2006-2010 41 injuries, and 2 fatalities 2 fatalities 2006-2010 2 fatalities, with Utica having among the highest injury rate from motor vehicle crashes in Brooklyn crashes and speeding.

For the St. Johns Place to Carroll Street segment, the proposed plan relocated the bus lane to the southbound approach, provided more parking between President and Carroll Streets, and reduced bus delays on the southbound approach to Eastern Parkway, yielding a net gain of 19 peak period parking spaces proposed changes Net gain 19 peak period parking spaces 19 parking spaces. Across the broader corridor the study proposed converting one travel lane to an offset bus lane to reduce speeding and maximize parking, adding left turn bays at some intersections and prohibiting left turns elsewhere, and implementing Transit Signal Priority with optimized corridor signal timings corridor treatments. TSP would run from Kings Highway to Broadway in partnership with NYCT, and signal cycles would increase from 60 seconds to 90-120 seconds signal timing and TSP.
