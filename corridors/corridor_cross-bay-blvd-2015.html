Cross Bay Boulevard (north of 165 Av)

corridor_cross-bay-blvd-2015

The Cross Bay Boulevard (north of 165 Av) segment is a sub-corridor of the larger Woodhaven / Cross Bay SBS corridor in Queens, proposed as part of the Select Bus Service project serving the Q52 and Q53 routes Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevard (Q52/53). The SBS project was based on the existing Q52/53 LTD bus route, spanning 14 miles from Woodside to the Rockaways with 30,000+ daily bus riders corridor statistics. Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards had been designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor since 2009 Vision Zero designation.

The design for this segment specified offset bus lanes, with SBS buses stopping at bus bulbs while local and express buses stop at the curb, maintaining parking and deliveries at the curb offset bus lane design features corridor design summary for Cross Bay Blvd. This design differed from the adjacent Woodhaven Blvd segment, which used main road bus lanes and median stations Woodhaven Blvd design. Within this Cross Bay segment, two design options were presented at the April 30, 2015 public design workshop: Option 1 featured 2 travel lanes each direction with left-turn bays, while Option 2 featured 3 travel lanes each direction with shared left-turn lanes; based on Community Advisory Committee feedback, Option 2 was shown in the design plans, though Option 1 was also to be analyzed for safety and traffic CAC feedback and Option 2 preference.

A dedicated public design workshop for Cross Bay Boulevard was held on April 29, 2015 Cross Bay Blvd workshop date. The overall corridor design followed the selection of Concept 2 (Main Road Bus Lanes) as the preferred corridor design, which was described as offering substantial transit improvement, the most potential for pedestrian and safety improvements, and balance between local vehicle access and thru traffic Concept 2 rationale. At the time of these presentations, a Q52 extension further east on the Rockaway Peninsula was under study by MTA Bus in response to community requests Q52 extension consideration extension study details.
