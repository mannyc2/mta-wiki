Webster Avenue SBS Corridor

corridor_webster-avenue-sbs

The Webster Avenue SBS Corridor runs from the Harlem River in Manhattan to The Hub in The Bronx, serving the Bx41 route with approximately 22,000 daily riders 22000 riders SBS coming to Webster Avenue corridor map. Bx41 SBS replaced the Bx41 Limited while the local route remained the same, with SBS fare equal to the local bus fare SBS fare details.

Proposed design features included bus bulbs extending sidewalks to the bus lane, dedicated offset bus and right-turn lanes, pedestrian safety islands and medians, transit signal priority, new bus shelters, off-board fare collection machines, and local bus stops on the same blocks as SBS stations wherever possible bus bulbs pedestrian amenities dedicated bus lanes transit signal priority station design details.

In August 2012 the project team surveyed 158 businesses 158 businesses near Bx41 Limited stops, finding that over 85% rely on Webster Avenue curb space for deliveries 85 percent and that customer parking and double parking were concerns, information intended to guide DOT in improving curb regulations business survey. The project schedule ran from a Design Ideas phase in Winter 2011-12 through Draft Corridor Plan development in Spring 2012, refinement in Fall 2012, and Final Design and Implementation from Winter 2012-13 to 2013-2014 project schedule.

DOT and NYCT hoped to achieve on Webster Avenue the results seen on earlier SBS routes, including 15% to 20% faster trips 15% to 20% faster trips percent, 33% reduction in time spent at bus stops 33 percent, 10% reduction in all other travel time 10 percent, over 95% customer satisfaction 95 percent, and a 10% ridership increase on the entire route 10 percent SBS success metrics hope to achieve similar results.
