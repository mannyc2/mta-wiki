M86 Corridor

corridor_86th-street

The M86 Corridor runs east-west across Manhattan on 86th Street, connecting the Upper West Side and Upper East Side via the Central Park Transverse Road, from West End Avenue/Broadway to York Avenue and East 92nd Street the corridor connects the Upper East Side and Upper West Side the route runs from West End Av and Broadway to York Av via 86th St. It was identified as a potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 "Bus Rapid Transit: Phase II Study" identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study in 2009.

Before SBS conversion, the M86 bus carried over 24,000 daily passengers and ranked as the second-busiest crosstown bus route in Manhattan with the highest per-mile ridership in New York City M86 carried over 24,000 daily passengers, second-busiest crosstown bus route. Because ridership had been declining due to rising travel times and poor reliability, the corridor was selected for Select Bus Service—a joint MTA New York City Transit and NYC DOT program applying Bus Rapid Transit principles ridership dropping due to rising travel times and declining reliability SBS is a joint program between MTA NYCT and NYC DOT. The M86 SBS launched on July 13, 2015 M86 SBS launched on Monday, July 13, 2015.

Early results showed an eastbound travel time reduction of 11% (from 23.7 to 21.1 minutes) and a westbound reduction of 8% (from 22.0 to 20.3 minutes), comparing February 2015 to February 2016 eastbound reduced 11% from 23.7 to 21.1 minutes, westbound 8% from 22.0 to 20.3 minutes. Overall ridership grew 7% and reliability improved 10%, while a July 2016 customer survey found 96% satisfaction with overall service 7% ridership growth, 8-11% travel time decrease, 10% reliability improvement, 96% customer satisfaction 96% satisfaction for overall service.

The corridor received queue jump bus lanes, off-board fare payment at nearly all stations, and real-time passenger information and wayfinding totems—the first SBS route to have them at multiple locations at launch first SBS route to have real time passenger info totems at multiple locations at launch off-board fare payment at all stations except certain next-to-last stops. Six pedestrian-safety neckdowns and four bus bulbs at Lexington and Third Avenues were also added as part of capital construction six neckdowns and four bus bulbs being added for pedestrian safety four bus bulbs included, in both directions at Lexington and 3rd Avenues.

The M86 SBS served as a model for the parallel M79 crosstown corridor, which was analyzed using similar street design and stop patterns M79 analysis will investigate similar treatments used for M86 SBS. The Second Avenue Subway Q service began operating at 86th Street on December 31, 2016, and MTA NYCT planned to monitor its effect on M86 SBS ridership Second Avenue Subway Q expected to have an effect on M86 SBS ridership.
