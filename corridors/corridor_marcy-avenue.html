Marcy Avenue, Borinquen Place to Broadway, Brooklyn

corridor_marcy-avenue

The corridor is Marcy Avenue from Borinquen Place to Broadway in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, a three-block stretch where three Williamsburg Bridge Plaza-bound bus routes converge Marcy Av, Borinquen Pl to Broadway confluence of three bus routes. The B24 (2024 average daily ridership 2,359), B60 (12,226), and Q54 (13,411) all experience delays and slower speeds along this tail-end stretch, compounding earlier en-route delays and causing late terminal arrivals B24 B60 Q54 ridership and delays. The BQE North and South (2024) study recommended providing a bus priority lane to alleviate these delays BQE study recommendation.

Traffic congestion is common along the corridor, especially on weekday late afternoons before the PM rush hour lane along the east curb takes effect traffic congestion timing. Buses must navigate a very tight right turn from southbound Marcy Avenue onto westbound Broadway, often competing for space with other turning vehicles and improperly stopped traffic on eastbound Broadway difficult right turn. On the west side of Marcy Avenue between S 4 Street and S 5 Street, delivery vehicles frequently occupy the bus stop for extended periods loading zone demand.

NYC DOT has proposed a Bus Priority Street Improvement Project (SIP ID 12263) for the corridor under the Better Buses program, presented to Brooklyn Community Board 1 in June 2025 and September 2025 SIP ID CB1 Transportation Committee June 2025 CB1 Full Board September 2025. Proposed treatments include converting a general travel lane to a bus priority lane (all hours), having the bus lane merge into the general travel lane, adding a new neighborhood loading zone, replacing a part-time parking lane with a full-time general travel lane, and widening the travel lane to facilitate safer turns for trucks and buses proposed pavement markings and treatments.
