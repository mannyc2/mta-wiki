The 181st Street Busway corridor (Broadway to Amsterdam) is a roughly half-mile busway on 181st Street between Broadway and Amsterdam Avenue in the Washington Heights neighborhood of Manhattan meeting_doc_37081. It launched on April 26, 2021, and serves five MTA bus routes: Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, and Bx36, benefiting over 66,000 daily riders meeting_doc_37081. Like other NYC busways, it prioritizes buses and trucks while permitting local access for other vehicles, which must exit the corridor at the next required turn busways.

The busway was celebrated at its launch by Mayor Bill de Blasio, MTA Chairman Patrick J. Foy (then Chairman Rodriguez), and NYC DOT Commissioner Hank Gutman as a milestone for bus customers and a key to New York's pandemic recovery meeting_doc_40141. The project was developed through a community advisory board process beginning in July 2020, with the final plan presented in early 2021 181st_street_jun2022.

Following the launch, operational adjustments included signal timing changes along Broadway in June 2021, bus stop consolidation on 181st Street and St. Nicholas Avenue in October 2021, and gantry signage installations in December 2021 to improve visibility of busway regulations 181st_street_jun2022 181st_street_jun2022 181st_street_jun2022. An on-street bus rider survey conducted in April 2022 collected community feedback, and a door-to-door business walkthrough in May 2022 documented concerns about customer confusion, signage, parking, and commercial loading hours 181st_street_jun2022 181st_street_jun2022.

Based on community feedback, a proposal was made to reduce busway hours to 6 AM to 8 PM, seven days a week, and to make the pilot busway permanent 181st_street_jun2022. The corridor is also designated as an ABLE (Automated Bus Lane Enforcement) route, disproportionately serving low-income New Yorkers, similar to the four ABLE routes in the Bronx meeting_doc_113986.
