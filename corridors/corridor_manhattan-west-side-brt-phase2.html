Manhattan West Side Corridor

corridor_manhattan-west-side-brt-phase2

The Manhattan West Side Corridor was identified as one of 16 potential BRT Phase II candidates by NYCT and NYC DOT following a citywide transit needs analysis and public outreach process, appearing as entry #12 on the short list of future corridors BRT Phase II Candidates table BRT Phase II Candidates table. It was not among the three corridors (Webster Avenue/Third Avenue, Hillside Avenue, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst) that NYCT and DOT selected as priority candidates for the next BRT planning effort Short list of three candidates.

During the BRT Phase II public outreach in spring 2009, the Manhattan workshop held June 18, 2009 in the Garment District drew 97 participants, the highest attendance of any of the seven borough workshops Workshop attendance table; in total, 324 participants attended across all workshops and over 650 respondents completed the online survey Workshop survey summary (Online Survey Respondents 650 respondents). Participants who discussed the West Side Corridor identified the far west side as a high growth area that lacks easy access to the subway system, and some participants suggested extending service north to Washington Heights with a potential connection to the existing Bx12 SBS West Side Corridor feedback.

Overall, the BRT Workshop Participants 324 participants and online survey indicated strong public support for BRT expansion, with 98% of workshop attendees and 97% of survey respondents expressing support Strong public support for BRT expansion.
