Madison Avenue, East 23rd Street to East 42nd Street, Manhattan

corridor_madison-avenue

Madison Avenue, East 23rd Street to East 42nd Street, Manhattan is a northbound bus priority corridor in Manhattan where NYC DOT has proposed extending existing double bus lanes south from East 42nd Street to East 23rd Street project scope proposal details. Bus lanes on Madison Avenue north of 42nd Street to 60th Street have existed since 1981 study area background, and the corridor is paired with existing southbound double bus lanes on 5th Avenue pairing with 5th Ave.

The study area carries 92,000 daily bus riders across 34 bus routes, including M1, M2, M3, M4, and Q32 local buses plus 29 express bus routes from other boroughs ridership and routes. Bus speeds in the corridor are slow, with local buses averaging 5.7 mph 5.7 mph and express buses averaging 7.5 mph 7.5 mph during weekday AM peak hours (October 2024), using M1, M2, M3, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM10 data speed table data note. Overall speeds have been recorded as low as 4.5 mph low speeds.

The proposed design adds two 11-foot bus-only lanes on the east side of Madison Avenue while maintaining a 12-foot curbside parking or rush-hour travel lane and one 11-foot general travel lane on the west side cross-section. The project also includes updated curb management retaining approximately 78% of existing parking spaces curb management and Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) expansion covering future Madison Avenue bus lanes ACE expansion.

The NYC Streets Plan Update 2026 identifies extending the Madison Avenue double bus lanes south from 42nd Street to 23rd Street as a priority project to resume after prior delays streets plan priority. Earlier planning for this corridor dates back to at least 2008, when NYC DOT presented a Madison Avenue double bus lane extension to 23rd Street as a bus-priority / hot-spot treatment paired with the 34th Street corridor and the citywide BRT program 2008 presentation BRT program map.
