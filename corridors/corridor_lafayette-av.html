Lafayette Av

corridor_lafayette-av

Lafayette Av is designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor in Brooklyn, and NYC DOT is studying safety improvements along the corridor Vision Zero Priority Corridor designation. The corridor runs roughly from Fulton St to Patchen Av and varies in street width and design: from Fulton St to South Elliott Pl it is 33' wide with a buffered bike lane and a 10' travel lane Lafayette Av segment Fulton to S Elliott Pl; from South Elliott Pl to Classon Av it expands to 40' with a buffered bike lane matching portions of DeKalb Av Lafayette Av segment S Elliott Pl to Classon Av; from Classon Av to Marcy Av the 40' width continues but the buffered bike lane is removed, creating safety concerns for cyclists Lafayette Av segment Classon to Marcy Av; and from Marcy Av to Patchen Av the street narrows to roughly 33' with no delineated space for bikes Lafayette Av segment Marcy to Patchen Av.

The corridor carries the B38 route, which serves approximately 18,000+ average daily riders B38 daily ridership (B38 average daily riders 18000 riders per day). Bus speeds on the corridor average around 4-8 MPH B38 speed range, and speeds can be as low as 4 mph on parts of the route bus speed low. There were 858 injuries on the corridor in the past five years, including 51 severe injuries or deaths corridor injuries.

During the Summer 2024 G Train shutdown, temporary bus lanes were installed along portions of DeKalb and Lafayette Avenues, leading to speed increases of up to 21% on DeKalb Av and up to 5% on Lafayette Av temporary bus lane speed results. NYC DOT is incorporating lessons learned from those temporary lanes into the study, including red paint, signage, and enforcement lessons learned.

The study area covers 215,000 people, of whom 9% have a disability; citywide, bus rider median annual income is approximately $30,000 compared to $47,000 for drivers demographics. The project timeline envisioned completing the first round of Community Board outreach in December 2024, continuing engagement through Winter/Spring 2025, presenting a refined proposal to Community Boards in Spring 2025, and implementing the project in Summer/Fall 2025 project timeline.

Note: This corridor page documents Lafayette Av specifically, while the parallel companion corridor DeKalb Av has its own separate page.
