Gun Hill depot service area

corridor_gun-hill-depot-area

The Gun Hill depot service area encompasses bus routes serving disadvantaged communities in the northeast and central Bronx from the Gun Hill depot. The MTA has prioritized this corridor for zero-emission bus deployment due to long-standing environmental justice concerns over emissions and air-quality issues hydrogen buses and Gun Hill depot.

In late 2022, the MTA accepted an $8 million NYSERDA Electric Truck & Bus Challenge grant to deploy two hydrogen fuel cell buses at the Gun Hill depot NYSERDA $8M grant award. The two buses are 40-foot New Flyer Xcelsior CHARGE H2 buses with a range of over 300 miles, using hydrogen primarily produced with 100% renewable energy from hydropower, and were scheduled to enter passenger service by the end of 2024 hydrogen bus specifications. The MTA will install a hydrogen fueling station at the depot capable of fueling up to 40 buses hydrogen fueling station capacity.

The hydrogen fuel cell buses operate on approximately eight routes serving the northeast and central Bronx. The MTA described this deployment as a step toward demonstrating hydrogen as a complement to the battery-electric fleet on the path to an all zero-emission bus fleet by 2040 zero-emission fleet goal by 2040.
