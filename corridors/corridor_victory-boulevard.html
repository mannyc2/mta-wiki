Victory Boulevard, Bay Street to Wild Avenue, Staten Island is a major Staten Island transit corridor carrying approximately 25,789 average daily riders on 16 bus routes that connect to the Staten Island Ferry Victory Boulevard statistics. The corridor is also a Vision Zero priority corridor: from 2019 to 2023 it recorded 619 total injuries, including 60 severe injuries, ranking it among the top 10% most dangerous corridors in Staten Island Traffic safety data 2019-2023. Bus speeds are slow, dropping below 8 mph during morning peak and below 7 mph during midday and evening periods based on October 2024 MTA data AM bus speeds Midday bus speeds. On Victory Boulevard north of Jersey Street, 48% of vehicular road users are bus riders, while the remaining 52% travel by car Modal share data.

Curbside peak-direction bus lanes have existed on segments of Victory Boulevard since 1963, but many remain unpainted and lack enforcement Existing bus lane history. Red paint was added only between Bay Street and St. Marks Place in 2020 Existing treatments. NYC DOT and MTA are now proposing upgrades: red painting and extended hours (Northeast: 6-9 AM, Southwest: 3-7 PM) between Bay Street and Forest Avenue, new curbside bus lanes between Forest Avenue and Eddy Street, and additional treatments including rubber speed bumps for turn calming and up to 10 potential bus stop accessibility upgrades in Community Board 1 Proposed bus lane hours Proposed upgrades Bus stop accessibility locations. Community outreach has included an existing conditions discussion with Staten Island elected officials on February 10, 2025, and a CB 1 Transportation Committee presentation in May 2025, with a separate CB 2 existing conditions presentation on June 2, 2025 covering Wild Avenue to Willowbrook Avenue Outreach to date CB 2 presentation date. The roadway width varies significantly along the corridor—from 30 to 60 feet between Forest Avenue and Wild Avenue, and between Willowbrook Road and Richmond Avenue it functions with two travel lanes in each direction, narrowing to one lane each way between Richmond Avenue and Wild Avenue Roadway width CB 2 roadway conditions.
