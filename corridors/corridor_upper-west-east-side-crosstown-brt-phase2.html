Bus Rapid Transit Phase II: Future CorridorsBUS RAPID TRANSIT PHASE II: Future CorridorsBus Rapid Transit Phase II: Public Input

The Upper West Side/Upper East Side Crosstown Corridor was identified as one of 16 potential future BRT corridors during the BRT Phase II planning process, which evaluated over 40 candidate corridors based on five criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity 16 potential corridors identified Phase II candidate listing. The corridor was not selected for the short list of three candidates (Webster Avenue/Third Avenue, Hillside Avenue, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst) that were prioritized for the next planning effort short list of three.

At the Manhattan BRT workshop held on June 18, 2009 in the Garment District, which drew 97 participants—the highest turnout of any borough workshop Manhattan Workshop Attendance 97 participants workshop attendance table—Manhattan residents voiced overwhelming support for better east-west crosstown service, particularly across Central Park crosstown service support. Participants ranked this corridor as the highest priority among all Manhattan corridors under discussion highest priority ranking. Within the corridor, 86th Street received the greatest attention; participants stated that the M86 bus is "very slow and over-crowded" M86 slow and over-crowded. 96th Street, 72nd Street, and 66th Street were also mentioned as important crosstown streets within the corridor other streets mentioned.

The BRT Phase II public outreach effort overall found strong support for BRT expansion: 97% of online survey respondents and 98% of workshop attendees expressed support Online Survey Respondents 650 respondents BRT Workshop Participants 324 participants public support for BRT.
