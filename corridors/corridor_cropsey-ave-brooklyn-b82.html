Cropsey Avenue (Southern Brooklyn)

corridor_cropsey-ave-brooklyn-b82

Cropsey Avenue (Southern Brooklyn) is the southernmost segment of the proposed B82 Select Bus Service route, running north from the terminus at Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street in Brooklyn B82 SBS begins at Cropsey Ave & Bay 37 St. The B82 Limited was being converted to B82 SBS, with a planned launch in July 2018 July 2018 launch. The corridor carries 28,000 daily riders 28000 riders on the B82 Limited and Local combined, and roughly 9,800 of those riders travel through Community Board 11 serves 28,000 daily riders 9,800 CB 11 riders use the B82 each day.

Along the Cropsey Avenue corridor, B82 delays were attributed to time in motion (48% 48 percent), signal delay (29% 29 percent), bus stop dwell time (21% 21 percent), and other delays (2% 2 percent) delay breakdown table. Safety issues identified in CB 11 included long pedestrian crossings along Cropsey Avenue and difficult bus turns at the Bay Parkway and Kings Highway intersection Key Safety Issues in CB 11.

The proposed street design at the Cropsey Avenue, Bay 37 Street, and Harway Avenue intersection included a concrete pedestrian refuge, a new concrete bus boarding island, painted curb extensions for shorter crossings, and a daytime bus layover space that converts to overnight parking Cropsey Ave at Bay 37 St 2018 Changes diagram. The route also connects to the D, N, F, B, Q, and L subway lines and 21 bus routes connects to subway lines and bus routes. The Cropsey Avenue corridor feeds northward into Bay Parkway, Kings Highway, Flatlands Avenue, and Pennsylvania Avenue as part of the broader B82 SBS alignment proposed B82 SBS route map.
