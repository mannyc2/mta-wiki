Hunts Point Corridor

corridor_hunts-point-bronx

The Hunts Point Corridor (identified in the source as "Hunts Point Peninsula") was highlighted as a high priority for Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) during the Phase II public workshops held in spring 2009 Hunts Point Peninsula heading. All five participant tables identified Hunts Point — particularly its western section — as a high priority for BRT, both for employees who worked in the area and for residents All five tables identified Hunts Point. The source notes that Hunts Point was one of the underserved areas that participants most often focused on for new transit service, and that Bronx participants specifically cited it as a neighborhood underserved by the subway system underserved areas like Hunts Point highest priority on areas underserved by subway.

Participants expressed a need for improved transit within Hunts Point, as well as better connections to other areas in the Bronx and beyond. Specific suggestions included converting the Bx6 route to BRT converting Bx6 to BRT. The Bronx workshop took place on May 28, 2009 at The Hub, drawing 29 participants — the smallest borough turnout of the seven workshops held citywide Bronx workshop 29 participants. Broader survey data from the same Phase II outreach effort showed that 52% of Bronx survey respondents reported riding the bus 4–7 days per week, the second-highest rate among the five boroughs Bronx bus usage 52% 4-7 days/week.
