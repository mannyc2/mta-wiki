Bus Rapid Transit Phase II: Future CorridorsBUS RAPID TRANSIT PHASE II: Future Corridors

The Southern Brooklyn East-West Corridor was identified as one of 16 potential future corridors during the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II planning study, which was initiated in the fall of 2008 by the New York City Department of Transportation and MTA New York City Transit BRT Phase II study initiated. NYCT and DOT compiled a list of over 40 potential BRT corridors 40 corridors based on a transit needs analysis and public feedback, then narrowed the field to 16 using five evaluation criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity 16 corridors identified from over 40. The corridor appears as route number 7 under "BRT Phase II Candidates" in the study's corridor table Table of Routes listing Southern Brooklyn East-West as candidate 7.

The corridor was not selected for the short list of three priority candidates (Webster Avenue/Third Avenue, Hillside Avenue, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst), which were identified for the next detailed BRT planning effort Short list of 3 candidates identified. The Phase II public outreach effort, conducted in the spring of 2009, included seven citywide workshops and an online survey that drew over 325 workshop participants 325 people, representatives from 47 community and business groups 47 groups, staff from 23 local elected officials' offices 23 offices, and over 600 survey respondents 600 people Public outreach metrics from spring 2009.
