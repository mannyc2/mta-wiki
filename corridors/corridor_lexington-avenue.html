Lexington Avenue, 60th Street to 52nd Street, Manhattan

corridor_lexington-avenue

This corridor covers an eight-block segment of Lexington Avenue, 60th Street to 52nd Street, Manhattan in midtown Manhattan, where NYC DOT has proposed upgrading the existing curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane Lexington Avenue Bus Lane Upgrades 60th Street to 52nd Street. Eight MTA bus routes travel through this segment — BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM11, SIM22, SIM26, and SIM6 — carrying approximately 71,000 daily riders 8 MTA bus routes and 71,000 riders. Average bus speed on the corridor is 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours average speed 6 mph overall, 5 mph daytime.

The existing curbside bus lane is frequently blocked by loading and passenger pick-up/drop-off activity, which contributes to the low speeds existing curbside bus lane often blocked. In the past five years, there have been 88 injuries in the project area, six of which were severe 88 injuries, 6 severe. Since congestion pricing was implemented, traffic on Lexington has reduced by up to 17% in the AM peak and up to 21% in the PM peak, and NYC DOT expects the proposed bus lane upgrade to accommodate existing traffic volumes congestion pricing traffic reduction and DOT capacity expectation.

The proposed design converts the curbside bus lane to a dedicated offset bus lane, preserving a curbside lane for quick pick-up/drop-off and a parking lane proposed street cross-section: parking, through lanes, offset bus lane, curbside drop-off. Enforcement would use bus-mounted cameras under the MTA Automated Camera Enforcement Program enforced by bus-mounted camera. A similar offset design was implemented on Lexington Avenue from 96th Street to 60th Street in 2019, where bus speeds have since increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35% similar design implemented 96th-60th Street in 2019, results. NYC DOT presented the proposal to Community Board 5 (September 15, 2025), Community Board 6 (October 6, 2025), and Community Board 8 (October 8, 2025), with implementation targeted for Fall 2025 or Spring 2026, followed by curb regulation refinements in 2026 next steps and implementation timeline.
