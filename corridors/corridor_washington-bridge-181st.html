Washington Bridge and West 181 Street Corridor

corridor_washington-bridge-181st

The Washington Bridge and West 181 Street Corridor runs from University Avenue to Broadway and covers both the Washington Bridge and West 181 Street corridor metrics. It is 0.8 miles long, serves five routes (Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, Bx36), and carries 73,000 combined daily riders corridor metrics. The corridor connects neighborhoods across the Bronx with Washington Heights in Upper Manhattan, providing subway connections and inter-city transit access at the George Washington Bridge Bus Terminal, and is highly congested due to its location adjacent to the George Washington Bridge and the Cross-Bronx Expressway background.

As part of the Bronx Bus Network Redesign planning process, MTA and NYCDOT scored 46 Bronx corridors using criteria including ridership demand, bus speed, and reliability; the Washington Bridge and West 181 Street Corridor ranked among the top ten highest-scoring corridors where bus priority improvements would provide the greatest benefit top corridors list corridor analysis methodology. West 181 Street is 50 feet wide with one travel lane in each direction, a center turning lane, and curbside parking; during afternoon rush hour the eastbound curbside lane becomes a bus-only lane street configuration. The Bx11 and Bx13 were both upgraded to 8-minutes-or-better all-day frequency, sending more buses through this congested corridor and necessitating additional transit priority treatments frequency upgrade frequency table.

The 2021 addendum to the Final Plan treats the corridor in two segments: the West 181st Street portion (Amsterdam Avenue to Broadway) was completed as a busway in April 2021, while the Washington Bridge portion (Amsterdam Avenue to University Avenue) remains a "Future Plan" priority corridor with the same route set addendum status table completed projects.
