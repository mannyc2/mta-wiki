Kings Highway (CB 11, Brooklyn)

corridor_kings-hwy-brooklyn-b82-cb11

Kings Highway in Community Board 11 (Brooklyn) is a narrow roadway with one lane in each direction that serves as an important east-west transit corridor connecting to D, N, and F subway stations p017_c0001 p017_c0002. The B82 route serves 28,000 daily riders systemwide, with about 9,800 of those riders boarding in CB 11 p003_c0003 p012_c0002.

As part of the B82 Select Bus Service project presented to CB 11 in February 2018, bus stop delays (21%) 21 percent, signal delays (29%) 29 percent, in-motion time (48%) 48 percent, and other delays (2%) 2 percent were documented as the composition of bus travel time p008_c0002. A large majority of visitors to Kings Highway (75-80%) arrive without a car, on foot or by transit p020_c0002.

Four bus lane segments were proposed in CB 11, operating part-time (7-10 AM and 4-7 PM): westbound from Bay Pkwy to W 12 St and from W 8 St to W 5 St; eastbound from W 10 St to W 7 St and from Van Sicklen St to McDonald Ave p022_c0004 p022_c0007. The net parking change from these transit improvements was a loss of 24 spaces in terms of raw count, or one net 24-hour parking spot when accounting for new spaces created along medians and from removing two bus stops p034_c0003 p024_c0004.

Pedestrian safety improvements were also planned at three intersections (Kings Hwy & W 11 St, Quentin Rd & W 11 St, Kings Hwy & W 10 St), converting striped median "dead space" into painted pedestrian refuge areas and creating new parking spaces p023_c0003 p023_c0004 p023_c0005.

The B82 corridor was identified as a candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study p003_c0002. The B82 SBS was planned to replace the B82 Limited, operating the same stops but for more hours of the day p010_c0002.
