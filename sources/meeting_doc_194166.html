### [p002_c0001] Overview of the Transit Adjudication Bureau
[p002_c0002] The Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB) processes Notices of Violation (NOVs) issued for violations of the rules governing conduct in subway and bus facilities of the New York City transit system, including fare evasion. TAB enforces these rules by processing NOVs and imposing civil penalties, with the goal of promoting safety, compliance, and accountability on transit. NOVs may be issued by either the NYPD, MTAPD, or the EAGLE Team. TAB also administers an appeals process for individuals who wish to challenge an NOV.
[p002_c0003] TAB revenue consists of payments from: (1) fines in response to NOVs; (2) amounts collected through the garnishment of New York State tax refunds via the Statewide Offset Program (SWOP) for individuals who fail to pay; (3) MTA Bus reimbursements; and (4) other revenue sources.
[p002_c0004] TAB was established by the New York State Legislature and is an administrative tribunal within New York City Transit.
### [p002_c0005] A note on comparing 2025 data to earlier years
[p002_c0006] On January 1, 2025, the fine structure for fare evasion violations changed. There were no changes to non-fare evasion violations. 1 Instead of flat fines ($100 on NYCTA subways and buses and $50 on MTA Bus Company buses), fines for fare evasion are now based on the number of violations committed during a four-year period:
[p002_c0007]

```text
Full-fare customers Fair Fare NYC participants Warning (first offense) Warning; no fine Tier Two 2 (second offense) $100 fine Those who pay on time in full receive a $50 credit on an OMNY card $50 fine Those who pay on time in full receive a $25 credit on an OMNY card Tier Three 2 (third offense) $150 fine $75 fine
```
[p002_c0008] _1 Fines for non-fare evasion violations can be found at MTA Rules of Conduct._
[p002_c0009] _2 Individuals in the Tier 2 and 3 categories who are eligible for the Fair Fares NYC program but are not enrolled have an opportunity to get their fine waived by enrolling in the program between the violation issuance date and hearing date._

### [p006_c0001] TAB Hearings
[p006_c0002] A case is adjudicated when a respondent requests a hearing, and a determination is made by a hearing officer.
[p006_c0003] In 2025 year to date, approximately 89% of the fare evasion offenses resulted in warnings. As a result, both the number of case adjudications and the amount of money collected by TAB have decreased.
