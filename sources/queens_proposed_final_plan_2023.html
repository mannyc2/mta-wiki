## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] Why Redesign the Queens Bus Network?
[p003_c0002] Queens has more bus routes, and bus riders, than any other borough
- [p003_c0003] ▪ 113 bus routes that serve ~800,000 customers on an average weekday
[p003_c0004] Queens is growing and changing - demographics and travel patterns are shifting
- [p003_c0005] ▪ Jamaica, Flushing, and Long Island City are booming, as are employment centers located farther away from the subway
[p003_c0006] The Queens bus network has not substantially changed in decades
- [p003_c0007] ▪ Many routes follow old trolley lines; incremental changes have resulted in slow, indirect routes with closely-spaced bus stops
[p003_c0008] As our customers' needs change, we must change with them
[p003_c0009] _3_

[p004_c0002] Bus service performance and ridership has been decreasing over the past few years

### [p005_c0001] How Are We Redesigning the Bus Network?
### [p005_c0002] Customer Priorities
[p005_c0003] We asked customers how we should prioritize our efforts to develop a better bus network. They answered with the four priorities below. These priorities are the goals for the redesign.
### [p005_c0004] Reliable Service
[p005_c0005] Customers want to be able to rely on buses to arrive when expected
### [p005_c0006] Faster Travel
[p005_c0007] Customers expressed concerns about delays, slow bus service, and congestion
### [p005_c0008] Better Connections
[p005_c0009] Customers want improved intra- and inter-borough connections
### [p005_c0010] Simplified Service
[p005_c0011] Customers want bus service that's simple and easy to understand
[p005_c0012] _5_

## Page 6

### [p006_c0001] How Are We Redesigning the Bus Network?
### [p006_c0002] Redesign Strategies
[p006_c0003] We have used the following strategies to help achieve the four customer priorities:
### [p006_c0004] Simplify the Network with Improved Route Design
- [p006_c0005] • Straighter and more direct routing • New route types • Less redundant service and fewer route variants
### [p006_c0006] Enhance Connectivity
- [p006_c0007] • Improve the bus network grid to create new connections • Create new routes to address gaps in the bus network • Strengthen interborough bus travel to the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Manhattan
### [p006_c0008] Improve Frequency
- [p006_c0009] • Build a better all-day frequent network • Allocate service to align with routing changes and to better meet customer needs
### [p006_c0010] Balance Bus Stops
- [p006_c0011] • Increase stop spacing to speed up buses and improve reliability for customers • Improve average stop spacing based on new route types
### [p006_c0012] Expand Bus Priority with NYC DOT
- [p006_c0013] • More busways, bus lanes, and other treatments to speed up service and improve reliability
### [p006_c0014] Improve Transit Equity
- [p006_c0015] • Focus improvements in areas with greater need for transit service
### [p006_c0016] Improve Accessibility
- [p006_c0017] • Strengthen connections to ADA-accessible subway stations, fill gaps in the bus network, maintain bus stops where they are most needed, and work with NYC DOT to improve accessible bus stop conditions
[p006_c0018] _6_

- [p026_c0008] • Following the public hearing, if there are any significant revisions made to the proposals in the Proposed Final Plan, the MTA will publish a Proposed Final Plan Addendum summarizing those final changes based on public feedback.
### [p026_c0009] Board Vote
- [p026_c0010] • The MTA Board will hear and vote on the proposed redesign of the Queens Bus Network.
### [p026_c0011] Implementation
- [p026_c0012] • If approved, the MTA would begin the process of implementation. • Due to the size of the Queens bus network, implementation is anticipated to take several months of both internal work and external work in coordination with NYC DOT.
