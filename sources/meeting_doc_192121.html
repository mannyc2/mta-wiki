### [p003_c0008] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0009] 
Demetrius Crichlow, President, NYCT, delivered the President's Report.
[p003_c0010] 
President Crichlow reflected on a year of progress anchored to two priorities: safety and service. He summarized multiple accomplishments over the past year.
[p003_c0011] 
Specifically, he stated Subways reached 85% on-time-performance in September, which was the best September in modern record-keeping history. He reported similar metrics for August and May. President Crichlow also noted Subway's one billionth subway ride occurred earlier than expected. He credited data-driven operations (including the Performance Analysis Group and the Speed Team), service delivery, car equipment, maintenance of way, and coordination with NYPD.
[p003_c0012] 
He reported R211 cars were introduced on additional lines, including their first deployment in the Bronx and on the Staten Island Railway. He noted his team planned and executed robust alternative service during major outages (including Crosstown CBTC Project on the G and Rockaway South Channel Bridge work) to keep riders connected during capital work.
[p003_c0013] 
He stated in accordance with the goals set by Governor Hochul, cameras were installed in every subway car, and platform barriers are on track for 100+ stations.
[p003_c0014] 
He reported the multi-year Queens Bus Network Redesign was implemented. He stated ACE automated camera enforcement (ACE) now operates on 47 routes, and has yielded numerous benefits.

[p004_c0001] 
He discussed continued excellent service from Paratransit, including 94% of pick-ups occurring within a thirty-minute window.
[p004_c0002] 
Noting safe and reliable service is conditioned upon six strategic priorities, President Crichlow discussed each priority: (1) deliver better service; (2) promote safety & respect; (3) increase appeal for customers; (4) provide 21 st Century bus service; (5) achieve financial stability; and (6) strengthen and expand the network.
[p004_c0003] 
William Amarosa, Senior Vice President, Subways, NYCT, delivered the Subways Report. He highlighted very strong September metrics, including 85% weekday on-time-performance for the month, as well as strong weekend service. He also reported very strong elevator and escalator reliability, with elevator reliability at 98.3%.
[p004_c0004] 
Frank Farrell, Senior Vice President, Department of Buses, NYCT and MTA Bus, delivered the Buses Report. He highlighted the expansion of ACE to four additional routes and noted the program continues to foster improvements in bus speeds and to enhance safety for all.
[p004_c0005] 
Rachel Cohen, Vice President, Paratransit, NYCT, delivered the Paratransit Report. She reported September was a busy month, with 950,000 trips completed for more than 1.3 million riders. She noted although there was a small drop from the previous month, OTP remained well above the 94% goal.
[p004_c0006] 
Bernard Jackson, Chief Operating Officer, NYCT, delivered the Ridership Report. He highlighted a new post pandemic high for weekday paid subway ridership in September. On the Buses side, he highlighted 1.48 million for paid weekday ridership, also a positive metric.
[p004_c0007] 
President Crichlow recognized 22 individual employees as Transit All-Stars for the month, and thanked them for their contributions.

### [p004_c0009] 6. SAFETY AND SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0010] 
Keith Shine, Deputy Chief, Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0011] 
Deputy Chief Shine reported for September, the subway system recorded 158 crimes, a 16.8% decrease (32 fewer) year-over-year, driven by declines in grand larceny (down 25%) and felony assault (down 18%). He stated year-to-date crime is down approximately 4.2%. He stated officers made 24,000+ arrests, with notable growth in violation arrests that target highly visible, quality-of-life offenses to improve rider perceptions of order and safety.

### [p005_c0001] 7. PROCUREMENTS
[p005_c0002] 
Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, NYCT, delivered the Procurements Report.
[p005_c0003] 
He stated there were two items this month. He stated the first item was for a pre-priced parts agreement with NFI Parts. He stated it covers various replacement parts identified as replaceable only from or through NFI which are required for NYCT and MTA Bus to maintain their bus fleets. He stated NFI is the sole distributor and re-seller of these parts.
[p005_c0004] 
He stated the second item was for a contract modification with MotivePower, a Wabtec company, to purchase an additional 45 diesel battery hybrid locomotives. He stated the base contract was awarded in 2000 for 25 locomotives. He stated the locomotives are for work purposes and can operate on battery power when in tunnels and tubes.
[p005_c0005] 
Due to lack of quorum, there was no vote on the procurements.
