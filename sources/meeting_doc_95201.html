### [p001_c0002] Standard Follow-Up Report: Transit Adjudication Bureau, 2 nd Quarter 2022
[p001_c0003] The purpose of this quarterly report is to update the Transit Committee on Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB) activities and outcomes, as reflected by several Key Indicators.
[p001_c0004] TAB is the statutory administrative tribunal that receives, processes, adjudicates and pursues collection of civil penalties arising from the tens of thousands of civil summonses that are issued each year for violations of the Transit Rules of Conduct.
[p001_c0005] This quarterly reporting on TAB activities and outcomes commenced in 1992. The report provides the Committee with metrics, covering the most recently completed quarter, for the following Key Indicators:
- [p001_c0006] • Number of TAB violations received by TAB • Number and dollar amount of payments TAB received • TAB revenues and expenses for the quarter • Number of cases adjudicated

### [p002_c0001] Transit Adjudication Bureau, Second Quarter 2022
[p002_c0002] The following is a comparison of the key indicators for the second quarter of 2022 as compared to the same period in 2021.
- [p002_c0003] • TAB violations issued in the second quarter of 2022 (Q2 2022) increased by 62.2%, from 27,748 in 2021 to 45,001 in 2022. • TAB received 33,551 payments in Q2 2022, a 4.7% decrease from the 35,205 received in Q2 2021. Direct payments increased by 41.5% (from 15,112 to 21,387) compared to the second quarter of 2021. The number of second quarter payments received from state tax refunds decreased from 20,093 in 2021 to 12,164 in 2022. • Total revenue for Q2 2022 was 5.9% higher than in 2021 ($4,753,275 versus $4,488,127). Receipts from direct payments in Q2 2022 increased by 40.9% ($1,987,687 compared to $1,410,417 in 2021). Receipts from state tax refunds for Q2 2022 relating to outstanding judgments from prior years totaled $2,777,502, representing a 10.3% decrease from Q2 2021 state tax refund receipts of $3,097,082. • TAB revenue for Q2 2022 exceeded expenses by $3,854,674. This compared to a margin of $3,511,596 for Q2 2021. Second-quarter expenses decreased by 8% relative to 2021 ($898,601 compared to $976,531).
