### [p004_c0004] Summer Sendoff

Cited by 3 records

[p004_c0005] 
Well, another summer has come and gone and looking back at the past few months in ridership, we see that the annual seasonal swoon was certainly in effect, and it was one that was more or less proportional to pre-COVID summer ridership dips. Nonetheless, LIRR weekday daily ridership still surpassed the 200,000 mark most days – even during the dog days – and both railroads saw significant ridership growth vs. the June-through-August months of 2022. Summer non-commutation ridership grew at a faster pace than commutation on both railroads, with both Metro-North and LIRR non-commutation totals hovering at an impressive 96% of pre-COVID levels.
[p004_c0006] 
This was also a very good year for LIRR summer tours, with sales to-date increasing 22% over last year.

Cited by 1 record

[p004_c0007] 
As if on cue, ridership climbed once again beginning in late-August with the LIRR recording its highest Saturday since the pandemic on August 19, and there were some top-ten Sundays in August as well. The second highest LIRR post-COVID weekday occurred on September 5, the day after Labor Day.
[p004_c0008] 
Similar to Long Island Rail Road, June is typically one of the top ridership months of the year on Metro-North, which experienced a typical dip in total ridership of 6% in July and a gentle return of 3% in August. However, it's worth noting that August 2023 returned to the 68% share of 2019 seen in June, which may be attributed to weekend Yankees-Red Sox series in both June and August, one of which coincided with a post-COVID Saturday record of 124,000 riders on August 19. Ridership totals from the first half of September indicate that the climb is likely to continue.

Cited by 2 records

[p004_c0009] 
And, as Novak Djokovic and Coco Gauff brought down the house at the National Tennis Center, Long Island Rail Road service was a major player off the court with robust service to Mets-Willets Point throughout the U.S. Open. From August 22 through September 10, almost 227,000 tickets to and from Mets-Willets Point were sold. Mixed-in with these totals were nine Mets home games, but the majority were tennis fans and we are very pleased with those numbers.

### [p012_c0005] Data Review
[p012_c0006] 
Ridership continues to grow in impressive numbers on the LIIRR. July 2023 ridership increased 22% compared to July of last year, representing nearly 70% of July 2019 ridership (or pre-COVID ridership). August also saw growth compared to the same month last year, with ridership increasing 23% and representing nearly 74% of August 2019 numbers. Year-to-date, ridership is over 26% above 2022, representing nearly 70% of ridership compared to the same time in 2019. Non-commutation ridership also continues to steadily grow faster than commutation ridership, suggesting that more riders are using the LIIRR for non-work trips rather than work trips. In July, non-commutation ridership grew 25%, while commutation ridership increased over 17%. A similar story in August unfolded: non-commutation ridership rose nearly 28%, while commutation ridership increased almost 16%.

Cited by 4 records

[p012_c0007] 
The average weekday ridership in August increased just over 4% compared to July, reflecting more trips during the week. Average weekend ridership showed higher growth as well. Saturdays rose just over 5%, while Sundays went up just under 5% compared to last month, primarily due to discretionary summer travel.
### [p012_c0008] Moving Forward
[p012_c0009] 
August broke new weekend ridership records. August 19 marked the highest Saturday ridership since the beginning of the pandemic with 144,075 riders on the LIIRR. Several Sundays also broke the top ten Sunday post-COVID ridership records. LIIRR also provided extra summer service to Long Island, offering discounted escorted tours and packages to many popular attractions. Leisure ridership to summer attractions including tours, cruises and beaches increased significantly, placing Long Island among the top popular local destinations. Home games of New York's own sports teams and local events also brought riders to LIIRR this summer.

> [p026_c0003] A photograph of the existing Pelham Substation, a temporary structure made of metal containers, elevated on a platform with a yellow safety railing. [p026_c0005] 
This month, Metro-North and MTA Construction & Development are awarding a design-build contract to replace the decades-old Pelham Substation on the New Haven Line. Installed in the 1980s, the existing substation was a temporary solution to extend the range of the third rail/overhead catenary switchover territory that exists on the New Haven Line. Today, the device is nearing the end of its useful life and is no longer supported by the manufacturers, making it difficult for Metro-North crews to maintain.

Cited by 4 records: New Haven Line Pelham Substation Replacement +3 more

[p026_c0006] 
Under this new design-build contract, the project will replace the existing substation with a new, walk-in substation connected to the railroad's third rail system. The substation will include all necessary electrical, communications, and circuitry equipment to improve power supply capacity and resiliency, all while meeting operational safety requirements. The substation will also be more resilient to extreme weather conditions, with the facility constructed on top of a waterproof cable vault and all critical equipment elevated to withstand flooding conditions. After the new substation is commissioned and the cutover of the new substation to the power system is completed, the existing substation will be decommissioned and demolished.

Cited by 1 record: New Haven Line Pelham Substation Replacement

[p026_c0007] 
This project will both improve the New Haven Line's power supply capacity, as well as harden the resiliency of the railroad's power infrastructure. Substations are critical links in railroad infrastructure – they distribute power to the third rail and catenary systems, which in turn run the trains. Without updated substations, Metro-North's equipment cannot function properly. The new Pelham Substation will contribute to a more reliable and resilient railroad.
