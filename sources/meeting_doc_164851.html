### [p002_c0014] 3. METRO-NORTH PRESIDENT’S REPORT:

### [p003_c0001] Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees Meeting Minutes | 3 January 27, 2025
[p003_c0002] 
President Rinaldi highlighted Metro-North's 2024 achievements and record-breaking performance, including a 12.1% increase in ridership in 2024 as compared to 2023 for a total of 67.4 million customers and the highest on-time-performance, 98.3%, in Metro-North's history.
[p003_c0003] 
President Rinaldi discussed the results of the Fall 2024 Customer Count Survey, resulting in a total systemwide customer satisfaction of 85%, a two percent increase from the Spring 2024 survey results.
[p003_c0004] 
President Rinaldi then highlighted Metro-North's efforts at climate change resiliency, speed initiatives on the New Haven Line, and new gating procedures for select trains at Grand Central Terminal.
[p003_c0005] 
President Rinaldi reported on the use of technology to improve Metro-North's operations, specifically the integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) initiatives to drive safety, efficiency, and innovation. These initiatives include AI-Aided Trespasser Detection System, Train Door Detection System, precision LiDAR data for detecting infrastructure changes, use of AI to detect vegetation and infrastructure changes, LaserTrain collection of data for obstacle detection, and use of predictive maintenance pilots to optimize maintenance processes.
[p003_c0006] 
President Rinaldi mentioned that monthly ticket holders enjoy unlimited travel on either railroad and can bring up to two additional passengers for just one dollar per person per way.
[p003_c0007] 
President Rinaldi reported on the ribbon cutting ceremony on Tuesday, December 17 at the new Haverstraw Ferry Terminal. The ferry connects Haverstraw to the Ossining Station, offering west of Hudson residents with access to the Hudson Line.
[p003_c0008] 
President Rinaldi shared that Metro-North will receive funding as part of the federal Grade Crossing Elimination Grant that has been awarded to eliminate various crossings throughout the system to enhance the safety of motorists and passengers alike. Additionally, Metro-North will receive a $675,000 grant from the New York State Office of Parks, Recreation, and Historic Preservation for the improvement of Grand Central Terminal's terrazzo floors.
[p003_c0009] 
President Rinaldi outlined several 2025 State of the State proposals relating to the Hudson Line made by Governor Hochul, including reducing trip time for the Super-Express train to under 90 minutes from Poughkeepsie to Grand Central Terminal, track interlocking and signal enhancements, yard capacity improvements, signal redesign near Yonkers, and second track on Amtrak Connection at Spuyten Duyvil.
[p003_c0010] 
President Rinaldi discussed the analysis of future projects as part of the MTA's upcoming Capital Program, including adding a third track to the Harlem Line, connecting Hudson Line service to Penn Station, and exploring enhanced connectivity for West of Hudson service.
[p003_c0011]

### [p004_c0006] 4. LIRR PRESIDENT'S REPORT:
[p004_c0007] 
LIRR President Rob Free ("President Free") provided an update for 2024 ridership on the LIRR. There was an increase of 15.7% in 2024 compared to 2023 for a total of 75.5 million customers. This is 82.9% compared to 2019 pre-COVID levels. The 2024 level of ridership progressively increased each month and reached levels closer to 90% at the end of the year. Non-commutation ridership increased by 19.7% over 2023, and monthly ticket ridership increased by 23.4% over 2023.
[p004_c0008] 
President Free provided an update for LIRR's on-time performance ("OTP"). The total OTP for 2024 was 95.65%. The OTP has been increasing, even while providing more service, with 13,000 more trains than in 2023.
[p004_c0009] 
President Free stated that one of the main goals when he became the President of LIRR was to improve the customer experience at the LIRR. He discussed the positive results of the most recent Customer Satisfaction Surveys. Overall station satisfaction increased, along with seat availability and transfer satisfaction at Jamaica Station.
[p004_c0010] 
President Free reported on the Federal Railroad Administration's grant awarded to the New York State Department of Transportation to eliminate the grade crossings at Ocean Avenue and Pond Road in Ronkonkoma.

### [p005_c0001] Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees Meeting Minutes | 5 January 27, 2025
[p005_c0002] 
President Free announced the inaugural LIRR Safety Excellence Awards on January 9 to recognize employees whose proactive and sustained efforts to improve safety for the workforce and riders were deemed outstanding. President Free congratulated awardees Steve Prokopiou of Maintenance of Equipment, Steve Trancheff of Engineering, Chris Smith of Transportation and the team consisting of Jennifer Uihlein, Nicole Jones, Nadia Pinheiro, Rosina Morales and Christine Cardillo of the Stations Department. In addition, the Stations Department was honored with the separate and first-ever President's Safety Excellence Award, which recognizes an operating department that has demonstrated continuous improvement in overall safety performance and innovation.
[p005_c0003] 
President Free thanked the employees for their hard work and response to the recent extreme cold weather and snow events in the last two weeks.
[p005_c0004] 
In response to a question from Board Member Valdivia regarding the customer satisfaction result survey for Atlantic Terminal and how it can be increased, President Free stated that the Capital Program identifies a new fleet to replace the aging M3s which will improve fleet reliability. Additionally, the Capital Program's planned infrastructure improvements will improve the customer experience.
[p005_c0005] 
In response to a question from Board Member Bringmann regarding the current policy for waiting rooms during extreme cold weather, President Free stated that the waiting rooms have been open 24/7, although it leaves us vulnerable to vandalism, such as the recent event with the bathrooms in Laurelton. Board Member Bringmann stated that there used to be email communications notifying the public of the waiting room status. President Free stated that he will look into the communications.
[p005_c0006] 
Board Member Garodnick asked about the impact of the LIRR's 13,000 additional trains that President Free mentioned in his remarks and whether the expectations for 2025 is for a similar increase in trains. President Free stated that the additional trains were due to the operation of Grand Central Madison, and similar increases are not anticipated in the future. President Free stated that for 2025 ridership trends, some lines have more than adequate service and they are seeing increased ridership. Board Member Bringmann commended LIRR for the increased service on the Ronkonkoma Branch, with half-hourly service.
[p005_c0007] 
The complete presentation is filed with the records of this meeting. The video recording of the meeting produced by the MTA and maintained in the MTA records contains a complete record of President Free's remarks.
