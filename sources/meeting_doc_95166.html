### [p002_c0001] Executive Summary
### [p002_c0002] □ EEO
[p002_c0003] As of June 30, 2022 , MTA Bus's workforce consisted of 3,773 employees of which females represented 13% (479) and minorities represented 84% (3,161) of the total workforce.
- [p002_c0004] □ MTA Bus's workforce decreased by 209 employees in comparison with the second quarter 2021 (2Q21) workforce. □ Female percentage of representation remained the same and the percentage of Minority representation decreased by 10%. □ The net change for female employees during the 2nd Quarter 2022 (hires versus separations) was positive 18 employees . □ The net change for minority employees during the 2nd Quarter 2022 (hires versus separations) was positive 97 employees . □ There were 3 veterans hired, and no veterans separated during 2nd Quarter 2022.

### [p005_c0002] □ D&I in Motion – All Agency Employee Resource Groups Quarterly Highlights
[p005_c0003] June 2022
> [p005_c0004] A group of people, including several women in purple clothing, standing together outdoors for a walk. They are positioned in front of a building with a sign that partially reads "The New York State Museum".
[p005_c0005] Black Employee Group for Inclusion and Networking (B.E.G.I.N.) – Hosted a celebration in honor of Juneteenth (June 19 th ). B.E.G.I.N. held a 2.5-mile walk in honor and celebration of the “Opal Walk for Freedom” a walking campaign started by Ms. Lee Opal to make Juneteenth a national holiday. The women dressed in purple (in picture to the left) also participated in the African Burial Ground event.
[p005_c0006] Pride Express – In commemoration of the 53 rd anniversary of the Stonewall Uprising Pride Express conducted a historical walking tour to the Stonewall National Monument.
[p005_c0007] Also, a Lunch and Learn on LGBTQ+ history was held with a focus on Stonewall. The discussion centered on the LGBTQ+ movement, a look back on the events leading to the Stonewall riots, the uprising and the gains in civil and human rights.
