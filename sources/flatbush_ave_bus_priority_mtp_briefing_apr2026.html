### [p004_c0001] Introduction and Project Update
- [p004_c0002] • DOT presented the Flatbush Av Center-Running Bus Lane design in Fall 2025, but split implementation of the design into two phases • Last fall, DOT installed center-running bus lanes between Livingston St and State St • This spring, DOT will install center-running bus lanes and bus boarding islands on Flatbush Av between State St and Grand Army Plaza, as well as new pedestrian spaces and updates to parking and loading • Project is first part of multi-phased effort to improve bus service on entirety of Flatbush Avenue

## Page 5

### [p005_c0001] Why Center-Running Bus Lanes on Flatbush Av?
### [p005_c0002] Demonstrated Need for Bus and Safety Improvements
- [p005_c0003] • 69,000 average daily bus riders on 6 MTA bus routes use northern section of Flatbush Av with speeds as slow as 4 mph • Majority of people get to Flatbush Av by walking or public transit • Flatbush Av is located in a Vision Zero Priority Area
### [p005_c0004] Project Design is the Result of Four Years of Outreach and Support
- [p005_c0005] • 45+ community, stakeholder, and elected meetings • 375 respondents across three surveys • Diverse coalition in support of the project (community, electeds, non-profits, unions)
### [p005_c0006] Design Maximizes Bus Speed, Safety, and Vehicle Access
- [p005_c0007] • Center-running bus lanes dramatically improve bus speeds, shorten crossing distances, and calm traffic • New parking regulations support freight and bike delivery needs

[p010_c0002] To improve traffic flow, NYC DOT will reverse the direction of Pacific St between Flatbush Av and 4 th Av to westbound

### [p012_c0001] B63 Route Revision
### [p012_c0002] COBBLE HILL-BOUND
- [p012_c0003] • Left-turning buses at Atlantic Av would have blocked the more frequent B41 and B67 buses traveling straight in the bus lanes along Flatbush Av. • Buses will turn left onto Bergen St from 5 th Av, right onto 4 th Av, and left onto Atlantic Av and continue along the regular route
### [p012_c0004] BAY RIDGE-BOUND
- [p012_c0005] • B63 buses would not have been able to use the dedicated bus lane to turn right onto 5 th Av. Instead, they would have had to use the general traffic lane, reducing the benefits of bus priority treatments • Buses will turn right onto 4 th Av from Atlantic Av, left onto Dean St, and right onto 5 th Av and continue along the regular route
### [p012_c0006] BUS STOP CHANGES
- [p012_c0007] • Discontinue stop at 5 th Av & Bergen St • Establish stop at Bergen St & 5 th Av

### [p014_c0001] Construction Phasing
- [p014_c0002] Construction will be coordinated in four phases: Phase 1 : Removal of two (2) existing concrete islands at Flatbush Avenue and Atlantic Avenue. Removal of existing markings. Phase 2 : Construction on one side of Flatbush Avenue – installing concrete elements, temporary markings, temporary bus stops, temporary bus reroute Phase 3 : Construction on other side of Flatbush Avenue Phase 4 : Installation of markings, signage, remaining signal infrastructure and vertical elements installations (flex posts, quick kurb, bike corrals, etc.) Throughout construction, work zone signage and protection used to safely direct drivers while construction crews operate Flatbush Av remains open for general vehicular travel throughout construction
