### [p004_c0003] On Ridership & OTP
[p004_c0004] 
June was another busy ridership month, bolstered by extra summer service to the East End, favorable weather and many concerts and sporting events - including the subway series at Citi Field, the T20 Men's Cricket World Cup and many others contributing to strong off-peak ridership growth.
- [p004_c0005] • June 2024 ridership increased 14.6% compared to June 2023 and represented 83.2% of June 2019. • Year-to-date, ridership is 17.3% above 2023. • Non-commutation ridership surpassed the same month in 2019 for the 10th consecutive time. • We broke another one-day post-COVID ridership record on Wednesday, June 25th with 271,784 customers. • June 8th and 9th also gave us our best post-Covid Saturday and Sunday. • May's total OTP came in at 94.3%...a slight dip compared to the last few months, but still above goal. Year to date total OTP as of the end of June was 95.7%.
### [p004_c0006] New Timetables & Spotliting Jamaica OTP
[p004_c0007] 
Not to rush the summertime away, but we'll be introducing new timetables on September 3rd – and the improvements will be many. Among them will be an added AM peak train to Brooklyn for Valley Stream, Rosedale, Laurelton and Locust Manor customers; three midday, off peak trains per hour to Atlantic Terminal and an AM Peak Oyster Bay Branch train being extended through to Penn Station - thereby doubling the number of Oyster Bay-to-Penn direct trains for the first time in our history.
[p004_c0008] 
The new schedules also adjust the western terminals of selected trains during the midday, late evening, and weekend hours to increase the number of customers with direct service to Penn Station while continuing to offer robust service to
[p004_c0009] 
those who prefer Grand Central Madison.
[p004_c0010] 
This comes as the result of the continual analysis of customer riding patterns and feedback, which has shown a stronger preference for Penn Station on the part of off-peak customers than those who ride the train during rush hour.
[p004_c0011] 
For those customers who do have to transfer, schedules have been adjusted systemwide during these hours to provide more evenly spaced departures, reducing average wait times.
[p004_c0012] 
The LIRR is targeting improvements to on-time performance at Jamaica that will maximize the customer experience and reduce missed transfer opportunities. Given the sheer number of trains and branches that all converge at Jamaica, it will be a sizable undertaking - but one that we trust will lead to better transferability through Jamaica.
### [p004_c0013] Amityville and Lindenhurst the Latest to Join Accessibility Train

On June 28th, I joined MTA Chair & CEO Janno Lieber, Construction & Development President Jamie Torres-Springer, labor leaders, disability advocates, contractors, employees and project team members at Lindenhurst Station to commemorate the debut of ADA accessibility there – and at Amityville Station almost three weeks prior. These two stations combine for more than 200 train stops each weekday.
[p004_c0015] 
Expanding the reach of mass transit is always a priority of the LIRR, with the ultimate goal of making all 126 of our stations ADA accessible. With the opening of new elevators and associated ADA-related site improvements such as curb ramps, LED lighting, signage and walkway enhancements, Amityville and Lindenhurst became the second and third LIRR stations to be made accessible this year. Copiague was made fully accessible back in May and Massapequa Park is scheduled to follow suit sometime in the fall – all four stations on the Babylon Branch, alone.

### [p006_c0006] Data Review
[p006_c0007] 
LIRR ridership continued to grow when compared to the same month of the prior year. June 2024 ridership increased 14.6% compared to June 2023, representing 83.2% of June 2019. Commutation ridership increased 5.0% and Non-Commutation ridership increased 20.5%, marking the 10th consecutive month that Non-Commutation ridership surpassed the same month in 2019, exceeding it by 15.8%. Non-Commutation trips continue to drive the overall ridership growth supported by higher growth in the off-peak market, which increased 34.5% above last June. Year-to-date, ridership is 17.3% above 2023, representing 80.2% of the ridership compared to the same period in 2019.
[p006_c0008] 
The average weekday ridership in June 2024 increased 5.3% compared to May 2024. The average weekend ridership increased 9.0% with Saturdays increasing 6.9% and Sundays increasing 11.5% compared to last month. Seventeen days were placed among the top highest ridership records, including the highest weekday ridership on June 25th with 271,783 customers, and the highest Saturday and Sunday ridership (June 8th and 9th with 159,020 and 132,945 customers respectively) since the pandemic.

### [p006_c0016] Data Review

Through June, farebox revenue was $3.1 million higher than the budget due to higher-than-expected ridership, partially offset by lower yield per passenger.

### [p007_c0013] Data Review
[p007_c0014] 
In June, on-time performance (OTP) was 94.3%, above the goal of 94%, while 2024 year-to-date OTP is 95.7% -- also above goal. 6 of twelve branches operated at or above goal: Babylon, Atlantic, Port Jefferson, Port Washington, Ronkonkoma, and West Hempstead. Year-to-date, ten branches met the OTP goal: Babylon, Atlantic, Far Rockaway, Huntington, Montauk, Oyster Bay, Port Jefferson, Port Washington, Ronkonkoma, and West Hempstead.
[p007_c0015] 
Seventeen incidents in June resulted in ten or more late, cancelled, or terminated trains. A trespasser strike east of Mineola Station was the most significant event, causing 49 late trains, delaying customers 21 minutes on average and reducing monthly OTP by less than one percent.
[p007_c0016] 
The fleet mean distance between failures, a metric that measures the average number of miles a railcar or locomotive travels before failing and causing a delay, operated at 152,588 miles in May, exceeding the target of 150,000 miles.
### [p007_c0017] Moving Forward
[p007_c0018] 
Summer months can bring sweltering temperatures to the region and LIRR will be taking precautions to ensure our customers and employees stay safe. During extreme heat events, LIRR extends waiting room times, initial terminals use only partial door operations, and there are equipment inspections for hot cars. In addition, infrastructure is patrolled for any potential track instability or abnormalities.

### [p008_c0003] Accessibility Upgrades Completed on the Babylon Branch
> [p008_c0004] A photograph of a modern, multi-story building with large glass windows and a prominent 'Long Island Rail Road' sign. The building is situated under a concrete overpass, and a person is walking past the entrance.
[p008_c0005] 
_LIRR unveiled a new elevator at Amityville on the Babylon Branch_
[p008_c0006] 
This month, LIRR and MTA Construction & Development completed accessibility upgrades at two stations, Amityville and Lindenhurst, on the Babylon Branch. Copiague Station was completed in May. These stations are part of the seven stations receiving new elevators and ADA accessibility upgrades as part of ADA Package 1, which also includes elevator replacements for two stations, Valley Stream and Auburndale. Laurelton, Locust Manor, Massapequa Park, and St. Albans are the remaining stations in Package 1.

Cited by 1 record

[p008_c0007] 
Over the last several years, the LIRR has been gradually upgrading stations to improve ADA accessibility. When Package 1 is complete in Q4 2025, 117 of 126 stations – approximately 92% – of LIRR stations will be fully ADA accessible. Laurelton, Locust Manor, Massapequa Park, and St. Albans are the remaining stations in Package 1. Accessibility improvements at these stations strengthen the network of accessible stations and allow commuters of all abilities to access public transportation on the LIRR. In total, the package includes seven new elevators and the replacement of two elevators and one escalator.
[p008_c0008] 
Other accessibility amenities recently installed at Amityville, Copiague, and Lindenhurst are also planned for the remainder of the package and include new ADA compliant path-of-travel to accessible parking spaces, ADA accessible bathrooms, and new signage for improved wayfinding. The project scope also entails improvements to the station environment like new LED lighting, new communications systems, bird proofing on outdoor surfaces, and other amenities to make for an improved customer experience.
[p008_c0009] 
Amityville, Copiague, and Lindenhurst were all completed under an aggressive sixteen-month schedule made possible by innovative Design-Build contract bundling and a committed C&D Project Team.
### [p008_c0010] Oyster Bay Gets Its Day
[p008_c0011] 
On September 3, LIRR will introduce its next timetable change and among the customer benefits contained therein is one that will surely bring a smile to the faces of those who live along the Oyster Bay Branch.
[p008_c0012] 
In September of 2023, our Service Planning Department introduced a comprehensive slate of scheduling changes to the Oyster Bay Branch designed to reduce crowding and shorten travel times, which came about as the result of a sizable community engagement effort – many conversations with the region’s elected officials, community leaders and commuters who felt that they should have received more of a benefit from the initial Grand Central Madison schedules introduced in early 2023.
[p008_c0013] 
Though a host of operational challenges – such as four miles of single track, the hilly topography of the branch, limited diesel equipment and the track configuration at Mineola – prevent us from an exponential increase of service along the branch, we continuously analyze our service data and make whatever adjustments we can make to improve the customer experience.
[p008_c0014] 
Come September, that analysis will manifest itself in a second dual-mode, one-seat-ride AM Peak train – made possible by extending the recently-added 7:17 AM Oyster Bay to Hunterspoint Avenue all the way to Penn Station.
[p008_c0015] 
We trust that customers along the branch will love the latest addition to their morning schedule. Kudos to all who made it a reality — a true testament to what can be achieved through solid public and community partnerships.

### [p012_c0003] Ridership
[p012_c0004] 
June 2024 was another strong month for ridership, and the summer is off to a good start in July, with robust discretionary ridership on the weekends and numerous days of logging nearly 10,000 rides to and from Yankee Stadium.
[p012_c0005] 
In June 2024, Metro-North served 5.76 million customers, an increase of 9.1% from June 2023 and representing 78.1% of June 2019 pre-COVID ridership. Average weekday ridership rose 1.7% from May and represented 72.2% of June 2019 levels. Weekend ridership is growing, too, with an 8.0% increase from June 2023, standing at 87.7% of June 2019 pre-COVID levels. Again this month, the average weekday and average weekend ridership levels are a post-pandemic record.
[p012_c0006] 
Ridership has remained strong through late June and into July. The last week of June brought our highest total ridership since the pandemic, with new records for average weekday and average Tuesday-Thursday ridership. Typically, we see a weekday ridership dip in July and again in August, with the onset of the vacation season, and July has been no exception, but we continue to track about 9% above 2023's weekday ridership totals.
[p012_c0007] 
The rate of ridership growth compared to 2023 has slowed, to be expected as we climb closer to 2019 levels, but it continues to climb at a rate well above pre-pandemic levels, at about 1% per month since December.

### [p016_c0003] Critical Park Avenue Viaduct Replacement Project Continues
[p016_c0004] 
_Crews work to replace the aging Park Avenue Viaduct_
[p016_c0005] 
This month, MTA Construction & Development and Metro-North teams are replacing major segments of the Park Avenue Viaduct (PAV), the elevated steel structure along Park Avenue serving four Metro-North tracks and carrying about 750 trains and hundreds of thousands of passengers on a typical weekday.
[p016_c0006] 
Much of the steel infrastructure of the PAV dates to the 1890s. While the structure has received partial repairs over the years, approximately 45% of the original remaining PAV girders are over 100 years old, and recent inspections discovered structural defects that necessitate a full replacement of the rapidly aging structure.
[p016_c0007] 
The project scope encompasses the design and construction of new foundations, substructure, superstructure, drainage, track and systems (including direct fixation fastener system), communication and signals, traction power, utility relocations, and while obtaining necessary road closure permits. In addition, there is limited but critical Force Account work before, during, and after the bridge replacements which helps to ensure that the railroad infrastructure is inspected and accepted prior to returning the tracks to revenue service.
[p016_c0008] 
Beyond the primary goal of fortifying the structure's reliability, the project team seeks to mitigate noise and vibration levels by employing stronger materials, which provides a smoother and more efficient transit experience for all commuters.
[p016_c0009] 
In late June, the project team completed the demolition of four spans of the existing viaduct and installed precast bridge units in its place. The next bridge replacement outages, which involve demolition and installation of the new spans, are scheduled for mid and late July.
