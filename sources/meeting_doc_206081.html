### [p005_c0002] 2. CHAIR'S REMARKS
[p005_c0003] Chair Lieber opened the meeting by thanking all attendees and public speakers for their participation.
[p005_c0004] Chair Lieber reported that the MTA achieved a significant legal victory in recent weeks when a federal court ruled that the federal government cannot terminate the congestion pricing program. A week later, Judge Liman issued an additional decision dismissing the Trucking Association's lawsuit. Taken together with prior rulings, these decisions provide strong affirmation of the MTA's position — consistent with Governor Hochul's — that the congestion pricing program is firmly grounded in established law, is operating effectively, and is here to stay.
[p005_c0005] Chair Lieber also noted progress on another longstanding matter: the release of a $600 million FEMA reimbursement related to the MTA's extensive COVID-era cleaning efforts. Chair Lieber credited Governor Hochul's continued focus on securing overdue federal funding and recognized Congressman Andrew Garbarino for bipartisan support in helping advance this effort. Chair Lieber expressed appreciation for Congressman Garbarino's commitment to representing all constituents and affirmed the MTA's willingness to work with any member of Congress prepared to deliver results for riders.
[p005_c0006] Chair Lieber reported that the MTA filed a lawsuit last week to compel the federal government to release funding for Phase 2 of the Second Avenue Subway project. Chair Lieber referenced a recent visit to 125th Street and Park Avenue, where preparatory work adjacent to the Metro-North station is underway to create space for the tunnel boring machine. Chair Lieber stated that the project remains on schedule and on budget; however, additional federal funds must be released before the MTA can responsibly commit to the next billion-dollar contract.
[p005_c0007] Chair Lieber also addressed ongoing challenges involving Amtrak, noting that the MTA continues to advocate vigorously for Penn Station Access and expanded service to Albany. While the Albany extension is paused, the MTA is engaging with NYSDOT to better understand Amtrak's contract to operate the Empire Line north of Poughkeepsie. Chair Lieber emphasized that improved clarity is essential, given the effective monopoly created by the current arrangement. In addition to the Albany discussion, Chair Lieber noted a significant potential market for Hudson Line service directly to Penn Station via the West Side, as reflected in the 20-Year Needs Assessment. [p006_c0002] Chair Lieber noted that, thanks to last year's operating budget agreement spearheaded by Governor Hochul, the MTA is not facing a major financial crisis this budget cycle. However, two legislative items remain high priorities:
[p006_c0003] Number one is Governor Hochul's auto insurance reform proposal, which could save the MTA approximately $50 million annually by reducing liability exposure related to vehicle crashes. Chair Lieber noted that transit systems across the state, Albany, Syracuse, Buffalo, Rochester, as well as government fleets such as sanitation, police, and EMS, are similarly affected by a cascade of lawsuits that divert funds away from service delivery for New Yorkers.
[p006_c0004] The second priority is legislation addressing toll evasion, which currently costs the MTA more than $300 million per year. With bipartisan sponsorship in both chambers, the bill would strengthen enforcement tools targeting the two most common types of toll evasion: obscured or covered license plates, and nonpayment by Persistent Toll Violators (PTVs). Chair Lieber highlighted progress from the joint multi-agency task force established two years ago to reduce "ghost plates," which has achieved a 53 percent reduction overall and a 42 percent reduction in the last year. Chair Lieber emphasized that further statutory authority is needed, including closing loopholes that allow violators to avoid penalties by re-registering vehicles under different names.
[p006_c0005] Chair Lieber discussed ongoing efforts to combat fare evasion. Chair Lieber noted that MTA C&D Deputy Chief of Staff, Cathy Li would present on testing and implementation of new fare gates, a critical component of the Blue-Ribbon Panel's 2022 recommendations. Chair Lieber reiterated that fare payment is both a financial and morale issue for New Yorkers, and that improvements to the physical fare environment are essential.

### [p016_c0005] Purpose
[p016_c0006] Bridges and Tunnels (“B&T”), in accordance with the All-Agency Service Contract Procurement Guidelines, is seeking Board approval to award a competitively solicited personal service contract to M & J Engineering PC (“M&J”), a NYS certified minority-owned business, for construction management and inspection services for a bridge preservation project located at the Bronx Whitestone Bridge and the Throgs Neck Bridge, in the amount of $4,262,875 and a duration of 52 months.
### [p016_c0007] Discussion
[p016_c0008] Contract PSC-25-3079 (the “Contract”) requires the services of a dedicated engineering consultant firm to provide construction management and inspection services to support bridge preservation requirements under contract WBM-389 TNM-402 – Bridge Preservation at the Bronx Whitestone Bridge and the Throgs Neck Bridge (the “Project”). The Contract consultant will provide technical services including but not limited to the following: (1) utilize project management methods overseeing the Project from start to finish, focusing on controlling costs, schedule, quality, safety, and scope; (2) provide professional oversight of the implementation phase of the Project, ensuring the contractor’s performance meets the MTA Construction and Development (“C&D”) Project Scope and General Requirements; and (3) provide key functions of quality control, risk management, and coordination with C&D engineering teams.
[p016_c0009] The consultant selection for the Contract was determined via a one-step qualifications-based Request for Proposals (“RFP”) procurement process. [p023_c0006] Metro-North Railroad (“MNR”), on behalf of itself and the Long Island Rail Road (“LIRR”) (collectively “the Railroads”), requests Board approval to modify the miscellaneous procurement contract awarded to eLock Technologies LLC (“eLock”) in order to add four suburban commuter rail stations for the following New York State locations: Mount Vernon East and Irvington stations (MNR); Lindenhurst and Ronkonkoma stations (LIRR), and extend the contract to provide for the 12-month pilot period upon completion of installation (anticipated July 2026).
[p023_c0007] On June 25, 2025, the Railroads awarded this contract to eLock, a qualified and experienced vendor, through a competitive Request for Proposals (“RFP”) process, to supply and install secure bicycle parking cages with integrated racks at up to six predetermined suburban commuter rail stations in New York State. Up to three installations were planned within MNR territory and up to three within LIRR territory. [p023_c0010] Survey data and customer feedback confirm that concerns regarding theft and inadequate parking are key barriers to biking to transit. By providing enclosed, secure bicycle parking at six suburban commuter rail stations, this pilot will encourage more customers to bike to and from stations, reduce reliance on cars, and test the effectiveness, security, and user experience of enclosed bike cages to inform of potential future expansion.
