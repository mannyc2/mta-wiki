### [p001_c0004] Background

Cited by 1 record

[p001_c0005] 
The SIM4 and SIM8, and their variants the SIM4X and SIM8X, are weekday peak period, peak direction, express bus routes operating between Staten Island's South Shore and Downtown/Midtown Manhattan.

Cited by 3 records: SIM4, SIM4X, SIM8

[p001_c0006] 
The SIM4X and SIM8X both serve only two bus stops on Staten Island, but they make all full-route SIM4 and SIM8 stops, respectively, in Manhattan. These variants were established as part of the Staten Island Express Bus Redesign in August 2018 to experiment with leveraging the Staten Island Mall Park-and-Ride to attract ridership.

Cited by 3 records: SIM8X +2 more

[p001_c0007] 
The SIM4X and SIM8X trips are underutilized, on average, the SIM4X trips are running approximately 15% full and the SIM8X trips are running approximately 25% full.

Make better use of underutilized resources by discontinuing the SIM4X and SIM8X variants of the SIM4 and SIM8, respectively, and reinvest into seven full-route SIM4 trips and two full-route SIM8 trips while meeting schedule loading guidelines.

### [p002_c0006] Purpose
[p002_c0007] 
The purpose of this staff summary is to gain presidential approval for and to inform the New York City Transit/MTA Bus Committee of a proposal to discontinue the SIM4X and SIM8X, which are variants of the SIM4 and SIM8, respectively and convert some trips to full-route trips.
### [p002_c0008] Discussion

Cited by 3 records

[p002_c0009] 
The SIM4 and SIM8 and their variants the SIM4X and SIM8X, respectively, are weekday, peak-period, peak direction express bus routes operating between Staten Island's South Shore along Richmond Avenue, and Downtown/Midtown Manhattan. The SIM4 and SIM4X serve Downtown Manhattan, and the SIM8 and SIM8X serve Midtown. The SIM4X and SIM8X trips are shorter variants of their full route services, making just two stops on Staten Island (at the Staten Island Mall Park-and-Ride and one other stop), but serving all the same stops in Manhattan as the full-route. See Maps 1 and 2 for the travel path and bus stops of the SIM4 and SIM4X. See Maps 3 and 4 of the travel paths and bus stops of the SIM8 and SIM8X.

Cited by 4 records: SIM4, SIM4X, SIM8 +1 more

[p002_c0010] 
The SIM4X and SIM8X were introduced as part of the 2018 Staten Island Express Bus Redesign to leverage the Staten Island Mall Park-and-Ride facility, making one stop at the Park-and-Ride, and then running non-stop to/from Manhattan. In January 2019, one more stop in Staten Island was added in each direction near their first/last stops in Staten Island.

Cited by 4 records

[p002_c0011] 
There are currently 15 trips per weekday operated on the SIM4X, seven morning trips to Manhattan and eight afternoon/evening trips to Staten Island. The SIM8X currently operates 16 trips per weekday, seven morning trips to Manhattan and nine afternoon/evening trips to Staten Island.

### [p003_c0001] Staff Summary
> [p003_c0002] The logo for the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) New York City Transit. It features a black circle with the white letters "MTA" inside, followed by the text "New York City Transit" in a bold, black, sans-serif font.

Cited by 12 records

[p003_c0003] 
The SIM4X and SIM8X trips are underutilized. On average, the SIM4X trips are running approximately 15% full and the SIM8X trips are running approximately 25% full. Discontinuing the SIM4X and SIM8X variants and converting some of these trips into full-route SIM4 and SIM8 trips would better use underutilized resources. It is proposed to add a total of seven trips to the SIM4 (three trips to Manhattan and four trips to Staten Island) and two trips to the SIM8 (one trip each to Manhattan and Staten Island) while meeting bus schedule loading guidelines.
### [p003_c0004] Recommendation

Cited by 20 records: SIM4X and SIM8X Discontinuation and Conversion +19 more

[p003_c0005] 
Make better use of underutilized resources by discontinuing the SIM4X and SIM8X variants of the SIM4 and SIM8, respectively, and reinvest into seven full-route SIM4 trips and two full-route SIM8 trips while meeting schedule loading guidelines.
### [p003_c0006] Alternatives

Cited by 6 records: SIM4X and SIM8X Discontinuation and Conversion +5 more

[p003_c0007] 
Do nothing. Service adjustments to better utilize resources while meeting passenger ridership demand would not be made.
### [p003_c0008] Impact on Funding

Cited by 1 record

[p003_c0009] 
The net decrease in operating costs of this change is $2.5 million per year.
### [p003_c0010] Implementation Date

Cited by 1 record

[p003_c0011] 
Fall 2025
