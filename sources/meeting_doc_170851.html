### [p003_c0001] Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees Meeting Minutes | 3 March 24, 2025
### [p003_c0002] 5. LIRR PRESIDENT’S REPORT:
[p003_c0003] LIRR President Rob Free (“President Free”) provided LIRR ridership and performance highlights for the month of February, which has seen consistent ridership growth with an increase of 6.3% compared to February 2024 and 84.9% of February 2019 ridership. LIRR had an on-time performance (“OTP”) of 96.41% for February 2025, which is the best February in recorded history.
[p003_c0004] President Free spoke of the March 18 celebration of National Transit Employee Appreciation Day where he worked as a track member under the supervision of Track Foreman Joe Famulari and attended an event at Penn Station with the Chair and all transit agency presidents in recognition of all employee’s efforts. President Free was accompanied by Joe and Chief Train Dispatcher Mark Allen at the event. President Free thanked all the employees of the LIRR for the work they perform every day.
[p003_c0005] President Free acknowledged and thanked two LIRR Transportation Managers, Stephen Light and John Petrone for their heroic efforts in aiding an unresponsive passenger at Grand Central Madison on January 30. They were also recently honored as ABC7 Heroes of the Week. President Free acknowledged the Fire Brigade’s assistance in the incident.
[p003_c0006] President Free announced the completion of the Mastic-Shirley Station renewal project, which included a new sheltered plaza, new LED lighting, railings, signage, platform and ADA ramp repairs, digital information screen, and upgraded security and communication system. President Free stated the work was performed by LIRR in-house workforce and emphasized the need for a fully funded Capital Program. President Free thanked labor partner Anthony Simon, Suffolk County, the Town of Brookhaven, local civic leaders, and MTA Construction and Development.
[p003_c0007] President Free shared the excitement of the Big East Tournament featuring St. John’s Red Storm college basketball team. Celebrations were ongoing throughout the week at Penn Station, including a pep rally, music, speeches, and performances by Big East school bands.
[p003_c0008] President Free re-emphasized the need for a fully funded Capital Program by highlighting the major renovation work performed over the past few years and the future projects that are critical to the operations of the LIRR. This work includes switch and capacity upgrades at Queens Interlocking, Jamaica Capacity Improvements, signal renewals from Huntington to Smithtown, and from Babylon to Patchogue, upgrades to Wreck Lead Bridge between Long Beach and Island Park, and six substation renewals.
[p003_c0009] President Free concluded his remarks by acknowledging and congratulating Board Member Vincent Tessitore on his retirement. President Free noted that Board Member Tessitore is most proud of the revised training program for Conductors at the LIRR. President Free also highlighted Board Member Tessitore’s respect for the craft of Conductor. President Free thanked Board Member Tessitore for all he has given to the LIRR.

### [p004_c0010] 6. METRO-NORTH PRESIDENT'S REPORT:
[p004_c0011] Metro-North Executive Vice President & Chief Operating Officer Justin Vonashek ("Executive Vice President Vonashek") presented the President's Report on behalf of President Catherine Rinaldi.
[p004_c0012] Executive Vice President Vonashek provided the latest ridership trends for February, including on-time performance ("OTP") of 98.5%, 100% OTP on March 9, 2.2% increase in ridership compared to February 2024, and 6.5% increase in weekday ridership compared to February 2024.

### [p005_c0001] Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees Meeting Minutes | 5 March 24, 2025
[p005_c0002] Executive Vice President Vonashek highlighted schedule changes set to begin March 30, which are necessary to support critical infrastructure improvements and maintenance work. Executive Vice President Vonashek noted that train run times have significantly improved, which will be further expanded by the addition of “Super Express” trains on the Hudson Line.

[p005_c0004] Executive Vice President Vonashek announced the inaugural run of Locomotive No. 222 on February 28, the latest addition to the Heritage Locomotive Series.

Executive Vice President Vonashek extended his heartfelt gratitude to all the Metro-North employees for all they do every day.
[p005_c0006] Executive Vice President Vonashek reported on the second Confidential Close Call Reporting (C 3 RS) Symposium hosted by Metro-North on March 12-13 at Grand Central Terminal.
[p005_c0007] Executive Vice President Vonashek acknowledged the contributions of women at Metro-North in celebration of Women’s History Month.
[p005_c0008] Executive Vice President Vonashek introduced Acting Senior Vice President of Operations, Evan Zucarelli and Acting Vice President of System Safety, Brian O’Donnell.
[p005_c0009] Executive Vice President Vonashek provided an overview of his background as he looks forward to taking the leadership role as President of Metro-North. Executive Vice President Vonashek congratulated President Catherine Rinaldi and Board Member Tessitore for their retirement.
