### [p003_c0008] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0009] President Crichlow delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0010] He stated that 2024 was a strong year, and discussed the organization’s robust response to last week’s service disruptions in Downtown Brooklyn. He thanked the employees, the FDNY, and the NYPD. He commended Conductor Theresa Footman, who assisted with the F train evacuation, and who received several commendations from customers.
[p003_c0011] President Crichlow discussed advancements with the OMNY program, including upgrading all reduced-fare customers to OMNY, installing new vending machines, and introducing the student OMNY cards. He thanked the Revenue Control team for their support.
[p003_c0012] He discussed challenges with the Rockaway A Train shutdown, and measures being taken to assist customers during this time. He also reported progress with electric bus deployment, including the purchase of 265 additional electric buses thanks to the Congestion Pricing Program. He further discussed subway upgrades, including more CBTC-equipped cars and R211 cars, and noted the benefits of these additions.
[p003_c0013] President Crichlow reported ridership was at record post-pandemic numbers last Thursday, and steps to combat fare evasion have been fruitful. He emphasized the criminal justice system must punish fare evaders.
[p003_c0014] Mandeep Lali, Acting Senior Vice President, Subways, NYCT, delivered the Subways Report and discussed performance metrics.

## Page 4

[p004_c0001] Frank Annicaro, Senior Vice President, NYCT Department of Buses and MTA Bus, delivered the Buses Report and discussed performance metrics.
[p004_c0002] Rachel Cohen, Acting Vice President, Paratransit, NYCT, delivered the Paratransit Report and discussed performance metrics.
[p004_c0003] President Crichlow discussed the Congestion Pricing Program and stated he looks forward to better and safer service, as a result. He reported increased service in 2025 on local and express buses. He also discussed additions to subway service, as well as improvements with CBTC. He reported enhanced service due to Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE), as well.
[p004_c0004] He discussed the importance of hiring new bus operators and stated a goal of adding 2,000 more this year. He emphasized the importance of expanding systemwide accessibility, and detailed efforts in that regard including installing new accessible elevators and improving elevator performance. He noted the months of November and December reflected the best elevator performance in a decade.
[p004_c0005] President Crichlow reported a banner year with Paratransit. In particular, he noted improvements to on-time performance, the booking process, the AAR App, and broker services.
[p004_c0006] President Crichlow stated ensuring customers arrive at their destination safely is his greatest priority. He outlined several safety improvements, including more camera installations in the subways, expanded platform barriers at some locations, expanded SCOUT teams, and further collaboration with MTA PD and NYC mental health clinicians to assist individuals needing treatment. He noted as a result, transit crime is down and ridership has grown.
[p004_c0007] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.

### [p004_c0011] 7. SAFETY AND SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0012] Joseph Gulotta, Chief, Transit, NYPD delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0013] Chief Gulotta reported crime in the month of November decreased from last year. Specifically, he noted robberies and grand larcenies were down. He reported increases in arrests and summonses. He also noted nineteen illegal firearms were recently seized.

### [p005_c0002] 8. ACCESSIBILITY REPORT
[p005_c0003] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, MTA, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p005_c0004] Mr. Arroyo stated the return of Congestion Pricing is an exciting time, as it will enable funding for new accessible stations and improved Access-A-Ride service. He discussed the Individual Disability Exemption Plan (IDEP) which exempts customers with disabilities from the toll. He also discussed software testing on OMNY vending machines to facilitate use by individuals with disabilities.

### [p005_c0006] 9. PROCUREMENT
[p005_c0007] Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, Operations, MTA provided a report on procurement, which included one action. He stated the action is a request for approval of a modification to the base Contract R34211, awarded to Kawasaki Rail Car, Inc., for the exercise of Option 2 for the purchase of 435 R211 cars at an estimated price of $1.27 billion.
[p005_c0008] Upon motion duly made and seconded, the Committee approved the procurement.
