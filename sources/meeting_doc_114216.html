## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] What is Access-A-Ride?
### [p003_c0002] “Complementary” Paratransit Service
[p003_c0003] Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), transit agencies are required to provide complementary paratransit service
[p003_c0004] Its intent is to serve as a "safety net" for individuals, who, because of their disabilities, are unable to use fixed-route service
[p003_c0005] No limits on trip purpose or travel within service area
[p003_c0006] Federal policy outlines paratransit as a next day, shared-ride service that is "not intended to reflect that of a taxi service"
[p003_c0007] Federal policy allows paratransit providers to charge up to twice fixed-route fare
[p003_c0008] _3_

### [p006_c0003] A Flexible and Spontaneous Service for ADA Paratransit Customers
- [p006_c0004] • On-demand service in a taxi or for-hire vehicle • Customer books directly with vendor via app • Similar to popular app-based services • Sedans and wheelchair accessible vehicles

[p006_c0006] To enable access to all that the city has to offer – employment, education, health care, recreation, and social opportunities –and make paratransit part of the integrated accessible transit system.

## Page 7

### [p007_c0001] Lessons from Phase 1
### [p007_c0002] Phase 1 Pilot (2017 – Present)
- [p007_c0003] • Test of on-demand concept for 1,200 Access-A-Ride customers • Unlimited trips per month • Unlimited subsidy per month • No plan on data collection, expansion, or budget • Nearly all participants saw increase in trip frequency • 81% took 25 or fewer trips per month • 89% took 40 or fewer trips per month • $12M - $16M annual budget • Top 6% of customers use 50% of the budget • Annual cost of $98k per top customer

## Page 8

### [p008_c0001] Goals of E-Hail Phase 2
### [p008_c0002] Understand Program Costs and Customer Benefits
[p008_c0003] Pilot an E-Hail program that is more financially sustainable and more consistent with overall AAR strategic approach:
- [p008_c0004] • A more reliable traditional Access-A-Ride service for everyday trips • Availability of E-Hail service in addition to traditional Access-A-Ride for added flexibility and spontaneity • Pilot data will reveal cost and benefits to enable recommendations on the E-Hail program within 1 year

### [p009_c0001] E-Hail Expansion Reflects National Best Practices
[p009_c0002] Expanding E-Hail to 3,600 total customers
- [p009_c0003] • Add 2,400 randomly selected customers representative of AAR
[p009_c0004] Adding new on-demand providers
- [p009_c0005] • Arro, Corporate Transportation Group (CTG), The Drivers Cooperative, Uber
[p009_c0006] Enhancing flexibility and spontaneity for triple the number of customers beginning August 1
- [p009_c0007] • E-Hail Distance program: $4 co-pay | 25 trips a month for up to $44 each • E-Hail High Volume program: $4 co-pay | 40 trips a month for up to $29 each • Continued access to unlimited traditional AAR at the base fare
[p009_c0008] In-line with more mature on-demand Paratransit programs across the U.S.
- [p009_c0009] • Co-pay, monthly trip allocation, trip subsidy limits • San Francisco, Boston, Las Vegas
