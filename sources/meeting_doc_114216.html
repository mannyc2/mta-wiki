## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] What is Access-A-Ride?
### [p003_c0002] “Complementary” Paratransit Service

Cited by 2 records

[p003_c0003] 
Under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), transit agencies are required to provide complementary paratransit service

Cited by 1 record

[p003_c0004] 
Its intent is to serve as a "safety net" for individuals, who, because of their disabilities, are unable to use fixed-route service
[p003_c0005] 
No limits on trip purpose or travel within service area
[p003_c0006] 
Federal policy outlines paratransit as a next day, shared-ride service that is "not intended to reflect that of a taxi service"
[p003_c0007] 
Federal policy allows paratransit providers to charge up to twice fixed-route fare
[p003_c0008]

### [p007_c0001] Lessons from Phase 1
### [p007_c0002] Phase 1 Pilot (2017 – Present)
- [p007_c0003] • Test of on-demand concept for 1,200 Access-A-Ride customers • Unlimited trips per month • Unlimited subsidy per month • No plan on data collection, expansion, or budget • Nearly all participants saw increase in trip frequency • 81% took 25 or fewer trips per month • 89% took 40 or fewer trips per month • $12M - $16M annual budget • Top 6% of customers use 50% of the budget • Annual cost of $98k per top customer
