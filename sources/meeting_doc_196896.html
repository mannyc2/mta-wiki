### [p004_c0004] President's Message
[p004_c0005] 
2026 is officially here. Last year was an incredible year for Transit. Subways hit six milestone months with historic on-time performance highs outside of COVID years, culminating in the best on-time performance year we've had since modern reliable recordkeeping began, by achieving a weekday on-time performance of 83.7%. With a fleet bolstered by 75 state-of-the-art R211 cars, the Staten Island Railway broke a record of its own by hitting a weekday on-time performance of 96.9%, a record high outside of the pandemic era. This achievement occurred as ridership continued to grow—nearly 1.9 billion trips were taken across all subways, buses, and paratransit services, an approximate seven percent increase over 2024. ### [p008_c0008] Data Review
[p008_c0009] 
In December 2025, weekday subway On-Time Performance (OTP) ended at 82.8%. While this represents a decline from November’s 84.4%, performance exceeded December 2024 by 2.4 percentage points, continuing year-over-year reliability gains. December’s results reflect a combination of effective incident recovery—most notably a rapid recovery from a crane boom incident—and the continued benefits of schedule adjustments implemented in November. Reduced crew availability and a number of challenging track incidents offset some of these gains.
[p008_c0010] 
December’s results reinforced important signs of progress in system performance. Our continued shift toward data-driven operations and scheduling strengthened service resilience while supporting more consistent train operations across multiple lines. The November schedule changes contributed to improved On-Time Performance, reduced customer journey times, and fewer service disruptions such as trip cancellations and lateness-related skips. Collectively, these outcomes demonstrate how targeted, analytics-driven adjustments can improve recovery from incidents and deliver a more reliable rider experience.

### [p013_c0002] Data Review
[p013_c0003] 
2025 was a record-breaking year, with Paratransit seeing significant ridership increases, steady service reliability, and customer experience improvements. Paratransit provided over 11 million trips in 2025; a 20% increase compared to 2024. We hit record ridership in October with more than 1 million completed trips. The trend continued through December, with more than 968,000 completed trips in the month and a single-day record of 49,800 trips scheduled for December 17.
[p013_c0004] 
Despite significant ridership increases, we continued to meet performance goals. On-time performance was down slightly for December compared to November, but remained within 1 percentage point of both November 2025 and December 2024. With broker service, we completed 90% of trips within the 20-minute window and 96% of trips within the 30-minute window in December, well above our goal of 94%. Our primary carrier service completed 86% of pickups within the 20-minute window and 93% within the 30-minute window from the promise time. We finished 2025 with an overall 30-minute OTP of 97% and a 20-minute OTP of 92%.
[p013_c0005] 
No-shows remained well below the 3 per 1,000 goal on all service modes throughout 2025. On both primary and broker service, no-shows remained under 2.0 per 1,000 for December, with primary carrier decreasing by 0.4 per 1,000 trips compared to December 2024 and broker service remaining flat. While we continue to strive toward our stretch goal of 94% of pickups within 20 minutes of promise time, the continued reliability of our service—including during gridlock alert days, weather events, and literally our busiest days ever—is a testament to the hard work and dedication of every team member.
[p013_c0006]
