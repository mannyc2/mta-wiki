### [p003_c0002] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0003] 
President Richard Davey delivered the President's Report.

Cited by 1 record

[p003_c0004] 
President Davey emphasized that NYCT put safety first in the wake of the solar eclipse and the earthquake that occurred in April. He highlighted a successful event that took place on March 30 th , the Fair Fares day of action, where staff from the City's Department of Social Services were paired with NYCT station staff. He said together they informed customers about the Fair Fares Program and enrolled customers on the spot if they were eligible.

Cited by 2 records: Fair Fares program +1 more

[p003_c0005] 
President Davey announced the completion of major track reconstruction in the 63rd Street Tunnel which had involved substantial disruption for some customers. He thanked customers for their patience and noted that customers on the F line can now look forward to decades of reliable service.

Cited by 1 record

[p003_c0006] 
President Davey reported that April marked the celebration of the one-year anniversary since 2,300 station agents stepped out of the booth to interact with customers and provide enhanced customer service. He reported that 78% of customers who interact with an agent out of the booth are satisfied and are twice as likely to feel safe on the platform.

Cited by 2 records

[p003_c0007] 
President Davey reported that on April 15 th , NYCT showed off the first of 15 battery-electric Access-A-Ride vehicles. He explained that this pilot fleet will be comprised of vans and larger cutaway buses, and NYCT will monitor performance to assess future expansion. President Davey commended the work of the Paratransit team on this pilot.

Cited by 2 records: Battery-Electric Access-A-Ride Pilot +1 more

[p003_c0008] 
President Davey reported that the Station Re-NEW-Vation program continues apace and that NYCT is continuing to upgrade every subway station with brighter, more comprehensive LED lighting.

Cited by 1 record: Re-"NEW"-vation Program

[p003_c0009] 
President Davey reported that ridership growth continued in April, especially on subways, with average weekday ridership up about 80,000 paying customers as compared to the previous year. He stated NYCT eclipsed the 4 million paying customers threshold on the subway five times this month, which is becoming a regular occurrence.

Cited by 2 records

[p003_c0010] 
President Davey commended the NYCT team that is representing NYCT at the APTA International Bus Roadeo in Portland Oregon, where NYCT operators and maintainers compete with and demonstrate to their peers the hard work it takes and the rewarding job of moving New York City. He wished the selected employees luck and expressed his appreciation.
[p003_c0011]

### [p004_c0005] 6. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0006] 
Norman Grandstaff, Deputy Chief of Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report.

Cited by 3 records

[p004_c0007] 
Deputy Chief Grandstaff reported that that April saw a 24.7% decrease in crime and year-to-date crime was down 6.4%. He stated that the continued decrease in overall crime in April is due in great part to the significant increase in deployment of uniformed officers since February. Deputy Chief Grandstaff noted the NYPD's commitment to the continued deployment of visible officers in the system and enhanced focus on fare evasion enforcement. He also reported a continued increase in arrests and summonses.

### [p004_c0009] 7. ACCESSIBILITY UPDATE
[p004_c0010] 
Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, MTA delivered a presentation on Paratransit.

Cited by 1 record

[p004_c0011] 
Mr. Arroyo announced that new elevators were completed earlier this April at the Metropolitan Av (G line) and Lorimer St (L line) stations in Brooklyn, resulting in the number of accessible stations at NYCT surpassing 150. He also reported that since 2020, five new ADA stations have opened in Greenpoint and Williamsburg, improving accessibility for these growing neighborhoods.

### [p005_c0001] 8. PROCUREMENT
[p005_c0002] 
Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, Operations, MTA, presented the procurement package.

Cited by 1 record

[p005_c0003] 
Mr. Montanti stated that the procurement package included one action this month, a request for Board ratification of an immediate operating need and award of contracts to Hayden AI and Seon USA Corp for Automated Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE). He stated that the awards to both Hayden and Seon are for the purchase, installation, operation, and maintenance of ABLE systems and reported that the combined total award amount for Seon and Hayden is $141.5 million.

Cited by 6 records: MTA ABLE (Automated Bus Lane Enforcement) Program +5 more

[p005_c0004] 
Mr. Montanti reported that Procurement is actively engaged in negotiations with both vendors to add the enforcement of bus stop and double-parked vehicle laws to these contracts, and as these modifications are also being conducted as immediate operating needs, they will be presented to the Board at a subsequent meeting for ratification following negotiation and award.

Cited by 1 record

[p005_c0005] 
Upon motion duly made and seconded, the procurement was approved by the Board.
