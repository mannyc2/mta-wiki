### [p001_c0003] Project Overview
[p001_c0004] Select Bus Service (SBS) is coming to Nostrand, Rogers, and Bedford Avenues through a partnership between the New York City Department of Transportation (DOT) and MTA New York City Transit (NYCT). See the photo below to learn about SBS improvements, which will shorten travel time and improve reliability for the 44,000 daily riders on the B44 route.
### [p001_c0005] How SBS Will Work on the B44:
- [p001_c0006] ▪ B44 SBS replaces the B44 Limited ▪ B44 local route remains the same ▪ Northbound SBS will travel on Rogers instead of New York Ave, for faster and more direct service ▪ SBS fare = local bus fare
### [p001_c0007] Proposed SBS Design on Rogers Avenue
[p001_c0008] Parking in midday, nights, and weekends;
[p001_c0009] Travel Lane in AM and PM peak periods
[p001_c0010] Travel Lane
[p001_c0011] Dedicated Bus Lane & Right Turn Lane:
[p001_c0012] Offset bus lane allows buses to move quickly and preserves parking
[p001_c0013] Parking along the curb except at bus stops
[p001_c0014] Transit Signal Priority (Not Pictured): Buses will get an extended green light at certain intersections, reducing stop time at red lights

### [p002_c0003] Project Update
[p002_c0004] DOT and NYCT are working hard to bring SBS to Nostrand and Rogers Avenues. This includes:
### [p002_c0005] Meetings with the Community Advisory Committee (CAC), Community Boards and the public to:
- [p002_c0006] ▪ Review the locations of SBS stations ▪ Describe existing and projected traffic conditions ▪ Describe options being considered to reduce congestion and improve traffic flow
### [p002_c0007] Final Design of SBS Stations
[p002_c0008] DOT, NYCT, and the Department of Design and Construction are in the process of designing SBS stations. The design includes:
- [p002_c0009] ▪ Bus Bulbs: Sidewalks at SBS stations will extend out to the bus lane. Buses will not need to pull in and out of traffic. Higher curbs on the SBS platform will allow for easier boarding. ▪ Fare Collection Machines: Customers pay their fare at the SBS stop before they board, shortening the time that buses are stopped. ▪ New Bus Shelters ▪ Local Bus Stops on the same block as SBS stops
### [p002_c0010] Merchant Surveys
[p002_c0011] Over 1,500 businesses along Nostrand, Rogers, and Bedford Avenues are being asked to complete a survey about their loading and parking needs by the end of July 2011. This information will guide DOT in improving curb regulations in order to discourage double-parking.

### [p003_c0003] Project Schedule
> [p003_c0004] The timeline shows the following phases and milestones: Final Design: A teal arrow spans from Summer 2011 to Fall 2011. Construction: A green arrow spans from Spring 2012 to Fall 2012. Start of SBS Service: A blue arrow spans from Winter 2012-2013 onwards. Key milestones (CAC and Community Board meetings) are indicated by blue circles with arrows pointing to the timeline: Final Design: CAC and Community Board meetings to discuss final design, including parking regulation changes and loading zones. Construction: CAC and Community Board meetings to discuss construction schedule. Start of SBS Service: CAC and Public Open House to review all elements of upcoming SBS implementation.
### [p003_c0005] SBS Success in NYC
[p003_c0006] SBS has already been implemented on the Bx12 route in the Bronx (2008) and the M15 route on First and Second Avenues in Manhattan (2010). SBS on the Bx12 and the M15 has provided significant benefits:
- [p003_c0007] ▪ 15% to 20% faster trips ▪ 33% reduction in time spent at bus stops ▪ 10% reduction in all other travel time ▪ Over 90% customer satisfaction ▪ 10% ridership increase on the entire route, including SBS and local
[p003_c0008] DOT and NYCT hope to achieve similar results on Nostrand and Rogers Avenues.
