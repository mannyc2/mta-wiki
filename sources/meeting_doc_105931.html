### [p002_c0010] 4. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p002_c0011] 
Before delivering the President's Report, President Richard Davey provided notice that the New York City Transit Authority recently entered into a proposed settlement agreement that would resolve two class action lawsuits relating to the accessibility of the subways for those with mobility disabilities. He stated if approved by the Court, the MTA will add elevators or ramps to stations, subject to the terms and conditions of the settlement agreement, so that by 2055, at least 95% of the MTA's inaccessible stations as identified in the settlement agreement will provide stair-free paths of travel. He stated that information about the terms of the settlement agreement and related court hearings can be found in our website at new.mta.info\accessibility\ADA settlement notice.

### [p003_c0004] 5. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p003_c0005] 
Michael Kemper, Acting Chief of Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report. He stated 2022 has been a challenging year regarding crime in the transit system, especially when compared to 2021, when ridership was lower. He reported overall crime is up by a very concerning 41.6%.

He provided details on the “Cops, Cameras and Care” program implemented in October of 2022. He reported the program has been very beneficial. He noted police presence and enforcement in the subway system has increased. He reported that January 2023 crime statistics showed improvement.
