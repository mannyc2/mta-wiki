## Page 2

### [p002_c0001] Recap: Extending Transit's Reach
[p002_c0002] In January 2023, MTA published a Strategic Action Plan to improve bicycle, pedestrian, and micromobility access across the entire MTA system through:
- [p002_c0003] • Improved bike parking at subway and commuter rail stations • Enhanced bike infrastructure on buses • Safer routes to transit and bridges • Better biking and pedestrian lanes on bridges

## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] Bike parking at subway stations
[p003_c0002] As of this month, 99% of subway stations have bicycle racks within 100 feet of a station entrance
[p003_c0003] Since rollout in January 2023, bike racks were installed at 34 subway station entrances
[p003_c0004] By Q2 2024, all 472 subway stations will have bicycle racks
- [p003_c0005] ● Stations with new bike racks installed in 2023 ○ Stations to receive new bike racks in 2024
> [p003_c0006] A map of New York City showing the locations of subway stations. The map is populated with numerous circular markers. ### [p004_c0001] Expanding bike access to commuter rail stations
[p004_c0002] In November, NYCDOT installed a cluster of bike racks at Metro-North Fordham Station
[p004_c0003] 85% of commuter rail stations will have new or additional bike racks installed by 2024 (6 LIRR stations, 1 LIRR employee facility, and 13 Metro-North stations)

## Page 5

### [p005_c0001] Front-of-bus bike racks
[p005_c0002] In November, MTA installed front-of-bus bike racks on 3 key Select Bus Service routes that provide critical bike access connections across MTA bridges:
- [p005_c0003] • Q44 SBS (Bronx – Queens) • S79 SBS (Staten Island – Brooklyn) • M60 SBS (Manhattan – Queens)
[p005_c0004] Four local bus routes also have front-of-bus bike racks: S53, S93, Q50, Bx23

### [p007_c0001] MTA's Personal Electric Vehicle policy
> [p007_c0002] Personal electric vehicles on buses • No charging • Turn it off and keep it off • Fold it • Don't block seats, doors, or emergency equipment Personal electric vehicles are not allowed on MTA Express Buses. Policy does NOT apply to ADA mobility devices. Always give priority to people with disabilities. See the policy: new.mta.info/pev
