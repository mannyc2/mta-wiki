### [p006_c0005] Discussion

Cited by 2 records

[p006_c0006] 
Metro-North Railroad (“MNR”), on behalf of itself and the Long Island Rail Road (“LIRR”) (collectively “the Railroads”), requests Board approval to modify the miscellaneous procurement contract awarded to eLock Technologies LLC (“eLock”) in order to add four suburban commuter rail stations for the following New York State locations: Mount Vernon East and Irvington stations (MNR); Lindenhurst and Ronkonkoma stations (LIRR), and extend the contract to provide for the 12-month pilot period upon completion of installation (anticipated July 2026).

Cited by 11 records: Secure Bike Parking Pilot +10 more

[p006_c0007] 
On June 25, 2025, the Railroads awarded this contract to eLock, a qualified and experienced vendor, through a competitive Request for Proposals (“RFP”) process, to supply and install secure bicycle parking cages with integrated racks at up to six predetermined suburban commuter rail stations in New York State. Up to three installations were planned within MNR territory and up to three within LIRR territory. At the time of award, MTA Construction and Development (“C&D”) funding was available for only two suburban commuter rail stations: Beacon (MNR) and Amityville (LIRR).

Cited by 9 records: Secure Bike Parking Pilot +8 more

[p006_c0008] 
Of the five firms that submitted proposals, eLock’s understanding and approach to the project, coupled with its extensive and highly relevant experience in secure bike cages, made them the technically preferred proposer and strongest candidate for this project. eLock currently operates 23 bike shelters for five public agencies in the states of California, Oregon, and Washington.

Cited by 1 record

[p006_c0009] 
This pilot program supports MTA’s commitment to improving bicycling and micromobility access to and from transit stations. Approximately two-thirds of commuter rail customers currently access stations by car. Although several stations are located in areas suitable for cycling, infrastructure limitations, particularly the lack of secure bicycle parking, prevent more customers from choosing cycling.

Cited by 3 records

[p006_c0010] 
Survey data and customer feedback confirm that concerns regarding theft and inadequate parking are key barriers to biking to transit. By providing enclosed, secure bicycle parking at six suburban commuter rail stations, this pilot will encourage more customers to bike to and from stations, reduce reliance on cars, and test the effectiveness, security, and user experience of enclosed bike cages to inform of potential future expansion.

Cited by 1 record

[p006_c0011] 
This pilot program was initially funded for two suburban commuter rail stations at $199,200 each, totaling $398,400. On October 21, 2025, MTA executed a grant agreement with New York State Energy Research and Development Authority (“NYSERDA”) for up to $1,516,928 to support the Secure Bike Parking Pilot. This grant enables the Railroads to proceed with all six of the locations that were part of the original solicitation, which will all be installed at the same time following approval of this change order. eLock has agreed to honor its original pricing per location. The addition of the four locations totals $796,800, increasing the overall contract value from $398,400 to $1,195,200.
