In his opening remarks President Torres-Springer pledged to continue pursuing C&D's broad goal to build "faster, better, cheaper" that was initiated under his predecessor as head of C&D, Janno Lieber. He then provided a focused presentation on progress underway to improve station accessibility in the subway system, a key priority of C&D. Of note among several ongoing initiatives is that this month C&D will seek Board approval to establish for the first time a public-private partnership to deliver 13 stations through the Rapid Station Accessibility Upgrade Program -- a design-build-finance-maintain scheme structured to ensure that following construction completion, the contractor remains responsible for providing a specified level of service to the facility in accordance with contract provisions. Beyond action related to station accessibility projects, C&D will also be seeking approval of 13 additional awards this month, notably including the Queens Blvd East CBTC signal system. ### [p005_c0006] C&D Capital Program Update
[p005_c0007] 
Following a video on the Penn Station Master Plan, Mr. Handler provided an update on work underway on the LIRR Concourse, the latest phase of the overall Penn Station program. This $539M project will increase the width of the concourse (from 30 to 57 feet) for improved customer circulation, raise the ceiling height (to 18 feet without obstruction), and will provide other enhancements to the customer experience. While the project is on budget and schedule -- with a contractual completion date of March 2023 -- Mr. Handler noted that having resequenced certain elements of the work, project completion in late 2022 is now anticipated. He then cited several challenges inherent in the project, including working in an active railroad and subway station environment, and complex coordination among a number of stakeholders including multiple transit and municipal agencies, utilities, and major private sector properties located adjacent to the project. Mr. Handler concluded his presentation with a brief look-ahead to work in 2022, including installation of mechanical, electrical, and plumbing (MEP) systems and station finishes.

[p070_c0005] 
The relocation services required for SAS-2, as defined in the RFP and required under the Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Act ("URA"), the applicable Federal statute for federally funded projects, are complex and significantly exceed the scope of typical relocation services. Several tiers of outreach were conducted between June and October of 2021 to stimulate competition. In the first outreach, six (6) firms were contacted to garner interest before the RFP release. The firms responded that they: a) lacked specialization in the relocation services required in the Scope of Work, b) were located outside of New York State and were unable to take on work in New York or; c) could not execute all the services as required by the RFP. The Federal Transportation Administration offered the names of five (5) potential proposers that were invited to the pre-proposal conference. Overall, three firms attended the pre-proposal conference. Due to the specialized nature of the required relocation services, only one (1) proposal was received from O.R. Colan. One (1) other potential competitor lacked the requisite capacity to perform as a prime and could not comply with all Federal regulations specific to relocation work. The firm subsequently pursued sub-consultant opportunities with O.R. Colan.
[p070_c0006] 
The proposal was evaluated and scored by a Selection Committee consisting of representatives from C & D, utilizing the following pre-established selection criteria: Plan of Approach, Experience in Relevant Areas, Management, Experience of Prime and Subconsultant Key Personnel, Current Workload of Prime and Sub-Consultants, Quality Assurance Plan and Other Relevant Matters.
[p070_c0007] 
The Committee reviewed the technical proposal, conducted a virtual presentation, and unanimously determined O.R. Colan to be technically qualified. The Committee was impressed with the Consultant's investigative plan to identify the needs of displaced persons, and their commitment to quickly mobilize staffing needs. Additionally, the Consultant demonstrated significant and successful experience calculating the amount of relocation assistance required (all allowable payments and moving costs etc.), for displaced residents, owners and commercial tenants under URA. O.R. Colan's proposal demonstrated extensive knowledge of the work required, thirteen (13) years' experience on large-scaled projects, with a strong technical approach to effectively address potential scheduling issues and the needs of other persons impacted by the related work.
[p070_c0008] 
After the technical evaluation, the Committee reviewed O.R. Colan's original cost proposal. Several rounds of negotiations were conducted focusing on levels of effort, staffing, hourly rates, and overhead rates. O.R. Colan submitted a Best and Final Offer, with costs and rates for SAS-2 of $5,792,205. A Cost Analysis was performed and the agreed upon amount was found to be fair and reasonable. The Contract also anticipates that additional projects for relocation will be identified through the term of the Contract and that these projects will be added through Task Orders at the same rates negotiated under the base contract. The not-to-exceed amount established for this portion of the contract is $5,260,759.
