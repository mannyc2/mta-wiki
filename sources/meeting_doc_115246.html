### [p003_c0002] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0003] 
President Davey delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0004] 
President Davey spoke about the beginning of summer and the celebration of freedoms such as LGBTQIA+ Pride and Juneteenth. He said that as the co-Executive Sponsor of the Pride Express Employee Resource Group, he was proud to be part of an organization that celebrates LGBTQIA+ customers and employees not just during pride month, but every day of the year. He noted the many efforts undertaken by the MTA to support Pride month such as Pride posters and digital displays and thanked the TWU Local 100 for their support.

Cited by 3 records

[p003_c0005] 
President Davey spoke about 67 transit employees who were recognized for their achievements in areas such as safety, leadership and diversity at the NYCT President’s Biannual Awards Ceremony. He also acknowledged 10 NYCT Bus Operators highlighted on the TODAY show who joined Al Roker for a ride on one of the newest electric buses, including 42-year veteran Bus Operator Tony DeLucca.

Cited by 4 records

[p003_c0006] 
President Davey noted a slight dip in overall subway customer satisfaction, but reported on improvements in customer journey time performance, and an improvement in satisfaction with on-board cleanliness and staff helpfulness. He also spoke about the Station Re-NEW-vation Program and the goal to integrate renovations in a total of 50 stations by the end of 2023. He noted the addition of 36 bathrooms at 18 additional subway stations that will be reopening on a rolling basis beginning in July.

### [p004_c0004] 6. ACCESSIBILITY
[p004_c0005] 
Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p004_c0006] 
Arroyo stated that the Accessibility Group is partnering with the Customer Experience team to offer employee training on how to make digital content fully accessible using all the latest available tools and standards. He reported that to date, employees have been trained across three departments and he looks forward to building on their positive feedback as they reach thousands of MTA employees in the months to come.
[p004_c0007] 
Arroyo spoke about the continuation of retrofitting along 57 routes as part of the MTA Bus Open Stroller Pilot Program, and spoke about the success of the recent Bus Festival event where NYCT showed off the newest bus model with a stroller space. He also discussed the continued work to install Detectable Warning Surfaces (DWS) at remaining stations.

Cited by 3 records: Open Stroller Pilot Program, Detectable Warning Surfaces (DWS) Installation +1 more
