### [p028_c0001] Brochure Example Front
- [p028_c0002] Will update for Tremont Av and widely distribute prior to launch, in print & digital
[p028_c0003] 
Transit & Truck Priority Starting April 26, 2021, 181st St, from Amsterdam Ave to Broadway, will become a Busway as part of a one-year pilot project designed to: ■ Increase bus speeds and reliability for the 5 bus routes that travel along this corridor ■ Improve truck movement, deliveries, and parking along the corridor New 181st St. Busway Regulations 6 AM – 10 PM / 7 days a week Only buses, trucks, and emergency vehicles may make through trips between Amsterdam Ave and Broadway. In the eastbound direction, only buses, trucks, and emergency vehicles may use 181st St between Broadway and Wadsworth Ave. All other vehicles make local trips to access the curb and garages, but must make the next available right turn off the Busway; Passenger vehicles may drop-off, pickup, and short-term park all along the corridor Passenger vehicles are allowed access to every block along the corridor, except for the eastbound block between Broadway and Wadsworth Avenue. Frequently Asked Questions Are private vehicles allowed on the Busway? Yes, all vehicles are allowed to enter the Busway at each block to access the curb and garages, but must turn right at the next block, except between Broadway and Wadsworth, where eastbound travel is limited to bus, trucks, and emergency vehicles. Why can't eastbound private vehicles enter the Busway from Broadway? The entire southern curb of 181st St, between Broadway and Wadsworth Ave is a bus stop with no space for parking. Therefore, only buses and trucks can use this block in the eastbound direction. Where can I be dropped off or picked up? Pickups, drop-offs, and short-term parking are allowed throughout the corridor. Which vehicles are considered trucks? Any vehicle that has more than two axles OR six or more wheels is considered a truck. How is the Busway enforced? Restrictions are enforced through warnings and summonses given by NYPD traffic agents in addition to automated cameras. How are curb regulations enforced? Curb regulations are enforced by NYPD traffic agents; future automated cameras will only be used for bus lane enforcement. How will this project be monitored and evaluated? Data on bus performance, safety, parking, traffic, and trucks will be collected and publicly reported regularly. Community engagement will enable public input. Learn more at nyc.gov/busprojects VISION ZERO NEW YORK CITY DOT MTA BETTER BUSES Restart Begins April 26, 2021 181st St. Busway Pilot Project Amsterdam Ave. to Broadway BETTER BUSES Restart
