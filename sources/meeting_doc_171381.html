### [p005_c0002] 2. CHAIR'S REMARKS
[p005_c0003] 
Chair Lieber thanked everyone who joined the meeting today, especially the public speakers. Chair Lieber started off by acknowledging that it has been a busy month at the MTA but before he discusses the improvements and the challenges, the Chair wanted to note that it has been five years since the pandemic first rocked New York City. Chair Lieber noted that MTA lost 177 employees between 2020 and 2022, most of whom succumbed to COVID. The MTA will never forget their sacrifice, and we continue to honor the work of the brave workforce who labor for the MTA still today.
[p005_c0004] 
Chair Lieber noted that the New York has had a great comeback from the pandemic but remembers that it wasn't guaranteed. We had to fight to keep transit service running for essential workers even when we had just 10% of the normal ridership. The Chair stated we could not have run that kind of service without the determination and the commitment of our 70,000 MTA employees. New York prevailed, MTA kept going during COVID and increased service. Five years later, that decision has proved to be a wise one.
[p005_c0005] 
Chair Lieber stated that systemwide buses are 4% faster, due to the Congestion Relief Zone and to Automated Camera enforcement. Speed improvements in the Congestion Relief Zone are so great that MTA is now piloting and experimenting with removing scheduled time points so that the buses just move as quickly as the traffic. Results are positive thus far: riders are saving minutes in travel time and there a lot less bus bunching. In addition, there are big gains on the M42, the M50 and the Q32. On subways, weekend on-time performance (OTP) beat our own goal at 84%. Weekday OTP is a little below goal but has beaten February of last year. In other avenues, we are continuing to make progress. The Rockaway Rehab Project is a stunning success in terms of operating alternate service with the replacement shuttle on the Rockaway Peninsula running at 99% on time performance. Also, all the cars were taken back to the shop to be inspected and maintained while a new fleet was brought in. For the railroads, LIRR weekend ridership is pushing 100% of pre-Covid levels. Metro-North is implementing new schedules that will improve run times on multiple New Haven trains while also adding stops at Marble Hill and Yankee Stadium, just in time for opening day.

### [p015_c0006] ACTION/RECOMMENDATION

MTA Construction & Development (“C&D”) requests Board approval to award a publicly advertised and competitively solicited contract for special inspection and testing services (the “Contract”) to Atlas ATC Engineering Inc. (“Atlas”) in the not-to-exceed amount of $6,407,607 for a duration of 48 months.
### [p015_c0008] DISCUSSION
[p015_c0009] 
C&D utilizes third party inspection and testing companies to perform the special inspections and tests required by the New York State Building Code, the American Society for Testing and Materials and in accordance with other standards and requirements contained in C&D Contracts. A Request for Proposals for the Contract was publicly advertised resulting in the submission of proposals from 15 firms.
[p015_c0010] 
The proposals were evaluated via a one-step qualifications-based procurement process required by the Brooks Act for federally funded engineering services contracts. Under the Brooks Act, contracts are negotiated with the firm that is determined to be the most technically qualified by the selection committee based on established evaluation criteria. Price is not a consideration in the selection or ranking of the firm.
