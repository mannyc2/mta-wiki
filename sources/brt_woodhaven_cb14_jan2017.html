### [p004_c0001] Select Bus Service in New York City
[p004_c0002] Select Bus Service (SBS) is New York City's brand name for Bus Rapid Transit: an improved bus service that offers fast, frequent, and reliable service on high-ridership bus routes.
[p004_c0003] SBS has brought, on average:
- [p004_c0004] • 10-30% faster bus speeds • About 10% increase in ridership • More reliable service • Customer satisfaction of 95% • Safer streets / reduction in crashes
[p004_c0005] There are 12 Select Bus Service routes in operation serving all 5 boroughs.
> [p004_c0006] The map illustrates the 12 Select Bus Service (SBS) routes in New York City, highlighted in blue. The routes are labeled as follows: Bx41 : Webster Av (Westchester County) Bx12 : Fordham Rd (Westchester County) Q44 : Main St / Cross Bronx Service Rd (Queens) M60 : 125th St / Astoria Blvd / LGA (Queens) M86 : 86th St (Queens) M34/M34A : 34th St (Queens) M23 : 23rd St (Queens) M15 : 1st Av / 2nd Av (Queens) Q70 : BQE / LGA (Queens) B46 : Utica Av (Brooklyn) B44 : Nostrand Av / Rogers Av (Brooklyn) S79 : Hylan Blvd (Staten Island) A legend at the bottom right indicates that the blue lines represent 'Existing SBS'. ### [p006_c0001] Woodhaven / Cross Bay SBS corridor
- [p006_c0002] • Based on the existing Q52/53 LTD bus route • 30,000+ daily bus riders • 14 miles long from Woodside to the Rockaways • Important north/south transit corridor for Queens • Provides connections to 8 subway lines, over 20 bus routes, and the LIRR

### [p016_c0001] SBS bus stop spacing
[p016_c0002] In order to improve travel time and reliability, SBS bus stops are spaced farther apart than Local Bus Stops. In the Rockaways, the Q52/53 LTD currently makes many Local stops.
> [p016_c0003] The diagram illustrates the spacing of bus stops for three different services: Local, LTD, and SBS. It features three horizontal lines representing the bus routes, each with a corresponding colored arrow at the right end. The Local route is represented by a blue line, the LTD route by a purple line, and the SBS route by a cyan line. Vertical lines represent the bus stops, with white circles at the intersections of the route lines and the stop lines. The SBS route has fewer stops (5 stops) compared to the Local route (10 stops) and the LTD route (7 stops), demonstrating that SBS bus stops are spaced farther apart.
