## Page 2

### [p002_c0001] Nostrand Ave/Rogers Ave Corridor
[p002_c0002] 9.3 miles from Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay
[p002_c0003] Currently served by B44 bus route
- [p002_c0004] • 42,000 weekday riders – 7th busiest bus route in city • 2,000 people board in CB 1 • Buses travel at an average speed of 7-8 mph
[p002_c0005] Within a ¼ mile:
- [p002_c0006] • 300,000 residents • 62% of households do not own a car (69% in CB 1) • 60% of residents commute by transit (58% in CB1, compared to 21% by car, truck, or van)
> [p002_c0007] The map illustrates the Nostrand Ave/Rogers Ave Corridor, a 9.3-mile stretch of the city's transit network. It runs north-south, starting from the Williamsburg Bridge in Manhattan and ending at Sheepshead Bay in Brooklyn. The corridor is primarily served by the B44 bus route, which is shown as a red dashed line. Major subway stations are marked with blue squares (SBS) and red circles (Subway Stations). ### [p005_c0001] SBS Results in NYC
[p005_c0002] SBS has been operating on Fordham Rd in the Bronx since 2008, and on 1 st and 2 nd Aves in Manhattan since 2010, providing significant benefits:
- [p005_c0003] • 15% to 20% faster trips • Over 90% customer satisfaction • 10% ridership increase on the entire route, including SBS and local

### [p009_c0001] Local Bus Service Changes in CB1
