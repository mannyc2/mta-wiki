### [p002_c0004] 2. NEW YORK CITY TRANSIT
[p002_c0005] 
Demetrius Crichlow, President, NYCT, delivered the President's Report.

Cited by 2 records

[p002_c0006] 
President Crichlow stated that since he took over as President, his two priorities have been safety and improving performance. He noted that projects like the Rockaways line work support those priorities. He reported that he was excited to celebrate the on time and on budget completion of the project and the restoration of service to the Rockaways with Chairman Lieber and Governor Hochul earlier that month. He explained that after Hurricane Sandy, there had been emergency repairs but that more work was necessary. He noted that the project included rebuilding portions of the line, including parts of the Rockaway viaduct and components of the South Channel Bridge.

Cited by 1 record

[p002_c0007] 
President Crichlow indicated that he was proud of his team's extensive public outreach and communication efforts and delivery of robust shuttle and express bus service to support the roughly 10,000 riders affected by the 17 week outage. He noted the 99% performance level of the shuttle operations. He thanked the people who worked in the field: hourly employees, bus operators, train operators, station agents, supervisors, and the management team.

Cited by 1 record

[p002_c0008] 
President Crichlow discussed how his team is making a big investment in safety by supporting the platform barrier initiative. He stated that new capital funding will allow us to meet the goal of installing stainless steel barriers at 150 stations by the end of the year.

## Page 3
[p003_c0001] 
He noted that he had visited the MTA's Tiffany Street iron shop with Chairman Lieber to see the work our skilled metal workers do on these barriers. He reported hearing from customers and employees that the new barriers give them an added piece of mind on a crowded platform. He indicated that the team has recently installed these barriers at 46 th street on the M and R lines, at Wall Street on the 2 and 3 lines, and at Canal Street on the J and Z lines and that the next barriers will be installed at 125 th Street on the 2 and 3 lines, Parsons and Archers on the E and J lines, and 23 rd Street on the 6 line.
[p003_c0002] 
President Crichlow spoke about how initiatives like the platform barriers are indicative of the broader effort to bring more capital work in-house at Transit and to do the work with their specialized skilled teams that are part of NYCT's workforce. He commended their efforts as part of the 2025-2029 Capital Plan.
[p003_c0003] 
He thanked the NYPD Operations Division and noted that NYCT and the NYPD have worked together to make sure NYCT is a safe place. He noted that the NYPD Transit Bureau's Special Operations division takes on some of the most challenging assignments and asked everyone to recognize the work that they do.
[p003_c0004] 
President Crichlow reported that on time performance in March and April was at a generational high and that every month in 2025 has been better than the same month of last year. He spoke about performance gains on several lines and changes on how work is scheduled and how trains are dispatched. He reported that on-time performance this year is up by 10.4 points on the 6 line and 6 points on the J. He also referred to performance gains on the B and D lines.

Cited by 3 records

[p003_c0005] 
President Crichlow then spoke about elevators in the system. He indicated that the system has 393 elevators, 50 of which are privately owned. He stated that 33 of the elevators are in the process of capital rehabilitation. He reported that elevator availability is currently at 98%.

Cited by 3 records

[p003_c0006] 
President Crichlow highlighted that 96% of Access-A-Ride customers make it to their destinations within 30 minutes, and 91% make it to their destinations within 20 minutes.

Cited by 4 records

[p003_c0007] 
William Amorosa, Senior Vice President, Subways, NYCT delivered the Subways Report. He reported that Subways achieved 84.5% weekday on-time performance in April, which was more than a point better than April of last year. He reported that weekend on time performance continued to be very strong at 86.4%. He explained how Subways line managers are looking at information at a granular level to identify and address problematic issues with particular trips and lines. He further stated that they are working with the Performance Analysis unit and their partners at Operations Planning to come up with new strategies and pilots in order to produce better results.

He provided an update on the partnership with the Department of Transportation to launch ACE bus lane enforcement. He also reported on the launch of the Queen's bus network redesign, phase 1 this summer.

### [p004_c0003] 3. JOINT RAILROAD

Cited by 1 record

[p004_c0004] 
LIRR President Rob Free began the report for April ridership with 6.8 million riders, which is an 11.6% increase compared to April 2025 and 88% of April 2019. The year-to-date (YTD) total ridership increased 10% compared to 2024.

Cited by 5 records

[p004_c0005] 
In response to a question from Board Member Mihaltses whether the 10% increase in total ridership has any relationship to congestion pricing, President Free stated that the LIRR has seen consistent increases over the last few years, so it does not appear to be a direct correlation to congestion pricing.
[p004_c0006] 
President Free stated that on-time-performance (OTP) for the month of April was 97.08%. President Free noted a significant 28% reduction in customer injuries in March 2025 as compared to March 2024.

### [p006_c0006] 5. TRIBOROUGH BRIDGE AND TUNNEL AUTHORITY
[p006_c0007] 
TBTA President Sheridan stated that on May 12th, TBTA was honored to welcome Board Chair Lieber and C&D President Torres-Springer, along with bike and accessibility supporters, to celebrate the JFK Bridge's newly constructed ADA-compliant bike-ped ramp from the Manhattan span onto Randall's Island. Improvements like this, along with similar projects at the Henry Hudson and Cross Bay bridges, are important upgrades that advance the MTA's mission of supporting more biking, walking, and micro-mobility across our system.

Cited by 1 record

[p006_c0008] 
President Sheridan stated that this month TBTA proudly celebrates milestone anniversaries at the Hugh L. Carey Tunnel, which turned 75 on May 25th, and the Cross Bay Veterans Memorial Bridge, which turned 55 on May 28th. Historical exhibits spotlighting both crossings are on display in the Vanderbilt Passage window spaces at Grand Central Terminal.

Cited by 2 records

[p006_c0009] 
As we approach summer storm season, annual flood door testing at the Hugh L. Carey and Queens-Midtown tunnels commenced in May and will continue through June during overnight, low-volume traffic periods. These doors were installed in 2017 as part of the Agency's long-term flood mitigation program.
[p006_c0010] 
Turning to toll evasion enforcement, as TBTA moves into its second year of targeted operations, President Sheridan stated that enforcement by the multi-agency task force has yielded almost 1,100 arrests, more than 47,000 summonses, and nearly 4,600 vehicles towed. Those identified are responsible for more than $55 million dollars collectively owed to the multi-agencies. The task force now includes 14 partner agencies, with the recent addition of the Suffolk County Police Department and the New York State Department of Environmental Conservation Police. The task force remains committed to eradicating ghost plate practices, including by stopping any vehicle with an intentionally obscured or counterfeit license plate.
