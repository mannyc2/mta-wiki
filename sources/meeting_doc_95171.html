### [p002_c0001] Executive Summary
### [p002_c0002] □ EEO
[p002_c0003] As of June 30, 2022 , NYCT's workforce consisted of 46,707 employees of which females represented 18% (8,537) and minorities represented 81% (37,893) of the total workforce.
- [p002_c0004] □ NYCT's workforce decreased by 1,026 employees in comparison with the first quarter 2021 (1Q21) workforce. □ Female percentage of representation remained the same and the percentage of Minority representation increased by 1%. □ The net change for female employees during the 2 nd Quarter 2022 (hires versus separations) was positive 304 employees . □ The net change for minority employees during the 2 nd Quarter 2022 (hires versus separation) was positive 1,082 employees . □ 43 veterans were hired, and 39 veterans separated during 2 nd Quarter 2022.

### [p003_c0001] Executive Summary Cont.
### [p003_c0002] ■ D&I in Motion – All Agency Employee Resource Groups Quarterly Highlights
[p003_c0003] MTA Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) help foster a diverse and inclusive workplace aligned with our mission, values and goals, and offer a sense of community and belonging to all MTA employees.
[p003_c0004] March – Empowering Women in Transportation (EWT) joined 100+ employees and MTA colleagues for a series of events celebrating “Women Providing Healing, Promoting Hope”.
> [p003_c0005] A graphic titled "March is Women's History Month" with a silhouette of a person jumping in the air against a sunset background. The text includes: "Join Empowering Women in Transportation and MTA colleagues for a series of events this month, celebrating 'Women Providing Healing, Promoting Hope.'". It lists three events: March 12 - Celebrate International Women's Day with MTA women; March 13 - Women and Men Together, with special guest Dr. Sarah Langston, MTA's chief equity officer; and March 21 - Celebrate the Women of the MTA with a virtual recognition and honor.
> [p003_c0006] A photograph of a group of people sitting around a large conference table in a meeting room, engaged in a discussion.
[p003_c0007] April – Pride Express facilitated a lunch and learn utilizing games to educate employees of famous individuals within the LGBTQ+ community. They also informed participants of their upcoming events.
[p003_c0008] May – DDCR launched an All-Agency ERG Membership Drive. Over seven hundred (700+) members from all agencies joined one or more of the 9 Employee Resource Groups.

### [p004_c0001] Executive Summary Cont.
### [p004_c0002] □ D&I in Motion – All Agency Employee Resource Groups Quarterly Highlights
[p004_c0003] May 2022
[p004_c0004] Veterans – Memorial Day Celebration - The MTA Veteran's Employee Resource Group (ERG) held a noontime observance on May 26 at 130 Livingston in Brooklyn. They also visited the WWII Memorial Wall that includes the names of MTA workers who paid the ultimate sacrifice during WWII.
### [p004_c0005] TransportAsian, Multicultural and Latinos & Friends -
[p004_c0006] Hosted a Cross Cultural Exchange discussion to exchange distinct ideas, experiences and perspectives on culture, identify and more.
[p004_c0007] TransportAsian – Hosted a dynamic dialogues roundtable discussion with MTA senior executives of the Asian/Pacific Islander communities. They also presented 2 cultural performances.

### [p005_c0001] Executive Summary Cont.
### [p005_c0002] □ D&I in Motion – All Agency Employee Resource Groups Quarterly Highlights
[p005_c0003] June 2022
> [p005_c0004] A group of people, including several women in purple clothing, standing together outdoors for a walk. They are positioned in front of a building with a sign that partially reads "The New York State Museum".
[p005_c0005] Black Employee Group for Inclusion and Networking (B.E.G.I.N.) – Hosted a celebration in honor of Juneteenth (June 19 th ). B.E.G.I.N. held a 2.5-mile walk in honor and celebration of the “Opal Walk for Freedom” a walking campaign started by Ms. Lee Opal to make Juneteenth a national holiday. The women dressed in purple (in picture to the left) also participated in the African Burial Ground event.
[p005_c0006] Pride Express – In commemoration of the 53 rd anniversary of the Stonewall Uprising Pride Express conducted a historical walking tour to the Stonewall National Monument.
[p005_c0007] Also, a Lunch and Learn on LGBTQ+ history was held with a focus on Stonewall. The discussion centered on the LGBTQ+ movement, a look back on the events leading to the Stonewall riots, the uprising and the gains in civil and human rights.
