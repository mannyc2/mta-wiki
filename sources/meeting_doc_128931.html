### [p003_c0001] 5. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p003_c0002] Chief Michael Kemper delivered the safety and security report.
[p003_c0003] Chief Kemper discussed an incident on a Manhattan Bound C train, which is still an active case. He discussed the crime figures for October 2023, which reflect a 5.7% increase in crime in the subways as compared to the previous year. Chief Kemper explained that there is still an overall year to date reduction in crime rates in the subways. He stated that officer engagement in the subways remains robust and noted that theft of service enforcement remains a benchmark of NYPD's strategy, with an increase of the issuance of summonses and, when necessary, arrests. Chief Kemper also discussed the efforts to curb subway surfing. He reported that there have been no surfing related injuries and/or deaths since the start of the school year. He then discussed technological efforts used to address subway surfing.
[p003_c0004] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0005] 6. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0006] President Richard Davey delivered the President's Report.
[p003_c0007] President Davey reported on the efforts to improve customer service by delivering Faster, Cleaner, and Safer service. He then focused on efforts to deliver faster service.
[p003_c0008] President Davey reported that for subways, the On-Time Performance for 2023 thus far is above 83%, the best annual performance since electronic record keeping began over a decade ago. He noted Customer Journey Time Performance is at 84.9%, also a historic high.
[p003_c0009] President Davey then stated that subway service has improved on eight lines on nights, weekends, and mid-days so far this year, reducing headways by two minutes in each instance. He stated that Phase III of enhanced service will go live in December on the C, N, R, and G lines, bringing faster, more frequent subway service to even more customers.
[p003_c0010] President Davey reported that the Maintenance of Way team has installed 730 track panels which reduce defects in some of our most problematic areas to maintain. He noted that the SPEED Unit continues to identify sections of track where the speed can be safely raised to shorten journey times. He then noted that the SPEED unit has been providing 1:1 coaching with Train Operators to improve performance by safely reducing run times.
[p003_c0011] President Davey stated that the most important tool to speed up bus trips, reduce wait times, and deliver faster services is bus priority lanes and busways on the city streets. He stated that the Gun Hill Road bus lane was completed, which will speed up trips crosstown, benefitting nearly 40,000 customers. President Davey thanked Jaime Cho from Operations Planning, who was the project manager for the Gun Hill Road project. President Davey noted that the eastbound busway on Livingston Street opened in downtown Brooklyn, which will benefit over 50,000 bus customers daily.

[p004_c0001] President Davey discussed the progress of the Department of Buses on twenty-nine priority routes. President Davey noted that year to date, 52% of the routes showed improvements in three or more metrics.

### [p004_c0007] 7. AUTOMATIC BUS LANE ENFORCEMENT AND AUTOMATED CAMERA ENFORCEMENT
[p004_c0008] Frank Annicaro, Senior Vice President, Buses delivered a report on Automatic Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE) and Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE).
[p004_c0009] Annicaro discussed efforts to make buses faster and cleaner. Annicaro also explained that buses are critical to equity and accessibility, disproportionately serving low-income, minority, and disabled New Yorkers. Annicaro then discussed strategies implemented to increase the speed of buses in the short term.
[p004_c0010] Annicaro noted that well enforced bus lanes are the most effective way to increase bus speeds. Annicaro discussed the initiative to improve service on the twenty-nine worst performing high ridership bus routes. Annicaro discussed the progress made with ABLE.
[p004_c0011] Sunil Nair, Chief Officer of Bus Technology, discussed plans to expand ABLE to ACE, which will be implemented in May 2024 and have a wider impact on city streets. Nair explained that this will increase enforcement not only on bus lanes, but along the entire bus routes. He indicated that the program will be a demonstration over four years. ACE will be activated on fourteen

### [p005_c0007] 8. RIDERSHIP CAPACITY
[p005_c0008] Sarah Wyss, Acting Chief Operations Planning, delivered a report on Capacity.
[p005_c0009] Wyss first discussed capacity on the subways. She explained that there is capacity for additional ridership on most lines. Wyss discussed the potential impact of congestion pricing on subway capacity. She then discussed bus capacity. Wyss explained that there are some routes which are reaching capacity, which will be addressed by adding additional buses.

### [p005_c0011] 9. ACCESSIBILITY
[p005_c0012] Rachel Cohen, Deputy Chief Accessibility Officer, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p005_c0013] Cohen stated that the theme of the month is the “faster” part of faster, cleaner, safer, and stated that this is a perfect example of where the Agency’s accessibility and customer satisfaction priorities and goals are in sync.
[p005_c0014] Cohen spoke about the importance of keeping bus lanes and bus stops clear and accessible. Cohen stated that DOT and the Department of Sanitation are helping to ensure that bus stops are kept clear of garbage, debris, snow, and ice.
