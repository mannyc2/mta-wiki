### [p006_c0005] Discussion:

Cited by 1 record

[p006_c0006] 
As a result of a multiagency Joint Procurement, Long Island Rail Road (“LIRR”), on behalf of itself and Metro-North Railroad (“MNR”) (collectively the “Railroads”), requests Board approval to award a five-year noncompetitive purchases and public works contract for a Positive Train Control (“PTC”) Technical Service Agreement to Xorail Inc., a Wabtec Company (“Xorail”) in the estimated value of $36,456,013.

Cited by 8 records: PTC Technical Service Agreement - Xorail +7 more

[p006_c0007] 
The Railroads are among a series of railroads and commuter agencies nationwide that are required to maintain a PTC System. PTC can help prevent train-to-train collisions, over-speed derailments, and injuries to workers as a result of unauthorized incursions by a train into work zones. In 2013, the Railroads awarded a joint venture, PTC Consortium contract for the design, integration and delivery of PTC systems necessary to comply with the Railroad’s obligations under the Rail Safety Improvement Act (“RSIA”) of 2008 and applicable FRA Regulations. The PTC Consortium contractors included Wabtec; Bombardier Transportation (Holdings) USA Inc.; and Siemens Rail Automation Corporation. The members of the PTC Consortium do not intend to conduct business as a single entity beyond the point of close-out of the existing PTC contracts. The PTC Consortium contract is currently in the close-out process and will not be renewed. The work each Consortium member performed pursuant to the Railroad’s PTC Contract was, in large part, proprietary to each firm. As such, in order for the Railroads to maintain and upgrade as necessary the existing PTC System it is necessary to enter into sole source agreements with each firm individually for future expansion, enhancements and support to the existing PTC system. This is the first such agreement. The Railroads are working with the other contractors of the former PTC Consortium on similar technical support agreements which will be presented to the Board at a later date.

Cited by 1 record

[p006_c0008] 
Wabtec provided the Communication Management Unit, Office Communications Manager, as well as other essential components of the overall PTC system. Xorail Inc. (“Xorail”), a Wabtec Company, is the sole responsible source for providing technical support for the proprietary Wabtec PTC systems and its subsystems, hardware, software and interfaces to other Railroad systems. No other firms have access to the proprietary designs, specifications, and drawings for the existing Wabtec portions of the PTC System. Therefore, no other firm can provide the required services.

Cited by 2 records

[p006_c0009] 
The Railroads issued a Request for Proposal (“RFP”) to Xorail with a general scope of work to establish a technical service agreement which shall include engineering and technical support of the PTC System. The Railroads conducted a technical evaluation of the proposal submitted by Xorail. It was determined that Xorail demonstrated an acceptable approach to the work and met the RFP requirements.
[p006_c0010] 
The Railroads held a series of technical and contractual meetings with Xorail to clarify the requirements of the PTC System service agreement. The Railroads and Xorail were able to establish a mutual agreement of the required scope and are finalizing contract terms and conditions. Under the agreement, the Railroads and Xorail negotiated one-year fixed pricing for hourly labor rates and annual adjustments in the subsequent years of the contract. The final agreed-upon rates reflected an average 10 percent reduction in the labor rates originally proposed by Xorail. A labor index adjustment by the U.S. Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Statistics will be applied on each year of the contract thereafter. The MTA Cost Price Analysis Unit has determined the negotiated labor rates are fair and reasonable.

Cited by 2 records

[p006_c0011] 
The contract will be task order based. The Railroads will develop specific task assignments and solicit proposals from Xorail using the negotiated labor rates. The level of effort for each task release will be negotiated prior to the assignment of the task order.

### [p007_c0010] Alternatives
[p007_c0011] 
None recommended. PTC is a federal mandate. No other firms have access to the proprietary designs, specifications, and drawings for the existing Wabtec portions of the PTC System and therefore cannot readily provide these services. In addition, the Railroads lack available in-house technical personnel to perform the scope of work associated with this contract.

Funding for work under this contract will be taken from the funding sources applicable to each task released. This could include operating, capital or federally funded sources. Based upon availability of funds, approval from the Board will be requested if the options are to be exercised.
