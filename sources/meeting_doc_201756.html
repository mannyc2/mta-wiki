### [p001_c0008] Purpose
[p001_c0009] To advise the MTA Board about the Capital Projects Public Hearing and intent to file for and accept Federal grants for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2026.
### [p001_c0010] Discussion
[p001_c0011] Enclosed is documentation prepared for Board review in accordance with established practice to provide the Board with an overview of the Capital Project Public Hearing for federal funding. The required FFY 2026 certifications and assurances, as well as the filing and grant acceptance, will be made pursuant to the standing authorization from the Board.
[p001_c0012] Projects authorized to be submitted for federal funding are referenced in the published Notice of Public Hearing and described in the attached document MTA Notice of Public Hearing and Description of Projects for FFY 2026.
[p001_c0013] In accordance with FTA regulations, a public hearing to solicit comments on projects proposed for federal funding was advertised beginning January 2, 2026. Written comments (via mail or the MTA.info website) were solicited, and a public hearing was held on Tuesday, February 3, 2026 (both in person and via Zoom) (the "Hearing").
[p001_c0014] At the Hearing, the Director of Capital Funding Management read into the record the federal funding amounts and the total package of projects included in the Notice, totaling approximately $14.382 billion, including significant investments in rolling stock replacement, bus purchases, Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) improvements and state of good repair projects. While the package of projects heard is much larger than the MTA's typical annual federal assistance, it provides flexibility in project selection, supports multi-year planning, and allows prioritization of critical infrastructure and accessibility improvements.
[p001_c0015] Following the overview of the projects, the Hearing invited public input. Twelve members of the public gave testimony in-person and four provided comments virtually via Zoom. In addition, 41 online comments and two written notes were submitted. Collectively, these comments discussed continued federal funding of MTA's infrastructure and assets, raised important concerns, and provided suggestions to improve operations and future projects. Key themes included advocating for reliable service, prioritizing station accessibility, and improving rider experience and safety. Participants stressed the need for the MTA to accelerate station accessibility projects stating that the 2055 timeline is too distant, and supported faster and more effective construction of station elevators and replacement of older rail/bus fleets for more dependable service. Further, there was significant support for large scale expansion projects, including the Interborough Express (IBX) and the proposed Second Avenue Subway 125th Street extension to Broadway. A transcript of the Hearing and written comments are available on the Director's Desk.
