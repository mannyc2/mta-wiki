### [p002_c0003] NYC teen killed while subway surfing on train across Williamsburg Bridge
[p002_c0004] 
By Larry Celona and Allie Griffin
[p002_c0005] 
February 21, 2023 | 12:49am | Updated
[p002_c0006] 
A 15-year-old boy died while “subway surfing” on a train crossing the Williamsburg Bridge Monday evening, police said.

Cited by 1 record

[p002_c0007] 
The teen was riding on the roof of a northbound J train heading across the bridge when he slammed his head on a part of the structure around 6:45 p.m., according to the NYPD.

Cited by 2 records

[p002_c0008] 
He fell beneath the train and was pronounced dead at the scene.
[p002_c0009] 
The MTA halted J and M train service between the Marcy Av and Delancey St-Essex St stations for several hours as officers investigated. Both trains were still running with delays past 11 p.m., according to the agency.
[p002_c0010] 
The victim’s identity has been withheld pending family notification.
[p002_c0011] 
In a statement, NYC Transit President Richard Davey warned of the dangers of subway surfing.

Cited by 4 records

[p002_c0012] 
“We cannot stress enough how dangerous it is to ride on the outside of trains,” Davey said. “Our hearts go out to loved ones at yet another tragic time. We implore other families to speak with their children on the real dangers of what can seem like a thrill but is too often deadly.”
