### [p006_c0004] On Ridership & OTP

[p006_c0005] 
LIRR ridership remained strong in April with the weather starting to warm up and the summer travel season inching closer:
- [p006_c0006] • April 2024 ridership increased 18.8% compared to April 2023. • Non-commutation ridership surpassed the same month in 2019 for the 8th consecutive month – this time by 11.4% • We achieved our highest one-day post-COVID ridership on Tuesday, May 14 with 260,745 customers, and our load weight data shows that we're doing very well during the so-called “shoulder” periods – which are just before the AM and PM peak. In fact, during the AM Peak shoulder, we're actually exceeding 2019 customer count numbers and are just about right at 2019 levels during the PM shoulder. • LIRR customers continue to take advantage of Grand Central Madison service. Again, such an amazing job by our Operations teams!

### [p010_c0005] Data Review
[p010_c0006] 
LIRR ridership continued to grow when compared to the same month of the prior year. April 2024 ridership increased 18.8% compared to April 2023, representing 78.8% of April 2019. Commutation ridership increased 13.1% and Non-Commutation ridership increased 23.1%, marking the 8th consecutive month that Non-Commutation ridership surpassed the same month in 2019, exceeding it by 11.4%. Non-Commutation trips continue to drive the overall ridership growth indicating higher demand for off-peak travel. Year-to-date, ridership is 18.6% above 2023, representing 79.0% of the ridership compared to the same period in 2019.

Cited by 1 record

[p010_c0007] 
The average weekday ridership in April 2024 increased +1.3% compared to March 2024. The average weekend ridership increased +6.9% with Saturdays increasing +7.1% and Sundays increasing +0.7% compared to last month. Many weekdays were placed among the top ten highest ridership records, including the 2nd highest weekday ridership on Tuesday, April 9th with 251,964 customers since the pandemic.
### [p010_c0008] Moving Forward
[p010_c0009] 
LIRR customers continue to take advantage of the GCM service. In April, GCM ridership reached 1,579,340 customers (based on load weigh data). Customers are shifting toward 35% share for trips to GCM and 65% share to Penn Station on weekdays, with higher concentration to GCM during Peak periods (38% GCM and 62% Penn). On weekends, 31% of the trips arrive at GCM and 69% arrive at Penn Station. Local sports games/playoffs (i.e., NY Rangers, NY Islanders, NY Knicks) and events at local venues continue to bring additional ridership.

### [p014_c0002] Repairs to West Side Storage Yard Underway
> [p014_c0003] A photograph showing the interior of the West Side Storage Yard during construction. Multiple sets of railway tracks are visible, with some tracks being replaced or repaired. The yard is illuminated by overhead lights, and various construction materials and equipment are scattered around the tracks.
[p014_c0004] 
_Repairs to West Side Storage Yard started this month, which was damaged during Superstorm Sandy_
[p014_c0005] 
The Long Island Rail Road and MTA Construction and Development recently completed the rehabilitation of the West Side Storage Yard, which sustained extensive damage as a result of the flooding during Superstorm Sandy. The electrical equipment was especially affected by the flood of salt water, which makes electrical conduits much more vulnerable to corrosion. The project encompasses the replacement of several of the yard's old heavy rail systems with new equipment at the modern standards as well as general repairs to the yard's operating systems such as CCTV recording systems, Public Announcement machinery, radio base stations, fire alarm components, and fiber optic cable panels.

Cited by 2 records: West Side Storage Yard Rehabilitation +1 more

[p014_c0006] 
The West Side Storage Yard is the biggest storage yard on the West side of Manhattan, containing 30 tracks and serving as a critical terminal point for commuter rail road trains. During construction, Long Island Rail Road force account had to carefully schedule work to stay within available track outages to fully replace tracks as well as the switches and the many components of signal infrastructure. During these outages, trains used Penn Station as a temporary turning point while the West Side Yard was out of service.

Cited by 2 records: West Side Storage Yard Rehabilitation +1 more

[p014_c0007] 
Other components that were replaced during this project are third rail infrastructure, including disconnect switches, the protective boards that cover the live rail, and the electric feeder cables. Much of the equipment used in the replacements were significantly modern upgrades from the old technology, some of which dated back to the 1980s.
