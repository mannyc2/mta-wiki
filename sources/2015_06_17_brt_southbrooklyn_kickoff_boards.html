### [p002_c0001] TRANSIT SERVICE IN SOUTH BROOKLYN
> [p002_c0002] Key east-west bus routes >20,000 daily riders (thick blue line) <20,000 daily riders (thin blue line) Other Features Other bus routes (grey line) Subway station (white circle) 0 0.5 1 Miles
[p002_c0003] 9 subway routes serve south Brooklyn and mainly run North-South.
[p002_c0004] 14 bus routes provide East-West service
- [p002_c0005] • B1 • B2 • B3 • B4 • B6 • B7 • B8 • B9 • B11 • B36 • B64 • B82 • B100
[p002_c0006] with a combined ridership of
[p002_c0007] 178,000 per day
> [p002_c0008] Service need: South Brooklyn Crosstown
[p002_c0009] The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need .
[p002_c0010] Key issues raised at the 2009 public workshops included the following:
- [p002_c0011] • Bus trips are long and slow • Many parts of south Brooklyn are underserved by transit • A bus trip across south Brooklyn can take up to 2 hours
