### [p003_c0005] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0006] 
Demetrius Crichlow, President, NYCT, delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0007] 
President Crichlow spoke about remembering those lost on September 11 th . He reported that he addressed members of NYPD Transit District 4 at their annual ceremony and thanked them for their efforts to keep the system safe and the sacrifices called for in their line of work. He shared a story of two NYCT workers, Structure Maintainer Willian “Billy” Johnston and his father, MOW Superintendent William Johnston. He shared that after joining the FDNY in 2000, Billy responded to the World Trade Center on the morning of September 11 th , 2001, becoming one of the 343 fire fighters lost on that day. President Crichlow explained that after 9/11, NYCT and the NYPD conducted threat assessments in the subway’s security system, and the system’s emergency exit hatches were updated. He noted that up until that time, the locking mechanism could be easily loosened and opened, and that Superintendent Johnston designed a new mechanism which proved difficult to open. He shared that in memory of Superintendent Johnson’s son, the newly designed lock was named the “Johnston Lock,” and the tool for opening the emergency exit hatches was named the “Billy Bar,” which are now used systemwide.
[p003_c0008] 
President Crichlow reported on the summer’s historic performance, newly launched programs, and the implementation of a big service improvement. He reported that major felonies declined by 22% compared to August and thanked NYCT’s NYPD partners for this improvement. He reported that Subway On-Time Performance reached 85.1% in August, marking one of the best August results in the past decade, outside of COVID. He noted that with the support of Albany and NYCT’s partners over the last several years, service has increased, which is validated by the recent OTP statistics.
[p003_c0009] 
President Crichlow reported the completion of the replacement of the Staten Island Railway’s 50-year-old fleet with the full deployment of new R211S cars, congratulated the Subways Department, and noted that it was a generational achievement for Staten Island. He addressed system challenges due to extreme weather, particularly power outages at West 4th Street, and emphasized the importance of full funding for the 2025–2029 Capital Program to modernize infrastructure.
[p003_c0010] 
President Crichlow discussed the implementation of Phase 2 of the Queens Bus Network Redesign. He commended the over 700 MTA employees who filled more than 3,000 Customer

### [p004_c0008] 6. SAFETY AND SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0009] 
Joseph Gulotta, Chief, Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0010] 
Chief Gulotta reported that there was a 22% reduction in crime compared to August 2024 which was largely driven by a decrease in robberies and felony assaults. He reported that from January to August there was a 3.8% reduction in crime in the subways system. He highlighted an increase in police officers in trains where 78% of crimes occur, and the emphasis on rules violations that impact the quality of a rider's journey and their perception of safety.

### [p004_c0012] 7. SPECIAL FEATURE
[p004_c0013] 
Chris Pangilinan, Advisor, NYCT and Alan Foster, Director of Rail Network Planning, NYCT, delivered a presentation on the F and M subway lines swap project.

## Page 5
[p005_c0001] 
Mr. Pangilinan and Mr. Foster reported that the F and M swap project was the first redesign of the subway network since the 2 nd Avenue opening in 2017. They indicated that it will be an improvement in service and customer experience on 4 subway lines through increased reliability and shorter travel times. They reported that their presentation was focused on the tunnels crossing the East River between Queens and Manhattan, where the F and M trains will swap the tunnels they go through to cross the river. They stated that this will result in significant improvement in service along the E, F, M and R trains. They reported that it will be a weekday only change and that it will affect just 8 stations. They discussed the benefits for riding customers, the projected schedule and next steps for roll-out.
### [p005_c0002] 8. PROCUREMENT
[p005_c0003] 
Geoffrey Ray, Assistant Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, NYCT, delivered the Procurement Report.
[p005_c0004] 
Mr. Ray reported that this month's NYCT Procurement package included two items. He stated that the first is a request for ratification of two Automated Camera Enforcement ("ACE") contract modifications to Hayden AI, Inc. and Seon USA Corp. He explained that the contracts were awarded under a continuing Immediate Operating Need ("ION"), for the provision and operation of Automated Bus Lane Enforcement ("ABLE") systems in May 2022 and April 2024 respectively. They noted that the ABLE project name was changed to ACE. The modifications would expand ACE enforcement, add coverage for bus stops, double parking, bike lanes, and paratransit stops. The estimated values of the modifications are $218.6 million (Hayden) and $40.1 million (Seon), bringing the total contract values to $301.7 million and $98.5 million, respectively.
[p005_c0005] 
Mr. Ray reported that the second item is a request for ratification of the ION contract awarded to RideCo Us Inc. for the introduction of real-time optimization capabilities and automated adjustments to paratransit trip schedules and routes in response to unexpected events. The estimated value of the contract is $72 million spanning up to 102 months, which includes up to 18 months for implementation and 7 years of operation and maintenance.
[p005_c0006] 
Upon motion duly made and seconded, the Committee approved the procurement package.
