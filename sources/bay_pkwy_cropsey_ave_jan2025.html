## Page 4

### [p004_c0001] Why Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue?
- [p004_c0002] • Study areas: – Bay Parkway from Avenue J to Shore Parkway (2.4 miles) – Cropsey Avenue from Bay Parkway to 26 th Avenue (0.6 miles) • 35,000 daily bus riders – B6 Local and Limited – B82 Local and Select Bus Service – X28/X38 Express bus • Connections to F, N, and D trains • Bus speeds as low as 3 miles per hour on Bay Parkway • Vision Zero Priority Corridor: 25 people killed or seriously injured (2019-2023)

[p005_c0002] Transit goals of the Streets Plan:

## Page 9

### [p009_c0001] Bay Parkway Bus Speeds
- [p009_c0002] • Buses are as slow as 5 mph throughout the day, and 3 mph during the busiest hours • Bus speeds are slowest: • Approaching major intersections, such as Kings Highway and Ocean Parkway • Near subway stations at 86 th Street and 65 th Street • In the middle of the day • Double parking is significant contributor to low speeds along the corridor.

## Page 12

### [p012_c0001] Existing Conditions
- [p012_c0002] • Bay Parkway: Two travel lanes and curbside parking lane in each direction • 60 feet wide through most of corridor • No median or left-turn bays
[p012_c0003] _Bay Parkway at 84 th Street: Two B6 buses bunched in front of one another_
- [p012_c0004] • Cropsey Avenue: Two travel lanes and wide curbside parking lane in either direction, plus wide median • Most blocks are 86 feet wide, including 14 feet median • Concrete median island on some blocks • Median becomes left turn bay approaching intersection with Bay Parkway
[p012_c0005] _Cropsey Avenue at 26 th Avenue: School bus in front of truck and X28 express bus_
