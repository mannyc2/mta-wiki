## Page 4

### [p004_c0001] 79 th Street
> [p004_c0002] A map of the 79th Street corridor in Manhattan, New York City. The map highlights the M79 SBS subway line in dark blue, which runs from West 79th Street in the west to East 79th Street in the east. It also shows the M86 SBS subway line in light blue, which branches off from the M79 SBS line at West 86th Street. The map includes street names such as West End Avenue, Amsterdam Avenue, Columbus Avenue, Central Park West, East 86th Street, East 80th Street, East 79th Street, York Avenue, and East End Avenue. It also shows bus routes M5, M10, M15 SBS, M101, M102, M103, M3, M4, M12, and M31. The Hudson River is visible on the left side of the map.
- [p004_c0003] • Major transit corridor, hosts M79-SBS: • 13,500 daily riders • High ridership by route mile vs. other routes • Connects to 4 subway lines • Connects to 16 bus routes, including M15-SBS • Cultural destinations: • American Museum of Natural History • Metropolitan Museum of Art • Central Park • Riverside Park • 26 people have been killed or severely injured along 79 th Street SBS corridor in the past 5 years (2020 - 2024): • Killed or Severely Injured (KSI) rate of 11.5 per mile (Top 33% in Manhattan) • Fatal crash at 2 nd Avenue /E 79 th Street in 2024

### [p007_c0001] 79th Street SBS – Transit & Traffic Improvements
- [p007_c0002] • Instances of bus bunching decreased by 6% . • Bus dwell times at bus stops decreased by 21% . • Bus ridership on the M79-SBS increased by 9% in the first year after the launch. • Car travel times decreased by 4% to 8% along the corridor. • Bus speeds improved by an average of 8% . • Number of late buses decreased by up to 45% . • Bus Speeds (2024): • 6.9 MPH (M79-SBS) vs. 6.0 MPH (Manhattan) • On-time performance (2024): • 81.4% (M79-SBS) vs. 69.5% (NYC)
