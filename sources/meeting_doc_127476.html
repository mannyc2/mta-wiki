### [p003_c0006] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0007] 
President Davey stated that New York City Transit was working hard to deliver faster, cleaner and safer service to its millions of daily customers.
[p003_c0008] 
President Davey commended the resilience of the entire Transit team for its response to the September 29th historic rainfall from Tropical Storm Ophelia. He discussed the challenges New York City Transit faced from the storm and applauded the hard work of all employees involved, including customer service agents and the Department of Buses and Subways.

### [p004_c0006] 6. ACCESSIBILITY
[p004_c0007] 
Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p004_c0008] 
Arroyo noted that October is National Disability Employment Awareness Month and commended the MTA for what it is doing to support employment for people with disabilities.
[p004_c0009] 
Arroyo announced that the MTA launched a new, paid internship program through the Partnership for Inclusive Internships (PII) program, developed by AHRC and supported by the New York State Office of Adult Career and Continuing Education Service- Vocational Rehabilitation (ACCES-VR) and the New York State Commission for the Blind (NYSCB). Arroyo explained that the internship program matches young people with disabilities with opportunities for paid work, which helps build careers. Arroyo announced that the MTA has already hosted the first group of six (6) summer interns through the PII program.

Cited by 2 records: Partnership for Inclusive Internships (PII) Program +1 more

[p004_c0010] 
Arroyo described improvements in accessibility including improvements made at Hoyt St. (2,3) in downtown Brooklyn, E 146 th (6) in the South Bronx, and the New Dorp station on the Staten Island Railway. Arroyo noted that the improvements at the Hoyt station were fully funded and will be maintained by Macy's, an example of MTA engaging the private sector to improve station access without requiring MTA capital dollars.

[p004_c0011] 
Arroyo reminded the Committee that the MTA's Advisory Committee for Transit Accessibility (ACTA) has open positions and is accepting applications until November 20, 2023. He instructed those who are interested in joining ACTA those to log-on at Paratransit's website to see instructions on how to apply.

### [p005_c0002] 7. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p005_c0003] 
Chief Michael Kemper, delivered the safety and security report.

Chief Kemper reported that there has been a 12.5% reduction in crime in the subways when compared to September 2022. He noted that from January through September the overall crime index has decreased 5% from 2022. He also stated that shooting incidents in the subways are down 62.5%. Chief Kemper reported that arrests and issuance of summonses have also increased.

### [p005_c0006] 8. Procurements
[p005_c0007] 
Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, Operations, stated the procurement package included one action this month.
[p005_c0008] 
Montanti reported that the item was a purchase contract to Prevost Car (US) Inc. for the purchase of 250 over-the-road diesel express buses with an option for up to 131 additional buses for a total of 381 buses, as well as related items such as spare parts, special tools and equipment, technical documentation, and training. He stated that the final estimated amount for this award is $214 million for the base buses and an additional $111.7 million for the options, resulting in a total of $325.8 million.
