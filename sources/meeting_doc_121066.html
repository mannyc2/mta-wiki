### [p007_c0004] Discussion:

Cited by 16 records: Preteckt Prognostic Maintenance Services Modification +15 more

[p007_c0005] 
NYC Transit is seeking Board approval to modify the sole-source miscellaneous service contract awarded to Preteckt, Inc. (“Preteckt”) to perform prognostic maintenance services for NYC Transit, Department of Buses (“DOB”) and MTA Bus Company (“MTABC”) to increase the quantity of buses monitored from 1,528 to 5,000; extend the term of this test and evaluation by one year (December 1, 2023–November 30, 2024); and add an estimated $3.3 million for expected expenditures.

Cited by 12 records: Preteckt Prognostic Maintenance Services Modification +11 more

[p007_c0006] 
The base contract for the prognostic maintenance of the exhaust aftertreatment subsystem on 1,528 buses was awarded to Preteckt in December 2022 in the amount of $962,640 following a two-year, no charge proof-of-concept pilot, which was the result of the efforts of the 2019 Transit Tech Lab. The Preteckt system is designed to analyze raw sensor data collected from the exhaust aftertreatment subsystem by the existing bus telematics system to provide prognostic maintenance alerts and repair plans which can be used to reduce in-service interruptions. Since its initial deployment at NYC Transit, this system has successfully helped to both identify potential road calls before they occur and optimize labor hours and material costs.

Cited by 11 records: Preteckt Prognostic Maintenance Services Modification +10 more

[p007_c0007] 
Preteckt submitted its initial proposal in the amount of $3.54 million ($59/bus/month), which represents a $6.50/bus/month (12.4 percent) increase over the current pricing of $52.50/bus/month. Following negotiations, Preteckt reduced its proposal to $3.3 million ($55/bus/month), which represents a savings of 7 percent or $240,000 from its original proposal.

Cited by 7 records

[p007_c0008] 
The final pricing for this modification has been reviewed by the Cost-Price Analysis Unit and found to be fair and reasonable.

Cited by 1 record

[p007_c0009] 
It should be noted that DOB and MTABC have determined that the use of prognostic maintenance services to analyze data and create prognostic alerts and repair plans will be effective and beneficial to the overall bus maintenance strategy. As a result, a competitive Request for Proposal that includes the monitoring of this and additional bus systems is being developed and will be advertised in early 2024. Eight companies responded to a Request for Information advertised by Procurement in early 2023.
