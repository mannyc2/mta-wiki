### [p009_c0001] RELATED PROJECTS
### [p009_c0002] NYC DOT Congested Corridors Project
[p009_c0003] 
The Citywide Congested Corridors Project (CCCP) is a study of selected roadways across the five boroughs with the goals of improving mobility and safety for all street users, air quality, and the quality of life. The study is consistent with the City's goal of building "Complete Streets" that accommodate all street users including pedestrians, bicyclists, transit users and motorists.
[p009_c0004] 
The Woodhaven Boulevard Congested Corridors Project implemented a series of safety and mobility improvements on Woodhaven Boulevard between Queens Boulevard and Liberty Avenue between 2011 and 2015, including new pedestrian refuge medians, curb extensions, signal timing improvements, bus lanes, service road striping, and intersection geometry updates. The Q52/Q53 Select Bus Service project was a long-term recommendation from the study.
[p009_c0005] 
_Offset bus lanes between Dry Harbor Rd and Metropolitan Ave improve transit mobility while maintaining curb access_
[p009_c0006] 
_Pedestrian refuges at key intersections improve pedestrian crossing safety_
[p009_c0007] 
_Updated roadway markings between Union Tpke and Rockaway Blvd organize traffic and improves safety through standard lane widths_
### [p009_c0008] NYC DOT Eastern Rockaway Access Study
[p009_c0009] 
Access to Opportunity is a multiyear transportation study in the Eastern Rockaways to improve residents' access to goods, services, and jobs. This study is a collaboration with the Department of Housing, Preservation, and Development (HPD) to align transportation investments with land use and housing investments. Improvements stemming from this access-based approach can include not only roadway improvements, but also transit, walking, and cycling improvements, and even land use recommendations. For more information about this project, visit www.nycdotfeedbackportals.nyc/eastern-rockaway-access-study
### [p009_c0010] MTA Rockaway Beach Rail Corridor Evaluation

Cited by 1 record

[p009_c0011] 
The Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) recognizes that opportunities may exist along retired rail rights-of-way within the region, including the former Rockaway Beach Rail corridor which runs between Rego Park and Rockaway Park. MTA will conduct an evaluation of the former rail corridor in 2016-2017. The Woodhaven / Cross Bay Blvd SBS project, which operates entirely on City streets, is not in competition with the potential future use of the deactivated Rockaway Beach Line.

### [p011_c0004] Offset bus lanes
[p011_c0005] 
Where the bus travels one lane away from the curb next to parking; vehicles can enter the bus lane to turn right or access the curb.
[p011_c0006] 
_Example: Existing bus lanes Woodhaven Blvd_
### [p011_c0007] Main road bus lanes
[p011_c0008] 
The bus travels in the curbside lane of the main roadway to avoid turning and merging conflicts with other vehicles.
[p011_c0009] 
_Example: Pelham Pkwy, the Bronx_
### [p011_c0010] Targeted bus improvements
[p011_c0011] 
Small sections of bus lane, signal priority "queue jumps" and other targeted improvements help buses avoid conflicts approaching key intersections.
[p011_c0012] 
_Example: Bus lane queue jump on 86 St, Manhattan_
### [p011_c0013] Curbside bus lanes
[p011_c0014] 
Where the bus lane runs along the curb next to traffic; typically in operation only during rush hours to allow for parking off-peak.
[p011_c0015] 
_Example: Hylan Blvd, Staten Island_

### [p012_c0002] Median SBS Stations
[p012_c0003] 
Median SBS stations with shelters, seating, public art, and real-time information make bus stops a better place to wait. Passengers only have to cross the service roads (one lane of calm traffic) to access the bus stop.
[p012_c0004] 
_Typical median station rendering (91 Av)_
[p012_c0005] 
Real-time bus arrival screen tell you when your next bus is coming and provides local neighborhood maps
[p012_c0006] 
Higher curb with detectable warning tiles provide an almost-level boarding experience, like the subway
[p012_c0007] 
Main road bus lanes allow buses to pull straight into stations with no conflicts from parked cars
[p012_c0008] 
Shelters & benches provide a comfortable waiting environment
[p012_c0009] 
Taller fencing with specialized art design to improve perception of safety for bus riders standing on median stations
[p012_c0010] 
Extended medians with concrete bollards shorten pedestrian crossings and provide refuge space for waiting

### [p015_c0001] TRAFFIC ANALYSIS
### [p015_c0002] How does the proposed plan improve traffic?
[p015_c0003] 
Consistent 3 general travel lanes throughout corridor reduces merging/diverging behavior at physical pinch points (i.e. Union Tpke overpass)
[p015_c0004] 
Restricting key left turns improves thru traffic flow
[p015_c0005] 
Bus lanes organize traffic and carry more people than a mixed travel lane
[p015_c0006] 
New slip lanes balance traffic between the main and service road
[p015_c0007] 
Longer signal cycles give more green time to Woodhaven / Cross Bay Blvds
[p015_c0008] 
Longer left-turn bays at key locations to keep turns out of thru travel lanes
