### [p008_c0001] Corridor Description
[p008_c0002] 9.3 miles from Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay
[p008_c0003] Currently served by B44 bus route
- [p008_c0004] • 44,300 weekday riders – 5 th busiest bus route in city • Buses travel at an average speed of 7-8 mph
[p008_c0005] Within a ¼ mile:
- [p008_c0006] • 300,000 residents • 62% of households do not own a car • 60% of residents commute by transit

### [p009_c0001] Project Features
[p009_c0002] B44 Limited will become SBS; B44 Local will continue as before
[p009_c0003] Offset bus lanes with bus bulbs at stations for 5 miles
[p009_c0004] Transit Signal Priority for 3.8 miles from Flatbush Avenue to Sheepshead Bay
### [p009_c0005] For the Entire Route:
- [p009_c0006] • New low-floor buses • Next generation fare collection • Branding of stations and buses

### [p011_c0001] Select Bus Service Has Fewer Stops Than Limited Stop Service
> [p011_c0002] The diagram illustrates the difference in the number of stops between three types of bus services. It features a grid with 12 vertical lines, each labeled 'Station' at the top. The grid is organized into three horizontal rows, each representing a different bus service type: Local: Represented by a red line. It includes stops at all 12 stations, indicated by 12 white circles. Limited: Represented by a green line. It includes stops at all 12 stations, indicated by 12 white circles. SBS: Represented by a blue line. It includes stops at only 6 stations, indicated by 6 white circles. Each row ends with a large arrow pointing to the right, indicating the direction of travel. The SBS line has fewer stops than the Local and Limited lines.
