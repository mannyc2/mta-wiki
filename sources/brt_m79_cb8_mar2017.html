### [p003_c0001] Select Bus Service in New York City
[p003_c0002] 
Select Bus Service (SBS) is New York City's brand name for Bus Rapid Transit: an improved bus service that offers fast, frequent, and reliable service on high-ridership bus routes.
[p003_c0003] 
SBS has brought:
- [p003_c0004] • 10-30% faster bus speeds • About 10% increase in ridership • More reliable service • Customer satisfaction of 95% • Safer streets / reduction in crashes
[p003_c0005] 
There are 12 Select Bus Service routes in operation serving all 5 boroughs.

### [p006_c0001] M79 Project Background
[p006_c0002] 
The M79 bus corridor was identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the “Bus Rapid Transit: Phase II Study” (2009).
- [p006_c0003] • Request for M79 SBS from local elected officials and community • Heavily used route with slow trips • Carries over 18,000 passengers per day over about 2 miles • Crucial connection to 1 6 B C trains • Serves areas far from the subway • Connection to 16 bus routes including M15 SBS

### [p014_c0001] East Side Treatments
[p014_c0002] 
Summary: Reorganize E 79 St to meet transit, traffic, and safety needs, especially for daytime demands along the corridor
> [p014_c0003] The diagram illustrates the proposed street layout and traffic treatments along the East Side corridor. The map shows a grid of streets including 5 Avenue, Madison Avenue, Park Avenue, Lexington Avenue, 3 Avenue, 2 Avenue, 1 Avenue, York Avenue, and East End Avenue. E 79 Street runs horizontally across the grid, with E 80 Street and E 79 Street branching off. The map highlights several key areas for treatment: 1) Sidewalk widening at the intersection of 79th St and 5th Ave, indicated by a yellow callout. 2) A series of treatments from Park Avenue to York Avenue, including wider travel lanes, curbside bus queue jump lanes, left and right turn bays, wide parking lanes, and more east/west signal time for 79 St, indicated by a yellow callout. 3) Throughout the corridor, the addition of midday loading zones, indicated by a yellow callout. The map also shows existing infrastructure like bus stops and signal locations.
