### [p003_c0001] A once-a-generation, strategic overhaul of LIRR service
- [p003_c0002] ▶ Provide more frequent and reliable service to more customers ▶ Space out trains more evenly, providing more frequent service while reducing bunching ▶ Introduce true reverse peak service to promote job growth on Long Island ▶ Provide better access to transit in historically underserved communities

## Page 4

### [p004_c0001] What these new schedules mean for Brooklyn, Queens and Long Island
- [p004_c0002] ▶ A LIRR train will pull into Manhattan (Penn or Grand Central) approximately every 3 to 6 minutes in the peak ▶ Customers headed to Midtown East will be able to go straight to Grand Central – no subway ▶ Trains will arrive into Atlantic Terminal appx. every 12 minutes in the peak and every 20 minutes in the off-peak ▶ More than 500 additional station stops in Brooklyn and Queens every day
[p004_c0003]
