### [p004_c0011] The North Star: Increasing Customer Satisfaction
[p004_c0012] 
As we move into the fall, we're glad to see customers coming back to the system with increased frequency as students return to schools and workers return to offices. September was the first month since before the pandemic where more than 90 million subway trips were taken. We are pleased to report an average weekday ridership figure of over 3.4 million, with a new weekday record high of almost 3.9 million riders on September 21. Buses also had a record high of nearly 1.5 million average weekday rides in September, surpassing the high from October 2021. Paratransit continues to recover customers faster than any other Transit mode, with ridership in August hitting 84.6% of the August 2019 number.

Cited by 3 records

[p004_c0013] 
We are reinvigorating our own business practices to improve both customer experience and travel times, with the North Star goal of increasing overall customer satisfaction. Customers are telling us through the monthly pulse survey that wait times are becoming a more important factor in their decision to utilize transit, especially for bus customers. Satisfaction for subway and paratransit remained steady, while we saw a slight dip for bus in September as schools reopened and the increased traffic in the city affected performance. As a result, we continue to be laser-focused on improving service in measurable ways to move the needle. Customer Journey Time Performance (CJTP), Additional Bus Stop Time and Additional Platform Time for bus and subways also ticked up in September - showing us that our service performance mirrors customer perception.

Cited by 1 record

[p004_c0014] 
There are several initiatives underway to improve service within each of our operating groups, including: additional busways to speed up routes with high ridership, increased Automated Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE) to reduce bus lane infringements, bus network redesigns, a focus in subways on line-by-line performance management, and congestion pricing to reduce traffic in lower Manhattan and clear the way for our buses and paratransit vehicles.
[p004_c0015] 
Nearly one third of our bus customers told us last month that we need to improve wait times to increase their satisfaction – a figure nearly 3 times higher than any other factor chosen. One of the main levers to reduce wait times and increase speeds is the implementation of bus lanes and enforcement along existing bus lanes. In both 2021 and 2022, we saw a drop in bus performance in September when compared to August which corresponded with a drop in city traffic speeds, showing the real need for clear bus lanes and reduced congestion.

### [p009_c0005] Data Review
[p009_c0006] 
Overall subway experience satisfaction held steady at 59% in September and remains 10 percentage points higher than May 2022. The 7, G, L and Q lines are our highest rated, with overall satisfaction of 70% or greater. Recent improvement was seen on the J and the G, following the end of planned maintenance work. We're seeing increasing scores on cleanliness and remain focused on further improvement, with extra attention in upper Manhattan stations. Local, Limited and Select Bus satisfaction decreased by 5% in August to 62% in September. This decrease is driven by drops in key service attributes such as wait time (-4%), travel time (-5%) and service reliability (-5%). Overall satisfaction is remains greater in Manhattan (71%) when compared to other boroughs (54% to 60%). The overall Access-A-Ride (AAR) experience satisfaction rate remained at 66% from August to September. Slightly more than one quarter of customers (26%) are very satisfied with AAR. On time pick-up continues to be the most mentioned improvement needed to increase satisfaction. AAR users who reside in the Bronx and on Staten Island are the most satisfied with rates of 72% and 71%. Those who live in Brooklyn and Manhattan are less satisfied with rates of 62% and 63%.

### [p011_c0001] RIDERSHIP
### [p011_c0002] Data Review
[p011_c0003] 
With the end of summer vacation, combined with a new surge in employers bringing employees back to the office and increased tourist activity, September saw post-pandemic ridership highs on both subway and bus. Compared to the previous monthly highs in May, average weekday subway ridership rose 5.2% to 3.48 million, and average weekday bus ridership rose 1.5% to 1.46 million. Those levels represent 60.1% and 63.0% of pre-COVID levels respectively. However, the monthly September totals were somewhat suppressed by a number of school-closed days (both prior to Labor Day and the Jewish holidays). If looking only at school-open weekdays, subway grew 9.4% from May, and bus grew by 4.5%. Similarly, comparisons to pre-COVID September school-open levels rose to 62.4% on subway and 64.9% on bus. Meanwhile, weekend ridership is now at 72.4% of pre-COVID levels on subway and 64.4% on bus.

Cited by 5 records

[p011_c0004] 
The gains in subway trips to and from the Manhattan Central Business District were especially strong, with a 15% increase vs. May. In time-of-day patterns, subway growth was strongest in evenings, up 12% after 7pm. The traditional peak hours were strong as well. The early portion of the morning peak and middays also grew, but at slower rates. On the bus side, the strongest growth occurred in the hours associated with school travel, 6am-8am and 2pm-4pm, perhaps indicating stronger attendance over the end of last school year.

### [p027_c0003] Subways Winter Prep
[p027_c0004] 
The Department of Subways is in the process of updating the winter operations plan and technical bulletins as part of the annual winter preparedness cycle culminating in a departmental tabletop exercise. Snow fighting equipment (including 61 locomotives, 38 rider cars, and 10 snow blowers) for the mainline and yards snow clearing has been inspected and prepared for winter operations as well as stockpile consumable items like salt/sand. During storm response, the snow fighting equipment, in most cases, will allow for train revenue service operation from terminal to terminal.
[p027_c0005]

The Incident Command Center and divisional local storm centers are checked and readied for winter operations and activated depending on the severity of the snow event. DOS Winter Storm plans follow an escalating response approach that allows for adjusting the response depending on forecast and actual field conditions.
