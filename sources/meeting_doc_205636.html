### [p006_c0005] Purpose
[p006_c0006] Bridges and Tunnels (“B&T”), in accordance with the All-Agency Service Contract Procurement Guidelines, is seeking Board approval to award a competitively solicited personal service contract to M & J Engineering PC (“M&J”), a NYS certified minority-owned business, for construction management and inspection services for a bridge preservation project located at the Bronx Whitestone Bridge and the Throgs Neck Bridge, in the amount of $4,262,875 and a duration of 52 months.
### [p006_c0007] Discussion
[p006_c0008] Contract PSC-25-3079 (the “Contract”) requires the services of a dedicated engineering consultant firm to provide construction management and inspection services to support bridge preservation requirements under contract WBM-389 TNM-402 – Bridge Preservation at the Bronx Whitestone Bridge and the Throgs Neck Bridge (the “Project”). The Contract consultant will provide technical services including but not limited to the following: (1) utilize project management methods overseeing the Project from start to finish, focusing on controlling costs, schedule, quality, safety, and scope; (2) provide professional oversight of the implementation phase of the Project, ensuring the contractor’s performance meets the MTA Construction and Development (“C&D”) Project Scope and General Requirements; and (3) provide key functions of quality control, risk management, and coordination with C&D engineering teams.
[p006_c0009] The consultant selection for the Contract was determined via a one-step qualifications-based Request for Proposals (“RFP”) procurement process. The RFP was publicly advertised, and the solicitation notice was distributed to 660 firms. Of the 60 firms that viewed or downloaded the RFP, eight submitted proposals:
[p006_c0010]

```text
1. ALRA Engineers PC (“ALRA”) 5. M & J Engineering PC (“M & J”) 2. ENTECH Engineering PC (“ENTEC”) 6. SC Engineers, PC (“SC ENG”) 3. Gannett Fleming Engineers (“GFE”) 7. Tectonic Engineering Consultants (“TECON”) 4. HVEA Engineers (“HVEA”) 8. ZOFS Engineering PC (“ZOFS”)
```

### [p007_c0001] Staff Summary
### [p007_c0002] Bridges and Tunnels
### [p007_c0003] Page 2 of 2
[p007_c0004] The selection committee (“SC”), consisting of representatives from C&D Delivery and B&T Contracts, evaluated the proposals utilizing the following pre-established selection technical criteria: Record of Performance of the Same or Comparable Activities; Qualifications of Specific Personnel Proposed; Qualification of Firm for Specific Type of Work; Demonstrated Quality of Professional Services Proposed; Depth of Understanding of the Project and Potential Problems; and Proposed Work Schedule.
[p007_c0005] After evaluating the technical proposals and ranking the firms highest to lowest, the SC opened the price proposals: ALRA – $4,838,412; ENTECH – $4,815,721; GFE – $7,888,893; HVEA – $5,325,947; M & J – $4,759,696; SC ENG – $6,257,702; TECON – $5,998,368; and ZOFs – $4,792,525. The proposal price spread of 66 percent is due to the consultants’ varied staffing plans: a mix of full- and part-time positions.
[p007_c0006] While all firms were rated technically acceptable, the SC invited the two highest-rated firms for oral presentations: M & J and SC ENG. The proposers demonstrated how their labor resources would be strategically allocated to ensure efficient execution of construction management and inspection activities. Following oral presentations, the SC was impressed by M & J’s technical expertise. They demonstrated a thorough understanding of B&T and C&D standards and a strong capability to support critical bridge assets. The SC selected and negotiated with M&J, as the highest-rated firm, focusing on proposed Other Direct Costs and level of effort. After negotiations, the SC requested that M & J submit a revised price proposal based upon the proposed savings discussed. M & J’s revised price proposal was for $4,262,875, which compared favorably to the engineer’s estimate and is considered to be fair and reasonable.
[p007_c0007] M & J has previous experience delivering construction management and inspection services for major bridge preservation and rehabilitation projects, including projects located at the Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge, the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge, and the Bronx Whitestone Bridge, providing the firm with extensive insight into the bridge preservation performance requirements. M & J has successfully completed work of identical scope and magnitude.
[p007_c0008] The SC unanimously recommended M & J for award. Based on its evaluation of the technical proposal, as well as information presented during the oral presentations and negotiations, the SC determined that M & J’s proposal offers the best value to the MTA. M & J’s proposal showed a thorough understanding of the scope of work, challenges, risks involved, and ways to ensure successful completion of the project. M & J’s proposed staff demonstrated expertise in construction management and inspection services for the bridge preservation project.
[p007_c0009] M & J proposed nine labor categories with an average hourly rate of $70.67, and individual rates ranging from $50 to $98. The variation in hourly rates reflects differences in associated qualifications. A price and cost analysis was conducted and determined that the labor rates are consistent with pricing submitted by other offerors and the proposed efficient staffing plan is consistent with the required inspection services.
[p007_c0010] The contract resulting from this procurement has been evaluated to determine the necessity and appropriate scope, if any, of cybersecurity requirements, as well as any requirements under federal, state, and local law and regulations. M & J has certified its compliance with Cybersecurity Terms and Conditions.
[p007_c0011] M & J has certified that pursuant to Executive Order 16, it is not doing business in Russia.
### [p007_c0012] MBE/WBE/SDVOB Information
[p007_c0013] In accordance with State law, the MTA Department of Diversity and Civil Rights has established goals of 15 percent MBE, 15 percent WBE, and 6 percent SDVOB on this contract. M&J has submitted MBE, WBE, and SDVOB utilization plans that satisfy the goal requirements.
