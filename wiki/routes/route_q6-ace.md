The [[route:route_q6-ace|Q6]] is a Queens bus route that runs along Sutphin Blvd / Rockaway Blvd and is one of 60 bus routes where the Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program is active [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE program active on Q6 and 60 routes]]. The ACE program, expanded by the 2023 NYS Legislature, uses bus-mounted cameras to issue civil-penalty violations to vehicles that occupy bus lanes, double park along bus routes, or block bus stops [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE description and 2023 legislation]]. It is administered jointly by the MTA, the New York City Department of Finance (DOF), and the New York City Department of Transportation (DOT) [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE partnership agencies]].

On each ACE route including the Q6, the MTA and City issue warning notices for 60 days before fine-bearing violations begin [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|60-day warning period]]. [[metric:metric_ace-fine-start-50|Fines start at $50]] and increase by [[metric:metric_ace-fine-increment-50|$50 per additional violation]], up to a [[metric:metric_ace-fine-max-250|maximum of $250 per violation]] [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|fine structure]]. Violations are issued once every two hours, require observation by two buses, and are reviewed by a City employee before issuance [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|two-bus observation and city review]]. [[metric:metric_ace-multiple-ticket-rate|About 10% of violators receive multiple tickets]], indicating the program deters repeat offenses [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|10% multiple ticket rate]].

[[metric:metric_ace-weekday-riders|Over one million average weekday riders]] benefit from ACE across all 60 routes, and when combined with dedicated bus lanes and street upgrades, camera enforcement has helped produce speed gains of nearly 30% on some route segments [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|1M riders and speed gains]]. Revenue from ACE violations supports MTA operations, including improved bus service [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE revenue supports operations]]. [[metric:metric_ace-near-bus-stop-access|98% of New Yorkers live within 1/4 mile of a bus stop]], underscoring the importance of keeping bus lanes and stops clear [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|98% near bus stop access]].
