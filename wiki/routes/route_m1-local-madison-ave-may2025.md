[[route:route_m1-local-madison-ave-may2025|M1 Local Bus]] is one of 34 bus routes carrying 92,000 daily riders in the Madison Avenue study area between East 23rd and East 42nd Street, where NYC DOT and the MTA have proposed extending double bus lanes to improve bus speeds and reliability [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|92,000 daily bus riders on 34 routes]]. Bus lanes have existed on Madison Avenue north of 42nd Street to 60th Street since 1981, and the May 2025 presentation described the southern segment as lacking dedicated bus lanes despite serving the M1 along with the M2, M3, M4, Q32, and 29 express bus routes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|34 bus routes including M1 through Q32]].

In the study area, buses carry 55% of roadway users during AM and PM rush hours while using less than a quarter of road space, yet bus speeds are as low as 4.5 miles per hour—well below the Manhattan average of 6.2 mph and the NYC average of 8.1 mph [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|55% of roadway users on buses]]. In October 2024, local buses (including the M1) averaged 5.7 mph during the weekday 6-10 AM peak period [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0002|Local bus speed 5.7 mph AM peak]]; the speed data used M1, M2, M3, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM10 data [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0005|Note on speed data sources]].

The proposal would extend existing 24/7 bus lanes from East 42nd Street south to East 23rd Street, creating double bus lanes on the east side of Madison Avenue while maintaining west curb parking and loading, one general travel lane on most blocks, and existing right turns [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p016_c0002|Proposal: extend 24/7 bus lanes]]. The plan also includes updated curb management that retains approximately 78% of existing parking spaces and adds half-block left turn bays at four cross streets [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p018_c0002|Curb management retains ~78% parking]]. Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) is also being expanded on the M2 and M4 local bus routes, with a future update planned for the new Madison Avenue bus lanes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p019_c0002|ACE expansion on M2 and M4]].

Madison Avenue was identified as a Bus Priority Corridor in the 2023 NYC Streets Plan Update [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p005_c0004|Madison Ave identified in 2023 Streets Plan]]. The project timeline included design refinement and traffic analysis in Spring 2025, planned implementation in Summer 2025, and ongoing performance monitoring in Fall-Winter 2025 [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p022_c0002|Project next steps]]. The proposal was presented to Manhattan Community Board 5 on May 19, 2025, and to Community Board 6 on June 2, 2025 [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p001_c0002|CB5 presentation May 2025]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb6_jun2025#p001_c0002|CB6 presentation June 2025]]. A precedent for this approach exists: 5th Avenue double bus lanes, implemented in 2018, produced local speed increases of 6-12% and express speed increases of 11-20% [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p008_c0002|5th Ave double bus lanes speed results]].
