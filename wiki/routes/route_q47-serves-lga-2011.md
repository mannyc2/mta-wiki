The [[route:route_q47-serves-lga-2011|Q47]] is a Queens bus route that served LaGuardia Airport as of June 2011, one of five bus routes (alongside the M60, Q33, Q48, and Q72) serving the airport at that time [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p006_c0004|LGA bus routes 2011]]. At a November 2, 2011 public workshop for the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis—a study led by NYCDOT that had selected Bus Rapid Transit as the locally preferred mode—participants raised several concerns about the Q47 [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p001_c0005|Public Meeting #2 date and format]] [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p001_c0013|BRT selected as locally preferred mode]].

Participants reported that the Q47 is often backed up and that there are no buses at the 74th Street Terminal (Victor Moore Bus Arcade) [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p003_c0008|Q47 backed up, no buses at 74th St Terminal]]. Another comment noted the Q47 is often stuck behind sanitation vehicles [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p003_c0008|Q47 stuck behind sanitation vehicles]]. These operational issues contributed to broader concerns about bus service to LGA, where only seven percent of passengers arrived by bus [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p001_c0012|7% arrive by bus]]. The 2011 data presented at the first public meeting showed that over two-thirds of LGA passengers come from New York City, with [[metric:metric_lga-passengers-from-manhattan|47% from Manhattan]], [[metric:metric_lga-passengers-from-other-boroughs|20% from other boroughs]], and [[metric:metric_lga-passengers-from-elsewhere|33% from elsewhere]] [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p007_c0005|LGA passenger origins]] [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p007_c0003|over 2/3 from NYC]]. By mode, [[metric:metric_lga-other-mode-share|63% of travelers arrived by taxi/livery, 23% by personal car, 10% by transit, and 4% by other means]] [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p008_c0006|LGA mode share]].
