The [[route:route_q1-queens|Q1 bus route]] is included in the Queens Bus Network Redesign set of routes analyzed in the equity evaluation appendix [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p006_c0008|route listing]]. The source identifies the Q1 as a "good example of a frequency increase benefiting several Tier 1 and 2 areas" [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p006_c0011|route description]], referencing the three-tier equity framework used in the Redesign process. By offering more frequent service, the Q1 provides all-day frequent service at local stops on Hillside Avenue, improving wait times for customers [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p006_c0011|route description]]. The enhanced frequency along with the extension to Sutphin Boulevard and Jamaica Avenue near the Jamaica LIRR station significantly reduces travel times for residents in the area, where nearly 70% of the population face commutes longer than 45 minutes [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p006_c0011|route description]].

The equity evaluation was conducted using a three-tier framework developed for the Queens Redesign, informed by Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and a broader set of socioeconomic factors including transit dependency, minority representation, income, access to opportunity, and mobility needs [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p002_c0005|equity framework]]. Under this framework, borough-wide, the proposed plan results in a 4% increase in the population served by the all-day frequent network in Tier 1 areas (where transit-dependent and historically underserved communities are most concentrated), a 5% increase in Tier 2 areas, and an 8% increase in Tier 3 areas [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p007_c0008|network increase narrative]]. Across all Queens, the all-day frequent network proposed population reaches [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-total-proposed-pop|All-Day Frequent Network Total Proposed Population]] (61% of the borough population), up from [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-total-existing-pop|All-Day Frequent Network Total Existing Population]] (56%) under the existing network, an increase of [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-total-increase-pop|All-Day Frequent Network Total Population Increase]] people (5%) [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p007_c0007|population table]]. The Q1 route serves Hillside Avenue, a major corridor, and the frequency increase directly supports these equity goals by improving access to all-day frequent service in areas with higher equity concern.
