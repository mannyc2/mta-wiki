## Route Context

Southern Brooklyn Select Bus Service is the SBS version of the B82 in southern Brooklyn. NYC DOT's SBS route index says service began on October 1, 2018, benefiting 28,000 daily passengers and connecting riders to the D, N, F, Q, B, and L trains, B44 SBS, B46 SBS, and 19 other bus routes. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001]

Pre-launch materials framed the route as a conversion of the B82 Limited/Local corridor into SBS. The corridor had been identified as a Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study, served 32,000 daily riders on the B82 Limited and Local, and functioned as an east-west connection across Southern Brooklyn. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p003_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p003_c0003]

## Service Pattern

Planning decks described the service change as "B82 Limited is Becoming B82 SBS": SBS would begin at Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street, serve the same stops as the existing Limited, operate for more hours of the day, and leave current stops served by the B82 Local except for selected eastbound Kings Highway stop removals. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p010_c0001] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p010_c0002]

The proposed alignment followed Cropsey Avenue, Bay Parkway, Kings Highway, Flatlands Avenue, and Pennsylvania Avenue, with SBS ending at Cropsey/Bay 37 Street while local service continued to Coney Island. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p011_c0002] A June 2017 deck also described proposed routing via Avenue K between Kings Highway and Flatlands Avenue, plus westbound routing via Glenwood Road to reduce turns, local-street bus traffic, and travel time. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb18_jun2017#p015_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb18_jun2017#p015_c0003] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb18_jun2017#p030_c0002]

By October 2018, the launch-era source says B82 SBS had launched on 10/01/18, replacing the B82 Limited. It ran from Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street to Pennsylvania Avenue in Starrett City, operated from 4:45 AM to 11:00 PM, and served many of the same stops as the former Limited while the B82 Local continued to serve current local stops. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002] The same source describes the post-launch route as serving 28,000 daily riders on B82 SBS and Local. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p004_c0002]

## Street And Operating Features

The B82 SBS package used core SBS treatments: dedicated bus lanes, signal priority, off-board fare collection, and all-door boarding. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0004] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0007] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0009] March 2018 materials say these elements were intended to support faster rides, fewer bus/traffic conflicts, better reliability, and less time stopped at red lights. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p005_c0006]

On Kings Highway, the March 2018 plan listed bus-lane segments in Community Board 15 and said the lanes would be active 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM Monday-Friday to move the most people at the busiest times. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p030_c0005] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p030_c0006] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p030_c0009] On Flatlands Avenue at Ralph Avenue, the October 2018 update described a simplified four-leg intersection, longer pedestrian crossing times, a pedestrian island, and a new curbside bus lane active 7 AM-7 PM Monday-Saturday. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p011_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p012_c0002]

## Later Corridor Context

Later Bay Parkway/Cropsey Avenue bus-priority materials still identify B82 Local and Select Bus Service as key routes in the study area. The 2025 draft proposal covers Bay Parkway from Avenue J to Shore Parkway and Cropsey Avenue from Bay Parkway to 26th Avenue, serving 35,000 daily bus riders across B6, B82, and X28/X38 service. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002] It proposes offset bus lanes on Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue, with bus lanes shifting curbside at some intersections, left-turn accommodation, block-by-block customization, and optimized traffic-light timing. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p007_c0002] The same deck says traffic analysis was still pending and would be shared as part of the final proposal. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p016_c0002]

MTA's Automated Camera Enforcement page lists B82-SBS among ACE bus routes, with major streets shown as Bay Parkway, Kings Highway, and Flatlands Avenue. [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001]
