## Route Identity

The B46/Utica Avenue SBS record is best read as the Select Bus Service variant of the B46 on the Utica Avenue/Malcolm X Boulevard corridor, not as a replacement for all B46 local service. Pre-launch planning described the project as building on an existing Bus Priority and Safety Project to upgrade B46 Limited to B46 SBS, while the route index records Utica Avenue Select Bus Service as beginning July 3, 2016 on the B46 in Brooklyn and benefiting 44,000 daily B46 corridor riders [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p014_c0002] [brt_route_index#p001_b0001].

## Pre-Launch Planning

The planning case for SBS reached back to the BRT Phase II candidate process: a 2014 CAC deck says the Utica Avenue B46 corridor had been identified as a potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 BRT Phase II Study. The same deck framed the corridor as slow and crowded, carrying nearly 50,000 passengers per day, with connections to the 3/4/A/C trains and 30 bus routes [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p010_c0002] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p010_c0003].

The 2015 service-planning decks describe a proposed package rather than already-operating SBS. One CB3 presentation says the B46 Limited would be upgraded to B46 SBS and lists off-board fare collection, dedicated bus lanes or a possible extension south of Church Avenue, transit signal priority, service changes to improve reliability, and capital improvements at SBS stations such as real-time passenger information, bus bulbs, pedestrian safety enhancements, shelters, plantings, and benches [2015_04_06_brt_uticaave_cb3_presentation#p008_c0002] [2015_04_06_brt_uticaave_cb3_presentation#p008_c0003].

The pre-launch materials also separated prior Utica Avenue bus-priority work from the SBS service plan. A May 2015 town hall deck says the B46 Limited averaged 7.5 mph in the AM peak and 6.5 mph in the PM peak, and reports that Summer 2014 bus lanes, left-turn bays, and turn restrictions from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue were followed by 8-15% decreases in B46 Limited/local travel times and 20-25% decreases in peak-direction vehicle travel times [2015_05_11_brt_uticaave_cb3_town_hall_presentation#p008_c0002] [2015_05_11_brt_uticaave_cb3_town_hall_presentation#p015_c0003] [2015_05_11_brt_uticaave_cb3_town_hall_presentation#p015_c0006].

## Implemented Service

The service design preserved distinct SBS and local patterns. A May 2015 deck proposed a DeKalb Avenue northern terminal for B46 SBS, limited stops south of Avenue H, a 24-hour B46 Local extension to Williamsburg Bridge Plaza, and standard buses; an April 2016 deck similarly described B46 SBS running between Avenue U/Kings Plaza and DeKalb Avenue while B46 Local ran between Avenue U/Kings Plaza and Williamsburg Bridge Plaza [2015_05_11_brt_uticaave_cb3_town_hall_presentation#p013_c0002] [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p008_c0003].

The Winter/Spring 2016 newsletter describes the planned stop pattern in operational terms: B46 Local would be extended to Williamsburg Bridge Plaza and continue making local stops from DeKalb Avenue to Kings Plaza, while B46 SBS would make SBS stops from DeKalb Avenue to Kings Plaza and would not make local stops south of Avenue H [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0004] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0005] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0006] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0007].

## Bus-Priority Treatments

By the Winter/Spring 2016 newsletter, dedicated bus lanes were marked as completed in Fall 2015, with B46 buses described as having their own dedicated lane, and B46 SBS buses were described as equipped with TSP technology to communicate with signals so buses spent less time waiting at lights. The April 2016 CB17 deck also listed launch-preparation work including resurfacing Utica Avenue from Clarkson Avenue to Kings Highway, adjusting bus stops, installing fare machines, installing Bus Time wayfinding signs, and adding shelters and benches where feasible [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0003] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0005] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0007] [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p010_c0002].

## Later Malcolm X / Utica Work

The 2019-2020 Malcolm X/Utica materials are later corridor follow-up, not the original SBS launch record. They describe the B46 as Brooklyn's busiest route with 38,000 daily riders, say southbound ridership had increased in Bed-Stuy while speeds slowed on blocks without bus lanes, and then focus on bus-lane and operational work around Chauncey Street, Fulton Street, and Atlantic Avenue [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p004_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p017_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p019_c0002].

Those later decks report several implementation details: a northbound Bergen Street-to-Atlantic Avenue bus-lane segment installed in 2016 improved peak bus speeds 17-26%; a one-block southbound Fulton Street-to-Atlantic Avenue bus lane was photo enforced; and by the February/March 2020 decks, B46 SBS had been upgraded to articulated buses in January 2020 and the implemented bus lane was reported as improving bus speeds 54% in the AM peak and 106% in the PM peak [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p012_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p004_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p010_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p009_c0002].

## Camera Enforcement And Redesign References

Camera-enforcement sources identify B46-SBS as an ABLE/ACE route after the SBS launch. The 2024 bus-lane camera report says MTA on-bus mobile cameras were first tested in 2010-2011 and fully implemented under ABLE in 2019, lists B46-SBS among ABLE-monitored routes, and says MTA transitioned from ABLE to ACE in 2024; the MTA ACE page updated May 15, 2026 lists B46-SBS on Utica Avenue / Malcolm X Boulevard [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0002] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0004] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p003_c0002] [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001].

The Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign evidence is a later planning reference. Its draft plan says the network was reimagined for current and future customers, and its proposed service table keeps separate B46 and B46 SBS rows, with B46 listed as 24-hour service and B46 SBS listed as 5:30 AM-10:30 PM with proposed headways of 3 minutes in the AM peak, 7 midday, 5 PM peak, 5 early evening, and 8 late evening [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p005_c0003] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p044_c0003].

## Caveats

The page merges B46+, B46-SBS, B46 SBS, and some B46 route aliases; local and limited service records remain intertwined but should not be treated as identical to the SBS service. Pre-launch sources contain planned launch references before the documented July 3, 2016 start, so this page uses July 3, 2016 as the implemented launch date. Repo evidence reviewed here lists B46-SBS under ABLE/ACE, but does not provide a B46-specific ABLE activation date. Malcolm X/Utica 2019-2020 evidence is a localized follow-up around Bed-Stuy/Atlantic Avenue, not proof that every B46 SBS segment received the same treatment.
