The [[route:route_e-nyct-2025|E Subway Line]] is one of four subway lines — along with the F, M, and R — that operate along the Queens Boulevard corridor in Queens and Manhattan [[cite:meeting_doc_186641#p002_c0009|Queens Boulevard lines identified]]. A major multi-year signal modernization project along Queens Boulevard was nearing completion as of 2021, promising more frequent and reliable service on the E, F, M, and R trains [[cite:meeting_doc_65171#p005_c0006|signal modernization project status]].

In December 2025, the MTA implemented the F/M route swap, the first permanent change to the subway network since 2017, permanently swapping the routes the F and M take between Queens and Manhattan during the week [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p009_c0011|F/M swap launch details]]. Although the swap directly changes F and M routing, it is expected to benefit all four Queens Boulevard lines — including the E — by reducing delays, increasing reliability, and easing crowding for approximately 1.2 million daily riders on the E, F, M, and R lines combined [[cite:meeting_doc_186641#p002_c0009|benefit to all four lines]] [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p009_c0011|1.2 million riders estimate]]. The November 2025 subway systemwide on-time performance improvement to 84.4% was partly driven by data-driven schedule adjustments, though E-line-specific metrics have not been isolated in available sources [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p008_c0009|subway OTP November 2025]].
