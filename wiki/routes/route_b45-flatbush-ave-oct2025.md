Under the Flatbush Avenue Bus Priority project, a potential terminal swap between [[route:route_b45-flatbush-ave-oct2025|B45]] and the B63 is being explored [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_sept2025#p016_c0001|Potential Terminal Swap – B45 & B63]]. Swapping the B45 and B63 terminals would prevent the left-turning B63 from blocking the B41 on Flatbush Avenue [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_sept2025#p016_c0002|Terminal swap detail]]. Under the proposal, the westbound B45 would cross Flatbush Avenue and continue along Atlantic Avenue to terminate at Brooklyn Bridge Park [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_cb6_oct2025#p013_c0002|WB B45 routing detail]]. The northbound B63 would turn left onto Flatbush Avenue from 5th Avenue and proceed along Flatbush Avenue and Livingston Street to terminate in Downtown Brooklyn [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_cb6_oct2025#p013_c0002|NB B63 routing detail]]. All existing stops would continue to be served under the swap [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_cb8_oct2025#p013_c0002|All stops served]].

The B45 appears on corridor maps as one of several bus routes that operate on or near Flatbush Avenue, alongside the B63, B69, B103, B67, B65, and B41 [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p011_c0004|Flatbush Ave corridor map]]. The terminal swap is part of a broader package of bus priority improvements, including center-running bus lanes, boarding islands, and pedestrian safety treatments, proposed by NYC DOT and the MTA along the Flatbush Avenue corridor [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_sept2025#p006_c0002|Center-running bus lanes]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_sept2025#p014_c0002|Bus routing and stop consolidation]].
