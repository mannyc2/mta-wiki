The [[route:route_meeting-doc-160441-q28|Q28 (NYCT)]] is a local bus route serving Bayside and Flushing in Queens, identified for running time adjustments as part of a December 2024 MTA Staff Summary recommending bus service enhancements in support of Congestion Pricing [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0004|Q28 listed as Bayside-Flushing running time adjustments]]. The summary, prepared by MTA Operations Planning under Chief Christopher Pangilinan and Project Manager Robert Lai, proposed running time adjustments on 16 local bus routes/route combinations where utilization is high or where routes have reliability issues [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p002_c0004|staff summary author details]][[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p003_c0007|16 local routes selected for utilization or reliability]]. Operated by New York City Transit as a local service, the Q28's specific running time adjustments were under development at the time of the summary and planned for implementation in the summer 2025 schedule [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p003_c0007|details under development, summer 2025]].

Across the local bus improvement program of which the Q28 is a part, approximately 29 additional buses are needed, secured through rehabilitation of buses planned for retirement, at a one-time first-year cost of roughly $5 million [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p003_c0007|29 additional buses via rehabilitation]][[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0010|$5M first-year rehab cost]]. The annualized operating cost increase attributable to local bus routes under this program is approximately $6.6 million, within a total systemwide annualized increase of roughly $8 million [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0010|$6.6M local and $8M total annual cost]].
