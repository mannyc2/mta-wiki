The [[route:route_x64-201110-qbb|X64 Express Bus Route]] is an express bus route operating in Queens, documented in the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study presented to local Community Boards in Spring-Summer 2010 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0003|Presentation date]]. The study recorded a daily ridership of 220 for the X64 (marked as an estimated volume), part of 7,920 total daily express bus riders across all routes using the Queensboro Bridge [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|Express bus daily ridership table]]. Across the bridge, approximately 16,000 total bus trips occur each day, including about 8,000 local riders, with 110 buses crossing during the PM rush hour (one bus every 30 seconds) [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|QBB corridor overview]].

A rider survey of 511 express bus respondents (including X64 riders) found that 88% were traveling to or from work, 81% rode the bus daily, and 52% sometimes used the subway as an alternative, citing speed or convenience [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Bus rider survey findings]]. Among express riders, 80% perceived traffic congestion on their bus during PM hours, identifying the LIE, Queensboro Bridge, and Queens Boulevard as problem locations; of those who perceived delay on the bridge, 62% reported an average delay of 5-15 minutes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Bus rider survey findings]]. Express riders suggested "Bus lanes," "Enforcement of bus lanes," and routing changes as improvements for trip speed [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Bus rider survey findings]].

The study measured PM express bus travel times across three bridge approach segments; one listed express segment averaged 7 minutes 29 seconds (15.0 mph) between 4:00-5:00 PM, 9 minutes 19 seconds (12.2 mph) between 5:00-6:00 PM, and 5 minutes 49 seconds (21.2 mph) between 6:00-7:00 PM [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p011_c0002|PM express travel times]]. The study's goal was to identify cost-effective ways to improve bus speed and reliability for local and express buses using the QBB [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p003_c0004|Study goal]].
