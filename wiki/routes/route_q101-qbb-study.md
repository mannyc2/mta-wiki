The Q101 Local Bus Route was studied as a key local route within the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, conducted by the New York City Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) and presented to local Community Boards in spring-summer 2010 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0001|NYC DOT logo]] [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0003|Presentation to local Community Boards]] [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p001_c0002|Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study]]. The Q101 provides direct service between Queens and Manhattan, serving as a key transit link for residents of Astoria and Long Island City [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|Q101 provides direct service between Queens and Manhattan]].

Average weekday ridership was 850 total (450 eastbound, 400 westbound), making it the smallest of the three local bus routes using the Queensboro Bridge (QBB) compared to the Q60 at 2,710 and the Q32 at 4,340 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p007_c0002|Average Weekday Route Daily table]]. At the time of the study, over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delayed their bus [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|Over 75% of QBB riders report congestion delay]]. The study identified that congestion on the QBB was a significant cause of bus delay, with QBB travel times increasing significantly during rush hour [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|Congestion on the QBB is a significant cause of bus delay]].

The study included a specific recommendation addressing the Q101: Recommendation #1B, which proposed reconfiguring 60th Street to provide two westbound bus lanes and stops for the Q60 and Q101 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p009_c0003|60 th Street Bus Stop Recommendation #1B]] [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p009_c0005|Solution: Reconfigure 60 th Street to provide 2 WB bus lanes and stops]]. The study also documented the existing Q101 bus stop on Second Avenue and evaluated a proposed 1/2-mile loop route to allow Q101 buses to return eastbound on the QBB [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p008_c0001|Q101 Second Avenue Bus Stop Existing Issue]] [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p008_c0003|Existing Q101 bus stop]] [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p008_c0004|1/2-mile loop to return EB on QBB]]. Express buses using the QBB carried a total of 7,920 daily riders across QM and X routes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|Express Bus Route Daily table]] ([[metric:metric_express-bus-total-daily-ridership|Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB]]).
