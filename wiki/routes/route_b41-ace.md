The Proposed B41 Local is the primary bus route on Flatbush Avenue, operating local and limited-stop service between Downtown Brooklyn and Kings Plaza/Bergen Beach [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p011_c0002|B41 route description from kickoff meeting]]. Under the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan, it would be a 24-hour route with proposed frequencies of 8 minutes in the AM Peak, 10 minutes Midday, 8 minutes in the PM Peak, 10 minutes in Early Evening, and 20 minutes in Late Evening; improvements include more direct routing, improved stop spacing, improved frequency, and fewer route patterns with service only to Kings Plaza [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p040_c0003|B41 proposed route improvements]] [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p043_c0003|B41 proposed service hours and frequency]].

Ridership on [[route:route_b41-ace|Proposed B41 Local]] was reported at 22,500 average daily riders in early 2023 presentations [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb14_jan2023#p006_c0002|B41 ridership from CB14 meeting]] and over 28,000 average daily riders in mid-2024 materials [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb8_jun2024#p004_c0005|B41 ridership from CB8 meeting]]. Nearly 40% of all B41 trips start, end, or travel through the Flatbush Avenue bus priority project area, and 80% of those trips begin or end south of Grand Army Plaza [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb8_jun2025#p004_c0002|B41 trip patterns from CB8 June 2025]]. The route is part of the Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program on Flatbush Av / Livingston St [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|B41 ACE route listing]].

The Flatbush Avenue Bus Priority project planning has occurred over several years with more than 45 community, stakeholder, and elected meetings [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p005_c0004|Four years of outreach summary]]. The B63 route revision at Atlantic Avenue was designed to avoid blocking the more frequent B41 and B67 buses traveling straight in bus lanes along Flatbush Avenue [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p012_c0003|B63 revision reasoning]]. The B41 also saw over a 3% increase in Customer Journey Time Performance (CJTP) as of mid-2023, one of two routes (along with the Q66) to achieve that benchmark [[cite:meeting_doc_113986#p015_c0011|B41 CJTP improvement from MTA operations report]].

Planned treatments for the Flatbush Avenue corridor include center-running bus lanes with bus boarding islands that would benefit the B41 by providing physically separated space for buses in the middle of the roadway [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2025#p013_c0002|Center-running bus lane benefits described]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2025#p020_c0009|Center-running lanes allow buses to move freely]]. Construction is anticipated to begin in Spring 2026 with bus lanes opening in Late Fall 2026 [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p017_c0003|Spring 2026 construction start]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p017_c0007|Late Fall 2026 bus lane opening]].
