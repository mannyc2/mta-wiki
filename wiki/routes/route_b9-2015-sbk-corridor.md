The [[route:route_b9-2015-sbk-corridor|B9]] appears in a 2015 list of east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn identified for potential BRT/speed improvements [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B9 listed among south Brooklyn routes]]. During the Flatbush Avenue Bus Priority project outreach from 2022–2023, the B9 was consistently described as a secondary route operating on Flatbush Ave, alongside the B67, B103, Q35, and BM2, with the B41 as the primary route [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p011_c0002|B9 as secondary route on Flatbush Ave]]. The Flatbush Ave corridor carries approximately 118,000 average daily bus riders across 12 routes and is a Vision Zero priority corridor [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb14_jan2023#p006_c0002|Flatbush Ave corridor statistics]] — [[metric:metric_ksi-flatbush-ave-5yr_2|164 killed or seriously injured on Flatbush Ave in 5 years]] were recorded in the five-year period preceding 2022 [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p006_c0002|164 KSI metric source]]. Buses that travel along Flatbush Ave provide connections to the B/D, N/Q/R, 2/3, 4/5, and S subway lines as well as the Long Island Rail Road [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb9_sept2022#p011_c0002|subway connections]].

The available sources create a scope tension: the 2015 kickoff board presents the B9 among east-west South Brooklyn routes, while the Flatbush Ave project sources reference it as a secondary route on a north-south arterial [[cite:flatbush_ave_cab1_nov2022#p016_c0002|B9 as Flatbush Ave secondary route]]. Further route-alignment details are not captured in these sources, and no treatment components or bus priority measures are specifically attributed to the B9 in this source set.
