[[route:route_bxm7|BxM7 Express Bus]] is a Bronx-Manhattan express bus route identified in the 116th Street / Morningside Avenue / Pleasant Avenue corridor study, which covers 2.2 miles between 114th and 113th Streets and serves over 65,000 daily bus passengers across 10 routes including the BxM7 [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p004_c0002|116th Street Study Area]]. The route also appears in the Madison Avenue Bus Improvement Project study area, where northbound express routes BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM10 begin service at 23rd Street and travel to Grand Central [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb6_jun2025#p009_c0003|Madison Avenue Route Diagram]], and the BxM7's speed data contributed to the Madison Avenue bus speed analysis from 23rd to 42nd Street [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb6_jun2025#p010_c0005|Bus Speed Data Note]].

Along the 116th Street corridor, express routes share road space with local buses experiencing severe congestion — the [[metric:metric_m116-bus-speed-am-less-than-4-mph|metric_m116_bus_speed_am_less_than_4_mph]] shows buses operate below 4 mph in some segments during weekday AM peak hours, and weekday congestion causes [[metric:metric_m116-daily-delay-785-hours-may2025|785 hours of delay to M116 passengers daily]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_may2025#p008_c0002|Congestion Delay Metric]]. Proposed treatments in the 116th Street study that would benefit express routes include offset and curbside bus lanes, left turn bays, pedestrian islands, and signalized mid-block crossings [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p005_c0002|CB10 Study Area June 2025]]. The [[route:route_bxm7|BxM7 Express Bus]] is operated by MTA New York City Transit under the Department of Buses, and the study is a joint effort with NYC DOT through the Better Buses initiative [[cite:better_buses#p001_b0001|Better Buses Program]].
