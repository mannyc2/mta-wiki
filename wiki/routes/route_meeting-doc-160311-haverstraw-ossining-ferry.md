[[route:route_meeting-doc-160311-haverstraw-ossining-ferry|Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry Route]] is a commuter ferry service connecting Haverstraw, Rockland County, with Ossining, Westchester County, across the Hudson River. After a successful demonstration project and MTA Board approval, service began in September 2000 [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry Service Start]]. The route operates Monday through Friday during morning and evening peak hours on regularly scheduled service [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Ferry Service Schedule]].

The route is operated by Port Imperial Ferry Corp., doing business as NY Waterway, under MNR contract [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p008_c0007|MN241812 Contract Listing]]. Service covers both the Haverstraw-Ossining and Newburgh-Beacon routes under the same contract [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0006|MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount]]. Ancillary services include daily parking facility maintenance, trash removal, snow removal, dock maintenance such as light fixture and stair tread replacement, and contingency bus services during ferry disruptions [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Ancillary Services Description]].

A prior competitively solicited contract (No. 1000044225) was awarded in 2014 for four years with three two-year renewal options [[cite:meeting_doc_95286#p002_c0007|Prior Contract Background]]. In September 2022, MNR sought board approval for a modification exercising the final two-year renewal option covering January 2023 through December 2024 [[cite:meeting_doc_95286#p001_c0007|2022 Modification Listing]].

The current contract MN241812 was approved for award at the December 2024 MTA Board meeting [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p003_c0001|Board Meeting Date]]. It is a four-year competitively solicited contract with three two-year options [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0006|MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount]]. The base amount is [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-base-amount|MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount]] and the total not-to-exceed value including options is [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-total-value|MN241812 Total Contract Value]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p008_c0007|MN241812 Total Contract Value]][[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0006|MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount]].

Through negotiations, MNR reduced NY Waterway's initial proposed rates from $846 per hour for operating services to [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-negotiated-operating-rate|MN241812 Negotiated Hourly Operating Rate]] (a 1.4 percent reduction) and from $42 per hour for parking services to [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-negotiated-parking-rate|MN241812 Negotiated Parking Rate]] (a 14.2 percent reduction) [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0003|Negotiated Rate Reductions]]. This produced total savings of approximately [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-total-savings|MN241812 Total Cost Savings]] (2.03 percent) over the base and option years [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0003|Total Cost Savings]].

The service is funded by [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-funding-mnroperating|MNR's Operating Budget (23 percent)]], [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-funding-federal|federal funding (39 percent)]], and [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-funding-nysdot|NYSDOT (38 percent)]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0010|Funding Split]]. The MTA Department of Diversity and Civil Rights established a 22.5 percent DBE goal for the contract [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0008|DBE Goal]]. The source states there are no alternatives for these ferry services [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0012|No Alternatives]].
