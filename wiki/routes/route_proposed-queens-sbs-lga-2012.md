The proposed Queens SBS route to LGA ([[route:route_proposed-queens-sbs-lga-2012|Proposed Queens SBS route to LGA]]) was developed through the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis, a partnership among the New York City Department of Transportation, MTA New York City Transit, MTA Bus Company, and the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey that began in May 2011 [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0003|Study partnership]]. The study identified Bus Rapid Transit, branded as Select Bus Service, as the best mode to improve airport access [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0004|BRT identified]].

The Queens route would offer speeds over 40% faster than current local service [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0021|Over 40% faster]], with features including limited stops, highway operation, and transit signal priority [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0024|Improvement features]]. Benefits cited for the route included improved regional connectivity via LIRR and subways and improved local route reliability [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0022|Improved regional connectivity]][[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0025|Improved local route reliability]].

The proposed Queens SBS route was part of a broader LGA transit improvement package. The existing M60 SBS route was projected to be 20% faster to LGA [[metric:metric_m60-sbs-20pct-faster-2012|M60 SBS route 20% faster to LGA]], while a proposed Bronx SBS route was projected to be 40% faster [[metric:metric_proposed-bronx-sbs-40pct-faster-2012|Proposed Bronx SBS route 40% faster]] [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0012|M60 SBS 20% faster]][[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0013|Bronx SBS 40% faster]]. Non-airport bus riders on the 125th Street corridor were also identified as a key market, with nearly 10,000 daily M60 riders traveling entirely on 125th Street, and bus improvements projected to benefit more than 32,000 daily riders of all routes on that corridor [[metric:metric_m60-10000-daily-riders-125th-2012|Nearly 10,000 daily M60 riders on 125th Street]][[metric:metric_32000-daily-riders-all-routes-125th-2012|More than 32,000 daily riders on 125th Street corridor]] [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0008|Ridership on 125th Street corridor]].

Implementation was planned for 2013/2014 following final alternatives analysis and detailed corridor design work [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0016|Next steps and timeline]].
