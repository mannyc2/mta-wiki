The [[route:route_q73-proposed-queens-redesign|Q73 (proposed)]] is a Local route introduced in the Queens Bus Network Redesign New Draft Plan, released in March 2022 as part of the MTA's effort to redesign the borough's bus network [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0010|Released New Draft Plan (Mar 2022)]]. Queens has [[metric:metric_115-bus-routes-queens|115 bus routes in Queens]] serving [[metric:metric_800k-riders-queens-weekday|800,000 weekday riders in Queens]], and the redesign aims to simplify routes, improve frequency, and rationalize stop spacing [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p002_c0003|115 bus routes that serve 800,000 customers]]. Under the redesign, local routes like the proposed Q73 target stop spacing of [[metric:metric_local-stop-spacing-800-1200|Local route stop spacing 800-1,200 feet]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p010_c0005|Local Routes 800 feet → 1,200 feet]].

The Q73 is proposed to serve a discontinued segment of the existing Q23 route, which was shown as a case study for network simplification on a map in the New Draft Plan [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p007_c0002|Discontinued segment would be served by the proposed Q73]]. The Q23 case study describes a more direct route with fewer turns and diversions, a faster corridor, and less redundant service [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p007_c0004|More direct route with fewer turns and fewer diversions]]. The Queens Redesign process began with a project launch in April 2019, published an Existing Conditions report in September 2019, and released the initial Draft Plan in December 2019, before the New Draft Plan was issued in March 2022 following a COVID-related pause and analysis of public feedback [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0002|Launched project with open houses and surveys (Apr 2019)]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0004|Published Existing Conditions report (Sep 2019)]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0006|Published Draft Plan (Dec 2019)]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0008|COVID pause on public engagement]].

As a proposed route introduced in a draft plan, the Q73's final alignment, service frequency, and implementation status remain subject to further public outreach and MTA board action [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0011|Conducting public outreach about the New Draft Plan (Mar-Jun 2022)]]. The route record is derived from a single map annotation and lacks detailed operational specifications such as terminal locations, span of service, or headways [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p007_c0002|Discontinued segment would be served by the proposed Q73]].
