The [[route:route_b7-2015-sbk-corridor|B7 Local]] appears on a 2015 list of south Brooklyn BRT candidate routes that also includes the B1, B2, B3, B4, B6, B8, B9, B11, B36, B64, B82, and B100, suggesting the route was under early consideration during the MTA/NYC DOT BRT planning process for the region [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|2015 BRT kickoff board]]. The B7 Local operates along Kings Highway, which is characterized as a major east-west transit corridor with narrow roadway, one lane in each direction, and a local truck route; during AM and PM rush hours buses come every 1-2 minutes along Kings Highway [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p016_c0002|CB 15 corridor description]].

An estimated 12,500 people in Community Board 15 use B82 Limited, B82 Local, or B7 service each day [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p014_c0005|CB 15 ridership snapshot]]. The Kings Highway corridor is also described as having a wide boulevard-style portion with narrow, deteriorating medians that serve as bus stops, and during rush hours buses come once per minute [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb14_may2017#p017_c0002|CB 14 corridor description]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p021_c0002|March 2018 corridor description]].

The B7 Local shares its Kings Highway running way with the B82 Local and B82 Limited, and the corridor was the focus of extensive BRT planning between 2015 and 2018 that proposed dedicated bus lanes, median reconstructions, and improved bus stop amenities—all treatments that would also serve B7 riders [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p016_c0002|shared corridor context]]. Medians serving B7 bus stops at Kings Highway and Nostrand Avenue and at Kings Highway and East 27 Street were reconstructed to wider, ADA-accessible medians in 2017, with larger shelters added [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p018_c0002|median reconstruction at E 27 St]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p019_c0002|median reconstruction at Nostrand Ave]].
