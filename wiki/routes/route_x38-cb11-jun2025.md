[[route:route_x38-cb11-jun2025|X38]] is an express bus route serving the Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue corridors in Brooklyn, documented as part of the Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue Bus Priority and Safety Improvements Draft Proposal presented to Brooklyn Community Board 11 Transportation Committee on June 16, 2025 [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p001_c0002|CB 11 Transportation Committee]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p001_c0003|June 16, 2025]]. The proposal is a joint MTA-NYC DOT BetterBuses initiative [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p001_c0001|Draft Proposal title]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p001_c0005|BETTERBUSES]]. The study areas are Bay Parkway from Avenue J to Shore Parkway (2.4 miles) and Cropsey Avenue from Bay Parkway to 26th Avenue (0.6 miles) [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002|Study areas]]. Across these corridors there are [[metric:metric_35k-daily-bus-riders|35,000 daily bus riders]] served by the B6 Local and Limited, B82 Local and Select Bus Service, and the X28 and [[route:route_x38-cb11-jun2025|X38]] Express bus, with connections to F, N, and D subway trains [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002|35,000 daily bus riders]].

Bus speeds drop as low as [[metric:metric_bus-speed-3-mph-bay-pkwy|Bay Parkway low bus speed]] on Bay Parkway, with buses averaging 5 mph throughout the day and 3 mph during peak hours [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p022_c0002|Bus speeds 5 mph/3 mph]]. The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with [[metric:metric_ksi-22-2020-2024|22 people killed or seriously injured 2020-2024]] between 2020 and 2024, and [[metric:metric_2-more-deaths-2025|2 more deaths in 2025]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002|KSI and 2025 deaths]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p023_c0004|Injury table]]. There were [[metric:metric_517-injuries-bay-pkwy-2020-2024|517 people injured in crashes on Bay Parkway 2020-2024]] on Bay Parkway from 2020-2024, including [[metric:metric_20-severe-injuries-bay-pkwy-2020-2024|20 severely injured on Bay Parkway 2020-2024]] and [[metric:metric_2-fatalities-bay-pkwy-2020-2024|2 fatalities on Bay Parkway 2020-2024]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p023_c0004|Injury table]]. The census tracts around the two corridors have [[metric:metric_110k-residents-near-corridors|110,000 residents near study corridors]], with [[metric:metric_transit-commute-share|52% commute by transit]], [[metric:metric_car-commute-share|32% commute by car]], and a [[metric:metric_avg-commute-44-min|44-minute average travel time to work]]; [[metric:metric_44-percent-no-vehicle|44% of households with no vehicle]] and [[metric:metric_43-percent-one-vehicle|43% of households with 1 vehicle]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p021_c0002|Demographics]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p021_c0004|Commute mode split]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p021_c0006|Vehicle ownership]].

The draft proposal would repurpose one of two general travel lanes for offset bus lanes along Bay Parkway, maintaining the left travel lane and curbside parking lane [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p009_c0002|Repurpose travel lane]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p007_c0002|Offset bus lanes proposal]]. Similar offset bus lanes are proposed for Cropsey Avenue with block-by-block customized design [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p007_c0002|Cropsey proposal]]. The proposal cites successful offset bus lane precedents: on 21st Street in Queens (weekday peak bus speeds up [[metric:metric_21st-st-bus-speed-increase-17pct|21st Street Queens - bus speeds up 17%]], injuries [[metric:metric_21st-st-injuries-decrease-22pct|down 22%]]) and on Utica Avenue in Brooklyn (weekday peak bus speeds up [[metric:metric_utica-ave-bus-speed-increase-19pct|Utica Ave Brooklyn - bus speeds up 19%]], injuries [[metric:metric_utica-ave-injuries-decrease-7pct|down 7%]]) [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p013_c0003|21st Street results]] [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p013_c0006|Utica Avenue results]]. Proposed implementation is targeted for late 2025 or 2026 [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p017_c0008|Proposed implementation]].
