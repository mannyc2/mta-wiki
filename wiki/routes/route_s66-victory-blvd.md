The [[route:route_s66-victory-blvd|S66]] is one of five local bus routes on Victory Boulevard in Staten Island, alongside the S46, S48, S61, and S62 [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p008_c0004|S66 listed among local routes on Victory Boulevard]]. Victory Boulevard carries 16 total bus routes (local, limited, and express) serving approximately 25,789 average daily riders and facilitating connections to the Staten Island Ferry [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p004_c0003|corridor-wide ridership and route count]]. The same set of local routes, including the S66, is consistently referenced across MTA and NYC DOT outreach presentations to Staten Island Community Boards 1 and 2 [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p008_c0004|Winter 2025 CB 1 presentation]] [[cite:victory_blvd_wild_ave_willowbrook_ave_cb2_jun2025#p007_c0004|June 2025 CB 2 presentation]].

The corridor faces significant bus speed and safety challenges. During morning peak hours (6-10 AM), bus speeds on Victory Boulevard drop below 8 mph, and during afternoon and evening hours, speeds fall below 7 mph [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p010_c0004|AM bus speeds]] [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p012_c0004|PM bus speeds]]. From 2019 to 2023, Victory Boulevard recorded 619 injuries, including 60 severe injuries, ranking it in the top 10% most dangerous corridors in Staten Island [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p007_c0002|safety ranking and injury data]]. North of Jersey Street, 48% of vehicular road users on Victory Boulevard are bus riders [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p013_c0002|bus rider share]].

Existing bus priority treatments on Victory Boulevard date back to 1963, with unpainted curbside peak-period bus lanes that lack enforcement [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p005_c0002|existing lane conditions]]. NYC DOT and MTA are studying upgrades including red paint, extended hours, and new curbside bus lane segments as part of the Victory Boulevard Transit and Safety Improvements project [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p015_c0002|curbside concept treatments]] [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_little_clove_rd_may2025#p020_c0002|design summary for CB 1 section]]. Implementation for the CB 1 section (Bay Street to Little Clove Road) was expected in Summer 2025, while the CB 2 section (Wild Avenue to Willowbrook Avenue) has a Spring 2026 target [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_little_clove_rd_may2025#p022_c0005|Summer 2025 implementation]] [[cite:victory_blvd_wild_ave_willowbrook_ave_cb2_jun2025#p019_c0002|Spring 2026 implementation for CB 2]].
