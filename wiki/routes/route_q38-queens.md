The [[route:route_q38-queens|Q38 bus route]] is a Queens bus route described in the redesign equity appendix as operating as a loop, with its start and end points approximately a third of a mile apart between Rego Park and Corona — neighborhoods classified within Tier 1 (Significant Equity Consideration) and Tier 2 (Moderate Equity Consideration) areas under the Queens Bus Network Redesign equity framework [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Q38 route split description]].

Under the Proposed Final Plan Addendum of the Queens Bus Network Redesign, the Q38 is identified for a route-split treatment intended to enhance service reliability [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p019_c0003|Route Improvements Summary table]]. The proposal would divide the existing Q38 loop into two separate routes. Service along 62/63 Drs, Penelope Avenue, Juniper Valley Road, and Metropolitan Avenue between Rego Park and Maspeth is proposed to be maintained [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Q38 route split description]]. The northern segment of the existing Q38 would be replaced by the proposed Q14, a new route that would also incorporate the northern portion of the existing Q23 in Corona and East Elmhurst and the existing Q39 on Forest Avenue, creating a new connection between East Elmhurst and Ridgewood and expanding access for residents in several Tier 1 block groups [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Q38 route split description]].

The split-routes strategy, as applied to the Q38, is part of a broader set of redesign improvements aimed at addressing persistent service issues such as unreliable routes and confusing service patterns, with particular attention to moderate and significant equity areas [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p005_c0004|Route Improvement Assessment methodology]].
