The G Subway Line ([[route:route_meeting-doc-115256-g-subway|G Subway Line]]) serves Brooklyn and Queens as the Crosstown Line. On June 29, 2023, President Richard Davey, Senior Vice President of Subways Demetrius Crichlow, and local officials announced at Hoyt-Schermerhorn Sts Station that weekend service would increase on the G, J, and M lines, and a second batch of R211 subway cars went into service the same day [[cite:meeting_doc_115256#p002_c0004|G/J/M weekend service announcement June 2023]]. Customer satisfaction on the G line reached 75% in June 2023, up 5% from May, the highest among subway lines cited in the same metrics report [[cite:meeting_doc_115256#p005_c0004|G line satisfaction June 2023]] (see [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-115256-g-line-satisfaction|G line customer satisfaction June 2023]]).

The G line has been positioned as a modernization benchmark. An September 2024 MTA Board Meeting slide described a "Model G Train Project" that "Sets a New Standard" [[cite:meeting_doc_152171#p003_c0003|Model G Train Project]], and another presentation slide titled "Replicating G Train Success on the A Line" indicates the G's operational model is being extended to other routes [[cite:meeting_doc_152171#p004_c0002|Replicating G Train Success on the A Line]]. A Crosstown Line Signal Modernization project was underway, with G trains scheduled to resume service between Church Av and Court Sq starting September 3 at 5 AM [[cite:meeting_doc_152171#p003_c0002|Crosstown Line Signal Modernization]].
