The [[route:route_meeting-doc-129371-bx23|Bx23]] is a local bus route operated by MTA Bus Company (MTABC) connecting Co-op City with the Pelham Bay Park Station in the Bronx [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0004|Bx23 service description]]. In a 2023 update on the Extending Transit's Reach Strategic Action Plan, MTA reported that the Bx23 was one of four local bus routes equipped with front-of-bus bike racks, joining the S53, S93, and Q50 [[cite:meeting_doc_129371#p005_c0004|front-of-bus bike racks on local routes]]. A separate October 2022 presentation on bicycle, pedestrian, and micromobility access confirmed that permanent bike racks had been installed on the same four bus routes crossing MTA bridges [[cite:meeting_doc_98651#p005_c0002|permanent bike racks installed]].

As part of a congestion pricing-related service enhancement proposal, the Bx23 was identified for running time adjustments among a package covering eight express bus routes and sixteen local bus routes/route combinations [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0007|service enhancement recommendation]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0004|Bx23 running time adjustments]]. The package's [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160441-total-annual-cost|annual operating cost]] was estimated at approximately $8 million, of which the [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160441-mtabus-annual-cost|MTA Bus Company annual cost allocation]] was roughly $2.9 million per year [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0010|MTA Bus Company cost allocation]]. A [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160441-first-year-bus-rehab|first-year local bus rehabilitation cost]] of $5 million would also be needed to rehabilitate 29 local buses [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0009|first-year bus rehabilitation cost]]. Local bus route adjustments were scheduled for implementation in Summer 2025 [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p005_c0006|local bus implementation date]].
