## Route Role

B46 Local is the all-stop layer of the B46 corridor, which 2013 Utica materials mapped from Williamsburg Bridge Plaza to Kings Plaza via Broadway and Utica Avenue and described as making all stops while the Limited-Stop pattern skipped stops between DeKalb Av/Malcolm X Blvd and Avenue H/Utica Av. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p003_c0003]

The route was treated as a major bus-priority candidate because Utica-area materials described limited subway access, more than 49,000 weekday bus passengers, below-Brooklyn-average B46 speed, congestion, bunching, and the B46 as New York City's second-busiest bus route. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p003_c0002]

## Local, Limited, And SBS Planning

In the pre-SBS service pattern, B46 LTD generally ran between Kings Plaza and Williamsburg Bridge Plaza, B46 LTD made all local stops north of DeKalb Ave and south of Ave H, and B46 Local generally ran between Kings Plaza and DeKalb Ave. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p014_c0002]

The SBS plan was framed as an upgrade of B46 Limited to B46 SBS, with off-board fare collection, dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, reliability-oriented service changes, and capital improvements at SBS stations. [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p008_c0002] [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p008_c0003]

The proposed service plan kept high-ridership Limited stops and key transfers, made SBS the limited-stop pattern south of Avenue H, extended local service to the full corridor 24 hours per day, and set DeKalb Ave as the SBS northern terminal. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p015_c0002]

The Winter/Spring 2016 plan stated that B46 Local would extend to Williamsburg Bridge Plaza, make all stops along Broadway 24 hours per day, and continue making all local stops from DeKalb Av to Kings Plaza. [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0004] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0005]

The same planned-stop page separated the local and SBS patterns by stating that B46 SBS would make SBS stops from DeKalb Av to Kings Plaza and would not make local stops south of Avenue H. [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0006] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0007]

## Utica Avenue Treatments

The Utica SBS outreach began with a November 13, 2014 DOT/MTA workshop on upgrading B46 Limited to SBS, improving bus service while maintaining traffic flow and curb access, and increasing safety along Utica Avenue. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0001]

Workshop materials tied the corridor to the 2009 BRT Phase II Study, a 61% transit commute share among nearby residents, slow and crowded bus trips, and community ranking of the Utica Avenue B46 corridor as one of two corridors most in need of improvements. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010]

DOT's 2014 precursor work added 1.3 miles of offset and curbside bus lanes between St. Johns Place and Church Avenue, left-turn changes, and loading zones, and the same summary reported B46 Limited and Local travel-time decreases of 8-15 percent in the bus-lane sections. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p003_c0002]

A 2013 study deck focused on Utica Avenue from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue, named Eastern Parkway, Empire/Lefferts, and Church Avenue as key intersections, and listed goals to improve pedestrian and driver safety, improve bus travel times, and maintain parking and curb access. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p002_c0004]

That 2013 deck also included traffic-signal changes and Transit Signal Priority from Kings Highway to Broadway as proposed operating tools. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p017_c0003]

## Malcolm X / Utica Follow-On

The 2019 Malcolm X / Utica materials kept the B46 route frame but shifted to the northern bottleneck: May 2019 described B46 SBS as launched in July 2016, said southbound ridership had increased in Bed-Stuy, and said bus speeds had slowed on blocks without bus lanes. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p004_c0002]

The same May 2019 deck identified Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave as a 2019 priority project because of slow peak speeds, unreliable travel times, high ridership, and high bus volumes. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p008_c0002]

For Chauncey St to Atlantic Ave, the May 2019 deck identified Fulton St as the second-busiest B46 stop with 3,800 daily boardings and southbound traffic backing up from Atlantic Ave to Fulton St and Chauncey St. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p012_c0002]

The June 2019 proposal called for a southbound curbside bus lane from Fulton St to Atlantic Ave, preserved overnight parking, and described the lane as letting buses bypass traffic approaching Atlantic Ave. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p014_c0002]

The June 2019 schedule put Fulton St-to-Atlantic Ave implementation in summer 2019, the B46 articulated-bus upgrade in 2019/2020, and Chauncey St-to-Fulton St implementation as TBD. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p017_c0002]

By March 2020, the materials stated that B46 SBS had been upgraded to articulated buses in January 2020 and listed spring 2020 for Chauncey St-to-Fulton St implementation. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p004_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p016_c0002]
