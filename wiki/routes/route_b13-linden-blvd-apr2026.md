The [[route:route_b13-linden-blvd-apr2026|B13]] appears as one of six bus routes in the MTA/NYC DOT Linden Blvd Traffic Safety and Bus Priority Improvements proposal presented to Brooklyn Community Board 5 on April 20, 2026 [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p001_c0002|CB5 presentation date]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p020_c0003|B13 route map]]. The proposal covers the 1.1-mile Linden Blvd corridor from Fountain Av to Conduit Av, a [[metric:metric_road-width-140-ft|Road width 140 feet]] road with [[metric:metric_10-lanes-corridor|10 travel lanes]] where [[metric:metric_bus-speed-4-mph|bus speeds drop as low as 4 mph]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p004_c0002|corridor overview]]. The corridor serves [[metric:metric_60000-daily-riders|60,000 daily riders]] across six routes and runs through census tracts where [[metric:metric_households-no-car-57-pct|57% of households no vehicle]] and [[metric:metric_public-transit-commute-54-pct|54% commute via public transit]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p010_c0002|demographics]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p010_c0003|transit mode share]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p010_c0005|vehicle availability]].

Linden Blvd is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor; between 2019 and 2023 there were [[metric:metric_safety-total-injuries-330|330 total injuries (2019-2023)]], [[metric:metric_safety-severe-injuries-13_2|13 severely injured (2019-2023)]], and [[metric:metric_safety-fatalities-2|2 killed (2019-2023)]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p011_c0002|safety record]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p011_c0005|injury table]]. The Linden Blvd and Euclid Av intersection is a Vision Zero Priority Intersection and the corridor is a Priority Youth Injury Area [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p011_c0002|priority designations]]. The presentation describes the road as split into a main line and service road, with buses running in local access lanes and getting stuck behind double-parked cars, and [[metric:metric_crosswalk-spacing-1200-ft|crosswalks up to 1,200 feet apart]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p009_c0002|existing conditions]].

Under the proposal, the B13 routing remains unchanged, but buses would travel on the main road instead of the service road, with new boarding island bus stops at Euclid Av, Crescent St, Eldert Ln, and 79 St [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p020_c0002|bus service changes]]. The project aligns with the HPD Jewel Streets Neighborhood Plan (released October 2025) [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p007_c0002|plan release date]] [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p007_c0004|plan strategies]]. Construction is expected to begin in late summer or fall 2026, with completion and repaving in spring/summer 2027, followed by performance monitoring [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p024_c0002|project timeline]].

The map label "Bronx, NY" in the source OCR is a known error; the Linden Blvd corridor is located in Brooklyn and Queens [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p004_c0002|Brooklyn and Queens reference]]. Other bus routes on the corridor include B14, B15, B20, and Q8 [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p020_c0003|route map]].
