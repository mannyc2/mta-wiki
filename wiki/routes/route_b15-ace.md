The B15 is an MTA bus route operating primarily on Marcus Garvey Boulevard and New Lots Avenue, and it is one of six routes serving the Linden Blvd corridor between Fountain Avenue and Conduit Avenue in Brooklyn and Queens [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p004_c0002|Linden Blvd corridor overview]]. The corridor carries approximately 60,000 daily riders across six routes, and the B15 is also affected by a no-stop zone between Linden Boulevard / 79 Street and Lefferts Boulevard in both directions [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_jan2025#p008_c0003|B15 no-stop zone]].

Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) was recently activated on the B15, along with the M31, with both routes beginning their 60-day warning period on April 13 [[cite:meeting_doc_205546#p011_c0010|ACE activation on B15]]. More than one million daily riders across 60 routes now benefit from ACE [[cite:meeting_doc_205546#p011_c0010|ACE reach]].

Along the Linden Blvd corridor, bus speeds have been measured as low as 4 mph [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p004_c0002|bus speeds 4 mph]]—a [[metric:metric_bus-speed-4-mph|metric: bus speeds as low as 4 mph]] that reflects congestion from double-parked vehicles and commercial traffic in the single travel lane [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p012_c0002|congestion causes]]. An earlier January 2025 presentation to Community Board 5 cited bus speeds as low as 6 mph [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_jan2025#p004_c0002|earlier bus speed metric]], reflected in [[metric:metric_bus-speed-as-low-as-6-mph|metric: bus speeds as low as 6 mph]]. The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor; between 2019 and 2023 there were 330 total injuries, 13 severe injuries, and 2 fatalities [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_jan2025#p010_c0004|safety data table]], documented as [[metric:metric_safety-injuries-330|metric: 330 total injuries]], [[metric:metric_safety-severe-injuries-13|metric: 13 severe injuries]], and [[metric:metric_safety-fatalities-2_2|metric: 2 fatalities]].

The MTA and NYC DOT are pursuing Linden Blvd Traffic Safety and Bus Priority Improvements, which were presented to Brooklyn Community Board 5 in January 2025 and again in April 2026 [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p001_c0002|CB5 April 2026 presentation]]. Proposed changes include boarding island bus stops at Euclid Avenue, Crescent Street, Eldert Lane, and 79 Street, with buses traveling in the main roadway instead of the service road [[cite:linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_apr2026#p020_c0002|proposed routing changes]].
