## Route Scope

This page is best read as the Bx12 Select Bus Service route on Fordham Road/Pelham Parkway, with camera-enforcement material attached to the same route identity. Source decks describe Select Bus Service as New York City's BRT version first used on the Fordham Road-Pelham Parkway Bx12 SBS route, show the Bx12 SBS corridor as Fordham Road/Pelham Parkway, and map stations from Broadway/207th Street through Fordham Road, Pelham Bay Park, Co-op City, and Bay Plaza. The staged MTA ACE source separately lists "Bx12-SBS, Pelham Pkwy / Fordham Rd," while the route dictionary evidence uses the internal item "BX12+" [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p006_c0004] [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p009_c0001] [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p009_c0002] [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001] [ace_routes_dataset_dictionary#p001_b0065].

## First SBS Implementation

Official planning materials repeatedly use the Bx12 as the prototype for New York City SBS/BRT. The Phase II outreach report says DOT and NYCT piloted the city's first BRT route, the Bx12 Select Bus Service on Fordham Road in the Bronx, starting in summer 2008; later Fordham Road-Inwood materials say MTA and DOT launched the first SBS route on the Bx12 in 2008 [brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p006_c0005] [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p004_c0002].

## Service Package And Early Results

The Bx12 SBS package included new or upgraded curbside bus lanes, red coloration, overhead signs, retail-core delivery windows, off-board fare collection, transit signal priority, a simplified service pattern, and new shelters. A 2009 CAC deck also gives Fordham Road SBS an average station spacing of 0.6 mile [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p010_c0002] [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p017_c0003].

Early results cited in 2012 Webster Avenue materials report Bx12 SBS implementation in June 2008, a 20% reduction in running time, a 7% ridership increase from June 2008 to June 2009, and 98% customer satisfaction. A broader BRT/SBS comparison table records Bx12 SBS at $10 million in cost excluding buses, a 20% travel-time change, and 45,400 first-year weekday riders [201205_brt_webster_cac#p006_c0003] [201205_brt_webster_cac#p006_c0005] [201205_brt_webster_cac#p006_c0007] [201205_brt_webster_cac#p006_c0009] [brt_routes_fullreport#p015_c0006].

## Ridership And Speed History

By 2023, Fordham Road-Inwood project materials described the Bx12 as the busiest bus route in the Bronx and the second busiest in New York City after the M15. The same source frames Fordham Road as a critical crosstown and Bronx-Manhattan corridor, with 85,000 average daily bus riders on five routes: Bx12 SBS and Local, Bx9, Bx17, and Bx22 [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p004_c0002].

A route history table in the 2023 Fordham Road-Inwood deck shows combined Bx12 local/SBS daily ridership rising from 42,500 in 2007 to 48,500 in 2014 and 2015, then falling to 40,500 in 2019. The same table shows Bx12 SBS speed at 9.0 mph in 2008, 9.1 mph from 2009 through 2012, and 7.5 mph in 2018 and 2019 [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p007_c0003].

## ABLE And ACE Enforcement

ABLE belongs here as a route-specific enforcement overlay, not as the original SBS implementation. Fordham Road materials say ABLE was implemented on all Bx12-SBS buses on November 18, 2022, with a 60-day warning period and summonses starting January 17, 2023. The same source says ABLE enforces against vehicles parking or standing in bus lanes during bus lane hours, requires two buses to capture the same vehicle five minutes apart, records license plate/photo/video/location information, and uses DOT human review [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p049_c0002].

In the 2023 CB6 presentation, the Fordham Road ABLE segment between Sedgwick Avenue and Southern Boulevard issued nearly 16,000 tickets between November 18, 2022 and April 21, 2023; the table reports Fordham Road average speed increasing from 6.0 mph in October 2022 to 6.3 mph in April 2023, a 4.7% change, with 15,799 violations. The source also says enforcement alone had not restored speeds to prior levels because blockages remained frequent [fordham_rd_inwood_cb6_jun2023#p024_c0002] [fordham_rd_inwood_cb6_jun2023#p024_c0004] [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p024_c0002].

## Recent Fordham Road Context

Later Fordham Road sources are corridor context for the Bx12 SBS, not a Bx12-only service plan. The 2023 Fordham Road-Inwood materials identify Alternative A, offset bus lanes on Fordham Road, as the preferred treatment to improve bus speeds, reliability, and curb demand while adding 150+ parking/loading spaces; the same source warns that double parking can still block offset lanes [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p043_c0002] [fordham_rd_inwood_cab_may2023#p045_c0003].

The 2026 Fordham Road Bus Priority decks broaden the scope further: they say buses on Fordham Road travel as slow as 5 mph and are unreliable, affecting 130,000 average daily riders, and propose upgrading current bus lanes to offset lanes and widened curbside lanes. The design description includes converting many sub-standard curbside bus lanes into loading/parking lanes and converting the adjacent travel lane into a 24/7 offset bus lane between Hoffman Avenue and Sedgwick Avenue [fordham_rd_sedgwick_ave_bronx_river_pkwy_cb5_may2026#p003_c0002] [fordham_rd_sedgwick_ave_bronx_river_pkwy_cb5_may2026#p006_c0002].

## Caveats

The target record is merged and noisy: it combines `BX12+`, `Bx12-SBS`, Bx12 Local/Fordham Road corridor material, ABLE/ACE enforcement, and generic SBS reference examples. Fordham Road corridor ridership figures are not always Bx12-only; the 85,000 and 130,000 rider figures are corridor/project scopes. Speed metrics also vary by scope, so historical Bx12 route table values should not be conflated with ABLE segment speeds.
