The [[route:route_b2-2015-sbk-corridor|B2]] route is listed among 14 east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn that were identified as part of the South Brooklyn Crosstown Select Bus Service study corridor [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0004|14 bus routes provide East-West service]]. The east-west bus routes in this corridor carried a combined ridership of 178,000 per day [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0007|178,000 per day]]. The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0009|priority service need]]. At the time of the June 2015 kick-off meeting, seven Select Bus Service routes were operating citywide [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0006|seven SBS routes operating in NYC]], with documented performance outcomes including 15–23% faster bus service [[metric:metric_sbs-faster-bus-15-23-pct|SBS bus service speed improvement]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0007|15-23%]], ridership increases averaging 110% [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-110-pct|SBS ridership increase]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0009|↑110%]], 95% bus rider satisfaction [[metric:metric_sbs-rider-satisfaction-95-pct|SBS rider satisfaction]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0013|95%]], and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors [[metric:metric_sbs-crash-reduction-20-pct_3|SBS crash reduction]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0015|↓20%]]. The project was led by NYC DOT, with Matt Kroneberger serving as Community Outreach Coordinator [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p009_c0003|Contact Matt Kroneberger]]. This page represents the [[route:route_b2-2015-sbk-corridor|B2]] specifically in its role within the 2015 South Brooklyn Crosstown corridor study, not the full operational history of the B2 bus route.
