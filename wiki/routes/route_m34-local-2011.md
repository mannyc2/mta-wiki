The [[route:route_m34-local-2011|M34 Bus Route (Local)]] was a local crosstown bus operating the length of 34th Street in Manhattan between the Javits Center and the FDR Drive [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p019_c0002|M34 route description]]. By 2009 its performance was poor: the average one-way travel time was 28 minutes for the 2-mile route, yielding an average speed of only 4.3 mph, marginally faster than walking [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p020_c0003|28-minute travel time]] [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p009_c0009|4.3 mph speed]]. Of the total trip time, only 40% was spent in motion, while dwell time consumed 38% and waiting at traffic signals consumed 22% [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p032_c0003|time breakdown]]. These metrics — [[metric:metric_m34-oneway-travel-time-28min|M34 one-way travel time 28 minutes]], [[metric:metric_m34-avg-speed-43-mph|M34 average speed 4.3 mph]], [[metric:metric_m34-time-in-motion-pct|M34 time in motion 40% of trip]], [[metric:metric_m34-dwell-time-pct|M34 dwell time 38% of trip]], and [[metric:metric_m34-signal-delay-pct|M34 signal delay 22% of trip]] — captured the baseline conditions before major improvements.

In 2011, NYCDOT and NYCT planned near-term improvements for the M34 including the introduction of off-board fare collection and additional bus lane camera enforcement [[cite:20110314_34th_cac4_slides#p007_c0002|2011 planned improvements]]. NYCDOT Project Manager Will Carry presented these changes at the March 2011 Community Advisory Committee meeting, explaining that they would apply to both the M34 and M16 routes [[cite:201103_34th_cac_summary#p001_c0006|Will Carry presentation]]. Earlier CAC discussions in January 2011 had addressed stop consolidation along the corridor, including proposals to consolidate stops at Madison and Fifth Avenues to meet NYCT stop-spacing guidelines and create expanded loading zones [[cite:201103_34th_cac_summary#p004_c0001|stop consolidation]].

Future development in the study area was projected to generate an estimated 5,000 to 6,000 new bus riders, requiring 34 additional AM-peak and 31 additional PM-peak bus trips [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p021_c0018|future ridership growth]] — captured as [[metric:metric_total-future-new-bus-riders|Total future new bus riders 5,000-6,000]]. The M34 formed one of two bus routes (along with the M16) serving the 34th Street corridor during this period, operating as a local service with plans to evolve toward Select Bus Service features.
