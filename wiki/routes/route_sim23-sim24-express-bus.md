The [[route:route_sim23-sim24-express-bus|SIM 23 / SIM 24 Express Bus Routes]] are express bus routes in Staten Island that were previously operated by the City of New York and subsequently taken over by the MTA [[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p016_c0014|MTA Capital Program 2020-2024 Amendment #2, page 16]]. The MTA's takeover of these routes is funded through the proposed Amendment #2 to the MTA 2020-2024 Capital Program, which adds $7 million in City Capital funds specifically for bus purchases associated with the transfer [[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p016_c0014|City Capital funding for SIM 23/24 bus purchases]]. This amendment, proposed to the MTA Board on July 27, 2022, encompasses a total program of $55.442 billion across all MTA agencies [[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p001_c0007|Proposed to MTA Board July 27, 2022]][[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p011_c0001|Total Program $55.442 billion]]. The $7 million in City Capital funds is part of a broader $100 million increase in State of New York Capital funds and $542 million in other sources included in the amendment [[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p016_c0011|State funds increase $100 million]][[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p017_c0009|Other sources $542 million]]. The routes, which the source identifies as SIM23 and SIM24, represent Staten Island express bus service that transitioned from city to MTA operational control during the 2020-2024 capital planning period [[cite:meeting_doc_91711#p016_c0014|SIM 23/24 previously operated by City of New York]].
