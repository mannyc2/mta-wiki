[[route:route_bx42-tremont-cab1|Bx42]] is a Bronx bus route that operates on the Tremont Avenue Corridor. As part of the Bx40/Bx42 service group, the route was restructured under the MTA Bronx Bus Network Redesign implemented in June 2022, which gave Bx36 and Bx40/Bx42 straight, direct routing so that Bx40/Bx42 serves Tremont Avenue east of Rosedale Avenue and the two route pairs no longer switch streets at Webster Avenue [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p007_c0002|Bronx Bus Network Redesign routing description]]. The redesign also consolidated bus stops to improve travel time and reliability [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p007_c0002|Bus stop consolidation]].

The Tremont Avenue Corridor is a 6.8-mile major east-west transit corridor in the Bronx from Sedgwick Avenue to Boston Road serving six routes including Bx42 with a combined daily ridership of approximately 69,000 [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p032_c0004|2019 Bronx Bus Network Plan corridor profile]]. The study area for the Tremont Avenue Bus Priority Project extends from University Avenue to Boston Road, where the Bx36 Limited and local carry nearly 29,000 daily bus passengers ([[metric:metric_bx36-daily-ridership_4|Bx36 daily bus passengers]]) as the 4th busiest bus route in the Bronx [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p005_c0002|Study area description]].

The corridor's census tracts total approximately 118,000 residents ([[metric:metric_residents-census-tracts|Residents in Tremont Av census tracts]]), with 72% of households lacking access to a private vehicle ([[metric:metric_households-no-vehicle_3|Households without vehicle access]], above the city average of 55%) and 78% commuting via public transit, walking, or biking ([[metric:metric_commute-public-transit|Commute via public transit/walking/biking]], above the city average of 71%) [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p008_c0003|Demographic summary]]. The [[metric:metric_median-household-income_3|median household income]] is $31,000 (below the city average of $67,000) and [[metric:metric_avg-travel-time-to-work|average travel time to work]] is 45 minutes [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p008_c0003|Income and commute data]].

From 2016 to 2020, over 1,100 people were injured in crashes on Tremont Avenue ([[metric:metric_crash-injuries-total-table|Total injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020 (from table)]]), with 43 severe injuries ([[metric:metric_crash-injuries-severe|Severe crash injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]) and 2 fatalities ([[metric:metric_crash-fatalities|Crash fatalities on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]) [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p009_c0002|Safety summary]]. The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with three designated Vision Zero Priority Intersections at Grand Concourse, Anthony Avenue, and Boston Road [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p009_c0002|Vision Zero designation]]. The injury breakdown includes 215 pedestrian injuries ([[metric:metric_pedestrian-injuries|Pedestrian injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]), 95 bicyclist injuries ([[metric:metric_bicyclist-injuries|Bicyclist injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]), and 797 motor vehicle occupant injuries ([[metric:metric_motor-vehicle-occupant-injuries|Motor vehicle occupant injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]), with a total of 45 KSI ([[metric:metric_ksi-total|Total KSI on Tremont Av 2016-2020]]) [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p009_c0004|Injury summary table]].

The Tremont Avenue Bus Priority Project is a joint NYC DOT and MTA effort, with plans for a half-mile busway segment, offset bus lanes, transit signal priority, and bus lane camera enforcement [[cite:tremont_ave_bus_priority_cab1_dec2022#p019_c0002|Toolkit list]].
