The [[route:route_meeting-doc-160311-newburgh-beacon-ferry|Newburgh-Beacon Ferry Route]] began service in October 2005 after a successful demonstration project and MTA Board approval, following the similar Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry service which launched in September 2000 [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Newburgh-Beacon Ferry Service Start]]. Both routes provide regularly scheduled commuter ferry service Monday through Friday during morning and evening peak times, and NY Waterway provides contingency bus services in the event of ferry disruptions [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Ferry Service Schedule and Contingency]]. Ancillary services include parking facility maintenance, trash and snow removal, and dock maintenance such as light fixture and stair or ramp tread replacement [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0007|Ancillary Services]].

The ferry services on this route are covered under competitively solicited contract MN241812 with Port Imperial Ferry Corp., doing business as NY Waterway [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p008_c0007|NY Waterway Contract Listing]]. Metro-North Railroad issued a request for proposals to 16 firms on September 13, 2024, and received a single proposal from NY Waterway on October 25, 2024 [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0008|RFP Issuance Date]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0009|Single Proposal Received Date]]. The Selection Committee unanimously selected NY Waterway for negotiations after evaluating its technical proposal [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0010|Selection Committee Unanimous Recommendation]].

The total contract value is $44,293,662, comprising a four-year base amount of $16,781,539 and three two-year options totaling $27,511,723 [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p008_c0007|Contract Total Amount]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p009_c0006|Base and Options Amounts]]. Through negotiations, MNR secured a reduced hourly operating rate of $834 (down from the initial $846) and a reduced parking rate of $36 per hour (down from $42), contributing to the [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-total-savings|Total Cost Savings]] of approximately $916,412, or 2.03 percent, across the base and option years [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0003|Negotiated Rate Reductions and Savings]]. The [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160311-mn241812-operating-reduction|Operating Rate Reduction]] was 1.4 percent and the parking rate reduction was 14.2 percent [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0003|Rate Reduction Percentages]]. Funding for the ferry service comes from MNR's Operating Budget at 23 percent, federal funding at 39 percent, and the NYS Department of Transportation at 38 percent [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p010_c0010|Funding Source Breakdown]]. The Metro-North Committee reviewed the procurement on December 16, 2024, and the MTA Board approved it on December 18, 2024 [[cite:meeting_doc_160311#p003_c0001|Committee and Board Meeting Dates]].
