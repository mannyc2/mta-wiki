The [[route:route_s59-hylan-2011|S59 local bus]] is a Staten Island local route serving Richmond Avenue, and it was a key component of the service plan when the S79 was converted to Select Bus Service on September 2, 2012 [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p007_c0008|S59 along Richmond Avenue]]. Under the SBS restructure, the S79 became the limited-stop S79 SBS, while the S59 and S78 continued to serve all local stops with added service [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p001_c0002|S59 and S78 continue local stops]]. At community meetings, the plan was presented as the S59 and S78 providing local service alongside the streamlined S79 SBS [[cite:2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p008_c0005|S59, S78 provide Local service]]. Stakeholders had expressed concern that local routes would become overcrowded after the S79 reduced its stops from 80 to 18; to address this, frequency and service span were increased on the S59 and S78 [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p007_c0008|increased frequency and service span]]. A free "3rd leg" transfer was also offered to customers who started on a local bus, transferred to the S79 SBS, and then connected to a bus or subway in Brooklyn [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p007_c0008|free 3rd leg transfer]]. Between 2011 and 2013, combined ridership on the S59, S78, and S79 grew by 6.6%, compared to only 1.2% growth on other Staten Island local routes [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p002_c0003|6.6% combined ridership increase]]. Customer satisfaction surveys conducted before (June 2012) and after (April 2013) SBS implementation showed that S78 riders reported [[metric:metric_s78-wait-time-pre|average wait times]] decreasing from 8.4 to 5.9 minutes [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p018_c0010|S78 wait time decrease]] and [[metric:metric_s78-travel-time-customer-pre|average travel times]] decreasing from 27.9 to 23.7 minutes [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p019_c0003|S78 travel time decrease]].
