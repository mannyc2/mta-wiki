[[route:route_q35-flatbush-cab1|Q35 (MTA Bus)]] is operated by MTA Bus Company (MTABC) and is classified as a secondary route on Flatbush Avenue in the corridor outreach packets [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p011_c0002|Secondary routes include B9, B67, B103, Q35, and BM2]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cab1_nov2022#p016_c0002|Secondary routes include B9, B67, B103, Q35, and BM2]]. A later service-revision staff summary identifies the same route as "Q35 (MTABC): Rockaway Park-Midwood" and proposes running time adjustments [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0004|Q35 (MTABC): Rockaway Park-Midwood • Running time adjustments]].

In a service enhancement proposal documented in meeting doc 160441, the Q35 was listed among 16 local bus routes/route combinations recommended for running time adjustments as part of a broader Spring 2025/Summer 2025 service revision [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p004_c0004|11. Q35 (MTABC): Rockaway Park-Midwood • Running time adjustments]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0007|Provide frequency increases and/or running time adjustments on eight express bus routes and 16 local bus routes/route combinations]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0013|Local bus routes: Summer 2025]]. The same proposal records a total annualized operating-cost increase for the full package and a separate MTA Bus Company annual-cost share; both are package-level costs rather than Q35-specific costs [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160441-total-annual-cost|full package annualized operating cost increase]] [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-160441-mtabus-annual-cost|MTA Bus Company annualized operating cost share]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0009|annualized increase in operating cost of approximately $8 million]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160441#p001_c0010|approximately $2.9 million per year is for MTA Bus Company bus routes]].

Flatbush Avenue, along which the Q35 operates as a secondary route, has been identified as a Vision Zero priority corridor where [[metric:metric_ksi-flatbush-ave-5yr_2|164 people were killed or seriously injured in the most recent 5-year period]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb14_jan2023#p006_c0002|Flatbush Ave is a Vision Zero priority corridor for safety improvements]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p006_c0002|164 people have been killed or seriously injured on Flatbush Ave in the last 5 years]]. The NYC DOT and MTA have been conducting community outreach on the corridor since a kickoff meeting on June 30, 2022, including presentations to Brooklyn Community Boards that list the Q35 among the secondary routes serving the corridor [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb9_sept2022#p011_c0002|Secondary routes include B9, B67, B103, Q35, and BM2]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_brooklyn_bus_priority_kick_off_june2022#p022_c0003|Hold Kickoff Meeting]].
