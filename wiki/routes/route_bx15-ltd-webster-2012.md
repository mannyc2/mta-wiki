## Scope Note

This page is best read as a Bx15/Bx15 Limited service-change page, not as the main Webster Avenue SBS page. In the Webster Avenue materials, the SBS conversion belongs to the Bx41 corridor; the Bx15 appears as a related Third Avenue service plan running parallel to Webster Avenue, with local and limited-stop variants reorganized around The Hub, Fordham Plaza, and Harlem/125th Street [2012_11_sbs_webster_cac4_summary#p001_c0008] [2013_01_webster_sbs_newsletter#p002_c0012].

## Webster And Third Avenue Service Plan

The late-2012 Webster Avenue service plan described two Bx15 patterns. The Bx15 Local was presented as replacing the Bx55 route, which had been weekday-only, with an all-times local route between The Hub and Fordham Plaza. The Bx15 LTD was described as making local stops between Harlem 125th Street and The Hub, then limited stops between The Hub and Fordham Plaza; the same slide also stated that there would be no Third Avenue bus service north of Fordham Plaza [2012_11_sbs_webster_cac4#p032_c0002].

A meeting summary gives the clearest prose version of the proposal: alongside the introduction of Bx41 SBS, NYCT would rename the Bx55 Limited as the Bx15 Limited, provide limited-stop service between The Hub and Fordham Plaza on Third Avenue, and provide local service between West Harlem and The Hub. The same summary says the Bx15 Local would provide all-stop service between The Hub and Fordham Plaza on Third Avenue [2012_11_sbs_webster_cac4_summary#p001_c0008].

The same plan repeated through early-2013 Webster community-board materials. Slides for CB1, CB3, CB4, CB5, CB6, and CB7 show the before/after framing as Bx15 Local and Bx15 LTD, with the Bx15 Local serving local stops between The Hub and Fordham Plaza and the Bx15 LTD serving local stops from Harlem 125th Street to The Hub plus limited stops from The Hub to Fordham Plaza [2013_02_sbs_webster_bx_cb1#p026_c0005] [2013_02_sbs_webster_bx_cb1#p029_c0002] [2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p040_c0002].

## Relationship To Bx41 SBS

The Bx15 evidence should stay separate from the Bx41 SBS route story. The 2014 Webster Avenue progress report says that before Bx41 SBS, the Bx41 operated on Webster Avenue while the Bx15 operated on 3rd Avenue, a parallel corridor at most 0.3 miles away; it also says the Bx55 operated partly on Webster Avenue and partly on 3rd Avenue [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p011_c0004].

The same progress report frames the Bx15 Limited as part of the service restructuring that accompanied Bx41 SBS launch: the duplicative northern-Webster portion of the Bx55 was eliminated, the remaining 3rd Avenue portion of the Bx55 was renamed the Bx15 Limited, and the interborough Bronx-to-Manhattan segment of the Bx15 was served by the Bx15 Limited [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p011_c0005].

The only extracted performance metric that directly ties Bx15/55 to the Webster package is corridor-wide rather than route-specific: ridership on the Third/Webster Avenue corridor between The Hub and Fordham Road, across the Bx41 and Bx15/55, increased by more than 7%, or about 3,400 daily riders. That should not be read as a Bx15-only ridership increase [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p020_c0007].

## 125th Street Role

The 125th Street materials are a separate evidence cluster. They list Bx15 with M60, M100, and M101 as bus routes on 125th Street, and a 2011 weekday-boardings table gives Bx15 8,838 boardings on the corridor out of 32,630 total for the four listed routes [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0001] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0004] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0007].

In the M60 SBS planning and implementation materials, Bx15 is treated as a local-stop companion route rather than the SBS route itself. March 2013 materials state that M60 SBS would make limited stops while Bx15, M100, and M101 would make local stops along 125th Street; later implementation materials continue to say that Bx15, M100, and M101 stop on every block or serve all local stops on 125th Street [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p013_c0005] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p003_c0005].

A later MTA Automated Camera Enforcement page also lists Bx15 among Bronx ACE routes and identifies Third Avenue as its major street. That is useful as a current route/street association, but it should not be used to infer details about the 2012-2014 Webster Avenue service change beyond the Bx15/Third Avenue linkage already present in the historical sources [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001].

## Caveats

The materialized identity is noisy: it merges Bx15 Local, Bx15 LTD, Third Avenue/Webster evidence, 125th Street/M60 evidence, and a later ACE record. The route page frontmatter currently says `display_name: "Bx15 Local"` while the payload also includes `route_name: "Bx15 Limited"`; the synthesis above treats both variants as a combined Bx15/Bx15 LTD service-change record. The strongest historical scope is the Webster/Third Avenue Bx15 Local/LTD restructuring tied to Bx41 SBS launch. The 125th Street evidence supports Bx15's local-stop role and 2011 boardings on 125th Street, but not a Bx15 SBS project.
