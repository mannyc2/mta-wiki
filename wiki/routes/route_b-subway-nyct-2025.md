The [[route:route_b-subway-nyct-2025|B Subway Line]] operates in Brooklyn as a subway route serving stations including Church Avenue and Kings Highway. Timetable updates that launched in early November 2025 improved the B's resilience to incidents, helped trains stay on time across the line, and significantly reduced customer wait times across November [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p008_c0010|B schedule improvement November 2025]]. The B line's Customer Journey Time Performance was reported at 80% [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p009_c0008|CJTP by line]]. On November 25, 2025, the MTA completed station and accessibility upgrades at the 118-year-old Church Avenue station, which serves the B and Q lines, including a new entrance on East 18th Street and two new elevators [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p024_c0003|Church Avenue completion]] [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p024_c0004|Church Avenue station B Q]]. The elevator project at Church Avenue was part of a broader program that saw 34 elevator replacements completed across the system in 2025 [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p026_c0007|34 elevator replacements]]. Along the Kings Highway corridor in Brooklyn, proposed curbside changes included relocating the B/Q taxi stand from Kings Highway to East 16th Street, alongside installing bus lanes along eight block faces and adding or lengthening four turn bays [[cite:brt_kings_highway_aug2018#p001_c0006|taxi stand relocation]] [[cite:brt_kings_highway_aug2018#p001_c0007|bus lanes turn bays taxi stand]].
