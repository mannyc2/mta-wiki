The [[route:route_b6-2015-sbk-corridor|B6]] is an east-west bus route in southern Brooklyn that appears in multiple planning records related to the B82 Select Bus Service corridor. It was listed among the bus routes considered in the 2015 South Brooklyn BRT kickoff [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B6 listed among South Brooklyn BRT corridor routes]]. On Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue, the B6 operates in both Local and Limited variants and is one of the routes serving approximately 35,000 daily bus riders in that study area, where bus speeds have been measured as low as 3 miles per hour on Bay Parkway [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002|B6 Local and Limited on Bay Parkway/Cropsey Ave with 35,000 daily riders]]. That corridor has also been designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, with 22 people killed or seriously injured from 2020 to 2024 and an additional 2 deaths in 2025 [[cite:bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p004_c0002|Vision Zero Priority Corridor safety data]].

During the B82 SBS planning process, a proposed re-route of the B6 westbound via Glenwood Road was described as a way to streamline both the B82 and B6 services [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb11_apr2017#p012_c0002|Proposed re-route westbound via Glenwood Rd streamlines B82 and B6]]. This proposed rerouting was depicted on multiple community board presentation maps showing the existing B6 route in the Canarsie-Rockaway Parkway area alongside the proposed B82 SBS alignment [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p017_c0001|Existing B6 route map in Canarsie-Rockaway Pkwy area]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p018_c0001|Projected B82 SBS alignment relative to existing B6]]. The B6 continues to operate as a Local and Limited route and remains linked to corridor-level bus priority and safety planning on Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue.
