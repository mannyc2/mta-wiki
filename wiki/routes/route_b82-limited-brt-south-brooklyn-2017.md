The [[route:route_b82-limited-brt-south-brooklyn-2017|B82 Limited]] was the limited-stop service variant on the B82 corridor in Southern Brooklyn, carrying approximately 10,000 daily Limited riders out of 32,000 total daily riders on B82 Limited and Local combined [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_southbrooklyn_bkbsc#p008_c0002|ridership: 32,000 daily riders – 10,000 Limited, 22,000 Local]]. The route provided an important east-west transit connection across Southern Brooklyn, connecting to D N F B Q L subway lines and 21 bus routes, including B44 Nostrand SBS and B46 Utica SBS, and served areas far from the subway [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p003_c0003|corridor description]].

The B82 Limited did not operate during mid-day or evening hours [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p012_c0003|existing Limited service does not operate mid-day or in the evenings]]. The route was planned for conversion to Select Bus Service beginning in 2017, with community board presentations and iterative street design updates across multiple Brooklyn community districts including CB 11, CB 14, CB 15, and CB 18 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p012_c0002|B82 Limited will be converted to B82 SBS]].

B82 SBS service launched on October 1, 2018, replacing the B82 Limited [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002|B82 SBS service launched on 10/01/18, replacing the B82 Limited]]. The SBS service began at Cropsey Ave & Bay 37 St and ran to Pennsylvania Ave in Starrett City, serving many of the same stops as the former B82 Limited, but operating for more hours of the day (4:45 AM to 11:00 PM) [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002|service now operates from 4:45 AM to 11:00 PM]]. All former Limited stops continued to be served by the B82 Local, except for three eastbound stops planned for removal due to close proximity to nearby stops [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002|all current stops are served by the B82 Local, except for three stops planned for removal]].

The B82 Limited operated along Kings Highway, which was identified as a key corridor for bus priority treatments including one-way curbside bus lanes, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety improvements such as reconfigured medians and new crosswalks [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb11_apr2017#p016_c0006|Kings Hwy one-way curbside bus lanes, Bay Pkwy to McDonald Ave]]. Business surveys conducted along Kings Highway found that 67% of businesses reported double parking interferes with deliveries, and 46% reported vehicles at the curb interfere with deliveries [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0007|67%]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0008|of businesses report double parking interferes with deliveries]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0009|46%]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0010|of businesses report vehicles at curb interfere with deliveries]].
