The [[route:route_m34a-sbs|M16 Bus Route on 34th Street]] originated as the M16 local bus before being renamed to M34A Select Bus Service on November 13, 2011, for clearer passenger communication [[cite:201110_brt_34th_open_house_slides#p028_c0002|34th Street SBS Fall 2011 Open Houses - Renaming]]. Service levels and route alignment remained the same after the rename [[cite:201110_brt_34th_open_house_slides#p028_c0002|Service levels and route unchanged]]. The route operates as a crosstown bus on 34th Street in Manhattan, running between the Port Authority Bus Terminal (Ninth Avenue/West 42nd Street) and Waterside Plaza (FDR Drive/25th Street), using 34th Street between Eighth/Ninth Avenues and the FDR Drive/Second Avenue area [[cite:34thstreet_aa_final#p018_c0009|M16 route description]].

The 34th Street corridor is [[metric:metric_corridor-length-2-miles|approximately 2 miles long]], making a [[metric:metric_corridor-round-trip-4-miles|4-mile round trip]] across Manhattan [[cite:201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p014_c0010|Corridor length description]]. Prior to the implementation of off-board fare collection in November 2011, bus speeds were approximately 4.3 miles per hour, meaning each bus required about one hour for the round trip [[cite:201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p007_c0004|Bus speed ~4.3 mph]]; the [[metric:metric_bus-speed-approx-43-mph|current bus speed ~4.3 mph]] metric captures this baseline condition.

The route was part of the broader 34th Street SBS project jointly delivered by MTA NYCT and NYC DOT [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p003_c0003|SBS joint program description]]. Improvements planned alongside the SBS launch included off-board fare collection and bus lane camera enforcement [[cite:20110314_34th_cac4_slides#p007_c0002|2011 planned improvements - off-board fare collection and camera enforcement]]. The 34th Street Busway, established later, further restricted general traffic between Third Avenue and Ninth Avenue during 6 AM to 10 PM daily, permitting only buses, trucks with six or more wheels, Access-A-Ride vans, and emergency vehicles to travel through [[cite:34th_st_busway#p002_c0008|Busway access restrictions]] [[cite:34th_st_busway#p002_c0007|Busway hours]]. The busway aims to increase bus speeds and reliability for over 28,000 daily bus riders on the M34/M34A SBS and express bus routes [[cite:34th_st_busway#p002_c0005|28,000+ daily bus riders on M34/M34A SBS]].
