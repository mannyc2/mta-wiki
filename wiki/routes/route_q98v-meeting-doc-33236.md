The [[route:route_q98v-meeting-doc-33236|Q98V]] was a temporary vaccination shuttle route operated by the MTA as part of a pilot program launched on March 1, 2021, to provide enhanced bus service from Edgemere in Queens to a New York State mass COVID-19 vaccination site at York College [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|Q98V launch description]]. Buses ran every 30 minutes from 6:30 a.m. to 9 p.m. from Edgemere to York College, with new bus stops added adjacent to the vaccination site [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p041_c0003|Q98V pilot program details]]. The route was described by Craig Cipriano, then President of MTA Bus Company and Senior Vice President of the NYCT Department of Buses, during the March 2021 Transit and Bus Committee meeting [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0002|Craig Cipriano title]]. This service supported vaccination sites in traditionally underserved communities of color that were hit hard by the pandemic, with each site vaccinating 3,000 New Yorkers per day [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|vaccination site capacity]]. A companion route, the B98V, operated on a similar model from Pink Houses in Brooklyn to the Medgar Evers College vaccination site [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|B98V companion route]]. The Q98V is classified as a vaccination shuttle route serving Queens borough [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|route borough]].
