The [[route:route_s92-tsp-2017|S92 along Victory Boulevard, Staten Island]] was identified as a planned Transit Signal Priority corridor in NYC DOT's July 2017 TSP report [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p011_c0003|Victory Boulevard extension listed as planned TSP corridor]]. TSP was first deployed in New York City in 2006 on Victory Boulevard using infrared line-of-sight technology, though that early-generation system is no longer operational [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p005_c0004|TSP first introduced on Victory Boulevard in 2006]]. By the end of 2017, NYC DOT reported that it was ready to implement a new generation of TSP on the Victory Boulevard corridor serving the S62 and S92, contingent on MTA's planned procurement of updated bus technology [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p011_c0005|DOT ready to implement TSP on S62/S92 by end of 2017]]. A July 2017 DOT press release similarly cited the S62 and S92 along Victory Boulevard as routes where TSP was being pursued [[cite:tsp_status_2017#p001_b0001|S62 and S92 mentioned in DOT press release]]. As of the 2017 report, this was among eleven planned corridors for TSP expansion in the city, alongside routes such as the Q44 SBS on Main Street, the M60 SBS along LaGuardia/125th Street, and others [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p011_c0003|full list of planned TSP corridors]]. The report noted that TSP requires the MTA to install GPS-based technology on buses, while NYC DOT performs traffic analysis to adjust signal timing without adding new hardware at intersections [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p005_c0005|TSP system uses NYCWiN and existing signal controllers]].
