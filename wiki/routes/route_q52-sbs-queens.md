## Route Role

The route evidence in this wiki usually presents Q52 SBS as one half of the paired Q52/Q53 Select Bus Service project on Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards. Planning materials describe the corridor as based on the existing Q52/53 LTD bus route, 14 miles from Woodside to the Rockaways, with 30,000+ daily bus riders and connections to 8 subway lines, more than 20 bus routes, and the LIRR. Within that paired corridor, the Q52 had its own identity: one planning source states that Q52 Limited operated between Elmhurst and Arverne, and design-workshop maps showed the Q52 route in light blue while the Q53 was shown in dark blue [brt_woodhaven_may2016#p005_c0002] [2015_03_woodhaven_sbs#p041_c0002] [2015_04_16_brt_woodhaven_dw1_discussionmaterials#p001_c0003].

At the design-workshop stage, Q52 SBS planning was still partly unresolved. The workshop map showed proposed SBS/local stop categories and noted that Broad Channel and Rockaway bus stops would be discussed later, while the Q52 extension was still under review. A Q52/Q53 SBS slide also said the SBS would use the viaduct over Atlantic Avenue, that SBS would stop at 91 Avenue instead of Atlantic Avenue, and that new stops were planned at 101 Avenue and Pitkin Avenue [2015_04_16_brt_woodhaven_dw1_discussionmaterials#p001_c0003] [2015_04_23_brt_woodhaven_dw2_presentation#p008_c0003] [2015_04_23_brt_woodhaven_dw2_presentation#p008_c0005].

Later material describes the implemented service: Q52/Q53 SBS began on November 12, 2017, from Woodside to the Rockaways, primarily along Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards. The same post-launch source says the Q52 was extended farther east in the Rockaways to Beach 54 Street in April 2017 in anticipation of the SBS launch, that stops in the Rockaways and Broad Channel were consolidated, and that the 101 Avenue, Pitkin Avenue, and 91 Avenue stop changes were implemented [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p002_c0002] [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p008_c0002].

A 2024 Woodhaven capital-project map is useful for reading the Q52 route scope after implementation: it states that Q52 SBS and Q53 SBS buses provide the same service and make the same stops between Broad Channel and Rego Park. That means many Woodhaven Boulevard facts are documented as Q52/Q53 trunk facts, while Q52-specific distinctions show up around the Rockaway extension and later redesign proposals [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p004_c0003] [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p008_c0002].

## SBS Treatments And Launch Context

The route's SBS launch was tied to both bus-service changes and street treatments. Planning material described the Q52/53 SBS launch as including improved fare payment and branded service. A later capital-project source says 6.85 miles of bus lanes were installed on Woodhaven Boulevard as part of the Q52/Q53 SBS launch in 2017, split into offset, median, and curbside alignments from Queens Boulevard to North Conduit Avenue. The same project package included bus boarding islands for 12 stops, bus stop amenities, pedestrian safety measures at 14 intersections, and new ADA-compliant pedestrian refuges at 11 intersections [brt_woodhaven_may2016#p012_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p005_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p006_c0003] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p008_c0003] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p009_c0003].

The route problem statement was largely about rider volume, reliability, and bus delay. Sources cite about 20,000 daily riders on the Q52/Q53 and 30,000+ riders on the full corridor; earlier planning material also described buses stuck in congestion, long boarding times at busy stops, and one-way travel times varying between 55 and 85 minutes. A 2015 simulation compared Q52/Q53 AM-peak travel times under several design concepts and projected reductions versus "No Build," but that should be read as a planning model rather than a measured post-launch outcome [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p008_c0002] [brt_woodhaven_cb9_sept2016#p005_c0002] [2015_03_woodhaven_sbs#p016_c0004].

## Later Extension Questions

The later Queens Bus Network Redesign addendum keeps the Q52 Rockaway extension question alive in a different planning context. Appendix D says an extension to Beach 50th Street was proposed, while longer extensions were deferred because of resource limitations, and it frames further Q52 extension as a possible future equity improvement for the Rockaways. That proposal should be kept distinct from the April 2017 extension to Beach 54 Street that preceded the SBS launch [queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p017_c0006] [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p008_c0002].

## Caveats

The repo evidence often merges Q52 SBS with Q53 SBS; Q52-only facts are much thinner than paired Q52/Q53 corridor facts. The canonical aliases overlap Q52 LTD, Q52 SBS, and Q52/Q53 SBS, so the synthesis above treats Q52 LTD as the pre-SBS predecessor or baseline, not as identical to the post-launch SBS route. Rockaway extension evidence also needs reconciliation: the 2018 source says the Beach 54 Street extension happened in April 2017, while the 2024 appendix discusses a proposed Beach 50th Street extension and longer deferred extensions.
