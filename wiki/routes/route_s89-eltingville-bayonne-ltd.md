The [[route:route_s89-eltingville-bayonne-ltd|S89 Eltingville-Bayonne LTD]] is a limited-stop bus route in Staten Island that ran at 16.1 miles per hour in December 2024, notably faster than the Staten Island borough-wide average of 13.9 miles per hour for the same period [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p009_c0003|page 9, S89 speed of 16.1 mph]][[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p008_c0007|page 8, Staten Island December 2024 average speed 13.9 mph]].

The S89's December 2024 speed was higher than its borough's January 2019 average of 14.3 miles per hour, though Staten Island overall saw essentially flat speeds between those dates [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p008_c0007|page 8, Staten Island January 2019 vs December 2024 speeds]]. At the citywide level, average peak-hour bus speeds across all 316 routes stood at 7.6 miles per hour in December 2024, little changed from 7.8 miles per hour in January 2019 [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p007_c0010|page 7, citywide peak-hour speeds unchanged]].

The S89 is classified as a Limited (limited-stop) service variant operating in Staten Island, according to data from the February 2025 IBO report "Speeding Up Slowly: A Review of Initiatives to Improve Bus Speeds in New York City" [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p009_c0003|page 9, route label and type in source text]][[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p001_c0001|page 1, report title]][[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p002_c0002|page 2, February 2025 publication date]].

The route's speed of 16.1 mph was the highest among individual routes called out in the IBO report's discussion of wide variation across bus routes; for comparison, the M50 crosstown in Manhattan ran at just 4.2 miles per hour in the same period [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p009_c0003|page 9, S89 vs M50 comparison]]. This performance gap reflects broader borough-level patterns: Staten Island had the highest average bus speeds citywide while Manhattan had the lowest [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p008_c0004|page 8, Staten Island and Manhattan borough speed description]].
