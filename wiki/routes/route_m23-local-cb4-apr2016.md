The [[route:route_m23-local-cb4-apr2016|M23]] is a Manhattan Crosstown local bus route on 23rd Street, identified as the Lower Midtown Priority Corridor in the BRT Phase II study and in the CB Five District Needs Statement [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p006_c0002|M23 corridor description]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p006_c0002|CB5 corridor description]]. It carries 15,000 daily passengers with high per-mile ridership, serves areas far from subway lines, and provides access to PATH and the 1, 6, C, E, F, M, N, and R subway lines [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p006_c0002|M23 corridor details]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p006_c0002|CB5 corridor details]]. The route connects to 14 local and Select Bus Service routes, and 28 express buses use the corridor for a portion of their trips [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p006_c0002|M23 connections]].

Travel-time data from April 2016 showed that the M23 bus is not moving 51% of the time, with only 40% of travel time spent in motion and 28% spent at bus stops [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p007_c0002|M23 travel-time pie chart]]. Speeds in both the AM and PM peak periods drop below 4 MPH between East 23rd Street and 1st Avenue [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p008_c0002|AM speed map]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p008_c0003|PM speed map]]. The roadway width on 23rd Street varies from 52 to 66 feet, with irregular lane widths encouraging frequent lane changing and buses at stops blocking through traffic [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p013_c0002|roadway conditions]]. 23rd Street is also a Vision Zero Priority Corridor under the Manhattan Action Plan, with four priority intersections [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p010_c0004|Vision Zero 23rd St]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p010_c0004|Vision Zero 23rd St]].

The proposed Select Bus Service treatment for the [[route:route_m23-local-cb4-apr2016|M23]] included westbound bus lanes from 1st Avenue to 8th Avenue and eastbound bus lanes from 9th Avenue to 2nd Avenue [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p015_c0002|westbound bus lanes]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p015_c0003|eastbound bus lanes]]. Additional proposed design elements included offset bus lane and right-turn bay treatments at Park Avenue [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p029_c0002|Park Ave design]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p030_c0002|offset bus lane details]] and a range of parking and loading-zone changes along the corridor [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p022_c0002|8th-7th Ave parking]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p025_c0002|7th-6th Ave parking]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p028_c0002|6th-Madison parking]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p031_c0002|Madison-Lexington parking]]. Two stop consolidations were proposed: merging the closely spaced westbound stops at 5th Avenue and Broadway, saving time for 4,500 daily riders, and eliminating the Lexington Avenue stop to save time for over 8,000 daily riders [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p032_c0002|5th-Broadway consolidation]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p033_c0002|Lexington-3rd consolidation]].

Community engagement for the M23 SBS proposal included feedback from over 500 customers online and at bus stops, and a survey of [[metric:metric_182-businesses-surveyed|182 businesses]] for their loading and delivery needs conducted by NYC DOT in coordination with the Flatiron Partnership [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p011_c0002|community engagement]]. The projected timeline in the April 2016 presentation called for presenting draft plans to Community Boards 4 and 6, refining the plan through June 2016, presenting a final plan, implementing the project between July and September 2016, and evaluating SBS performance in 2017 and beyond [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p035_c0002|April-June refine]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p035_c0004|June final plan]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p035_c0006|July-Sept implementation]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p035_c0008|2017 evaluation]].
