The [[route:route_b4-2015-sbk-corridor|B4]] is one of thirteen east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn that were enumerated at a June 2015 kick-off meeting for the South Brooklyn Crosstown Select Bus Service study [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B4 in east-west route list]]. The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) had identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need, and the 2015 kick-off continued that planning effort [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0009|priority service need]].

The east-west routes including the B4 had a [[metric:metric_south-brooklyn-east-west-ridership|South Brooklyn east-west corridor combined ridership]] of 178,000 riders per day [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0007|178,000 per day]]. Key issues raised at 2009 public workshops included long and slow bus trips, underserved areas, and a cross-Brooklyn bus trip taking up to two hours [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0011|key issues]]. At the time of the study, existing SBS routes citywide had demonstrated a [[metric:metric_sbs-faster-bus-15-23-pct|SBS bus service speed improvement]] of 15–23% [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0007|15-23%]], a [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-110-pct|SBS ridership increase]] of up to 110% [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0009|↑110%]], [[metric:metric_sbs-rider-satisfaction-95-pct|SBS rider satisfaction]] of 95% [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0013|95%]], and a [[metric:metric_sbs-crash-reduction-20-pct_3|SBS crash reduction]] of up to 20% [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0015|↓20%]].

The kick-off boards were produced by NYC DOT's Transit Development Group, with Matt Kroneberger as community outreach coordinator [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p009_c0003|contact information]]. Improvements considered for the corridor included dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, improved station amenities, real-time passenger information, and pedestrian safety improvements [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p003_c0001|ways to improve bus service]]. The B4 was one of multiple east-west routes under consideration for potential SBS treatment along the corridor rather than the sole focus of the study.
