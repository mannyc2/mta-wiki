The [[route:route_qm24-qbb-study|QM24 Express Bus Route]] is an MTA express bus route connecting the Queens neighborhoods of Middle Village, Ridgewood, and Glendale to Midtown/Downtown Manhattan via the Long Island Expressway [[cite:fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p006_c0002|Fresh Pond Rd map callout]]. In the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, the QM24 was recorded with an estimated daily ridership of 150 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|QBB study ridership table]]. The route appears on a map of bus stops at Third Avenue and 57th Street in Manhattan, where it shares a stop with the QM4, QM10, and QM12 express routes; a bus weave maneuver from the bus-only lane onto 57th Street is described at that location [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p010_c0005|Third Avenue and 57th Street map description]].

The QM24 operates along the Fresh Pond Road corridor in Queens, which was identified as a 2019 priority project by NYC DOT due to slow bus speeds of 3.0 mph during the PM peak, compared to the Queens average of 6.4 mph [[cite:fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p005_c0002|Fresh Pond Rd priority project rationale]]. Proposed treatments for the Fresh Pond Road corridor included a curbside southbound bus lane from Bleecker Street to Putnam Avenue, active during PM peak hours with parking and loading permitted at other times [[cite:fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p018_c0002|Proposed curbside bus lane description]], as well as a bus stop consolidation plan reducing five stop pairs to four, with one pair eliminated and one pair relocated for even spacing [[cite:fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0002|Bus stop consolidation proposal]] [[cite:fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0005|One stop pair eliminated]].

The QM24 was also noted in the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, a 2010-2011 NYC DOT study that examined congestion mitigation strategies for express and local buses crossing the Queensboro Bridge. Recommendations from that study that affected QM24 operations included signal and pedestrian interval changes at intersections such as Third Avenue and 57th Street and Queens Plaza North and Crescent Street, with short-term improvements implemented in fall 2011 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p004_c0011|Fall 2011 implementation]].
