The [[route:route_bee-line-61-fordham-rd-workshop|Bee-Line 61]] is one of the Bee-Line routes (60–62) that travel on Fordham Road between the Bronx and Westchester County [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|Bee-Line 60-62 buses on Fordham Rd]]. The June 2, 2021 public workshop was held by NYC DOT and MTA to gather community input on transportation issues along the corridor [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p001_c0002|Public Workshop – June 2, 2021]]. Fordham Road carried [[metric:metric_93700-daily-riders-fordham-rd|93,700 average daily bus riders on 9 routes]] at the time, including Bee-Line 61 alongside Bx9, Bx12 SBS/Local, Bx17, Bx22, Bx34, and Bee-Line 60 and 62 [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|93,700 average daily riders on 9 routes]]. These bus routes provide connections to A, B/D, 1, 2/5, 4, and 6 subway lines and the Metro-North Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|subway and Metro North connections]]. The surrounding community served by Fordham Road routes had [[metric:metric_910000-residents-fordham-area|910,000 residents]], with [[metric:metric_62pct-no-vehicle-access-fordham-area|62% of households lacking vehicle access]] and [[metric:metric_71pct-public-transit-walk-bike|71% commuting via transit, walking, or biking]] [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p024_c0003|demographics of Fordham Road area]]. The workshop divided the corridor into five sections from Major Deegan Expressway to Boston Road and solicited feedback on transit, pedestrian safety, traffic, and parking issues, with follow-up workshops planned for Fall 2021 [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p014_c0003|workshop discussion format]] [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p017_c0005|future workshops Fall 2021]].
