The [[route:route_q70-sbs|Q70 SBS LaGuardia Airport]], also called the LaGuardia Link Q70, provides Select Bus Service connections between LaGuardia Airport and regional transit hubs in Woodside and Jackson Heights, Queens, where passengers may connect to the E, F, M, R, and 7 subway lines, the Long Island Rail Road, or other bus routes [[cite:q70_fare_free_service_increases_2025#p001_c0005|describes the route and connections]].

Nearly 9,000 riders use the Q70-SBS daily, but bus speeds along Broadway are as slow as 2.7 mph and buses frequently encounter heavy congestion and double-parked vehicles [[cite:broadway_69_st_roosevelt_ave_may2026#p004_c0003|daily ridership and speed data]]. The route was labeled on a 2008-2013 SBS implementation map as "Woodside-Jackson Heights to LGA" with a note that it was not branded as SBS at that time [[cite:brt_routes_fullreport#p006_c0002|SBS route map label]][[cite:brt_routes_fullreport#p006_c0003|not branded as SBS note]].

Ridership on the Q70 has trended upward since 2022, exceeding pre-pandemic 2019 levels; 2024 ridership was approximately 25 percent higher than in 2019, leading to higher-than-guideline bus loads on certain trips [[cite:q70_fare_free_service_increases_2025#p001_c0007|ridership growth data]]. In response, MTA added 18 weekday trips to increase frequencies, with the net increase in operating costs funded by the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey [[cite:q70_fare_free_service_increases_2025#p001_c0009|trip additions]][[cite:q70_fare_free_service_increases_2025#p001_c0011|PANYNJ funding]].

NYC DOT has been coordinating with PANYNJ on Q70-SBS improvements as part of the LaGuardia Access project, and a proposed Broadway Bus Priority project would convert a channelized median and left-turn bays into a 24/7 eastbound center-running bus lane between the BQE and Roosevelt Avenue, retain one travel lane per direction, and ban left turns at 72nd Street [[cite:broadway_69_st_roosevelt_ave_may2026#p004_c0005|DOT-PANYNJ coordination]][[cite:broadway_69_st_roosevelt_ave_may2026#p007_c0002|proposed center-running bus lane]]. These improvements are intended to address the slow bus speeds and congestion that affect the route's roughly 9,000 daily riders [[cite:better_buses#p001_b0001|Better Buses reference to 9,000 riders]].
