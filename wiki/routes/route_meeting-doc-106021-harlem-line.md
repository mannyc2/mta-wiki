The [[route:route_meeting-doc-106021-harlem-line|Harlem Line]] is a Metro-North Railroad commuter rail line serving stations in Manhattan and the Bronx.[[cite:meeting_doc_138236#p001_c0002|Metro-North Railroad]] In 2022 the line recorded 97.3% on-time performance.[[cite:meeting_doc_111726#p027_c0002|2022 OTP data]] Total ridership was approximately 14.79 million in 2022 (final estimate) and rose to 18.32 million in 2023, a year-over-year increase of 22.6%.[[cite:meeting_doc_106021#p010_c0002|2022-2026 ridership table]][[cite:meeting_doc_138236#p002_c0006|2023 annual ridership table]] In 2024, Harlem Line ridership increased 11.6% compared to 2023.[[cite:meeting_doc_170821#p007_c0011|2024 ridership increase]]

A design-build contract valued at $118,000,000 ([[metric:metric_meeting-doc-155001-contract-amount|Design-Build Contract Amount]]) was awarded to MLJ Contracting Corp. for ADA station improvements at three Harlem Line stations in the Bronx: Botanical Garden, Williams Bridge, and Woodlawn.[[cite:meeting_doc_155001#p001_c0005|contract summary]][[cite:meeting_doc_124206#p003_c0008|three stations identified]] The contract term is 930 calendar days ([[metric:metric_meeting-doc-155001-contract-duration|Contract Duration]]) with [[metric:metric_meeting-doc-155001-stipend-total|$300,000 in total stipend payments]].[[cite:meeting_doc_155001#p001_c0005|contract term and stipend]] Work includes new elevators, staircases, ramps, platform replacements, and upgraded customer information systems at all three stations.[[cite:meeting_doc_155001#p001_c0009|scope of work]]

A timetable change affecting the Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines took effect on October 5, 2025.[[cite:meeting_doc_186726#p001_c0009|October 2025 schedule change]] A subsequent schedule change was adopted effective March 29, 2026.[[cite:meeting_doc_201556#p001_c0009|March 2026 schedule change]] During a potential NJ Transit strike in 2025, the Harlem and Hudson Lines were identified as alternatives for West of Hudson riders, with a bus connection to White Plains Station on the Harlem Line offering 28 morning rush trains and a 36-minute ride to Grand Central.[[cite:meeting_doc_171491#p003_c0001|Harlem Line as alternative for West of Hudson riders]][[cite:meeting_doc_171491#p005_c0005|bus to White Plains Station on Harlem Line]]
