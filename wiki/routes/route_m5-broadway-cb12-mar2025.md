The [[route:route_m5-broadway-cb12-mar2025|M5]] is one of five MTA bus routes (alongside Bx7, Bx20, M4, and M100) that operate on Broadway between 157th Street and 220th Street in Manhattan, a corridor where NYC DOT is studying bus and safety improvements [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p005_c0002|M5 operates on Broadway corridor]]. The corridor carries [[metric:metric_30k-daily-riders-5-routes-may2024|30,000 daily riders on 5 bus routes]] combined, with [[metric:metric_bus-speed-4to7-mph|Bus speeds 4-7 mph]] for most of the day [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p004_c0002|Corridor conditions and ridership]].

The slowest bus speeds on the corridor occur between 171st Street and 186th Street, where speeds drop as low as [[metric:metric_slowest-speed-4mph-171-186|Slowest speed 4 MPH between 171st and 186th]], and the same segment carries the highest ridership with [[metric:metric_15000-daily-riders-171-186|15,000 daily riders between 171 and 186]] [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p006_c0002|Bus speeds and ridership detail]]. Traffic speeds in this area are also slow, at [[metric:metric_traffic-speed-under-11mph|Traffic speeds under 11 mph]] with [[metric:metric_traffic-volume-300-600-veh|Traffic volumes 300-600 vehicles per direction]] during the 5-6 pm peak [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p007_c0004|Traffic conditions on corridor]].

Safety conditions on Broadway between 157th and 220th Streets are significant: there were [[metric:metric_safety-total-injuries-1053|1,053 total injuries on Broadway corridor]], including [[metric:metric_safety-pedestrian-injuries-239|239 pedestrian injuries]], [[metric:metric_safety-bicyclist-injuries-183|183 bicyclist injuries]], [[metric:metric_safety-motor-vehicle-occupant-injuries-583|583 motor vehicle occupant injuries]], [[metric:metric_safety-severe-injuries-83|83 severe injuries]], and [[metric:metric_safety-fatalities-3|3 fatalities]], resulting in [[metric:metric_safety-killed-severely-injured-86|86 killed or severely injured]] [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p009_c0002|Safety data table]].

The corridor is home to [[metric:metric_180k-residents-pia|180,000 residents near corridor]], of whom [[metric:metric_81pct-hispanic-latino-nonwhite|81% Hispanic, Latino or non-white]] and [[metric:metric_15pct-disability|15% have a disability]], with [[metric:metric_76pct-no-vehicle-access|76% of households no vehicle access]] [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p008_c0002|Demographic and equity context]]. It is designated a NYC Streets Plan Bus Priority corridor and a Tier 1 Priority Investment Area [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p004_c0002|NYC Streets Plan designation]]. NYC DOT presented existing conditions to CB 12 in Winter 2025 and plans to develop a design proposal with continued community feedback targeting bus lanes, upgraded bus stops, updated curb regulations, and signal timing improvements [[cite:broadway_157_st_220_st_cb12_mar2025#p017_c0002|Next steps and planned treatments]].
