The [[route:route_b38|Proposed B38 Local]] is a bus route on Dekalb and Lafayette Avenues in Brooklyn, serving as a primary connection between Downtown Brooklyn, Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Bed-Stuy, Bushwick, and Ridgewood [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|CB 2 Existing Conditions Meeting Dec 2024]]. The route carries over 18,000 average daily riders and offers both Local and Limited-stop service, with Limited service operating weekdays from 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|B38 service description]]. During peak hours, buses arrive every 5 to 10 minutes, and the route provides transfers to every subway line in Brooklyn [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|B38 peak headway and subway connections]]. Speeds on the Dekalb-Lafayette corridor are slow, averaging around 4 to 8 MPH [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|corridor speed noted]].

Under the MTA's Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan, the proposed B38 Local would be a 24-hour route with more direct routing, improved stop spacing, and improved frequency [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p040_c0003|Draft Plan route improvements table]]. Proposed frequencies are 3 minutes during the AM Peak, 4 minutes Midday, 4 minutes during the PM Peak, 5 minutes in the Early Evening, and 12 minutes in the Late Evening [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p043_c0003|Draft Plan frequency table]]. NYC DOT and MTA are pursuing bus and safety improvements along the Dekalb-Lafayette corridor, where the B38 is the primary bus route [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0002|DOT and MTA seeking B38 improvements]]. The B38 also operates from the Grand Av Bus Depot, which serves ten bus routes including the B38 [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p007_c0004|Grand Ave Bus Depot routes noted]].
