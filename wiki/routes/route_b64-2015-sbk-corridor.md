The [[route:route_b64-2015-sbk-corridor|B64]] is one of 14 east-west bus routes serving the South Brooklyn Crosstown corridor, listed alongside the B1, B2, B3, B4, B6, B7, B8, B9, B11, B36, B82, and B100 on NYC DOT kick-off presentation boards from June 2015 [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|east-west route list]]. These east-west routes carried a combined ridership of 178,000 per day ([[metric:metric_south-brooklyn-east-west-ridership|South Brooklyn east-west corridor combined ridership]]) [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0007|combined daily ridership]].

The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0009|BRT Phase II priority]]. Key issues raised at 2009 public workshops included long, slow bus trips and the fact that an east-west bus trip across south Brooklyn could take up to 2 hours [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0011|2009 workshop issues]].

The South Brooklyn Crosstown kick-off meeting presented SBS improvements that on existing routes had yielded bus speed improvements of 15-23% ([[metric:metric_sbs-faster-bus-15-23-pct|SBS bus service speed improvement]]), ridership increases averaging 110% ([[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-110-pct|SBS ridership increase]]), 95% rider satisfaction ([[metric:metric_sbs-rider-satisfaction-95-pct|SBS rider satisfaction]]), and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors ([[metric:metric_sbs-crash-reduction-20-pct_3|SBS crash reduction]]) [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0007|speed metric]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0009|ridership metric]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0013|satisfaction metric]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0015|crash metric]]. Proposed project strategies included dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, transit signal priority, improved station amenities, real-time passenger information, and pedestrian safety improvements [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p003_c0001|improvement strategies]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p004_c0007|real-time info]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p004_c0009|pedestrian safety]].

The source presentation boards were published by NYC DOT, with community outreach coordinated by Matt Kroneberger of the Transit Development Group [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p009_c0003|contact information]]. The [[route:route_b64-2015-sbk-corridor|B64]] is recorded as a true bus route in Brooklyn, identified from the east-west route list in the June 2015 kick-off materials [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B64 route listing]].
