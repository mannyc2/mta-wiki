The Bx25 is a new local bus route created as part of the [[metric:metric_bronx-redesign-net-annual-cost|Bronx Local Bus Redesign]] [[cite:meeting_doc_68306#p037_c0008|Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021]], one of two new local routes proposed alongside the M125. It provides service from northern Co-op City to Allerton Avenue and Bedford Park, and shares the Allerton Avenue corridor with the Bx26 while maintaining the corridor's same frequency levels in the December 2021 proposal [[cite:meeting_doc_68306#p037_c0008|Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021]].

The route was implemented on June 26, 2022 as part of the Bronx redesign rollout [[cite:meeting_doc_79331#p053_c0005|NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022]]. It connects northern Co-op City and the Bay Plaza shopping center with Allerton Avenue and Bedford Park Boulevard, and was intended to provide faster, more direct trips for Co-op City residents as well as residents from elsewhere in the Bronx who want to shop or work at Bay Plaza [[cite:meeting_doc_79331#p053_c0005|NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022]]. The New York City Department of Transportation installed new bus panels and destination signs at stops including a location in Co-op City in preparation for the route's launch [[cite:meeting_doc_79331#p053_c0005|NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022]].

The redesign that created the Bx25 was presented for Board approval at the December 15, 2021 MTA Board meeting [[cite:meeting_doc_68306#p002_c0002|MTA Board Meeting Dec 2021]]. As a whole, the redesign increased the net annual operating cost of Bronx bus service by approximately $2.7 million ([[metric:metric_bronx-redesign-net-annual-cost|net annual cost increase]]), comprising $1.4 million in NYCT operating costs and $738,000 in MTA Bus Company operating costs implemented in summer 2022, plus an additional $568,000 in NYCT annual operating costs deferred to 2023 [[cite:meeting_doc_68306#p034_c0012|Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021]]. One-time costs included $1.6 million for staff training on the new routes ([[metric:metric_bronx-redesign-one-time-training|one-time training cost]]) and $255,000 for bus stop changes [[cite:meeting_doc_68306#p034_c0012|Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021]].
