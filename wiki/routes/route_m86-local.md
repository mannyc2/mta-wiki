The [[route:route_m86-local|M86 Local]] was the local bus service operating along Manhattan's 86th Street corridor between West End Avenue on the Upper West Side and York Avenue on the Upper East Side, providing connections to the 1, 4, 5, 6, B, and C subway lines as well as twelve bus routes including the M15 SBS [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p003_c0007|M86 route description]]. Prior to its conversion to Select Bus Service, the M86 carried over 24,000 daily passengers and was the second-busiest crosstown bus route in Manhattan (after the M14A/D) with the highest per-mile ridership in the city [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p003_c0009|M86 ridership and ranking]].

The M86 SBS launched on July 13, 2015, replacing the local service on the same corridor [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p006_c0005|M86 SBS launch date]]. During the transition period from August 2014 through September 2015, the [[metric:metric_m86-ridership-growth-7pct|M86 SBS Ridership Growth - 7%]] showed that SBS ridership grew as local ridership declined, with the local route carrying approximately 26,028 riders in August 2014 and declining to 21,000 by August 2015 [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p006_c0012|M86 Local to SBS ridership transition table]].

Travel time data from February 2015 (pre-SBS) compared to February 2016 (post-SBS) showed that the M86 Local eastbound travel time was 23.7 minutes, compared to 21.1 minutes for the M86 SBS [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p008_c0008|travel time reduction summary]], representing an [[metric:metric_eastbound-travel-time-reduction-11pct|eastbound travel time reduction of 11%]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb7_oct2016#p009_c0003|M86 Local eastbound 23.7 minutes]]. Westbound, the M86 Local took 22.0 minutes versus 20.3 minutes for the SBS, an [[metric:metric_westbound-travel-time-reduction-8pct|8% reduction]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb7_oct2016#p009_c0005|M86 Local westbound 22.0 minutes]].

Customer satisfaction surveys conducted in July 2016 found that 96% of riders were satisfied with the M86 SBS overall, with a similar share satisfied with speed of the ride [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p007_c0004|M86 SBS customer satisfaction survey results]]. The Second Avenue Subway Q service began on December 31, 2016 with a stop at 86th Street, and MTA NYCT noted it would monitor the Q's effect on M86 SBS ridership going forward [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p007_c0002|Second Avenue Subway Q effect monitoring]].
