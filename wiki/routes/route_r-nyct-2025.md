The [[route:route_r-nyct-2025|R Subway Line]] is one of four subway lines that operate along the Queens Boulevard corridor, alongside the E, F, and M lines [[cite:meeting_doc_186641#p002_c0009|Four subway lines operate along the Queens Boulevard line]]. A major signal modernization project along Queens Boulevard that was wrapping up after four years as of November 2021 promised more frequent and reliable service for the R and other lines that run along that stretch [[cite:meeting_doc_65171#p005_c0006|modernize the subway's antiquated signal system along Queens Boulevard]]. The R serves 57 Street Station in Manhattan, which also serves the N, Q, and W trains [[cite:meeting_doc_42866#p004_c0002|57 Street / 7 Av ADA Subway Station entrance]].

The most significant recent service change affecting the R was the F/M route swap, which launched on December 8, 2025, as the first permanent change to the subway network since 2017 [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p004_c0007|implemented the first permanent change to the subway network since 2017]]. This re-routing, which swapped the routes the F and M take between Queens and Manhattan during weekdays, is expected to reduce delays, increase reliability, and ease crowding for the approximately 1.2 million daily riders using the E, F, M, and R lines [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p009_c0011|approximately 1.2 million riders using the E, F, M, and R lines daily]]. The swap affects eight stations in Queens and Manhattan [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p004_c0007|impacting eight stations in Queens and Manhattan]].
