[[route:route_b44-brt-phase2|B44 (Nostrand Avenue)]] is a Phase I Select Bus Service (SBS) route in Brooklyn, classified as serving an "Underserved Area" due to limited subway access on the Nostrand Avenue corridor [[cite:brt_intro_to_phase2#p032_c0003|Nostrand Avenue SBS Phase I route table]]. The existing B44 local bus service carried 44,000 trips per day at the time of the BRT Phase II report, and new SBS service was planned to begin in 2011 [[cite:brt_intro_to_phase2#p032_c0003|Nostrand Avenue SBS Phase I route table]].

The B44 was one of five Phase I BRT routes developed jointly by MTA New York City Transit and the New York City Department of Transportation, alongside Fordham Road/Bx12, First/Second Avenues, 34th Street, and Hylan Boulevard [[cite:brt_intro_to_phase2#p018_c0005|Phase I BRT route list]]. The route operates along Nostrand Avenue and adjacent corridors from Williamsburg to Sheepshead Bay, connecting residential neighborhoods, commercial districts, and institutions such as Brooklyn College and Kings County Medical Center [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0003|B44 corridor description]]. The B44 also runs along Rogers Avenue and Nostrand Avenue, both designated Vision Zero priority corridors due to high rates of pedestrian injuries and fatalities [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p008_c0003|Vision Zero priority corridor designation]].

Average bus speeds in Brooklyn were approximately 7 miles per hour at the time of the Phase II report, highlighting the need for bus priority improvements on high-ridership corridors like the B44 [[cite:brt_intro_to_phase2#p013_c0009|Average bus speeds by borough table]].
