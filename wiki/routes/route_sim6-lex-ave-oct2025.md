The [[route:route_sim6-lex-ave-oct2025|SIM6]] express bus is one of eight MTA bus routes traveling on Lexington Avenue between 60th Street and 52nd Street, a corridor where roughly 71,000 riders depend on these routes daily and where average bus speed is just 6 mph overall (5 mph during daytime hours) [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|8 MTA bus routes, 71,000 riders, 6 mph avg speed]]. On an average weekday close to 6,000 daily riders use this slow 8-block segment, with about [[metric:metric_1900-boarding-lex-ave-60th-52nd|1,900 riders boarding]] and [[metric:metric_1800-alighting-lex-ave-60th-52nd|1,800 riders alighting]] here [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p006_c0002|6,000 daily riders, 1,900 boarding, 1,800 alighting]]. In the past five years there have been 88 injuries in the project area, [[metric:metric_6-severe-injuries-lex-ave|6 of them severe]] [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|88 injuries, 6 severe]].

The existing curbside bus lane is frequently blocked by loading and passenger pick-up/drop-off activity [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|curbside bus lane often blocked]]. A NYC DOT proposal (presented to Community Board 6 on October 6, 2025, and to Community Board 8 on October 8, 2025) would upgrade the curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p001_c0002|CB6 presentation]] [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_cb8_oct2025#p001_c0002|CB8 presentation]], a design similar to the offset lane implemented on Lexington Avenue from 96th to 60th Street in 2019, where bus speeds have since increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35% [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p007_c0002|offset lane design and 2019 results]].

The proposed offset lane is intended to improve bus speed and reliability by letting buses travel without being blocked by standing vehicles, and would include a curbside lane for quick pick-up/drop-off [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p010_c0007|proposed lane configuration: curbside parking, two through lanes, offset bus lane, quick PUDO lane]]. Enforcement would use the MTA Automated Camera Enforcement Program [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p009_c0003|bus-mounted camera enforcement]]. Implementation is planned for Fall 2025 or Spring 2026, with curb regulation refinements to follow in 2026 [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p012_c0003|next steps and timeline]].
