The [[route:route_qm4-qbb-study|QM4 Express Bus Route]] is an express bus service between Manhattan and Queens documented in the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, a 2010-2011 NYC DOT study of local and express buses that use the Queensboro Bridge (QBB) [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0002|QBB Study Title]]. The QM4 had 350 daily riders at the time of the study [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|QM4 Daily Ridership 350]]. Over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delays their bus [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|Over 75% Report Congestion Delay]]. The [[metric:metric_express-bus-total-daily-ridership|Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB]] metric captures the combined express bus volume, which totaled about 7,920 daily riders across all express routes using the bridge [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|Express Bus Daily Ridership Table]]. The QM4 was one of several QM express routes sharing a Third Avenue and 57th Street bus stop in Manhattan, where eastbound buses performed a weave maneuver from bus-only lanes to turn onto 57th Street [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p010_c0005|Bus Weave at 3rd Ave & 57th St]]. As part of the study, a Leading Pedestrian Interval was identified as an opportunity for eastbound bus signal priority at this intersection under Recommendation #2 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p010_c0004|Leading Pedestrian Interval Opportunity]]. The short-term improvements from Recommendations #1-4 were presented to local Community Boards in spring 2011 and implemented in fall 2011 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p017_c0004|Recs 1-4 Implemented Fall 2011]]. Recommendation #5 concerning additional analysis of express bus priority options on the Thomson Avenue ramps was noted as pending further work by DOT [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p017_c0006|Rec 5 Additional Analysis Pending]].
