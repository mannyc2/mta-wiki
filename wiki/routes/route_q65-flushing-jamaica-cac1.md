The Q65 is a local bus route in Queens, operating along 164 Street as part of the Flushing-Jamaica corridor [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0004|Q65 on 164 St]]. The route is one of several bus routes serving this corridor, alongside the Q20A/B and Q44 on Main Street and the Q25 and Q34 on Kissena Boulevard and Parsons Boulevard [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0004|Flushing-Jamaica Corridor served by]]. The Flushing-Jamaica corridor was identified for potential bus rapid transit improvements in a 2006 NYC BRT study and the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II report, which characterized the corridor by "long and slow bus trips" [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p007_c0003|Flushing to Jamaica corridor identified]] [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p007_c0004|long and slow bus trips]]. The route [[route:route_q65-flushing-jamaica-cac1|Q65 on 164 St]] runs through an area with over 90,000 daily bus riders [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0005|Over 90,000 daily bus riders]]. In total, 13 MTA bus routes use Main Street near Roosevelt Avenue, generating more than 35,000 bus-to-subway transfers to the 7 train -- the largest volume of bus-to-subway transfers at any station in the city [[cite:brt_flushing_jamaica_oct2016#p003_c0002|13 MTA bus routes use Main Street]]. This route record was established from a 2014 Community Advisory Committee presentation and an October 2016 NYC DOT traffic management deck, reflecting the early planning phase for potential Select Bus Service improvements along the Flushing-Jamaica corridor.
