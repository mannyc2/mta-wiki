The Q102 is a local bus route providing service between Astoria, Queens and Roosevelt Island, Manhattan via Long Island City [[cite:meeting_doc_85816#p229_c0005|Q102 route description]]. The route operates on weekdays, Saturdays, and Sundays from 5am to 1am, with a one-way distance of approximately 6 miles [[cite:meeting_doc_85816#p230_c0009|Q102 operating details]]. Pre-COVID-19 pandemic ridership was approximately [[metric:metric_q102-weekday-ridership|Q102 Pre-COVID Weekday Ridership]] per weekday, [[metric:metric_q102-saturday-ridership|Q102 Pre-COVID Saturday Ridership]] per Saturday, and [[metric:metric_q102-sunday-ridership|Q102 Pre-COVID Sunday Ridership]] per Sunday [[cite:meeting_doc_85816#p230_c0009|Q102 pre-COVID ridership]]. As part of a project to add bus priority on 21st Street in Long Island City, the NYC Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) will prohibit left turns from northbound 21st Street onto 41st Avenue and install a left turn bay on northbound 21st Street at 40th Avenue, which will impact the travel path of the Roosevelt Island-bound Q102 and several of its stops [[cite:meeting_doc_85816#p229_c0005|21st Street bus priority project]]. The Q102 was also mentioned in an MTA Board Meeting presentation alongside the B62 extension to Astoria Houses and the Q103 reroute, both starting August 21 [[cite:meeting_doc_187251#p005_c0003|Queens bus changes at MTA Board Meeting]].
