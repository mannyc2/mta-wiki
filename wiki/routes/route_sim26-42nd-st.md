[[route:route_sim26-42nd-st|SIM26]] is a Staten Island Express Bus Route that operates along 42nd Street in Manhattan as one of six Staten Island express routes using the corridor [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p006_c0003|6 Staten Island Express Bus Routes use 42nd St]]. The 42nd Street Transit Improvements project, a Better Buses Action Plan priority identified in 2017 due to slow bus speeds, serves SIM26 along with M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, and SIM30 across a 2.0-mile corridor from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006|Routes served on 42nd St corridor]]. Across all routes using 42nd Street, about 16,000 daily bus passengers travel the corridor and buses carry 79% of people while using only 33% of street space [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p008_c0002|Buses carry 79% of people on 42nd St]].

Average bus speeds along the 42nd Street corridor were 4.2 mph in the AM peak and 2.9 mph in the PM peak [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006|Average bus speeds 4.2 mph AM / 2.9 mph PM]]. The M42 local route, which shares the corridor, shows that eastbound weekday running times range from 18.8 minutes overnight (12-6am) up to 34.4 minutes during the midday (10am-3pm), while westbound weekday running times reach 35.2 minutes in the PM peak (3-7pm) [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p010_c0003|M42 running time table]].

SIM26 also travels on Lexington Avenue between 60th Street and 52nd Street, one of eight MTA bus routes on that segment (alongside BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM11, SIM22, and SIM6) [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|8 MTA bus routes on Lexington Ave]]. On average, 71,000 riders depend on these Lexington Avenue routes daily, where the average bus speed is 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|Lexington Ave bus speeds and ridership]].

The proposed 42nd Street design changes include offset bus lanes (24/7), curb space reallocation for commercial loading during overnight hours, bus stop consolidation, and turn restrictions aimed at improving bus speeds and reliability [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p025_c0002|Offset bus lane and loading hour proposals]] [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p028_c0002|Street design changes]]. On Lexington Avenue, a similar 24/7 offset bus lane upgrade is planned between 60th and 52nd Streets, converting existing curbside bus lanes to improve speed and reliability, with implementation targeted for fall 2025 or spring 2026 [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_cb8_oct2025#p012_c0003|Lexington Ave offset bus lane next steps]].

The corridor serves as a major east-west commuter route connecting Port Authority Bus Terminal, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal, and bus delays on 42nd Street can affect reliability city-wide [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p007_c0003|Up to 210 buses per hour on 42nd St, delays impact city-wide reliability]].
