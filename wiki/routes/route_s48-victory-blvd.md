[[route:route_s48-victory-blvd|S48]] is a Staten Island local bus route operating along [[corridor:corridor_victory-boulevard|Victory Boulevard]], identified as one of five local routes (S46, S48, S61, S62, S66) that serve the corridor [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p008_c0004|Victory Boulevard local bus routes]]. The corridor carries approximately 25,789 average daily riders across 16 total bus routes that connect to the Staten Island Ferry, and S48 is among the local services whose riders may benefit from the bus priority and safety improvements NYC DOT and MTA have been studying since winter 2025 [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p004_c0003|Corridor ridership and route count]] [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p001_c0002|Winter 2025 outreach]].

Existing bus lane segments on Victory Boulevard have remained unpainted and peak-period-only since 1963, with red paint added only between Bay Street and St. Marks Place in 2020 [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p005_c0002|Existing bus lane history]]. NYC DOT is studying potential upgrades including red paint, extended hours, and extension of bus lanes south of Forest Avenue, all of which would affect the S48 and other local routes on the corridor [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p015_c0002|Proposed treatments]]. The S48 operates in a corridor where bus speeds drop below 8 mph in the morning and below 7 mph in the afternoon and evening based on October 2024 data [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p010_c0004|AM bus speeds below 8 mph]] [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p011_c0004|Afternoon bus speeds below 7 mph]].

More granular ridership data specific to the S48—such as stop-level boardings, headways, and time-of-day patterns—has not yet been extracted from available sources. The existing record for S48 identifies it as a true local route in Staten Island [[cite:victory_blvd_bay_st_wild_ave_winter2025#p008_c0004|S48 listed among local routes]].
