The [[route:route_q52-q53-sbs-proposed-2015-05|Q52/53 SBS (Proposed)]] route record captures a 2015-2016 proposal to introduce Select Bus Service on Woodhaven and Cross Bay Boulevards in Queens, based on the existing Q52/53 Limited bus route [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p008_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.8]]. The proposed corridor runs approximately 14 miles from Woodside to the Rockaways and serves about 30,000 daily bus riders [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p008_c0002|Source CB6 May 2015 p.8]]. Within a 15-minute walk of the corridor live roughly 400,000 residents, 43% of whose households do not own a car and 60% of whom commute by transit [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p008_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.8]].

Community outreach had identified several key issues: unreliable and slow bus service during rush hour, long and dangerous pedestrian crossings, and congestion that leads to difficult trips for buses and drivers [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p010_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.10]]. The corridor was designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, with over 3,000 injuries and 22 fatalities (17 pedestrian) recorded between 2009 and 2013 [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p012_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.12]]. On an average weekday over 3,100 Q52/53 trips started within Community Board 9, and one-way travel time could vary by up to 30 minutes, ranging between 55 and 85 minutes [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p011_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.11]]. Q53 Limited buses were stopped almost half of the time [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p011_c0002|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.11]].

DOT and MTA committed to delivering the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Blvd SBS and proposed a short-term implementation project in 2017 followed by a long-term project [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb9_jan2016#p014_c0002|Source CB9 Jan 2016 p.14]]. The short-term package included a Q52/53 SBS launch with improved fare payment and branded service, bus lanes and transit signal priority, roadway and pedestrian safety improvements, and median bus stops along 1.3 miles of corridor where service roads exist [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb9_jan2016#p015_c0002|Source CB9 Jan 2016 p.15]]. The preferred design concept for Woodhaven Boulevard was "Main Road Bus Lanes," placing SBS buses in median stations with local buses stopping at the curb, while on Cross Bay Boulevard (north of 165th Avenue) offset bus lanes were proposed with SBS buses stopping at bus bulbs [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p019_c0013|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.19]] [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p021_c0009|Source CB9 Jun 2015 p.21]]. Off-board fare collection with fare machines at every SBS stop was planned, allowing all-door boarding [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p034_c0002|Source CB6 May 2015 p.34]].

The proposal built on the track record of seven existing SBS citywide routes, which had achieved 15-23% speed increases, 10% ridership growth, over 20% crash reduction, and customer satisfaction above 95% [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p007_c0002|Source CB6 May 2015 p.7]]. A final decision on left-turn restrictions at Jamaica Avenue and elsewhere had not yet been made as of January 2016 [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb9_jan2016#p016_c0005|Source CB9 Jan 2016 p.16]].
