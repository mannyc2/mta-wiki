The [[route:route_proposed-bronx-sbs-lga-2012|Proposed Bronx SBS route to LGA]] was one of the proposed new routes developed through the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis, a study that began in May 2011 as a partnership among NYC DOT, MTA New York City Transit, MTA Bus Company, and the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0003|study partnership]]. The route is labeled as serving the Manhattan/Bronx area [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0008|Manhattan/Bronx area]]. The projected travel time savings for the proposed Bronx SBS route was [[metric:metric_proposed-bronx-sbs-40pct-faster-2012|Proposed Bronx SBS route 40% faster]] than current service [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0013|Proposed Bronx SBS route: 40% faster]].

Proposed improvement features for the Manhattan/Bronx SBS route included limited stops, dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, off-board fare payment, and luggage racks [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0016|Manhattan/Bronx improvement features]]. Improved reliability was identified as a key benefit [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0014|Improved reliability]]. Current service issues on the 125th Street corridor included long dwell times at bus stops, long signal delays, and traffic congestion [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0010|current service issues]].

The study found that non-airport bus riders on LGA-bound routes are an important market; [[metric:metric_m60-10000-daily-riders-125th-2012|nearly 10,000 daily M60 riders]] travel entirely on 125th Street, and bus improvements would benefit [[metric:metric_32000-daily-riders-all-routes-125th-2012|more than 32,000 daily riders of all routes]] on that corridor through reduced delays and improved reliability [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0008|non-airport bus rider market]]. Most airport employees who commute by transit live in Queens, Upper Manhattan, or the Bronx, and primarily ride the M60 and Q33 [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0011|employee commute patterns]][[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0012|employee transit modes]]. The next steps after October 2012 included selecting Locally Preferred Alignments, preparing a final Alternatives Analysis report, initiating detailed corridor analysis, and targeting implementation in 2013/2014 [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p002_c0016|next steps]].

This record represents a proposed route from the October 2012 LaGuardia Airport Access one-pager, labeled in the source under "Manhattan/Bronx" and grouped with the M60 SBS route [[cite:2012_10_lga_plan_one_pager#p001_c0008|Manhattan/Bronx label]]. It is not known from this source whether the route was implemented under this name or in this form.
