The [[route:route_q77-queens|Q77 bus route]] is proposed for an extension under the Queens Bus Network Redesign as a strategy to address gaps in service and improve connectivity in an area with moderate and significant equity concern identified as lacking bus service [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p014_c0005|Route Extension Strategy]]. The extension would bridge a service gap on Springfield Boulevard south of Conduit Avenue and on 147th Avenue west of Guy R. Brewer Boulevard [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p014_c0005|Route Extension Strategy]]. It would improve accessibility for residents in these underserved areas and connect to Guy R. Brewer Boulevard routes (Q111, Q113, and Q114), while also facilitating a connection to JFK Airport via the Q3 and Q6 routes [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p014_c0005|Route Extension Strategy]].

The Q77 extension evidence comes from the equity evaluation appendix to the Queens Bus Network Redesign Proposed Final Plan Addendum, which evaluates the redesign through an equity lens using three tiers of equity consideration [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p014_c0005|Route Extension Strategy]]. This batch has no separate source for existing Q77 alignments, service frequencies, or ridership data. The route's existing routing and operational characteristics remain unspecified in the cited records.
