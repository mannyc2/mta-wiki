The [[route:route_b3-2015-sbk-corridor|B3]] route was identified as one of 14 east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn that together carried approximately 178,000 riders per day as of the South Brooklyn Crosstown kick-off meeting in June 2015 [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0004|14 bus routes provide East-West service]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0007|178,000 per day]]. The route was listed alongside other east-west Brooklyn bus routes including the B1, B2, B4, B6, B7, B8, B9, B11, B36, B64, B82, and B100 [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B3 listed among east-west routes]]. The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) had previously identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor—which the B3 operates within—as a priority service need, and the 2009 public workshops raised concerns that bus trips across south Brooklyn could take up to 2 hours [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0009|Phase II Study priority]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0011|bus trips long and slow]]. Existing SBS metrics from that era reported that SBS routes achieved a 15-23% improvement in bus service speed ([[metric:metric_sbs-faster-bus-15-23-pct|SBS bus service speed improvement]]), an average ridership increase of 110% ([[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-110-pct|SBS ridership increase]]), 95% rider satisfaction ([[metric:metric_sbs-rider-satisfaction-95-pct|SBS rider satisfaction]]), and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors ([[metric:metric_sbs-crash-reduction-20-pct_3|SBS crash reduction]]) [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0007|15-23%]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0009|↑110%]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0013|95%]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0015|↓20%]]. The combined east-west route ridership is recorded as [[metric:metric_south-brooklyn-east-west-ridership|South Brooklyn east-west corridor combined ridership]] [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0004|14 bus routes provide East-West service]].
