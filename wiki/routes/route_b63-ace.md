[[route:route_b63-ace|B63]] is a local bus route operating between Bay Ridge and Cobble Hill in Brooklyn, with major streets including Atlantic Avenue and 5th Avenue [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|MTA Automated Camera Enforcement lists B63 as ACE route with major streets Atlantic Av / 5 Av]]. The route operates along Flatbush Avenue for parts of its alignment and is one of several routes—including B41, B67, B69, B45, and B103—that use the Flatbush Avenue corridor [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p011_c0004|Map of Flatbush Avenue corridor showing B63 and other bus routes]].

The Flatbush Avenue Bus Priority project, a joint MTA and NYC DOT initiative, will introduce center-running bus lanes on Flatbush Avenue, which require revisions to the B63 route [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p012_c0001|B63 Route Revision]]. The changes address operational conflicts: left-turning B63 buses at Atlantic Avenue would have blocked the more frequent B41 and B67 buses traveling straight in the bus lanes [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p012_c0003|Left-turning buses would have blocked B41 and B67]]. Under the proposed Cobble Hill-bound (northbound) revision, B63 buses will turn left from 5th Avenue onto Bergen Street, right onto 4th Avenue, and left onto Atlantic Avenue to continue along the regular route [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p012_c0003|Cobble Hill-bound detour route]]. For Bay Ridge-bound (southbound) buses, B63 will turn right onto 4th Avenue from Atlantic Avenue, left onto Dean Street, and right onto 5th Avenue [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p012_c0005|Bay Ridge-bound detour route]].

An alternative option considered swapping the B63 and B45 terminals to prevent the left-turn conflict on Flatbush Avenue [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_cb6_oct2025#p013_c0002|Swapping the B45 and B63 terminals prevents left turning B63 from blocking B41 on Flatbush Ave.]]. According to the September 2025 community presentation, the alternate-routing approach would have the northbound B63 stop at 5th Avenue and Bergen Street as the only missed stop [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_sept2025#p015_c0002|Alternate routing prevents left turning B63 from blocking B41 at Atlantic Ave]].

The Flatbush Avenue Bus Priority project is the result of four years of outreach, including over 45 community meetings [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p005_c0004|Project Design is the Result of Four Years of Outreach and Support]]. Concrete and signal construction is projected to begin in spring 2026, with bus lanes opening for operation in late fall 2026, followed by a monitoring and adjustment period through fall 2027 [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p017_c0003|Spring 2026: Begin concrete and signal construction]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p017_c0007|Late Fall 2026: Bus lanes open for operation]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_bus_priority_mtp_briefing_apr2026#p017_c0009|Fall 2026 – Fall 2027: Monitor and adjust as necessary]].
