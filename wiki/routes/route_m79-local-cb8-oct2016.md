The M79 Crosstown Local Bus [[route:route_m79-local-cb8-oct2016|M79 Crosstown Local Bus]] runs east along 79th Street from Broadway to East End Avenue in Manhattan [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p013_c0002|M79 bus route]]. It carries over 18,000 passengers per day over about 2 miles [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p012_c0003|M79 description]], recorded as [[metric:metric_m79-daily-ridership_3|M79 daily passengers]] and [[metric:metric_m79-corridor-length_3|M79 corridor length]]. The route connects to 1, 6, B, and C subway trains and to 16 bus routes including the M15 SBS [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p012_c0003|M79 description]].

The M79 experiences heavy congestion: only 43% of trip time is in motion [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p016_c0002|M79 trip breakdown]], with 26% at bus stop, 21% in delays, and 9% crawling below 2.5 mph, totaling over 50% of the trip spent not moving well [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p016_c0003|M79 performance claim]]. These are tracked as [[metric:metric_m79-pct-in-motion|M79 percent of trip in motion]], [[metric:metric_m79-pct-at-bus-stop|M79 percent of trip at bus stop]], [[metric:metric_m79-pct-delays|M79 percent of trip in delays]], and [[metric:metric_m79-pct-crawl|M79 percent of trip crawling]].

The M79 corridor was identified as a potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 "Bus Rapid Transit: Phase II Study" [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p012_c0002|M79 corridor SBS study]]. The M79 and M86 are parallel crosstown routes with similar street designs, and the M79 analysis investigated treatments used for the M86 SBS [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p011_c0002|parallel routes]]. The proposed timeline included finalizing traffic analysis and beginning off-board fare equipment construction in Fall 2016, presenting a draft plan to community boards in Winter 2017, and launching SBS in Spring 2017 [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0002|Fall 2016]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0003|Fall 2016 milestones]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0004|Winter 2017]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0005|Winter 2017 milestones]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0006|Spring 2017]] [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p018_c0007|Spring 2017 launch]]. One proposed change was discontinuing the eastbound stop at 81st Street and Amsterdam Avenue due to low ridership and narrow sidewalks [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb8_oct2016#p017_c0002|stop change proposal]].
