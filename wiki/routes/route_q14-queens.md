The [[route:route_q14-queens|Q14 bus route]] is a proposed local bus route in Queens introduced as part of the Queens Bus Network Redesign New Draft Plan released in March 2022 [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p003_c0010|Released New Draft Plan (Mar 2022)]]. The Q14 was designed to improve service reliability by replacing the northern loop segment of the existing Q38, which operates as a loop with start and end points approximately a third of a mile apart in Rego Park and Corona—neighborhoods within Equity Tier 1 and 2 areas [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Split Routes example]]. In addition to replacing the northern part of the Q38, the proposed Q14 would also serve the northern segment of the existing Q23 in Corona and East Elmhurst and the existing Q39 on Forest Avenue, thereby establishing a new connection between East Elmhurst and Ridgewood and expanding access for residents within several Tier 1 equity block groups [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Split Routes example]]. A map of the Q23 route in the New Draft Plan similarly indicates that a discontinued segment of that route would be served by the proposed Q14 [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p007_c0002|Q23 case study map]]. The Q14's creation through a route split reflects a broader redesign strategy of shortening and simplifying routes to enhance reliability, particularly in areas with higher equity concern [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p013_c0004|Split Routes example]]. The Queens bus network serves [[metric:metric_115-bus-routes-queens|115 bus routes in Queens]] carrying [[metric:metric_800k-riders-queens-weekday|800,000 weekday riders in Queens]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p002_c0003|115 routes 800k riders]], and local route stop spacing under the redesign targets [[metric:metric_local-stop-spacing-800-1200|Local route stop spacing 800-1,200 feet]] [[cite:meeting_doc_87961#p010_c0005|Bus stop spacing table]]. The proposed plan would increase the population served by the all-day frequent network by [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-total-increase-pop|all-day frequent network population increase]] ([[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-total-increase-pct|All-Day Frequent Network Total Percent Increase]] boroughwide), with Tier 1 areas seeing a [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-tier1-increase-pop|Tier 1 population increase]] ([[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-tier1-increase-pct|All-Day Frequent Network Tier 1 Percent Increase]]) increase [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p007_c0007|Equity tier population table]].
