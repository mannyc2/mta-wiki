## B82 Local In The Southern Brooklyn Corridor

B82 Local is the retained local service for the Southern Brooklyn B82 corridor, rather than the service variant converted into SBS. Early corridor materials traced the South Brooklyn east-west corridor to the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study and split B82 ridership into 32,000 daily riders, with 10,000 on Limited service and 22,000 on Local service. [2015_10_14_brt_southbrooklyn_bkbsc#p006_c0002] [2015_10_14_brt_southbrooklyn_bkbsc#p008_c0002] A 2016 corridor overview described the B82 corridor as 10 miles long, a key east-west connector along Kings Highway and Flatlands Avenue, and connected to six subway lines and the three highest-ridership bus routes in Brooklyn. [2016_01_20_brt_southbrooklyn_cb18#p009_c0002]

## Relationship To SBS

The later SBS plan is best read as a change to the Limited pattern alongside continued B82 Local coverage. September 2017 materials stated that B82 SBS would end at Cropsey/Bay 37 Street while the Local would continue serving the existing route to Coney Island; the same slide identified proposed reroutes via Glenwood Road and Avenue K. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0003] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0004] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0005] After launch, the October 2018 update said B82 SBS launched on 10/01/18 replacing the B82 Limited, began at Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street, ran to Pennsylvania Avenue in Starrett City, and that all current stops were served by the B82 Local except three planned eastbound Kings Highway removals. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002]

## Corridor Problems

Planning materials describe a slow and reliability-constrained corridor. The 2015 source gave B82 average route speed as 7.9 mph and average travel time as 88 minutes, and broke delay time into 48% in motion, 29% signal delay, 21% dwell time, and 2% other delays. [2015_10_14_brt_southbrooklyn_bkbsc#p008_c0002] [2015_10_14_brt_southbrooklyn_bkbsc#p008_c0003] September 2017 materials called out slow westbound routing between Flatlands Avenue and Kings Highway, traffic/parking/truck loading on Kings Highway, Rockaway Parkway station routing, and Flatlands/Pennsylvania Avenue stop access as contributors to slow speeds on the 10.2-mile westbound SBS route. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p009_c0002] The same packet summarized over half of a trip as delayed, crawling, or at a stop, with long on-board fare-payment lines and stop amenity/accessibility gaps. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p010_c0003] Safety issues included long crossings, lack of pedestrian refuges, limited visibility, complex intersections, difficult turns, narrow median bus stops, and top crash locations at Kings Highway and Flatlands Avenue intersections. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p011_c0002]

## Related Bus-Priority And Schedule Evidence

DOT's July 2017 TSP status report listed "B82 in Southern Brooklyn" among additional routes in development for Transit Signal Priority, a signal technology intended to change traffic lights in response to buses. [tsp_status_2017#p001_b0001] A schedule-change source proposed weekday B82 schedule modifications: it said frequency would decrease and running time would increase on the B82, with the attachment showing PM peak headway changing from 10 to 12 minutes and evening headway from 10 to 15 minutes. [meeting_doc_133291#p002_c0010] [meeting_doc_133291#p004_c0003] In 2025 Bay Parkway/Cropsey Avenue materials, B82 Local and Select Bus Service were still listed together in the study area, alongside B6 Local/Limited and X28/X38, within a corridor summary citing 35,000 daily bus riders, F/N/D subway connections, bus speeds as low as 3 mph on Bay Parkway, and Vision Zero injury history. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002] Those 2025 materials frame proposed bus lanes as still subject to traffic analysis and community review. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb11_jun2025#p016_c0002]
