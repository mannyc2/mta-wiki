The [[route:route_q34-queens|Q34 bus route]] runs between Jamaica and Whitestone in Queens, operating along the Kissena Boulevard and Parsons Boulevard corridor [[cite:2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p014_c0005|Q34: Jamaica to Whitestone]] [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0004|Q25, Q34 on Kissena Blvd/Parsons Blvd]] [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p006_c0010|Q34: Jamaica to Whitestone]]. It is a local (and limited-stop) route in the Queens bus network [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p011_c0004|Q34 absorbed by Q25 proposal]].

As of 2014, the Q34 carried approximately 7,054 weekday riders with a peak frequency of 10 minutes [[cite:2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p016_c0005|Route Weekday Ridership Peak Frequency Q34 7,054 10 minutes]]. The Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd corridor served by the Q34 and Q25 together carried approximately 26,000 daily riders [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p006_c0008|26,000 daily riders on]] [[metric:metric_daily-riders-parsons-kissena|26,000 daily riders on Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd]].

The Q34 was studied as part of the Flushing-Jamaica corridor BRT and Select Bus Service planning process led by NYC DOT and MTA NYCT [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010|Q25 LTD and Q34 on Kissena/Parsons Boulevards]] [[cite:brt_flushing_jamaica_oct2016#p003_c0002|Map of MTA Bus Routes]]. Under the Queens Bus Network Redesign proposed final plan, the Q34 would be eliminated as a standalone route: the proposed Q25 would absorb existing Q34 trips along Kissena and Parsons Boulevards, while the remaining portion of the Q34 not included in the Q25 proposal would be served by the new Q61, which would introduce weekend service to Linden Hill [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p011_c0004|Q34 absorbed by Q25 proposal]].

The Q34 was one of several overlapping routes on the Kissena/Parsons corridor that the Redesign identified as providing redundant service; the proposed simplification aims to improve reliability on a corridor that serves a high number of residents living below the poverty line [[cite:queens_addendum_equity_evaluation_appendix_d#p011_c0004|one of the Queens routes that serves the highest number of residents living below the poverty line]].
