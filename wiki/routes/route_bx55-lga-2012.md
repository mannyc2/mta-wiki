[[route:route_bx55-lga-2012|Bx55]] was identified by workshop participants as a potential route for Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) service to LaGuardia Airport during the second public meeting of the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis (LGA AA) on November 2, 2011 [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p001_c0005|Public Meeting #2 held November 2, 2011 with 23 participants]]. The study had already selected BRT as the locally preferred mode for improving airport access [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p001_c0013|BRT selected as locally preferred mode]]. Participants proposed a route described as "Bx41/Bx55 from northern Bronx to LGA via Robert F. Kennedy Bridge" alongside many other potential corridors [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p003_c0006|Participant-identified route: Bx41/Bx55 from northern Bronx to LGA via RFK Bridge]].

The workshop was held in a small-group format with [[metric:metric_workshop-23-participants|23 workshop participants]] and [[metric:metric_survey-18-returned|18 surveys returned]] afterward [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p006_c0008|18 surveys returned at workshop completion]]. In rider-preference voting exercises at the same event, participants most strongly favored a 60-minute, one-seat ride and express bus service (16 votes each), a 35-minute ride in a dedicated lane (16 votes), and highway routing (15 votes) [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p005_c0006|Rider preference voting results table]].

The route record for [[route:route_bx55-lga-2012|Bx55]] captures only this single source-documented mention in the LGA AA study; the route's operational history and current service status are not covered by this evidence [[cite:2012_04_20_brt_lga_workshop_summary#p003_c0006|Bx55 route mention in LGA AA workshop summary]].
