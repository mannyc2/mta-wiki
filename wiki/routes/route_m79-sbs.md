The [[route:route_m79-sbs|M79 Select Bus Service]] runs crosstown on 79th Street in Manhattan between Riverside Drive and FDR Drive, covering just over two miles [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0003|route description]]. The M79 bus corridor was first identified as a potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study [[cite:brt_m86_m79_cb7_oct2016#p012_c0002|phase II study]], and planning accelerated in 2016 following community board presentations and stakeholder outreach [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p005_c0003|community board consultations]]. The route was modeled on the success of the earlier M86 Select Bus Service [[cite:m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0010|modeled after M86]].

M79 SBS launched on May 21, 2017, replacing M79 Local service [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0003|launch date]]. The redesign brought dedicated bus lanes, signal timing improvements, off-board fare payment, and real-time passenger information to approximately 12,500 daily riders [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0003|improvements and ridership]]. The route provides a crucial connection to the 1, 6, B, and C subway lines and 16 bus routes, including the M15 SBS [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0003|connections]].

Within two years of launch, the route recorded an average 8% travel time reduction compared to pre-SBS M79 Local service, with ridership up 9% in the first year alone against a Manhattan-wide average increase of 0.5% [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0005|performance results]]. Car travel time on the corridor decreased by 4% on average across all time periods [[metric:metric_m79-sbs-car-tt-4pct|Car Travel Time Decrease Entire Route]], and 96% of M79 SBS riders reported satisfaction with the service [[cite:brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0005|satisfaction rating]]. Capital street-design treatments added later included curb extensions, bus pads, and pedestrian islands [[cite:brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p014_c0002|capital improvements]]. The route continues to be listed under MTA's automated camera enforcement program [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE listing]].
