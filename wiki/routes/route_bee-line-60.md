The Bee-Line 60 is a Westchester County Bee-Line bus route serving the Fordham Road corridor in the Bronx, connecting the borough to destinations in Westchester County [[cite:better_buses#p001_b0001|Better Buses Fordham Road project page]]. On Fordham Road between the Major Deegan Expressway and Boston Road, the [[route:route_bee-line-60|Bee-Line 60]] is one of nine bus routes that also include the Bx9, Bx12 SBS/Local, Bx17, Bx22, Bx34, and Bee-Line 61-62 [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes]]. These nine routes together carry 93,700 average daily riders, of which 40,300 use the Bx12 SBS and Local [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes]]. The Fordham Road corridor provides connections to the A, B/D, 1, 2/5, 4, and 6 subway lines and the Metro North Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p028_c0002|Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes]]. The corridor is also a Vision Zero priority corridor, with four priority intersections at Sedgwick Avenue, University Avenue, Grand Avenue, and Jerome Avenue [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p027_c0002|Fordham Road Vision Zero designation]]. The surrounding neighborhoods served by Fordham Road routes are characterized by high transit dependency: 62% of households have no access to a private vehicle, and [[metric:metric_71pct-public-transit-walk-bike|71% commute to work via public transit, walking, or biking]] [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p024_c0003|Fordham Road demographic data]]. In June 2021, NYC DOT held a public workshop on potential transit improvements along Fordham Road, reviewing the corridor in five sections from the Major Deegan Expressway to Boston Road [[cite:fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_jun2021#p001_c0002|Public Workshop June 2, 2021]].
