The Huntington Branch is a commuter rail branch operated by the MTA Long Island Rail Road [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|Performance Summary table listing Huntington Branch]]. In April 2023, the branch experienced temporary service adjustments when two of four main tracks were taken out of service between Jamaica and Harold Interlocking for approximately 55 hours during the Van Wyck Bridge Replacement project, during which some Huntington Branch trains operated on adjusted schedules [[cite:meeting_doc_107636#p001_c0007|Van Wyck Bridge Replacement service adjustments]].

Per the LIRR 2023 Annual Operating Report, the Huntington Branch recorded an overall on-time performance (within 5 minutes 59 seconds of scheduled arrival at final destination) of 92.5% for the year through December, compared to 90.0% in the same period of 2022, against an annual goal of 93.5% [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|Huntington Branch OTP row in Performance Summary table]]. By time period, 2023 OTP was 90.6% during AM Peak, 89.1% during PM Peak, 89.9% for Total Peak, 90.1% during Off Peak Weekday, and 89.9% on weekends [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|Huntington Branch OTP by period in Performance Summary table]].

The branch's 2023 OTP improvement of 2.5 percentage points over 2022 brought it closer to the annual goal, but it remained below target across all measured periods. The Huntington Branch is not separately listed in the report's elevator or escalator availability tables, which cover only a subset of LIRR branches [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p007_c0004|Elevator Availability table]] [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p007_c0005|Escalator Availability table]].
