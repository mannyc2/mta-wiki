The [[route:route_irt-flushing-line-7-208006|IRT Flushing Line (7)]] is an A Division subway route serving Manhattan and Queens across an east-west corridor [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p044_c0007|Mets-Willets Point Station on IRT Flushing Line]] [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p001_c0002|4 and 7 Line Schedule Changes]]. Two source documents at the MTA Capital Program Committee level document distinct activities on the 7 line.

At Mets-Willets Point Station on the 7 line, the MTA Capital Program Committee on May 20, 2026 considered a Staff Summary requesting approval of a $1,810,621 Design Agreement with Queens Future, LLC for State of Good Repair Inspection and Design Services [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p044_c0007|Staff Summary location and amount]]. Queens Future, a joint venture of Hard Rock Casino and Steve Cohen awarded a downstate gaming license in December 2025, has committed to making the station fully ADA-accessible and performing a major mezzanine renovation, platform/canopy painting, and windscreen and lighting replacement as part of the development [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p044_c0007|Queens Future commitments to station improvements]]. MTA had previously retained STV, Inc. in 2023 to produce a state of good repair inspection report for the station [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p044_c0007|2023 STV inspection]].

The second source document, a July 8, 2022 NYCT Staff Summary, describes weekday schedule adjustments on the 4 and 7 lines implemented in December 2022 to match post-COVID shifts in peak-period rider demand [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p001_c0002|Schedule Changes heading]] [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p002_c0007|Purpose: match ridership demand shifts]]. There was no net change in total daily trips on the 7 line, but one fewer crew was required for 7 service [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p003_c0004|No net trip change, one fewer crew]]. The frequency adjustments included moving one southbound 7 local and one southbound 7 express trip from the 9-10 a.m. hour to earlier morning slots as two local trips, and shifting one northbound 7 local and one northbound 7 express from the 5-7 p.m. hours to the 3-5 p.m. hours [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p003_c0002|7 line trip shifts]]. The attachment table shows specific frequency changes at 61 St-Woodside (southbound) and Queensboro Plaza (northbound) [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p004_c0003|Frequency table with 7 line data]]. The changes were estimated to save approximately $0.3 million annually [[cite:meeting_doc_91606#p001_c0009|Annual savings $0.3 million]].
