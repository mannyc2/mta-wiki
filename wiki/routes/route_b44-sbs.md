## Route Role

B44 SBS is the Select Bus Service version of the B44's Nostrand/Rogers corridor service. The 2016 progress report describes the B44 as one of Brooklyn's and New York City's highest-ridership bus routes, running along Nostrand Avenue and adjacent corridors from Williamsburg to Emmons Avenue in Sheepshead Bay, while serving residential neighborhoods, retail districts, Brooklyn College, Kings County Medical Center, subway connections, and other major destinations. A city SBS route index describes Nostrand Avenue-Rogers Avenue Select Bus Service as a 9.3-mile north-south route in Brooklyn [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0003] [brt_route_index#p001_b0001].

The route also sits inside the early citywide SBS/BRT rollout. The 2016 report says the B44 was the fifth and final Phase I SBS route to be implemented, the sixth SBS route overall, and one of nine SBS routes in the city at the time of that report [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p006_c0006].

## Service Pattern

The core service change was consistent across planning and launch materials: B44 Limited became B44 SBS, B44 Local continued, and the SBS fare matched the local bus fare. Northbound SBS shifted from New York Avenue to Rogers Avenue, while local service continued to serve New York Avenue; launch materials also state that B44 Nostrand/Rogers Avenues Select Bus Service began on November 17, 2013 [201011_nostrand_handout#p001_c0006] [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p001_c0001] [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p002_c0003] [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p002_c0020].

The launch map presents the B44 Select Bus Service route as primarily north-south along Nostrand Avenue, with listed stops and connections including Williamsburg Bridge Plaza/Bus Transfer Hub, Fulton Street, Church Avenue, Flatbush Avenue/Brooklyn College, Kings Highway, Avenue U, Avenue X, Shore Parkway, and Emmons Avenue/Knapp Street. Earlier CB 8 planning material described the Nostrand/Rogers corridor as a 9.3-mile stretch of the B44 route from the Williamsburg Bridge area to Sheepshead Bay, with SBS shown in bus lanes and mixed traffic [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p001_c0003] [201110_nostrand_cb8_slides#p002_c0007].

## What Changed

Before SBS, the progress report says B44 Limited service was often slow, unreliable, and crowded because of traffic congestion on Nostrand and New York Avenues, high passenger boardings, and double parking. NYCT and NYC DOT's package included off-board fare collection, bus lanes, the northbound reroute to Rogers Avenue, stop consolidation, traffic improvements, larger buses, bus bulbs, new shelters, and greenery [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0004].

The Rogers Avenue reroute was not just a naming change. The progress report says moving northbound B44 SBS from New York Avenue to Rogers Avenue shortened the northbound route from 9.9 to 9.5 miles, put the SBS on a wider street where bus lanes and bus bulbs could be installed, and kept B44 Local service on New York Avenue for local access. North of Fulton Street, the report says local and SBS routes travel together on Bedford Avenue [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0004] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0005].

The route's rider-facing changes included 62-foot, three-door articulated buses replacing 40-foot, two-door B44 Limited buses for SBS, which the progress report identifies as the first use of those buses in Brooklyn. Fifteen bus bulbs were built, eight on Nostrand Avenue and seven on Rogers and Bedford Avenues, and Bus Time information became available on B44 SBS in February 2014 [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0006] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p019_c0006] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p019_c0007] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p019_c0009].

## Reported Outcomes

The 2016 progress report measured faster end-to-end trips after SBS implementation: northbound travel times were 31% faster in the AM and 20% faster in the PM, while southbound times were 19% faster in the AM and 15% faster in the PM. The same section says those percentage reductions equaled about 15 to 28 minutes saved for riders traveling most of the route [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0004] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0005] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0006] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0010].

The delay breakdown points to both stop-time and traffic-time reductions. The report's table shows total B44 Limited travel time at 83.2 minutes versus 65.2 minutes for B44 SBS; time stopped at bus stops fell from 25.8 to 15.4 minutes, and time stopped in traffic fell from 20.0 to 12.5 minutes [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0011].

Ridership evidence is more nuanced than a simple increase after SBS. The report's route table shows B44 total average weekday ridership at 36,989 in 2015, up 2.7% from 2014 but below 2011-2013 levels; separately, it says B44 SBS ridership increased 10% from 2014 to 2015, from 17,249 to 18,986 average weekday riders, while B44 Local ridership dropped 4% over the same period [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0002] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0008] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0010].

Customer survey results favored the SBS service pattern. The progress report says B44 SBS riders were more satisfied on average than B44 Limited riders, with 46% of B44 SBS riders very satisfied compared with 14% of B44 Limited riders before implementation; it also says 18% of surveyed B44 SBS riders were new to the B44 [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p026_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p026_c0004].

Safety evidence should be read as corridor evidence attached to the route. The progress report says Nostrand Avenue and Rogers Avenue were Vision Zero priority corridors and that, in the two years before implementation, there were an average of 583 traffic injuries per year between Atlantic Avenue and Flatbush Avenue on those avenues. Its summary reports a 37% reduction in traffic injuries where bus bulbs or neckdowns shortened crossings, and a 2% reduction where bus lanes were added [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p008_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0006].

## Caveats

This route page overlaps heavily with project and corridor identities for Nostrand/Rogers SBS; some facts belong naturally on `project_nostrand-rogers-sbs` or `corridor_nostrand-avenue`. The canonical route record also includes later ACE/ABLE, TSP, and generic SBS-route-list sources; the synthesis above leaves those out except as checked context because they are not core route-definition evidence. Earlier planning records contain planned 2012 or summer 2013 start dates, but the launch date above uses the November 17, 2013 launch flyer and progress-report evidence.
