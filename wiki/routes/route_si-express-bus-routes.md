The [[route:route_si-express-bus-routes|Staten Island Express Bus Routes]] are six express services — SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, SIM26, and SIM30 — that use 42nd Street in Manhattan. [[cite:42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p006_c0001|Map showing SIM8/8X/22/25/26/30 on 42nd St]] They are among eight MTA bus routes across three boroughs operating on the corridor, carrying approximately [[metric:metric_daily-passengers-42nd-st|16,000 daily passengers]] with up to [[metric:metric_buses-per-hour-42nd-st|210 buses per hour]]. [[cite:42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p006_c0003|8 routes, 3 boroughs, 16,000 daily passengers, 210 buses/hr]]

The 42nd Street Transit Improvements project, a Better Buses Action Plan priority, listed these SIM express routes among the services it serves along the 2.0-mile corridor from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive. [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006|Routes served and corridor length]] At the time of the 2019 plan, average bus speeds on 42nd Street were 4.2 mph in the AM peak and 2.9 mph in the PM peak, well below the [[metric:metric_manhattan-peak-bus-speed|Manhattan peak bus speed average]] of 5.6 mph and the [[metric:metric_citywide-peak-bus-speed|citywide peak bus speed average]] of 7.7 mph. [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006|Average bus speeds: 4.2 mph (AM) / 2.9 mph (PM)]] Buses carry 29% of people on the street while using 33% of street space, and pedestrians account for 63% of people. [[cite:42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p007_c0002|Mode share: Bus 29%, Pedestrian 63%]]

This information comes from a June 2019 NYC DOT and MTA presentation to Manhattan Community Board 5 as part of the Better Buses Action Plan outreach. [[cite:42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p001_c0001|42nd Street Transit Improvements presentation]]
