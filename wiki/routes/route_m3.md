The [[route:route_m3|M3 Bus Route]] is a local bus route in Manhattan. It appears in at least three distinct NYC DOT bus priority study areas. Along the 116th Street corridor from Morningside Avenue to Pleasant Avenue (a 2.2-mile CB10 study area), the M3 is one of ten bus routes serving over 65,000 daily passengers [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p004_c0002|CB10 Feb 2025 presentation listing M3 among 10 routes]]. In the Manhattan Avenue and Morningside Avenue CB9 study area, the M3 is one of three bus routes (with the M7 and M116) carrying over 40,000 daily passengers [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p004_c0002|CB9 Feb 2025 presentation listing M3 among 3 routes]]. On Madison Avenue between East 23rd and 42nd Streets, the M3 is one of four local bus routes (M1, M2, M3, M4) plus the Q32 serving 92,000 daily riders across 34 total routes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|Madison Avenue CB5 May 2025 presentation listing M3 among local routes]].

Bus speeds in these corridors are low. On Madison Avenue in October 2024, local buses including the M3 averaged [[metric:metric_local-speed-5-7-mph-6-10am-oct2024|5.7 mph]] during the 6-10 AM weekday peak, while express buses averaged [[metric:metric_express-speed-7-5-mpg-6-10am-oct2024|7.5 mph]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0002|October 2024 speed table showing M3 among routes measured]]. Safety data for the CB9 study area (2019-2023) records 7 pedestrian injuries, 10 bicyclist injuries (2 severe), and 14 motor vehicle occupant injuries (1 severe), with no fatalities [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p012_c0006|Injury summary table 2019-2023]].

NYC DOT has proposed a Madison Avenue Bus Improvement Project that would create double bus lanes between 23rd and 42nd Streets, extending the existing double bus lane pattern north of 42nd Street [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p016_c0001|Proposal for double bus lanes south of 42nd Street]]. On the 116th Street corridor, DOT presentations identify challenges including traffic congestion, double parking, and bus speeds below 4 mph in some segments, with a stated vision of making bus service fast, reliable, and on-time [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0003|Vision: make bus service fast, reliable and on-time]][[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0005|Challenges: traffic congestion, double parking]].
