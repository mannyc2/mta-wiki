The [[route:route_b8-2015-sbk-corridor|B8]] is one of 13 east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn identified as part of the South Brooklyn east-west corridor during the NYC DOT's South Brooklyn Crosstown kick-off meeting in June 2015 [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0005|B8 listed among east-west bus routes]]. These 13 routes together carry approximately 178,000 riders per day, with 9 subway routes in the area running mainly north-south [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0007|178,000 combined daily ridership]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0003|9 subway routes serve south Brooklyn]]. The corridor was first identified as a priority service need by the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study in 2009 [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0009|BRT Phase II Study identified priority]]. Key issues raised at 2009 public workshops included that bus trips were long and slow, many parts of south Brooklyn were underserved by transit, and a bus trip across south Brooklyn could take up to 2 hours [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p002_c0011|key issues from 2009 workshops]]. Existing SBS routes citywide had demonstrated measurable improvements: a 15-23% speed improvement ([[metric:metric_sbs-faster-bus-15-23-pct|SBS bus service speed improvement]]), a 110% average increase in ridership ([[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-110-pct|SBS ridership increase]]), 95% bus rider satisfaction ([[metric:metric_sbs-rider-satisfaction-95-pct|SBS rider satisfaction]]), and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors ([[metric:metric_sbs-crash-reduction-20-pct_3|SBS crash reduction]]) [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0007|15-23% faster bus service]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0009|110% ridership increase]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0013|95% rider satisfaction]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p001_c0015|20% crash reduction]]. The kick-off boards presented improvement strategies including dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, improved station amenities, real-time passenger information, and pedestrian safety improvements [[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p003_c0001|ways to improve bus service]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p004_c0007|real-time passenger information]][[cite:2015_06_17_brt_southbrooklyn_kickoff_boards#p004_c0009|pedestrian safety improvements]].
