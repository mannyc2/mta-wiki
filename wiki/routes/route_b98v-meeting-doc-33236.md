[[route:route_b98v-meeting-doc-33236|B98V]] was a new temporary bus route launched on March 1, 2021, running from Pink Houses in Brooklyn to the state COVID-19 vaccination site at Medgar Evers College [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|B98V route described in Transit and Bus Committee Meeting]]. The route operated as a pilot program with buses running every 30 minutes from 6:30 a.m. to 9 p.m. [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p041_c0003|B98V pilot program details]].

The route served NYCHA buildings and community centers in Brooklyn, providing enhanced transit access to a mass vaccination site in a traditionally underserved community of color [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p041_c0003|B98V vaccination equity context]]. A companion route, Q98V, operated simultaneously from Edgemere in Queens to the York College vaccination site [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|Q98V companion route]]. The two vaccination sites together had the capacity to vaccinate 3,000 New Yorkers each day [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0005|vaccination site capacity]].

The bus report section of the March 2021 Transit and Bus Committee Meeting — where [[route:route_b98v-meeting-doc-33236|B98V]] was presented — was introduced by Craig Cipriano, who served as President of MTA Bus Company and Senior Vice President of the NYCT Department of Buses [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p040_c0002|Craig Cipriano role]].

This was a time-limited pilot vaccination shuttle, not a permanent route addition to the NYCT or MTA Bus network [[cite:meeting_doc_33236#p041_c0003|B98V described as pilot program]]. No data on ridership, cost, or service duration for [[route:route_b98v-meeting-doc-33236|B98V]] was found in the source document beyond the launch and operating schedule.
