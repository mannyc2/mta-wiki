The [[route:route_b65-flatbush-ave-cb2|B65]] is one of several bus routes that travel on Flatbush Avenue between Livingston Street and Grand Army Plaza in Brooklyn, appearing as a southbound route on a corridor map from a June 2024 Community Board 2 presentation [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p011_c0004|a map of the Flatbush Avenue corridor showing bus routes]]. The corridor's Bus Priority project, led by NYC DOT, focuses on improving bus speeds in the Downtown Brooklyn/Northern Flatbush section where the B65 operates alongside routes such as the B41, B45, B63, B67, B69, and B103 [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p010_c0002|focus on Downtown Brooklyn/Northern Flatbush section]]. The B65's presence on the corridor means it is affected by the same conditions that drive the project: [[metric:metric_bus-speed-below-4mph-downtown-bk_2|slow bus speeds below 4 mph in Downtown Brooklyn]], [[metric:metric_flatbush-ave-daily-bus-riders|Over 132,000 average daily bus riders]] across 12 routes, and the corridor's status as a [[metric:metric_55-ksi-since-2019|Vision Zero Priority Corridor with 55 people killed or severely injured since 2019]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p004_c0007|Flatbush Ave is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor]]. Brooklyn's average non-express weekday peak bus speed is 6.3 mph, and the B41 — the primary Flatbush Ave route — averages just 4.0 mph northbound at 8am and 4.0 mph southbound at 4pm [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p012_c0002|Average non-express weekday peak bus speed in Brooklyn 6.3 mph]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p012_c0003|B41 northbound 8am speed 4.0 mph]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p012_c0004|B41 southbound 4pm speed 4.0 mph]]. The corridor serves [[metric:metric_985k-residents|985,000 residents in corridor community boards]] across its community boards, [[metric:metric_59pct-no-vehicle|59% of households have no access to a private vehicle]], and [[metric:metric_76pct-transit-walk-bike|76% commute to work via public transit, walking, or biking]] [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p005_c0003|985,000 residents, 59% no vehicle, 76% transit/walk/bike]]. The B65's specific role on the corridor is limited relative to the primary B41 route, and the extent to which bus priority treatments will directly address conditions on the short section used by the B65 depends on future project phases [[cite:flatbush_ave_cb2_jun2024#p010_c0002|DOT plans to study bus priority treatments in other sections in future phases]].
