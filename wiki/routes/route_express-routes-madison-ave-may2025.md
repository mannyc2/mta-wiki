The 29 express bus routes on Madison Avenue are a set of bus routes originating from other boroughs that serve the study area between East 23rd and East 42nd Street, as documented in a May 2025 NYC DOT presentation to Manhattan Community Board 5 [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p001_c0002|Community Board 5 presentation May 2025]]. These express routes operate alongside five local routes (M1, M2, M3, M4, Q32) on a corridor that carries [[metric:metric_daily-bus-riders-92000_2|92,000 daily bus riders]] and sees [[metric:metric_over-1000-buses-daily_2|Over 1,000 buses on 34 routes serve the Study Area weekday]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|Study area context and ridership]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p009_c0002|Over 1,000 buses from 34 routes]]. Bus lanes have existed on Madison Avenue from East 42nd to East 60th Street since 1981, but the presentation says the segment between 23rd and 42nd Street lacks dedicated bus lanes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|Existing bus lanes since 1981]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p006_c0002|Project scope 23rd to 42nd]].

Buses carry [[metric:metric_55-pct-roadway-users-on-buses|55% of roadway users on buses]] in AM and PM rush hours while using less than a quarter of road space [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|55% of roadway users on buses]]. Bus speeds are as low as [[metric:metric_bus-speeds-as-low-as-4-5-mph|Bus speeds as low as 4.5 mph on Madison Avenue]], below the Manhattan average of 6.2 mph and NYC average of 8.1 mph [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|Bus speeds as low as 4.5 mph]]. In October 2024, express bus speeds in the study area ranged from [[metric:metric_express-speed-7-5-mpg-6-10am-oct2024|Express bus speed 7.5 mph AM peak October 2024]] in the AM peak to [[metric:metric_express-speed-5-8-mpg-10am-3pm-oct2024|Express bus speed 5.8 mph midday October 2024]] midday and [[metric:metric_express-speed-6-2-mpg-3-7pm-oct2024|Express bus speed 6.2 mph PM peak October 2024]] in the PM peak [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0002|Speed table AM peak]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0003|Speed table midday]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0004|Speed table PM peak]].

The 29 express routes include Bronx lines BxM3, BxM4, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, BxM10, BxM11, and BxM18; Brooklyn lines BM1, BM2, BM3, BM4, and BM5; Queens lines QM21, X63, X64, and X68; and Staten Island lines SIM4C, SIM6, SIM11, SIM23, SIM24, SIM31, SIM33C, X27, X28, X37, and X38 [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|29 express routes from other boroughs]]. The proposed Madison Avenue Bus Improvement Project would extend double bus lanes south from 42nd to 23rd Street, alongside updated curb management and Automated Camera Enforcement expansion shown as underway on M2 and M4 local buses in the May 2025 presentation [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p016_c0001|Proposal: double bus lanes south of 42nd]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p019_c0002|ACE expansion on M2 and M4]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p018_c0002|Updated curb management]].
