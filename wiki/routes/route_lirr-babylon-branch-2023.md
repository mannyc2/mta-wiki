The [[route:route_lirr-babylon-branch-2023|Babylon Branch]] is a commuter rail branch operated by the MTA Long Island Rail Road, serving Suffolk County [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|via the 2023 Annual Operating Report]]. According to the LIRR 2023 Annual Operating Report, the branch recorded an overall on-time performance of 93.9% for the year, falling short of the 96.8% annual goal [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|Babylon Branch OTP table]]. Performance varied by period: AM Peak reached 90.1%, PM Peak 90.7%, and Off-Peak Weekday 94.1%, while Weekend OTP was 94.8% [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|Babylon OTP breakdown]]. This compares to a 93.2% overall OTP in 2022 [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p003_c0004|2022 comparison]].

During 2023, the branch experienced multiple service adjustments due to track work on adjacent lines. On December 6-8, Montauk Branch switch maintenance between Valley Stream and Rockville Centre required eastbound and westbound schedule adjustments on the Babylon Branch [[cite:meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0008|Winter Trackwork Programs 2022]]. On April 8, 2023, Babylon Branch trains operated on adjusted schedules during Montauk Branch track maintenance between Valley Stream and Jamaica [[cite:meeting_doc_107636#p001_c0007|Spring Trackwork Programs 2023]]. During the Van Wyck Bridge Replacement weekends of April 15-16 and April 22-23, some Babylon Branch trains also ran on adjusted schedules [[cite:meeting_doc_107636#p001_c0007|Van Wyck Bridge replacement]].

In a separate service event on March 17, 2025, the LIRR operated two additional westbound trains from the Babylon Branch for customers traveling to the New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade [[cite:meeting_doc_164866#p002_c0008|St. Patrick's Day Parade service]].

Funding for the track work programs that affected Babylon Branch service was contained in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets [[cite:meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0010|funding source]] [[cite:meeting_doc_107636#p001_c0009|funding note]].

The LIRR systemwide recorded 18,365 delay minutes in 2023, with [[metric:metric_lirr-delay-infrastructure-2023|Infrastructure Maintenance]] contributing 16.1% (2,957 minutes), [[metric:metric_lirr-delay-fleet-2023|Fleet]] 13.7% (2,523 minutes), [[metric:metric_lirr-delay-capital-projects-2023|Capital Projects]] 2.2% (402 minutes), [[metric:metric_lirr-delay-external-2023|External Factors]] 19.9% (3,646 minutes), and [[metric:metric_lirr-delay-customer-asst-2023|Customer Assistance]] 6.4% (1,167 minutes) [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p005_c0002|System Categories of Delay 2023]]. The systemwide [[metric:metric_lirr-avg-delay-per-late-train-2023|average delay per late train]] was -11.2 minutes, excluding canceled or terminated trains [[cite:meeting_doc_133321#p004_c0001|Avg Delay per Late Train]].
