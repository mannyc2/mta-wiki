## Route Role

Bx6 Local is the local-stop layer of the Bx6 South Bronx crosstown corridor. Early planning maps describe the existing Bx6 local route through Washington Heights, Yankee Stadium, Melrose Avenue, Westchester Avenue, Hunts Point, and the Hunts Point Cooperative Market, while the spring 2017 newsletter calls the Bx6 "an important interborough route serving the South Bronx and Upper Manhattan." [2015_11_20_brt_south_bronx_crosstown_kickoff#p004_c0002] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0015]

The corridor's bus-priority role was tied to high transit dependence and network connectivity: planning materials say it served nearly 25,000 daily riders, connected to the 1, C, 4, B, D, 2, 5, and 6 subway lines, Metro-North, and 20 bus routes including Bx41 SBS, and had 76% of households within a quarter-mile of the route without a vehicle. [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p007_c0003]

## Relationship To Bx6 SBS

The South Bronx Crosstown SBS service plan kept Bx6 Local as the local complement to Bx6 SBS. A 2016 diagram splits the "Existing Bx6" into "Bx6 SBS" stopping only at SBS stops and "Bx6 local" stopping at every stop; March 2017 materials say Bx6 Local would serve nearly all current stops, with frequencies split between Bx6 SBS and Bx6 Local. [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p013_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb4_mar2017#p010_c0003]

The fare-collection split was also explicit: local fares were to be paid on the bus, while SBS fares were to be paid on the street at fare machines. The newsletter describes off-board fare payment as paying before boarding and then boarding through any door. [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p018_c0002] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0004]

## Planning And Status

NYC DOT and MTA began planning transit and safety improvements for the Bx6 corridor in fall 2015. Outreach records list a November 2015 Bronx kickoff meeting, on-street outreach from November 2015 through June 2016, 578 individual comments, more than 1,300 Bx6 SBS information cards, an online feedback portal, and community/elected-official briefings. [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0015] [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p008_c0002]

The launch target shifted in the planning record. June 2016 materials projected a spring 2017 Bx6 SBS launch; spring/March 2017 materials instead showed implementation and service planning in spring 2017, service starting or launching in summer 2017, and a 2018-2020 capital-project phase. A later 2022 update records Bx6 Select Bus Service as launched on September 3, 2017. [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p022_c0002] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p002_c0010] [brt_bx6_cb4_mar2017#p040_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p004_c0002]

## Treatments And Outcomes

The bus-priority package belongs mainly to the Bx6 SBS/corridor project rather than to Bx6 Local alone. Planning materials list signal priority, off-board fare collection, and all-door boarding; the newsletter adds bus lanes, boarding islands, real-time bus arrival information, and wider sidewalks. By May 2022, implemented improvements included bus lanes, fare machines, boarding islands, and the eastbound 161st Street Bus Only Tunnel. [brt_bx6_cb4_mar2017#p005_c0004] [brt_bx6_cb4_mar2017#p005_c0007] [brt_bx6_cb4_mar2017#p005_c0009] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0015] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0019] [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0021] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p005_c0001] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p005_c0005]

Before SBS, planning materials said it took 56 minutes on average to travel from Hunts Point to Riverside Drive over 4.8 miles, with more than 25% of the trip spent boarding passengers and the bus moving less than half the time. A 2022 update says that after SBS launched, buses moved 11% to 16% faster end-to-end; its tables compare Bx6 Lcl in October 2016 with Bx6 SBS in October 2017 and October 2018. [brt_bx6_south_bronx_june2016#p009_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p007_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p007_c0004] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p007_c0006]

The same 2022 update reports improved reliability, with fewer late buses and less bus bunching, while also noting that bus ridership in both the Bronx and along the Bx6 route had been decreasing, with the Bx6 corridor declining at a slower rate than the borough average. [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p010_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p010_c0004] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p010_c0006] [brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p011_c0002]

## Later Related Work

The Bronx Bus Network addendum is a separate later timing thread: it says Bx6 SBS, Bx6 Local, and Bx5 changes, plus all-door boarding, were postponed until 2023 or full OMNY deployment, and it identifies Story Avenue from Bronx River Avenue to White Plains Road as a present-implementation corridor with Bx5 existing and Bx6 SBS after redesign. [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p003_c0003] [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p003_c0004] [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p005_c0003] [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p007_c0010]

A later East 161st Street/Bx6 capital project is related to the corridor rather than to Bx6 Local by itself. The 2026 source describes a project near Yankee Stadium adding westbound bus-only lanes, converting the 161st Street underpass to buses only, adding pedestrian safety upgrades and bus-rider amenities, creating a protected center-running bus lane on East 161st Street, and continuing construction through 2028. [161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001]
