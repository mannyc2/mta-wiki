The [[route:route_m2-ace|M2 Local Bus]] operates along 5th Avenue, Madison Avenue, and Adam Clayton Powell Boulevard and is listed as a route subject to MTA automated camera enforcement [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|M2 ACE route listing]]. On Madison Avenue between East 23rd and East 42nd Streets, the M2 is one of four local bus routes (along with the M1, M3, and M4) serving approximately 92,000 daily bus riders across 34 bus routes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|Madison Avenue study area]]. In October 2024, local buses in this corridor averaged 5.7 mph during the 6-10 AM weekday peak period [[metric:metric_local-speed-5-7-mph-6-10am-oct2024|Local bus speed 5.7 mph AM peak October 2024]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0002|October 2024 speed data using M1, M2, M3]]. A proposal would extend existing 24/7 bus lanes from East 42nd Street south to East 23rd Street, creating double bus lanes on Madison Avenue through the study area [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb6_jun2025#p016_c0002|Double bus lanes proposal]]. The M2 is also undergoing expansion of Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) on this corridor [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p019_c0002|ACE expansion for M2 and M4]]. At a September 2012 public workshop on 125th Street, slow boarding and disembarking of passengers was noted as a particular problem for the M2 around Adam Clayton Powell Boulevard, and buses were reported to be frequently delayed by red lights and traffic congestion [[cite:2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p005_c0004|M2 delay issues on 125th Street]].
