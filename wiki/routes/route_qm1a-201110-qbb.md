The QM1A express bus route served Queens, recorded in the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study conducted by the New York City Department of Transportation [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0001|NYC DOT logo]] and presented to local Community Boards in Spring-Summer 2010 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0003|presentation timing]]. The study identified the Queensboro Bridge (QBB) as a major bus corridor carrying [[metric:metric_qbb-daily-bus-trips|16,000 bus trips per day]], including [[metric:metric_qbb-daily-local-riders|about 8,000 local riders]], with [[metric:metric_qbb-pm-rush-buses|110 buses during the PM rush hour]] or one bus every 30 seconds [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|QBB corridor metrics]]. Over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delays their bus [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|congestion report]].

Among all express bus routes documented, the QM1A had the highest daily ridership at 2,020 passengers, contributing to a total [[metric:metric_express-bus-total-daily-ridership|express bus total daily ridership of 7,920]] across all QBB express routes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|express route daily table]]. The QM1A, along with the QM1, QM2, QM2A, QM10, QM11, and QM12, used a deadhead route operating on the bridge's lower level approach [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0006|deadhead route note]].

A rider survey collected responses from [[metric:metric_express-rider-survey-respondents|511 express bus riders]] using the QBB [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|survey data table]]. Of these, [[metric:metric_express-rider-work-trip-pct|88% were traveling to or from work]] and [[metric:metric_express-rider-daily-bus-pct|81% rode the bus daily]] [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|express rider demographics]]. [[metric:metric_express-rider-subway-alternative-pct|52% of express riders reported sometimes using the subway]] as an alternative, citing destination, time of day, or that the subway is "faster" [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|subway alternative]]. During PM hours, 80% of express riders perceived traffic congestion sometimes, often, or always on the LIE, Queensboro Bridge, and Queens Boulevard [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|congestion perception PM]]. Of those express riders who perceived delay on the bridge, 62% said the average delay was 5-15 minutes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|express delay duration]]. Suggested improvements from express riders included "Bus lanes," "Enforcement of bus lanes," and routing suggestions [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|express improvement suggestions]].

The route was part of the broader study goal to identify cost-effective ways to improve bus speed and reliability for local and express buses using the QBB [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p003_c0004|study goal]].
