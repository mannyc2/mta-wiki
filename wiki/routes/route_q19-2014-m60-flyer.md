The [[route:route_q19-2014-m60-flyer|Q19]] is documented in the 2014 M60 Select Bus Service launch flyer published by NYC DOT for the May 25, 2014 service launch on 125th Street [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0002|M60 SBS launch flyer]]. The flyer notes that the Q19 makes all local stops on Astoria Blvd, while the M60 SBS serves 125th Street in Manhattan [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003|Q19 local stops on Astoria Blvd]]. Along with the Bx15, M100, and M101—which continued making all local stops on 125th Street—the Q19 continued to serve all local stops on its own corridor when the new Select Bus Service began operation [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0004|Q19 continued local stops]].

The flyer identifies the [[route:route_q19-2014-m60-flyer|Q19]] as a Queens local route operating along Astoria Blvd, distinct from the Manhattan-focused M60 SBS corridor on 125th Street [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003|Q19 on Astoria Blvd]]. The record's structured data lists the route's borough as Queens, its route type as local, and its service variant as local, matching the flyer's depiction [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003|Q19 local on Astoria Blvd]].

The flyer was produced jointly by MTA New York City Transit and the New York City Department of Transportation, with a contact listed as Josh Orzeck at NYC DOT for further information [[cite:2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0013|Josh Orzeck contact]]. No further details about the Q19's route terminals, span of service, or frequency are available from this source.
