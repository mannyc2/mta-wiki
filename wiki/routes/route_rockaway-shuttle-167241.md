The [[route:route_rockaway-shuttle-167241|Rockaway Shuttle train]] is a Queens-based subway shuttle service that has delivered 99% on-time performance since January 2025 [[cite:meeting_doc_167241#p009_c0009|Rockaway Shuttle 99% OTP since January]]. It operates as part of the Rockaway Resiliency project, maintaining transit access to the Rockaway peninsula while it is separated from the rest of the subway system [[cite:meeting_doc_167241#p009_c0009|Rockaway Resiliency project service]]. Because the shuttle cars cannot reach maintenance facilities during the separation, the NYCT team executed a complicated fleet swap — moving the cars to Pitkin Yard for routine maintenance [[cite:meeting_doc_167241#p009_c0009|fleet swap for maintenance]]. New York City Transit President Demetrius Crichlow rode the shuttle with New York City Council Member Selvena Brooks-Powers, who represents the district; riders told them the new service pattern improved their daily commute [[cite:meeting_doc_167241#p004_c0010|ride with Council Member Brooks-Powers]]. The same March 2025 NYCT performance document notes systemwide that [[metric:metric_major-transit-crimes-down-29pct|major transit crimes are down 29% year to date]] and [[metric:metric_arrests-up-71pct-ytd|arrests are up 71% year to date]], with increased NYPD patrols supporting a safer travel environment for shuttle and other riders [[cite:meeting_doc_167241#p009_c0009|systemwide safety context]].
