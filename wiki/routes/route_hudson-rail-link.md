The [[route:route_hudson-rail-link|Hudson Rail Link Bus Service]] (HRL) is a fixed-route scheduled feeder bus service operated by Metro-North Railroad that connects MNR's Spuyten Duyvil and Riverdale stations with surrounding neighborhoods in the northwest Bronx, an area described as geographically challenging and not easily accessible by other public transportation [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0008|Service Description]]. Arrow Rail Link Service, Inc. (formerly SNT Bus, Inc.) maintains and operates 16 MNR-owned buses to provide the service Monday through Friday during peak and off-peak times at a [[metric:metric_hrl-fare|fare]] of $2.90 per ride, with buses coordinated to meet arriving and departing MNR rail service between 6:00 a.m. and midnight at both stations [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0008|Fare and Operations]].

The original contract for HRL service was approved by the MTA Board in July 2016 for a four-year base term, with two three-year renewal options [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0006|Original Contract Approval]]. The contract's [[metric:metric_hrl-original-amount|original amount]] was $8,561,334, and prior modifications and the first renewal option total [[metric:metric_hrl-prior-modifications|HRL Prior Modifications Total]] [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0003|Contract Summary Table]]. Prior modifications were issued to address interim extensions during the pandemic, and the MTA Board approved the exercise of the remaining two years of the first renewal option in September 2021 [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0006|First Renewal Approval]]. The source table lists the [[metric:metric_hrl-current-amount|current contract amount]] as $17,324,186 [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0003|Current Amount]].

In September 2023, MNR sought Board approval to exercise the second and final three-year renewal option from October 1, 2023 through September 30, 2026, with a [[metric:metric_hrl-contract-request-amount|modification request]] of $8,684,603 (representing 50.13% of the current amount) to be funded by MNR's Operating Budget [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0005|Second Renewal Request]] [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0014|Funding Source]]. Contract pricing is based on negotiated hourly rates adjusted annually by 3.5 percent [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0007|Pricing Formula]]. MNR has stated it has no alternative means to provide these services in-house [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0016|No Alternatives]].

HRL ridership grew 5.7 percent between 2016 and 2019 [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0009|Ridership Growth]]. In 2019, [[metric:metric_hrl-daily-ridership-2019|average daily ridership]] was 1,601 and [[metric:metric_hrl-annual-ridership-2019|annual ridership]] was 406,193 [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0009|2019 Ridership]]. After a steep pandemic-related decline in 2020, ridership has trended upward; in 2022, [[metric:metric_hrl-daily-ridership-2022|average daily ridership]] was 597 and [[metric:metric_hrl-annual-ridership-2022|annual ridership]] was 151,723 [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0009|2022 Ridership]]. As of the source document (September 2023), [[metric:metric_hrl-daily-ridership-2023|HRL Average Daily Ridership 2023]] was 721, or [[metric:metric_hrl-pandemic-recovery-pct|HRL 2023 recovery percentage]] of pre-pandemic levels, with expectations of continued recovery [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0009|2023 Ridership]] [[cite:meeting_doc_121156#p006_c0010|Recovery Outlook]].
