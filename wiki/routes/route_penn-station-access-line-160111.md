The Penn Station Access Line is a planned commuter rail route that extends Metro-North Railroad's New Haven Line into Penn Station New York, adding four new accessible stations in the Bronx [[cite:meeting_doc_160111#p001_c0014|PSA project description]]. The 2025 Final Proposed Budget (November 2024) projected first ridership of 0.429 million annual riders in 2028, with zero ridership in all prior years [[cite:meeting_doc_160111#p008_c0002|PSA ridership projection in 2025 budget]]. Service was expected to commence in November 2027, with hiring for train crews, operations managers, and maintenance staff planned to start in 2025 [[cite:meeting_doc_160111#p001_c0014|PSA service commencement and hiring timeline]].

The 2025 Final Proposed Budget authorized 9 Penn Station Access positions in 2025, growing to 80 in 2026, 153 in 2027, and 231 in 2028 [[cite:meeting_doc_160111#p009_c0002|PSA positions in November 2024 plan]]. By the February 2026 Financial Plan (reflecting the 2026 Adopted Budget), a "Re-Timing of Penn Station Access" adjustment had been applied, reducing the 2026 position count to 45 per month and shifting costs and positions into later years [[cite:meeting_doc_199096#p019_c0002|PSA positions in 2026 Adopted Budget monthly detail]] [[cite:meeting_doc_199096#p001_c0006|Re-timing of Penn Station Access listed as MTA Plan adjustment]] [[cite:meeting_doc_199096#p002_c0008|Re-Timing of Penn Station Access dollar and position adjustments]]. The re-timing was listed alongside MTA Operating Efficiencies as an MTA Plan Adjustment adopted by the Board in December 2025 [[cite:meeting_doc_199096#p001_c0002|MTA Board adoption December 2025]] [[cite:meeting_doc_199096#p001_c0006|MTA Operating Efficiencies and re-timing]]. The Penn Station Access Line is classified as a true commuter-rail route and is an extension of the New Haven Line [[cite:meeting_doc_160111#p001_c0014|PSA as New Haven Line extension]].
