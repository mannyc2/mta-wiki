The QM2A is an express bus route using the Queensboro Bridge (QBB), studied as part of the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study presented by NYC DOT to local community boards in Spring-Summer 2010 [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p001_c0002|The Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study]]. The study recorded a daily ridership of 720 riders on the QM2A, with a total of 7,920 express bus riders using the QBB daily across all express routes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|Express bus daily ridership table]].

The QBB is a major bus corridor carrying 16,000 bus trips per day, including about 8,000 local riders, with 110 buses during PM rush hour (one bus every 30 seconds) [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|QBB corridor overview]]. Of the express riders surveyed on the QBB, 511 responded, with 88% traveling to or from work, 81% riding the bus daily, and 68% sometimes using the subway as an alternative [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Express rider survey table]]. A total of 80% of express riders (PM) reported perceiving traffic congestion on their bus sometimes, often, or always, with locations including the LIE, Queensboro Bridge, and Queens Boulevard; of those who perceive delay on the bridge, 62% reported an average delay of 5-15 minutes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Express rider survey table]].

For local buses using the QBB, the survey found 313 respondents, with 75% traveling to or from work, 68% riding the bus daily, and 68% sometimes using the subway (citing "faster" or "more convenient") [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Local rider survey table]]. Of local riders, 76% perceived traffic congestion sometimes, often, or always, with locations including the Queensboro Bridge and Queens Plaza; 58% of those perceiving delay reported 5-15 minutes on the bridge [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Local rider survey table]]. The QM2A also has a designated deadhead route on the Manhattan side, shared with QM1, QM1A, QM2, QM10, QM11, and QM12 buses [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0006|Deadhead route description]].

Key metrics for the QM2A and QBB corridor are documented across several source tables within the study [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|Express bus daily ridership table]][[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|QBB corridor overview]][[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|Rider survey table]]. These include [[metric:metric_express-bus-total-daily-ridership|Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB]] of 7,920, [[metric:metric_qbb-daily-bus-trips|QBB Daily Bus Trips]] of 16,000, and [[metric:metric_qbb-pm-rush-buses|QBB PM Rush Hour Buses]] of 110. The [[metric:metric_express-rider-survey-respondents|Express Bus Rider Survey Respondents]] totaled 511 and [[metric:metric_local-rider-survey-respondents|Local Bus Rider Survey Respondents]] totaled 313.
