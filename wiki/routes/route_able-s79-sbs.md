The [[route:route_able-s79-sbs|S79-SBS]] (internal route ID S79+) is a Staten Island-to-Brooklyn Select Bus Service route operated by MTA New York City Transit under the Automated Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE) program [[cite:ace_routes_dataset_dictionary#p001_b0057|ACE dataset lists S79+ as route item]]. The route launched on September 2, 2012, as S79 Select Bus Service along Hylan Boulevard and Richmond Avenue, connecting the Staten Island Mall area to the 86th Street subway station in Bay Ridge, Brooklyn [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p001_c0001|S79 Select Bus Service begins September 2, 2012]] [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p002_c0005|Bus lanes on Hylan and Richmond Avenues]].

Bus lane treatments were part of the initial service package: curbside bus lanes on Hylan Boulevard between Lincoln Avenue and Steuben Street, in effect on weekdays from 6-9 AM northbound and 3-7 PM southbound, plus offset bus lanes on Richmond Avenue (Platinum Avenue to Forest Hill Road and Shirley Avenue to Hylan Boulevard) in effect at all times [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p002_c0018|Hylan Blvd bus lane hours]] [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p002_c0020|Richmond Ave bus lane hours]]. The 2014 Hylan Boulevard final report documented both curbside and offset lane types along the corridor [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p010_c0003|Two types of bus lanes painted on project corridor]].

Transit Signal Priority (TSP) was implemented on the S79 SBS / Hylan Boulevard corridor in 2015, one of five corridors receiving a new generation of TSP since 2012 [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p005_c0007|TSP implemented on 5 corridors since 2012, including S79 SBS/Hylan Boulevard in 2015]]. Travel time data comparing October 2012 (before TSP) to April 2016 (after TSP) showed AM peak northbound trips from Staten Island to Brooklyn fell from 43.8 minutes to 39.2 minutes, a 10.5% decrease [[cite:tsp_report_2017#p008_c0004|S79 SBS travel times before/after TSP]].

The S79-SBS was an early ABLE camera route. Onboard bus lane enforcement cameras were first deployed on the Q44 and S79-SBS routes [[cite:meeting_doc_98311#p015_c0011|First ABLE cameras implemented on Q44 and S79 SBS]]. The route was listed among active ABLE routes through 2023 [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0004|S79-SBS listed in ABLE route list]] and continued under the Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program along Hylan Boulevard and Richmond Avenue [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE lists S79-SBS with Hylan Blvd / Richmond Av]]. In November of that period, front-of-bus bike racks were installed on the S79 SBS as one of three key SBS routes providing bike access connections across MTA bridges [[cite:meeting_doc_129371#p005_c0003|S79 SBS among SBS routes with front-of-bus bike racks]].
