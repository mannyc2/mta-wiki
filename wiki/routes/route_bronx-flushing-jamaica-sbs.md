The [[route:route_bronx-flushing-jamaica-sbs|Q44-SBS]] (Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service) began service on November 29, 2015, replacing the Q44 Limited along its route from Merrick Boulevard in Jamaica to the Bronx Zoo via Main Street [[cite:brt_route_index#p001_b0001|service start date]] [[cite:2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p007_c0002|Q44 from Bronx Zoo to Jamaica via Main Street]] [[cite:2015_06_09_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb12#p009_c0002|Q44 LTD replaced by Q44 SBS]]. The Q20A/B local service continued unchanged alongside the new SBS service [[cite:2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p007_c0003|Q20A/B local continues as is]].

The route was prioritized for the SBS upgrade based on daily ridership of over 29,000 and its role as a key inter-borough connection between Queens and the Bronx [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p007_c0002|Q44 prioritized based on ridership]] [[cite:2015_04_02_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb9_presentation#p007_c0004|vital Bronx-Queens inter-borough link]]. Planned treatments included offset bus lanes in Flushing, peak-period curbside bus lanes in Jamaica, improved fare collection, transit signal priority, and Vision Zero safety improvements at priority intersections such as Main Street/Roosevelt Avenue and Sutphin Boulevard/Archer Avenue [[cite:2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0001|Flushing offset bus lanes]] [[cite:2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p015_c0001|Jamaica peak-period curbside bus lanes]] [[cite:2015_09_22_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p007_c0001|bus lanes, fare machines, curb changes]] [[cite:2015_04_02_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb9_presentation#p004_c0003|improved fare collection]] [[cite:2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0006|priority intersection list]].

The Q44 SBS was one of the initial routes equipped with Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE), also known as Automated Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE), using bus-mounted cameras to enforce bus lane and bus stop rules [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0004|Q44-SBS listed among ABLE routes]] [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE route on Main St / Cross Bronx Service Rd]] [[cite:meeting_doc_95176#p015_c0010|first of 300 additional equipped buses on Q44 SBS starting September]]. The route also received transit signal priority (TSP) technology [[cite:tsp_status_2017#p001_b0001|TSP on Q44 SBS]]. In 2023, the MTA installed front-of-bus bike racks on the Q44 SBS as part of an interborough bike access initiative [[cite:meeting_doc_129371#p005_c0003|Q44 SBS bike racks]].
