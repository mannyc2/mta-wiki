The M15 Local/Limited, operated alongside the M15 Select Bus Service (SBS) variant, is one of the busiest routes in the MTA bus network. In 2023, the M15/M15 SBS carried over 16 million passengers, placing it among the highest-ridership routes in the system [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p005_c0009|M15/M15 SBS carried over 16 million passengers in 2023]]. The route is classified as a Local/Limited service type, which typically serves all stops or skips certain stops but generally lacks dedicated infrastructure [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p005_c0009|M15 route classification]].

The [[metric:metric_daily-ridership-estimate|metric_daily_ridership_estimate]] for the citywide bus network is approximately 1.4 million passengers, and Brooklyn led boroughs with over 104 million passengers in 2023 [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p005_c0008|1.4 million daily riders and Brooklyn ridership]]. The citywide average bus speed for calendar year 2024 was about 8.1 miles per hour, largely unchanged from the 8.2 mph average in January 2019, reflecting a broader stagnation despite multiple City and MTA initiatives [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p007_c0003|citywide average speed 8.1 mph in 2024]].

The report notes that the M15/M15 SBS figure of over 16 million passengers is cited as an example of the high-ridership routes within an MTA bus network that spans over 300 routes across more than 3,200 miles [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p005_c0009|MTA bus network size]]. These summary statistics appear in the IBO report "Speeding Up Slowly: A Review of Initiatives to Improve Bus Speeds in New York City" [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p001_c0001|report title]] published by the New York City Independent Budget Office in February 2025 [[cite:speeding_up_slowly_2025#p002_c0002|February 2025]].
