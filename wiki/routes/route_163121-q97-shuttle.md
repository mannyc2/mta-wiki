The [[route:route_163121-q97-shuttle|Q97]] is a temporary shuttle bus route established to provide alternate service during the Rockaway Line rehabilitation project, which commenced on January 17, 2025 [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0003|January 17 commencement]]. The route runs nonstop between Howard Beach-JFK Airport and Far Rockaway-Mott Av, offering one of two shuttle bus options for Rockaways residents during the four-month subway shutdown [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0008|Q97 nonstop service description]]. The Q97 operates alongside the Q109, which follows a local routing with stops at Broad Channel and Beach 90 St [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0008|Q109 local route]].

The shuttle bus service is free to riders, with 53 AM and 55 PM weekday peak buses deployed across both routes [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p011_c0010|free shuttle service and bus counts]]. Additional express service was added on the QM15, QM16, and QM17 to provide more one-seat rides between the Rockaways and Manhattan, and the Far Rockaway Ticket on the Long Island Rail Road was discounted to $2.75 [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0008|LIRR discount]] [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p025_c0002|QM15 extra service]].

The shuttle service is part of a $393 million capital project to harden and modernize the Rockaway Line, which suffered extensive damage from Superstorm Sandy in 2012 [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0005|project cost and scope]]. The project is led by Hugo Zamora, serving as the dedicated New York City Transit project lead overseeing the customer experience [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0007|Zamora project lead]]. Work is expected to be completed in May 2025, at which point the Q97 shuttle service will end [[cite:meeting_doc_163121#p025_c0003|completion timeline]].
