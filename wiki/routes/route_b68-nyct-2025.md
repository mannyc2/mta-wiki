The B68 is a Brooklyn bus route operating on Coney Island Avenue and Brighton Beach Avenue [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE lists B68 on Coney Island Av / Brighton Beach Av]]. On December 8, 2025, the MTA expanded the Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program to include the B68, along with the B60 in Brooklyn and the M57 in Manhattan, with a 60-day warning phase during which vehicles blocking bus lanes, bus stops, or double-parked would receive warning notices instead of fines [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p010_c0011|December 8, 2025 ACE expansion to B68, B60, M57]]. The ACE program is administered jointly by the MTA, the New York City Department of Finance, and the New York City Department of Transportation [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE partnership description]]. The cited ACE program page describes coverage on [[metric:metric_ace-active-routes|60 bus routes]] and benefits for [[metric:metric_ace-weekday-riders|over one million average weekday riders]] [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE active on 60 routes, over 1M riders]].

The B68 joins 51 Brooklyn and citywide routes already covered by ACE as of late 2025 [[cite:nyct_key_performance_metrics_doc194001#p011_c0009|51 routes already covered by ACE, B68 among three new additions]]. During the 60-day warning phase, motorists receive educational notices; fine-bearing violations begin after the warning period ends [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|60-day warning notices before fines begin]]. ACE violations carry escalating civil penalties starting at $50 and increasing by $50 per violation up to a maximum of $250, with each violation reviewed by a city employee before issuance [[cite:mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001|ACE fine structure and city employee review]].
