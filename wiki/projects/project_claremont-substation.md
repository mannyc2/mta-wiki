## Overview

The Claremont Substation Project is a Metro-North property-acquisition and electrical-substation project in the Bronx. July 2023 materials identify the site as 3876-3880 Park Avenue, Block 2904 Lot 10, with 1049 Ogden Realty Company, LLC as seller; the requested action was authorization to acquire the property by negotiated agreement or eminent domain for a new Claremont Substation, with compensation still to be determined at that stage [meeting_doc_115331#p064_c0005]. The site was described as a vacant three-story industrial building on roughly 7,405 square feet, to be demolished after acquisition so MTA could build a substation supporting Metro-North Harlem Line operations [meeting_doc_115331#p064_c0007].

## Purpose

The stated purpose is traction-power capacity on the Harlem Line: Metro-North said existing substations in Melrose and Tremont were overloaded and too far apart, and that the Claremont substation would increase load capacity, improve service reliability, and add feeders to the existing third rail and local utility infrastructure [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0008].

## Process

By May 22, 2024, the MTA Board had approved EDPL Determination and Findings for project property interests, including acquisition by eminent domain or otherwise [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0003]. The EDPL record says MTA held a public hearing on March 7, 2024, accepted written comments through March 15, 2024, and estimated the property interests would be needed in Q4 2024 [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0004] [meeting_doc_140406#p004_c0012] [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0006]. The same findings state the project was exempt from SEQRA because the affected property was contiguous to transportation land, less than ten acres, and the acquisition would not materially change the character of the prior transportation use [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0009] [meeting_doc_140406#p006_c0010].

## July 2024 Acquisition Action

In July 2024 staff summaries, the requested action shifted to authorization to enter into a purchase contract for the premises. These materials describe the premises as a 7,530+/- square-foot lot and 17,955+/- square-foot industrial building, with a $2,250,000 purchase price [meeting_doc_146891#p001_c0008]. They also state that July 2023 authorization and May 2024 EDPL findings were already in place, and that MTA Real Estate and the seller had reached a preliminary agreement for MTA to acquire the property in lieu of condemnation at the asking price, described as in line with an independent appraisal completed November 27, 2023 [meeting_doc_146891#p001_c0011] [meeting_doc_146891#p001_c0012].

## Caveats

July 2024 materials describe a preliminary agreement and requested authorization to enter into a contract of sale; they do not by themselves state that acquisition had closed or construction had begun [meeting_doc_146891#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_146891#p001_c0012].

The public-comment summary is terse: it only says comments requested repair of existing Harlem-Line substations in the Bronx, and that all comments were considered [meeting_doc_140406#p007_c0003] [meeting_doc_140406#p007_c0004] [meeting_doc_140406#p007_c0005].
