The evidence for this page spans two related Webster Avenue bus-priority efforts: the original
Bx41 Select Bus Service corridor project and a later 2019 southbound bus-lane gap treatment near
the Cross Bronx Expressway. The SBS corridor was framed around the existing Bx41 route, 5.3 miles
from The Hub to Williamsbridge, carrying more than 20,000 daily riders and serving neighborhoods
where many residents lived within walking distance of the route.
[2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p007_c0002] [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p013_c0003]

The early project goals were to speed buses and improve reliability, improve safety for all
corridor users, and support community needs. By the 2013 implementation plan, the proposed SBS
package converted Bx41 Limited service to Bx41 SBS, added four miles of offset bus lanes with
bus-bulb stations, and included off-board fare collection, low-floor buses, station and bus
branding, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety improvements.
[2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p005_c0003] [2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p005_c0005]
[2012_02_06_brt_webster_cac1#p005_c0007] [2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p011_c0002]

The implementation was staged. The 2013 work focused on roadway markings and transit service,
including milling and paving, lane markings, red bus-lane paint, selected pedestrian islands or
medians, fare machines, bus stop changes, curb regulation changes, and start of service at the end
of June. The later capital phase was described as bus bulbs and other capital construction.
[2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p042_c0008] [2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p043_c0003]
[2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p043_c0006] [2013_03_sbs_webster_bx_cb7#p042_c0012]

NYCT and NYCDOT launched Bx41 SBS on June 30, 2013. The 2014 progress report says the launch used
off-board fare collection, eight miles of offset bus lanes, intersection traffic improvements,
enhanced stations, improved scheduling, and better customer information. It reports 19-23% faster
Bx41 SBS speeds compared with the former Bx41 LTD, 11-16% faster Bx41 Local speeds, a 25% increase
in Bx41 ridership, maintained traffic speeds and volume, and pedestrian safety improvements along
the corridor. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p005_c0005]
[2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p005_c0007]

The later Better Buses evidence narrows in on a short southbound segment: Webster Avenue between
E 176th Street and E 174th Street. The 2019 plan identified a gap where southbound traffic
approaching the Cross Bronx Expressway entrance blocked buses and through traffic because the
southbound bus lane stopped at E Tremont Avenue and did not return until E 174th Street. Proposed
improvements were a southbound offset bus lane, a physical barrier separating bus and right-turning
movements, and signal timing adjustments. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p035_c0001]
[better_buses_action_plan_2019#p035_c0008] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p035_c0011]

By the December 2019 Bronx CB5 update, the added southbound bus lane was reported as producing a
37% PM-peak bus-speed improvement, with pedestrian safety changes calming turns onto the Cross
Bronx Expressway and a pedestrian island still planned for spring 2020. The same update's table
shows Bx41 Local and SBS running time down 11% in the 6-10 AM period and 37% in the 3-7 PM period
for October 2018 versus October 2019. [bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p003_c0004]
[bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p003_c0006]
