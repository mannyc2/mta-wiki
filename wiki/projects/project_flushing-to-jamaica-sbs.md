## Overview

Flushing to Jamaica Select Bus Service was a planning-stage SBS effort for corridors between Downtown Flushing and Jamaica, centered on Main Street and Kissena/Parsons Boulevards. NYCDOT and MTA framed the early outreach as a way to gather community feedback on those two corridors. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0001]

The project followed earlier BRT planning work: the corridor had been identified in the 2006 NYC BRT Phase I study and the 2009 Phase II report, and was described in the 2014 CAC presentation as having "long and slow bus trips." [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p007_c0003] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p007_c0004]

## Corridor And Routes

The June 2014 CAC materials described the Flushing-Jamaica Corridor as served by Q20A/B and Q44 on Main Street, Q25 and Q34 on Kissena/Parsons Boulevards, and Q65 on 164 Street, with over 90,000 daily bus riders in the broader corridor. [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0004] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0005]

The fall 2014 workshop summary narrowed the SBS study discussion to Q20A/B, Q44LTD, Q25 LTD, and Q34 service on Main Street and Kissena/Parsons Boulevards, describing those four lines as carrying 68,000 daily riders. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010] A January 2015 board later summarized the Flushing-Jamaica SBS study area as Main Street and Kissena-Parsons Boulevards, 13.9 miles, with 68,000 daily bus riders. [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0002] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0007] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0012]

## Needs Identified

The workshop summary reported that Q44LTD and Q25LTD were delayed during AM and PM peaks nearly 50% of their travel time; it attributed 29% of delay to buses stuck in traffic or at red lights, and said both corridors averaged less than 10 mph. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0013]

Safety was also part of the case for the project. The summary cited bike and pedestrian crash locations from 2008 to 2012, naming Roosevelt Avenue, Archer Avenue, Jamaica Avenue, Hillside Avenue, Sanford Avenue, and Union Turnpike as the top six locations. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0014] Community comments also flagged dangerous crossings, sidewalk crowding, and conflict points on Kissena, Main Street, Parsons, Jamaica, Archer, and Sutphin. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p004_c0014]

## Outreach And Planning

Early outreach included two public workshops, one at York College in Downtown Jamaica and one at PS244 in Downtown Flushing, with 42 residents, riders, community board members, advocates, and elected-official representatives participating. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0002]

The project schedule in the June 2014 CAC materials listed Summer 2014 data collection and analysis, Winter 2014/2015 corridor selection and concept design, 2015 corridor-plan development, and a TBD final-design step that included finalizing the design, developing an implementation plan, and launching SBS service. [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p020_c0008] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p020_c0009] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p021_c0008] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p021_c0009] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p022_c0008] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p022_c0009] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p023_c0007] [2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p023_c0009]

By January 2015, project materials listed a CAC kickoff, Public Workshop #1, Public Workshop #2 on street design, bus lanes, and stop locations, plus Pomonok Houses and Queens Hospital SBS briefings. [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p004_c0009] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p004_c0012] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p004_c0013] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p004_c0015] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p004_c0017]

## Design Themes

The January 2015 boards stated goals to improve transit service for neighborhoods far from the subway, strengthen transit connections to retail and job centers in and between Downtown Flushing and Jamaica, and implement safety upgrades in key Vision Zero priority corridors. [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p011_c0002] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p011_c0003] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p011_c0004]

The same board set described SBS features as dedicated bus lanes, faster fare collection, improved station amenities, signal priority for buses, and real-time passenger information. [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0001] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0004] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0009] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0011] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0013] [2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0015]

Related but separate corridor work appeared alongside the SBS study: Flushing in Motion signal control, and a DDC sidewalk-widening project on Main Street between 38th Avenue and 41st Avenue. [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p003_c0003] [2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p003_c0005]
