Atlantic Tunnel Lighting is more than a timetable placeholder: a June 2024 Capital Program Committee variance page identifies it as L8070102, the Atlantic Tunnel Lighting Improvement Project, with a $10M budget/EAC, construction phase status, 33% phase complete, and current substantial completion in December 2026 rather than the December 2024 date at award. [meeting_doc_143601#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_143601#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_143601#p119_c0008]

The stated scope is to replace and upgrade deteriorated lighting systems in the LIRR Atlantic Avenue Tunnel. The source says existing tunnel lighting had been in service for 30 to 60 years, in a corrosive environment where fixtures, switchgear, and wiring were treated as having a 25-year useful life. The work covers light fixtures, wire, cable, conduit, distribution equipment, primary power, transformers, and switches in the Dunton-to-ENY east-end section of the tunnel. [meeting_doc_143601#p119_c0009]

The project is framed as a safety and operations-support investment: effective tunnel lighting is described as necessary for employee work areas, emergency egress for passengers and crew, facility/equipment security, and inspections or maintenance of track, signals, power, and communications infrastructure inside the tunnel. [meeting_doc_143601#p119_c0009]

The timetable-change sources show why this project keeps appearing in service-planning material. LIRR staff summaries list Atlantic Tunnel Lighting among projects supported by schedule changes for March 4-May 19, 2024; May 20-September 2, 2024; November 11, 2024-March 2, 2025; March 3-May 19, 2025; and May 19-September 1, 2025. [meeting_doc_133331#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_133331#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_138226#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_138226#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_155021#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_155021#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_164866#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_164866#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_170846#p001_c0004]

The June 2024 variance explanation ties the schedule slip to lack of manpower and track access. It says daytime outages were no longer available because they had delayed revenue trains into and out of Atlantic Terminal, and that service planning modified the timetable to support night outage work for the installations. [meeting_doc_143601#p119_c0010] [meeting_doc_143601#p120_c0003]
