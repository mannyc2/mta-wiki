## Source-Time Snapshot

Metro-North presented the SC42 Dual Mode Locomotive Project as a reliability and emissions upgrade for service to non-electrified territory, replacing aging P32 diesel / GE P32AC-DM locomotives with Siemens-designed dual-mode diesel-electric locomotives. [meeting_doc_176356#p015_c0003] [meeting_doc_176356#p015_c0004] [meeting_doc_111791#p039_c0003] The stated benefits were Tier IV clean-diesel performance, 84% lower nitrogen oxides and 86% lower particulate matter in diesel territory, electric-mode service over 102 miles instead of 4 miles in Manhattan, and 25,000 metric tons of annual carbon reduction. [meeting_doc_176356#p015_c0006] [meeting_doc_176356#p015_c0008]

## Order And Schedule

The original MNR base order was awarded to Siemens in March 2021 for 19 locomotives, with an 8-vehicle option also exercised; May 2023 materials showed Q2 2027 base-order completion, a $414 million budget/EAC, and potential options for MNR, LIRR, CDOT, and NYSDOT. [meeting_doc_111791#p039_c0003] [meeting_doc_111791#p036_c0002] By June 2025, the project table had expanded to a $738 million budget and Q2 2030 option-completion schedule, with listed orders/options for 27 MNR locomotives, 6 CTDOT locomotives, 13 Battery + AC MNR locomotives, and 22 LIRR locomotives. [meeting_doc_176356#p017_c0003] The June 2025 program summary separately listed 44 LIRR SC42 option-3 locomotives and future options for CDOT, MNR, NYDOT, and LIRR, so the option universe is broader than the original MNR base-plus-option order. [meeting_doc_176356#p034_c0004]

## Delivery And Watch Items

The June 2025 timeline said contract award was in March 2021, design was complete in spring 2023, prototype qualification testing at MNR occurred in December 2024, qualification testing was expected to finish in June 2025, and revenue service was projected for July 2025. [meeting_doc_176356#p016_c0003] At that time, locomotives 303 and 304 had been delivered and were undergoing commissioning/dynamic testing, with three more locomotives expected in August 2025. [meeting_doc_176356#p016_c0003] The IEC said production was on schedule and the first two locomotives arrived earlier than planned, but it flagged software issues on MNR-property testing as a possible delay to conditional acceptance of the first two clean-diesel locomotives. [meeting_doc_176356#p036_c0007] [meeting_doc_176356#p039_c0006] It also noted that Diesel Exhaust Fluid storage/dispensing was outside the locomotive project and that Metro-North had identified a temporary solution so in-service testing of first arrivals could proceed. [meeting_doc_176356#p038_c0006] For the Battery + AC option, the IEC called battery performance and longevity key challenges while the team worked through design-phase options to maximize battery efficiency. [meeting_doc_176356#p039_c0008]
