## Scope And Program Context

42nd Street Transit Improvements was a 2019 Better Buses Action Plan project for 42nd Street in Manhattan. The Action Plan listed the item as "06 42nd St, 12th Ave to FDR Dr" and said 42nd Street had been identified in 2017 as a Bus Forward priority corridor because of slow bus speeds; the project would investigate upgrades and extensions to existing curbside bus lanes and safety improvements along the corridor. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0005] The community-board material tied the project to the Mayor's 2019 State of the City, the April 2019 Better Buses Action Plan, a goal to "Improve bus speeds 25% by 2020," and 24 priority projects announced for 2019 across all five boroughs. [42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p004_c0002]

The corridor scope was crosstown. The September update said the plan extended from 12 th Ave to FDR Drive, and the Action Plan listed a corridor length of 2.0 miles. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p004_c0002] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006] The Action Plan route list named M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, SIM26, and SIM30, with total daily ridership of 16,000 and average bus speeds of 4.2 mph (AM) / 2.9 mph (PM). [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006] The community-board decks framed the corridor more broadly as used by 8 different MTA bus routes across 3 boroughs: M42 Manhattan Route, X68 Queens Route, and 6 Staten Island Express Bus Routes, with about 16,000 daily MTA bus passengers and up to 210 buses per hour. [42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p007_c0003]

## Service Need

The September CB6 update described 42 nd St as an important crosstown transit corridor carrying 8 MTA bus routes with 16,000 daily riders, with AM/PM bus speeds of 4.0 MPH compared to a citywide average of 7.7 MPH. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p004_c0002] The same deck said buses occupied 33% of street space but carried 79% of people. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p005_c0002] For the M42, it said bus travel times were nearly double overnight times through the day and that M42 buses spent more than 40% of their time stalled in traffic, at red lights, or crawling. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p006_c0005] It also described running times varying by 20-40 minutes, leading to bunching, unpredictable waits, and crowding. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p007_c0002]

## Proposed Design

The June CB6 deck said fieldwork and video analysis found underused bus lanes because of very narrow 9' width and occasional blockages, along with common pickup/drop-off activity and short-duration commercial loading. [42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p015_c0002] It also said 42 St was marked for 2 travel lanes with 1 curbside bus lane in each direction, in effect 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM except Sunday, but rarely functioned that way because of loading needs and traffic conditions. [42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p018_c0003]

The proposed curb and lane rules shifted toward offset bus lanes and more explicit curb management: offset bus lanes would be in effect 24/7; curbside bus lanes would be in effect 7 AM - 7 PM all days except at bus stops or other existing No Standing Anytime locations; 7 PM - 7 AM would be allocated to metered commercial/truck loading; and other curb space would be allocated to metered commercial/truck loading, hotel loading zones, and authorized parking. [42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p025_c0002] Other proposed elements included consolidating Lex Ave and 3 rd Ave stops to a single stop in each direction, studying new split signal phasing for the EB left turn from 42 nd St onto 8 th Ave, and proposed bus queue jump signals at 2 nd Ave, 3 rd Ave, Lexington Ave, and 10 th Ave. [42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p022_c0003] [42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p023_c0003] The CB5 turn-restriction table listed changes at 7 Av, 6 Av, and Lexington Av, including all-times restrictions for several turns and extended bus-excepted restrictions at Lexington Av. [42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p023_c0002]

By the September update, the design examples included quick kurb, 7s bus queue jump signals, offset bus lane all times, curbside bus lane 7A-7P, truck-loading windows, and a relocated EB bus stop around Lexington Av and 3 Av. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p012_c0002]

## Community-Board And Source-Time Status

The staged source set includes presentations to Community Board 6 on June 3, 2019, Community Board 4 on June 19, 2019, Community Board 5 on June 24, 2019, and an update to Community Board 6 on September 4, 2019. [42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p001_c0002] [42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p001_c0002] [42nd_st_cb5_jun242019#p001_c0002] [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p001_c0002] The June schedule described corridor design presentations in June 2019, "Summer 2019" adjustments and street design implementation, and "Fall 2019" post-implementation data collection and monitoring. [42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p027_c0002] The September update refined that schedule to July-August 2019 plan refinement and updates, September 2019 presentation update to CB 6, September-October 2019 project implementation including new markings and signage, and Fall 2019-Spring 2020 post-implementation data collection and monitoring. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p021_c0002]

## Caveats And Open Questions

The cited sources support planned/proposed and source-time implementation status through September 4, 2019; they do not by themselves verify final build-out or post-implementation results. The route lists differ by source: the Action Plan names M42 plus SIM routes, while the community-board material also names X68 and describes 8 routes across 3 boroughs. M101/M102/M103 appear in related records as transfer context at Lexington Ave and 42 St, not in the Action Plan route-served list cited above. Dense OCR figure/table blocks carry several treatment details; turn-restriction specifics and quick-kurb locations should be checked against slide images before converting them into stricter structured records.
