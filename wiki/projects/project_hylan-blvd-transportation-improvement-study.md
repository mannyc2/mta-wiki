## Study Scope

The Hylan Boulevard Transportation Improvement Study was a corridor planning effort presented by MTA New York City Transit and NYC DOT for Hylan Boulevard and Richmond Avenue in Staten Island. The source set frames it as a public study process, beginning with a December 2, 2010 public meeting and continuing through June 2011, September 2011, March 2012, and Newsletter 2 / Winter 2012 materials. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p001_c0001] [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p001_c0002] [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p001_c0004] [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p001_c0006] [201106_hylan_slides#p001_c0003] [201106_hylan_slides#p001_c0005] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p001_c0003] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p001_c0005] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0001] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0002]

The study corridor was broader than a single route. June 2011 materials describe the "Hylan Boulevard/Richmond Avenue Study Corridor" with a proposed SBS route, Staten Island Railway stations, transit hubs, and four typical study locations. [201106_hylan_slides#p005_c0005] September 2011 materials likewise show a Hylan Boulevard/Richmond Avenue proposed SBS route and typical locations with different land-use and parking conditions. [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p002_c0002]

## Why It Was Studied

The project was framed around traffic congestion, long transit commute times, bus reliability, and safety. The December 2010 and September 2011 materials state that Hylan Blvd was "the only continuous North-South route on the south shore," carried 28,000 vehicles on a typical weekday, had congested intersections causing travel time delays, and had safety needs for motorists, transit riders, and pedestrians. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p004_c0004] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p004_c0004] The same problem statement says 72% of transit commuters had trips longer than 60 minutes, 20% of people in the Hylan Blvd corridor commuted by transit, one third of SI bus commuters lived along Hylan Blvd, S78 and S79 local buses carried 16,000 trips/day, and eight express bus routes carried 15,000 trips/day. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p004_c0004] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p004_c0006]

The stated goals were to improve traffic flow, reduce bus travel time and improve reliability, improve transit access to Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Staten Island destinations, and improve safety for all corridor users. Earlier materials also included maintaining access to Hylan Boulevard or Hylan Blvd businesses as a project goal. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p005_c0004] [201106_hylan_slides#p003_c0004] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p004_c0008] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p004_c0004]

## Study Process

The study process combined data collection, alternative development, public input, and implementation planning. December 2010 materials list traffic volume and turning movement counts, a parking survey, transit ridership analysis, a local merchant survey, and stakeholder/public feedback as data-collection inputs. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p010_c0006] The next steps were to develop two or three alternatives, evaluate effects on transit travel times, traffic flow, pedestrian and traffic safety, parking, delivery access, and other stakeholder issues, then select a preferred alternative and evaluate effects at 25 intersections. [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p011_c0005] [2010_12_02_brt_hylan_open_house_slides#p013_c0005]

By June 2011, the study said "Understand the Corridor" was complete, "Identify Potential Options" was in progress, a draft concept plan was expected in summer 2011, a preferred plan with stakeholders in fall 2011, and project implementation was targeted for 2012 or 2013. [201106_hylan_slides#p004_c0005] [201106_hylan_slides#p004_c0007] [201106_hylan_slides#p004_c0009] [201106_hylan_slides#p004_c0011] [201106_hylan_slides#p004_c0013] Winter 2012 materials show the process at "Present Final Plan (Winter/Spring 2012)" with "Implement the Project (Fall 2012-2013)" still ahead. [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p002_c0015] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p002_c0017] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p002_c0018]

## Proposed And Recommended Improvements

The study's toolbox included traffic and safety treatments, bus service changes, and technology. September 2011 materials list left-turn lane improvement projects, signal timing optimization, advanced signals, pedestrian islands, sidewalks, wider medians, curb or offset bus lanes, simplified service patterns, longer stop spacing, streamlined route paths, Transit Signal Priority, real-time bus information, and an advanced payment system. [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p003_c0001] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p003_c0003] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p003_c0008] [201109_brt_hylan_introductory_info#p003_c0013]

The September 2011 proposed improvements included a peak-period bus lane from Steuben Street to Midland Avenue, in effect 6am - 9am and 3pm - 7pm Monday thru Friday, for "S79 SBS and all local and express bus services." The same block proposes four Select Bus Service stations between Steuben Street and Midland Avenue, faster fare collection initially for S79 SBS, and real-time bus arrival information. [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p001_c0001] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p001_c0005] It also proposed Hylan Boulevard pedestrian and roadway work such as resurfacing, median upgrades where needed, worn pavement marking replacement, pedestrian ramps, sidewalk reconstruction, and median pedestrian refuges where feasible. [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p001_c0006] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p001_c0007]

Other September 2011 boards extended the treatment set along the corridor: Midland Avenue to Tysens Lane included Transit Signal Priority, a long-term bus lay-by, and traffic signal timing improvements; Tysens Lane to Richmond Avenue included TSP, pedestrian refuge islands, possible landscaped medians, bus stop spacing changes, sidewalk construction near bus stops, and horizontal alignment changes within the existing right of way; Richmond Avenue included left-turn lane, TSP, pedestrian refuges, and seven SBS stations between Hylan Boulevard and SI Mall. [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p005_c0001] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p005_c0003] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p005_c0004] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p005_c0005] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p006_c0001] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p006_c0006] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p007_c0001] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p007_c0005] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p007_c0007] [201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p007_c0008]

By March 2012, the proposed improvements were summarized as speeding S79 service with fewer stops and a more direct route, providing better public information, adding bus lanes in select areas, optimizing traffic signals, completing Hylan Boulevard left-turn bay capital projects, adding left-turn bays on Richmond Avenue, and implementing pedestrian safety enhancements throughout the corridor. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p007_c0004] The bus-lane element was narrowed to "2 miles between Lincoln Avenue and Steuben Street" on Hylan Boulevard, described as the "Express Route to the Bridge." [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p012_c0004] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p012_c0005] Traffic signal improvements included optimizing signal timings throughout the project area, adding Transit Signal Priority along the S79 route, and piloting an advance signal treatment at key congested intersections. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p016_c0004] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p016_c0005] Pedestrian safety improvements included extending medians into crosswalks to provide pedestrian refuge at 9 locations, constructing sidewalks where needed to connect to bus stops, repairing damaged sidewalks, installing curb ramps, and constructing a median bus station at Richmond and Yukon Aves. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p019_c0004] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p019_c0005]

## Relationship To S79 SBS

The study is closely related to, but not identical with, the S79 SBS record. In June 2011, the bus service plan said the proposed Select Bus Service would replace local S79 service, with S78 and S59 continuing to serve local stops, SBS stations at high-ridership and key transfer locations, faster routing near Staten Island Mall and Eltingville Transit Center, and faster fare collection. [201106_hylan_slides#p022_c0004] [201106_hylan_slides#p022_c0005] [201106_hylan_slides#p022_c0006] In March 2012, short-term bus service improvements included introducing limited stop spacing on S79, rerouting S78 via Steuben Street, and adjusting S79 for more direct trips near Staten Island Mall and Eltingville Transit Center. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p008_c0004] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p008_c0005]

Winter 2012 materials explicitly present "S79 SELECT BUS SERVICE IMPROVEMENTS COMING IN 2012" within the Hylan Boulevard Transportation Improvement Study. The listed improvements include streamlined S79 service with fewer stops and a more direct route, optimized signal timing, TSP, bus lanes, pedestrian refuge islands, advance signals, upgraded medians, sidewalk improvements, left turn bays, and improved bus stations. [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0001] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0003] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0004] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p001_c0005] The same newsletter maps corridor improvements and notes S79 SBS routing details at Staten Island Mall, Richmond Avenue, and Eltingville Transit Center. [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0001] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0003] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0009] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0012] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0014] [2012_sbs_hylan_winter_newsletter#p003_c0015]

## Performance And Safety Evidence

The staged sources include both existing-condition evidence and projected plan benefits. Existing-condition slides identify AM congestion primarily northbound between Guyon and Seaver Avenues, PM congestion heaviest at New Dorp Lane and Tysens Lane, and bus passenger delays caused by traffic congestion, frequent express bus stops, stopped buses, parked cars, narrow lanes, and paying fare on bus. [201106_hylan_slides#p007_c0004] [201106_hylan_slides#p007_c0005] [201106_hylan_slides#p008_c0004] [201106_hylan_slides#p008_c0005] [201106_hylan_slides#p009_c0004] [201106_hylan_slides#p009_c0006] The March 2012 recommended-plan benefits were stated as 20% faster bus trips, more predictable bus arrivals, less delay at traffic signals, minimal impact for general traffic, safer pedestrian crossings, and better access to bus stops. These should be read as plan benefits stated in the presentation, not as observed after-implementation outcomes. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p023_c0003] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p023_c0004]

## Caveats And Open Questions

The 20% faster bus trips claim is presented as a "Benefits of Recommended Plan" statement, not as a measured post-implementation result in these staged sources. [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p023_c0003] [2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p023_c0004]

The implementation schedule changes across source time: target 2013 in December 2010, target 2012 or 2013 in June/September 2011, Fall 2012-2013 in Winter 2012, and March 2012 next steps of street changes in summer 2012, bus service changes in September 2012, and long-term improvements in 2013.
