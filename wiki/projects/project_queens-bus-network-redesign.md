Wave 13 meeting records add useful board-process context to the Queens Bus Network Redesign. The
April 2024 public-hearing request describes the plan as a redesign to improve connections,
reliability, travel speed, and service simplicity through more direct routes, bus stop balancing,
key-route frequency, and expanded bus priority. It also says MTA partnered with NYC DOT to identify
24 Queens priority corridors for possible bus-priority improvements, with phased implementation then
targeted for mid-2025. [meeting_doc_138431#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_138431#p001_c0006]

The meeting-doc trail also shows the project moving from planning into implementation and budgeting.
The November financial plan tied higher NYCT and MTA Bus expenses to QBNR implementation, including
funding for 47 additional bus operators and 10 managers at NYCT and 78 additional MTA Bus positions.
In January 2025, staff were still seeking Board approval for the Proposed Final Plan Addendum, with
implementation planned in two phases for summer and fall 2025 pending approval. [meeting_doc_157946#p018_c0005]
