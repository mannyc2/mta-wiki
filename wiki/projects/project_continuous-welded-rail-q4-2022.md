## Overview

Continuous Welded Rail (CWR) is tracked here as a Long Island Rail Road Maintenance of Way track-maintenance program, reported in quarterly Track, Structures, Stations and Facilities updates. The record spans Q4 2022, Q2 2023, and Q3 2023 source material. [meeting_doc_104766#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_115196#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_124271#p001_c0002]

In Q4 2022, LIRR reported CWR as a systemwide program covering 1,017 rail miles excluding yards. The quarter planned 40,000 lineal ft. and installed 54,262 lineal ft., or 136% of goal. The listed work included Atlantic Hall to Valley, Hempstead Bellerose to Park 2, and added Mainline locations at Queens to Carle Place and Divide 2 to Divide 3. [meeting_doc_104766#p002_c0003]

By the Q2 2023 update, the CWR work had shifted to a different set of locations, including Atlantic Brook to Nostrand Ave., Atlantic Beaver Curve, Mainline Divide 4 to Divide 6, Mainline Metro-Harold, Mainline Mail Dock Curve, Port Jefferson PJ Single, Port Jefferson Divide 4 to Divide 5, and Valley Interlocking. The source-stated total for the line items was 95,032 lined ft. planned and 15,203 lined ft. installed, or 16% of goal. [meeting_doc_115196#p002_c0003]

The Q3 2023 update shows substantial progress on that 2023 work package: 112,410 lineal ft. planned and 82,581 lineal ft. installed, or 73% of goal. Completed or near-complete locations included Valley Interlocking at 6,450 of 6,450 lineal ft., Port Jefferson PJ Single at 35,880 of 35,800 lineal ft., Mainline Divide 4 to Divide 6 at 4,696 of 4,600 lineal ft., and Atlantic Beaver Curve at 2,118 of 2,200 lineal ft. Slower-moving items included Atlantic Brook to Nostrand Ave. at 0 installed, Mainline Mail Dock Curve at 1,993 of 9,626 lineal ft., and Port Jefferson Divide 4 to Divide 5 at 4,604 of 10,004 lineal ft. [meeting_doc_124271#p002_c0003]

## Reading Notes

The Q2 2023 source has an internal inconsistency: the CWR heading says 62,800 lined ft. planned, while the line-item total says 95,032 lined ft. planned. [meeting_doc_115196#p002_c0003] The sources also alternate between "lineal ft." and OCR'd "lined ft."; this page preserves those source-time terms rather than normalizing the unit labels.
