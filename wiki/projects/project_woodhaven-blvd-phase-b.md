## Project Role

Woodhaven Blvd Phase B is the design-stage capital project for Woodhaven Boulevard from Union Turnpike to 107 Av. The September and December 2024 capital-project decks list it with CB5, CB6, CB9, and CB10, estimated construction in 2026, alongside two related Woodhaven capital projects: Woodhaven Blvd / Metropolitan Av and Woodhaven Blvd Phase C. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p016_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p016_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb6_sept2024#p016_c0002]

The Phase B scope reads as a capital follow-up to an existing bus-lane corridor, not a change to bus-lane alignment or hours. The project would maintain the alignment and operational hours of the bus lanes; upgrade 16 bus stops, 17 sidewalk extensions, 12 shortened crossings, and 38 medians/median tips; and add 7 crossings, 3 raised crosswalks, more than 75 street trees, and more than 10,000 square feet of landscaped area. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p021_c0003] The CB6 deck repeats the same treatment package. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb6_sept2024#p021_c0003]

## Design Details

The site-plan material reinforces the pedestrian-accessibility emphasis. One Phase B plan sheet identifies ADA-compliant median pedestrian refuges, sidewalk extensions, a raised crosswalk, refurbished medians and landscaping, reconstructed ADA-compliant sidewalk, and the bus-lane layer. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p022_c0002]

## Schedule

The schedule should be read as document-time planning guidance from the 2024 decks. Phase B was listed as in Design, with estimated construction in 2026. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p016_c0002] The next-steps slides list design completion in mid-2025 and construction start in early 2026; the CB5 and CB6 versions carry the same milestone text. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p025_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p033_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb6_sept2024#p036_c0002]

## Corridor Context

The same decks describe Woodhaven Boulevard as a major Q52/Q53 SBS corridor with more than 30,000 daily riders, connections to Queens Boulevard and the Rockaways, 8 subway lines, 20 local/express bus routes, and Vision Zero Priority Corridor status since 2019. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p004_c0002] They treat the 2017 Q52/Q53 SBS launch as the prior bus-priority build-out: 6.85 miles of Woodhaven bus lanes, split into offset, median, and curbside alignments from Queens Boulevard to North Conduit Av. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb10_dec2024#p005_c0002]

Those 2017 figures are context for the corridor, not measured impacts of Phase B itself. The CB5 deck says bus travel times improved by an average of 3 minutes in each direction, on-time performance improved up to 80%, and northbound Q52/Q53 late arrivals fell from 52% of the time to 10% of the time in a 2019-vs-2017 comparison. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p011_c0002] Corridor safety context is also reported separately: the deck says 56 people were killed or severely injured on Woodhaven Boulevard in 2019-2023, compared with 69 in 2014-2018, a 19% decrease; another slide lists pedestrian injuries down 20%, cyclist injuries down 80%, motor-vehicle crashes down 36%, and total incidents down 33%. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p004_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p012_c0003]
