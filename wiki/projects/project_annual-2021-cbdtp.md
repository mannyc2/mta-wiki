## Overview

The Central Business District Tolling Program (CBDTP) is MTA Bridges and Tunnels' congestion-pricing tolling program for Manhattan's Central Business District. MTA materials describe it as authorized by the 2019 MTA Reform and Traffic Mobility Act and designed to reduce congestion in the CBD, generate net revenue for $15 billion of MTA capital work, and toll vehicles entering or remaining in Manhattan south of and inclusive of 60th Street, excluding the FDR Drive and West Side Highway corridors. [meeting_doc_102771#p048_c0014] The 2021 annual report also frames CBDTP as intended to reduce traffic congestion, improve air quality, promote mass transit usage, and raise revenue for key capital projects. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p038_c0003]

## Design, Review, And Launch

In 2021, B&T was still in design and environmental review. It continued preliminary design of tolling infrastructure, held over 25 meetings, logged over 7,000 public comments, and advanced the Federal Highway Administration environmental assessment and related traffic study. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p038_c0003] A December 2022 staff summary says FHWA approval to proceed with the EA came in March 2021; the EA was released on August 10, 2022; the comment period closed on September 23, 2022; and a favorable FHWA decision would lead to a FONSI and contractor notice to proceed. [meeting_doc_102771#p049_c0003]

By the 2024-2027 financial plan, FHWA had issued a favorable FONSI on June 26, 2023, and B&T had issued notice to proceed; the contractor then had up to 310 days to finish design, development, testing, and installation. [meeting_doc_127706#p017_c0001] The same plan projected CBDTP net revenue of $400 million in 2024 and $1 billion annually beginning in 2025 to support the MTA Capital Program, while another financial-plan section flagged litigation risk around the scheduled mid-2024 implementation and timing of capital-program revenues. [meeting_doc_127706#p017_c0003] [meeting_doc_127701#p009_c0009]

The program then hit a 2024 pause. June 2024 Board material says New York State announced a pause on June 5, 2024, and that the MTA Reform and Traffic Mobility Act remained in effect; the Board extended implementation from about June 2024 until after execution of the required tolling agreement among the project sponsors and FHWA. [meeting_doc_144021#p005_c0001] [meeting_doc_144021#p006_c0008] November 2024 material adopted a toll-rate phase-in: 2025-2027 at 60% of March 2024 adopted rates, 2028-2030 at 80%, and full March 2024 rates beginning in 2031. [meeting_doc_157771#p004_c0004]

The infrastructure plan described a Congestion Relief Zone where vehicles are charged upon entry, with detection points at entrances, exits, and excluded roadways and a northern boundary generally between 60th and 61st Streets. [meeting_doc_134026#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_134026#p005_c0002] It also shows public-facing signage for the zone and describes enforcement approaches for occupied vehicles, parked vehicles, and license-plate obstruction. [meeting_doc_134026#p007_c0007] [meeting_doc_134026#p015_c0003] [meeting_doc_134026#p016_c0004] [meeting_doc_134026#p016_c0005]

CBDTP launched on January 5, 2025, with MTA Bridges and Tunnels tolling vehicles entering the Congestion Relief Zone. [meeting_doc_165006#p076_c0002] Early 2025 B&T updates state that it continued to reduce congestion through March, with March CRZ daily average entries of 496,009 and total CBD entries 13% below the historical March average. [meeting_doc_170871#p047_c0003] [meeting_doc_170871#p047_c0004] April reporting says the program had been collecting tolls for nearly four months; despite a roughly 2% month-to-month rise in CRZ entries from March to April, CBD entries remained 12% below the historical April average. [meeting_doc_174221#p070_c0002]

## Caveats

Status is source-time dependent: inspected records describe CBDTP as design-stage in 2021, scheduled for mid-2024 in 2023 financial-plan material, paused in June 2024, phased in by November 2024 Board action, and launched on January 5, 2025. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p038_c0003] [meeting_doc_127701#p009_c0009] [meeting_doc_144021#p005_c0001] [meeting_doc_157771#p004_c0004] [meeting_doc_165006#p076_c0002]

The post-launch outcome evidence used here is early: March and April 2025 B&T updates only, not a long-term evaluation. [meeting_doc_170871#p047_c0003] [meeting_doc_174221#p070_c0002]
