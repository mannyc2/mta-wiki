## Overview

The Student OMNY Card Program shifted student fare media from Student MetroCard toward OMNY in 2024: MTA meeting minutes first recorded that New York City school students would begin receiving Student OMNY cards and that the cards offered expanded benefits. [meeting_doc_151651#p004_c0006] By the fall update, MTA described schoolchildren tapping the new cards on buses, said the cards had been distributed with New York City Public Schools, and stated that they were active 365 days a year and 7 days a week. [meeting_doc_151656#p004_c0006]

MTA framed the program as an expansion of the Student MetroCard because it allowed students to use transit for after-school and weekend activities, and later described the rollout as giving New York City public school students access to transportation for after-school, weekend, and summer activities. [meeting_doc_151656#p004_c0006] [meeting_doc_160446#p004_c0011]

By the September report, MTA stated that 1.5 million Student OMNY cards had been distributed, including to public school students. [meeting_doc_151656#p004_c0007] [meeting_doc_151656#p004_c0008] In the same broader OMNY transition, MTA said Student OMNY and Reduced-Fare OMNY together expanded OMNY services to approximately 3 million people and required public outreach so customers would not feel left behind. [meeting_doc_160446#p005_c0002]
