## Context

Grand St Bridge Reconstruction appears in the late-2024 Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority presentation deck as a related DOT project, with a formal environmental review process expected to begin in 2025. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p013_c0002]

The bridge reconstruction is shown alongside two other nearby DOT projects: the Queens Boulevard Capital Project, described as pedestrian, bike, and bus improvements starting construction in Fall 2026, and the Grand St, Metropolitan Av, Morgan Av Street Improvement Project, described as addressing blocked bike lanes, bus stops, sidewalks, poor visibility, and slow bus speeds. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p013_c0002]

## Transit Relevance

The surrounding Grand St/Grand Av study area runs from Metropolitan Av to Queens Blvd, carries over 51,000 daily bus passengers on the Q54, Q58, and Q59, and is connected to the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot and Central Maintenance Facility, which the deck says serves 10 routes. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p004_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p004_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p004_c0002]

The Grand Avenue Bus Depot slide identifies the bridge as an operating constraint: it says truck traffic and congestion from Grand St Bridge create significant pull-in and pull-out difficulties, resulting in longer wait times and less reliable service for riders. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p007_c0005] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p007_c0005] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p007_c0004]

That same depot slide says 1,032 buses pull in and out every weekday, including more than 80 movements during the busiest hour, and it says Grand Avenue improvements that prioritize bus service and depot access would result in more reliable, faster buses in an area lacking direct subway service. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p007_c0005] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p007_c0005] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p007_c0004]

## Relationship To The Bus-Priority Project

The bridge reconstruction should be read as a related capital or environmental-review item, not as the same thing as the Grand St/Grand Av bus-priority implementation schedule, because the DOT Projects slide gives the bridge only the 2025 environmental-review milestone while the bus-priority next-steps slide separately projects implementation in late 2025 or 2026 subject to feedback and analysis. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p022_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p022_c0003]

The bus-priority deck places Grand St/Grand Av in the Queens bus-priority corridor context and says MTA is coordinating with NYC DOT on targeted corridors including Grand Av/Grand St as part of the Queens Bus Network Redesign context.
