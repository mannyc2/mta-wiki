By the completed wave 11 after-report evidence, M79 SBS had moved from planning into post-launch
evaluation. The service launched on May 21, 2017, replacing M79 Local service on 79th Street from
FDR Drive to Riverside Drive, and the report describes a just-over-two-mile route serving 12,500
daily bus riders with bus lanes, signal timing improvements, off-board fare payment, and real-time
passenger information. [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p002_c0003]

The launch package included 0.75 miles of bus lanes, 12 Bus Time/WalkNYC information displays, and
10 intersections with signal timing improvements. The after report summarizes results as about 8%
faster bus travel times, ridership up as much as 9% from June 2017 to June 2018, and 96% rider
satisfaction compared with 84% for pre-SBS M79 Local service. [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p008_c0003]
[brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p008_c0004] [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p008_c0005]
[brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p015_c0002] [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p018_c0002]
[brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p023_c0002]

Safety and capital follow-up remained part of the M79 story: the after report cites a 19% reduction
in total crashes and a 41% reduction in pedestrian-injury crashes, then describes a 79th Street SBS
capital project with bus bulbs, curb extensions, pedestrian islands, and bus pads.
