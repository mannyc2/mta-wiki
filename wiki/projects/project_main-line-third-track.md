## Overview

The LIRR Main Line Third Track is described as completed through an October 3, 2022 celebration for the third and final segment between Floral Park and Hicksville. [meeting_doc_104741#p005_c0005]

The project added a 9.8-mile span that the source says gave LIRR more operational flexibility, improved safety, minimized disruptions, and supported a 41% systemwide service increase once full GCM service went live. [meeting_doc_104741#p005_c0005]

The same source says the addition created two-way and reverse-peak service along the LIRR Main Line and framed that service pattern as supporting regional economic growth. [meeting_doc_104741#p005_c0005]

## Delivery Notes

Chair Lieber highlighted the elimination of eight grade crossings as part of the Third Track project. [meeting_doc_100261#p004_c0015]

A later cost-savings slide labels LIRR Third Track and says proactive management led to on-time completion $100 million under budget. [meeting_doc_157976#p014_c0004] [meeting_doc_157976#p014_c0005]

## Program Context

One project slide places LIRR Third Track under the "Strengthen & Expand the Network" theme. [meeting_doc_131981#p018_c0001] [meeting_doc_131981#p018_c0002]

Another slide frames LIRR Third Track under "Strengthen & Expand the Network" and "Knocking out Capital Projects." [meeting_doc_147341#p004_c0001] [meeting_doc_147341#p004_c0002] [meeting_doc_147341#p004_c0003]

A cost-savings slide places LIRR Third Track alongside "Committed to Cost Savings." [meeting_doc_157981#p015_c0003] [meeting_doc_157981#p015_c0004]

## Overview

The October 24, 2022 committee minutes describe the LIRR Main Line Third Track as the third and final section between Carle Place and Hicksville, opened on Monday, October 3, with Governor Hochul presiding at a Westbury ribbon-cutting ceremony. [meeting_doc_100306#p004_c0003]

The December 2022 performance report describes the same milestone as completion of the third and final segment of the long-awaited, fully operational third track between Floral Park and Hicksville. [meeting_doc_102681#p006_c0007]

The project is described with two closely related length figures: the December report calls it a 9.8-mile span, while the November committee minutes refer to improvements along ten miles of Main Line track. [meeting_doc_102681#p006_c0007] [meeting_doc_102686#p009_c0005]

## Delivery And Benefits

The October minutes say the project opened on time and $100 million under budget, and they frame its benefits as especially important together with Grand Central Madison service expected before the end of 2022. [meeting_doc_100306#p004_c0003]

The December report says the 9.8-mile span gives LIRR more operational flexibility to improve safety, minimize disruptions, increase systemwide service by 41% with full service to Grand Central, and create true two-way and reverse-peak service along the Main Line. [meeting_doc_102681#p006_c0007] [meeting_doc_102681#p007_c0001]

The November minutes place Third Track in a larger C&D narrative that also includes the 2018 Double Track investment, Grand Central Madison, Jamaica Capacity Improvements, and Harold Interlocking work. [meeting_doc_102686#p009_c0005]

## Grand Central Madison Context

The October minutes connect Third Track's customer and operations benefits to the coming opening of Grand Central Madison service before the end of 2022. [meeting_doc_100306#p004_c0003]

The October minutes also say LIRR was using pre-revenue testing, emergency drills, and physical-characteristics training to prepare crews for the new Grand Central Madison route and territory. [meeting_doc_100306#p004_c0003]

The November minutes say Grand Central Madison would transform LIRR service by increasing peak service by 40% and off-peak service by 65%, while also describing Third Track as fully operational, on time, and under budget. [meeting_doc_102686#p009_c0005]
