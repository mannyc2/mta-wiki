## Source-Time Snapshot

As of the March 2024 source packet, LIRR described the St. Albans work as a comprehensive ADA station upgrade: Phase 1 was scheduled for six weekends beginning April 6 and 7, and Phase 2 construction was expected to begin in the fall. [meeting_doc_135261#p008_c0013] The March 25 committee minutes separately record the same phase sequence, but only say Phase 1 would begin in April. [meeting_doc_138201#p004_c0001] [meeting_doc_138201#p004_c0006]

Upon completion, the listed customer-facing scope included a first station elevator for St. Albans, a reconstructed pedestrian tunnel, new HelpPoint emergency kiosks, enhanced security and communication systems, and upgrades to lighting, parking, sidewalks, and staircases. [meeting_doc_135261#p008_c0014] [meeting_doc_135261#p009_c0001]

For the work period, the March 2024 packet says busing would be in place during weekend outages and directs affected customers to TrainTime or mta.info for schedules and travel information. [meeting_doc_135261#p009_c0002] [meeting_doc_135261#p009_c0004]

## Community Context

LIRR placed St. Albans within a broader group of ADA and station projects in Queens, Nassau, and Suffolk, saying it had been meeting with elected officials, municipalities, civic associations, local agencies, and nearby residents while designs were still in early stages. [meeting_doc_135261#p015_c0003] [meeting_doc_135261#p015_c0004] St. Albans was one of the station communities listed for this outreach, and the packet includes an illustrative rendering of the upgraded St. Albans LIRR Station in Queens. [meeting_doc_135261#p015_c0005] [meeting_doc_135261#p015_c0008]
