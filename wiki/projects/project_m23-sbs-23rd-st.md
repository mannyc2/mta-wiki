## Project Need

M23 Select Bus Service is the 23rd Street Manhattan crosstown SBS project, introduced in the October 13, 2015 kickoff materials and tied to the M23 bus route on 23rd Street. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p001_c0001] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p001_c0002] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p005_c0001] The source set describes the route as carrying 15,000 daily M23 passengers, offering access to PATH and subway lines, connecting to 14 local and SBS buses, and sharing the corridor with 28 express buses for part of their routes. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p005_c0002]

The need case was framed around slow crosstown service: the kickoff deck says over 25% of a trip was spent boarding passengers and about 90% was spent stopped or stuck in traffic. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p006_c0002] A later CB4 draft-plan deck broke M23 travel time into In-Motion 40%, Stopped in Traffic 23%, At Bus Stop 28%, and Crawling <2.5mph 9%, with the callout that the bus was not moving 51% of the time. [brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p007_c0002] Street conditions were also part of the case: roadway width was listed as 52' - 66', with irregular lane widths causing frequent lane changing and buses at stops blocking through traffic. [brt_m23_cb4_apr2016#p013_c0002]

The project also sat inside a safety frame. The kickoff deck identifies the Manhattan Action Plan's 23rd Street Priority Corridor and four priority intersections. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p008_c0002] Its 2009-2013 corridor table lists Ped 226 total injuries, 30 severe injuries, 4 fatalities, and 34 KSI; Bike 88 total injuries, 5 severe injuries, 1 fatality, and 6 KSI; and Vehicle 400 total injuries, 30 severe injuries, and 30 KSI. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p008_c0003]

## Treatments

The SBS package was presented as a set of improvements for faster and more reliable bus service. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p010_c0002] The kickoff treatment set included dedicated bus lanes, signal priority for buses, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, improved station amenities, and pedestrian safety improvements. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0002] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0004] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0010] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0012] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0002] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0007] The same source ties these treatments to faster bus rides, fewer bus-traffic conflicts, less time stopped at red lights, less time waiting at bus stops, better visibility, and clearer, shorter pedestrian crossings. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0006] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0007] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0009] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p011_c0015] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0008] [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0009]

By the June 2016 final-plan materials, the bus-lane limits were listed as "Westbound bus lanes: 1 st Ave - 8 th Ave" and "Eastbound bus lanes: 10 th Ave - 2 nd Ave," with lanes in effect 24/7 except westbound between 7 th and 8 th Ave. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p004_c0002] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p004_c0003] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p004_c0005] Turn-restriction material lists existing restrictions at WB 9 th Ave, WB 7 th Ave, EB 6 th Ave, WB 5 th Ave, and EB/WB Park Ave S, then a combined existing/new list that adds EB 8 th Ave, Lexington Ave, Broadway, and RT 6 Ave. [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p007_c0003] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p008_c0002] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p008_c0003]

The stop plan shifted from current M23 stops to proposed M23 SBS stops in the June 2016 materials. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p006_c0001] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p006_c0003] Specific stop changes included removing the westbound 5 Av stop, where existing stops were described as only 400' apart and the change was expected to save time for 4,500 riders per day. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p007_c0001] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p007_c0002] The June CB6 deck also proposed removing the Lexington Ave stop, citing no bus or subway connection, lower ridership than adjacent stops, 500' spacing to Park Ave and 3 rd Ave, a time saving for 8,000 riders per day, and planned construction that would force the stop to be revoked for an extended period. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p008_c0001] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p008_c0002] At the east end, the 20 th St Loop stop was to remain, with the loop stop adjusted closer to the crosswalk and the 1 st Ave stop possibly pulled away from the intersection to accommodate fare-machine placement. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p009_c0001] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p009_c0002]

Real Time Passenger Information Signs were also part of the June materials. CB5 locations listed EB 6 th Av, EB Broadway, EB Park Av S, WB Park Av S, and WB Broadway; CB6 locations listed WB 20 th St/1 st Ave, WB 1 st Avenue, WB 2 nd Avenue, and WB 3 rd Avenue. [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p010_c0001] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p010_c0003] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p010_c0004] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p012_c0001] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p012_c0003] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p012_c0004]

## Chronology

The staged chronology starts with the October 13, 2015 kickoff presentation. [2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p001_c0002] The June 2016 outreach slide says the process included an October 2015 project kickoff with elected officials and on-street outreach to 500+ M23 customers, an October 2015-May 2016 online feedback portal collecting 100+ customer comments, a February 2016 CB 4, 5 and 6 Tri-Board Meeting, a March 2016 Business Loading Survey, April 2016 draft-plan presentations to Community Boards 4, 5 and 6, May 2016 additional stakeholder outreach, and June 2016 final-plan presentations to Community Boards. [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0003] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0005] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0007] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0009] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0011] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0013] [brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0015]

The June CB6 final-plan deck lists June-September project implementation, followed by 2017-beyond evaluation of SBS performance, study of more robust options if supported by the community, and continued community outreach. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0004] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0005] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0006] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0007] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0008] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0009] A later staged route index says 23rd Street Select Bus Service began service on November 6, 2016 on the M23 bus route in Manhattan, benefitting 15,000 daily riders. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001]

## Expected Benefits And Limits Of The Evidence

The project benefits were stated as improved bus speeds, reliability, and access to subway transfers; safety improvements in priority Vision Zero locations; maintained through traffic with turn lanes where needed; and minimal effects on loading and parking. [brt_m23_cb6_apr2016#p032_c0002] In this source set, those are planning or expected-benefit claims rather than a source-specific post-implementation performance evaluation.

## Caveats And Open Questions

The bus-lane limits changed across planning sources: the March 2016 CB5 draft lists eastbound bus lanes as 9 th Ave - 2 nd Ave, while June 2016 final-plan material lists 10 th Ave - 2 nd Ave. [brt_m23_cb5_mar2016#p015_c0003] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p004_c0003] The November 6, 2016 launch date comes from the staged `brt_route_index` routes page, not from the project planning decks themselves. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001] The checked M23 project sources state 2017-beyond evaluation as a next step, but the checked matching source set does not provide a post-implementation evaluation result. [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0006] [brt_m23_cb6_jun2016#p013_c0007]
