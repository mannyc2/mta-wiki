## Summary

The Rockaway Line Rehabilitation & Resiliency Project is a $393 million capital project to safeguard Rockaway Line bridges and viaducts and support reliability for the next 50+ years. [meeting_doc_160271#p001_c0005] The line is served by the A and S Shuttle trains, links the Rockaway Peninsula with the rest of New York City, and was described in December 2024 materials as carrying over 9,000 average weekday riders. [meeting_doc_160271#p001_c0004]

The core construction scope is the reconstruction of the elevated Hammels Wye and rehabilitation of electrical and mechanical systems on the South Channel Bridge. [meeting_doc_160271#p001_c0005] Finance Committee minutes give the planned outage window as January 17, 2025 through May 19, 2025, and state that a 17-week outage was needed because the work could not be completed through piecemeal outages. [meeting_doc_163201#p003_c0005]

By the January 2025 NYCT performance report, MTA described the project as having commenced on January 17 and as rebuilding significant portions of the Rockaway Line between the Rockaway peninsula and Howard Beach; that report says over 10,000 riders rely on the line daily to cross Jamaica Bay. [meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0003] The same report says much of the line's infrastructure is nearly 70 years old, with the South Channel Bridge and Hammels Wye especially vulnerable to extreme weather, and frames the project as hardening vulnerable segments while also doing state-of-good-repair work on track, stations, and electrical components. [meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0005]

## Rider Mitigation

MTA paired the outage with temporary fare and service mitigations. The December staff summary sought approval for fare promotions during the A Train outage, intended to provide alternatives between the Rockaway Peninsula and the rest of New York City. [meeting_doc_160271#p001_c0003] Those promotions included discounting peak and off-peak LIRR Far Rockaway tickets to $2.75 and replacing suspended S Rockaway Park Shuttle service with a special fare-free S Shuttle train serving peninsula stations between Far Rockaway-Mott Avenue and Rockaway Park-Beach 116 Street, except for the first and last outage weekends when modified free shuttle buses would replace it. [meeting_doc_160271#p002_c0001]

The January performance report adds the shuttle-bus pattern: Q97 nonstop between Howard Beach-JFK Airport and Far Rockaway-Mott Avenue, and Q109 between Howard Beach-JFK Airport and Beach 67 Street with stops at Broad Channel and Beach 90 Street. [meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0008] It also says extra QM15 service was added and that a subway shuttle would run between Rockaway Park and Far Rockaway to connect peninsula trips and shuttle buses. [meeting_doc_163121#p025_c0002]
