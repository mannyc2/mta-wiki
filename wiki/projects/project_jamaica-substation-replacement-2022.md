## Summary

The Jamaica Substation Replacement Project is a LIRR traction-power state-of-good-repair project in Richmond Hill, Queens, replacing an existing Jamaica Substation that MTA described as beyond its useful life. [meeting_doc_102911#p046_c0009] [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0005] In the December 2022 board materials, MTA Construction & Development requested approval to award Verde Electric Corp. a publicly advertised and competitively solicited design-build contract for $51,983,502 and a 45-month term. [meeting_doc_102911#p046_c0005] [meeting_doc_102911#p046_c0007]

## Context

MTA describes the substation as a critical DC power source for LIRR tracks with dense train traffic near Jamaica, with added demand from Grand Central Madison service and Jamaica Capacity Improvements. [meeting_doc_166926#p014_c0003] The replacement is being built near the existing substation, west of Jamaica Station in Richmond Hill at 132nd Street and 91st Avenue, with the old substation intended to remain in service until the new infrastructure is constructed and cut over. [meeting_doc_166926#p014_c0004]

## Scope

The contract scope covers design, fabrication, and installation of a new AC/DC traction power substation in a prefabricated modular building along the LIRR Mainline Branch in Queens. [meeting_doc_102911#p046_c0009] Later project descriptions specify a three-story modular building assembled on new foundations, with pre-installed power components including AC switchgear, rectifiers, DC switchgear, control cabinets, a motor generator, and associated equipment. [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0006] [meeting_doc_166926#p014_c0005] The work also includes retaining-wall and access-road reconstruction, new transformers outside the modular buildings, a new Con Edison feeder cable, and relocation of two existing feeders. [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0006] [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0007]

## Delivery Notes

The March 2024 project update says design-build construction started in December 2022 and was slated for completion in October 2025. [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0007] The same update says the old substation would be demolished only after the new substation had been fully tested and become operational, while the March 2025 description says decommissioning and demolition would follow successful acceptance testing and burn-in. [meeting_doc_135261#p014_c0007] [meeting_doc_166926#p014_c0005]
