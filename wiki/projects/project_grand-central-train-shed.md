Grand Central Train Shed is a Metro-North and MTA Construction & Development repair effort focused on the roof and supporting structure of the Grand Central Terminal Train Shed; MTA describes the train shed as both the railroad yard north of the tracks and a structure whose roof supports roadways and sidewalks spanning dozens of street-level blocks, helping commuter rail service pass through Grand Central. [meeting_doc_133296#p014_c0003]

The repair need is structural: MTA describes the train shed framing as steel columns, steel cap beams, steel filler beams, reinforced concrete deck, waterproofing membrane, and roadway pavement, and says the structure was in poor condition after deteriorated waterproofing allowed water intrusion to weaken concrete and metal elements. [meeting_doc_133296#p014_c0004] Sector 1 was underway in the February 2024 report, replacing about 70,000 square feet of Upper Level roof structure around 270 Park Avenue, with roadway demolition above, nearly 1,600 tons of below-street steel superstructure, removal of steel supports, and repair of girders and columns supporting the stringers and deck. [meeting_doc_133296#p014_c0006]

The project appears in MTA capital-program materials as an active construction effort: the April 26, 2023 board presentation names "Grand Central Train Shed" under "Strengthen & Expand Network" and shows train-shed interior construction conditions. [meeting_doc_109976#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_109976#p012_c0001] [meeting_doc_109976#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_109976#p012_c0003] By the November 18, 2024 Capital Program Update, "Grand Central Trainshed Phase 1" was reported as trending ahead of schedule and $19 million under budget. [meeting_doc_157976#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_157976#p016_c0011]
