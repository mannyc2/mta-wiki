## Project Role

The 79th Street SBS Capital Project is described as capital follow-up work to the 2017 M79 Select Bus Service launch, intended to add permanent street elements along the M79 SBS route after the initial street-improvement package. [better_buses#p001_b0001] [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p025_c0002]

In the fall 2018 CB7 and CB8 update decks, the project goal was to improve bus speeds, reliability, and pedestrian safety through bus bulbs, curb extensions, bus pads, and pedestrian islands along 79th Street. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p011_c0002] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p012_c0002]

The 2018 project timeline showed DDC beginning initial design in 2019, preliminary and final design in 2020-2021, and construction beginning in 2022. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p011_c0003] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p012_c0003]

## Why It Followed The 2017 Launch

The 2017 M79 SBS launch included improved fare collection at all stops, 0.75 miles of bus lanes, 12 new Bus Time/WalkNYC information displays, and signal timing improvements at 10 intersections. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p004_c0001] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p004_c0002] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p004_c0003] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p004_c0004] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p004_c0005]

The fall 2018 update reported that M79 SBS was on average 8% faster than previous M79 service, spent 21% less time stopped at bus stops, spent 5% less time stopped in traffic, had ridership up 9% one year after launch, and had 96% customer satisfaction compared with 84% for pre-SBS M79 service. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p006_c0002] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p006_c0002]

The 2020 after-report says the capital project would build on those 2017 improvements by using bus bulbs to reduce bus weaving, shorten pedestrian crossings, and expand stop and sidewalk space; curb extensions and pedestrian islands to shorten crossings and provide refuge; and bus pads to reinforce roadway pavement at stops. [brt_m79_after_report_feb2020#p025_c0002]

## 2018 Capital Scope

For the West Side in CB7, the 2018-19 capital project listed 3 bus bulbs, 6 curb extensions, 4 bus pads, and 1 pedestrian island. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p013_c0001] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p013_c0002]

CB7 site examples identified a bus bulb at W 79th Street and Broadway, concrete curb extensions at W 79th Street and Riverside Drive, a curb extension at W 79th Street and Central Park West, and two bus bulbs at W 79th Street and Amsterdam Avenue. [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p014_c0003] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p014_c0005] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p014_c0007] [brt_m79_cb7_nov2018#p014_c0009]

For the East Side in CB8, the 2018 capital project listed 1 bus bulb, 11 curb extensions, 6 bus pads, and 1 pedestrian island. [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p014_c0001] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p014_c0002]

CB8 site examples identified a 4-foot bus bulb or sidewalk widening at 79th Street and 5th Avenue, several curb extensions at 79th Street and Madison Avenue, curb extensions on the west corners at 79th Street and 3rd Avenue, and curb extensions on the east corners at 79th Street and Lexington Avenue. [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p015_c0003] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p015_c0005] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p015_c0007] [brt_m79_cb8_nov2018#p015_c0009]

## Later Capital Framing

NYC DOT's Better Buses page later described the same corridor project as planned transit and pedestrian safety improvements on 79th Street from Riverside Drive to FDR Drive, including bus bulbs, upgraded bus stops, new bus pads, updated ADA elements, curb extensions, improved median pedestrian refuges, refurbished sidewalks, stormwater infiltration basins, and new landscaping. [better_buses#p001_b0001]

The April 2025 CB7 and CB8 decks use the name "79th Street Crosstown SBS, Capital Project" and list a corridor package that would maintain bus-lane alignment, bus-lane hours, and 2017 traffic-signal changes; upgrade 15 bus stops, 14 bus pads, and 9 median tips or pedestrian refuges; and add 4 bus bulbs, 13 sidewalk extensions, and more than 20 street trees. [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p011_c0001] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p011_c0003] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb8_apr2025#p011_c0001] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb8_apr2025#p011_c0003]

The April 2025 CB7 deck gives a total estimated cost of $60 million, including a $25 million DOT scope and a $35 million DEP scope. [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p012_c0002] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p012_c0003] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p012_c0005] [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb7_apr2025#p012_c0008]

The 2025 materials also explain the remaining need for capital work: some improvements could not be completed with in-house materials, pedestrian safety measures would not have full impact until made permanent, some stops still had crowding or circulation-space problems, some bus operations issues remained, and parts of the roadway and sidewalk needed capital repair. [79_st_riverside_dr_fdr_dr_cb8_apr2025#p009_c0002]
