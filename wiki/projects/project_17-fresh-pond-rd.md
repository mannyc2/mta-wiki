## Overview

Fresh Pond Rd Transit Improvements was framed in 2019 as a Better Buses priority project for Fresh Pond Rd between Metropolitan Ave and Putnam Ave, a 1/2-mile corridor with continuous southbound traffic backups in the afternoon and evening. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p001_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p005_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p007_c0002]

The Better Buses Action Plan described the segment as a 2017 Bus Forward segment with slow bus speeds, especially southbound in the PM, and heavy off-route bus traffic heading to the Fresh Pond depot at Fresh Pond Rd and 67th Ave. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p040_c0012]

The action plan listed Q58, QM24, QM25, and QM34 as served routes, 30,000 total daily riders, and average bus speeds of 5.9 mph in the AM and 3.0 mph in the PM. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p040_c0013]

## Need And Demand

The Fresh Pond presentation identified slow PM-peak bus speeds of 3.0 mph, compared with a 6.4 mph peak Queens average, alongside high route ridership and high bus volumes. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p005_c0002]

The southbound bus-speed chart for Metropolitan Ave to Putnam Ave says buses were slowest from 1-7 PM, and the ridership chart says PM ridership was highest from 2-8 PM. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p008_c0003] [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p009_c0003]

DOT's merchant survey said the agency visited every business between Metropolitan Ave and Putnam Ave, gave 65 surveys, and found that 61% of businesses reported average deliveries under 15 minutes, 66% wanted more delivery zones, and 35% saw some delivery vehicles park on side streets. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p011_c0002]

A shopper survey over 45 responses across three April 2019 survey days found that respondents used multiple modes to reach Fresh Pond Rd, including walking at 71%, subway at 42%, bus at 40%, personal car at 22%, and bike at 16%. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p013_c0003] [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p013_c0004]

## Proposed Treatments

The core southbound proposal was a curbside bus lane from Bleecker St to Putnam Ave, active 2-8 PM Monday-Friday, with parking and loading allowed at other times. [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p018_c0002]

The Better Buses Action Plan summarized potential improvements as a curbside southbound bus lane for buses and right turns, Q58 stop consolidation, loading zones on the east curb, loading zones and meters on western spur streets, and a study of a northbound queue-jump signal at Putnam Ave. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p040_c0018]

The June 18 committee deck proposed consolidating bus stops from five stop pairs about two blocks apart to four stop pairs about three blocks apart, retaining three pairs, moving one pair for even spacing, and eliminating one pair. [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p001_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p021_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0003] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0004] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p022_c0005]

The June 18 parking plan said the bus lane would repurpose 70 spaces during the PM peak, including 55 metered and 15 alternate-side parking spaces, while adding 61 new metered spaces on spurs, keeping two-hour metered parking Monday-Saturday 9 AM-7 PM, allowing ASP at other times, and creating or retaining loading-zone changes including Grove St. [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p001_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p024_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p025_c0002]

The June 18 proposal-development notes said DOT preserved alternate-side parking on select spurs, eliminated the proposal to reduce two-hour meters to one hour on the busiest blocks, and was working with MTA to address Fresh Pond Depot issues in response to community requests. [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p001_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p026_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p026_c0003]

## Schedule And Engagement

The May deck was presented to Community Board 5 Transportation Committee on May 21, 2019. [fresh_pond_rd_may2019#p001_c0002]

The June 18 next-steps slide listed outreach and proposal development through May and June, including a May 17 local elected-officials briefing, May 30 Farmers Oval Civic Association, June 6 Ridgewood Property Owners & Civic Association, June 6 Glendale Property Owners' Association, June 10 Liberty Park Civic Association, June 12 CB 5 Full Board, June 18 CB 5 Transportation Committee, and summer implementation. [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p001_c0002] [fresh_pond_rd_tc_jun2019#p027_c0002]
