## Scope And Timeline

The Fare-Free Bus Pilot was a time-limited bus fare pilot, not a citywide fare policy. The 2023 New York State budget mandated the MTA to select and operate one fare free bus route in each borough for 6-12 months. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p002_c0002] Board materials tied the program to Part NN of Chapter 56 of the New York Laws of 2023, requiring Board approval and operation for a minimum of six months and a maximum of one year. [meeting_doc_114166#p145_c0010] Earlier budget materials listed "($15) million annually" for a "Pilot program providing five fare-free bus routes in New York City." [meeting_doc_112231#p003_c0002]

The selected routes were Bx18A/B in the Bronx near Mt. Eden, M116 in Manhattan/Harlem, B60 in Brooklyn passing through Williamsburg and Canarsie, Q4 in Queens passing through Jamaica and Cambria Heights, and S46/S96 in Staten Island passing through St. George. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p002_c0006] Route selection considered equity for low income and disadvantaged communities, access to employment and commercial areas, existing route capacity, limited crowding conditions, service adequacy, minimal overlap with other MTA bus services, and fare evasion levels. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p002_c0004] The pilot began September 24, 2023, and fare collection resumed on September 1, 2024. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p002_c0005]

## Evaluation Design

The final evaluation separated the first nine months of the pilot from the summer months. MTA described the initial assessment as comparing September to May pre-pilot and pilot periods, then evaluating June through August to complete the full pilot program evaluation, because ridership trends vary between school months and summer months. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p004_c0002] The rider survey evidence is attributed to RSG, with a survey of 3,600 Pilot riders and 2,200 pre-Pilot riders. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p007_c0010]

## Findings

MTA summarized five main findings: ridership increased on all routes; new ridership was limited because existing riders used the fare-free routes more than new route riders; service performance had mixed results; fare evasion on nearby routes was consistent with systemwide rates; and foregone revenue and related costs amounted to more than $16.5M over the pilot period. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p003_c0003]

Ridership increased in both school and summer periods. During school months, weekday ridership increased +30% across the pilot routes, compared with System: +6%; route-level weekday increases were B60 +34%, Bx18A/B +29%, M116 +29%, Q4 +35%, and S46/S96 +22%. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p005_c0004] School-month weekend ridership increased +38% across pilot routes, compared with System: +8%. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p005_c0006] During summer months, weekday ridership increased +40% across pilot routes, compared with System: +9%, and weekend ridership increased +40%, compared with System: +10%. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p006_c0003] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p006_c0004] Survey findings narrowed that growth: while 12% of riders were new to the route, existing riders used the route more frequently and drove ridership increases. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p007_c0002]

Operations and speed results were mixed. In school months, the detailed speed table shows Fare-Free Avg end-to-end bus speed changing from 7.0 to 6.8 mph, a -2.2% change, while Systemwide changed from 8.1 to 7.9 mph, a -2.3% change. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p013_c0004] In summer months, Fare-Free Avg speed changed from 7.0 to 6.7 mph, a -4.3% change, while Systemwide changed from 7.0 to 6.9 mph, a -1.5% change. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p013_c0006] Dwell times per route increased more on pilot routes than systemwide in both school months (+7.0% vs +1.0%) and summer months (+8.2% vs +0.7%). [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0007] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0008] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0009] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0007] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0008] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0009] Customer journey time performance also dipped: 2.2pp on pilot routes vs 1.6pp systemwide in school months, and 2.6pp on pilot routes vs 2.2pp systemwide in summer months. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0010] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0011] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p008_c0012] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0010] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0011] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p009_c0012]

Other operations tables show similar unevenness. Service Delivered for Fare-Free Avg moved from 96.7% to 95.1% in school months and 96.3% to 94.4% in summer months; Systemwide moved from 95.4% to 95.6% in school months and 95.0% to 94.5% in summer months. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p012_c0005] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p012_c0007] Wait Assessment for Fare-Free Avg moved from 71% to 69% in school months and 74% to 71% in summer months; Systemwide moved from 70% to 68% and 71% to 68%. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p017_c0005] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p017_c0007]

For fare evasion, the detailed table covers nearby bus routes and subway stations. In school months, Nearby Fare-Free Average changed from 47.0% to 55.1%, a +8.1 percentage-point change, while Systemwide changed from 35.5% to 42.7%, a +7.2 percentage-point change. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p018_c0003] In summer months, Nearby Fare-Free Average changed from 48.3% to 58.7%, a +10.4 percentage-point change, while Systemwide changed from 34.2% to 41.7%, a +7.5 percentage-point change. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p018_c0004]

The evaluation estimated the full cost of the pilot program from September 2023 through August 2024, including foregone revenue on pilot routes, additional lost revenue, and operational costs. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p010_c0002] The cost table reports a 12-Month Total of $16,475,000: $10,880,000 in Farebox Revenue Loss, $1,722,000 in Revenue Impact to Nearby Services, $2,788,000 in Incremental Access-A-Ride Costs, and $1,085,000 in One-Time & Upfront Costs. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p019_c0002]

For safety, verbal and physical assaults against bus operators on fare-free routes declined from 47 pre-pilot to 32 during the pilot, a -31.9% change, while Systemwide declined from 1,663 to 1,407, a -15.4% change. Q4 was the route-level exception in the pilot table, increasing from 5 to 9, or +80.0%. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p024_c0003] The assault comparison uses September 2022-August 2023 as the Pre-Pilot period and September 2023-August 2024 as the Pilot period. [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p024_c0004]

## Caveats And Open Questions

The authorizing Board material says net operating costs of the fare-free bus route program shall not exceed Fifteen Million Dollars ($15,000,000), while the evaluation reports a broader 12-Month Total of $16,475,000 across farebox loss, nearby-service impacts, Access-A-Ride costs, and one-time/upfront costs. Those should not be treated as the same accounting category without more evidence. [meeting_doc_114166#p145_c0012] [fare_free_bus_pilot_evaluation#p019_c0002]

The summer fare-evasion summary slide reports 12.9pp and 9.4pp, while the detailed fare-evasion table reports +10.4 and +7.5 percentage-point changes. This page uses the detailed table.
