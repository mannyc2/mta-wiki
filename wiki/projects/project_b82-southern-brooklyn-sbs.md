## Project Context

B82 Southern Brooklyn Select Bus Service was planned for a major east-west bus corridor across
Southern Brooklyn. Project materials say the Southern Brooklyn B82 corridor was identified as a
potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study, and describe
the corridor as serving 32,000 daily riders on the B82 Limited and Local, connecting to the D, N, F,
B, Q, and L subway lines and 21 bus routes, and serving areas far from the subway.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p003_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p003_c0003]
A Starrett City presentation summarized the corridor as 10 miles long, with 32,000 daily riders,
and a key east-west connector along Kings Highway and Flatlands Avenue.
[brt_south_brooklyn_starrett_city#p008_c0002]

The project was framed around slow and unreliable bus trips, difficult stop access, and safety
problems. One planning slide showed a 10.2-mile westbound B82 trip and called out slow routing
between Flatlands Avenue and Kings Highway, traffic/parking/truck loading on Kings Highway, the
Rockaway Parkway routing, and Flatlands/Pennsylvania Avenue access to stops.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p009_c0002] Another broke trip time into 48% in motion, 29% signal delay,
21% at bus stops, and 2% other delays, then summarized that more than half the trip was spent
delayed, crawling, or at a stop. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p010_c0002]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p010_c0003] Safety materials identified long crossings, lack of pedestrian
refuges, limited visibility, complex intersections, difficult turns, narrow median bus stops, and
five top crash locations along Kings Highway and Flatlands Avenue.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p011_c0002]

## Service And Routing

The service plan converted the B82 Limited into B82 SBS. In the CB15 presentation, DOT/MTA said B82
SBS would serve the same stops as the existing B82 Limited within CB15, operate for more hours of
the day than the Limited, and leave all current stops served by the B82 Local.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p012_c0002]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p012_c0003] September 2017 materials described proposed
routing changes: westbound service via Glenwood Road, a connection from Kings Highway to Flatlands
Avenue via Avenue K instead of Flatbush Avenue, and B82 SBS ending at Cropsey/Bay 37 Street while
the local continued to Coney Island. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0003]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0004]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_sept2017#p012_c0005]

By October 2018, the launch-era source states that B82 SBS had launched on 10/01/18, replacing the
B82 Limited. That source says the SBS began at Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street, ran to Pennsylvania
Avenue in Starrett City, operated from 4:45 AM to 11:00 PM, and left current stops served by the B82
Local except for three planned eastbound stop removals on Kings Highway.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002] The same October source describes the post-launch
route as serving 28,000 daily riders on B82 SBS and Local, with the same subway and bus-route
connection role described in earlier planning materials. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p004_c0002]

## Street Treatments

The project combined SBS operating features with street redesigns. General SBS features in the
project materials included dedicated bus lanes, signal priority, off-board fare collection,
all-door boarding, improved station amenities, real-time passenger information, and pedestrian
safety improvements. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0002] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0004]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0007] [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p005_c0009]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p007_c0006]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p007_c0009]

On Kings Highway, the CB15 draft plan proposed bus lanes and curb management together: targeted
loading zones, side-street metered parking where possible, and bus lanes to improve speed and
reliability. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p022_c0003]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p022_c0004] The same source listed Kings Highway curbside
bus-lane segments, main-road bus lanes from East 23 Street to Nostrand Avenue, and safety
improvements at Kings Highway/Quentin Road/East 12 Street and Kings Highway/Nostrand Avenue; it
also noted that bus lanes would serve all buses, emergency vehicles, and right turns.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p023_c0004]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p023_c0005]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p023_c0006] Proposed station-area upgrades at Kings
Highway/Nostrand Avenue included larger lighted shelters, real-time information, off-board fare
payment, reconstructed level landing platforms, wider medians, added seating, and ADA-accessible
stops. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p031_c0002]

The October 2018 update shows that Flatlands Avenue/Ralph Avenue remained a major safety focus:
prior conditions included a lightly used street complicating the intersection, long pedestrian
crossings, heavy turning traffic, and the highest number of B82-corridor crashes in 2010-2014, with
103 crashes and 134 injuries. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p011_c0002] The updated design
described a simplified four-leg intersection, left-turn bans, longer pedestrian crossing time, a
pedestrian island, and a new curbside bus lane active 7 AM to 7 PM Monday through Saturday.
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p012_c0002]

## Timeline

The record shows a long outreach and design process before launch. Project engagement kicked off in
2015, with an online feedback portal, on-street outreach, stakeholder meetings, and community-board
meetings. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82#p007_c0002] The CB15 May 2017 deck planned draft-plan
presentations in spring 2017, design updates and initial implementation for critical
safety/transit-accessibility locations in summer 2017, final-plan presentations in fall 2017, and
implementation plus launch in 2018. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0003]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0005]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0007]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0009] A January 2018 source still described the launch
as planned for summer 2018, with fare machine construction beginning in winter 2018 and stop
amenities installed in spring-summer 2018. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_jan2018#p024_c0003]
[brt_south_brooklyn_b82_jan2018#p024_c0005] The later October 2018 source gives the actual launch
date as 10/01/18. [brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002]
