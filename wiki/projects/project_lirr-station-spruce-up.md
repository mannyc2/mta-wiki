## Overview

The LIRR Station Spruce-Up Program is framed in LIRR's November 2024 performance materials as a customer-experience initiative: the report says improving the customer experience was a strategic priority, then describes the rollout of a Station Spruce-Up program with an 11-station goal. [meeting_doc_157676#p021_c0006] [meeting_doc_157676#p021_c0007] By that update, LIRR in-house forces had completed 10 of the 11 stations and were awaiting delivery of a door needed to complete the final station. [meeting_doc_157676#p021_c0008] The work varied by station and could include new paint, brighter and more energy-efficient lighting, bathroom facelifts, floor and wall tile replacement, signage, and platform improvements. [meeting_doc_157676#p021_c0009]

The November 18, 2024 Committee of the Whole minutes record the same status in President Free's report: the program aimed at 11 stations, 10 were complete, and the eleventh was almost complete pending delivery and installation of one final door. [meeting_doc_160096#p005_c0004] By the March 24, 2025 Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees meeting, the program had a dedicated "Special Presentation - Station Spruce Up," with Chief Stations Officer Theresa Dorsey giving an overview/update and Deputy Chief Engineer Donald Olson describing cross-team work and highlights from completed stations. [meeting_doc_170851#p007_c0002] [meeting_doc_170851#p007_c0003] [meeting_doc_170851#p007_c0004]

The 2024 Annual Ridership Report treats the station Spruce-Up program as one of several LIRR initiatives associated with customer satisfaction improvements, saying the program resulted in increased cleaning and enhanced customer experience. [meeting_doc_170826#p005_c0001] That source also reports broader LIRR satisfaction gains, including overall station satisfaction rising to 84% from 82%, but it groups Spruce-Up with other initiatives rather than isolating it as the sole cause. [meeting_doc_170826#p005_c0001]
