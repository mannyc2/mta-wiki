## Project Scope

This page is about the 2014 Utica Avenue bus-priority and safety street-design project, not the later full B46 SBS service. The September 2014 CAC deck describes the project as offset and curbside bus lanes on 1.3 miles of Utica Avenue from St. John's Place to Church Avenue, plus left-turn restrictions, left-turn bay additions, and parking/loading changes. [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0003] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0005] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0007] In the same deck, Utica Avenue SBS is a follow-on plan that would build on this existing project to upgrade B46 Limited to B46 SBS. [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p014_c0002] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p014_c0003]

## Corridor Need

The project sat on the Utica Avenue B46 corridor, which the 2014 materials describe as a 2009 BRT Phase II SBS candidate, slow and crowded, and carrying nearly 50,000 passengers per day as the city's second-busiest bus route. [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p010_c0002] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p010_c0003] The safety framing was central too: the September CAC deck says Utica Avenue had among Brooklyn's highest motor-vehicle-crash injury rates, with 5 fatalities, 54 severe injuries, and 60% of vehicles traveling above the 30 mph speed limit in 2008-2012. [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p011_c0002] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p011_c0003]

## Treatments

The core package combined transit priority with turn and curb management. The transit element was offset and curbside bus lanes; the traffic/safety element was left-turn restrictions intended to improve pedestrian safety and left-turn bays intended to maintain traffic flow; the curb element added 19 parking spaces near Eastern Parkway, removed 22 spaces along the southern part of the corridor, and added loading zones for businesses. [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0003] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0005] [2014_09_16_brt_uticaavenue_cac1_presentation#p012_c0007]

## Early Evaluation

By the November 13, 2014 public workshop, the source describes "sections where bus lane was installed" and reports that B46 Limited and local travel times decreased 8% to 15%, while vehicle travel times decreased 20% to 25% in the peak direction; the footnote limits those figures to AM and PM peaks. [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p014_c0003] [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p014_c0004]

## Relationship To Later SBS Planning

The November workshop still treated full Utica Avenue SBS as a planned next layer: it says the SBS project would build on the bus-priority project to upgrade B46 Limited to B46 SBS and "might include" off-board fare collection, additional bus lanes, transit signal priority, reliability-oriented service planning, and station capital improvements. [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p015_c0002] [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p015_c0003] Its timeline framed the 2014 project as something to monitor and evaluate, while later work would identify street-design changes south of Church Avenue and north of St. John's Place and leave a potential 2015 SBS service launch in the final-design step. [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p017_c0008] [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p018_c0008] [2014_11_brt_uticaave_pw1#p020_c0008]
