## Summary

Re-"NEW"-vation is NYCT's station-improvement program built around using planned weekend closures to deliver station and facility upgrades. It was presented to the Committee on Operations on November 29, 2022, with Demetrius Crichlow discussing the program and Jim Compton providing more detail on the work [meeting_doc_102806#p003_c0012].

The program is tied to the agency's customer-satisfaction agenda: the same 2022 President's Report said customers most wanted faster service, cleaner stations and vehicles, a safer environment, better weekend service, and better communications [meeting_doc_102806#p003_c0009]. A later Grand St example shows the visible before/after pattern: stained white wall tile before the work, then cleaned tile after Re-NEW-vation [meeting_doc_109976#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_109976#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_109976#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_109976#p003_c0004].

## Timeline And Scale

By June 2023, President Davey described a goal to integrate Re-NEW-vations at 50 stations by the end of 2023, alongside reopening 36 bathrooms at 18 additional subway stations on a rolling basis beginning in July [meeting_doc_115246#p003_c0006]. In September 2023, NYCT reported that Atlantic Av L was the 48th station upgraded and that the program was on track to ReNEWvate more than 50 subway stations by year-end [meeting_doc_121046#p005_c0002].

The 50th station milestone was later identified as Junction Boulevard 7 in Corona, Queens, completed on October 12 as structural and cosmetic renovations [meeting_doc_124296#p002_c0005]. The October 2023 metrics report said NYCT had already Re-NEW-vated 52 stations and was on track for 61 by the end of the year [meeting_doc_124296#p004_c0008] [meeting_doc_124296#p005_c0002].

## Work Included

The October 2023 special feature describes the program as delivering overhauled, cleaner stations after weekend shutdowns. Reported work to that point included painting over 2.6 million square feet of station surface, replacing over 87,000 square feet of wall tile and over 4,000 square feet of floor tile, and replacing or newly installing 16,000 light fixtures [meeting_doc_124296#p029_c0004]. It also notes station-specific work such as rebuilding three stairwells at Junction Blvd, pouring fresh mezzanine concrete at 7 Av B/Q, and adding bird-deterrence measures at Nereid Av [meeting_doc_124296#p029_c0005].

In February 2024, NYCT said the program would continue into 2024 after upgrades at 53 stations in 2023, adding new LED lights, new draining systems, and crack repairs to reduce tripping hazards [meeting_doc_133231#p005_c0004]. The first listed 2024 round included stations such as 66 St-Lincoln Center, Fort Hamilton Pkwy, 161 St-Yankee Stadium, Jamaica-Van Wyck, and Sutter Av-Rutland Rd [meeting_doc_133231#p005_c0005] [meeting_doc_133231#p005_c0006] [meeting_doc_133231#p006_c0002]. By April 2024, President Davey reported that the Station Re-NEW-Vation program continued apace and that NYCT was continuing to upgrade every subway station with brighter, more comprehensive LED lighting [meeting_doc_140461#p003_c0008].

## Caveats

The September 2023 source says the 50th station milestone would be reached in September, while October sources identify the completed 50th Station Re-NEW-vation as Junction Boulevard 7 on October 12; use the October source for the precise milestone date [meeting_doc_121046#p005_c0002] [meeting_doc_124296#p002_c0005].

The October 12 milestone block does not itself state the year, but it appears in an October 2023 performance report prepared for the October 23, 2023 committee meeting [meeting_doc_124296#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_124296#p003_c0002].

Status is document-time: sources show the project as presented in 2022, scaling through 2023, and continuing in 2024; they do not support a final current completion status beyond April 2024's "continues apace" statement [meeting_doc_140461#p003_c0008].
