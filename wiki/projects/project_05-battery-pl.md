## Overview

Battery Place Bus Priority is a Lower Manhattan bus-priority project focused on Battery Place between West Street and Broadway. In the Better Buses Action Plan, DOT listed it as "05 Battery Pl, West St to Broadway" and described Battery Place as a "major bottleneck point" for Staten Island- and Brooklyn-bound express buses and other transit traveling to and from Lower Manhattan and the Hugh Carey Tunnel. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0006]

The project sits inside the Better Buses Action Plan's 2019 project program. The plan said the 2019 street-design projects were meant to support faster bus speeds, bus-stop accessibility, and Vision Zero safety goals, and Battery Place appears in the 24-project 2019 list. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p017_c0002] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p017_c0003] The later Battery Place presentation ties the project back to the Mayor's 2019 State of the City goal to improve bus speeds 25% by 2020 and the April 2019 release of the Better Buses Action Plan. [battery_pl_oct2019#p002_c0002]

## Why Battery Place Was Targeted

The Better Buses Action Plan framed Battery Place as a short but important connector: corridor length 0.1 miles, total daily ridership 28,000, and routes served including BM1, BM2, BM3, BM4, QM7, QM8, QM11, QM25, SIM1, SIM1c, SIM2, SIM3c, SIM4, SIM4c, SIM4x, SIM5, SIM15, SIM32, SIM33c, SIM34, SIM35, X27, and X28. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0007]

The October 2019 presentation said Battery Place was identified as a priority project because of slow bus speeds and unreliable bus travel times, a high volume of buses, and long passenger travel times. [battery_pl_oct2019#p003_c0002] Its issue list added that Battery Place delay affected 15 express bus routes; the 4-7 pm peak period saw 190 trips serving over 5,500 daily customers; and the area had traffic delays accessing West Street, poor curb management/enforcement, and pedestrian safety/comfort concerns at the crosswalk. [battery_pl_oct2019#p004_c0004]

## Treatment Package

The April 2019 plan's potential improvements were to install curbside bus lanes in both directions, adjust bus stops to improve bus operations, update travel lanes to minimize conflicts between buses and other traffic, and adjust signal timing to prioritize bus movements. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0008] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0009] The project was illustrated with New Bus Lane and Curb Management treatment icons. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0003] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0004]

By the October 2019 CB1 presentation, MTA, NYCDOT, and NYPD had studied bus and traffic movements in the area. [battery_pl_oct2019#p004_c0002] The study recommendations shown there included improved TEA placement and instruction, marked completed; updated curb regulations on West Street to keep the curb lane clear, marked completed; stop consolidation on Broadway and Battery Place; and a queue jump signal on Battery Place at Broadway. [battery_pl_oct2019#p005_c0003] [battery_pl_oct2019#p006_c0003]

The street-design slides show an existing westbound layout with Bus Stop, RT Only, RT Only, and Thru Only lanes, followed by a proposed westbound layout with Bus Only Signal, Bus Only RT, RT Only, and Thru/RT elements. [battery_pl_oct2019#p008_c0002] [battery_pl_oct2019#p008_c0003] [battery_pl_oct2019#p009_c0002] [battery_pl_oct2019#p009_c0003] [battery_pl_oct2019#p009_c0006] [battery_pl_oct2019#p009_c0013]

## Status And Follow-Up

The source-backed status is mixed by document date: the April 2019 Action Plan presented Battery Place improvements as potential improvements, while the October 2019 presentation marked some study recommendations completed. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0008] [battery_pl_oct2019#p005_c0003] The October 2019 next-steps slide still listed implementation, monitoring, bus performance/speed, further stop consolidation, traffic, and curb-regulation enforcement, so the available sources support project design and selected completed recommendations but do not provide a before/after performance outcome for Battery Place. [battery_pl_oct2019#p010_c0002]
