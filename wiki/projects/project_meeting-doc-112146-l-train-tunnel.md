## Overview

The L Train Tunnel Project is presented in MTA chair/board materials mainly as an example project, not as a detailed scope narrative: it appears under "Strengthen & Expand Network" in the May 2023 chairman's presentation, under "Strengthen & Expand the Network" in the January 2024 chair's update, and under both "Strengthen & Expand the Network" and "Knocking out Capital Projects" in the July 2024 chair's presentation. [meeting_doc_112146#p010_c0001] [meeting_doc_131981#p019_c0001] [meeting_doc_131981#p019_c0002] [meeting_doc_147341#p007_c0001] [meeting_doc_147341#p007_c0002] [meeting_doc_147341#p007_c0003]

By November 2024, the same project was also being used in a cost-savings frame: a chair's presentation lists "L Train Tunnel Project" on a "Committed to Cost Savings" slide. [meeting_doc_157981#p015_c0002] [meeting_doc_157981#p015_c0004]

## Later Use As A Delivery Precedent

In an April 30, 2025 board resolution about Amtrak's East River Tunnel shutdown, MTA cited the L Train Tunnel Project as an example of a night-and-weekend shutdown approach that "cut six months off the schedule and $100 million off the budget." [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0004] The same resolution says MTA urged Amtrak to consider that approach, while Amtrak instead favored a full shutdown of two East River tunnels over a three-year period. [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0004] [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0005]
