## Overview

This page covers the capital-construction portion of M86 SBS: six neckdowns and four bus bulbs being added along the route for pedestrian safety. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0004] In the progress report, this was a post-launch next step rather than the initial SBS rollout: M86 SBS launched on July 13, 2015, and the pedestrian-safety construction began after several delays in June 2016. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p006_c0005] [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0008]

The report connects the work to corridor safety concerns on 86th Street, where several intersections had poor safety records and were classified as Vision Zero and Safe Routes to School priorities. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p003_c0021] The planned safety response was to improve pedestrian visibility and shorten crossing distances. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p003_c0021]

## Treatments

Neckdowns are described as curb-line expansions into the roadway at crosswalks to reduce pedestrian crossing distance, and six were included in the M86 SBS project. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0005] Bus bulbs are described as curb extensions at bus stops that keep buses from pulling in and out of the moving lane, and four were included in both directions at Lexington and 3rd Avenues. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0006] [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0007] The report also says shelters would be added at Lexington and Third Avenues as part of the bus-bulb construction capital project. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p006_c0002]

## Source-Time Status

As of the report, construction was being performed by NYC Department of Design and Construction (DDC) and was anticipated to be completed in summer 2017. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0008] The report gives summer 2017 as an anticipated completion date; the inspected source text does not confirm final completion. [m86_sbs_progress_report_2017#p011_c0008]
