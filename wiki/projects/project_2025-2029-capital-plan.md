## Overview

The 2025-2029 Capital Plan was described in committee materials as a statutory submission due October 1, with an MTA Board vote required in September, and the same discussion said the plan excluded projects paused due to Congestion Pricing. [meeting_doc_151861#p005_c0015]

The plan-development approach combined a bottom-up read from the 20-Year Needs Assessment's inventory of more than six million component assets with top-down benchmarking against other large transit and infrastructure systems. [meeting_doc_151861#p005_c0016]

By the May 2025 board exhibit, the plan was framed as a $68.4 billion program, made up of $65.4 billion subject to CPRB approval and $3.0 billion in Bridges and Tunnels investment. [meeting_doc_174181#p084_c0005]

The May 2025 exhibit says the MTA Board approved the plan on September 25, 2024, CPRB representatives for the State Senate and Assembly vetoed it on December 24, 2024 because funding sources had not yet been fully identified, and the enacted FY2026 State Budget set up resubmission with sources identified. [meeting_doc_174181#p084_c0003]

## Investment Logic

The development presentation grouped the next plan's investments into steady investment adjusted for inflation, increased investment, and expansion opportunities. [meeting_doc_151861#p005_c0016]

The increased-investment examples included 2,000 railcars for subways and railroads, power upgrades, infrastructure fortification, pump replacement, drainage systems, electric buses, and charging infrastructure in bus depots. [meeting_doc_151861#p005_c0017] [meeting_doc_151861#p006_c0001]

The steady-investment examples included accessibility expansion, signal work for more than one million daily riders, 60 miles of subway track replacement, LIRR and Metro-North track needs, regular bus replacement, and bridge-and-tunnel maintenance. [meeting_doc_151861#p006_c0002]

The May 2025 exhibit specifies $10.9 billion for new railcars, including orders for 1,500 subway cars and 500 railroad cars to replace cars from the 1980s that had reached the end of useful life. [meeting_doc_174181#p086_c0003] [meeting_doc_174181#p086_c0004]

The May 2025 exhibit also specifies at least 75 miles of CBTC signal replacement on the Broadway N/Q/R/W, Liberty Avenue A, Rockaway A/S, and Nassau Street J/Z lines. [meeting_doc_174181#p087_c0004]

The accessibility program continues with at least 60 subway stations and at least six railroad stations slated for ADA improvements, with the exhibit saying those investments would put about 70% of subway rides to or from accessible stations and keep the system on track for 95% subway-station accessibility by 2055. [meeting_doc_174181#p097_c0004]

## Expansion And Bus-Relevant Items

The development presentation said the plan would include Interborough Express funding and would support 900,000 Brooklyn and Queens residents within 0.5 miles of the proposed route. [meeting_doc_151861#p006_c0003]

The same development presentation said the plan would advance planning for the 125 St Tunnel, Staten Island North Shore Busway, Ridgewood Busway, and LIRR diesel-territory capacity improvements. [meeting_doc_151861#p006_c0003]

The May 2025 exhibit assigns $2.75 billion to the Interborough Express and says the plan will complete design, get shovels in the ground, complete environmental review and preliminary engineering, and begin construction. [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0003] [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0004] [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0012]

The Interborough Express is described in the May 2025 exhibit as a light-rail conversion of a lightly used freight railway that would serve almost one million nearby residents and connect 17 subway lines, more than 50 bus routes, and Long Island Rail Road. [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0008]
