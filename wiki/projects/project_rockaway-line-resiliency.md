## Overview

Rockaway Line Resiliency and Rehabilitation is a C&D Design-Build package for rehabilitation and flood-resiliency work along NYCT's Rockaway Line in Queens. The award packet requested a $392,685,186, 1,339-calendar-day contract with Schiavone Construction, and capital-program tracking said the resiliency/viaduct work and South Channel Bridge repair were bundled into one contract to improve coordination, minimize outages, and reduce public impacts [meeting_doc_102776#p102_c0007] [meeting_doc_102776#p027_c0009].

The project responds to the line's exposure across the Jamaica Bay estuary: MTA materials say Superstorm Sandy caused embankment breaches and signal-cable impacts that fully suspended service for seven months, and describe the project purpose as protecting critical assets so service can resume quickly after future storm surge events [meeting_doc_131491#p015_c0006].

## Scope

The contract scope includes viaduct repairs at Hammels Wye and along the east and west branches, flood mitigation along the line, a new signal tower and crossover at Beach 105th Street Station with related traction-power, signal, and utility work, and replacement of electrical and mechanical components on the South Channel Bridge [meeting_doc_102776#p102_c0009]. Later project materials describe the Rockaway Line Viaduct as about 5 miles of elevated structure from Beach 116th St-Rockaway Park to Far Rockaway-Mott Avenue, with steel and concrete repairs plus FRP wrapping [meeting_doc_163236#p012_c0003].

The Rockaway Line provides A train service between northern Manhattan, Brooklyn, and the Rockaway community; a January 2025 project review says the line serves over 9,000 daily riders [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0012] [meeting_doc_163236#p015_c0004].

## Status

Reported status moved from 15% complete in January 2024, with August 2026 substantial completion forecast and a $599M current / $611M forecast budget, to 25% complete in February 2024, when the IEC said the project was on schedule and budget despite risks from marine-environment construction, swing-bridge work, and stakeholder coordination [meeting_doc_131491#p016_c0006] [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0007].

By January 2025, the project was reported 29% complete, with August 2026 substantial completion forecast and a $599M current / $599M forecast budget; MTA materials said work was on schedule and on budget, with focus on the 17-week outage [meeting_doc_163236#p012_c0004] [meeting_doc_163236#p012_c0005]. February 2025 materials said the Hammels Wye and South Channel Bridge work remained on schedule for the planned 17-week timeline, while a broader update noted a four-month general service diversion, steady overall progress, and a temporary halt of track, signal, and communications work at Beach 105th Street for technical evaluation [meeting_doc_164901#p005_c0008] [meeting_doc_164901#p008_c0009].

## Caveats

Status is document-time and changes by source date; do not treat the 15%, 25%, or 29% figures as timeless current status [meeting_doc_131491#p016_c0006] [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0007] [meeting_doc_163236#p012_c0004].

February 2025 materials expected the 17-week shutdown work to be delivered by May 19, 2025 or potentially earlier; verify with a later source before marking that milestone complete [meeting_doc_164901#p006_c0008].

Beach 105th Street track, signal, and communications work was temporarily halted for technical evaluation in the February 2025 update [meeting_doc_164901#p008_c0009].
