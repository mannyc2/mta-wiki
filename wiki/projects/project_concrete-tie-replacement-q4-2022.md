Concrete Tie Replacement is a LIRR systemwide track-maintenance program reported in quarterly Maintenance of Way Division updates. The figures below are from the "Track Projects Systemwide" slides and exclude yards. [meeting_doc_104766#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_104766#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_104766#p002_c0002]

In the 2022 Q4 update, the program was listed against 501,250 systemwide concrete ties, with 32,260 planned and 33,489 installed, or 104% of goal. The location breakdown was Mainline Queens to Carte Place, Atlantic Hall to Valley, Hempstead Bellerose to Park 2, plus Mainline Divide 2 to Divide 3 as an added location. [meeting_doc_104766#p002_c0003]

The 2023 Q2 update used a 534,740-systemwide-concrete-tie frame and listed 11,749 planned ties, but 0 installed, or 0% of goal. All three listed 2023 Q2 locations, Port Jefferson PJ Single, Port Jefferson Divide 4 to Divide 5, and Mainline Divide 4 to Divide 6, were still at 0 installed in that report. [meeting_doc_115196#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_115196#p002_c0003]

By the 2023 Q3 update, the same named program had 11,450 planned ties and 10,145 installed, or 88% of goal. Port Jefferson PJ Single was reported at 8,971 installed against 8,950 planned, Mainline Divide 4 to Divide 6 at 1,174 installed against 1,150 planned, and Port Jefferson Divide 4 to Divide 5 remained at 0 installed. [meeting_doc_124271#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_124271#p002_c0003]

Read as a sequence, the record shows an above-goal Q4 2022 package on Mainline, Atlantic, and Hempstead locations, followed by a 2023 package focused on Port Jefferson and Mainline Divide 4 to Divide 6 that had no installations reported in Q2 but substantial progress by Q3. The sources do not state whether these are one continuous capital project or recurring annual Maintenance of Way program buckets. [meeting_doc_104766#p002_c0003] [meeting_doc_115196#p002_c0003] [meeting_doc_124271#p002_c0003]
