## Program Summary

MTA describes the Fuel Hedge Program as a budget-risk tool that locks in future fuel prices. The program was authorized by the MTA Board in December 2011 and is aimed at dollar-cost averaging roughly half of MTA's ULSD expenses. In the October 28, 2024 Finance Department review, the July 2024 Financial Plan projected $216 million of total 2024 fuel expense, including $85 million of hedged ULSD costs, with 24 outstanding hedges across four counterparties. The same review reports an average locked-in next-12-month rate of $2.48/gallon versus an average budgeted rate of $2.60/gallon [meeting_doc_155146#p002_c0003].

## Hedge Ladder

The portfolio is designed to hedge 50% of projected fuel purchases over the next 12 months, laddered over time. Each monthly hedge is competitively bid and structured to lock prices for 4% of projected monthly purchases expected 13-24 months after the hedge execution date [meeting_doc_155146#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_155146#p003_c0002].

The December 2022 snapshot shows active ULSD hedges from January 2023 through November 2024: January-December 2023 are listed at 50% of expected gallons purchased, while later months taper to 4% by November 2024. That packet notes that expected gallons were based on pre-COVID consumption [meeting_doc_104726#p002_c0002] [meeting_doc_104726#p002_c0003].

By the October 2024 annual review, the ladder shows 100% hedged for October 2024 through August 2025, then tapering from 92% in September 2025 to 59% in August 2026 as later ladder months fill in [meeting_doc_155146#p003_c0004].

## Reported Effects

The October 2024 review says monthly forward hedges defer major market changes into future budgets and reduce immediate exposure to fuel-price spikes. It also says hedges had a slight negative impact over the first two quarters of 2024 but had produced $60 million in savings since January 2020 [meeting_doc_155146#p004_c0002]. The same review's charted series shows the 2022 spike: unhedged fuel reached $4.78/gallon in May 2022, while the fuel price including charges and hedges peaked at $3.54/gallon in November 2022 [meeting_doc_155146#p004_c0003].

## Caveats

Annual-impact tables are point-in-time comparisons against each report's stated adopted-budget baseline, so figures should not be blended without preserving the baseline year [meeting_doc_104726#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_121006#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_138301#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_140416#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_143396#p003_c0002].

The program includes CNG, but the 2023-2024 snapshots state there were no active CNG hedges [meeting_doc_140416#p002_c0003].

The October 2024 review says current hedges have final maturity in 2026; the inspected blocks do not state post-2026 coverage [meeting_doc_155146#p002_c0003].
