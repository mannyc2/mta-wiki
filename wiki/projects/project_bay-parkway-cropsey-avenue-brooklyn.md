## Context

NYC DOT and the MTA describe Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue as a Better Buses project proposing bus priority and safety improvements on Bay Parkway from Avenue J to Shore Parkway and on Cropsey Avenue from Bay Parkway to 26th Avenue, with a stated goal of improving bus service and reliability for 35,000 daily riders. [better_buses#p001_b0001]

The study area is defined as 2.4 miles of Bay Parkway and 0.6 miles of Cropsey Avenue, serving the B6 Local and Limited, B82 Local and Select Bus Service, and X28/X38 express bus, with connections to the F, N, and D trains. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p004_c0002]

Early project materials framed the corridor around both speed and safety: Bay Parkway bus speeds were cited as low as 3 mph, and the corridor was described as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with 25 people killed or seriously injured during 2019-2023. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p004_c0002]

By the June 2025 draft proposal, the safety framing had shifted to 22 people killed or seriously injured during 2020-2024, plus two additional deaths in 2025, one on Cropsey Avenue and one on Bay Parkway. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p004_c0002] [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p023_c0003]

The January 2025 materials identify Bay Parkway as a Bus Priority Corridor in the 2023 Streets Plan Update and within Tier 2 Priority Investment Areas. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p005_c0004]

The same materials connect the corridor to the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign, saying MTA and NYC DOT were coordinating on Brooklyn bus service with special consideration for bus priority corridors. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p006_c0002]

Existing conditions were described as two travel lanes plus curbside parking in each direction on Bay Parkway, two travel lanes plus wide curbside parking and a wide median on Cropsey Avenue, frequent double-parking and left turns blocking travel lanes, major-intersection traffic build-ups, and bus bunching caused by congestion. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p005_c0002]

The June 2025 draft proposal calls for offset bus lanes on Bay Parkway between Avenue J and Cropsey Avenue and on Cropsey Avenue between Bay Parkway and 26th Avenue. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p007_c0002]

On Bay Parkway, the draft design would repurpose one of two general travel lanes for an offset bus lane while maintaining the left travel lane and curbside parking lane. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p009_c0002]

At select Bay Parkway intersections about every four to five blocks, the draft design would shift the offset bus lane to the curb to create left-turn bays, separating left-turn queues from through traffic. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p010_c0002]

On Cropsey Avenue, the draft design would repurpose the right travel lane between the left travel lane and curbside parking lane while maintaining the left travel lane and curbside parking lane. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p012_c0002]

The draft proposal also says DOT was studying additional safety improvements at the most dangerous intersections, customizing Cropsey Avenue block by block, and optimizing traffic light timing for traffic flow and safety. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p007_c0002] [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p012_c0002]

Prior corridor work included a 2018 Cropsey Avenue project from Bay Parkway to 26th Avenue with widened median double-yellow lines, new parking and travel lane lines, pedestrian islands with flexible delineators, a bus boarding island for the B6 and B82-SBS layover in front of I.S. 281, and curb regulation and signage changes. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p015_c0002]

An upcoming capital project at the Bay Parkway and Cropsey Avenue intersection was described as a Department of Environmental Protection water-main project that would incorporate bus stop and pedestrian improvements. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_jan2025#p015_c0002]

The June 2025 draft-proposal timeline placed the project at "Present Draft Design & Finalize Traffic Analysis," followed by feedback, a refined proposal, final design and implementation, and monitoring and adjustments. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p015_c0002]

The June 2025 traffic-analysis slide says DOT's analysis included existing traffic volumes at 59 intersections, an existing-conditions traffic model, analysis of how the proposal would affect traffic patterns, an origin-destination study, and potential traffic-signal timing changes. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p016_c0002]

The same traffic-analysis slide says DOT would share analysis findings with the community as part of the final proposal. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p016_c0002]

The June 2025 draft proposal explicitly notes that design and project scope details were subject to change in the final proposal. [bay_pkwy_cropsey_ave_cb12_jun2025#p007_c0005]
