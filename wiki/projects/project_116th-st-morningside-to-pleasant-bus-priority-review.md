## Source-Time Status

This record is the February/March 2025 Bus Priority Review stage for 116th Street, with decks presented to CB10's Transportation, Landmarks and Historic Preservation Committee in February 2025, CB9's Uniform Services & Transportation Committee in February 2025, and CB11's Public Safety & Transportation Committee in March 2025. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p001_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p001_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p001_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p001_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p001_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p001_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_mar2025#p001_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_mar2025#p001_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_mar2025#p001_c0003]

The review decks describe an existing-conditions and toolkit phase where Community Boards 9, 10, and 11 would discuss needs and wants, feedback would help shape DOT's draft proposal, and winter/spring 2025 work would continue outreach, traffic analysis, draft plans, and proposal refinement. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p024_c0003]

## Study Limits And Need

The full 116th Street study area is described as 116th St., Manhattan Av., Pleasant Av., and Morningside Av. between 114th St. and 113th St., totaling 2.2 miles, carrying over 65,000 daily bus passengers across M3, M7, M102, M116, BxM1, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM11, and functioning as critical crosstown service with subway connections to the 2, 3, 6, B, and C. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p004_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_mar2025#p004_c0002]

CB9's western focus narrows the study area to Manhattan Av. between 110th St. and 113th St. and Morningside Av. between 113th St. and 114th St., with over 40,000 daily bus passengers across M3, M7, and M116 and a neighborhood roadway connection between Harlem and the Upper West Side. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p004_c0002]

The review's goals were to make bus service fast, reliable, and on-time; make the corridor safer to walk and drive; and maintain access to businesses, residences, loading, or programmable space depending on the community-board segment. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p015_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0003]

## Existing Conditions

The CB10 and CB11 decks say buses on 116th St. were slow and unreliable, moved below 4 mph in some segments, and incurred 785 hours of weekday delay for M116 passengers daily. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p007_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_mar2025#p007_c0002]

The CB9 deck says bus service was slow and unreliable near Manhattan Avenue, with buses below 5 mph in some segments and slowdowns caused by traffic congestion and double parking. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p008_c0002]

The full study area had 91,400 residents within a quarter mile, 79% car-free households, 87% commuting by transit, walking, or biking, a Priority Youth Injury Area and Senior Area designation, and a truck route from Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Blvd. to 1st Av. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p012_c0003]

The full study area had 573 crash injuries from 2019-2023, including 34 severe injuries and 4 deaths, and the deck identifies it as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor and among Manhattan's top 10% most dangerous corridors by KSI per mile. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p013_c0002]

CB9's narrower study area had 10,400 residents within one-eighth mile, 76% car-free households, 86% commuting by transit, walking, or biking, 32 crash injuries from 2019-2023, and 3 severe injuries. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p011_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p012_c0002]

## Toolkit And Constraints

The review decks present a toolkit rather than a final design, including transit tools such as offset bus lanes, curbside bus lanes, queue-jump signals, and improved stop spacing. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p018_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p019_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p019_c0012] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p018_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p018_c0006]

The decks also list bus-stop, pedestrian-safety, parking, and other tools, including benches, leaning bars, physical accessibility, bus boarders, pedestrian islands, curb extensions, mid-block crossings, left-turn bays, traffic signal timing, Transit Signal Priority, bus-lane camera enforcement, tree pits and streetscape improvements, Automated Pedestrian Signals, and DOT Art. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p020_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p021_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p022_c0001] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p023_c0002]

The roadway constraints described in the review include 116th St. at 60 feet curb to curb, Manhattan Av. at 42 feet curb to curb, Pleasant Av. at 60 feet curb to curb, and Morningside Av. between 114th St. and 113th St. narrowing from 42 feet to 22 feet. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_feb2025#p017_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p016_c0002]
