## Source-Time Snapshot

The M-9 trains are described as part of LIRR's modernization strategy for more reliable and comfortable equipment, operating in LIRR electric territory including Grand Central Madison. [meeting_doc_111791#p011_c0002] [meeting_doc_111791#p011_c0003]

The project scope covered 202 cars, split between a 92-car base order and 110 option cars, with Kawasaki identified as supplier; the IEC review says Kawasaki received the base contract in September 2013, the option was exercised in July 2017, and the M9 is a PTC-equipped, self-propelled EMU for electrified territory. [meeting_doc_111791#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_111791#p015_c0003]

Customer-facing features cited for the cars include closed-loop armrests, improved bathroom amenities, outlets at each row of seats, car-number displays, a powered B-end door, and CCTV. [meeting_doc_111791#p011_c0005]

## Schedule, Cost, And Risk

As presented in the project update, conditional acceptance was forecast for September 2023, the order still totaled 202 cars, and LIRR said the schedule and budget had remained on track since the last CPC report. [meeting_doc_111791#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_111791#p012_c0003]

The June committee minutes summarized the same project as 202 cars with a $736 million budget and a September 2023 conditional-acceptance forecast. [meeting_doc_113971#p005_c0012]

The IEC schedule review separated LIRR's September 2023 acceptance-completion forecast from the IEC's December 2023 forecast: LIRR reported 162 cars conditionally accepted, 22 vehicles in testing, and 18 remaining cars planned for delivery to LIRR property by July 2023, while the IEC attributed conditional-acceptance delays to vestibule flooring repairs, door/lavatory and other quality rework, field modification instructions, and workmanship issues. [meeting_doc_111791#p016_c0003]

The June minutes carried that tension forward: LIRR was still forecasting acceptance completion of the last of 202 vehicles in September 2023, but the IEC said quality issues left risk to that date and made December 2023 acceptance completion likely. [meeting_doc_113971#p006_c0003]

On cost, the May IEC review says LIRR reported a $735.7 million budget and $730.8 million estimate at completion, and the IEC forecast completion at or near the current budget after reviewing expenditures, change orders, contingency, delay costs, and remaining-work risk. [meeting_doc_111791#p017_c0003]

## Reliability And Oversight

LIRR described continued oversight during testing and acceptance to ensure M-9 cars were safe and reliable, and reported that accepted M-9 cars had MDBF of 280,000 miles versus a 150,000-mile contract requirement, more than 86% above the requirement. [meeting_doc_111791#p013_c0002]

The IEC supported LIRR's practice of withholding vehicle acceptance until quality issues were addressed under contractual requirements, while also noting that MDBF continued to far exceed contract requirements. [meeting_doc_111791#p018_c0003]

The main IEC concern was supplier quality at delivery: the IEC said Kawasaki had not achieved the level of quality needed at vehicle delivery to meet contractual requirements, and the June minutes connected those M9 quality lessons to a recommendation that NYCT closely monitor Kawasaki's R211 quality program. [meeting_doc_111791#p018_c0005] [meeting_doc_113971#p006_c0003]
