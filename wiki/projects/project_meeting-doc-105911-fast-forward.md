## Overview

Fast Forward is described in these ADA compliance reports as NYCT's commitment to add accessible stations beyond prior accessibility work: NYCT committed in 2018 to 50 additional accessible stations, increased that commitment to 70 in 2019, and stated the goal that no subway rider be more than two stops away from an accessible station anywhere in the system. [meeting_doc_105911#p002_c0006] [meeting_doc_133246#p006_c0003] [meeting_doc_164781#p006_c0002]

## How Stations Were Prioritized

NYCT reported that, in 2019, it consulted with the disability community, community boards, and elected officials, and held a February 6, 2019 public event where every geographic area across the system was reviewed for station-prioritization feedback. [meeting_doc_105911#p003_c0001] The prioritization factors listed in the reports were systemwide coverage and reducing gaps between accessible stations, transfer points and terminals, proximity to major activity centers, ridership, demographics, and cost/constructability. [meeting_doc_133246#p006_c0004] [meeting_doc_164781#p006_c0003]

## Related Accessibility Context

As contextual accessibility inventory, the same reports list accessible non-Key stations going beyond the Revised Key Station Plan: 41 in the December 2022 report, 48 in the December 2023 report, and 56 in the December 2024 report. [meeting_doc_105911#p005_c0006] [meeting_doc_133246#p005_c0005] [meeting_doc_164781#p005_c0002]

A related later accessibility commitment appears in the June 2022 class-action settlement. The December 2022 report says MTA would add elevators or ramps to create a stair-free path at 95 percent of currently inaccessible subway stations by 2055; the December 2023 and December 2024 reports instead phrase the settlement term as having accessibility construction contracts in place by 2055. [meeting_doc_105911#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_133246#p006_c0005] [meeting_doc_164781#p006_c0004]
