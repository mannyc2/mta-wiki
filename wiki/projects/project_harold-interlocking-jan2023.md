## Overview

Harold Interlocking is an integrated rail-capacity project rather than a standalone service plan. The CPC materials define it as part of the Northeast Corridor Congestion Relief Project to reconstruct Harold Interlocking and create the Eastbound Reroute and Westbound Bypass: two grade-separated or conflict-free routes for Amtrak and Metro-North trains traveling between Penn Station and the Hell Gate/New Haven Lines; LIRR trains, especially Port Washington Branch trains, are also expected to use the new tracks. [meeting_doc_104701#p018_c0006] [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0005]

The physical scope centers on capacity through Harold: box structures under main-line tracks, approach structures, support facilities, roughly 12,000 linear feet of track, third rail, catenary, and switches, plus Loop Track Interlocking reconstruction for movements to and from the East River tunnels and Sunnyside Yard. [meeting_doc_115231#p012_c0006] [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0006]

## Relationship To Other Projects

The Harold work supports Penn Station Access by adding operational flexibility for LIRR and Metro-North Penn Station Access trains; a separate Penn Station Access source describes the new Metro-North service as entering Queens via the Hell Gate Bridge and connecting to the LIRR Main Line at Harold Interlocking to reach Penn Station. [meeting_doc_104701#p018_c0008] [meeting_doc_115181#p002_c0007]

It also intersects with Grand Central Madison/East Side Access operations: T/Loop Interlocking work upgrades loop tracks and Penn Lead/DS tracks to reduce a single point of failure for moves into and out of the East Side Access Midday Storage Yard. [meeting_doc_104701#p019_c0006] [meeting_doc_146956#p013_c0006]

## Status Timeline

In the January 2023 and July 2023 CPC materials, Harold Interlocking carried an October 2028 program-completion forecast and a $1.4B budget, with an HSIPR grant amendment in progress to remove Amtrak car-washer scope and the program described as approximately 50% complete. [meeting_doc_104701#p018_c0005] [meeting_doc_115231#p012_c0008] The July 2024 update, stated elsewhere in the same CPC packet as current as of June 30, 2024, kept October 2028 and $1.4B as both original and forecast values, and raised reported progress to approximately 62% complete. [meeting_doc_146956#p010_c0003] [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0008] [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0009]

The car-washer treatment changed between snapshots: in 2023 the grant amendment was still in progress and expected to affect cost and schedule forecasts, while by July 2024 the source says the grant amendment had removed the car-washer scope from MTA's grant and that Amtrak would be responsible for the facility after MTA C&D completes Loop Interlocking. [meeting_doc_104701#p018_c0009] [meeting_doc_115231#p012_c0007] [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0007]

## Component Notes

For the Eastbound Reroute, the January 2023 update described cut-and-cover tunnel work during a 12-month outage and a July 2023 projection to return service after reconstructing tracks, third rail, and catenary. [meeting_doc_104701#p018_c0011] By July 2023, the box structure was complete, trackwork and third rail were in progress, the return-to-service projection had moved to September 2023, and the element was listed as 74% complete. [meeting_doc_115231#p013_c0004] By July 2024, the box was still reported complete, track/catenary/third-rail/signal work was in progress, substantial completion was forecast for December 2024, and the element was 88% complete. [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0012] [meeting_doc_146956#p013_c0004]

For the Westbound Bypass, the source snapshots move from a contract being developed for planned Q2 2024 award in January 2023, to planned Q4 2024 award in July 2023, to a construction contract and MTA/Amtrak term sheet nearing completion in July 2024. [meeting_doc_104701#p019_c0008] [meeting_doc_115231#p013_c0008] [meeting_doc_146956#p013_c0008] The expected operating benefit is conflict-free westbound train operations for LIRR trains from Jamaica, Port Washington Branch trains, Metro-North Penn Station Access service, and Amtrak. [meeting_doc_115231#p013_c0008] [meeting_doc_146956#p013_c0008]

## Delivery Constraints

The repeated constraint is access to Amtrak-controlled resources and track outages. The January 2023 source lists risks around Amtrak and LIRR support resources, outage provisions, and competing regional work including Penn Station, East River Tunnels, Penn Station Access, and Gateway. [meeting_doc_104701#p019_c0014] The July 2024 source says progress had historically been constrained by limited Amtrak Force Account support and outage availability, and that MTA worked with Amtrak on outage/personnel prioritization and contractor training to reduce Amtrak staffing requirements. [meeting_doc_146956#p012_c0010]
