## Source-Time Status

The 116th Street Bus & Pedestrian Priority Project is documented here as a May-June 2025 proposal/update process, not as a completed-project record. The June decks were presented to CB10's Transportation, Historic Preservation & Landmarks Committee, Community Board 11 Public Safety & Transportation Committee, and CB9's Uniform Services & Transportation Committee. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p001_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p001_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p001_c0002] The project timeline places the corridor in the NYC Streets Plan in 2021, then field observations and traffic data, February/March 2025 existing-conditions presentations, May proposal presentations, June updated or draft proposal presentations, engineering review and approval, and "Summer 2025: * Tentative* begin Implementation." [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p032_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p023_c0002]

## Study Limits And Need

The source decks split the corridor by community board. CB10 describes a 1.1-mile study area on 116th St., Manhattan Av., and Morningside Av. between 114th St. and 113th St.; CB11 describes a 1.1-mile study area on 116th St. and Pleasant Av.; CB9 focuses on Manhattan Av. between 110th St. and 113th St. and Morningside Av. between 113th St. and 114th St. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p005_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p005_c0002]

The need case combines bus delay, crash history, and street-user access. CB10 says buses on 116th St. are slow and unreliable, move under 4 mph in some segments, and weekday congestion causes 785 hours of daily delay to M116 passengers. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p008_c0002] CB10 also reports 248 crash injuries from 2020-2024 in its study area, with 12 severe injuries and 2 deaths, and identifies the area as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor and Zone. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p007_c0002] CB11 reports 317 injuries, 20 severe injuries, and 1 death from 2020-2024, ranking the CB11 study area in the top 10% most dangerous corridors in Manhattan by KSI per mile. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p006_c0002] CB9 reports 37 injuries, 4 severe injuries, and 1 death in its study area from 2020-2024. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p008_c0002]

## Routes And Street Users

The project serves a corridor with heavy bus use and neighborhood destinations. CB10 cites over 64,000+ daily bus passengers across 10 bus routes and names M3, M7, M102, M116, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM11, while also calling 116th Street a critical crosstown service with subway connections to 2, 3, 6, B, and C. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p005_c0002] CB11 cites over 36,000+ daily bus passengers across M102, M116, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM11. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p004_c0002] CB9 frames the western segment as serving M3, M7, and M116, with connections between Harlem and the Upper West Side and destinations including Columbia University, Morningside Park, St John the Divine, stores, and restaurants along 116th St. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p005_c0002]

Outreach themes were not only bus speed. CB10 feedback called for safer walking and shopping space, continued traffic analysis for lane reductions, accessible access to Morningside Park, loading zones and short-term parking, and bus-lane enforcement using DOT stationary cameras and ACE on-bus cameras coordinated with NYPD. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p012_c0004] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p012_c0006] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p012_c0008] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p012_c0010] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p012_c0012]

## Proposed Treatments By Area

For CB9, the June proposal adds 0.15 miles of bus lanes, 9,800+ ft2 of painted pedestrian space, one new plaza through slip-lane closure, 880+ ft2 of concrete pedestrian space, and 2+ new street tree pits. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p021_c0002] The specific western design repurposes the Morningside Avenue slip lane for pedestrians and potential programming, proposes a 3-block Manhattan Av. curbside bus lane operating 7am-7pm, removes stop #400484 at Manhattan Av./W. 112th St. for stop consolidation, and repurposes parking for curbside bus lanes, daylighting, and a bike corral for 18+ bicycles at Morningside Ave. and 116th St. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p012_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p013_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p015_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p017_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p018_c0002]

For CB10, the June summary adds 1.15 miles of bus lanes, maintains all CB10 bus stops, adds 18,700+ ft2 of painted pedestrian space, daylighting at 10 intersections, one new plaza from slip-lane closure, 880+ ft2 of concrete pedestrian space, three new pedestrian midblock crossings, one bus queue-jump signal, and nine left turn bays. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p030_c0002] Key elements include an offset bus lane on 116th St. from Manhattan Av. to 5th Av. that maintains parking, mid-block crossings between Frederick Douglass Blvd and 5th Avenue, and an east-west bus queue-jump signal at 116th Street and Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Blvd. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p022_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p021_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p023_c0002]

For CB11, the June summary adds 1.1 miles of bus lanes, 14,400+ ft2 of painted pedestrian space, daylighting at 11 intersections, asphalt art at 120th St/Pleasant Ave, 1,100+ ft2 of concrete pedestrian space, 7 new street tree pits, 10 new parking spaces, 0.4 miles of parking-protected bike lanes, two bus queue-jump signals, and seven left turn bays. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p032_c0002] The eastern design includes an offset bus lane on 116th St. from 5 Av. to Pleasant Av., a daytime curbside bus lane between 5th Av. and Madison Av. that reverts to parking in the evenings, queue-jump signals at Madison Avenue and Lexington Avenue, and an upgraded parking-protected bike lane that maintains parking. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p019_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p020_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p021_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p022_c0002]

## Caveats And Open Questions

The inspected May/June 2025 records establish proposal status and tentative implementation timing, not final approval, installation, or post-implementation outcomes. Several design quantities are explicitly provisional: calculations are estimates and subject to change based on engineering judgement and traffic analysis. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p030_c0003] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb11_jun2025#p015_c0002] [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p021_c0004] Parking changes are also source-qualified as subject to traffic analysis and community feedback. [116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb10_jun2025#p026_c0002] Open questions remain about whether the summer 2025 tentative implementation began and which CB-specific treatments were installed or revised after engineering review.
