## Overview

The January 2023 Capital Program Committee materials describe the LIRR Main Line Expansion as adding a 10-mile third track on the LIRR Main Line to reduce train congestion, enable bi-directional peak-hour service, and support reverse commuting. [meeting_doc_104701#p021_c0003] The same update says the project also included eight street-level grade-crossing eliminations, rail bridge improvements, five station reconstructions, and new parking garages. [meeting_doc_104701#p021_c0004]

As of that January 2023 update, the new third track had been put into service in October 2022, while punchlist work continued along the corridor. [meeting_doc_104701#p021_c0007] The project status table listed April 2023 substantial completion, a budget line of $2.588B / $2.488B, and approximately 96% completion; the narrative said substantial completion remained on schedule for Q2 2023. [meeting_doc_104701#p021_c0005] [meeting_doc_104701#p021_c0006]

A later IEC note in the same January 2023 book said the project budget of $2.48B and Estimate at Completion of $2.44B were unchanged, expenditures to date were $2.3B, the EAC continued to trend within budget, and risks had been effectively managed for pending claims and closeout costs. [meeting_doc_104701#p043_c0003]

## Service Context

The April 2023 May timetable-change staff summary still listed Main Line Expansion among continuing projects supported during the timetable period. [meeting_doc_109341#p001_c0005] In the 2023 Annual Ridership Report, LIRR linked the Main Line 3rd Track to new reverse-peak service on the Port Jefferson and Ronkonkoma branches and doubled off-peak Ronkonkoma service. [meeting_doc_138216#p003_c0007] The same report framed Grand Central Madison and Main Line Expansion together as supporting enhanced flexibility and a 41% overall service increase to New York City. [meeting_doc_138216#p009_c0002]
