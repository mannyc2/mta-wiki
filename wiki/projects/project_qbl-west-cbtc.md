## Overview

Queens Boulevard Line West CBTC is the western Queens Boulevard signal-modernization segment from Union Turnpike through 50 St/8 Av on the 53 St line and 21 St/Queensbridge on the 63 St line. The work included CBTC equipment for 335 R-160 subway cars, the technical foundation for B-Division Automatic Train Supervision, and interoperability between Siemens and Thales equipment; MTA described it as NYCT's first interoperable CBTC project. [meeting_doc_100281#p012_c0011]

## Timeline And Status

The project had CBTC in service on all sections by February 2022, later than the original March 2021 in-service forecast. The same status table listed the budget moving from $663.1 million to $734.5 million and showed LK Comstock, Siemens, and Thales all in the mid-to-high 90 percent complete range. [meeting_doc_100281#p013_c0003] [meeting_doc_100281#p013_c0004]

The service milestone did not mean the project was operationally finished. A June 2023 update said ATS-B was in service but not yet ready to take over operations at the RCC, and that performance remained far below contract requirements despite Siemens software upgrades and intensive MTA/Siemens management attention. [meeting_doc_113971#p012_c0003] [meeting_doc_113971#p012_c0004] A companion signals-plan table summarized the mixed picture as incidents reduced by 50 percent while software and maintainability issues remained, with forecast completion moving from Q2 2023 to Q4 2023. [meeting_doc_114211#p010_c0002]

The interoperability program continued to mature after that. March 2024 materials say Automatic Train Operation was placed in service and Thales R-160 trains entered revenue service, treating Thales interoperable train operation as a milestone for confidence in multi-supplier software. [meeting_doc_135341#p010_c0006] By the July 2025 CPC update, MTA said stability and maintainability had continued improving through software upgrades and system optimization, and that the Siemens contract reached Substantial Completion in March 2025; a later table in the same packet calls Siemens the final contract and notes that this was three months later than previously reported. [meeting_doc_179731#p013_c0004] [meeting_doc_179731#p049_c0003]

## Delivery And Cost Context

The project was delivered through three main contracts awarded in 2015-2016: Siemens and Thales for Phase 1 CBTC system work, L.K. Comstock for Phase 2 auxiliary wayside/wayside installation and removal of legacy signal equipment, with SYSTRA as technical and engineering consultant and Parsons as project-management consultant. [meeting_doc_113971#p039_c0005]

Cost and schedule pressure are a recurring theme. A December 2022 IEC summary forecast the core project two years late because of technical issues affecting system stability, said contractors were progressing on interoperability validation, and flagged at least a $70 million budget shortfall from Transit Authority Labor and Engineering Force Account overruns. [meeting_doc_102776#p006_c0011] By June 2023, the budget had increased by $53 million to $720 million, the Estimate at Completion was $743 million, and the source warned the EAC could rise further because of a June-to-December 2023 Substantial Completion delay, additional TAL/EFA costs, and extension of the technical/engineering contract. [meeting_doc_113971#p041_c0003] March 2024 materials then put the budget at $734 million, project EAC at $747 million, and the IEC forecast at $752 million based on December 2024 Substantial Completion. [meeting_doc_135341#p027_c0003]

Later procurement items show how closeout and performance work continued around the main CBTC deployment. In October 2023, a Siemens modification resolved claims for additional work and delays and moved that contract's Substantial Completion date to December 31, 2024; a Thales modification resolved added-work claims and extended Thales' Substantial Completion date to August 31, 2022, the date the source says it had achieved. [meeting_doc_124291#p045_c0007] [meeting_doc_124291#p046_c0008] The Thales scope included changes driven by MTA interoperability-interface updates, additional CBTC database releases, and zone-controller capacity expansion. [meeting_doc_124291#p046_c0009] April 2025 materials also describe a modification for a database validation tool, cloud-based ATS expandability tool, and controller software changes to improve performance and reliability. [meeting_doc_170976#p050_c0008]
