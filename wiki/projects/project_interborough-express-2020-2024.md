## Overview

The Interborough Express (IBX) is described in the source set as a light-rail/network expansion project linking Brooklyn and Queens. In July 2024 committee minutes, C&D Project CEO Charles Gans described IBX as a light rail project to be built within an existing rail corridor between Bay Ridge and Jackson Heights; design was progressing, C&D was pursuing Federal Transit Administration funding, outreach had included more than 35 meetings, and next steps included continued outreach plus an RFP for preliminary engineering [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0007]. Later 2025 plan materials frame IBX as rapid transit between Brooklyn and Queens that would turn a lightly used freight railway into light rail connecting 17 subway lines, more than 50 bus routes, and Long Island Rail Road [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0004] [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0008].

## Funding And Plan Status

In September 2024, C&D said the 2025-2029 Capital Plan would include IBX funding to support 900,000 Brooklyn and Queens residents within 0.5 miles of the proposed route [meeting_doc_151861#p006_c0003]. The September board packet placed IBX inside the $5.250 billion MTA Major Projects and Expansion category [meeting_doc_152076#p005_c0006], and the board presentation listed "Start building the Interborough Express" among plan highlights [meeting_doc_152161#p003_c0004].

For the 2020-2024 program, October 2024 Amendment #4 added $58.3 million for IBX to the MTA Expansion Program [meeting_doc_155486#p008_c0011]. The amendment table lists element G80801 at $58,261,183, up from zero in Amendment #3 [meeting_doc_155486#p008_c0013]. The stated funding mix was $52 million from New York State, of which $43 million increased the funding envelope and $9 million offset other local sources, plus $15 million from the federal RAISE grant program [meeting_doc_155486#p009_c0006].

## Route, Benefits, And Next Steps

An April 2025 board presentation showed IBX as one of the proposed capital-plan projects and mapped a route from Roosevelt Avenue to Brooklyn Army Terminal [meeting_doc_171486#p009_c0003] [meeting_doc_171486#p009_c0004] [meeting_doc_171486#p013_c0001] [meeting_doc_171486#p013_c0002]. The May 2025 capital-plan exhibit gave IBX a $2.75 billion budget line and said the plan would complete environmental review, design, and preliminary engineering, and begin construction [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0003] [meeting_doc_174181#p093_c0012]. A July 2025 chair presentation also carried an "Advancing the IBX" slide for "MTA Interborough Express" [meeting_doc_180421#p006_c0002] [meeting_doc_180421#p006_c0003].

## Public Process

For FFY 2025 federal funding, the February 2025 board authorization package included Interborough Express under Construction and Development as a $40.00 million item [meeting_doc_164931#p007_c0001] [meeting_doc_164931#p007_c0006]. The staff summary says the hearing covered projects proposed for federal funding, and its comment summary includes both support for investment in the Interborough Express Project and concerns about costs, schedules, accessibility, and project priorities [meeting_doc_164931#p001_c0009] [meeting_doc_164931#p001_c0016].

## Caveats

The May 2025 exhibit says the 2025-2029 Capital Plan was approved by the MTA Board on September 25, 2024, vetoed by CPRB appointees on December 24, 2024 because funding sources were not fully identified, and was to be resubmitted after the enacted FY2026 State Budget [meeting_doc_174181#p084_c0003].

The FFY 2025 federal hearing record says listed projects may be more than the MTA ultimately seeks funding for, that MTA may or may not do listed projects with federal funds, and that final FFY 2025 federal amounts were not yet known [meeting_doc_164931#p018_c0003] [meeting_doc_164931#p019_c0004].

For the 2020-2024 amendment, the source says certain work may not proceed without CPRB approval and that program element increases above 10% require CPRB approval [meeting_doc_155486#p009_c0009].

## Overview

The Interborough Express (IBX) is described in these MTA materials as a light-rail project connecting Brooklyn and Queens. In December 2023, MTA said the project would connect communities in the two boroughs to 17 subway lines and the Long Island Rail Road, while significantly reducing travel times; at that point, town halls in Elmhurst and Sunset Park were being used for public feedback before formal environmental review [meeting_doc_128921#p021_c0003] [meeting_doc_128921#p021_c0004].

## Status And Funding Signals

By January 2024, NYCT's performance report identified formal engineering for IBX as one of the major capital projects for the year [meeting_doc_131541#p013_c0010]. In the April 2024 federal-grant action, MTA sought authorization to file for and accept FFY 2024 federal grants, and the attached project list with estimated federal requests in millions included "Interborough Express $32.00" under MTA Capital Construction & Development [meeting_doc_138351#p001_c0009] [meeting_doc_138351#p005_c0002] [meeting_doc_138351#p007_c0016] [meeting_doc_138351#p007_c0017]. In November 2024 minutes, C&D reported that full design would begin, that it had released an RFP for a preliminary engineering team, and that 2025-2029 capital-plan funding would allow C&D to complete design for a line expected to serve more than 100,000 daily riders [meeting_doc_160246#p009_c0003].

## Delivery Scope

The July 2025 board book framed the next procurement as a C&D request for Board approval to award preliminary-design services to Jacobs/HDR JV, not to exceed $165,980,654, with an optional $8,623,010 [meeting_doc_179916#p003_c0005] [meeting_doc_179916#p003_c0007]. The same action item described delivery as currently contemplated in two phases: first preparing the corridor, including demolition, utility relocation, environmental remediation, bridge/tunnel/retaining-wall work, and freight-infrastructure repositioning; then installing the light-rail system, stations, storage yard, operations center, traction power, signals, and other revenue-service work [meeting_doc_179916#p003_c0009]. The preliminary-design contract would give Jacobs/HDR 24 months to develop preliminary design and prepare bridging documents and procurement support for one Design-Build contract for each phase, with an option to split phase 1 into three Design-Build packages if MTA determines that would save schedule or cost [meeting_doc_179916#p003_c0010].

## Caveats

The December 2023 source says MTA was preparing to formally begin environmental review; it does not show that environmental review had begun [meeting_doc_128921#p021_c0003].

The July 2025 board-book evidence is a request for Board approval to award the Jacobs/HDR contract, so it should not be treated by itself as evidence that the award or notice-to-proceed had occurred [meeting_doc_179916#p003_c0007].
