## Overview

Fordham Road Select Bus Service is documented in the January 14, 2010 First Avenue/Second Avenue SBS Community Advisory Committee deck as an SBS precedent example, with a dedicated slide titled "Fordham Road Select Bus Service." [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p001_c0001] [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p001_c0002] [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0002]

The Fordham Road SBS slide lists curbside bus lane, off-board fare collection, and transit signal priority as project features. [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0003] [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0004] [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0005] [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0006]

A 2012 Webster Avenue SBS CAC summary later frames Fordham SBS as a comparison case, stating that Fordham SBS was designed to fit existing conditions while Webster Avenue SBS needed to address the corridor's future potential. [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p001_c0008]

The same 2012 summary says the Webster Avenue presentation described BRT and SBS features and the results of the Bx12 SBS on Fordham Road. [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p001_c0009]

## Reported Results

The 2010 deck reports a 20% reduction in running time for Fordham Road SBS. [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0008]

The 2010 deck reports a 10% ridership increase from October 2007 to October 2008. [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0009]

The 2010 deck reports that 98% of customers were satisfied or very satisfied. [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0010]

The slide's image description identifies a Bx12 SBS bus stopped at a curbside bus lane with a destination sign for "BX12 BAY PLAZA CO-OP CITY." [2010_01_14_brt_1st2nd_cac3#p016_c0011]

## Planning Relevance

For later Webster Avenue SBS planning, Fordham Road served as both a results benchmark and a design contrast: the Webster meeting summary says the presentation covered Bx12 SBS results on Fordham Road, while Borough President Diaz distinguished Fordham's existing-condition design from Webster's future-growth planning context. [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p001_c0008] [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p001_c0009]

The Webster Avenue discussion also captures an SBS design tradeoff relevant to the Fordham comparison, because Mr. Orosz said time savings come from bus lanes, traffic signal priority, and off-board fare collection, and he described offset bus lanes as a possible way to preserve valuable curbspace. [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p002_c0006]

Near Fordham Plaza, the Webster Avenue meeting record notes parking and curbspace pressure, a planned 250,000-square-foot Fordham Plaza redevelopment with a proposed charter school, and a planned bus-only street on 3rd Avenue between East 189th Street and Fordham Road for better bus circulation. [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p002_c0006] [2012_02_brt_webster_cac1_summary#p003_c0003]
