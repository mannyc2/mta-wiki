## Scope

Culver Line CBTC modernizes signals between West 8 St and Church Av in Brooklyn, adds CBTC and new signal facilities at Ditmas Av, Bay Pkwy, and Avenue X, and covers 4.7 route miles across 12 subway stations. [meeting_doc_179731#p014_c0007]

The work also includes interlocking upgrades, station equipment facilities, and significant special trackwork on the elevated structure. [meeting_doc_179731#p014_c0007]

The project used a combined installer-and-supplier contract model; Tutor Perini was selected in 2019 as primary contractor and installer, with Siemens supplying CBTC signaling technology. [meeting_doc_113971#p014_c0007]

## Status Trajectory

A November 2022 status table showed original substantial completion in August 2022, forecast substantial completion in August 2024, budget figures of $482M and $469.3M, and approximately 83% completion. [meeting_doc_100281#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_100281#p014_c0004]

At that stage, the documented delay was tied to special ties with an 18-month lead time, rework, Avenue X solid-state interlocking progress, and a need to finish Ditmas Ave special trackwork before signal modernization could proceed. [meeting_doc_100281#p014_c0009]

By June 2023, the update reported Ditmas Ave special trackwork substantially complete after FFU tie installation weekends and Bay Parkway 470 CIR breakdown testing complete. [meeting_doc_113971#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_113971#p014_c0010]

By March 2024, the status table showed forecast substantial completion in October 2024, budget figures of $482M and $469.3M, and about 93% completion. [meeting_doc_135341#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_135341#p011_c0011]

The same March 2024 update said all interlockings had been placed in service and a first CBTC section had entered revenue service after software/database delays. [meeting_doc_135341#p011_c0013]

## In-Service And Closeout

A September 2024 Transit performance document said normal G train service had returned and CBTC installation on the Culver line had finished. [meeting_doc_151656#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_151656#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_151656#p004_c0009]

The same document said the new signals would make travel through southern Brooklyn faster and more efficient. [meeting_doc_151656#p005_c0003]

A July 2025 status table listed CBTC in-service as Q3 2024 actual, substantial completion as August 2022 / October 2024 / September 2025 across original, last-report, and forecast columns, budget as $482M / $469M / $463M across the same columns, and project completion at approximately 99%. [meeting_doc_179731#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_179731#p014_c0009]

The July 2025 narrative said CBTC in-service was successfully completed in September 2024, including Automatic Train Operation, while outstanding contractual work moved substantial completion from October 2024 to September 2025. [meeting_doc_179731#p014_c0010]

It also said the budget forecast had dropped by $6M because actual soft costs were lower than budgeted costs, while excluding potential contractor claims. [meeting_doc_179731#p014_c0010]
