## Overview

Metro-North's Brewster Yard Improvements Program is a Harlem Line yard-capacity project involving permanent and temporary easements for the MNR Brewster Yard Improvements Project, with the stated purpose of reconfiguring the existing Brewster railyard, extending it for new and longer trains, and expanding servicing and maintenance capacity for the Harlem Line fleet [meeting_doc_131536#p001_c0007] [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0008]. A May 2024 performance document describes Brewster Yard as over capacity and frames the program in three phases: Southeast Station Parking and Access Improvements, Northward Yard Expansion, and Existing Yard Reconfiguration [meeting_doc_140286#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_140286#p028_c0004].

## Phase 1

Phase 1 relocates Southeast Station customer surface parking to a new garage east of the station and mainline tracks so future yard expansion can proceed [meeting_doc_131536#p001_c0009] [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0009]. Major elements include a five-level, 1,320-space parking garage with intermodal areas, a vehicular bridge from Independent Way, a pedestrian bridge to the station platforms, and a new road connection to the existing parking lot and future rail yard [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0009]. The later project update adds customer-facing details including rooftop solar, a new waiting room, bus shelters, kiss-and-ride zones, a vehicle flyover bridge, a second means of egress, and a pedestrian overpass from the existing station [meeting_doc_140286#p028_c0005] [meeting_doc_140286#p028_c0006].

## Approvals And Procurement

MTA held an EDPL public hearing on December 11, 2023, took oral and written comments through December 22, and said comments were reviewed and made part of the record [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0004] [meeting_doc_131536#p002_c0006]. On January 31, 2024, the MTA Board approved acquisition of property interests by eminent domain or otherwise and adopted EDPL Determination and Findings for the project [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0003]. Finance Committee materials and minutes also identify the action as approval of Determination and Findings for acquiring property interests in Southeast, NY [meeting_doc_131656#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_133361#p004_c0013]. The January 2024 Board action items record an approved design-build award to Civetta-Stonebridge J.V. for the new 1,320-space multi-level parking garage and Southeast Station access improvements, at $149,747,500 over 826 calendar days [meeting_doc_131811#p013_c0002].

## Claimed Benefits

The source-stated benefits combine operations, parking, customer access, and sustainability: reduced deadheading between New York City and Southeast, more parking to support ridership and reduce waitlists, safety and security improvements, expanded rolling stock storage, drop-off/pick-up zones, covered pedestrian walkways, expanded ridership, and energy-conserving facilities [meeting_doc_131696#p006_c0010].

## Caveats

The inspected sources support approval, property-acquisition findings, and a contract award; they do not establish project completion or opening. The May 2024 source says the project was "currently in the first phase" and describes bridge/egress work as being constructed [meeting_doc_140286#p028_c0005] [meeting_doc_140286#p028_c0006].

Public comments raised concerns about project location and drainage/changes in grade; the staff summary says MTA, C&D, and project staff would continue working with affected parties to mitigate impacts [meeting_doc_131536#p004_c0005] [meeting_doc_131536#p004_c0006] [meeting_doc_131536#p002_c0007].

The property-interest need date was an estimate: the EDPL materials say required property interests were "currently estimated as Q3 2024" [meeting_doc_131536#p003_c0006].
