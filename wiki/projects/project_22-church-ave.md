## Summary

Church Ave Transit & Traffic Improvements is the Church Ave bus-priority project tied to DOT's Better Buses Action Plan. The Action Plan set a citywide bus-speed goal of increasing average speeds by 25% by the end of 2020, and its Church Ave entry appears as "22 Church Ave, E 7th St to Flatbush Ave." [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p009_c0002] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p045_c0001] The Church Ave project decks use a broader local study frame: Flatbush Ave to Ocean Pkwy (0.9 miles), divided into "Flatbush Ave to E 16 St - Commercial Core" and "E 16 St to Ocean Pkwy - Residential & Neighborhood Stores." [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p006_c0002]

DOT's Action Plan entry says Church Ave was identified as part of Bus Forward because of slow bus speeds on a narrow corridor carrying high volumes of bus and truck traffic, with delays regularly caused by double parking, truck loading, and long traffic queues. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p045_c0005] It lists the routes served as B35, B103, BM3, and BM4, with total daily ridership of 45,000 and average bus speeds of 4.9 mph in the AM and 3.6 mph in the PM. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p045_c0006]

## Transit Need

The Church Ave decks frame the project as a 2019 priority because of slow bus speeds: 4.25 mph during peak, compared to a 6.7 mph peak Brooklyn avg, plus high route ridership and high bus volumes. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p005_c0002] They also break out the service pattern: B35 with 29,000 daily riders and described as Brooklyn's 3 rd busiest bus route, B103 with 13,000 daily riders, and BM3 and BM4 express buses with direct service along Ocean Ave to Midtown & Downtown. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p007_c0002]

The passenger count evidence is bus-heavy. On Church Ave between E 19 St and Ocean Ave, bus passengers made up 72% of travelers during the AM peak and 69% during the PM peak. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p009_c0002] [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p009_c0003] The B35 speed chart says the route was consistently slow, at 3-5 mph from 7 AM-7PM in both directions. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p010_c0003]

## Public Process And Curb Conditions

Public-facing materials show the project moving through outreach in 2019: CB 14 Transportation Committee on May 30, Church Ave BID Board Meeting on June 13, CB 12 Transportation Committee in June TBD, Community Board 12 on June 25, and Council Member Eugene Public Forum on July 30. [church_ave_cb14_tc_may2019#p001_c0002] [church_ave_cb14_tc_may2019#p031_c0002] [church_ave_cb12_jun2019#p001_c0002] [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p001_c0002] The July forum said Street Ambassadors visited every business on Church Ave from Flatbush Ave to Ocean Pkwy, and that 76 merchants took a survey on delivery and loading schedules, vehicles, challenges/needs, and loading zone preferences. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p013_c0002]

Those outreach blocks explain why curb management was central to the project. The merchant survey reported that, from Flatbush Ave to E 16 St, 70% said their average delivery takes 15+ minutes and 49% requested additional loading zones; from E 16 St to Ocean Pkwy, the figures were 22% and 27%. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p014_c0002] The commercial-core summary describes high curb demand, frequent double parking and illegal parking, and says 1-2 double-parked vehicles can shut down traffic. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p018_c0002] A typical 16-space commercial-core block was recorded as being used by 416 vehicles in 14 hours. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p019_c0002]

## Treatments And Status

The Action Plan's potential improvements were broad: install curbside bus lanes, update curb regulations for truck loading and short-term metered parking, investigate turn bans or turn bays, and adjust bus stops. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p045_c0011] The Flatbush Ave-to-E 16 St design focused on curb regulation changes: unmetered 15-minute zones for select spaces between E 21 St and E 18 St, added or extended loading zones on high-delivery-demand blocks, and metered parking on select side streets. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p022_c0002] The public forum explained that shorter-term parking on the busiest blocks between E 21 St and E 18 St was intended to support pickups, drop-offs, quick errands, and higher turnover: 12 cars per 12 hours for a 1-hour space versus 48 cars per 12 hours for a 15-min space. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p023_c0002]

For the E 16 St-to-Ocean Pkwy portion, the July proposal described curbside bus lanes in both directions, active 7am-7pm Monday-Saturday, with parking allowed other times. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p028_c0002] The proposal said the bus lane would repurpose 113 spaces part-time 7a-7p M-Sa, including 61 metered and 52 unmetered spaces, mitigated by 59 new metered spaces around the corner plus loading zones. [church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p030_c0003]

By the October 1, 2019 project update, the implementation-plan bus-lane segment was stated as curbside bus lanes in both directions from Marlborough Rd to E 7 St, active from 7am-7pm Monday-Saturday, with parking allowed other times. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p001_c0002] [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p028_c0002] That update says work began September 23 to install new bus lane markings, new 15-min parking signage, new meters on side streets, and truck loading; work would continue through mid-October; and bus lanes would be activated on October 23 with bus lane signs installed. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p033_c0002] The same update says the activated bus lane would repurpose 98 spaces part-time 7a-7p M-Sa, including 61 metered and 37 unmetered spaces, mitigated by 59 new metered spaces around the corner and added loading zones. [church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p030_c0005]

## Caveats And Open Questions

The source extents do not line up perfectly. Better Buses lists "Church Ave, E 7th St to Flatbush Ave" and 0.8 miles, while the Church Ave project decks describe Flatbush Ave to Ocean Pkwy as 0.9 miles; the October implementation plan then states bus lanes from Marlborough Rd to E 7 St. The May/July proposal used E 16 St to Ocean Pkwy and 113 repurposed spaces; the October update used Marlborough Rd to E 7 St and 98 repurposed spaces. This page treats October as the later implementation-plan source. The inspected records do not include a later post-implementation performance outcome for Church Ave. Nearby bus-lane examples in the decks should not be read as Church Ave results. No matching source-gap records were found for these Church Ave sources.
