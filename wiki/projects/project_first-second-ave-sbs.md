## Corridor And Purpose

First Avenue/Second Avenue SBS was the M15 Select Bus Service project for the East Side Manhattan corridor between South Ferry and 125th Street. Early project materials describe the corridor as 8.5 miles long and already served by the M15 bus route [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0004] [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0005]. A later progress report frames the corridor as a critical north-south bus service for neighborhoods far from subway stations, where slow and unreliable M15 service was attributed to traffic congestion and heavy passenger boardings [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0003].

## Launch And Service Design

Planning materials identified October 10, 2010 as the launch date for M15 Select Bus Service [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p003_c0006]. The launch package replaced M15 Limited service with M15 SBS [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p010_c0006]. A post-launch community-board presentation summarized the implemented service elements as more than 10 miles of dedicated bus lanes, NYPD and camera enforcement, off-board fare payment, revised station spacing, and low-floor three-door buses [2011_05_02_brt_1st2nd_cb6#p003_c0006]. The September 2010 launch deck also described more than 10 miles of new or improved bus facilities, six locations with shelters and fare machines, and a protected-bike-lane program large enough to double protected bike lanes citywide [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p003_c0007] [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p009_c0011] [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p003_c0009].

## Outcomes Reported After Implementation

The November 2011 progress report says the project used proof-of-payment fare collection, new bus lanes, offset bus-lane treatments, route simplification, new buses, improved scheduling, enhanced stations, and better customer information to improve speed and reliability [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0004]. It reported a 15-18% travel-time improvement for M15 service, a 9% increase in M15 ridership, a 21% reduction in traffic injuries in sections with full treatments, maintained traffic speeds and volumes, and an 18-177% increase in cycling [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0006]. The same report's safety table shows total crashes down 7% and total injuries down 14% in the before/after comparison period [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p022_c0004].

Outreach was also substantial: the progress report says the project team participated in 37 community meetings between May 2009 and June 2011, including CAC meetings, open houses, community-board presentations, and direct outreach to businesses and institutions [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p006_c0010].
