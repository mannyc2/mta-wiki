Fulton St / Jamaica Av / Pennsylvania Av is a top-ranked Brooklyn bus priority corridor identified by NYC DOT in collaboration with the MTA as part of the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0004|Fulton St / Jamaica Av / Pennsylvania Av listed among 17 top-ranked corridors]]. The corridor was one of 49 Brooklyn corridors studied for potential bus priority street improvements, and one of the 17 that ranked highest in the NYC DOT evaluation process [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0002|49 corridors identified, 17 top-ranked]].

The corridor identification was based on criteria including demand for bus service, bus performance, feasibility of implementing street treatments (considering traffic levels and street widths), the corridor's role in the transit network, and neighborhood demographics and equity metrics [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0008|criteria for corridor identification]]. The MTA is working together with NYC DOT to expand bus priority improvements on corridors throughout the city as part of the redesign effort [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0003|MTA and NYC DOT collaboration]].

This corridor sits within the broader context of the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign, which identified that in 2019, average bus speeds in Brooklyn were the second lowest among the five boroughs at 7.0 MPH, down 5 percent from 7.4 MPH in 2014 [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p009_c0009|2019 Brooklyn bus speed 7.0 MPH]]. Bus ridership in Brooklyn fell 14 percent from 2016 to 2019 [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p008_c0006|Brooklyn bus ridership decline 14%]]. Existing all-day frequent service (every 10 minutes or better) on 26 routes covers 77% of Brooklyn residents within 1/4 mile, while the proposed network would extend that coverage to 87% of residents [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p045_c0002|proposed frequent network access 87%]] [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p009_c0006|26 existing all-day frequent routes]].

As a corridor slated for future study, Fulton St / Jamaica Av / Pennsylvania Av is positioned for potential bus priority treatments such as dedicated bus lanes, busways, queue jump intersections, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety or accessibility upgrades [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0005|potential bus priority treatments toolkit]]. The corridor is listed alongside 16 other Brooklyn priority corridors identified by NYC DOT.
