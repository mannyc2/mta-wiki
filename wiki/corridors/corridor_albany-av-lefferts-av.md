The Albany Av / Lefferts Av / E New York Av / Pitkin Av corridor was identified as one of 17 top-ranked Brooklyn corridors to be studied for potential bus priority street improvements as part of the NYC DOT and MTA Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign process [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0002|49 corridors studied]]. The corridors were evaluated based on demand for bus service, bus performance, feasibility of implementing new street treatments, the corridor's role in the transit network, and neighborhood demographics and equity metrics [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0008|selection criteria]].

This corridor appears among [[metric:metric_existing-all-day-frequent-routes|26 routes]] that currently operate frequently all-day in Brooklyn [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p009_c0006|existing frequent routes]]. The broader [[corridor:corridor_albany-av-lefferts-av|Albany Av / Lefferts Av / E New York Av / Pitkin Av]] corridor is part of the NYC Streets Plan bus priority program, under which NYC DOT is working collaboratively with the MTA on the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0004|NYC Streets Plan partnership]].
