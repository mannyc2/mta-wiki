[[corridor:corridor_bedford-avenue-201011|Bedford Avenue]] is a short corridor in Brooklyn that was included in the Nostrand Avenue / Rogers Avenue Select Bus Service (SBS) project area developed through a partnership between NYC DOT and MTA New York City Transit [[cite:201011_nostrand_handout#p001_c0004|Project Overview]]. The corridor's role in the SBS project was limited to a bus lane segment: the bus lane was installed at the right curb on Bedford Avenue between DeKalb Avenue and Flushing Avenue [[cite:2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p004_c0004|Nostrand SBS Project Features]]. This bus lane segment was part of a wider SBS construction project that also covered Nostrand Avenue south of Atlantic Avenue and Rogers Avenue, with the DDC project HWK 1130 building SBS features on all three streets south of Atlantic Avenue [[cite:2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p012_c0002|DDC Project HWK 1130]]. On the March 2011 project map shown to Community Board 3, Bedford Avenue was depicted with both BRT in bus lane (dashed red line) and BRT in mixed traffic (solid red line) segments [[cite:2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p007_c0003|Project Map Legend]]. Unlike the primary Nostrand and Rogers Avenue corridors, Bedford Avenue was a secondary component of the project, receiving a targeted bus lane treatment rather than the full suite of SBS station and service improvements planned for the main corridor [[cite:2012_06_nostrand_sbs_cac5_slides#p011_c0004|SBS bus bulb stations at DeKalb Ave and Fulton St]].
