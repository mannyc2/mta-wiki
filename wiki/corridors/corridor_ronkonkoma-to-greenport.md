The [[corridor:corridor_ronkonkoma-to-greenport|Ronkonkoma (KO) to Greenport (GY)]] corridor is a LIRR route on eastern Long Island that was surveyed by the LIRR Office of Security Drone Program [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p006_c0001|Ronkonkoma (KO) to Greenport (GY)]]. The drone survey covered [[metric:metric_55-miles-surveyed-16-hours|approximately 55 miles of track over 16 hours]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p006_c0002|survey details]]. Findings along the right of way included vegetation growth, scrap rail, debris, and hazards [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p006_c0002|survey findings]]. The survey formed part of the agency's first Drone Program, created by the LIRR Office of Security to monitor and identify issues on the LIRR system through aerial support for emergencies, surveys and assessments, engineering inspections, public event support, and night operations [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p002_c0002|Drone Program overview]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p002_c0003|program capabilities]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p004_c0002|drone capabilities]]. The program operates with three FAA Part 107 licensed pilots and three drones, available 24/7 [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p003_c0001|program details]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p003_c0002|three pilots]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p003_c0003|three drones]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p003_c0004|24/7 operations]]. A satellite map in the presentation shows the corridor route from KO to GY along a coastal area, with a scale indicating distances up to 50 miles [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p006_c0004|satellite map]]. The corridor length of approximately 55 miles recorded in this wiki page is derived from the drone survey coverage rather than from official LIRR track charts or timetables; the actual mainline distance between these stations may differ. The presentation also includes drone aerial views of Yaphank Station and a licyard survey area along or near the corridor [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p005_c0002|Yaphank Station]] [[cite:meeting_doc_151771#p005_c0006|licyard survey]].
