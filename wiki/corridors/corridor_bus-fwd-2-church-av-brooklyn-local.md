Church Av between Coney Island Av and Flatbush Av in Brooklyn was identified as a local bus priority corridor in NYC DOT's 2017 BUSFORWARD report [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Church Av listed among corridors addressed]]. The corridor was selected as part of a broader program by NYC DOT and the MTA to address issues affecting bus speed, using tools such as bus lanes, stop spacing changes, and signal priority [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|Corridors subject to change]]. A map in the same report shows Church Av between these endpoints as one of the targeted "long and slow trip segments," "slow speed segments," or "unreliable segments" identified through analysis of Bus Time data from October 2014 and 2016 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0006|Church Av shown on corridor segment map]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p015_c0002|Bus Time data analysis]]. Planning and outreach for this first round of priority corridors was expected to take place over the following year, with implementation anticipated in 2018 and 2019 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|Implementation timeline 2018-2019]]. Separately, the same report also lists Church Avenue as corridor G in the Brooklyn section of 21 candidate corridors identified for the next phase of Select Bus Service expansion, but this page is scoped to the local bus-priority segment between Coney Island Avenue and Flatbush Avenue [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|21 candidate SBS corridors listing]]. Citywide, bus speeds averaged 7.4 mph, with speeds below 4 mph in busy commercial districts, and the SBS program has demonstrated 10-30% faster bus speeds and about a 10% ridership increase on implemented routes [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|7.4 mph average bus speed]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0004|SBS speed and ridership results]].
