[[corridor:corridor_east-163rd-street|E 163rd Street]] is a Bronx corridor included in the broader East 161st Street / Bx6 capital improvement project, with limits from Washington Avenue to Tiffany Street and a construction segment from Intervale Avenue to Tiffany Street [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|March 2026 press release]]. The corridor serves approximately [[metric:metric_25000-daily-bus-riders|25,000 daily riders]] on the Bx6 Select Bus Service (SBS) line and passes near Yankee Stadium, which draws [[metric:metric_3-million-visitors-yearly|more than three million visitors annually]] [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|March 2026 press release]].

After Bx6 SBS launched on September 3, 2017, end-to-end bus travel times improved [[metric:metric_bus-travel-time-improvement-11to16pct|11% to 16%]], and on 161st Street specifically, eastbound bus speeds improved [[metric:metric_161st-st-bus-speed-improvement-33to43pct-eb|33% to 43%]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p007_c0002|travel time improvement data]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p009_c0002|speed improvement data]]. Reliability also improved: eastbound late bus arrivals dropped from [[metric:metric_late-buses-eastbound-2016-22pct|22.0% in 2016]] (Bx6 local) to 14.1% in 2017 after SBS conversion [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0004|late bus table]], and eastbound peak-period bus bunching fell from [[metric:metric_bunching-eastbound-6to10am-2016-22pct|22.5% in 2016]] to [[metric:metric_bunching-eastbound-6to10am-2017-2pct|2.3% in 2017]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0006|bunching table]].

A May 2022 presentation proposed specific improvements at intersections along the corridor, including bus bulbs and curb extensions at 163rd Street and Prospect Avenue, and bus stop upgrades at 163rd Street and Elton Avenue [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p033_c0001|Proposed Improvements: 163rd Street]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p039_c0003|163rd St & Prospect Av diagram]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p034_c0003|163rd St & Elton Av map]]. The broader capital project, which also covers East 163rd Street, includes [[metric:metric_370-ada-ramps|370 ADA-compliant pedestrian ramps]], [[metric:metric_57-new-trees|57 new trees]], about [[metric:metric_8000-plantings|8,000 plantings]], [[metric:metric_100-lighting-signal-poles|more than 100 new lighting and signal poles]], [[metric:metric_16-fire-hydrants|16 upgraded fire hydrants]], reconstruction of [[metric:metric_45000-sq-yards-roadway|approximately 45,000 square yards of roadway]], [[metric:metric_180000-sq-ft-sidewalk|more than 180,000 square feet of sidewalk]], and upgrades to [[metric:metric_4500-ft-water-mains-sewers|more than 4,500 feet of water mains and sewers]] [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|infrastructure list in press release]]. Construction was expected to continue through 2028 [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|construction timeline]].

The corridor is in Heat Vulnerability Index zones 1, 2, and 5, and an existing eastbound bus-only tunnel under the Grand Concourse serves the route, with a westbound tunnel proposed to complement it [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p015_c0002|Heat Vulnerability Index]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p018_c0002|westbound tunnel proposal]].
