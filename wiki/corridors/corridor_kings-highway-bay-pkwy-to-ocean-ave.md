The Kings Highway corridor between Bay Pkwy and Ocean Ave in Brooklyn is a narrow commercial and shopping street with one travel lane in each direction, described as a major east-west transit corridor and a local truck route [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p016_c0001|Existing: Kings Highway (Bay Pkwy to Ocean Ave)]]. It carries B82 Local, B82 Limited, B7 Local, and other non-MTA buses, and during AM and PM rush hours buses arrive every 1 to 2 minutes along Kings Highway [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p016_c0002|corridor characteristics]]. Three subway stations (B/Q, F, N) serve the corridor.

The B82 route as a whole serves [[metric:metric_b82-daily-riders|32,000 daily riders on B82 Limited and Local]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p003_c0003|B82 daily riders]], and within Community Board 15 alone about [[metric:metric_b82-cb15-daily-riders|12,500 people use the B82 Limited, B82 Local, or B7 service each day]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p014_c0005|CB15 daily ridership]]. Daily subway transfers at Kings Highway stations are approximately [[metric:metric_daily-transfers-bq-b82|1,760 daily transfers from B82 to the B-Q subway]] and [[metric:metric_daily-transfers-f-b82|620 daily transfers from B82 to the F subway]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p014_c0003|daily subway transfers]].

Bus travel time on the B82 along this segment is composed of only [[metric:metric_b82-in-motion-pct|48% in-motion time]], with [[metric:metric_b82-signal-delay-pct|29% of time lost to signal delay]], [[metric:metric_b82-bus-stop-dwell-pct|21% of B82 trip time at bus stops]], and [[metric:metric_b82-other-delays-pct|2% to other delays]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p010_c0002|B82 transit time breakdown]].

A shopper survey conducted in March and April 2016 found that on weekdays 51% of visitors walk, 24% use transit, and 23% drive; on weekends the walk share rises to 58% [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p018_c0004|weekday mode split]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p018_c0007|weekend mode split]]. Among 235 businesses surveyed, 85% report customer visits of one hour or less, 67% say double parking interferes with deliveries, 46% say vehicles at the curb interfere with deliveries, and 96% have no off-street parking [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0004|85% visit duration]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0007|67% double parking]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0009|46% curb vehicles]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p019_c0012|96% no off-street parking]].

The corridor was identified for B82 Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p003_c0002|2009 BRT Phase II]]. Proposed treatments presented in May 2017 include curbside bus lanes on several segments, main road bus lanes from E 23 St to Nostrand Ave, safety improvements at intersections including Kings Hwy at Nostrand Ave, a Quentin Road reconfiguration at E 12 St, transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, and targeted loading zones [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p023_c0006|key design pieces]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p027_c0003|Quentin Rd reconfiguration]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p031_c0002|Kings Hwy at Nostrand Ave amenities]]. Systemwide SBS results cited for comparison show [[metric:metric_sbs-speed-improvement-10-30-pct|10-30% faster bus speeds]], [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-10-pct_4|about a 10% ridership increase]], and [[metric:metric_sbs-customer-satisfaction-95-pct_2|95% customer satisfaction]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p004_c0004|SBS systemwide results]]. Implementation was planned to begin in summer 2017 with a full B82 SBS launch targeted for 2018 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0004|Summer 2017 implementation]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p034_c0008|2018 launch target]].
