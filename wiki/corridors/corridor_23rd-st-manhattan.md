23rd Street in Manhattan is a crosstown corridor that runs from Chelsea Piers / 12th Avenue to Avenue C / E 20th Street [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p005_c0001|23 rd Street Manhattan Crosstown]]. The corridor was identified as a Priority Corridor in the Manhattan Action Plan with four Priority Intersections under the Vision Zero initiative, reflecting its significance as a multiagency safety priority [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p008_c0002|Manhattan Action Plan – 23 rd Street Priority Corridor; 4 Priority Intersections]].

The M23 route served approximately 15,000 daily passengers and provided connections to PATH, multiple subway lines, 14 local and SBS bus routes, and 28 express buses that use the corridor for part of their route [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p005_c0002|15,000 daily M23 passengers]]. Starting in October 2015, NYC DOT and MTA NYCT launched the M23 Select Bus Service planning process with a kickoff event and on-street outreach to over 500 M23 customers [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0002|October 2015:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0003|Project kickoff event with elected officials, CB's On-street outreach to 500+ M23 customers]].

The proposed street design included dedicated bus lanes operating 24/7, with westbound lanes from 1st Avenue to 8th Avenue and eastbound lanes from 10th Avenue to 2nd Avenue, except that the westbound lane between 7th and 8th Avenue was not 24/7 [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p005_c0002|Westbound bus lanes: 1 st Ave – 8 th Ave]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p005_c0003|Eastbound bus lanes: 10 th Ave – 2 nd Ave]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb4_jun2016#p005_c0005|Bus lanes in effect 24/7 except westbound between 7 th and 8 th Ave]]. Other proposed treatment components included pedestrian safety improvements such as shortened crossings, improved station amenities with real-time passenger information signs, and off-board fare payment [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0002|Improved Station Amenities]] [[cite:2015_10_14_brt_23rdst_kickoff#p012_c0007|Pedestrian Safety Improvements]].

The planning timeline moved from an October 2015 kickoff through a tri-board meeting with Community Boards 4, 5, and 6 in February 2016, a business loading survey in March 2016, draft plan presentations in April 2016, additional stakeholder outreach in May 2016, and final plan presentations in June 2016 [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0006|February 2016:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0007|CB 4,5 and 6 Tri-Board Meeting]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0008|March 2016:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0009|Business Loading Survey]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0010|April 2016:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0011|Presented draft plan to Community Boards 4, 5 and 6 Individual stakeholder meetings]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0014|June 2016:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p004_c0015|Present final plan to Community Boards]]. Project implementation was scheduled for July through September 2016, with a post-implementation evaluation and continued community outreach planned for 2017 and beyond [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p011_c0002|July – September:]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p011_c0003|Project implementation]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p011_c0004|2017 – beyond]] [[cite:brt_m23_cb5_jun2016#p011_c0005|Evaluate SBS performance]].
