Church Avenue in Brooklyn was identified as "SBS candidate G" among 21 candidate corridors for the next phase of the Select Bus Service program, as part of the Bus Forward plan developed by NYC DOT and the MTA. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|21 candidate SBS corridors list]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p004_c0002|21 candidate corridors identified]]

Church Avenue was also listed as a local bus priority corridor to be addressed for bus speed issues, running from Coney Island Avenue to Flatbush Avenue. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Church Av bus speed corridor]] The corridor appeared on a map of targeted bus segments categorized by long and slow trips, slow speed segments, and unreliable segments across the city. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0006|Church Av on targeted segments map]]

The [[corridor:corridor_bus-fwd-2-church-av-brooklyn-sbs-candidate|Church Avenue (SBS candidate G)]] corridor is one of several Brooklyn SBS candidates identified in the plan, alongside South Brooklyn East-West (multiple), Downtown Brooklyn to Ridgewood, Brooklyn Access to JFK, and Flatbush Avenue. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|Brooklyn SBS candidates listed]] The plan was driven by 2015 and 2017 City Council legislation directing NYC DOT to develop an updated SBS implementation plan in partnership with the MTA. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p002_c0001|2015 legislation]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p002_c0002|2017 legislation]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p002_c0003|Citywide Transit Plan]]

No further detail on the scope of SBS improvements for Church Avenue (such as specific treatments, timeline, or route alignments) has been found beyond its inclusion as a candidate corridor in the Bus Forward report. The candidate list was subject to change during planning and evaluation. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|list subject to change]]
