[[corridor:corridor_ibx-brooklyn-queens-rail|Interborough Express Corridor - Sunset Park to Jackson Heights]] is an existing 14-mile freight rail corridor between Sunset Park, Brooklyn and Jackson Heights, Queens, being developed for light rail passenger service as the Interborough Express (IBX) while preserving freight operations [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0005|May 2026 CPC presentation]] [[cite:meeting_doc_206071#p016_c0012|April 2026 CPC presentation]]. The current plan includes [[metric:metric_ibx-18-stations|18 stations]], connections to 17 subway lines, more than 50 bus routes, and the Long Island Rail Road [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0005|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. Projected ridership is estimated at approximately [[metric:metric_ibx-ridership-160k|160,000 daily riders]], with an anticipated end-to-end travel time of [[metric:metric_ibx-travel-time-32min|32 minutes]] [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0005|May 2026 CPC presentation]] [[cite:meeting_doc_206071#p016_c0012|April 2026 CPC presentation]].

Design development has produced several significant project refinements based on public feedback. Street-running has been eliminated from the project — IBX will instead travel below Metropolitan Avenue in a tunnel, reducing travel times [[cite:meeting_doc_206071#p017_c0011|April 2026 CPC presentation]] [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p011_c0002|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. In East New York, the Atlantic Avenue station has been relocated closer to Broadway Junction to improve transfer opportunities, and the proposed Sutter Avenue station has been removed to eliminate redundant connections and reduce overall travel times [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p011_c0002|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. The project is anticipated to expand economic opportunity significantly along the corridor: at the proposed Remsen Avenue station, jobs accessible within a 30-minute commute would increase nearly seven-fold from 141,000 to 955,000, healthcare access would increase four-fold, and accessible colleges would rise from 2 to 19 [[cite:meeting_doc_206071#p017_c0005|April 2026 CPC presentation]].

The IBX was added to the MTA's Capital Plan in 2025 but is not yet fully funded [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0007|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. Chair Lieber acknowledged that securing sufficient construction funding is the principal challenge facing the project, noting that the MTA is actively exploring multiple funding strategies [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p011_c0004|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. The initial phase of civil construction — focused on bridge, retaining wall, and right-of-way improvements — is estimated at approximately $3 billion [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p011_c0004|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. A two-year design development effort is underway organized around three workstreams: corridor due diligence and data collection (over half of geotechnical work and 75% of structural inspections completed), civil design, and systems design [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0008|May 2026 CPC presentation]] [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p011_c0001|May 2026 CPC presentation]]. A draft environmental impact statement is anticipated later in 2026 [[cite:meeting_doc_208006#p010_c0007|May 2026 CPC presentation]].
