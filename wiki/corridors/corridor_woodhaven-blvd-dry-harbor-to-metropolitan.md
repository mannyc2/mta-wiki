This segment of [[corridor:corridor_woodhaven-blvd-dry-harbor-to-metropolitan|Woodhaven Boulevard: Dry Harbor Road to Metropolitan Avenue]] in Queens received offset bus lanes (one lane away from the curb) installed by NYC DOT in August 2015, following the Woodhaven Blvd Congested Corridors Study [[cite:2015_07_brt_woodhaven_whh_flyer#p001_c0007|DOT offset bus lane description]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0005|bus only lanes installed August 2015]]. The bus lanes operate Monday through Friday from 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM, with parking remaining at the curb and vehicles permitted to enter the lane for right turns, curb cuts within 200 feet, parking access, or quick passenger pick-ups and drop-offs [[cite:2015_07_brt_woodhaven_whh_flyer#p001_c0008|bus lane operating rules]].

The corridor serves approximately [[metric:metric_30k-daily-bus-riders-woodhaven|30,000 daily bus riders on Woodhaven Boulevard]], with one-way bus trips ranging from [[metric:metric_one-way-trip-time-range|55 to 85 minutes]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0003|corridor statistics]]. The Woodhaven/Cross Bay Boulevards corridor was designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, with [[metric:metric_injuries-3000-plus-v2|3,000+ injuries]] and [[metric:metric_fatalities-22|22 fatalities recorded between 2009 and 2013]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0003|Vision Zero data]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0014|VZ corridor footnote]]. Community engagement began in Spring 2014, followed by a design workshop in Spring 2015 [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0007|community engagement]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0011|design workshop]].

The 2015 offset bus lanes were an early treatment within a broader phased plan to implement Woodhaven/Cross Bay Boulevards Select Bus Service (SBS) as a short-term project proposed for 2017, with a longer-term NYC Department of Design & Construction capital project to follow [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0007|phased SBS plan]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p002_c0004|short-term project details]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p002_c0006|longer-term capital project]]. The short-term SBS project was described as fully funded and would include Q52/53 SBS launch with improved fare payment and branded bus service, transit signal priority, roadway safety treatments, and median bus stops along 1.3 miles of the corridor between Park Lane South and Rockaway Boulevard [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p002_c0008|short-term fully funded]] [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p002_c0004|median bus stops 1.3 miles]]. DOT and MTA committed to monitoring travel times and adjusting signal timing as needed [[cite:brt_woodhaven_cb6_handout_2016#p001_c0005|monitoring commitment]].
