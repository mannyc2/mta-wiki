125th Street is a major Manhattan crosstown bus corridor. In the BRT Phase II materials, the 125th Street Crosstown Corridor was framed as a "difficult trip" corridor that links 10 subway lines and had existing bus services carrying 64,000 trips per day. [brt_intro_to_phase2#p034_c0004] Later 125th Street SBS planning focused on bus movement, pedestrian safety, and general traffic concerns raised in a September 19, 2012 DOT/NYCT public workshop. [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p002_c0005]

The main bus-operating problems documented before implementation were bunching, unreliable service, red-light delay, traffic congestion, long dwell times, double parking, and blocked bus stops. [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p004_c0009] [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p005_c0004] A 2013 planning deck listed the 125th Street bus routes as Bx15, M60, M100, and M101, with 32,630 combined weekday boardings in 2011 and 9,682 on the M60. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0008] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0009]

The M60 SBS package proposed limited stops, off-board fare collection, offset bus lanes, updated curb regulations, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety improvements. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0007] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0008] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0009] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0010] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0011] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p006_c0012] Planning analysis covered intersections from Morningside Avenue to Second Avenue, while the implemented 2014 bus lane section was between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p016_c0004] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0004]

M60 Select Bus Service was announced for Sunday, May 25, 2014, with dedicated bus lanes, camera enforcement, curbside parking/loading/bus stops retained, and new left-turn restrictions at Fifth and Lexington Avenues. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0002] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0004] Post-implementation materials say bus lanes were installed between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue, and later reported SBS trips 32-34% faster in the bus-lane section and local bus trips 7-20% faster there. [m60_sbs_125th_street#p008_c0005] [m60_sbs_125th_street#p011_c0004] [m60_sbs_125th_street#p015_c0004]
