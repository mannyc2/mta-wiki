The [[corridor:corridor_meeting-doc-151786-harlem-line|Harlem Line]] is operated by Metro-North Railroad as a commuter rail corridor between Grand Central Terminal and Wassaic, serving the Harlem Valley [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p007_c0002|Harlem Line farebox and ridership table]]. Effective October 6, 2024, the Harlem Line schedule included minor adjustments to weekday outbound trains later in the PM-Peak period to improve train spacing during Park Avenue Viaduct two-track outages [[cite:meeting_doc_151786#p001_c0011|October 6, 2024 schedule change description]]. The Park Avenue Viaduct carries all Metro-North trains entering Grand Central Terminal and is a critical single point of failure; its state-of-good-repair renewal is part of the 2020-2024 Capital Program, and the October 2024 schedules continued to support that project along with other track and bridge work across all three main lines [[cite:meeting_doc_151786#p001_c0008|Schedule supports Park Avenue Viaduct project]].

The MTA Metro-North Railroad July Financial Plan - 2025 Mid-Year Forecast reports that the Harlem Line generated $175.405 million in total farebox revenue over a 12-month period (January through December), comprising $60.237 million in commutation revenue and $115.168 million in non-commutation revenue [[metric:metric_harlem-line-farebox-revenue|Harlem Line Total Farebox Revenue]]. Over the same period, the Harlem Line carried 22.037 million total riders, split between 9.843 million commutation trips and 12.194 million non-commutation trips [[metric:metric_harlem-line-ridership|Harlem Line Total Ridership]]. These figures represent the line's service as part of Metro-North's East of Hudson operations [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p007_c0002|Harlem Line ridership data]].
