The [[corridor:corridor_congestion-relief-zone|Congestion Relief Zone]] (CRZ) is the tolled district in Manhattan where vehicles are charged upon entry under the Central Business District Tolling Program (CBDTP), commonly called congestion pricing [[cite:meeting_doc_165006#p076_c0002|CBDTP launch description]]. The zone includes local streets and avenues south of and including 60th Street, excluding the FDR Drive, West Side Highway/Route 9A, and the Hugh L. Carey Tunnel connections to West Street [[cite:meeting_doc_160966#p005_c0010|CRZ boundaries description]] [[cite:meeting_doc_160966#p005_c0009|CRZ heading]]. Its northern boundary has detection points between 60th and 61st Streets [[cite:meeting_doc_134026#p005_c0002|detection point location]] [[cite:meeting_doc_134026#p007_c0007|toll zone signage]].

MTA Bridges and Tunnels launched the CBDTP on January 5, 2025, and manages vehicle entries into the CRZ [[cite:meeting_doc_165006#p076_c0002|CBDTP launch date]] [[cite:meeting_doc_186951#p016_c0003|B&T CRZ management responsibility]]. Since the launch, the program has reduced traffic throughout the region; morning peak travel times decreased significantly at major crossings, with reductions as high as 36 percent in the Holland Tunnel and 23 percent on the Williamsburg Bridge, while the number of vehicles entering the zone is down by 12 percent [[cite:meeting_doc_186951#p004_c0005|CRZ traffic reduction results]]. The program has received industry recognition, including the American Public Transportation Association Innovation Award and the International Bridge, Tunnel and Turnpike Association Social Responsibility Award [[cite:meeting_doc_186951#p004_c0005|CRZ awards]].

Enforcement efforts include a multi-agency ghost plate task force that has removed [[metric:metric_ghost-car-vehicles-removed|5,488 vehicles]] through towing operations [[cite:meeting_doc_186951#p005_c0006|ghost car count text]] [[cite:meeting_doc_186951#p005_c0007|ghost car towing description]]. The CRZ toll generates revenue to fund transit improvements, including $15 billion allocated for capital improvements to the transit system [[cite:meeting_doc_160966#p008_c0005|capital improvements funding]].
