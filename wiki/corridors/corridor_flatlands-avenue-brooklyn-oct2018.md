The [[corridor:corridor_flatlands-avenue-brooklyn-oct2018|Flatlands Avenue corridor]] is a segment of the B82 Select Bus Service (SBS) route in Southern Brooklyn, spanning Flatlands Avenue from Avenue I to Ralph Avenue [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p013_c0001|Existing: Flatlands Ave (Ave I to Ralph Ave)]]. The corridor is served by both B82 SBS and B82 Local routes [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p008_c0002|B82 SBS service launched on 10/01/18]].

The B82 SBS service launched on October 1, 2018, replacing the B82 Limited and serving approximately [[metric:metric_b82-sbs-daily-ridership-oct2018|28,000 daily riders]] on the SBS and Local combined [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p004_c0002|Serves 28,000 daily riders]]. Citywide, Select Bus Service has delivered [[metric:metric_sbs-faster-speeds-oct2018|10-30% faster bus speeds]], [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-oct2018|about a 10% increase in ridership]], and [[metric:metric_sbs-customer-satisfaction-oct2018|customer satisfaction of 95%]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p005_c0004|10-30% faster bus speeds]].

The intersection of Flatlands Avenue and Ralph Avenue was a Vision Zero Priority Intersection with the highest number of crashes on the B82 corridor, recording [[metric:metric_flatlands-ralph-crashes-2010-2014|103 crashes]] and [[metric:metric_flatlands-ralph-injuries-2010-2014|134 injuries]] from 2010 to 2014 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p011_c0002|103 crashes 134 injuries]]. The existing five-leg configuration, complicated by Paerdegat Avenue South, created long pedestrian crossings and poorly synchronized traffic signals [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p013_c0002|Large 5-leg intersections]].

Proposed treatments for the Flatlands Avenue corridor include closing a slip lane at Ralph Avenue to simplify the intersection, adding left-turn bans (northbound and eastbound), increasing pedestrian crossing time by 4 to 13 seconds at all crosswalks, installing a pedestrian island, and adding a curbside bus lane active 7 AM to 7 PM Monday through Saturday [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p012_c0002|Increased pedestrian crossing time at all crosswalks]]. Between Avenue I and Ralph Avenue, the plan also includes making Avenue I one-way with new angled parking, longer green lights with better signal coordination, a bus lane/right-turn lane, and shorter pedestrian crossings [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mbca_oct2018#p014_c0002|Bus lane/right turn lane]].
