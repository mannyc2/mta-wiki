Lafayette Av is designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor in Brooklyn, and NYC DOT is studying safety improvements along the corridor [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p010_c0002|Vision Zero Priority Corridor designation]]. The corridor runs roughly from Fulton St to Patchen Av and varies in street width and design: from Fulton St to South Elliott Pl it is 33' wide with a buffered bike lane and a 10' travel lane [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p015_c0001|Lafayette Av segment Fulton to S Elliott Pl]]; from South Elliott Pl to Classon Av it expands to 40' with a buffered bike lane matching portions of DeKalb Av [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p016_c0001|Lafayette Av segment S Elliott Pl to Classon Av]]; from Classon Av to Marcy Av the 40' width continues but the buffered bike lane is removed, creating safety concerns for cyclists [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p017_c0001|Lafayette Av segment Classon to Marcy Av]]; and from Marcy Av to Patchen Av the street narrows to roughly 33' with no delineated space for bikes [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p018_c0001|Lafayette Av segment Marcy to Patchen Av]].

The corridor carries the B38 route, which serves approximately 18,000+ average daily riders [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|B38 daily ridership]] ([[metric:metric_b38-daily-ridership-18000|B38 average daily riders]]). Bus speeds on the corridor average around 4-8 MPH [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|B38 speed range]], and speeds can be as low as 4 mph on parts of the route [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|bus speed low]]. There were 858 injuries on the corridor in the past five years, including 51 severe injuries or deaths [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|corridor injuries]].

During the Summer 2024 G Train shutdown, temporary bus lanes were installed along portions of DeKalb and Lafayette Avenues, leading to speed increases of up to 21% on DeKalb Av and up to 5% on Lafayette Av [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p026_c0002|temporary bus lane speed results]]. NYC DOT is incorporating lessons learned from those temporary lanes into the study, including red paint, signage, and enforcement [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p026_c0002|lessons learned]].

The study area covers 215,000 people, of whom 9% have a disability; citywide, bus rider median annual income is approximately $30,000 compared to $47,000 for drivers [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p009_c0003|demographics]]. The project timeline envisioned completing the first round of Community Board outreach in December 2024, continuing engagement through Winter/Spring 2025, presenting a refined proposal to Community Boards in Spring 2025, and implementing the project in Summer/Fall 2025 [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p027_c0002|project timeline]].

Note: This corridor page documents Lafayette Av specifically, while the parallel companion corridor DeKalb Av has its own separate page.
