[[corridor:corridor_mnr-zone3-ny-135311|MNR Zone 3 (NY)]] is a geographic zone defined by MNR as part of a procurement for emergency and scheduled bus services under Contract #426684. The zone covers the Hudson line north of Croton Harmon to Poughkeepsie and the Harlem line north of White Plains to Wassaic, entirely within New York State [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0009|MNR Zone 3 (NY) definition]].

This corridor was established in a March 2024 MTA Board action, where Metro-North Railroad, on behalf of itself and Long Island Rail Road, requested authorization to award competitively solicited miscellaneous service contracts for bus services during scheduled and emergency track outages and service disruptions [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0005|MNR requests Board approval]]. The total estimated aggregate budget across both railroads is [[metric:metric_total-estimated-budget-55m-135311|$55 million]], with [[metric:metric_mnr-estimated-budget-30m-135311|$30 million allocated to MNR]] and [[metric:metric_lirr-estimated-budget-25m-135311|$25 million to LIRR]] [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0006|estimated aggregate budget summary]].

Contracts under this procurement carry a [[metric:metric_contract-term-5-years-135311|five-year term]], and zero-dollar contracts may be awarded to qualified firms with no guarantee of spend amount [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0006|contract term and zero-dollar clause]]. The RFP was advertised on June 13, 2023, and [[metric:metric_thirteen-proposals-received-135311|13 proposals]] were received by the July 24, 2023 deadline; the Selection Committee subsequently recommended [[metric:metric_recommended-firms-13-135311|13 firms]] for award [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0011|proposals received on July 24, 2023]] [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p007_c0003|13 recommended firms]].

MNR Zone 3 (NY) is one of five MNR zones used in this procurement, joining Zone 1 (CT) and Zone 2 (CT) in Connecticut and Zones 4 (NY) and 5 (NY) in New York [[cite:meeting_doc_135311#p006_c0009|MNR zone table]]. The corridor boundaries match the outer portions of MNR's two principal commuter lines in New York: the Hudson Line north of Croton-Harmon and the Harlem Line north of White Plains.
