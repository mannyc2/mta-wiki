The [[corridor:corridor_southeast-queens-brt-phase2|Southeast Queens Corridor]] was identified as one of 16 potential future Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) corridors through the BRT Phase II planning study initiated by NYCT and NYC DOT in the fall of 2008 [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p002_c0003|BRT Phase II study initiated]]. The study compiled an initial list of over 40 potential corridors ([[metric:metric_potential-corridors-over-40|Potential corridors initially identified]]) from a citywide transit needs analysis and public feedback, then evaluated them on five criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0003|16 corridors from over 40 candidates]].

Public outreach in the spring of 2009 included seven workshops across the city and an online survey. Over 325 people participated in the workshops ([[metric:metric_workshop-participants-325|Workshop participants]]), with representatives from 47 community and business groups ([[metric:metric_community-business-groups-47|Community and business group representatives]]) and staff from 23 local elected officials' offices ([[metric:metric_elected-officials-offices-23|Local elected officials' offices represented]]), while roughly 600 people responded to the online survey ([[metric:metric_survey-respondents-600|Online survey respondents]]) [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p002_c0006|public outreach details]].

The Southeast Queens Corridor appears as item 13 on the BRT Phase II candidates list, alongside corridors such as Utica Avenue, Flatbush Avenue, and Woodhaven Boulevard [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p004_c0002|list of 16 Phase II corridors]]. It was not among the three short-listed candidates (Webster Avenue/Third Avenue, Hillside Avenue, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst) selected for the next detailed BRT planning effort [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0005|short list of three candidates]]. The corridor is located in Queens. Further planning would require additional funding, community support, and progress on related transit projects [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0004|future development process]].
