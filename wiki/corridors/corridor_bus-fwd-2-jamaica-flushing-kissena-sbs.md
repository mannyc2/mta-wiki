The [[corridor:corridor_bus-fwd-2-jamaica-flushing-kissena-sbs|Jamaica to Flushing via Kissena Boulevard (SBS candidate S)]] corridor was identified as one of 21 candidate corridors citywide for the next phase of Select Bus Service expansion under the Bus Forward 2017 plan, published by NYC DOT [[cite:bus_forward_2#p004_c0002|21 candidate SBS corridors identified]]. The corridor is lettered "S" in the Queens borough listing and is described as "Jamaica to Flushing (via Kissena Boulevard)" in the candidate corridor table [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|Queens SBS candidate corridors listed]]. Nearly 2.5 million New Yorkers ride buses each day, but average citywide bus speed is only 7.4 mph, dropping below 4 mph in busy commercial districts [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|bus speed and ridership context]]. The SBS program has demonstrated that SBS routes see 10-30% faster bus speeds and about a 10% increase in ridership [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0004|SBS route performance metrics]]. The existing SBS network carries over 309,000 daily riders (12% of city bus ridership), and with proposed new SBS routes including this corridor, over 800,000 bus riders would be on routes with fast and reliable service, over 32% of city bus ridership [[cite:bus_forward_2#p007_c0001|SBS ridership coverage]]. NYC DOT and the MTA identified four key analyses to guide SBS expansion and local bus service improvements: access to rapid transit service, long and slow bus trips, slow bus speeds, and bus reliability [[cite:bus_forward_2#p013_c0002|four key analyses for targeting SBS investments]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p013_c0003|four analysis categories]].
