This corridor appears in the [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|BUSFORWARD 2017 report]] as one of several local bus priority corridors in Brooklyn where [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|NYC DOT and the MTA planned to address issues affecting bus speed]]. The report, published by NYC DOT [[cite:bus_forward_2#p001_c0003|p1]], identified the corridor as running from Van Sinderen Av/Fulton St to Jamaica Av/Georgia Av [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|p11 table]]. It was listed under a separate "Improving Local Bus Corridors" initiative, distinct from the 21 candidate SBS corridors identified elsewhere in the same report [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|p5]]. The Bus Forward program slated a first round of these local priority corridors for planning and outreach over the following year, with expected implementation in 2018 and 2019 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|p10]]. At the time of the report, [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-avg-bus-speed|average bus speed citywide was 7.4 mph]] and dropped to less than 4 mph in busy commercial districts [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|p3]]. The corridor list was noted as subject to change throughout the planning and evaluation process [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|p11]]. No specific implementation status or further details about this particular corridor have been recorded beyond its inclusion in the initial priority list.
