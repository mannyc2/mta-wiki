[[corridor:corridor_brt-b82-mar2018-kings-highway|Kings Highway (Southern Brooklyn)]] is a major east-west transit corridor in Brooklyn with two distinct segments. The eastern segment, between E 23 St and Ave K, is described as a wide, boulevard-style portion of Kings Highway with narrow, deteriorating medians that serve as bus stops and large, complex intersections [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p021_c0001|Existing: Kings Highway (E 23 St to Ave K)]]. The western segment, from Bay Pkwy to Ocean Ave, is a narrower roadway with one lane in each direction, a local truck route with frequent double-parking and truck-loading issues [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p024_c0001|Kings Highway (Bay Pkwy to Ocean Ave)]].

During AM and PM rush hours, buses come once per minute along Kings Hwy, used by the [[metric:metric_brt-b82-mar2018-daily-riders|B82 Limited and B82 Local]] (serving 28,000 daily riders), the B7 Local, school buses, and coach buses [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p021_c0002|Wide boulevard section description]]. Within Community Board 15 alone, 12,500 people use the B82 Limited, B82 Local, or B7 service each day [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p016_c0002|12,500 people in CB15]].

The Southern Brooklyn B82 corridor was identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p003_c0002|2009 BRT Phase II Study]]. The proposed SBS features include dedicated bus lanes (active 7-10 AM and 4-7 PM weekdays), transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, and improved bus stop amenities such as larger lighted shelters, wider medians, level landing platforms, and ADA-accessible stops with subway-style edge strips [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p005_c0002|Dedicated Bus Lanes]][[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p005_c0004|Signal Priority for Buses]][[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p023_c0005|Real-time passenger information screens and off-board fare payment]][[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p030_c0009|Bus lanes in effect 7-10 AM & 4-7 PM]].

In 2017, the Mayor committed [[metric:metric_brt-b82-mar2018-20m-kings-hwy-medians|$20 million to reconstruct Kings Hwy medians]] in CM Deutsch's district, and medians at Kings Hwy/E 27 St and Kings Hwy/Nostrand Ave were reconstructed to wider, ADA-accessible configurations [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p019_c0002|$20 M committed to reconstruct Kings Hwy medians]]. Citywide SBS experience shows [[metric:metric_brt-b82-mar2018-sbs-speed-increase|10-30% faster bus speeds]], [[metric:metric_brt-b82-mar2018-sbs-ridership-increase|about a 10% increase in ridership]], and [[metric:metric_brt-b82-mar2018-sbs-customer-satisfaction|95% customer satisfaction]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_mar2018#p004_c0004|SBS benefits list]].
