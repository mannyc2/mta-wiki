Richmond Avenue forms the western leg of the Hylan Boulevard/Richmond Avenue Select Bus Service corridor on Staten Island, carrying the S79 SBS route between the Staten Island Mall and Hylan Boulevard [[cite:201109_brt_hylan_proposed#p007_c0001|Proposed Improvements on Richmond Avenue]]. The broader Hylan Boulevard Transportation Improvement Study mapped the S79 corridor from the Staten Island Mall and Richmond Avenue area through Hylan Boulevard toward Bay Ridge, Brooklyn [[cite:2012_03_15_brt_hylan_meeting_slides#p010_c0006|Richmond Avenue corridor map]].

As part of the S79 SBS launch on September 2, 2012, bus lanes were installed on Richmond Avenue in two segments: between Platinum Avenue and Forest Hill Road, and between Shirley Avenue and Hylan Boulevard [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p002_c0005|Bus lane locations on Richmond Avenue]]. These bus lanes are in effect at all times and are located to the left of the curb lane, functioning as offset bus lanes [[cite:2012_08_hylan_start_of_service_flyer#p002_c0020|Offset bus lane details on Richmond Avenue]].

The 2014 Hylan Boulevard final report described these as offset (second-lane-from-curb) bus lanes that preserve curbside parking and loading activity [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p010_c0004|Offset bus lane description]]. On Richmond Avenue near the Staten Island Mall, approximately 0.6 miles of full-time offset bus lanes were installed from Platinum Avenue to just south of Forest Hill Road, converting a four-lane roadway with a wide center median into three travel lanes plus a bus lane and left-turn bays in each direction [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p007_c0002|Richmond Avenue bus lane design details]]. At the southernmost section of Richmond Avenue approaching Hylan Boulevard, a southbound offset bus lane was installed on the final quarter-mile to address morning peak left-turn queues [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p007_c0003|Southbound offset bus lane near Hylan Boulevard]].

Measured results from the first year of SBS service showed the S79 end-to-end travel time decreased from 71 to 62 minutes northbound and from 68 to 55 minutes southbound, a 13-19% improvement [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p014_c0005|End-to-end travel time results]]. On the Hylan Boulevard bus lane segment (which also benefits buses on Richmond Avenue), northbound S79 travel times during the AM peak dropped from 15.5 to 13.4 minutes (14%), while southbound PM peak times dropped from 17.1 to 10.6 minutes (38%) [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p014_c0007|Hylan bus lane travel time improvements]] [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p015_c0001|Travel time comparison table]].

Customer satisfaction surveys conducted before (June 2012) and after (April 2013) SBS implementation found that average self-reported ride times on the S79 decreased from 32.3 to 31 minutes systemwide, and from 54.6 to 46.5 minutes for riders traveling through the Hylan Boulevard segment between Richmond Avenue and Clove Road [[cite:2014_hylan_blvd_final_report#p019_c0003|Customer survey travel time results]]. These results are captured in the [[metric:metric_s79-travel-time-customer-pre|S79 customer self-reported travel time before SBS]] and [[metric:metric_s79-travel-time-customer-post|after SBS]] metrics, as well as in the [[metric:metric_s79-nb-bus-lane-seg-am-pre|northbound AM peak bus lane segment before SBS]] and [[metric:metric_s79-nb-bus-lane-seg-am-post|after SBS]] metrics.
