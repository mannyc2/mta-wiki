The [[corridor:corridor_webster-avenue-sbs|Webster Avenue SBS Corridor]] runs from the Harlem River in Manhattan to The Hub in The Bronx, serving the Bx41 route with approximately [[metric:metric_bx41-daily-riders-22000|22,000 daily riders]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0004|SBS coming to Webster Avenue]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p004_c0004|corridor map]]. Bx41 SBS replaced the Bx41 Limited while the local route remained the same, with SBS fare equal to the local bus fare [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0006|SBS fare details]].

Proposed design features included bus bulbs extending sidewalks to the bus lane, dedicated offset bus and right-turn lanes, pedestrian safety islands and medians, transit signal priority, new bus shelters, off-board fare collection machines, and local bus stops on the same blocks as SBS stations wherever possible [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0008|bus bulbs]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0009|pedestrian amenities]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0010|dedicated bus lanes]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p001_c0013|transit signal priority]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p002_c0009|station design details]].

In August 2012 the project team surveyed [[metric:metric_business-survey-158-businesses|158 businesses]] near Bx41 Limited stops, finding that [[metric:metric_business-survey-over-85-pct-deliveries-v2|over 85% rely on Webster Avenue curb space for deliveries]] and that customer parking and double parking were concerns, information intended to guide DOT in improving curb regulations [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p002_c0011|business survey]]. The project schedule ran from a Design Ideas phase in Winter 2011-12 through Draft Corridor Plan development in Spring 2012, refinement in Fall 2012, and Final Design and Implementation from Winter 2012-13 to 2013-2014 [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p003_c0004|project schedule]].

DOT and NYCT hoped to achieve on Webster Avenue the results seen on earlier SBS routes, including [[metric:metric_sbs-trip-time-reduction-15-20-pct|15% to 20% faster trips]], [[metric:metric_sbs-stop-time-reduction-33-pct|33% reduction in time spent at bus stops]], [[metric:metric_sbs-other-travel-time-reduction-10-pct|10% reduction in all other travel time]], [[metric:metric_sbs-customer-satisfaction-95-pct-v2|over 95% customer satisfaction]], and a [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-10-pct_6|10% ridership increase on the entire route]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p003_c0007|SBS success metrics]] [[cite:2012_10_webster_sbs_newsletter#p003_c0008|hope to achieve similar results]].
