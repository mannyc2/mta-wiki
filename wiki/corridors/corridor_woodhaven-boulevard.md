## Corridor Role

Woodhaven Boulevard should be read as the Woodhaven-specific corridor page, not a replacement for the broader Woodhaven/Cross Bay Boulevards corridor page. The strongest corridor limit here is Queens Boulevard to 107th Avenue: the Better Buses source describes proposed transit and pedestrian safety improvements on that stretch, delivered through three capital projects beginning with Phase B from Union Turnpike to 107th Avenue. [better_buses#p001_b0001] Older SBS design packets split the Woodhaven work into Queens Boulevard-to-Union Turnpike and Union Turnpike-to-Rockaway Boulevard workshop segments. [2015_04_23_brt_woodhaven_dw2_draftplans#p001_c0003] [2015_04_16_brt_woodhaven_dw1_draftplans#p001_c0003]

A 2024 capital-project deck describes Woodhaven Boulevard as a major bus corridor hosting Q52/Q53 SBS, with 30,000+ daily riders, connections to Queens Boulevard and the Rockaways, 8 subway lines, and 20 local/express bus routes. The map note in the same source says Q52 SBS and Q53 SBS provide the same service and stops between Broad Channel and Rego Park. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p004_c0002] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p004_c0003]

## SBS Design And 2017 Build-Out

In 2015, NYCDOT and MTA said they had chosen main-road bus lanes for Woodhaven Boulevard and offset bus lanes for Cross Bay Boulevard after data collection, site visits, and outreach; the stated design goal was to organize the road for drivers, pedestrians, and bus riders. [2015_04_01_brt_woodhaven_newsletter_1_spring2015#p001_c0004] The Woodhaven design centered on main-road lanes and median stations: service roads would be added between Eliot Avenue and Park Lane South, bus riders would board SBS, local, and express buses at median stations, and medians would double as pedestrian refuges. [2015_04_01_brt_woodhaven_newsletter_1_spring2015#p002_c0006]

The 2024 capital materials treat the 2017 Q52/Q53 SBS launch as the point when 6.85 miles of Woodhaven Boulevard bus lanes were installed: 3.95 miles of offset lanes from Queens Boulevard to Park Lane South, 1.53 miles of median lanes from Park Lane South to Rockaway Boulevard, and 1.37 miles of curbside lanes from Rockaway Boulevard to North Conduit Avenue. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p005_c0002] The same package lists bus boarding islands for 12 stops, bus stop amenities, pedestrian safety measures at 14 intersections, and new ADA-compliant pedestrian refuges at 11 intersections. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p006_c0003] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p007_c0003] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p008_c0003] [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p009_c0003]

## Reported Outcomes

The 2024 decks report post-implementation transit and safety results, but they should be treated as source-stated summary metrics rather than independently recalculated outcomes. Reported transit results include average bus travel-time improvement of 3 minutes in each direction, a 10% improvement, on-time performance improvement up to 80%, and northbound Q52/Q53 late arrivals shrinking from 52% of the time to 10% of the time in the 2019-vs-2017 comparison. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p011_c0002] Reported safety results include pedestrian injuries down 20%, cyclist injuries down 80%, motor-vehicle crashes down 36%, and total incidents down 33%. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p012_c0003]

## Capital Follow-Up

The same capital-project materials frame the next Woodhaven work as a sequence of three projects: Phase B from Union Turnpike to 107 Avenue in design with estimated 2026 construction; a Woodhaven Boulevard/Metropolitan Avenue project from Yellowstone Boulevard to 77 Avenue in scoping with estimated 2028 construction; and Phase C from Queens Boulevard to 66 Avenue in conceptual status with construction TBD. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p016_c0002] Phase B would maintain the alignment and operating hours of existing bus lanes, upgrade 16 bus stops, 17 sidewalk extensions, 12 shortened crossings, and 38 medians/median tips, and add 7 crossings, 3 raised crosswalks, 75+ street trees, and 10,000+ square feet of landscaped area. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb9_dec2024#p021_c0003] A September 2024 CB5 deck listed Phase B next steps as design completion in mid-2025 and construction start in early 2026. [capital_woodhaven_blvd_capital_projects_cb5_sept2024#p033_c0002]
