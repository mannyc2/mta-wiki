[[corridor:corridor_dekalb-av|Dekalb Av]] is a corridor in Brooklyn studied by NYC DOT and MTA for bus and safety improvements along the B38 route [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0002|NYC DOT and MTA seek to improve bus service along the B38 route]]. The corridor runs in two segments: Malcolm X Blvd to Cumberland St, and Cumberland St to Flatbush Av [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p013_c0001|Dekalb Av: Malcolm X Blvd to Cumberland St]] [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb3_dec2024#p014_c0001|Dekalb Av: Cumberland St to Flatbush Av]].

The B38 route carries 18,000+ average daily riders ([[metric:metric_b38-daily-ridership-18000|B38 average daily riders]]) [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|18,000+ average daily riders]], but bus speeds drop as low as 4 mph ([[metric:metric_b38-speed-4-mph|B38 bus speed as low as 4 mph]]) [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|as low as 4 mph]] and average around 4-8 MPH ([[metric:metric_b38-speed-4-8-mph|B38 speed range on Dekalb-Lafayette corridor]]) on the corridor [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p005_c0002|around 4-8 MPH on Dekalb-Lafayette corridor]]. There were 858 injuries on the corridor in the past five years ([[metric:metric_corridor-injuries-5yr|Injuries on corridor in past 5 years]]), including 51 severe injuries or deaths [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p004_c0003|858 injuries, including 51 severe injuries or deaths]]. Dekalb and Lafayette Aves are in a Vision Zero priority area, and Lafayette Av is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p010_c0002|Dekalb and Lafayette Aves are in a Vision Zero priority area]].

During the Summer 2024 G Train shutdown, temporary bus lanes along portions of DeKalb and Lafayette Aves increased speeds up to 21% on DeKalb Av ([[metric:metric_dekalb-speed-increase-21pct|Speed increase on DeKalb Av from temporary bus lanes]]) and up to 5% on Lafayette Av ([[metric:metric_lafayette-speed-increase-5pct|Speed increase on Lafayette Av from temporary bus lanes]]) [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p026_c0002|Up to 21% on DeKalb Av, up to 5% on Lafayette Av]]. NYC DOT is incorporating lessons from that pilot into a permanent design study [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p026_c0002|DOT is incorporating lessons learned]].

The study area has 215,000 residents; 18% live below the poverty threshold, 63% are Hispanic, Latino or non-white, and 9% have a disability ([[metric:metric_disability-rate-9pct|Population with a disability]]) [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p009_c0003|215,000 people, 18% poverty, 63% non-white, 9% disability]]. Citywide, bus rider median annual income is $30,000 ([[metric:metric_bus-rider-median-income-30k_3|Bus rider median annual income]]), compared to $47,000 for drivers ([[metric:metric_driver-median-income-47000|Driver Median Annual Income]]) [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p009_c0003|$30,000 vs. $47,000]].

Community outreach began with Community Board meetings in December 2024; a project proposal is planned for Spring 2025, with implementation targeted for Summer/Fall 2025 [[cite:dekalb_lafayette_cb2_dec2024#p027_c0002|December 2024 through Summer/Fall 2025 timeline]].
