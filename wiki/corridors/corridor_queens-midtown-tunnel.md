The [[corridor:corridor_queens-midtown-tunnel|Queens Midtown Tunnel]] is an MTA TBTA tunnel connecting Manhattan and Queens [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p023_c0003|QMT traffic data in May 2023 facility table]]. During the 12-month period ending May 2023, the tunnel recorded 1.68 total collisions per million vehicles, down from 3.54 in the prior June 2021-May 2022 period, and 0.20 injury collisions per million vehicles, down from 0.35 [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p035_c0005|total collision rates by facility table]][[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p035_c0003|injury collision rates by facility table]].

The tunnel is protected by 20-ton steel portal flood doors measuring approximately 29 feet wide by 14 feet high and nearly 2 feet thick, tested annually by TBTA Central Maintenance crews working with MTA Construction & Development partners [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p011_c0009|flood door specifications and testing]]. In the lead-up to hurricane season, flood door testing is conducted at each of the eight tunnel portals at the Queens Midtown and Hugh L. Carey tunnels [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p010_c0013|flood door testing at QMT and HLC portals]]. Additionally, a stop log system at the Queens Midtown Tunnel provides permanently deployable flood protection designed to a flood elevation 4 feet higher than the levels experienced during Superstorm Sandy in 2012, and is tested annually in May [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p012_c0002|stop log system at QMT]].
