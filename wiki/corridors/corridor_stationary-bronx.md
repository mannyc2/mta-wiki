This corridor groups six Bronx streets where the New York City Department of Transportation installed stationary bus lane cameras through 2023 as part of the DOT stationary camera program [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p007_c0004|Bronx header]][[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p007_c0005|Bronx corridor list]]. The six streets are 149th Street, Bronx River Avenue, East 161st Street, East 163rd Street, Fordham Road, and Webster Avenue [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p007_c0005|Bronx corridor list]]. Three of these corridors (149th Street, Fordham Road, and Webster Avenue, marked with asterisks in the source) also had mobile ABLE cameras in use through 2023, operated by the MTA [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p007_c0012|ABLE cameras also in use]].

Citywide, stationary bus lane cameras were installed at 188 locations across all five boroughs through 2023 [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p006_c0004|188 locations]]. The stationary camera system, implemented by NYC DOT, uses fixed cameras mounted above bus lanes that capture primarily driving-in-the-bus-lane violations, as distinct from the MTA's ABLE mobile cameras which capture no-standing and no-parking violations [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p009_c0001|stationary vs ABLE violation types]]. The [[corridor:corridor_stationary-bronx|Bronx stationary camera corridors]] are one of six borough-level corridor groups listed in the 2024 NYC Bus Lane Camera Enforcement report covering 2022-2023 [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p003_c0003|report scope]]. During that period the DOT spent $8,773,020 in 2022 ([[metric:metric_dot-operating-cost-2022|DOT stationary camera operating cost 2022]]) and $8,761,035 in 2023 ([[metric:metric_dot-operating-cost-2023|DOT stationary camera operating cost 2023]]) on stationary camera operating costs [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0001|DOT operating costs]].

The report notes that the graduated fine structure applies to all stationary camera violations: a first-offense base fine of $50, escalating to $100 for the second, $150 for the third, $200 for the fourth, and $250 for subsequent offenses within a 12-month period [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p003_c0001|graduated fine structure]]. Stationary violations had a 92% not-challenged rate in 2022-2023 ([[metric:metric_stationary-not-challenged-pct|Stationary violations not challenged percentage]]), with 6% challenged and ruled guilty [[cite:bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p016_c0002|stationary violation outcomes]].
