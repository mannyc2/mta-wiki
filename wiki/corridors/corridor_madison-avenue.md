[[corridor:corridor_madison-avenue|Madison Avenue, East 23rd Street to East 42nd Street, Manhattan]] is a northbound bus priority corridor in Manhattan where NYC DOT has proposed extending existing double bus lanes south from East 42nd Street to East 23rd Street [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p006_c0002|project scope]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb6_jun2025#p016_c0002|proposal details]]. Bus lanes on Madison Avenue north of 42nd Street to 60th Street have existed since 1981 [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|study area background]], and the corridor is paired with existing southbound double bus lanes on 5th Avenue [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p006_c0002|pairing with 5th Ave]].

The study area carries 92,000 daily bus riders across 34 bus routes, including M1, M2, M3, M4, and Q32 local buses plus 29 express bus routes from other boroughs [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|ridership and routes]]. Bus speeds in the corridor are slow, with [[metric:metric_local-speed-5-7-mph-6-10am-oct2024|local buses averaging 5.7 mph]] and [[metric:metric_express-speed-7-5-mpg-6-10am-oct2024|express buses averaging 7.5 mph]] during weekday AM peak hours (October 2024), using M1, M2, M3, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, and BxM10 data [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0002|speed table]] [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p010_c0005|data note]]. Overall speeds have been recorded as low as 4.5 mph [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p004_c0002|low speeds]].

The proposed design adds two 11-foot bus-only lanes on the east side of Madison Avenue while maintaining a 12-foot curbside parking or rush-hour travel lane and one 11-foot general travel lane on the west side [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p016_c0003|cross-section]]. The project also includes updated curb management retaining approximately 78% of existing parking spaces [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p018_c0002|curb management]] and Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) expansion covering future Madison Avenue bus lanes [[cite:madison_ave_e23_st_e42_st_cb5_may2025#p019_c0002|ACE expansion]].

The NYC Streets Plan Update 2026 identifies extending the Madison Avenue double bus lanes south from 42nd Street to 23rd Street as a priority project to resume after prior delays [[cite:streets_plan_update_2026#p002_c0002|streets plan priority]]. Earlier planning for this corridor dates back to at least 2008, when NYC DOT presented a Madison Avenue double bus lane extension to 23rd Street as a bus-priority / hot-spot treatment paired with the 34th Street corridor and the citywide BRT program [[cite:080421_brt_cb5_34th_street_fullboardpresentation#p005_c0003|2008 presentation]] [[cite:080421_brt_cb5_34th_street_fullboardpresentation#p003_c0008|BRT program map]].
