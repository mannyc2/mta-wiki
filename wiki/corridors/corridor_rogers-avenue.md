[[corridor:corridor_rogers-avenue|Rogers Avenue]] in Brooklyn serves as the northbound corridor for the B44 Select Bus Service north of Flatbush Avenue, paired with southbound service on Nostrand Avenue [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0004|Rerouting to Rogers Avenue]] [[cite:2010_06_08_brt_nostrand_cb1#p008_c0007|Northbound on Rogers]]. The corridor extends approximately 9.3 miles from the Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay [[cite:2010_06_08_brt_nostrand_cb1#p007_c0002|Corridor extent]]. As part of the B44 SBS implementation in November 2013, the northbound route was shifted from New York Avenue to Rogers Avenue, reducing the route length from 9.9 to 9.5 miles and allowing the bus to benefit from a wider street with multiple travel lanes [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0005|Rerouting benefits]].

Both Nostrand Avenue and Rogers Avenue have been identified as Vision Zero priority corridors due to a high rate of pedestrian injuries and fatalities; in the two years before SBS implementation there was an average of 583 traffic injuries per year between Atlantic Avenue and Flatbush Avenue on these two streets [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p008_c0003|Vision Zero]]. The width of Rogers Avenue helped the B44 SBS avoid delays from parking maneuvers and double-parked vehicles, and allowed for the installation of bus lanes and bus bulbs that reduced travel times [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0005|Rerouting benefits]]. Fifteen bus bulbs were constructed in total across the project corridors, eight on Nostrand Avenue and seven on Rogers and Bedford Avenues [[cite:brt_nostrand_progress_report_june2016#p019_c0007|Bus bulb count]].

The B49 local route also uses Rogers Avenue and shared in the benefits from the SBS infrastructure. [[metric:metric_b49-travel-time-am|B49 AM peak travel times]] in the bus lane section decreased by 7% (from 26.5 to 24.6 minutes) between Fall 2012 and Fall 2015, and [[metric:metric_b49-travel-time-pm|B49 PM peak travel times]] decreased by 11% (from 30 to 26.7 minutes) over the same period [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0006|B49 travel time table]] [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p025_c0008|B49 travel time claim]]. The B44 Local service was maintained on New York Avenue to continue serving local destinations there [[cite:brt_nostrand_progress_report_june2016#p015_c0005|Local service maintained]]. Commercial delivery windows were also implemented on Rogers Avenue, typically 10 AM to 12 PM on the east curb and 12 to 2 PM on the west curb, to keep the travel lane clear for buses [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p017_c0003|Delivery windows]].
