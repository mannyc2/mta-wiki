This segment of the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevards corridor at Queens Blvd and Hoffman Dr was described in a May 2015 presentation to Community Board 6 as part of the proposed Select Bus Service (SBS) project, with planned improvements including designated bus-only station areas and enhanced bus stops and transfers [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0005|Queens Blvd and Hoffman Dr]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0006|– Designated bus-only station areas – Improved bus stops / transfers]]. The corridor design summary placed this segment between Roosevelt Av / Broadway Av (which had no bus lanes) to the north and Woodhaven Blvd (which received main road bus lanes with median stations) to the south [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0003|Roosevelt Av / Broadway Av]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0007|Woodhaven Blvd]].

The larger Woodhaven / Cross Bay SBS corridor was approximately [[metric:metric_corridor-length-14-miles-2015|14 miles]] long from Woodside to the Rockaways, based on the existing Q52/53 LTD bus route, serving about [[metric:metric_daily-riders-30000-2015|30,000 daily bus riders]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p008_c0002|• Based on the existing Q52/53 LTD bus route]]. The corridor was designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with over [[metric:metric_vision-zero-injuries-3000-2015|3,000 injuries]] and [[metric:metric_vision-zero-fatalities-22-2015|22 fatalities (17 pedestrian)]] between 2009 and 2013, including [[metric:metric_cb6-fatalities-8-2015|8 fatalities (6 pedestrian)]] within Community Board 6 [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p012_c0002|• Vision Zero Priority Corridor]]. Existing Q53 northbound buses were found to be stopped almost half of the time, spending only 57% of trip time in motion, 25% at red lights, and 18% at bus stops, with one-way travel varying between 55 and 85 minutes [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p011_c0002|• One-way travel time can vary by up to 30 minutes]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p011_c0003|Status Percentage In Motion 57% Red Lights 25% Bus Stops 18%]].

Community feedback gathered during the outreach process had identified unreliable and slow bus service during rush hour, dangerous pedestrian crossings, and congestion as key concerns along the corridor [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p010_c0002|1. Bus service is unreliable and slow during rush hour]]. The selected Concept 2 design, featuring main road bus lanes with all buses using median stations, was projected to make the Q52/Q53 SBS service [[metric:metric_speed-improvement-25-35-pct-2015|25-35% faster]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p036_c0003|Faster bus service – bus only lanes and off-board fare collection will making riding the Q52/Q53 25-35% faster]]. Traffic simulation for the Concept 2 scenario showed northbound (peak direction) AM peak travel times improving from approximately 11.5 minutes in the no-build scenario to approximately 8 minutes [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p030_c0005|A bar chart comparing average vehicle travel time]].
