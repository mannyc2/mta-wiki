[[corridor:corridor_church-av|Church Av]] is a 0.9-mile bus priority corridor in Brooklyn running from Flatbush Avenue to Ocean Parkway, split into a commercial core from Flatbush Avenue to East 16th Street and a residential and neighborhood-stores section from East 16th Street to Ocean Parkway [[cite:church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p006_c0002|Church Ave corridor limits]]. It was identified by NYC DOT as a 2019 priority project because the B35 bus route, which operates along Church Avenue, had peak bus speeds of only 4.25 mph—well below the Brooklyn peak average of 6.7 mph—and carried approximately 45,000 daily riders [[cite:church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p005_c0002|Church Ave priority project rationale]]. Bus passengers make up [[metric:metric_bus-passengers-72-percent-am-peak|72% of travelers during the AM peak]] and [[metric:metric_bus-passengers-69-percent-pm-peak|69% during the PM peak]] on Church Avenue between East 19th Street and Ocean Avenue [[cite:church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p009_c0002|Bus mode share AM/PM peak]]. The B35 is Brooklyn's third busiest bus route with 29,000 daily riders and connects to the D, N, R, F, G, B, Q, 2, and 5 subway lines; the B103 limited-stop route also serves the corridor with 13,000 daily riders [[cite:church_ave_public_forum_jul2019#p007_c0002|B35 and B103 ridership]]. A shopper survey on Church Avenue found that 63% of respondents walked, 55% took the bus, and 49% used the subway to reach the corridor [[cite:church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p016_c0004|Shopper survey mode shares]]. Church Avenue was one of 49 Brooklyn corridors studied for potential bus priority improvements under the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0004|49 corridors list]]. The NYC DOT project proposed curbside bus lanes active 7 AM to 7 PM Monday through Saturday from Marlborough Road to East 7th Street, with parking and loading-zone mitigations [[cite:church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p028_c0002|Curbside bus lane proposal]]. Bus lanes were activated on October 23, 2019, repurposing 98 parking spaces part-time and adding 59 new metered spaces on side streets [[cite:church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p030_c0005|Bus lane activation date parking mitigation]]. A double-parking survey found that 72% of double-parked vehicles were personal cars, and 73% had not attempted to find a legal metered space [[cite:church_ave_project_update_oct2019#p015_c0004|Double parking survey]].
