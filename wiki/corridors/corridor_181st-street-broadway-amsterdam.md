The [[corridor:corridor_181st-street-broadway-amsterdam|181st Street Busway corridor (Broadway to Amsterdam)]] is a roughly half-mile busway on 181st Street between Broadway and Amsterdam Avenue in the Washington Heights neighborhood of Manhattan [[cite:meeting_doc_37081#p041_c0004|meeting_doc_37081]]. It launched on April 26, 2021, and serves five MTA bus routes: Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, and Bx36, benefiting over 66,000 daily riders [[cite:meeting_doc_37081#p041_c0004|meeting_doc_37081]]. Like other NYC busways, it prioritizes buses and trucks while permitting local access for other vehicles, which must exit the corridor at the next required turn [[cite:busways#p001_b0001|busways]].

The busway was celebrated at its launch by Mayor Bill de Blasio, MTA Chairman Patrick J. Foy (then Chairman Rodriguez), and NYC DOT Commissioner Hank Gutman as a milestone for bus customers and a key to New York's pandemic recovery [[cite:meeting_doc_40141#p042_c0003|meeting_doc_40141]]. The project was developed through a community advisory board process beginning in July 2020, with the final plan presented in early 2021 [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p005_c0003|181st_street_jun2022]].

Following the launch, operational adjustments included signal timing changes along Broadway in June 2021, bus stop consolidation on 181st Street and St. Nicholas Avenue in October 2021, and gantry signage installations in December 2021 to improve visibility of busway regulations [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p007_c0003|181st_street_jun2022]] [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p007_c0005|181st_street_jun2022]] [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p008_c0003|181st_street_jun2022]]. An on-street bus rider survey conducted in April 2022 collected community feedback, and a door-to-door business walkthrough in May 2022 documented concerns about customer confusion, signage, parking, and commercial loading hours [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p012_c0002|181st_street_jun2022]] [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p011_c0005|181st_street_jun2022]].

Based on community feedback, a proposal was made to reduce busway hours to 6 AM to 8 PM, seven days a week, and to make the pilot busway permanent [[cite:181st_street_jun2022#p018_c0003|181st_street_jun2022]]. The corridor is also designated as an ABLE (Automated Bus Lane Enforcement) route, disproportionately serving low-income New Yorkers, similar to the four ABLE routes in the Bronx [[cite:meeting_doc_113986#p015_c0009|meeting_doc_113986]].
