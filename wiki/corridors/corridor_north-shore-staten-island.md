The [[corridor:corridor_north-shore-staten-island|Staten Island North Shore Corridor]] was identified as a priority corridor during BRT Phase II public workshops held in spring 2009 [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0008|North Shore Corridor heading]]. Workshop participants were interested in using a BRT route to stimulate economic development in this area, specifically naming Richmond Terrace and Snug Harbor as growth areas for development and tourism [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0009|North Shore Corridor participant comments]]. Participants suggested extending the corridor south to the Teleport or Fresh Kills, and also proposed connections to NJ Transit's Hudson Bergen Light Rail (HBLR) in Bayonne [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0009|North Shore Corridor participant comments]].

At the Staten Island workshop held on June 16, 2009 in New Dorp, 39 participants took part, one of the smaller turnouts across the seven borough workshops [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p010_c0005|Workshop attendance table]]. More broadly, Staten Island workshop participants emphasized the need for better inter-borough transit connections and suggested a transit hub at Grasmere linking the North Shore corridor with the Staten Island Railroad [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0005|Staten Island overall trends]]. The corridor was discussed alongside Hylan Boulevard and a proposed Bayonne Connection as Staten Island's most-discussed transit needs [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0007|Hylan Boulevard heading]][[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p024_c0010|Bayonne Connection heading]].
