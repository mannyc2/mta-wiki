[[corridor:corridor_west-of-hudson-186701|West of Hudson]] is the Metro-North Railroad service territory west of the Hudson River, with operations distinct from the railroad's three East-of-Hudson lines (Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven). The July 2025 Mid-Year Forecast from MTA Metro-North Connection reports [[metric:metric_west-hudson-farebox-revenue|West of Hudson Farebox Revenue]] of $11.754 million for the January-through-December period, alongside [[metric:metric_west-hudson-ridership|West of Hudson Ridership]] of 1.134 million riders over the same span [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p007_c0002|p007_c0002]]. For context, the combined East-of-Hudson farebox revenue totaled $643.975 million and ridership reached 70.898 million over the same period, making West of Hudson service a relatively small share of Metro-North's overall financial picture [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p007_c0002|p007_c0002]]. The corridor receives subsidy support through both [[metric:metric_mta-subsidy-total|MTA Subsidy]] ($710.867 million across all Metro-North cash operations) and [[metric:metric_odot-subsidy-total|ODOT Subsidy]] ($265.187 million), with the ODOT funding reflecting a reimbursement arrangement between the Ohio Department of Transportation and Metro-North [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p005_c0002|p005_c0002]]. The corridor is served by a dedicated maintenance workforce reported as "Metro-North West" positions, which ranged from 29 to 31 monthly throughout the forecast year [[cite:meeting_doc_186701#p008_c0002|p008_c0002]].
