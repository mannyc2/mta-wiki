The Red Hook corridor was identified through BRT Phase II public workshops as an underserved area in Brooklyn that is difficult to reach without a car--four out of five participant tables at the Downtown Brooklyn workshop raised it, and it was also frequently discussed at the Flatbush workshop [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p018_c0010|Red Hook workshop discussion]]. The most common participant suggestion was for a BRT route between Red Hook and Downtown Brooklyn; participants also proposed routes from Red Hook to Long Island City, Greenpoint, and the Williamsburg East River waterfront [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p018_c0011|Red Hook suggested routes]]. The corridor was frequently added as an extension to other proposed corridors, including the Bushwick to Downtown Brooklyn Corridor and the Williamsburg East River Waterfront Corridor [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p018_c0011|Red Hook corridor extensions]]. The corridor map included in the BRT Phase II outreach report identifies the Red Hook area as a participant-identified underserved area warranting service need evaluation [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p019_c0003|Brooklyn service needs map]]. The [[corridor:corridor_red-hook-brooklyn|Red Hook Corridor]] remains a concept identified during this 2009 outreach phase; no specific route or implementation timeline was established from these workshops.
