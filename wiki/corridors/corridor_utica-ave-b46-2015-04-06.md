The [[corridor:corridor_utica-ave-b46-2015-04-06|Utica Avenue B46 corridor]] was first identified as a candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study and later appeared on the short list of 16 potential future BRT corridors [[cite:2015_04_06_brt_uticaave_cb3_presentation#p003_c0002|2009 BRT Phase II Study identification]] [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p004_c0002|short-list ranking #5]]. The B46 is the second-busiest bus route in New York City, carrying nearly 50,000 passengers per day and providing crucial connections to the 3, 4, A, and C subway lines along with 30 bus routes [[cite:2015_02_04_brt_utica_cac2_presentation#p006_c0003|second-busiest route, 50k daily riders]] [[cite:brt_uticaave_cb18_transportation_committee_2016#p003_c0003|subway and bus connections]]. The corridor is also designated a Vision Zero Priority Corridor [[cite:brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p033_c0002|Vision Zero Priority Corridor]].

Early bus lane treatments on Utica Avenue produced measurable improvements. In summer 2014, bus lanes, left-turn bays, and turn restrictions were installed from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue, resulting in a [[metric:metric_b46-travel-time-decrease-8-15_3|B46 Limited/local travel time decrease of 8% to 15%]] during peak periods, along with a 20% to 25% reduction in general vehicle travel times in the peak direction [[cite:brt_uticaave_cb18_transportation_committee_2016#p005_c0002..p005_c0004|2014 bus lane treatments and travel time results]].

The corridor later became part of the Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave transit improvements identified as a 2019 Better Buses Action Plan priority project [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p007_c0002|2019 priority project]]. A southbound curbside bus lane from Fulton Street to Atlantic Avenue was implemented in 2019, producing substantial bus speed improvements of 54% in the AM peak and 106% in the PM peak [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p010_c0002|Fulton to Atlantic bus lane results]]. A further extension of the southbound bus lane from Chauncey Street to Fulton Street was proposed for spring 2020 [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p015_c0002|Chauncey to Fulton proposed bus lane]] [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p017_c0002|implementation timeline]].
