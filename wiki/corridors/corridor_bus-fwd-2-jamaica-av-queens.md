[[corridor:corridor_bus-fwd-2-jamaica-av-queens|Jamaica Av, Queens (153rd St to 162nd St)]] is a local bus priority corridor identified in the Bus Forward plan published by NYC DOT [[cite:bus_forward_2#p001_c0003|NEW YORK CITY DOT]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p001_c0001|BUSFORWARD 2017]]. The corridor runs along Jamaica Avenue in Queens from 153rd Street to 162nd Street [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Queens Jamaica Av from 153rd St to 162nd St]]. It was listed among corridors across all five boroughs where NYC DOT and the MTA planned to address issues affecting bus speed, with the caveat that the list was subject to change during planning and evaluation [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|NYC DOT and the MTA will be addressing issues affecting bus speed on corridors such as the following]]. The Bus Forward program's local bus priority corridors were expected to move through planning and outreach with implementation targeted for 2018 and 2019 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|expected implementation in 2018 and 2019]].

The corridor was identified as part of a citywide effort to improve local bus service using tools from the bus priority toolkit, such as bus lanes, bus stop spacing adjustments, and transit signal priority, complementing the larger Select Bus Service expansion program [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0003|Using tools from the bus priority toolkit]]. At the citywide level, [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-avg-bus-speed|average bus speed citywide was 7.4 mph]] and [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-commercial-districts-speed|bus speed in busy commercial districts fell below 4 mph]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|average speed is less than 4 mph]]. The corridor identification drew on analyses of long and slow bus trips, slow bus speeds, and bus reliability using Bus Time data from October 2014 and 2016 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p015_c0002|MTA identified the slowest corridors in each borough during the weekday PM peak based on October 2014 and 2016 speeds]]. The corridor's documentation in the available source record consists solely of its listing in the Bus Forward corridor table [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Queens Jamaica Av from 153rd St to 162nd St]]; no subsequent design, implementation, or monitoring materials specific to this segment have been observed in the source corpus.
