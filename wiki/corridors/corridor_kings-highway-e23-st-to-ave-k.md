The [[corridor:corridor_kings-highway-e23-st-to-ave-k|corridor_kings_highway_e23_st_to_ave_k]] is the segment of Kings Highway from E 23 St to Ave K in Brooklyn, described as a wide, boulevard-style portion of the street with narrow, deteriorating medians that serve as bus stops and large, complex intersections [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p029_c0001|p029_c0001]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p029_c0002|p029_c0002]]. It functions as a major east-west transit corridor, serving the B82 Local, B82 Limited, B7 Local, school buses, and coach buses; during AM and PM rush hours, buses run approximately once per minute along Kings Highway [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p029_c0002|p029_c0002]].

[[metric:metric_b82-daily-riders|metric_b82_daily_riders]]: system-wide, the B82 Limited and Local serve roughly 32,000 daily riders [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p003_c0003|p003_c0003]]. [[metric:metric_b82-cb15-daily-riders|metric_b82_cb15_daily_riders]]: within Community Board 15, approximately 12,500 people use the B82 Limited, B82 Local, or B7 service each day [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p014_c0005|p014_c0005]]. [[metric:metric_daily-transfers-bq-b82|metric_daily_transfers_bq_b82]] and [[metric:metric_daily-transfers-f-b82|metric_daily_transfers_f_b82]]: daily subway transfers from the B82 include roughly 1,760 riders at the B-Q station and 620 riders at the F station [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p014_c0003|p014_c0003]].

[[metric:metric_b82-in-motion-pct|metric_b82_in_motion_pct]], [[metric:metric_b82-signal-delay-pct|metric_b82_signal_delay_pct]], [[metric:metric_b82-bus-stop-dwell-pct|metric_b82_bus_stop_dwell_pct]], and [[metric:metric_b82-other-delays-pct|metric_b82_other_delays_pct]]: travel-time analysis on the corridor found that buses are in motion only 48% of the time, with signal delay accounting for 29%, bus stop dwell time for 21%, and other delays for 2% [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p010_c0002|p010_c0002]]. Over half of each bus trip is spent delayed, crawling, or at a bus stop [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p010_c0003|p010_c0003]]. Across the SBS program citywide, [[metric:metric_sbs-speed-improvement-10-30-pct|metric_sbs_speed_improvement_10_30_pct]], [[metric:metric_sbs-ridership-increase-10-pct_4|metric_sbs_ridership_increase_10_pct]], and [[metric:metric_sbs-customer-satisfaction-95-pct_2|metric_sbs_customer_satisfaction_95_pct]] have documented typical improvements of 10-30% faster bus speeds, roughly 10% ridership increases, and customer satisfaction of 95% [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p004_c0004|p004_c0004]]. This corridor segment was the subject of a proposed B82 Select Bus Service plan presented to CB 15 in May 2017, which included curbside and main-road bus lanes, safety improvements at intersections, and reconfiguration of Quentin Road at E 12 Street [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p023_c0006|p023_c0006]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_cb15_may2017#p027_c0001|p027_c0001]].
