The [[corridor:corridor_manhattan-av-cb9-feb2025|Manhattan Avenue (110th to 113th Streets)]] corridor is part of the NYC DOT BETTERBUSES program and carries over 40,000 daily bus passengers across the M3, M7, and M116 routes, serving as a critical crosstown connection to the B and C subway lines [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p004_c0002|corridor description]]. The study area covers Manhattan Avenue between 110th and 113th Streets, and Morningside Avenue between 113th and 114th Streets [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p004_c0002|CB9 study area]]. Roughly 10,400 residents live within 1/8 mile of the corridor [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p011_c0003|10,400 residents]]. Of those households, 76% are car-free, far exceeding the city average of 55%, and 86% of residents commute via transit, walking, or biking, compared to 71% citywide [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p011_c0003|car-free and commute statistics]]. Travel mode split is 69% transit, 14% walking, 12% car, and 3% biking [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p011_c0005|travel mode split]].

Bus service along the corridor experiences severe delays, with buses moving at less than 5 mph in some segments, driven by traffic congestion and double-parking [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p008_c0002|slow bus speeds]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p010_c0002|slow speeds near 116th Street]]. Between 2019 and 2023, 32 people were injured in crashes in the study area, including 7 pedestrians, 10 bicyclists (2 severe), and 14 motor vehicle occupants (1 severe) [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p012_c0002|crash injuries]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p012_c0006|injury summary table]].

A prior Morningside Avenue Vision Zero Pedestrian Project completed in 2014 achieved a 40% reduction in pedestrian injuries through pedestrian islands, left-turn lanes, stop signs, signal infrastructure, and crosswalks [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p007_c0002|Vision Zero project]]. The stated vision for the corridor includes making bus service fast, reliable, and on-time; improving safety; ensuring residential access; adding commercial vehicle loading; and exploring programmable space, while challenges cited include traffic congestion, double-parking, and circulation needs [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0003|vision]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0005|challenges]]. Potential treatments under consideration include curbside bus lanes, improved stop spacing, and queue jump signals [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p018_c0002|curbside bus lane]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p018_c0004|stop spacing]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p018_c0006|queue jump signal]]. The project timeline targets continued outreach through Winter/Spring 2025 with implementation anticipated in Summer/Fall 2025 [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p024_c0003|implementation timeline]].
