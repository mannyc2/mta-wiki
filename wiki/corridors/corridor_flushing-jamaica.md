The [[corridor:corridor_flushing-jamaica|Flushing-Jamaica Corridor]] was first identified in the initial NYC BRT study (2006) and the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II report (2009), and was later listed as a BRT Phase II candidate corridor [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p007_c0003|Flushing to Jamaica corridor identified in initial NYC BRT study (2006) and BRT Phase II report (2009)]]. Early planning materials described the corridor as characterized by "long and slow bus trips" [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p005_c0003|Characterized by "long and slow bus trips"]]. It serves multiple bus routes: Q20A/B and Q44 on Main Street, Q25 and Q34 on Kissena Boulevard/Parsons Boulevard, and Q65 on 164th Street [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0003|Flushing-Jamaica Corridor served by]] [[cite:2014_06_11_brt_flushingjamaica_cac1_presentation#p016_c0004|Q20A/B, Q44 on Main St, Q25, Q34 on Kissena Blvd/Parsons Blvd, Q65 on 164 St]].

Main Street carries roughly [[metric:metric_daily-riders-main-street|42,000 daily riders]] on the Q44 (Jamaica to West Farms) and Q20A/B (Jamaica to College Point) routes [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p006_c0004|42,000 daily riders on]]. Parsons Boulevard/Kissena Boulevard carry roughly [[metric:metric_daily-riders-parsons-kissena|26,000 daily riders]] on the Q25 (Jamaica to College Point) and Q34 (Jamaica to Whitestone) routes [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p006_c0008|26,000 daily riders on]]. The Q44 was prioritized for a Select Bus Service upgrade based on its ridership—over 29,000 daily—its role as a key connection between Queens and the Bronx, and the opportunity for on-street bus priority on Main Street [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p007_c0002|Q44 was prioritized for SBS upgrade based on]] [[cite:2015_06_15_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb8_presentation#p007_c0003|Ridership – Over 29,000 per day on Q44]].

The BRT Phase II study (2009) listed the Flushing-Jamaica Corridor as one of 16 potential future BRT corridors, appearing in the candidate table as candidate 14 [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p004_c0002|Table of Routes Short List for Next Select Bus Service Route ... 14 Flushing-Jamaica Corridor]]. Public outreach for the Phase II effort included seven workshops across the city and an online survey, with results summarized in a June 2010 public input report [[cite:brt_future_corridors#p002_c0006|Public input was the second key component of the BRT Phase II effort]] [[cite:brt_future_corridors#p002_c0007|The workshop and survey participants provided DOT and NYCT with a wealth of feedback]].
