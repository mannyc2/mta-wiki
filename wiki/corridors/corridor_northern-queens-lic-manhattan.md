The [[corridor:corridor_northern-queens-lic-manhattan|Northern Queens-LIC-Manhattan Corridor]] was identified through BRT Phase II public outreach conducted by NYC DOT and MTA NYCT in spring 2009 [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p022_c0007|Northern Queens-LIC-Manhattan Corridor heading]]. The report combined the pre-identified DOT/NYCT Queens-Manhattan corridor with a participant-identified northern Queens east-west corridor; almost all tables at the Jackson Heights workshop (the [[metric:metric_queens1-workshop-attendance|Queens I Workshop]] with 52 participants) suggested extending the Queens-Manhattan corridor east via Northern Boulevard to connect with Downtown Flushing [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p022_c0008|combined corridor description]]. Some participants suggested further extensions east to Nassau County and south to Jamaica along the proposed Jamaica to Flushing Corridor [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p022_c0008|further extensions]]. Participants saw this corridor as filling a need for improved intra-Queens transit and as relieving congestion on the 7 train and the Long Island Expressway [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p022_c0008|relieving congestion]]. The corridor was among several Queens priority corridors discussed at both the Jackson Heights and Jamaica workshops, where participants also identified a broader need for east-west service across northern Queens extending from Manhattan to the Nassau County border [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p022_c0005|east-west service need]].
