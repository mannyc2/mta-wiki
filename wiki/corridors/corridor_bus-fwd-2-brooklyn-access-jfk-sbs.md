This corridor was identified as candidate "J" ("Brooklyn Access to JFK") among the 21 candidate SBS corridors that NYC DOT and the MTA selected for the next phase of Select Bus Service expansion [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|21 candidate SBS corridors, corridor J]]. The corridors were chosen through data analysis, workshops, on-street surveying, and online feedback as part of the Bus Forward / Citywide Transit Plan process [[cite:bus_forward_2#p004_c0002|21 candidate corridors identified]]. The corridor spans Brooklyn and Queens, connecting to JFK Airport [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|Brooklyn Access to JFK listed under Brooklyn]].

The same Bus Forward source describes the SBS toolkit used across the program, including off-board fare collection, bus-only lanes, transit signal priority, and improved station amenities; those tools are program context, not proof that each treatment had already been selected for Brooklyn Access to JFK [[cite:bus_forward_2#p008_c0002|bus priority toolkit]]. Across the existing SBS network, routes had seen 10-30% faster bus speeds, roughly 10% ridership increases, and 95% customer satisfaction, while the source projected that adding the proposed corridors would expand SBS coverage from over 309,000 daily riders to over 800,000 riders [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0004|SBS performance metrics]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p007_c0001|existing and proposed SBS ridership]].

The [[corridor:corridor_bus-fwd-2-brooklyn-access-jfk-sbs|Brooklyn Access to JFK (SBS candidate J)]] corridor is one of several Brooklyn SBS candidates, alongside Church Avenue, South Brooklyn East-West, Downtown Brooklyn to Ridgewood, and Flatbush Avenue [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|Brooklyn corridor list]]. Future planning and outreach phases were expected for implementation in 2018 and 2019 under the Bus Forward program [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|local bus priority implementation timeline 2018-2019]].
