This corridor is the intersection of Lexington Avenue and 125th Street in Manhattan, documented through a January 6, 2015 presentation by MTA to Community Board 11's Transportation Committee [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p001_c0002|CB11 Transportation Committee meeting]]. A diagram of the intersection shows curb extensions (bus bulbs) at all four corners with bus stop icons at each quadrant along Lexington Avenue [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p013_c0002|intersection diagram with bus bulbs]].

Curb extension proposals for the 125th Street and Lexington Avenue intersection were first presented to CB11 in 2013, and MTA noted that the community had an opportunity to shape the proposal while still in the design phase [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p013_c0003|125th/Lex curb extension proposals presented 2013]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p013_c0004|opportunity to shape proposal]]. As of the January 2015 meeting, next steps included refining the Lexington Avenue curb extension proposal and conducting additional traffic and transit monitoring [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p021_c0002|refine curb extension proposal]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p021_c0004|parking observations and traffic data]].

The M60 Select Bus Service launched on May 25, 2014, operating along 125th Street with dedicated offset and curbside bus lanes installed between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p008_c0002|service launched May 25, 2014]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p007_c0003|bus lanes Lenox Av to Second Av]]. The [[metric:metric_m60-buslane-7to10am-2014|M60 SBS travel time in the bus lane section 7-10 AM Oct 2014]] of 10.2 minutes compared favorably to 14.8 minutes in October 2013, and SBS trips were reported as 32-34% faster in the bus lane section [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p010_c0003|M60 bus lane section travel times chart]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p010_c0004|SBS 32-34% faster]]. Local bus trips (Bx15/M100) were 7-20% faster in the same section, with the [[metric:metric_bx15-m100-buslane-7to10am-2014|Bx15/M100 travel time 7-10 AM Oct 2014]] at 11.5 minutes versus 13.5 minutes in October 2013 [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p011_c0003|Bx15/M100 bus lane section travel times chart]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p011_c0004|local buses 7-20% faster]]. End-to-end M60 SBS eastbound travel time from West 106th Street to LaGuardia Airport improved 11-14%, with the [[metric:metric_m60-tt-7to10am-endtoend-2014|7-10 AM end-to-end time]] falling from 59 to 52 minutes [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p009_c0003|end-to-end travel time table]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p009_c0004|SBS 11-14% faster end-to-end]].

Eastbound taxi speeds on 125th Street between Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue were generally faster after implementation, though midday and early-afternoon speeds were unchanged or slightly slower, with the [[metric:metric_taxi-speed-5to8pm-2013|5-8 PM taxi speed Oct 2013]] at approximately 8.5 MPH rising to 9.2 MPH in October 2014 [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p012_c0003|taxi speed chart]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p012_c0004|taxi trips faster EB, unchanged/slower WB]].

The supporting treatments associated with this corridor include dedicated bus lanes, parking and commercial loading changes (metered parking on 125th Street between Fifth and Third Avenues, 200 new parking spaces on 124th and 126th Streets, and 9 new AM commercial loading zones), Bus Time wayfinding panels, and the proposed curb extensions at the Lexington Avenue intersection [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0003|metered parking Fifth to Third Avenues]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0004|200 new parking spaces 124th/126th Streets]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0005|9 new AM commercial loading zones]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p004_c0003|Bus Time wayfinding panel at Madison Av]] [[cite:2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p004_c0004|additional panels 2015]].
