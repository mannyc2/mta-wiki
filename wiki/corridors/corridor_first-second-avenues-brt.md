## Overview

The First Avenue/Second Avenue corridor is the M15 route operating primarily on First and Second Avenues along the East Side between South Ferry and 125th Street. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p004_c0003] [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p004_c0004]

Before SBS, source materials described the corridor as 8.5 miles from South Ferry to 125th Street, served by the M15, with over 57,000 weekday riders and 537,000 residents within a quarter-mile. [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0004] [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0005] [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0006] [2009_09_23_brt_1st2nd_cac2#p014_c0008]

The 2011 progress report describes the M15 as one of New York City's busiest bus routes and attributes slow, unreliable service to heavy traffic congestion, high passenger volumes, and peak-period boarding times that could take several minutes at many stops. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p004_c0003]

## Planning Path

The corridor was selected by the 2006 NYC BRT study, advanced through PlaNYC in 2007, identified as the next SBS corridor in 2008, and launched as M15 Select Bus Service on October 10, 2010. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p007_c0003]

Outreach included CAC meetings, Community Board meetings, public open houses, and door-to-door business outreach before launch. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p007_c0003]

NYCT and NYCDOT implemented M15 SBS on October 10, 2010. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0003]

## Service And Street Design

The service plan replaced M15 Limited with M15 SBS while M15 Local remained 24-hour service. [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p010_c0006] [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p011_c0006]

The launch-period schedule listed M15 SBS as running northbound from 6am to 11pm, southbound from 5am to 10pm, every 4-5 minutes in the peak and every 7-8 minutes off-peak. [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p011_c0006] [2010_09_14_brt_1st2nd_cac5#p011_c0008]

The core SBS package combined proof-of-payment fare collection, new bus lanes, offset bus lanes, route simplification, new buses, improved scheduling, enhanced stations, and better customer information. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0004]

Offset bus lanes were located in the second lane from the right curb and were in effect at all times, while curbside bus lanes typically operated from 7:00am-10:00am and 2:00pm-7:00pm with 10:00am-2:00pm reserved for commercial loading in most locations. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p008_c0006] [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p008_c0007]

The proof-of-payment system used station MetroCard and coin fare machines, receipts, and all-door boarding, and the progress report states that M15 SBS dwell time was reduced by 40%. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p009_c0004]

Bus lane camera enforcement was authorized in summer 2010, fixed cameras were installed along First and Second Avenues after SBS launch, and fixed, bus-mounted, and mobile cameras had issued more than 30,000 violations by July 2011. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p011_c0003] [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p011_c0004]

The street design also added protected bicycle lanes on First Avenue from 1st Street to 34th Street, on Second Avenue from 34th Street to 23rd Street, and on Second Avenue from 14th Street to Houston Street. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p013_c0004]

Pedestrian islands were constructed along First Avenue from Houston Street to 34th Street and on Second Avenue from 34th Street to 23rd Street where street width allowed. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p014_c0003]

## Staged Elements

Bus bulbs were planned as sidewalk extensions to meet the offset bus lane at twelve locations, with completion expected in 2012. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p011_c0007]

Active transit signal priority south of Houston Street was expected by early 2012, while the one-way avenue signal progression already provided passive priority where buses could keep up with traffic. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p012_c0007]

## Outcomes

The 2011 progress report summarized results as a 15-18% travel-time improvement, 9% M15 ridership increase, 21% traffic-injury reduction in sections with full treatments, maintained traffic speeds and volumes, and an 18-177% cycling increase. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p003_c0006]

Red bus-lane paint before SBS launch was associated with a 55% decline in illegal bus-lane driving and a 35% decline in vehicles standing in the bus lane. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p019_c0009]

The progress report's safety table shows total crashes down 7%, crashes with injuries down 10%, and total injuries down 14% in the cited seven-month after period compared with before-period averages. [201111_1st2nd_progress_report#p022_c0004]

A summary table for First Avenue/Second Avenue lists implementation in October 2010, an overall project cost of about $17.5 million, and 18% first-year travel-time savings. [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0016] [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0017] [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0018] [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0022] [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0023]

That same summary itemizes project elements and costs as offset and curbside bus lanes at $5 million, transit signal priority at $1 million, off-board fare payment at $4 million, and bus bulbs at $7.5 million. [brt_routes_fullreport#p012_c0021]
