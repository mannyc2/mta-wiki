This Brooklyn corridor, identified as Malcolm X/Fulton St to Utica Av/Eastern Pkwy, appears in the 2017 Bus Forward plan as one of the local bus priority corridors that NYC DOT and the MTA committed to examining for bus speed improvements. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Bus Forward corridor list showing Malcolm X/Fulton St to Utica Av/Eastern Pkwy]] The plan describes it as running from Malcolm X/Fulton St to Utica Av/Eastern Pkwy in Brooklyn. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Brooklyn corridor listing]] The corridor was identified when the citywide [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-avg-bus-speed|average bus speed]] was 7.4 mph and [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-commercial-districts-speed|bus speeds in busy commercial districts]] were below 4 mph. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|average bus speed 7.4 mph and less than 4 mph in commercial districts]]

Unlike the 21 candidate SBS corridors identified elsewhere in the plan, this corridor falls under the "Improving Local Bus Corridors" initiative, which targets segments where buses are slow, unreliable, or carry long and slow trips, using tools from the bus priority toolkit such as bus lanes, bus stop spacing improvements, and transit signal priority. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0003|description of bus priority toolkit on local bus corridors]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0005|Figure 4 description of targeted segments]] NYC DOT and the MTA expected to examine a first round of these priority corridors through planning and outreach over the following year, with implementation in 2018 and 2019, though the plan cautions that the list of corridors is subject to change during the planning and evaluation process. [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|expected implementation in 2018 and 2019]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|corridor list subject to change]]

Related Brooklyn corridors listed alongside this one in the same plan include Church Av (from Coney Island Av to Flatbush Av) and Van Sinderen Av/Fulton St (to Jamaica Av/Georgia Av). [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|other Brooklyn corridors in Bus Forward plan]]
