The NYC DOT "Bus Lanes in NYC" brochure identifies [[corridor:corridor_buslane-brochure-161st-street|161st Street]] as an example of a street with center-running or median bus lanes in the Bronx [[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0018|Center-running or median bus lanes heading]]. The brochure describes center-running bus lanes as being "in the middle of the roadway" and states that "vehicles are not permitted to enter the bus lanes for any reason while the bus lane is in effect," with right-turns, pick-ups, and drop-offs directed to the service road [[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0019|Center-running bus lane description]]. The other streets cited alongside 161st Street as examples of this bus lane type are Woodhaven Boulevard and Kings Highway [[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0019|Center-running bus lane description]]. This is one of four bus lane types covered in the brochure, alongside curbside bus lanes, offset bus lanes, and busways [[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0015|Curbside bus lane description]][[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0017|Offset bus lane description]][[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0021|Busway description]].

The corridor's mention in this brochure is limited to an example location; no route assignments, service changes, or detailed operational hours for 161st Street are provided in this source. The source is a general-public brochure published by NYC DOT explaining bus lane rules and enforcement citywide [[cite:buslane_enforcement_brochure#p001_c0001|Why a Bus Lane heading]].
