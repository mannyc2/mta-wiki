This corridor covers Bronx River Avenue in the Soundview neighborhood of the South Bronx in the Bronx. On December 3, 2021, the New York City Department of Transportation announced the completion of bus-priority improvements along three miles of Soundview streets — Story Avenue, Bronx River Avenue and White Plains Road — serving [[metric:metric_45000-daily-riders-served|45,000 daily riders]] on MTA buses [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]]. Before the project, service on these corridors was slow and unreliable, with average peak-period speeds at about 5 MPH and daytime bus speeds averaging as low as 5 MPH, captured in [[metric:metric_peak-speed-5-mph-before|peak-speed]] and [[metric:metric_daytime-speed-5-mph-before|daytime-speed]] metrics [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]].

The bus lane along Bronx River Avenue is a traffic-lane bus lane in effect at all times, while the curbside bus lanes on Story Avenue and White Plains Road are in effect Monday–Friday 7 AM to 7 PM [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]]. The project serves MTA bus routes Bx5, Bx36, and Bx39 and was coordinated with the MTA's Bronx Bus Network Redesign [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]]. Treatment components include transit signal priority, painted curb extensions at five intersections for pedestrian safety, updated daytime parking regulations, and angled parking added on adjacent side streets at community request [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]].

Enforcement is handled by the NYPD and DOT automated bus lane cameras with a 60-day warning period followed by fines starting at $50 and increasing to $250 for subsequent violations within one year [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]]. The Soundview bus lanes are part of a broader push: DOT added [[metric:metric_seven-bus-lane-miles-bronx-since-2020|seven bus lane miles in the Bronx since 2020]], including this project and lanes on 149th Street, Edward L Grant Highway, and University Avenue [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]]. Citywide, bus lanes and busways installed that year were speeding the commutes of [[metric:metric_half-million-daily-riders-citywide|over half a million daily riders]] [[cite:soundview_bus_priority_press_release_2021#p001_b0001|DOT press release Dec 2021]].
