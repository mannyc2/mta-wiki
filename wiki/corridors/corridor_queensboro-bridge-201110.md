The [[corridor:corridor_queensboro-bridge-201110|Queensboro Bridge (QBB)]] carries approximately 16,000 bus trips per day, including about 8,000 local riders, and sees 110 buses during the PM rush hour, or one bus every 30 seconds [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|QBB corridor statistics]]. The Q101 provides direct service between Queens and Manhattan, serving as a key transit link for residents of Astoria and Long Island City [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|Q101 service description]]. Local bus routes using the bridge include Q60 (2,710 average weekday riders), Q101 (850), and Q32 (4,340), totaling about 7,900 local riders per day [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p007_c0002|local bus route ridership table]]. Express bus routes, including QM1 through QM24 and X51 through X68, together carry about 7,920 daily riders [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p008_c0003|express bus ridership table]], represented by the [[metric:metric_express-bus-total-daily-ridership|Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB]].

Congestion on the QBB is a significant cause of bus delay. Over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders report that traffic congestion regularly delays their bus [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p004_c0003|congestion delay claim]]. A rider survey found that of those who perceive delay on the bridge, 58% of local riders and 62% of express riders reported delays of 5 to 15 minutes [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|rider survey delay data]]. The most commonly suggested improvements for trip speed were "bus lanes" and "more buses" from local riders, and "bus lanes," "enforcement of bus lanes," and routing suggestions from express riders [[cite:201110_qbb_approach_summary#p012_c0004|rider survey improvement suggestions]].

The NYC DOT conducted the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study beginning in Spring 2010 with data collection and problem identification [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p004_c0003|Spring 2010 phase]], followed by Community Board presentations and preliminary improvement options in Summer 2010 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p004_c0005|Summer 2010 phase]], traffic analysis and option screening in Fall/Winter 2010-2011 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p004_c0007|Fall/Winter 2010-2011 phase]], and findings presented to Community Boards in Spring 2011 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p004_c0009|Spring 2011 phase]]. The study focused on five areas: 60th St and 2nd Ave (bus safety), 57th St and 3rd Ave (bus priority opportunity), Crescent St and Queens Plaza North (pedestrian and bike safety), Thomson Ave and Van Dam St (traffic congestion and pedestrian safety), and Thomson Ave Ramps (traffic congestion) [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p005_c0003|study focus areas]]. Recommendations #1 through #4 were presented to local Community Boards in spring 2011 and implemented in fall 2011 [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p017_c0003|implementation timeline]]. Recommendation #5, concerning express bus priority on the Thomson Avenue ramps, was deferred for additional analysis [[cite:201110_qbb_summary_recommendations#p017_c0005|Recommendation #5 pending analysis]].
