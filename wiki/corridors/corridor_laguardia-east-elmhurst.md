The [[corridor:corridor_laguardia-east-elmhurst|LaGuardia/East Elmhurst Corridor]] was identified by NYCT and NYC DOT as one of 16 potential future Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) corridors and was placed on a short list of three candidates for the next Select Bus Service planning effort, alongside Webster Avenue/Third Avenue in the Bronx and Hillside Avenue in Queens [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0005|short list of three candidates]]. Unlike the other two short-listed corridors, the LaGuardia Airport/East Elmhurst Corridor is "comprised of several inter-related travel markets that could be tied together through one or more BRT services" and was expected to require a separate alternatives analysis with coordination from the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0005|LaGuardia/East Elmhurst description]].

The corridor also appeared in the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis, a study initiated in May 2011 by NYCDOT following a Federal Transit Administration grant awarded in December 2010 [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p004_c0003|FTA grant]] [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p004_c0004|AA begins]]. That analysis noted that bus service to LaGuardia Airport was often slowed by narrow streets and long dwell times, and that much of western Queens lacks easy access to the subway for local travel [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p006_c0005|bus service slowed]] [[cite:110622_lga_aa_slides#p009_c0003|western Queens lacks subway access]]. The outreach report for BRT Phase II found the corridor was cited as a high priority by Queens survey respondents and that the area was considered underserved [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p042_c0004|underserved area]].

During the 2009 public outreach phase, a total of 324 participants attended seven workshops across the five boroughs, and over 650 respondents completed the online survey [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p010_c0004|324 participants]] [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p006_c0008|over 650 respondents]]. Overall, 98% of workshop attendees and 97% of survey respondents expressed support for BRT expansion [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p004_c0011|strong public support]]. Per the BRT Phase II Future Corridors report, for each short-listed corridor NYCT and DOT planned to create a Community Advisory Committee and hold public workshops as part of an extensive community planning effort [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0004|Community Advisory Committee]].
