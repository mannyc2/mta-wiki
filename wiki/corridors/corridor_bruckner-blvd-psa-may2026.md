The [[corridor:corridor_bruckner-blvd-psa-may2026|Bruckner Boulevard (Southern Boulevard to E 144th Street)]] corridor in the Bronx comprises a portion of the Bruckner Boulevard right-of-way between Southern Boulevard and East 144th Street [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0007|Bruckner Boulevard location]].

At the May 20, 2026 MTA Board meeting, staff sought authorization to acquire approximately 8,544 square feet of property within this corridor from the New York State Department of Transportation for $1,200,000 [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0007|property details and compensation]]. The parcel consists of a portion of NYSDOT Map 29, Parcel 71, to be conveyed as Map 74-C, Parcel 118 [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0007|parcel designation]]. The source notes that NYSDOT's determined compensation was less than the MTA's own appraisal [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0011|compensation vs appraisal]].

The acquisition is intended for the construction, operation, and maintenance of an electric power substation in support of the Penn Station Access Project [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0003|PSA project context]]. As part of that project, MTA Construction and Development is modernizing Amtrak's Hell Gate Line infrastructure by realigning tracks, optimizing overhead catenary systems, building electrical power systems including substations, replacing and installing signal equipment, and constructing four new Metro-North Railroad passenger stations along the line [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0009|Hell Gate Line modernization scope]]. The new substation at this location is needed to meet the additional AC power demands on the railroad right-of-way created by increased train traffic [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0010|substation justification]].

The May 2026 Board materials contain no other corridor-specific actions beyond this property acquisition [[cite:meeting_doc_208146#p013_c0007|only corridor action item]].
