The [[corridor:corridor_morningside-av-cb9-feb2025|Morningside Avenue (113th to 114th Streets)]] corridor is the CB9 study area segment of the 116th Street Bus and Pedestrian Priority Project, a joint effort by NYC DOT's BETTERBUSES program and MTA NYCT [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p001_c0004|presented February 2025]] [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p001_c0002|to CB9's Uniform Services & Transportation Committee]]. The corridor carries over 40,000 daily bus passengers across the M3, M7, and M116 routes, providing critical crosstown connections to the 2, 3, 6, B, and C subway lines [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p004_c0002|CB9 study area description]].

The M116 bus operates at less than 4 mph in some segments due to traffic congestion and double-parking [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p006_c0002|bus speeds below 4 mph]]. Within the study area, [[metric:metric_car-free-households-76-percent|76% of households are car-free]] and [[metric:metric_commute-public-transit-walk-bike-86|86% of residents commute via transit, walking, or biking]], both well above city averages of 55% and 71% respectively [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p011_c0003|demographic data from 2022 ACS]]. From 2019 to 2023, the study area recorded 7 pedestrian injuries, 10 bicyclist injuries (2 severe), and 14 motor vehicle occupant injuries (1 severe) [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p012_c0006|injury summary table]].

Project goals articulated at the February 2025 meeting include making bus service fast and reliable, improving safety, ensuring residential access, adding commercial vehicle loading, and exploring programmable space [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0003|vision statements]]; identified challenges include traffic congestion, double-parking, and circulation needs [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_feb2025#p014_c0005|challenges listed]].

By June 2025, a draft proposal had been developed that included a curbside bus lane on Manhattan Avenue (7am-7pm weekdays, reverting to parking evenings and weekends) that would repurpose approximately 25 parking spaces [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p017_c0002|curbside bus lane parking conversion]], conversion of the Morningside Avenue slip lane into pedestrian plaza space offering more than 8,600 ft² of programmable space [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p019_c0002|pedestrian plaza details]], a painted curb extension at Morningside Avenue and 116th Street [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p016_c0001|painted curb extension]], concrete pedestrian islands, and bus stop consolidation [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p015_c0003|stop removal details]]. Implementation was tentatively targeted for Summer 2025, pending engineering review and approval [[cite:116_st_morningside_ave_pleasant_ave_cb9_jun2025#p023_c0002|project timeline]].
