The [[corridor:corridor_lower-montauk-branch|Lower Montauk Branch]] is a two-track, non-electrified rail corridor running 8.5 miles through Queens from Long Island City to Jamaica [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p026_c0003|LIRR Lower Montauk Branch runs 8.5 miles from Long Island City to Jamaica]]. Formerly part of the Long Island Rail Road, infrequent passenger service at 5 stations was discontinued in 1998 due to low ridership; the corridor currently serves only freight trains [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p026_c0003|previous passenger service discontinued in 1998]].

In January 2018, the New York City Department of Transportation published the Lower Montauk Branch Passenger Rail Study, prepared by AECOM, which found that joint passenger-freight operations on the Branch would be feasible [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p008_c0009|initial analysis shows it would be feasible to develop joint passenger-freight operations]]. The study proposed 10 station locations — the two termini at Long Island City and Jamaica plus eight intermediate stations roughly one mile apart [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p004_c0007|recommended 10 station locations]]. The service concept envisions passenger service from 5:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. at 6-minute peak and 15-minute off-peak headways, using FRA-compliant diesel multiple-unit rolling stock to allow shared operation with freight traffic [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p005_c0003|5am to 10pm, 6-min peak and 15-min off-peak headways]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p005_c0007|FRA-compliant DMU recommended]].

Projected ridership for a 2025 build year is [[metric:metric_ridership-weekday-21000|21,000 weekday]] and [[metric:metric_ridership-weekend-13000|13,000 weekend]] daily riders, totaling [[metric:metric_ridership-annual-5820000|5.82 million projected annual riders]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p006_c0003|21,000 weekday and 13,000 weekend daily riders]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p006_c0005|Weekday 21,000 Weekend 13,000 Annual 5,820,000]]. Annual fare revenue is estimated at [[metric:metric_fare-revenue-annual-15m|$15 million in projected annual fare revenue]] based on a $2.75 fare with free transfers to MTA bus or subway [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p006_c0006|annual fare revenues estimated at approximately $15 million]]. Passenger-only capital costs total [[metric:metric_capital-cost-passenger-1111m|$1.111 billion in passenger-only capital costs]] (2017 dollars), comprising transit infrastructure ($914M), fleet ($150M), maintenance equipment ($15M), and a yard/maintenance facility ($32M) [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p006_c0010|Transit Infrastructure $914 Fleet Costs $150 Maintenance Equipment $15 Yard/Maintenance Facility $32 Total Capital Costs $1,111]]. Maintaining freight service alongside passenger operations would require an additional [[metric:metric_freight-infra-cost-10b|$1.0 billion in additional freight infrastructure costs]] for added track and freight yards, for a joint total of approximately [[metric:metric_capital-cost-joint-22b|$2.2 billion in joint passenger-freight capital costs]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p006_c0012|additional $1.0B in upgrades]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p008_c0009|Capital costs while maintaining freight service would total approximately $2.2B]]. Annual operations and maintenance costs are estimated at [[metric:metric_om-cost-1car-48m|$48 million in annual O&M costs for one-car trains]] or [[metric:metric_om-cost-variable-consist-546m|$54.6 million in annual O&M costs for variable-consist trains]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p007_c0007|table showing O&M costs for one-car and variable-consist trains]].

An initial value-capture analysis of five key station areas — Woodhaven Blvd, 80th Street, Metro Mall, Grand Ave/Flushing Ave, and Fresh Pond/Metro Ave — found that tax-increment or development-rights mechanisms could support approximately [[metric:metric_value-capture-bond-total-309m|$309 million in value-capture bond support from five key station areas]], or about 14% of the joint passenger-freight capital cost [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p008_c0002|value capture could support approximately $309 million in bonds]] [[cite:lower_montauk_final_report_jan2018#p008_c0004|table of station-area bond support values]].
