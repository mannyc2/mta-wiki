This corridor runs along Grand Street/Grand Avenue from Metropolitan Avenue to Queens Boulevard in Queens and Brooklyn [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p001_c0001|Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements]]. It carries over 51,000 daily bus passengers on the Q54, Q58, and Q59, with the Q58 being the highest-ridership bus route in Queens [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p004_c0002|Over 51,000 daily bus passengers]]. The corridor provides critical crosstown service and connects to the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot and Central Maintenance Facility, which serve ten bus routes [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p004_c0002|MTA Grand Av Bus Depot & Central Maintenance Facility serve 10 routes]].

The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor and ranks in the top third of the most dangerous corridors in Queens [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p010_c0002|Vision Zero Priority Corridor]]. Between 2019 and 2023, 359 people were injured in crashes on Grand Avenue between Metropolitan Avenue and Queens Boulevard, with 25 severely injured and three fatalities [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0002|359 people injured in crashes]].

Bus speeds are as low as 4 miles per hour in some sections, with slow speeds concentrated near the Grand Street L train station, the Grand Avenue Bus Depot, and the Long Island Expressway [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p008_c0002|Bus speeds as low as 4 MPH]]. Contributing factors include traffic congestion, double parking, and high volumes of commercial vehicles [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p014_c0005|Traffic congestion, double parking & business access needs]].

The study area is approximately 50 feet wide along most of its length, with painted bike lanes between 74th Street and Queens Boulevard but no bike facilities in other sections [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p012_c0002|Street width around 50 feet]]. The area lacks direct subway service, making reliable bus service especially important for transit access [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p007_c0004|Area lacking direct subway service]]. NYC DOT presented safety and bus priority improvement concept designs to Queens Community Board 4 on November 26, 2024 and Queens Community Board 5 on December 17, 2024 [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p001_c0003|November 26, 2024]] [[cite:grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p001_c0003|December 17, 2024]].
