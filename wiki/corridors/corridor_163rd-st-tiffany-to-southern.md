The [[corridor:corridor_163rd-st-tiffany-to-southern|163rd Street]] corridor runs from Tiffany Street to Southern Boulevard in the Bronx. This segment received an eastbound bus lane as part of the Bx6 Select Bus Service improvements [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p008_c0002|corridor definition]]. On this segment, eastbound bus speeds improved by 6-23% after implementation [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p008_c0003|bus speed improvement claim]].

Eastbound weekday travel times on [[corridor:corridor_163rd-st-tiffany-to-southern|163rd Street]] improved across all time periods between October 2016 (pre-SBS Local service) and October 2019 (post-SBS). The 6 AM-10 AM period saw a [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-6-10am-improvement-6pct|6% improvement]], from 1.7 to 1.6 minutes. The 10 AM-3 PM period improved [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-10am-3pm-improvement-12pct|12%]], from 1.7 to 1.5 minutes. The 3 PM-7 PM period saw the largest gain at [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-3-7pm-improvement-23pct|23%]], dropping from 2.2 to 1.7 minutes. The 7 PM-Midnight period improved [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-7pm-midnight-improvement-19pct|19%]], from 1.6 to 1.3 minutes [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p008_c0004|travel time table]].

These travel time reductions on the 163rd Street segment contributed to the broader Bx6 SBS end-to-end improvement of [[metric:metric_bx6-sbs-travel-time-improvement-11-16pct|11-16%]] after the September 2017 SBS launch [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p007_c0002|end-to-end improvement]].

Bx6 combined Local/SBS weekday ridership declined from [[metric:metric_bx6-ridership-2013|24,548 in 2013]] to [[metric:metric_bx6-ridership-2019|21,700 in 2019]], but at a slower rate than the Bronx borough average [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p011_c0004|ridership chart]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p011_c0002|ridership decline claim]].

The eastbound bus lane on 163rd Street was one of several implemented improvements for the Bx6 SBS, along with bus lanes, off-board fare machines, boarding islands, and the 161st Street bus-only tunnel [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p005_c0001|implemented improvements]] through [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p005_c0005|bus-only tunnel]]. Proposed capital treatments for the corridor include curb extensions, bus bulbs, pedestrian islands, bus pads, and bus bulbs at specific intersections such as 163rd Street and Prospect Avenue [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p016_c0003|Prospect Ave bus bulb]] and 163rd Street and Intervale Avenue [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p019_c0003|Intervale Ave curb extensions]].
