## Corridor Scope

125th Street is treated in this record as a Manhattan crosstown bus corridor served by Bx15, M60, M100, and M101. [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0001] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0004] A 2011 weekday boardings table lists 32,630 total boardings on those four routes, with M60 the largest single route at 9,682. [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0007] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0008]

The corridor limits vary by source and stage. The 2013 analysis covered intersections from Morningside Avenue to Second Avenue; the implemented 2014 bus-lane section was between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue; the 2015 plan proposed extending bus lanes west from Lenox Avenue to Morningside Avenue. [2013_03_13_sbs_125th_cb10#p016_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p007_c0003] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p004_c0002]

## Planning Timeline

NYC DOT and MTA NYCT held a September 19, 2012 public workshop to identify issues affecting bus movement on 125th Street and possible solutions to improve bus speed and reliability; 96 people attended. [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p002_c0005] [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p002_c0011] A December 3, 2012 workshop introduced the proposed M60 Select Bus Service and reviewed September input, data analysis, off-board fare collection, dedicated off-set bus lanes, and other potential improvements. [2012_12_03_sbs_125th_workshop_summary#p002_c0005] [2012_12_03_sbs_125th_workshop_summary#p003_c0006]

By spring 2013, the project had moved into design refinement: the April workshop listed next steps to revise the design based on community input, finalize curb regulations and bus stop locations, continue studying pedestrian safety at Lexington Avenue and Amsterdam Avenue, and target implementation in 2013. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p018_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p018_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p018_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p018_c0005] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p018_c0006]

## Problem Definition

The operating problem was severe delay. One 2013 source says buses were stopped over 60% of the time, gives eastbound PM peak bus speed as 2.7 mph, and compares that with a 7.7 mph average NYC bus speed. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0015] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0016] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0017]

Workshop comments tied delays to bus bunching, unreliable M60 service, red lights, traffic near Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Boulevard/St. Nicholas Avenue, RFK Bridge rush-hour traffic, turning vehicles, dwell time, and double parking. [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p004_c0009] [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p005_c0004]

The M60 was not framed as only an airport route. A June 2013 presentation says half of M60 ridership was on 125th Street and only 11% was to the airport; another states that most riders were not traveling to or from LaGuardia Airport. [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p002_c0012] [2013_06_05_sbs_125th_cb9#p003_c0009]

## Treatment Package

The proposed package combined SBS operations, curb management, and traffic operations. SBS features included offset bus lanes, off-board fare collection, bus-lane enforcement, multi-door boarding, transit signal priority, and station/customer-experience upgrades. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0006] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0008] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0012] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0014]

The street design used offset bus lanes that replaced one general travel lane in each direction while maintaining curb access. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0003] The stop plan made M60 SBS limited-stop while preserving local service: M60 SBS stopped at every subway/rail station on 125th Street plus Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue, while Bx15, M100, and M101 local service and local stops remained. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0006]

Curb changes were part of the bus plan, not a side issue. The April 2013 proposal called for more consistent parking regulations, conversion of unmetered parking to one-hour metered parking, replacement of the Third Avenue-to-Second Avenue commercial loading zone with a curbside bus lane, and possible evening metered parking and morning loading zones. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p011_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p011_c0003]

## Implementation And Outcomes

A launch flyer announced M60 Select Bus Service for Sunday, May 25, 2014, with early-May street work for bus-lane painting, parking-regulation updates, and fare-machine installation. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0002] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0002] The same flyer described dedicated bus lanes between Lenox and Second Avenues, bus-lane camera enforcement, curbside parking/loading/bus stops remaining at the curb, new left-turn restrictions at Fifth and Lexington Avenues, and off-board fare payment before boarding. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0004]

A curb-regulation flyer described May 2014 changes as morning loading zones on 10 blocks, 227 new parking spaces on 124th and 126th Streets, longer bus stops, and faster, more reliable service for over 32,000 daily passengers. [2014_125th_st_curb_regulations#p001_c0007]

By January 2015, reported implementation items included 39 fare machines, 6 new bus shelters, M60 SBS stops focused on major destinations and transit connections, Bx15/M100/M101 continuing to stop on every block, metered parking between Fifth and Third Avenues, 200 new parking spaces on 124th/126th Streets, 9 new AM commercial loading zones, dedicated lanes between Lenox and Second, and a May 25, 2014 launch date. [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p003_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p005_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p005_c0005] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p006_c0003] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p006_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p006_c0005] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p007_c0003] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p008_c0002]

Reported results were section-specific. January 2015 slides say M60 SBS trips were 11-14% faster end to end, 32-34% faster between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue, and local bus trips were 7-20% faster in that same bus-lane section; taxi trips were generally faster eastbound and unchanged or slightly slower westbound. [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p009_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p010_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p011_c0004] [2015_01_08_brt_125th_cb9#p012_c0004] An April 2015 parking sample block reported parking-space turnover up 4% and double parking down over 91%. [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p018_c0007]

## Follow-On Work

The June 2015 CB10 update treated the east and west sides differently: SBS upgrades had improved M60 SBS across the corridor, with 32-34% faster service east of Lenox Avenue and 27-36% faster west of Lenox Avenue, while local bus service had improved only on the east side where bus lanes existed. [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p003_c0002] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p003_c0003] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p003_c0004]

The same source proposed extending bus lanes west from Lenox Avenue to Morningside Avenue, keeping parking and deliveries at the curb, using right-turn bays at key intersections, and implementing the west-side bus lane in summer 2015.
