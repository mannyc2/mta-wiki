New York Avenue in Brooklyn served as the northbound route for the B44 Limited bus between Flatbush Avenue and points north, but the B44 SBS was rerouted to Rogers Avenue to avoid the street's constraints [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0004|B44 SBS rerouted to Rogers Avenue]]. The street's narrow width and its two-way operation—unlike the wider one-way configurations on nearby Nostrand and Rogers Avenues—made it especially vulnerable to double parking and congestion that slowed bus service [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p008_c0006|New York Avenue narrow width and congestion]]. After the SBS launch in November 2013, B44 Local service was retained on New York Avenue north of Flatbush Avenue to continue serving local destinations along the street, while the SBS route used the parallel Rogers Avenue corridor [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0005|B44 Local maintained on New York Avenue]]. The rerouting allowed the northbound SBS route to benefit from a wider street with dedicated bus lanes, bus bulbs, and reduced interference from parking maneuvers [[cite:b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p015_c0005|rerouting benefits]]. The [[corridor:corridor_new-york-avenue|New York Avenue corridor]] is thus primarily significant as a former Limited routing that continues to carry local service, while the SBS improvements that transformed northbound travel times were realized on Rogers Avenue instead.
