[[corridor:corridor_rockaway-beach-blvd|Rockaway Beach Boulevard]] is a 3.7-mile primary east-west transit corridor in the Rockaways, Queens, that serves the Q22, Q52/Q53 SBS, and QM16 bus routes with approximately 36,000 daily riders [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p005_c0002|primary east-west transit connections]] [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p041_c0011|3.7 miles, routes, ridership]]. The NYC DOT Better Buses Action Plan identified it as a priority 2019 project due to slow bus speeds, unreliable travel times, high ridership, and community safety requests [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p003_c0002|priority 2019 project reasons]]. Bus speeds along the corridor range from 7.6 mph to 15.1 mph depending on segment [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p010_c0003|slow bus speeds]].

Q52/Q53 Select Bus Service launched in November 2017 with corridor-wide pedestrian safety and transit improvements, producing a 5-8% ridership increase, a 9-10% improvement in SBS travel times, and a 6% reduction in crashes [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p006_c0002|Q52/Q53 SBS launch results]]. However, no street improvements were made specifically within the Rockaways beyond bus service and routing enhancements [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p006_c0002|no street improvements in Rockaways]].

The project area recorded [[metric:metric_rockaway-injury-summary-total-injuries|124 total traffic injuries]] between 2013 and 2017, including 25 pedestrian injuries, 4 bicyclist injuries, and 95 motor vehicle occupant injuries, with 6 killed or seriously injured (KSI) [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p008_c0005|injury summary table]]. Three pedestrian fatalities occurred between 2013 and 2018 at Beach 59 St, Beach 73 St, and Beach 116 St [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p008_c0006|pedestrian fatalities]].

A 2018 Access to Opportunity Study identified numerous locations along the corridor for pedestrian and transit improvements [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p005_c0002|Access to Opportunity Study]]. The NYC DOT presentation to Queens Community Board 14 on June 3, 2019 proposed targeted improvements at six locations: B 116 St, the B 108 St–B 102 St segment, the B 102 St–B 94 St segment, B 90 St, the B 73 St–B 67 St segment, and B 59 St at Beach Channel Drive [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p001_c0003|CB14 presentation date]] [[cite:rockaway_beach_blvd_jun2019#p007_c0003|six project locations]]. Proposed treatments include bus lanes (24/7 and peak-hour), concrete median extensions, painted curb extensions, pedestrian islands, signal timing changes, bus stop relocations, and lane-marking improvements.
