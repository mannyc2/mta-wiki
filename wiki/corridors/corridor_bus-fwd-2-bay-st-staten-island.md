Bay St from Central Av to Victory Blvd on Staten Island was identified as a local bus priority corridor in the Bus Forward plan, a joint effort by NYC DOT and the MTA to improve bus speed and reliability citywide [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0002|Bay St from Central Av to Victory Blvd corridor listing]]. The corridor appeared alongside others in the Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, and Queens on a list of corridors where the agencies would address issues affecting bus speed, with the understanding that the list was subject to change during planning and evaluation [[cite:bus_forward_2#p011_c0001|corridor list subject to change]]. Bay St was also marked on a map of targeted bus corridor segments, color-coded to indicate whether segments suffered from long and slow trips, slow speeds, or unreliable service [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0006|Bay St on targeted segments map]]. The corridor was part of the "Improving Local Bus Corridors" initiative, which complemented the SBS program by applying bus priority toolkit treatments such as bus lanes, stop spacing adjustments, and transit signal priority to local routes [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0001|Improving Local Bus Corridors]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0003|bus priority toolkit for local corridors]]. NYC DOT and the MTA planned to begin examining a first round of these priority corridors with expected implementation in 2018 and 2019 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|expected implementation 2018-2019]]. Citywide metrics from the same report frame the rationale: city buses averaged 7.4 mph, and in busy commercial districts the average speed fell below 4 mph, with nearly 2.5 million daily riders affected [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|average bus speed 7.4 mph and less than 4 mph in commercial districts]].
