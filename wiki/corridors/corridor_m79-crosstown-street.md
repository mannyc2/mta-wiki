The [[corridor:corridor_m79-crosstown-street|M79 Corridor (79th Street Crosstown)]] runs approximately 2 miles across Manhattan on 79th Street, connecting the Upper West Side and Upper East Side. It was identified as a potential candidate for Select Bus Service in the Bus Rapid Transit: Phase II Study in 2009 [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p006_c0002|BRT Phase II Study]]. The corridor carries over 18,000 passengers per day over about 2 miles and serves as a crucial connection to the 1, 6, B, and C subway lines as well as 16 bus routes including the M15 SBS [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p006_c0003|M79 corridor description]].

[[metric:metric_m79-daily-ridership_2|M79 carries over 18,000 passengers per day]] and [[metric:metric_m79-corridor-length_2|M79 about 2 miles]]. At the time of outreach in early 2017, the M79 spent only 43% of its trip in motion, with 26% spent at bus stops, 21% in delays, and 9% crawling below 2.5 mph [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p010_c0002|M79 time breakdown table]]. Overall, over 50% of each trip was spent delayed, crawling, or stopped at a bus stop, and customers waited in long lines to pay on board [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p010_c0003|over 50% delayed, crawling, or stopped]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p010_c0004|long lines to pay on board]]. Five intersections along the corridor — at Broadway, Lexington Avenue, 3rd Avenue, 2nd Avenue, and 1st Avenue — were identified as high-crash Vision Zero locations [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p013_c0005|Vision Zero intersections]].

The SBS program has historically delivered consistent benefits: 10-30% faster bus speeds, about a 10% increase in ridership, more reliable service, 95% customer satisfaction, and safety improvements with crash reductions [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p003_c0004|SBS benefits summary]]. The M79 SBS project followed a sequenced implementation schedule in spring 2017: finalizing street designs and continuing bus stop construction (fare machines, wayfinding, real-time passenger info) from March to early April, beginning roadway and signage work in April, and launching M79 SBS service in late spring 2017 [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p026_c0003|March to early April tasks]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p026_c0005|April roadway and signage work]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p026_c0007|late spring launch]].

Treatment components addressed several intersections along the route, including bus lane additions at W 81 St and Central Park West, a relocated bus layover at W 79 St and Riverside Drive, a dedicated left-turn bay and protected phase at W 79 St and Broadway, signal and turn improvements at W 79 St and Columbus Avenue, and visibility and congestion changes at W 81 St and Columbus Avenue [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p015_c0002|W 79-Broadway treatment]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p016_c0002|W 79-Riverside treatment]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p018_c0002|W 79-Columbus treatment]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p020_c0002|W 81-Columbus visibility and congestion change]] [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p022_c0003|W 81-CPW bus lane treatment]]. The corridor map shows the M79 route running from W 81 Street to E 79 Street, passing through Central Park, with connections to the M86 SBS and numerous other bus routes [[cite:brt_m79_cb7_mar2017#p009_c0002|corridor map]].
