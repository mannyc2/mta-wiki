Kings Highway in Community Board 11 (Brooklyn) is a narrow roadway with one lane in each direction that serves as an important east-west transit corridor connecting to D, N, and F subway stations [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p017_c0001|p017_c0001]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p017_c0002|p017_c0002]]. The B82 route serves 28,000 daily riders systemwide, with about 9,800 of those riders boarding in CB 11 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p003_c0003|p003_c0003]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p012_c0002|p012_c0002]].

As part of the B82 Select Bus Service project presented to CB 11 in February 2018, [[metric:metric_b82-delay-bus-stops-21pct|bus stop delays (21%)]], [[metric:metric_b82-delay-signal-29pct|signal delays (29%)]], [[metric:metric_b82-delay-in-motion-48pct|in-motion time (48%)]], and [[metric:metric_b82-delay-other-2pct|other delays (2%)]] were documented as the composition of bus travel time [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p008_c0002|p008_c0002]]. A large majority of visitors to Kings Highway (75-80%) arrive without a car, on foot or by transit [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p020_c0002|p020_c0002]].

Four bus lane segments were proposed in CB 11, operating part-time (7-10 AM and 4-7 PM): westbound from Bay Pkwy to W 12 St and from W 8 St to W 5 St; eastbound from W 10 St to W 7 St and from Van Sicklen St to McDonald Ave [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p022_c0004|p022_c0004]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p022_c0007|p022_c0007]]. The net parking change from these transit improvements was a loss of 24 spaces in terms of raw count, or one net 24-hour parking spot when accounting for new spaces created along medians and from removing two bus stops [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p034_c0003|p034_c0003]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p024_c0004|p024_c0004]].

Pedestrian safety improvements were also planned at three intersections (Kings Hwy & W 11 St, Quentin Rd & W 11 St, Kings Hwy & W 10 St), converting striped median "dead space" into painted pedestrian refuge areas and creating new parking spaces [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p023_c0003|p023_c0003]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p023_c0004|p023_c0004]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p023_c0005|p023_c0005]].

The B82 corridor was identified as a candidate for Select Bus Service in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p003_c0002|p003_c0002]]. The B82 SBS was planned to replace the B82 Limited, operating the same stops but for more hours of the day [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p010_c0002|p010_c0002]].
