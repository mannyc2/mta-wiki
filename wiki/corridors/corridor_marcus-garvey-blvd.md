[[corridor:corridor_marcus-garvey-blvd|Marcus Garvey Blvd / Albany Av / Dean St]] is one of 17 top-ranked corridors identified for potential bus priority street treatments by the MTA and NYC DOT as part of the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan, published December 2022 [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p001_c0003|Draft Plan 12/01/22]]. The corridor was selected from 49 corridors studied based on criteria including demand for bus service, bus performance, feasibility of street treatments, corridor role in the transit network, and neighborhood demographics [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0008|corridor selection criteria]] [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0002|49 corridors, 17 top-ranked]]. The work is conducted jointly with NYC DOT under the NYC Streets Plan, which seeks to expand bus priority street improvements and bus stop amenities across the city [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0004|NYC Streets Plan partnership]].

The corridor is listed alongside other Brooklyn bus priority corridors such as Flatbush Av, Nostrand Av, Fulton St, Utica Av, and Malcolm X Blvd [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0004|17 top-ranked corridor list]]. As part of the Draft Plan, the proposed network includes 24 routes with frequency improvements in at least one time period [[metric:metric_24-routes-freq-improvement|24 routes with frequency improvements]], and an increase in all-day frequent service coverage to 87% of Brooklyn residents from 77% [[metric:metric_all-day-frequent-access-proposed|87% of Brooklyn residents would have access within 1/4 mile to the proposed all-day frequent network]]. No route-specific proposals for this corridor have been identified in the source document beyond its designation as a top-ranked bus priority study corridor.
