The [[corridor:corridor_bx6-brt-bx6-cb12-may2022|Bx6 Corridor]] is served by the Bx6 Select Bus Service (SBS) and Bx6 local routes, running from the Riverside Dr W Terminal in Washington Heights to the Food Center Dr Terminal in Hunts Point, traversing both Manhattan and the Bronx [[cite:brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p004_c0003|route map with terminals]]. The Bx6 SBS launched on September 3, 2017 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p004_c0002|launch date]].

After SBS launch, end-to-end bus travel times improved by 11% to 16% [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p007_c0002|post-launch speed improvement]]. On 163rd Street, where an eastbound bus lane was implemented, weekday travel time improvements ranged from -6% (6-10AM), to -12% (10AM-3PM), to -23% (3-7PM) as measured from October 2016 to October 2019 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p008_c0004|163rd St EB travel time table]]. The [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-6-10am-improvement-6pct|163rd St EB 6-10AM travel time improvement of -6%]], [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-10am-3pm-improvement-12pct|163rd St EB 10AM-3PM improvement of -12%]], and [[metric:metric_163rd-eb-3-7pm-improvement-23pct|163rd St EB 3-7PM improvement of -23%]] all derive from this data.

Bus reliability also improved, with fewer late arrivals and less bus bunching [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p010_c0002|reliability improvement statement]]. Peak-period bus bunching on the eastbound 6-10AM direction dropped from 22.5% in 2016 (Bx6 Local) to 1.0% in 2019 (Bx6 SBS) [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p010_c0006|bunching data table]]. Bus speeds spiked during the height of the pandemic but have been trending back toward pre-pandemic levels [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p009_c0002|pandemic speed trend]]. Ridership along the Bx6 corridor has been declining, but at a slower rate than the Bronx borough average [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p011_c0002|ridership decline comparison]].

Implemented improvements include bus lanes, off-board fare machines, boarding islands, and the 161st Street Bus Only Tunnel (eastbound) [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p005_c0001|Implemented Improvements section]]. Proposed capital project treatments include pedestrian islands, curb extensions, bus pads, and bus bulbs [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p013_c0001|Bx6 SBS Capital Project Treatments section]]. The project timeline envisioned design finalization in Winter/Spring 2023, procurement in Summer/Fall 2023, and construction start in Winter/Spring 2024 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p020_c0002|project timeline]].

The Bx6 SBS project was presented as a joint effort by MTA New York City Transit, NYC Department of Design and Construction, and NYC Department of Transportation [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p001_c0005|MTA logo]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p001_c0007|DDC logo]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p001_c0008|NYC DOT logo]]. The corridor spans multiple community boards, with presentations made to Manhattan CB 12, CB 10, CB 9 and Bronx CB 2, CB 4, and CB 3 in Spring 2022 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb2_edc_may2022#p020_c0002|community board outreach schedule]].
