[[corridor:corridor_throop-av-flushing-av|Throop Av / Flushing Av / Bergen St]] is a Brooklyn bus corridor identified by NYC DOT as one of the 17 top-ranked corridors for potential bus priority street improvements during the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign process [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0002|This process identified 49 corridors to be studied for potential bus priority street improvements, including 17 top ranked corridors.]]. The corridor appears as number 16 on the NYC DOT Brooklyn Bus Priority Corridors map, listed together with the full set of top-ranked corridors in the Draft Plan published December 2022 [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p021_c0002|Legend: ... 16 Throop Av / Flushing Av / Bergen St 17 Utica Ave]]. The identification process evaluated factors including demand for bus service, bus performance, feasibility of street treatments, the corridor's role in the transit network, and neighborhood demographics and equity metrics [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0008|Demand for bus service... Bus performance... Feasibility... The corridor's role in the transit network... Neighborhood demographics and equity metrics]]. As part of the redesign, the MTA is working together with NYC DOT to expand bus priority improvements on corridors throughout Brooklyn [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0003|As part of redesigning the Brooklyn Bus Network, we are working together with NYC DOT to expand bus priority improvements on corridors throughout the city.]]. Further specifics about planned treatments or routing changes on this corridor were not detailed in the Draft Plan document.
