[[corridor:corridor_kissena-parsons-boulevards-flushing-jamaica-2014|Kissena/Parsons Boulevards]] was one of two corridors considered for Select Bus Service between Downtown Flushing and Jamaica, identified in the NYC Bus Rapid Transit Phase I (2006) and Phase II (2009) studies [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010|BRT studies identified corridor]]. The corridor serves the Q25 LTD and Q34 bus routes [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010|Q25 LTD and Q34 routes]], and together with the parallel Main Street corridor carries roughly 68,000 daily riders across four bus lines [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010|68,000 daily riders]].

The Kissena-Parsons corridor is approximately 8.4 miles in length [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0009|8.4 miles]]. Bus service on the corridor faced severe congestion: limited-stop buses Q44LTD and Q25LTD were delayed during peak periods nearly 50% of their travel time, with 29% of delays caused by buses stuck in traffic or at red lights [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0013|peak delays nearly 50%]]. Overall bus speeds averaged less than 10 mph, with congestion hot spots including Parsons Boulevard itself [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0013|bus speeds below 10 mph]].

Public outreach began with a Community Advisory Committee kickoff in July 2014, followed by two public workshops in September and October 2014 that brought together 42 residents, bus riders, community board members, transit advocates, and elected officials [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p002_c0002|42 attendees at workshops]]. Community feedback highlighted dangerous crossings at Kissena Boulevard and Main Street, heavy pedestrian congestion, and safety concerns for cyclists on Kissena Boulevard [[cite:2014_brt_flushingjamaica_workshopsummary#p004_c0014|Kissena and Main Street crossings dangerous]]. The project is a joint effort between NYC DOT and MTA New York City Transit [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0020|MTA logo]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p006_c0022|NYC DOT logo]].

Proposed SBS features under consideration included dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare collection, transit signal priority, improved station amenities, and real-time passenger information [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0004|Dedicated Bus lanes]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0009|Faster Fare Collection]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0013|Signal Priority for Buses]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0011|Improved Station Amenities]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p002_c0015|Real-time Passenger information]]. Design alternatives included curbside bus lanes (as seen on Utica Avenue) and offset bus lanes (as seen on Webster Avenue) [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p012_c0001|CURBSIDE BUS LANE]] [[cite:2015_01_22_brt_flushingjamaica_pw3_boards#p013_c0001|OFFSET BUS LANES]].
