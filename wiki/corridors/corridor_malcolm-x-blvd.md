[[corridor:corridor_malcolm-x-blvd|Malcolm X Blvd]] is a Brooklyn bus priority corridor listed among 49 corridors studied by NYC DOT for potential bus priority street treatments, and one of 17 top-ranked corridors [[cite:brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0004|49 corridors identified for bus priority study]]. The Better Buses Action Plan identified the Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave segment from Chauncey St to Atlantic Ave as priority project #21, noting that the B46 — Brooklyn's busiest bus route — had received Select Bus Service upgrades in 2016 but that this bottleneck remained, with average bus speeds of 5.2 mph in the AM peak and 3.5 mph in the PM peak and total daily ridership of 42,000 [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0006|B46 priority corridor background]] [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0007|corridor metrics]].

Community Board 3 outreach began with a five-month on-street campaign in 2018 that reached over 800 members of the public and surveyed 619 participants, finding that the B46 was the second-most-used bus route in the district [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p005_c0002|B46 2nd most used route]] [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p005_c0002|outreach campaign details]]. The project was driven by slow bus speeds — as low as 3.4 mph during peak versus a Brooklyn average of 6.7 mph — along with high ridership, unreliability, and buses operating about every two minutes per direction at peak [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p007_c0002|project rationale]].

Phase 1 (implemented July–December 2019) installed one block of a southbound curbside bus lane from Fulton St to Atlantic Ave, with photo enforcement [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p017_c0002|project timeline]]. Results showed bus speeds improved by 54% in the AM peak and 106% in the PM peak, while traffic speeds improved by 18% and 17% respectively; overnight parking was preserved [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p010_c0002|bus lane results]]. Phase 2, proposed for Spring 2020, was to extend the southbound curbside bus lane north from Chauncey St to Fulton St, also preserving overnight parking [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p014_c0002|Chauncey St to Fulton St proposal]] [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p016_c0002|timeline including Spring 2020 implementation]]. The B46 was also slated for an upgrade to longer articulated buses in 2019/2020 [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p019_c0002|articulated bus upgrade]].

The Better Buses Action Plan additionally proposed a queue jump signal phase for northbound and southbound B46 buses at Utica Ave and Atlantic Ave [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0012|queue jump proposal]]. Several NYC DOT presentations tracked the project's progression through CB 3 Transportation Committee and Full Board meetings from May 2019 through March 2020 [[cite:malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p017_c0002|full timeline]].
