[[corridor:corridor_bus-fwd-2-manhattan-crosstown-sbs|Manhattan Crosstown Routes including 14th Street (SBS candidate L)]] was identified by NYC DOT and the MTA as one of 21 candidate corridors across the city for the next phase of Select Bus Service, published in the 2017 Bus Forward report [[cite:bus_forward_2#p004_c0002|21 candidate corridors identified]]. The corridor is designated candidate "L" in the report's list and covers Manhattan Crosstown Routes, with 14th Street named as a specific example [[cite:bus_forward_2#p005_c0001|Manhattan L Manhattan Crosstown Routes (multiple, including 14th Street)]].

The Bus Forward program responded to 2015 City Council legislation (sponsored by Council Member Brad Lander) and additional May 2017 legislation (sponsored by Council Member Ydanis Rodriguez) calling for an updated SBS implementation plan and broader transit improvement strategies [[cite:bus_forward_2#p002_c0001|2015 legislation]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p002_c0002|2017 legislation]]. NYC DOT and the MTA expected the first round of priority corridor planning and outreach to take place over the following year, with implementation in 2018 and 2019 [[cite:bus_forward_2#p010_c0004|implementation 2018-2019]].

The 14th Street corridor within this grouping later received a Transit & Truck Priority Pilot Project implemented by NYC DOT in October 2019, aiming to improve operations of the M14 A/D Select Bus Service while increasing safety on this Vision Zero Priority Corridor [[cite:14th_street_fall2019_monitoring#p002_c0006|14th Street TTP Pilot]]. [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-nearly-2-5-million-daily-riders|Nearly 2.5 million daily bus riders]] citywide experience average bus speeds of only 7.4 mph, and existing SBS routes have shown [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-sbs-speed-increase|10-30% faster bus speeds]] and [[metric:metric_bus-forward-2-sbs-customer-satisfaction|95% customer satisfaction]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0002|bus speeds and ridership]] [[cite:bus_forward_2#p003_c0004|SBS performance metrics]].
