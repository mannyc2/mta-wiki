This corridor covers an eight-block segment of [[corridor:corridor_lexington-avenue|Lexington Avenue, 60th Street to 52nd Street, Manhattan]] in midtown Manhattan, where NYC DOT has proposed upgrading the existing curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p001_c0001|Lexington Avenue Bus Lane Upgrades 60th Street to 52nd Street]]. Eight MTA bus routes travel through this segment — BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM11, SIM22, SIM26, and SIM6 — carrying approximately 71,000 daily riders [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|8 MTA bus routes and 71,000 riders]]. Average bus speed on the corridor is 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|average speed 6 mph overall, 5 mph daytime]].

The existing curbside bus lane is frequently blocked by loading and passenger pick-up/drop-off activity, which contributes to the low speeds [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|existing curbside bus lane often blocked]]. In the past five years, there have been 88 injuries in the project area, six of which were severe [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002|88 injuries, 6 severe]]. Since congestion pricing was implemented, traffic on Lexington has reduced by up to 17% in the AM peak and up to 21% in the PM peak, and NYC DOT expects the proposed bus lane upgrade to accommodate existing traffic volumes [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p005_c0003|congestion pricing traffic reduction and DOT capacity expectation]].

The proposed design converts the curbside bus lane to a dedicated offset bus lane, preserving a curbside lane for quick pick-up/drop-off and a parking lane [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p010_c0007|proposed street cross-section: parking, through lanes, offset bus lane, curbside drop-off]]. Enforcement would use bus-mounted cameras under the MTA Automated Camera Enforcement Program [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_cb8_oct2025#p009_c0003|enforced by bus-mounted camera]]. A similar offset design was implemented on Lexington Avenue from 96th Street to 60th Street in 2019, where bus speeds have since increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35% [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_cb8_oct2025#p007_c0002|similar design implemented 96th-60th Street in 2019, results]]. NYC DOT presented the proposal to Community Board 5 (September 15, 2025), Community Board 6 (October 6, 2025), and Community Board 8 (October 8, 2025), with implementation targeted for Fall 2025 or Spring 2026, followed by curb regulation refinements in 2026 [[cite:lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_cb8_oct2025#p012_c0003|next steps and implementation timeline]].
