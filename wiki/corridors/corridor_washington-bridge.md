The [[corridor:corridor_washington-bridge|Washington Bridge / 181 St]] corridor runs between Amsterdam Avenue in Manhattan and University Avenue in the Bronx via the Washington Bridge, and is designated as a "Future Plan" corridor under the MTA Bronx Bus Network Redesign [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p005_c0003|Bronx Bus Network Final Plan Addendum 2021 table]]. A 2019 NYC DOT presentation to Community Board 5 described the corridor as spanning from Amsterdam Ave to University Ave and carrying five MTA bus routes: Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, and Bx36, with a combined 111,400 weekday riders and 36,800 weekday riders crossing the Washington Bridge itself [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p020_c0003|Washington Bridge corridor profile]].

Average bus speeds on the corridor were reported at 9.9 mph during the AM Peak and 8.2 mph during the PM Peak, with slow bus speeds and traffic congestion described as key issues, especially at the intersections at the ends of the bridge [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p020_c0003|Washington Bridge corridor profile]]. The corridor was one of ten high-ranking Bronx corridors selected by NYC DOT for analysis as part of the MTA Bronx Bus Network Redesign, with four of the ten falling within Community Board 5: Tremont Avenue, University Avenue, Washington Bridge / 181 St, and Fordham Road / 207 St / University Heights Bridge [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p004_c0003|Bronx corridor selection methodology]].

NYC DOT studied a potential treatment of dedicated bus lanes on the Washington Bridge itself, proposing to convert one existing travel lane in each direction to a 10.5-foot bus lane (curb lane on each side) [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p023_c0005|Washington Bridge bus lane diagram]]. The anticipated benefits included increased bus speeds, direct travel time reductions for the 36,800 daily bridge-crossing riders, and improved reliability for all 111,400 riders across the five bus routes [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p023_c0004|Washington Bridge bus lane benefits]]. The next steps outlined for Winter/Spring 2020 included analyzing traffic and bus data, developing draft street design plans, and presenting those plans to Community Board 5 and local stakeholders [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p031_c0003|Winter/Spring 2020 next steps]], with detailed final plans and implementation work planned for later in 2020 [[cite:bx_cb5_projects_dec032019#p031_c0004|Later in 2020 next steps]].
