Use this corridor as the physical spine of the Q52/Q53 SBS record, but keep its length
source-specific. Planning and CB materials describe a 14-mile Woodside-to-Rockaways corridor, while
the Fall 2018 progress report describes an 11-mile route that began service on Nov. 12, 2017.
[2015_01_brt_woodhaven_cb9#p004_c0002] [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p002_c0002]

The completed wave 11 sources consistently frame Woodhaven/Cross Bay as both a high-ridership bus
corridor and a safety corridor. The 2018 progress report says the redesign brought SBS
improvements to 30,000+ daily riders, but also records 129 people killed or seriously injured on
the corridor between 2012 and 2016 and describes continued safety investment after launch.
[brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p002_c0002] [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p026_c0002]

Implemented SBS elements included main-road and offset bus lanes, median stations where service
roads exist, signal timing optimization, left-turn restrictions, off-board fare collection, and
pedestrian safety treatments such as widened medians, refuges, sidewalk extensions, additional
crossing time, and traffic calming. [brt_woodhaven_after_fall2018#p013_c0005]
