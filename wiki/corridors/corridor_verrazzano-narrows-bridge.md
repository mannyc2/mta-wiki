The [[corridor:corridor_verrazzano-narrows-bridge|Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge]] is a TBTA-operated suspension bridge connecting Brooklyn and Staten Island [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p023_c0003|VNB appears in B&T facility traffic table]]. In May 2023, the bridge carried an average of 229,397 weekday vehicles [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p023_c0003|Average Weekday Traffic - VNB May 2023]].

The bridge served as the final leg of the 45th TD Five-Boro Bike Tour on May 7, 2023, when nearly 30,000 cyclists crossed its lower level (closed to motorists) into Staten Island [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p005_c0012|TD Five-Boro Bike Tour crosses VNB]] [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p006_c0004|45th iteration - lower level closure]]. TBTA's coordinated event preparations began months in advance, involving Maintenance, Operations, and Emergency Management teams to ensure safety and efficient restoration of traffic [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p006_c0004|comprehensive event action plan]].

In 2023, TBTA adjusted its Wind Restriction and Coastal and Severe Storm protocols based on structural changes at the Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge, allowing cargo trucks to safely traverse the upper level at certain wind speeds [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p011_c0001|wind restriction adjustments based on VNB structural changes]]. Wind deflectors are installed on the upper level adjacent to the towers to diffuse wind pressure and reduce wind load on vulnerable vehicles [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p012_c0006|wind deflectors on VNB upper level]].

For the 12-month period ending May 2023, the bridge recorded a total collision rate of 6.42 collisions per million vehicles and a collisions-with-injuries rate of 1.83 per million vehicles, the highest among all B&T facilities in both categories [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p035_c0005|total collision rate 6.42 VNB]] [[cite:meeting_doc_115226#p035_c0003|injury collision rate 1.83 VNB]].
