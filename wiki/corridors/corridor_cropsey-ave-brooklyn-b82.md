[[corridor:corridor_cropsey-ave-brooklyn-b82|Cropsey Avenue (Southern Brooklyn)]] is the southernmost segment of the proposed B82 Select Bus Service route, running north from the terminus at Cropsey Avenue and Bay 37 Street in Brooklyn [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p010_c0002|B82 SBS begins at Cropsey Ave & Bay 37 St]]. The B82 Limited was being converted to B82 SBS, with a planned launch in July 2018 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p025_c0005|July 2018 launch]]. The corridor carries [[metric:metric_b82-daily-riders-28000|28,000 daily riders]] on the B82 Limited and Local combined, and roughly 9,800 of those riders travel through Community Board 11 [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p003_c0003|serves 28,000 daily riders]] [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p012_c0002|9,800 CB 11 riders use the B82 each day]].

Along the Cropsey Avenue corridor, B82 delays were attributed to time in motion ([[metric:metric_b82-delay-in-motion-48pct|48%]]), signal delay ([[metric:metric_b82-delay-signal-29pct|29%]]), bus stop dwell time ([[metric:metric_b82-delay-bus-stops-21pct|21%]]), and other delays ([[metric:metric_b82-delay-other-2pct|2%]]) [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p008_c0002|delay breakdown table]]. Safety issues identified in CB 11 included long pedestrian crossings along Cropsey Avenue and difficult bus turns at the Bay Parkway and Kings Highway intersection [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p007_c0005|Key Safety Issues in CB 11]].

The proposed street design at the Cropsey Avenue, Bay 37 Street, and Harway Avenue intersection included a concrete pedestrian refuge, a new concrete bus boarding island, painted curb extensions for shorter crossings, and a daytime bus layover space that converts to overnight parking [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p016_c0002|Cropsey Ave at Bay 37 St 2018 Changes diagram]]. The route also connects to the D, N, F, B, Q, and L subway lines and 21 bus routes [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p003_c0003|connects to subway lines and bus routes]]. The Cropsey Avenue corridor feeds northward into Bay Parkway, Kings Highway, Flatlands Avenue, and Pennsylvania Avenue as part of the broader B82 SBS alignment [[cite:brt_south_brooklyn_b82_feb2018#p011_c0002|proposed B82 SBS route map]].
