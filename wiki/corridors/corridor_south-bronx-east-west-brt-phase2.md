The [[corridor:corridor_south-bronx-east-west-brt-phase2|South Bronx East-West Corridor (Hunts Pt./Soundview)]] was identified as a BRT Phase II candidate through a joint NYCT and NYC DOT planning study initiated in fall 2008 [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p002_c0003|a planning study of a citywide BRT network was initiated in the fall of 2008]]. The study compiled over [[metric:metric_potential-corridors-over-40|40 potential BRT corridors]] from a citywide transit needs analysis and public feedback, then evaluated them on five primary criteria: community feedback, ridership potential, network benefits, bus lane and busway feasibility, and geographic diversity [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0003|NYCT and DOT compiled a list of over 40 potential BRT corridors]].

Public outreach in spring 2009 included seven workshops across the city with over [[metric:metric_workshop-participants-325|325 workshop participants]], representatives from [[metric:metric_community-business-groups-47|47 community and business groups]], staff from [[metric:metric_elected-officials-offices-23|23 local elected officials' offices]], and over [[metric:metric_survey-respondents-600|600 online survey respondents]] [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p002_c0006|Over 325 people participated in the workshops, including representatives from 47 community and business groups, and staff members from the offices of 23 local elected officials]].

From this effort, NYCT and DOT identified 16 potential future corridors, with the South Bronx East-West Corridor listed as number 4 among the BRT Phase II Candidates — distinct from the short list of three corridors (Webster Avenue/Third Avenue, Hillside Avenue, and LaGuardia/East Elmhurst) selected for the next detailed planning effort [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p004_c0002|Table of Routes ... 4 South Bronx East-West Corridor (Hunts Pt./Soundview)]]. The corridor was identified in both the full "Future Corridors" report and the "BRT Phase II Future Corridors" report [[cite:brt_future_corridors#p004_c0002|Table of Routes ... 4 South Bronx East-West Corridor (Hunts Pt./Soundview)]]. Further planning would depend on available funding, community support, and progress on related transit projects, with each corridor expected to receive a Community Advisory Committee and public workshop process [[cite:brt_phase2_future_corridors_rpt#p003_c0004|For each corridor, NYCT and DOT will create a Community Advisory Committee]].
