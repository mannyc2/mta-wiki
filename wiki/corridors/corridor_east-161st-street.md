The [[corridor:corridor_east-161st-street|E 161st Street]] corridor in the Bronx is a major crosstown route serving the Bx6 Select Bus Service (SBS), which launched on September 3, 2017 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p004_c0002|Bx6 SBS Launched September 3, 2017]]. The corridor serves more than [[metric:metric_25000-daily-bus-riders|25,000 daily bus riders]] and passes Yankee Stadium, which sees more than [[metric:metric_3-million-visitors-yearly|3 million visitors each year]] [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|25,000 daily riders and 3 million annual visitors]].

After SBS launch, buses on the Bx6 moved 11% to 16% faster end-to-end [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p007_c0002|11-16% bus travel time improvement]], a [[metric:metric_bus-travel-time-improvement-11to16pct|bus travel time improvement of 11-16%]]. On 161st Street specifically, bus speeds improved by 33% to 43% eastbound [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p009_c0002|33-43% eastbound speed improvement]], a [[metric:metric_161st-st-bus-speed-improvement-33to43pct-eb|161st St bus speed improvement of 33-43% eastbound]]. Bus reliability also improved significantly - eastbound late arrivals dropped from 22.0% in 2016 (local service) to 14.1% in 2017 after SBS launch [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0004|Late buses eastbound 2016 vs 2017]], and bus bunching eastbound during the 6-10 AM peak fell from [[metric:metric_bunching-eastbound-6to10am-2016-22pct|22.5% in 2016]] to [[metric:metric_bunching-eastbound-6to10am-2017-2pct|2.3% in 2017]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0006|Bunching eastbound 6-10AM 2016 vs 2017]].

In March 2026, NYC officials broke ground on a major capital project to reconstruct and redesign the corridor from Ruppert Place to Morris Avenue, including a fully protected center-running bus lane from Concourse Village West to just west of River Avenue [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|Center-running bus lane and project limits]]. The project will add westbound bus-only lanes, convert the existing eastbound bus tunnel under the Grand Concourse to two-way bus-only use, and install concrete boarding islands, curb extensions (bus bulbs), and pedestrian refuges [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|Bus tunnel conversion and pedestrian improvements]]. The capital scope includes full reconstruction of 161st Street from River Avenue to Park Avenue [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p016_c0002|Full reconstruction River Ave to Park Ave]].

Infrastructure improvements include [[metric:metric_370-ada-ramps|370 ADA-compliant ramps]], [[metric:metric_57-new-trees|57 new trees]], approximately [[metric:metric_8000-plantings|8,000 plantings]], more than [[metric:metric_100-lighting-signal-poles|100 new lighting and signal poles]], reconstruction of about [[metric:metric_45000-sq-yards-roadway|45,000 square yards of roadway]], more than [[metric:metric_180000-sq-ft-sidewalk|180,000 square feet of sidewalk]], and over [[metric:metric_4500-ft-water-mains-sewers|4,500 feet of water main and sewer upgrades]] [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|Infrastructure quantities]]. Construction is expected to continue through 2028 [[cite:161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001|2028 completion target]]. The project is implemented by NYC DDC and the project area is in Heat Vulnerability Index categories 1, 2, and 5 [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p001_c0007|NYC DDC]] [[cite:brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p015_c0002|Heat Vulnerability Index 1, 2, and 5]].
