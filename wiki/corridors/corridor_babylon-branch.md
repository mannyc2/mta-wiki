The [[corridor:corridor_babylon-branch|Babylon Branch]] is a branch of the MTA Long Island Rail Road. A LIRR staff summary submitted to the Long Island Committee on October 30, 2024 describes plans for a Thanksgiving Day Program on November 28, 2024 that included extra service originating from and terminating at the Babylon Branch [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0002|Staff Summary]]. On Thanksgiving morning, three extra westbound trains operated from Babylon (along with two from Ronkonkoma and one from Farmingdale) to transport parade-goers into New York City [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0008|Babylon extras]]. On Thanksgiving afternoon, four extra eastbound trains terminated at Babylon (along with four to Ronkonkoma and one to Huntington) for those returning home [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0008|Babylon extras]]. The program included six extra westbound trains total, with four destined for Penn Station and two for Grand Central Madison, and nine early afternoon eastbound extras, with four departing from Penn Station and five from Grand Central Madison [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0007|Thanksgiving service details]]. The same document also described a separate crossing renewal project on the Port Jefferson Branch near Syosset, but no track work was noted on the Babylon Branch itself [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0011|Crossing Renewal]]. Funding for both the holiday service and the track work program was contained in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p002_c0003|Impact on Funding]].
