The [[corridor:corridor_port-jefferson-branch|Port Jefferson Branch]] is a Long Island Rail Road corridor between Hicksville and Cold Spring Harbor [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0011|Port Jefferson Branch Crossing Renewal description]]. A single source is available so far: a staff summary dated October 30, 2024, that documents a crossing renewal program and a Thanksgiving Day service program [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0002|Staff summary header]].

The Syosset-Woodbury Road railroad crossing on the branch was scheduled for rehabilitation during the weekends of December 7-8 and December 14-15, 2024 [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0011|Crossing renewal dates]]. During those weekends, one of two main tracks was taken out of service between Hicksville and Cold Spring Harbor [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0011|Track out of service detail]]. The resulting temporary service adjustments required schedule changes on the Huntington, Port Jefferson, and Ronkonkoma Branches, with connecting Greenport service also adjusted [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p002_c0001|Temporary service adjustments]]. Funding for these projects was drawn from the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p002_c0003|Funding source]]. The current evidence covers only the Hicksville-to-Cold-Spring-Harbor segment where the crossing work occurred; no additional service, ridership, or infrastructure detail for the full branch has yet been captured from this single source [[cite:meeting_doc_157681#p001_c0011|Port Jefferson Branch limits from evidence]].
