The [[corridor:corridor_42nd-st-manhattan|42nd Street Corridor]] is a major commercial crosstown corridor in midtown Manhattan, running from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive, serving transit hubs including Port Authority Bus Terminal, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p008_c0002|42nd Street Corridor description]]. In 2017, it was identified as a Bus Forward priority corridor due to its slow bus speeds [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0005|42nd St Bus Forward priority]], and in April 2019 it was named as one of 24 priority projects in NYC DOT's Better Buses Action Plan [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p004_c0002|BBAP 24 priority projects]].

Average bus speeds along the corridor were 4.0 mph during peak hours, compared to the citywide average of 7.7 mph [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p006_c0003|Area bus speeds in context]]. Eight MTA bus routes across three boroughs use 42nd Street, carrying approximately 16,000 daily passengers with up to 210 buses per hour [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_jun032019#p007_c0003|Importance to bus network]]. Despite using only 33% of the street space, buses carry 79% of the people traveling along the corridor [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p008_c0002|Allocating street space]]. The [[metric:metric_bus-people-79-percent|Buses carry 79% of people on 42nd St using 33% of street space]] metric captures this imbalance. The M42 local route showed eastbound weekday running times of [[metric:metric_m42-eb-weekday-overnight-running-time|18.8 minutes overnight (12-6am)]], rising to [[metric:metric_m42-eb-weekday-6-10am-running-time|33.1 minutes in the 6-10am period]] and [[metric:metric_m42-eb-weekday-10am-3pm-running-time|34.4 minutes from 10am-3pm]], while westbound weekday trips took [[metric:metric_m42-wb-weekday-3-7pm-running-time|35.2 minutes in the 3-7pm period]] [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p010_c0003|M42 running time table]].

The project, jointly presented by NYC DOT and MTA under the Better Buses banner, was presented to Community Boards 4, 5, and 6 in June 2019 and to CB 6 again in September 2019 [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p001_c0002|CB6 Sep 4 2019]] [[cite:42nd_st_cb4_jun192019#p001_c0002|CB4 June 19 2019]]. Proposed treatments included offset bus lanes (24/7), bus queue jump signals (7-second phases), turn bays, quick kurb installations, turn restrictions, and curb management changes [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p010_c0002|WB bus queue jump signal]] [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p010_c0008|Offset bus lanes]] [[cite:42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p012_c0002|Lexington Ave design details]]. The routes served by this corridor include the M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, SIM26, and SIM30 [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p029_c0006|42nd St routes served]].
