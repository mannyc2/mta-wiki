The [[corridor:corridor_main-line-lirr|Main Line corridor]] is a vital segment of the MTA Long Island Rail Road network, encompassing Queens Interlocking where a multi-phased track, switch and signal renewal project is underway [[cite:meeting_doc_196911#p001_c0004|Queens Interlocking renewal described as multi-phased project]]. In advance of a signal cutover planned for later in 2026, signal testing is scheduled for the weekends of February 7-8, February 14-16, and February 21-22, during which two of four main tracks will be out of service within Queens Interlocking [[cite:meeting_doc_196911#p001_c0007|Main Line signal testing dates and scope]]. Once complete, the improved switch configuration and modernized signal system at Queens Interlocking is expected to add new routing options and improve operations through this segment of the Main Line corridor [[cite:meeting_doc_196911#p001_c0007|improved switch configuration and modernized signal system benefits]]. Temporary service adjustments on each testing weekend include bus service replacing train service between Stewart Manor and Hempstead, bypasses at certain stations, and hourly Ronkonkoma service on the Presidents Day weekend, with funding contained in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets [[cite:meeting_doc_196911#p002_c0001|temporary service adjustments described]] [[cite:meeting_doc_196911#p002_c0003|funding sources]].
