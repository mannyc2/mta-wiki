[[corridor:corridor_battery-pl-manhattan|Battery Pl]] is a short corridor in Lower Manhattan, running approximately 0.1 miles from West Street to Broadway, that was identified as one of 24 priority projects under NYC DOT's Better Buses Action Plan released in April 2019 as part of the Mayor's 2019 State of the City agenda [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p002_c0002|Better Buses Action Plan context]]. The corridor was flagged as a priority due to slow bus speeds, unreliable bus travel times, a high volume of buses, and long travel times for passengers [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p003_c0002|Battery Pl identified as priority project]]. The proposal was presented to Manhattan Community Board 1 on October 21, 2019 [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p001_c0002|CB1 presentation date]].

MTA, NYCDOT, and NYPD jointly studied bus and traffic movements in the area [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p004_c0002|Interagency study]]. The study found that Battery Pl delay affects 15 Express bus routes, with the 4-7 pm peak period seeing [[metric:metric_190-trips-peak-period|190 trips]] serving [[metric:metric_5500-daily-customers|over 5,500 daily customers]] [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p004_c0004|Issues identified]]. Additional issues identified included traffic delays accessing West Street, poor curb management and enforcement, and pedestrian safety and comfort concerns at the crosswalk [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p004_c0004|Issues identified]]. The Better Buses Action Plan set a citywide goal to [[metric:metric_improve-bus-speeds-25-percent|improve bus speeds 25% by 2020]] [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p002_c0002|Speed improvement target]].

Several study recommendations were completed, including improved Traffic Enforcement Agent placement and instruction, updated curb regulations on West Street to keep the curb lane clear, stop consolidation on Broadway and Battery Pl, and a queue jump signal on Battery Pl at Broadway [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p005_c0003|Completed recommendations]] [[cite:battery_pl_oct2019#p006_c0003|Completed queue jump and stop consolidation]]. The corridor serves 23 express bus routes including BM1-BM4, QM7/QM8/QM11/QM25, multiple SIM routes, and X27/X28, carrying a total of approximately 28,000 daily riders across all routes [[cite:better_buses_action_plan_2019#p028_c0007|Routes served and ridership]].
