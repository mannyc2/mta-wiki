This segment covers the Broad Channel / Rockaways portion of the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevards Select Bus Service corridor in Queens [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0011|Broad Channel / Rockaways]]. It spans approximately the southern end of the larger [[corridor:corridor_broad-channel-rockaways-2015-05|Broad Channel / Rockaways segment]], which runs about 14 miles from Woodside to the Rockaways and carries roughly [[metric:metric_daily-riders-30000-2015|30,000 daily bus riders]] [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p008_c0002|30,000 daily bus riders, 14 miles long]]. Unlike the Woodhaven Boulevard mainline section to the north, the Broad Channel / Rockaways segment was proposed to have no bus lanes, instead receiving targeted transit priority treatments and improved curbside bus stops [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p020_c0012|No bus lanes, targeted transit priority treatments, improved curbside bus stops]]. The proposed design for the full corridor was presented to Community Board 6 on May 13, 2015, as part of the outreach process for the Woodhaven / Cross Bay Boulevards SBS project [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p001_c0002|Presentation to Community Board 6 | May 13, 2015]]. At the time of the presentation, the corridor was identified as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with over 3,000 injuries and 22 fatalities between 2009 and 2013, including 8 fatalities within Community Board 6 [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p012_c0002|Vision Zero – 3,000 injuries, 22 fatalities, 8 in CB6]]. The Q53 Ltd route, which served this segment, experienced one-way travel times varying between 55 and 85 minutes with buses stopped nearly half the time [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p011_c0002|One-way travel time varies between 55 and 85 minutes]]; northbound trips spent only 57% of time in motion, with 25% at red lights and 18% at bus stops [[cite:2015_05_13_brt_woodhaven_cb6_presentation#p011_c0003|57% in motion, 25% red lights, 18% bus stops]].
