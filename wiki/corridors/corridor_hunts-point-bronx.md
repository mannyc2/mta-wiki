The [[corridor:corridor_hunts-point-bronx|Hunts Point Corridor]] (identified in the source as "Hunts Point Peninsula") was highlighted as a high priority for Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) during the Phase II public workshops held in spring 2009 [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p016_c0009|Hunts Point Peninsula heading]]. All five participant tables identified Hunts Point — particularly its western section — as a high priority for BRT, both for employees who worked in the area and for residents [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p016_c0010|All five tables identified Hunts Point]]. The source notes that Hunts Point was one of the underserved areas that participants most often focused on for new transit service, and that Bronx participants specifically cited it as a neighborhood underserved by the subway system [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p014_c0009|underserved areas like Hunts Point]] [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p016_c0005|highest priority on areas underserved by subway]].

Participants expressed a need for improved transit within Hunts Point, as well as better connections to other areas in the Bronx and beyond. Specific suggestions included converting the Bx6 route to BRT [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p016_c0010|converting Bx6 to BRT]]. The Bronx workshop took place on May 28, 2009 at The Hub, drawing 29 participants — the smallest borough turnout of the seven workshops held citywide [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p010_c0005|Bronx workshop 29 participants]]. Broader survey data from the same Phase II outreach effort showed that 52% of Bronx survey respondents reported riding the bus 4–7 days per week, the second-highest rate among the five boroughs [[cite:brt_phase2_outreach_rpt#p033_c0004|Bronx bus usage 52% 4-7 days/week]].
