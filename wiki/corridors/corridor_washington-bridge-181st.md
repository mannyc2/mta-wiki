The [[corridor:corridor_washington-bridge-181st|Washington Bridge and West 181 Street Corridor]] runs from University Avenue to Broadway and covers both the Washington Bridge and West 181 Street [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p028_c0005|corridor metrics]]. It is 0.8 miles long, serves five routes (Bx3, Bx11, Bx13, Bx35, Bx36), and carries 73,000 combined daily riders [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p028_c0005|corridor metrics]]. The corridor connects neighborhoods across the Bronx with Washington Heights in Upper Manhattan, providing subway connections and inter-city transit access at the George Washington Bridge Bus Terminal, and is highly congested due to its location adjacent to the George Washington Bridge and the Cross-Bronx Expressway [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p028_c0003|background]].

As part of the Bronx Bus Network Redesign planning process, MTA and NYCDOT scored 46 Bronx corridors using criteria including ridership demand, bus speed, and reliability; the Washington Bridge and West 181 Street Corridor ranked among the top ten highest-scoring corridors where bus priority improvements would provide the greatest benefit [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p013_c0004|top corridors list]] [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p013_c0002|corridor analysis methodology]]. West 181 Street is 50 feet wide with one travel lane in each direction, a center turning lane, and curbside parking; during afternoon rush hour the eastbound curbside lane becomes a bus-only lane [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p028_c0004|street configuration]]. The Bx11 and Bx13 were both upgraded to 8-minutes-or-better all-day frequency, sending more buses through this congested corridor and necessitating additional transit priority treatments [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p028_c0004|frequency upgrade]] [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p007_c0001|frequency table]].

The 2021 addendum to the Final Plan treats the corridor in two segments: the West 181st Street portion (Amsterdam Avenue to Broadway) was completed as a busway in April 2021, while the Washington Bridge portion (Amsterdam Avenue to University Avenue) remains a "Future Plan" priority corridor with the same route set [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p005_c0003|addendum status table]] [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p006_c0001|completed projects]].

The [[metric:metric_avg-stop-spacing-existing-bronx|Average Bus Stop Spacing - Existing Bronx]] reported that the Bronx's existing average stop spacing was 882 feet, shorter than international peer systems, and that the Final Plan would increase this to 1,092 feet [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p011_c0002|existing stop spacing]] [[cite:bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p011_c0003|proposed stop spacing]].
