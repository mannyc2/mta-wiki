### [p007_c0001] Corridor Description
[p007_c0002] 9.3 miles from Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay
[p007_c0003] Currently served by B44 bus route
- [p007_c0004] • 44,000 weekday riders – 5 th busiest bus route in city • 5,500 people board in CB9 • Buses travel at an average speed of 7-8 mph
[p007_c0005] Within a ¼ mile:
- [p007_c0006] • 300,000 residents • 62% of households do not own a car (65% in CB9) • 60% of residents commute by transit (67% in CB9, compared to 22% by car, truck, or van)

### [p008_c0001] Project Features
[p008_c0002] B44 Limited will become SBS; B44 Local will continue as before
[p008_c0003] Offset bus lanes for 5 miles, with bus bulbs at stations from DeKalb to Church Aves
[p008_c0004] Transit Signal Priority for 3.8 miles from Flatbush Avenue to Sheepshead Bay

### [p014_c0001] Project Benefits
[p014_c0002] Over the length of the corridor, bus lanes, transit signal priority, and quicker boarding would lead to 25% faster trips.
[p014_c0003] A trip from Empire Blvd to Flatbush Ave would be about 6 minutes faster, from 24 minutes today to 18 minutes with SBS.
[p014_c0004] Bus bulbs at SBS stations would allow easy, level boarding, with more pedestrian space and greenery.
[p014_c0005] Bus service would be more reliable, with less bus bunching.
