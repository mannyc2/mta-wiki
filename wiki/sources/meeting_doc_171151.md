### [p006_c0005] Discussion:
[p006_c0006] It is requested that the Board formally ratify the declaration of an Immediate Operating Need approved by the Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, MTA Procurement, waiving formal competitive bidding pursuant to Article III, Paragraph A, of the All-Agency Procurement Guidelines and Public Authorities Laws 1209 subsection 9(a) and 1265-a subsection 4(a) and approve the award of a three-month miscellaneous service contract to Sovereign Hydroseal East Inc. (“Sovereign”) for the injection of cementitious grout into the tunnel subbase underneath the track bed at Essex St. station.
[p006_c0007] The topography beneath Essex St. station on the J/Z line located on the Lower East Side of Manhattan is built above soil that is often waterlogged due to its proximity to the East River. Routine monitoring of this area detected ground settlement which had the potential to become problematic if not addressed in a timely manner. As a result, an Immediate Operating Need was declared to inject cementitious grout beneath the tunnel subbase to level the track.
[p006_c0008] To facilitate the work, a scope of work was developed that included dewatering of the area, ground coring, injection of cementitious grout under the track, permanent stabilization of four support columns, and post-performance monitoring of the area to ensure the work was successful. Procurement contacted 13 vendors, and the Department of Subways facilitated two site visits for potential contractors to inspect the work area and review the scope of work. Sovereign (1) was the only vendor that proposed on the work; (2) had the capacity and capability to quickly complete the project; and (3) has the experience, technical capability, and resources to perform the work required. One other firm was interested in the work but was unable to mobilize in the required timeframe.
[p006_c0009] Sovereign’s proposal included a firm-fixed price for mobilization, which included the delivery of cement to a staging area on the surface street above Essex St. station, preparation, staging, and coring of the track invert itself to appropriately prepare the area for cementitious grout injection. The pricing to inject the grout was for an estimated quantity of 6,000 cubic feet at a fixed unit price per cubic foot that included all materials, labor, and tools required for successful injection. Stabilization of the support columns were priced as a fixed lump sum per unit. The proposal also included a lump-sum amount for the monitoring of the area to ensure ongoing efficacy of the project. The majority of the work took place from January 18–20, 2025; ongoing monitoring will take place periodically into early April 2025. The total proposal amount of $1,721,650 was found fair and reasonable.
