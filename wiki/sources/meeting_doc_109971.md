### [p005_c0001] Harness technology and data to improve productivity: E-mirrors
[p005_c0002] Collisions involving bus mirrors accounted for ~1,800 collisions in 2022, 25% of all bus collisions
[p005_c0003] Collisions drive costs through increased maintenance for NYCT, and legal settlements
[p005_c0004] In addition, they disrupt service for customers
- [p005_c0005] • On average, each collision means two lost trips for that bus while maintenance takes place
[p005_c0006] Bus is exploring e-mirrors as a solution
- [p005_c0007] • Provide as good or better visibility to drivers leveraging camera footage displayed in the cab • Eliminate physical mirror and associated collisions
> [p005_c0008] A photograph taken from inside a bus cab. On the right side, a vertical digital display is mounted, showing a camera feed of the road ahead. The feed shows a road with a '50' speed limit marking on the pavement. To the left of the display, a physical traffic light is visible through the windshield, showing a red light. The background outside the window shows some buildings and trees. The image illustrates the use of e-mirrors to provide drivers with better visibility and reduce the risk of collisions.

### [p006_c0001] Sharpen management focus on cost drivers: Employee Availability
[p006_c0002] Available days per employee per year declined a total of 20 days between 2001 and 2021
> [p006_c0003] Year Available days per employee 2001 214 2002 214 2003 212 2004 210 2005 209 2006 211 2007 210 2008 212 2009 212 2010 210 2011 209 2012 209 2013 208 2014 207 2015 207 2016 207 2017 207 2018 207 2019 206 2020 187 2021 194
[p006_c0004] Lower availability affects both costs and service:
- [p006_c0005] • Cost . Lower availability requires maintaining reserve staff and paying overtime to backfill critical roles • Service . In '22, ~3% of scheduled Subway and Bus trips cancelled because no operator was available
[p006_c0006] Decline in availability driven by increase in days off for Workers' Compensation and Sick Leave
- [p006_c0007] • This long-term trend predates COVID • Increasingly, small number of employees drive the majority of days off

### [p007_c0001] Sharpen management focus on cost drivers: Employee Availability
[p007_c0002] Efforts underway to improve availability include:
- [p007_c0003] • Recognizing employees with perfect or exemplary attendance • Scaling successful Aware & Care program for employees working with managers improve their attendance record • Joint workgroups with Labor to reduce number of assaults on employees • Addressing employee feedback received from NYCT-wide engagement survey • Safeguard leave programs against abuse to preserve them for employees that need them
[p007_c0004] These efforts are bearing fruit as we have already seen a >2 day improvement in availability over the past year

### [p008_c0001] Develop common standards and best practices: Station Agents
[p008_c0002] Landmark agreement with TWU redefines the role of Station Agents and how they are deployed
[p008_c0003] As a result, new Station Agent program provides:
- [p008_c0004] • Better customer service, including 15 new Customer Service Centers • Increased hourly pay for Station Agents • Lower total costs to NYCT including overtime reduction

### [p009_c0001] Invest capital to reduce operating expenses: Energy savings
[p009_c0002] Reducing energy consumption both lowers costs and greenhouse gas emissions
[p009_c0003] Initiatives we are pursuing include:
- [p009_c0004] • Continuing to install LED lighting throughout Subway system and on trains • Replacing legacy boilers with energy efficient units • Rolling out new Energy Management System, and working with utilities to record actual vs. estimated usage • Conducting energy audits on facilities • Installing rapid roll-up doors at depots as part of campaign to reduce HVAC usage and expense • Piloting innovative technologies such as Regenerative Braking
