### [p001_c0001] LaGuardia Airport Access
### [p001_c0002] Overview
[p001_c0003] LaGuardia Airport (LGA) is the only New York area airport not served by rapid transit. Local buses serving the airport are slowed by long passenger boarding times and by operating in mixed traffic on local streets. In May 2011 the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis began with the purpose of developing faster and more reliable transit service to LGA. This study has been a partnership among New York City Department of Transportation, MTA New York City Transit, MTA Bus Company and Port Authority of New York and New Jersey.
[p001_c0004] Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) was identified as the transit mode best suited to improving service to LGA. Under the Select Bus Service (SBS) brand name, BRT has been implemented successfully on Fordham Road in the Bronx, 34th Street and First and Second Avenues in Manhattan and Hylan Boulevard on Staten Island.
### [p001_c0005] Study Goals
- [p001_c0006] ▪ Improve transit access to LGA ▪ Improve transit service in neighborhoods near LGA ▪ Provide transit improvements at a low cost and in a short timeframe
### [p001_c0007] Proposed New Routes
> [p001_c0008] Manhattan/Bronx
### [p001_c0009] Current Service Issues
[p001_c0010] Long dwell times at bus stops Long signal delays Traffic congestion on 125th Street
### [p001_c0011] Benefits
[p001_c0012] M60 SBS route: 20% faster to LGA
[p001_c0013] Proposed Bronx SBS route: 40% faster
[p001_c0014] Improved reliability
### [p001_c0015] Improvement Features
[p001_c0016] Limited stops Dedicated bus lanes Transit signal priority Off-board fare payment Luggage racks
> [p001_c0017] Queens
### [p001_c0018] Current Service Issues
[p001_c0019] Long dwell times at bus stops Long signal delays Slow travel speeds on residential streets
### [p001_c0020] Benefits
[p001_c0021] Over 40% faster than current local service
[p001_c0022] Improved regional connectivity via LIRR and subways
### [p001_c0023] Improvement Features
[p001_c0024] Limited stops Highway operation Transit signal priority
[p001_c0025] Improved local route reliability

### [p002_c0001] LaGuardia Airport Access
### [p002_c0002] Market Research
### [p002_c0003] Air Passengers
> [p002_c0004] Source: 2005 FAA Survey
[p002_c0005] Most air passengers originate in the Manhattan core and currently travel by taxi or hired vehicle.
[p002_c0006] Leisure travel is a growing market at LGA, and leisure travelers are more likely to ride transit.
### [p002_c0007] Non-Airport Bus Riders
[p002_c0008] Bus riders on LGA-bound routes not traveling to the airport are also a key market because their regular ridership helps to bolster the frequency of routes serving LGA. For example, nearly 10,000 daily riders of the M60 route are traveling entirely on 125th Street. Bus improvements would benefit more than 32,000 daily riders of all routes on this corridor through reduced delays and improved reliability.
### [p002_c0009] Airport Employees
> [p002_c0010] Source: 2011 Bus Rider Survey
[p002_c0011] Most airport employees who commute by transit live in Queens, Upper Manhattan, or the Bronx.
[p002_c0012] Many employees ride transit, primarily the M60 and Q33. Shift times impact the ability to use transit
