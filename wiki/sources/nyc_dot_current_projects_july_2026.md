### [p001_b0032] Bailey and Sedgwick Avenues, West 225th Street and Hall of Fame Terrace
[p001_b0033] NYC DOT is proposing corridor safety improvements to Bailey and Sedgwick Avenues between West 225 Street and Hall of Fame Terrace. The proposed improvements include a vehicle lane reduction with a new turn bay and turn signal, substantial pedestrian safety improvements like curb extensions and pedestrian islands, 2.2 miles of protected bike lanes, and bus service and accessibility improvements for the Bx12 and BxM3 buses. The project aims to calm traffic and promote safer vehicle behavior, improve pedestrian safety and accessibility, provide safe connections for cyclists in Kingsbridge Heights and University Heights, and reduce conflicts between all road users along this corridor. This project adds 1.1 miles to the Harlem River Greenway, an NYC DOT initiative that will install over 7 miles of safe cycling connections in the Bronx between Van Cortlandt Park to Randall’s Island.

### [p001_b0036] Baychester Avenue, Aldrich Street to Rombouts Avenue
[p001_b0037] NYC DOT is proposing safety improvements on Baychester Avenue between Aldrich Street and Rombouts Avenue and Donizetti Place east of Baychester Avenue in Co-Op City, the Bronx. This School Safety project intends to serve local residents and 5,800 + K through 12 students who attend 6 local schools, all a part of one of the largest educational campuses in NYC. On Baychester Avenue, the proposed design would reconfigure the moving lanes to calm traffic, reduce speeding, improve and expand existing school loading zones and add community parking. Pedestrian accessibility and safety would be further enhanced by the addition of painted and concrete curb extensions and redesigned median tip extensions. The proposed continuous concrete median on Donizetti Place will organize traffic, improve predictability and safety and significantly shorten pedestrian crossings.

### [p001_b0039] Boston Road, Third Avenue to East 169th Street
[p001_b0040] This school safety project aims to improve safety for students on Boston Road, East 166th Street, Home Street, and East 168th Street in the vicinity of several schools. NYC DOT is proposing to install standard bike lanes along Boston Road from Third Avenue to East 169th Street, on Home Street from Prospect Avenue to Boston Road, and on East 168th Street from Boston Road to Prospect Avenue; to install shared lane markings along East 168th Street from Franklin Avenue to Boston Road; to close two slip lanes at Boston Road and East 169th Street; to add two new crosswalks at Boston Road and East 166th Street; to convert one block of East 166th Street between Franklin Avenue and Boston Road to one-way westbound; and to install two painted curb extensions at Franklin Avenue and East 168th Street. The project will visually narrow the roadway to discourage speeding and reckless driving in front of schools, simplify traffic patterns to calm vehicular turns, and provide shorter crossing distances and additional pedestrian space. Although designed primarily to improve safety for students, this project will improve safety for New Yorkers of all ages.

### [p001_b0068] Fordham Road, Major Deegan Expressway to Boston Road
[p001_b0069] NYC DOT is investigating transit improvements along Fordham Road between the Major Deegan Expressway and Boston Road. Fordham Road is a vital crosstown Bronx corridor. Nine bus routes (Bx9, Bx12 SBS/Local, Bx17, Bx22, Bx34, Bee-Line 60-62 buses) carry 93,700 daily riders, with connections to the A, B/D, 1, 2/5, 4, and 6 subways, and Metro North Harlem, Hudson, & New Haven Lines. In 2008, NYC DOT and MTA launched the first Select Bus Service (SBS) route on the Bx12, which included curbside bus lanes and off board fare payment. Between 2008 and 2014, Bx12 bus speeds and ridership increased. In recent years, bus speeds have declined, which has led to long, unreliable commutes and declining ridership. NYC DOT will evaluate a range of potential design improvements to improve bus speeds and reliability, while also improving curb management and traffic safety.

### [p001_b0087] Harlem River Greenway - Bronx Implementation Plan
[p001_b0088] NYC DOT initiated a planning process to develop a 7-mile Harlem River Greenway in The Bronx, creating a continuous walking and cycling connection between Van Cortland Park and Randall’s Island. The Harlem River Greenway Implementation Plan aims to better connect Bronxites to their waterfront and provide a critical north-south bike commuting corridor. Bronx residents helped shape the plan through a community-based process, beginning with public workshops in April 2023. Several segments of the Harlem River Greenway have already been implemented, including along Bailey Avenue, Depot Place, and Bruckner Boulevard, and more segments are currently in planning and design.

### [p001_b0142] Adams Street & Boerum Place
[p001_b0143] The Adams Street and Boerum Place street improvement projects seeks to improve access, circulation and safety for cyclists to and from the Brooklyn Bridge bike path. The Brooklyn Bridge is a major connection for cyclists between Brooklyn and Manhattan and has seen cycling volumes increase over the years. Within the past 10 years to improve the connections for cyclists and pedestrians to and from the Brooklyn Bridge a shared cycling and walking path was constructed between Johnson Street and Tillary Street. This project seeks to continue a two-way bike lane between Johnson Street and Bergen Street. For the majority of the two-way bike lane, the alignment will be center running along the east side of the landscape median between Johnson Street and Schermerhorn Street. Due to connection challenges at Atlantic Avenue, the alignment of two-way bike lane will move to the west side of the street between Schermerhorn Street and Bergen Street. Installation of the two-way bike lane will follow the repaving of the street in the fall 2026; however, due to construction activity south of Atlantic Avenue, the repaving for this section will be held until a future year.

### [p001_b0152] Atlantic Avenue Great Streets Phase II
[p001_b0153] NYC DOT is planning a capital project on Atlantic Avenue between Logan Street and Rockaway Boulevard as part of Mayor de Blasio’s Vision Zero Great Streets Initiative. ### [p001_b0666] Jamaica Bay Greenway
[p001_b0667] The Jamaica Bay Greenway is a planned 28-mile network of pedestrian and bicycle paths connecting communities along the Jamaica Bay waterfront to more than 10,000 acres of city, state and federal parkland, including Rockaway Beach, Marine Park, Canarsie Pier, Floyd Bennett Field and the Jamaica Bay Wildlife Refuge. More than 10 miles of the Greenway already exist, serving roughly 100,000 local and regional visitors annually. When completed, multi-use paths and on-street bicycle markings will create a loop route around Jamaica Bay, allowing cyclists and walkers to commute, exercise and explore the Bay’s natural beauty from Sheepshead Bay and Spring Creek in Brooklyn to Howard Beach and the Rockaway Peninsula in Queens. Recently, more than two miles of protected path have been added to connect the Canarsie neighborhood in Brooklyn to the Greenway, and other segments are now in design.
