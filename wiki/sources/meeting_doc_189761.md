### [p003_c0011] President Sheridan's Remarks
[p003_c0012] President Sheridan began by spotlighting TBTA's 100th joint-agency enforcement operation, which was conducted on August 19th at the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge, where Governor Hochul had launched TBTA's inaugural operation in March 2024. President Sheridan was pleased to have stood along with TBTA's regional law enforcement partners to highlight this milestone dedicated to removing "ghost cars" from New York roadways. Such vehicles are virtually untraceable by traffic cameras and toll readers because of their fake, obstructed, and altered license plates. To date, targeted ghost plate operations have yielded more than 1,300 arrests, 60,000 summonses issued, and 5,400 vehicles towed, and have identified those responsible for over $60 million dollars owed collectively to all task force member agencies.
[p003_c0013] President Sheridan reiterated TBTA's renewed focus on tinted window enforcement in support of public safety on its crossings. Illegally tinted windows reduce driver visibility, pose risks to law enforcement during traffic stops, and allow the improper concealment of vehicle occupants and contents. TBTA's officers have been equipped with new tint meters that measure compliance with the Vehicle and Traffic Law. To date, 84% of TBTA's law enforcement personnel have been trained in the use of these new meters, and in 2025 TBTA is on pace to achieve a 21% increase in the number of summonses issued compared to 2023.
[p003_c0014] Turning to additional important safety initiatives, President Sheridan updated TBTA's announcement earlier this month of two legislatively authorized safety programs: Weigh-in-Motion and Work Zone Speed Enforcement:
- [p003_c0015] • Weigh-in-Motion ("WIM") uses roadway sensors and cameras to identify overweight trucks and issue violation notices to the vehicle owners. The fines are meant to deter operators who exceed established weight limits from crossing TBTA's bridges. These

[p004_c0001] overweight trucks cause millions in damage to TBTA structures each year. TBTA was authorized to implement WIM as a pilot program on the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge.
- [p004_c0002] • The Work Zone Speed Enforcement program, signed into law by Governor Hochul in 2021, was expanded in 2025 to include TBTA facilities. The program is designed to improve safety in roadway work zones through speed camera enforcement. Motorists who speed in work zones will be subject to fines. The cameras used are only operational when crews are actively working.
[p004_c0003] President Sheridan added that the public hearings on these programs will be held on October 6th and 7th, and that these programs are then subject to Board approval prior to implementation.
[p004_c0004] Finally, President Sheridan commended all those involved in the Stephen Siller Tunnel-to-Towers event that took place at the Hugh L. Carey Tunnel.
