### [p011_c0001] Proposal: Offset Bus Lane
- [p011_c0002] • Shift bus lane from west curb to the “offset” position, 96 th St to 60 th St • In effect at all times, improves service throughout the day • Enables commercial metered parking during morning peak
> [p011_c0003] The diagram illustrates the shift from an existing bus lane to a proposed offset bus lane. Both configurations are shown between a West Sidewalk and an East Sidewalk, with a total width of 51'. Existing Configuration: West Sidewalk 12' AM Peak Bus Lane / Parking Lane 10' Travel Lane 10' Travel Lane 10' Travel Lane 9' Parking Lane East Sidewalk 51' Proposed Configuration: West Sidewalk 9' Parking Lane / Right-Turn Bay 12' BUS 10' Travel Lane 10' Travel Lane 10' Parking Lane / Left-Turn Bay East Sidewalk 51' In the proposed configuration, the bus lane is shifted to the right, creating a 12' bus lane between the 9' parking bay and the 10' travel lane. The total width remains 51'.

### [p014_c0001] Proposal: Curb Regulation Updates
- [p014_c0002] • Existing regulations are generally metered parking 10am-7pm on west curb and 7am-7pm on east curb • Add commercial meters to improve curb access and reduce double parking
[p014_c0003]

```text
Land Use Proposed Weekday Regulation Purpose Generic commercial 7am-10am 3-hr commercial meters 10am-7pm 2-hr passenger meters • Accommodates morning loading • Serves business patrons throughout the day • Allows overnight parking Small stores / Restaurants 7am-4pm 2-hr commercial meters 4pm-7pm/10pm 2-hr passenger meters • Facilitates turnover in locations with high number of deliveries • Accommodates restaurant patrons in the evening • Allows overnight parking Large retail chain 7am-7pm 3-hr commercial meters • Facilitates large deliveries for businesses throughout the day • Allows overnight parking
```
