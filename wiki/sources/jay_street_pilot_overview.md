### [p001_c0002] Jay Street Busway Pilot Smith St./Livingston St. to Jay St./Tillary St.
### [p001_c0003] The project will:
- [p001_c0004] • Increase bus speeds and bus reliability along the corridor • Serve seven routes that carry 46,000 passengers per weekday • Help improve travel times for vulnerable communities and essential workers • Create a complete street that improves bicycle and pedestrian safety • Ease congestion on designated truck route • Reduce the impact of illegal parking

### [p001_c0007] How it works:
- [p001_c0008] • Buses, trucks, and bicycles only permitted to enter Jay Street from north and south • Local access from east and west • Johnson Street converted to one-way eastbound to provide local access • Smith Street/Jay Street protected bicycle lane maintained and enhanced • Increase parking enforcement and update curb regulations
[p001_c0009] The Jay Street Busway Pilot is part of Better Buses Restart . NYC is speeding up implementation on bus projects citywide to provide faster and more reliable bus service for essential workers and communities impacted by COVID-19.

### [p002_c0001] Jay Street Busway Pilot Smith St./Livingston St. to Jay St./Tillary St.
### [p002_c0002] FAQs
### [p002_c0003] Q. Why is the City installing a busway on Jay Street?
[p002_c0004] A. Jay Street between Livingston Street and Tillary Street hosts seven bus routes carrying 46,000 passengers. Jay Street is often congested and creates a bottleneck for buses. Midday speeds averaged 3.4 MPH. in Fall 2019 Limiting traffic will improve bus travel times, making for faster and more reliable travel for bus passengers.
### [p002_c0005] Q. Will this cause congestion on other streets?
[p002_c0006] A. The majority of diverting cars will use major parallel north-south corridors of Boerum Place/Adams Street and Flatbush Avenue, which can handle much more traffic than Jay Street. We will be monitoring traffic changes to nearby streets after implementation to identify potential issues.
### [p002_c0007] Q. How will illegal placard parking be addressed?
[p002_c0008] A. This pilot provides an opportunity for a coordinated effort to enforce illegal placard parking, which takes up valuable curb space and results in double parking. In addition, DOT will evaluate curb regulations and adjust where appropriate.
### [p002_c0009] Q. Will Willoughby Street and Lawrence Street Shared Streets/Open Streets be impacted by the busway project.
[p002_c0010] A. Portions of Lawrence and Willoughby Streets will serve as an access route to Jay Street for a small number of vehicles. This is compatible with the Willoughby Shared Street. We will be evaluating whether it is feasible to maintain those street segments as Open Streets.
### [p002_c0011] Q. Will there be restrictions on how local traffic can leave Jay Street?
[p002_c0012] A. No. Cars can exit the corridor in the most direct way possible, which may include to the south via Smith Street/Livingston Street, or to the north via Jay Street/Livingston Street.
### [p002_c0013] Q. What if I want to catch a ride with a for hire vehicle on Jay Street?
[p002_c0014] A. Taxis, Ubers, etc, will be subject to the same regulations as other automobiles and will need to approach from the east or west to pick up passengers.
### [p002_c0015] Community Outreach
- [p002_c0016] • DOT is committed to public engagement throughout this one year pilot • A Community Advisory Board will guide the pilot during and after implementation
[p002_c0017] After Implementation: DOT will monitor traffic and make adjustments to the Busway as needed
