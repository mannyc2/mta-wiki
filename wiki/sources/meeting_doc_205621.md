### [p002_c0002] Year-to-Date Budget Performance Summary
- [p002_c0003] • Total revenue of $213.1 million was $29.4 million lower than the Budget. This unfavorable variance was due to lower capital reimbursements partially offset by higher other operating revenues. • Through March 2026 ridership was 16.3 million, 2.2% above 2025, 19.2% below 2019 pre-COVID levels and 0.2% below the Budget. Commutation ridership of 7.4 million was 12.0% above 2025 and 11.9% above the Budget. Non-commutation ridership of 8.9 million was 4.7% below 2025 and 5.0% below the Budget. Farebox revenue of $151.8 million was essentially flat to Budget. • Total expenses before non-cash liability adjustments of $487.1 million were $20.3 million or 4.0% favorable to the Budget. The favorable variance is primarily driven by lower maintenance and other operating contracts as well as lower overall labor costs. • At the end of March, total headcount was 6,721, which was 225 lower than the Budget of 6,946. Non-reimbursable positions were 27 lower than the Budget and reimbursable positions were 198 lower than the Budget. • March YTD non-reimbursable operating results were $10.4 million or 3.0% unfavorable to the Budget. Non-reimbursable revenues through March were $1.6 million favorable to the Budget primarily due higher advertising, interest and station revenues partially offset by lower net Grand Central Terminal (GCT) retail revenues. Total non-reimbursable expenses were $10.6 million unfavorable primarily due to higher total labor costs partially offset by lower maintenance and other operating contracts.

### [p003_c0001] Revenues
- [p003_c0002] • Farebox Revenues were essentially flat to the Budget due to higher commutation ridership across all lines and higher average yield per passenger on Harlem and New Haven lines partially offset by lower non-commutation ridership across all lines and lower average yield per passenger across Hudson and New Haven lines. Total ridership through March was 16.3 million. This was 2.2% above 2025 and 0.2% lower than the Budget. • Other Operating Revenues were $1.6 million favorable to the Budget reflecting higher advertising, interest and station revenues partially offset by lower net GCT retail revenues.
### [p003_c0003] Expenses
[p003_c0004] Labor Expenses: $17.7 million favorable to the Budget.
- [p003_c0005] • Payroll was $0.2 million unfavorable to the Budget primarily driven by reduced capital project activity and timing of retiree payouts partially offset by the net impact of hiring and attrition as well as lower retro-active wage accruals. • Overtime was $1.1 million unfavorable to the Budget primarily driven by higher weather emergencies partially offset by lower programmatic maintenance, as planned work could not be completed during weather preparation and response efforts along with lower vacancy coverage. • Health & Welfare was $1.3 million unfavorable to the Budget due to higher rates and labor costs. • OPEB Current Payment was $0.7 million unfavorable to the Budget reflecting a higher number of retirees receiving healthcare premiums. • Pensions were essentially flat to Budget. • Other Fringe Benefits were $2.5 million unfavorable to the Budget reflecting higher employee claim provisions, rates and labor costs partially offset by lower employee reimbursements. • Reimbursable Overhead was $12.0 million unfavorable to the Budget primarily due to scheduling and timing changes in capital project expenditures.
[p003_c0006] Non-Labor Expenses: $7.1 million favorable to the Budget.
- [p003_c0007] • Electric Power was $3.2 million unfavorable to the Budget due to higher usage partially offset by lower rates. • Fuel was $1.2 million unfavorable to Budget due to higher rates partially offset by lower usage. • Insurance was $0.3 million favorable to Budget, reflecting lower insurance premiums. • Claims were $0.3 million favorable to the Budget, reflecting a lower passenger claim provision. • Maintenance and Other Operating Contracts were $6.7 million favorable to the Budget due to timing of invoice payments for miscellaneous maintenance and other operating contracts as well as lower real estate chargeback actuals now reflected in professional services partially offset by higher utilities.
