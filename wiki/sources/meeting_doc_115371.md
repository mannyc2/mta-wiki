### [p005_c0001] Harness technology and data to improve productivity
### [p005_c0002] Pursuing predictive maintenance to improve reliability while lowering costs
### [p005_c0003] Subways
[p005_c0004] Piloting new maintenance intervals for Car Equipment and Maintenance of Way
- [p005_c0005] • Major Car overhauls every ~6.5 years (recent intervals have been either 6 years or 7 years) • Routine Car inspections every 75-85 days or 11K-13K miles, up from 68-78 days or 10-12K miles • Switch maintenance at non-critical locations every 60 days, up from every 30 1
[p005_c0006] Results will be monitored to continue to optimize costs and reliability
### [p005_c0007] Buses
[p005_c0008] Expanding from the initial pilot of engine after-treatment monitoring on 326 buses up to 1,528 in 2023 and up to 5,000 buses in 2024
[p005_c0009] Since December 2022, 552 predictive maintenance repair plans have been completed, yielding an estimated cost avoidance of $1.2M
[p005_c0010] In addition, Bus is partnering with Procurement on a RFP to expand predictive maintenance to include other bus sub-systems beyond after-treatment
[p005_c0011] _1. To be reflected in November financial plan_

### [p009_c0001] Develop common standards and best practices
### [p009_c0002] Scale LEDs across the Subway system
[p009_c0003] LEDs provide multiple benefits compared to traditional fluorescent bulbs:
- [p009_c0004] • Lower energy consumption • Longer-lasting (resulting in lower material and labor costs for replacements) • Brighter light and greater feeling of safety
[p009_c0005] Subways has successfully piloted LEDs on cars, in stations, and in tunnels
[p009_c0006] As part of the Faster, Cleaner, Safer plan, Subways will be rolling out LEDs at scale over the coming years, driving higher customer satisfaction at lower cost
[p009_c0007] On average, LEDs last over 3x as long and consume ~50% less energy vs. fluorescents

### [p017_c0001] Develop common standards and best practices
[p017_c0002] M7 Standard Work optimization is a key productivity enabler that can create value from job time reduction and increased predictability
- [p017_c0003] • M7s are 30% of Metro-North's fleet & 65% of LIRR's • Utilizing Lean Six-Sigma principles to determine optimal sequencing and timing of the work to reduce overall time cars spend in the shop • Initial task-by-task review of FRA required 92-day Periodic Inspection will be performed in Croton-Harmon • Implementation teams will build skills and methodology for scaling this approach to other types of work across both railroads
