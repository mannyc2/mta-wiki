### [p002_c0001] Fast Facts

## Page 11

### [p011_c0001] How SCOUT Works: Key Elements
### [p011_c0002] Clinician-led
[p011_c0003] Engage
[p011_c0004] De-escalate
[p011_c0005] Encourage voluntary acceptance of services
### [p011_c0006] Police for Safety & Support
[p011_c0007] All officers receive NYS OMH crisis intervention training
[p011_c0008] Outreach workers report feeling safer, able to go deeper with challenging clients
### [p011_c0009] True Teams
[p011_c0010] Same officers & same clinicians, day in & day out
[p011_c0011] Builds trust & rapport
### [p011_c0012] “Help Right Now” Mindset
[p011_c0013] Focus on the hardest cases - clients who appear acutely psychotic and/or deeply entrenched
[p011_c0014] Goal: connect them to services on the spot
### [p011_c0015] Use All Available Powers
[p011_c0016] Power of persuasion: voluntary outcomes preferred
[p011_c0017] Involuntary transport power: where appropriate, used without hesitation and effectively
### [p011_c0018] Continuing Care
[p011_c0019] Work with hospital & shelter partners
[p011_c0020] Advocate for hospital admission or “safe haven” placement
[p011_c0021] Track for success
[p011_c0022] _11_
