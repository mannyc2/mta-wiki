## Page 2

### [p002_c0001] About the Pilot
[p002_c0002] The 2023 New York State budget mandated the MTA select and operate one bus route in each borough to be fare free for 6-12 months.
### [p002_c0003] Our route selections considered:
- [p002_c0004] • Equity for low income and disadvantaged communities, access to employment and commercial area(s) • Existing route capacity, limited crowding conditions, service adequacy • Minimal overlap with other MTA bus services • Fare evasion levels
[p002_c0005] Pilot began September 24, 2023. Fare collection resumes on September 1, 2024.
> [p002_c0006] The map displays the five fare-free bus routes selected for the pilot program, one in each borough: Manhattan: B60 route, connecting Williamsburg to Canarsie. Brooklyn: Q4 route, connecting Jamaica to Cambria Heights. Queens: S46/96 route, connecting St. George to Jamaica. The Bronx: M116 route, connecting Harlem to Mt. Eden. ## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] Evaluation Summary
### [p003_c0002] Main Pilot findings:
- [p003_c0003] 1. Ridership increased on all routes 2. Limited new ridership: existing riders used the fare-free routes more than new route riders 3. Service performance had mixed results 4. Fare evasion on nearby routes was consistent with systemwide rates 5. Foregone revenue and related costs amounted to more than $12M over nine months of the Pilot
