## Page 5

### [p005_c0001] MTA provides sustainable mobility to millions of New Yorkers
[p005_c0002] Transit reduces transportation-related carbon emissions in four ways:
> [p005_c0003] A blue silhouette of a car on the left, with a right-pointing arrow, and a blue silhouette of a train on the right.
### [p005_c0004] Mode shift effect
[p005_c0005] Shifting from cars to sustainable transportation modes
> [p005_c0006] A blue silhouette of an oil pumpjack.
### [p005_c0007] Upstream effect
[p005_c0008] Eliminating emissions from gasoline production and transportation
> [p005_c0009] A blue silhouette of two cars, one slightly behind the other.
### [p005_c0010] Congestion effect
[p005_c0011] Improving the operation of the roadway network by reducing congestion
> [p005_c0012] A blue silhouette of a bicycle.
### [p005_c0013] Land use effect
[p005_c0014] Enabling denser land uses that promote shorter trips, walking and cycling, and reduced car ownership
[p005_c0015] _Source: APTA 2018 Quantifying Greenhouse Gas Emissions from Transit https://www.apta.com/wp-content/uploads/Standards_Documents/APTA-SUDS-CC-RP-001-09_Rev-1.pdf_
[p005_c0016] _The MTA logo, consisting of the letters 'MTA' inside a circle._
