## Page 4

### [p004_c0001] Why Fordham Road?
- [p004_c0002] • In 2008, MTA and DOT launched the first Select Bus Service (SBS) route on the Bx12 • Bx12 is the busiest bus route in the Bronx, and second busiest in NYC after the M15 • Critical crosstown transportation corridor and serves as a major Bronx-Manhattan connection • 85,000 average daily bus riders on 5 routes (Bx12-SBS + Local, Bx9, Bx17, Bx22) • This area of the city is located within a Tier 1 Priority Investment Area in the NYC Streets Plan • Bus riders and pedestrians form the majority of roadway users on Fordham Road • 62% of households on Bx12 corridor have no access to a private vehicle. 71% commute to work via public transit, walking, or biking
> [p004_c0003] A photograph of a yellow and blue MTA bus stopped at a bus stop. Several people are waiting to board the bus. The bus has a 'CAUTION WIDE ROADWAY TRUCKS' sign and a 'This Bus Uses Clean-Air Technology' sign. The bus is at Fordham Rd at University Ave.
[p004_c0004] _Bx12 SBS, Fordham Rd at University Ave_
- [p004_c0005] • Fordham Rd is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with 4 VZ priority intersections at Sedgwick, University, Grand and Jerome Aves • 83 persons killed or significantly injured between 2014-2018 between Deegan and Boston Road
[p004_c0006] _4_

### [p005_c0001] Existing Bus Priority
- [p005_c0002] • Fordham Road currently has only curbside bus lanes • Along most of the Fordham Road, bus lanes are only in effect in peak periods, Monday-Friday, 7AM to 7 PM, with a two-hour midday window for deliveries • Westbound 7 AM to Noon, and 2 PM to 7 PM • Eastbound 7-10 AM, and Noon to 7 PM • Bus lanes on part of Fordham Road, from Sedgwick Ave to University Ave, are in effect 7am-7pm Monday-Friday, without the two-hour midday break • Pelham Parkway bus lanes are in effect 7AM-7PM, Monday through Friday

### [p006_c0001] Curb Demand Challenges on Fordham Road
- [p006_c0002] • Curbside bus lanes are a less efficient means of balancing bus priority and curb access • Deliveries are only permitted during a 2-hour window in the midday when the bus lanes are not in effect • Expeditious pick-ups and drop-offs by passenger cars are allowed at any time, regardless of bus lane hours, which causes further delays for buses needing to weave in and out of traffic • Observations and data show that existing curbside bus lanes are frequently blocked throughout the day

### [p010_c0001] Proposed Alternatives Summary
[p010_c0002] Three alternatives, initially proposed at CAB #3 in March 2022 have been under discussion/study:
[p010_c0003] Will continue to study
### [p010_c0004] Alternative A: Offset Bus Lanes in Both Directions:
[p010_c0005] Convert existing curbside bus lanes to offset lanes and extend offset lanes. In effect at all times (24/7).
### [p010_c0006] Alternative B: Eastbound Busway from Morris Av to Webster Av:
[p010_c0007] Eastbound through traffic restrictions on Fordham Rd, except for buses and trucks, with local access on all blocks. Offset lane in westbound direction and offset bus lanes as proposed in Alternative A outside the Busway section. In effect at all times (24/7).
### [p010_c0008] Alternative C: Two-Way Busway between Jerome & Webster Aves:
[p010_c0009] Both eastbound and westbound through traffic restrictions on Fordham Rd, except for buses and trucks, with local access on all blocks; also includes offset bus lanes proposed in Alternative A outside the Busway section. In effect at all times (24/7).

### [p019_c0001] Traffic Analysis Methodology
- [p019_c0002] 1. Count traffic & pedestrians at over 60 intersections from Pelham Parkway to University Heights Bridge, and on 207th Street, Kingsbridge Road and 188th St. 2. Analyze origin-destination with anonymized GPS data from drivers on Fordham Rd used to understand diversion routes likely under a busway alternatives 3. Model each intersection and analyze: • Signal timing • Number of lanes and turning movements (left, straight, right) • Time and number of pedestrians crossing streets • For multiple alternatives, scenarios & improvements 4. Validate model with observations in-person of traffic flow, parking movements, loading, etc. 5. Worst case scenario is analyzed: • Traffic analysis software analyzes worst hours of day, longest queues, diversion patterns. • Realistically, some drivers divert to other routes or choose to travel at different times of day • Traffic analyses were prepared for a range of scenarios from no volume reduction up to a 40% reduction.

### [p021_c0001] Alternative A: Offset Bus Lanes in Both Directions
### [p021_c0002] Traffic Regulations:
- [p021_c0003] • Bus-only lane located one-lane away from the curb in effect at all times, 24/7 • Single travel lane in both directions • Vehicles allowed to use offset bus lanes for right turns • Buses still pull up to the curb to pick up passengers before continuing into offset lane
### [p021_c0004] Offset Bus Lane Benefits:
- [p021_c0005] • Curbside lane available for pick-up/drop-off & deliveries according to posted regulations – deliveries in bus lane are prohibited • Buses have priority with fewer conflicts than curbside lanes • Automated enforcement for double parking • Reduced weaving by vehicles in single travel lane

- [p025_c0002] • Based on factors identified in the traffic analyses and listening to the community concerns, Alternative A – offset bus lanes along Fordham Rd – is the best treatment to improve overall bus speeds, reliability, and curb demand while minimizing the effects on buses on north/south corridors • Bus lanes are less likely to be blocked from illegal parking • New design addresses intense curb demand: approximately combined 150+ parking/loading spaces added to corridor instead of confined to two-hour window • Offset bus lanes are not foolproof – double parking can still block the bus lane, but they are an upgrade from curbside bus lanes • DOT and MTA will continue to monitor bus speeds, reliability, and curb demand and engage with all members of the Fordham community to ensure that the roadway works best for all users.

### [p029_c0001] How Does ABLE Work?
- [p029_c0002] • ABLE was implemented on all Bx12-SBS buses on November 18, 2022 with a 60 day warning period, with summonses issued starting January 17, 2023 • Enforces against vehicles parking or standing in a bus lane during the bus lane hours • Two buses must capture the same vehicle 5 minutes apart to issue a violation • ABLE cameras capture license plate information photos and videos, with location information • Human review by DOT process ensures violations are captured and issued according to program rules • When ABLE cameras are installed on new routes, DOT will issue warnings to motorists for the first 60 days • ABLE can enforce anywhere along bus lane, not just in fixed locations

### [p038_c0001] Alternatives B and C: Busway (Transit & Truck Priority) Overview
### [p038_c0002] Traffic Regulations within Busway:
- [p038_c0003] • All vehicles can access every block; in effect all times • Only buses, trucks, and emergency vehicles can drive through along the entire corridor
[p038_c0004] Trucks defined as any vehicle that has more than two axles OR six or more wheels
- [p038_c0005] • General vehicles allowed Local Access Only
[p038_c0006] Local Access: vehicles are allowed to drive on busway for local trips, pick-up/drop-off, and garage access but must make the next available right turn off Busway
### [p038_c0007] Busway Benefits:
- [p038_c0008] • Busway regulations reduce the level of congestion along the Busway corridor • All drivers who need to be on Fordham Road can get to the corridor; drivers who do not can take other paths • Reduced congestion helps improve bus speeds and reliability • Trucks do not divert through neighborhoods and deliveries are easier
