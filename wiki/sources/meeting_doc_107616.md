> [p001_c0002] The logo for the Metropolitan Transportation Authority (MTA) is a blue circle with the letters "MTA" in white.
### [p001_c0003] Metro-North Railroad
[p001_c0004] Date: March 15, 2023
[p001_c0005] To: Metro-North and Long Island Railroad Committee
[p001_c0006] From: Catherine A. Rinaldi CAR
[p001_c0007] Re: March 2023 Schedule Changes
[p001_c0008] Schedules will change on the Hudson, Harlem and New Haven lines effective Sunday, March 26, 2023, and will remain in effect through Saturday, June 10, 2023.
[p001_c0009] The March 26 schedule reflects hourly weekend service on the Hudson Line to support several significant State of Good Repair infrastructure improvements, some minor PM Peak weekday adjustments on the Harlem Line to alleviate station congestion at Southeast, and the continuation of the amended New Haven Line weekday schedule as a result of the elevator/ escalator- replacement project at Stamford, the busiest Metro-North station outside of Grand Central Terminal. There are no changes to weekday train frequencies. The schedules continue to support major MTA C&D and MNR Maintenance of Way projects that require extensive track outages. The significant Hudson Line work described below is expected to be completed by Saturday, June 10, 2023, and we will resume normal weekend service on Sunday, June 11, 2023.
[p001_c0010] The new schedule also heralds the arrival of Spring and, with it, the return of Trains to the Game for New York Yankees baseball at Yankee Stadium. Opening Day is Thursday, March 30, and Metro-North is ready with its popular Trains to the Game, extra stops on Hudson Line trains and shuttle trains from Grand Central Terminal. New York Football Club has already opened its season, which began March 11, and we'll operate shuttle trains from Grand Central Terminal and make extra stops on Hudson Line trains for all 34 of the team's home games.
### [p001_c0011] Hudson Line:
- [p001_c0012] • Weekday schedules have only minor changes at en-route stations. Customers should check the TrainTime app or website schedules for adjustments to their specific trains and stations. • Weekend service is reduced to one train per hour in each zone in each direction because of significant infrastructure-improvement projects in capacity-constrained areas, including the replacement of track switches at CP 8 near Morris Heights and at CP 10 near Marble Hill.
