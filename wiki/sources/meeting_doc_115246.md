### [p003_c0002] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0003] President Davey delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0004] President Davey spoke about the beginning of summer and the celebration of freedoms such as LGBTQIA+ Pride and Juneteenth. He said that as the co-Executive Sponsor of the Pride Express Employee Resource Group, he was proud to be part of an organization that celebrates LGBTQIA+ customers and employees not just during pride month, but every day of the year. He noted the many efforts undertaken by the MTA to support Pride month such as Pride posters and digital displays and thanked the TWU Local 100 for their support.
[p003_c0005] President Davey spoke about 67 transit employees who were recognized for their achievements in areas such as safety, leadership and diversity at the NYCT President’s Biannual Awards Ceremony. He also acknowledged 10 NYCT Bus Operators highlighted on the TODAY show who joined Al Roker for a ride on one of the newest electric buses, including 42-year veteran Bus Operator Tony DeLucca.
[p003_c0006] President Davey noted a slight dip in overall subway customer satisfaction, but reported on improvements in customer journey time performance, and an improvement in satisfaction with on-board cleanliness and staff helpfulness. He also spoke about the Station Re-NEW-vation Program and the goal to integrate renovations in a total of 50 stations by the end of 2023. He noted the addition of 36 bathrooms at 18 additional subway stations that will be reopening on a rolling basis beginning in July.
[p003_c0007] President Davey reported a dip in overall satisfaction with safety and security on trains and stations due to several incidents, but noted an improvement in overall subway safety satisfaction as a result of initiatives such as Station ReNew-vations and partnerships with “Cops, Cameras, Care”.
[p003_c0008] President Davey reported that crime in the transit system is on the decline and acknowledged MTA’s partnership with Chief Kemper and the addition of 1,000 uniform officers in the system.
[p003_c0009] President Davey announced that June 15th marked 100 years since the founding of the Brooklyn-Manhattan Transit Corporation (BMT) and he thanked those who made the celebration of that anniversary possible, which involved rolling out vintage BMT Standards on the F line.
[p003_c0010] President Davey reported on bus customer satisfaction, which he said has remained stable, and noted the first anniversary of the Bronx Local Bus Network Redesign - a key component of the “Faster, Cleaner, Safer” Plan to improve service delivery and increase customer satisfaction. He stated that the Department of Buses will continue to work closely with all stakeholders, including agencies like the New York City DOT and NYPD, to make service better.
[p003_c0011] President Davey spoke about overall customer satisfaction in Paratransit (AAR) and the continually increasing demand for AAR. He noted a 98% success rate for ride-time

### [p004_c0004] 6. ACCESSIBILITY
[p004_c0005] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p004_c0006] Arroyo stated that the Accessibility Group is partnering with the Customer Experience team to offer employee training on how to make digital content fully accessible using all the latest available tools and standards. He reported that to date, employees have been trained across three departments and he looks forward to building on their positive feedback as they reach thousands of MTA employees in the months to come.
[p004_c0007] Arroyo spoke about the continuation of retrofitting along 57 routes as part of the MTA Bus Open Stroller Pilot Program, and spoke about the success of the recent Bus Festival event where NYCT showed off the newest bus model with a stroller space. He also discussed the continued work to install Detectable Warning Surfaces (DWS) at remaining stations.
[p004_c0008] Lastly, Arroyo expressed MTA Accessibility's excitement to celebrate Disability Pride Month in July.
