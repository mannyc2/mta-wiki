### [p006_c0006] Discussion:
[p006_c0007] NYC Transit, on behalf of itself and MTA Bus Company (“MTABC”) (together, the “Agencies”) requests Board approval to award a sole-source, one-year estimated quantity contract for replacement parts to The Aftermarket Parts Company, LLC d/b/a NFI Parts (“NFI”) in the estimated value of $7,105,837 (the “Contract”). The Contract is the result of a multi-agency joint procurement and provides for the furnishing of various replacement parts that are required for the Agencies to operate and maintain their respective fleets of buses. NYC Transit currently operates buses from New Flyer, Orion Bus Industries (“Orion”) and Motor Coach Industries (“MCI”); and MTABC currently operates buses from New Flyer and MCI. The replacement parts included in this Contract are only obtainable from or through NFI because NFI is the sole distributor and reseller for New Flyer, Orion and MCI.
[p006_c0008] By utilizing a joint procurement approach for the Contract, the Agencies are able to combine their buying power to achieve the most favorable pricing from NFI. An award to NFI will also create significant efficiencies by automating the creation of purchase orders and eliminating the need for the Agencies to individually procure the replacement parts thereby reducing the frequency of the Agencies’ procurement cycles.
[p006_c0009] Under this Contract, the Agencies will continue to support their Scheduled Maintenance, Life Cycle Maintenance, and Shop Overhaul programs with the replacement parts at prescribed intervals to improve customer service by ensuring safe, reliable and available revenue service. The replacement parts will also be used for unscheduled bus maintenance and repairs.
[p006_c0010] This Contract will cover a total of 1,097 items with an estimated value of $7,105,837. The chart below summarizes the estimated amounts anticipated to be expended by each Agency over the one-year term of the contract.
[p006_c0011]

```text
NYC Transit MTABC TOTAL $5,031,291 $2,074,546 $7,105,837
```
[p006_c0012] The total dollars outlined above are based on historical data and projected/forecasted usage. New Flyer has agreed to hold firm fixed pricing for one year as there is market stability for these 1,097 items. The MTA Cost Price Analysis Unit performed a detailed analysis using comparative and historical data of previous purchases and determined the pricing to be fair and reasonable.
[p006_c0013] Any sole-source items that are not included in the 1,097 items on this new Contract will be purchased by the Agencies utilizing the Multi-Agency Informal Procurement Procedures.
[p006_c0014] NFI is the sole responsible source for the parts that will be provided under this Contract. No other vendor can access the proprietary designs, specifications, and drawings and, therefore, cannot readily provide these parts. The Controller’s Office found that there was reasonable assurance that NFI is financially qualified to perform this Contract.
[p006_c0015] NYC Transit advertised its intention to procure the replacement parts, for itself and MTABC, without competitive bidding, in accordance with the MTA All-Agency Procurement Guideline requirements. No other firms expressed interest in participating in this procurement.

### [p008_c0005] Discussion:
[p008_c0006] NYC Transit is requesting Board approval to exercise the Option of the contract with MotivePower, Inc., a Wabtec company (“MPI”) for the purchase of 45 additional diesel-battery hybrid locomotives (hereafter the “Option Locomotives”), including related non-car items such as spare parts, special tools, diagnostic test equipment, technical documentation and training for the Department of Subways at a total price of $386,179,926.
[p008_c0007] In July 2020, the Board authorized the award of Contract R34255 to MPI for the purchase of 25 diesel-battery hybrid locomotives, including related non-car items at a total price of $233 million. The contract included an option to purchase an additional 45 diesel-battery hybrid locomotives and related non-car items in the amount of $286.7 million, which price is subject to a price escalation pursuant to the express terms and conditions of the Contract (the “Escalation Provision”).
[p008_c0008] This action is to exercise the Option for 45 additional diesel-battery hybrid locomotives. Delivery of the Option Locomotives is anticipated to begin May 27, 2027, and be completed by September 20, 2029.
[p008_c0009] The Option Locomotives, like the locomotives in the base contract, are required to conduct critical in-house track maintenance and work in support of the Capital Plan as they transport flatcars bearing materials to, and debris from, worksites and provide support for work performed within the transit system by outside contractors. In addition to flatcars, the locomotives transport various other work cars (e.g., crane cars, pump trains and vacuum trains) throughout the transit system. These R34255 Option Locomotives will replace R37, R41, R43, R52 and R77 locomotives (all built between 1966 and 1983 and are beyond their useful lives of 35 years) in the NYC Transit fleet. These Option Locomotives are configured to optimize battery usage to minimize diesel emissions, while also providing the tractive effort required for any job in the NYC transit operating environment.
[p008_c0010] The Option price has been adjusted based on the Escalation Provision in the Contract, which included changes in labor and material cost indices, currency exchange rates and other agreed-upon factors for a total price of $389,028,960. However, NYC Transit was able to negotiate a reduction in the overall Option price with MPI based on a reduction of bonding requirements as well as an extension to the original 24-month warranty for the base locomotives to 36-months. The reduction in bonding requirements resulted in a savings of $2,849,034 for a final Option price of $386,179,926 for the forty-five (45) Option Locomotives. Based on the foregoing, the Option price is considered fair and reasonable.
[p008_c0011] MPI has the experience, technical capability, resources, and facilities to perform the work required. The Controller’s Office found that there was reasonable assurance that MPI can perform the work under this option.
[p008_c0012] MPI has certified that pursuant to EO 16, it is not doing business in Russia.
[p008_c0013] The change order for the Option Locomotives has been evaluated to determine the necessity and appropriate scope, if any, of cybersecurity requirements, including any requirements under federal, state and local law and regulations that should be added to the contract terms and conditions. The Authority is working with the vendor to include the applicable cybersecurity requirements in the contract for the Work, which shall be memorialized into a subsequent change order.
