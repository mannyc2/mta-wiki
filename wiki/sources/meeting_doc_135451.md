### [p003_c0001] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0002] President Davey delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0003] President Davey reported on the efforts made to respond to two major snow events in the month of February. He stated that on Monday February 12 th , Transit was able to deliver virtually 100% of scheduled service during this storm that resulted in between 4 and 8 inches of snow. He also reported that the Department of Buses kept service running smoothly.
[p003_c0004] President Davey reported that on February 1 st , NYCT made history by introducing the first modern open gangway subway cars in the United States on the C line. He noted the R211 cars allow customers the ability to seamlessly walk between cars, reducing crowding and improving accessibility. President Davey reported that the R211 has also rolled out on the A-line and that there are a total of 9 trains in service.
[p003_c0005] President Davey then provided the Committee with an update on the January pulse survey. He reported that in January Paratransit reached a new record of 79% customer satisfaction and that ridership on paratransit was 12.4% higher than in January 2023. President Davey reported the survey also indicated that 65% of Bus customers were very satisfied or satisfied with service. He stated that the speeds of buses have improved in the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Manhattan. He reported that travel time satisfaction increased by 6% in the Bronx and by 5% in Manhattan.
[p003_c0006] President Davey then reported that in early February he represented the MTA at the White House for a roundtable conversation on the future of Zero-Emission bus manufacturing in the United States. He stated that he has served as the Co-Chair of a national panel to identify solutions to the current lack of qualified manufacturers in the Electric Bus Space. He noted that NYCT has a unique role to play as North America’s largest bus fleet in driving the national agenda and ensuring that the manufacturing base is ready to support our pledge for an all Zero-Emission Bus Fleet by 2040.
[p003_c0007] President Davey then reported that NYCT unveiled a new, brighter Lafayette Avenue Station in Fort Greene, which is one of the first stations to benefit from NYCT’s commitment to replace all 150,000 subway station lights with brighter, safer LEDs. He stated new LEDs have been deployed at all 63 Re-NEW-vated stations, which has resulted in very positive overall satisfaction. President Davey explained that the LED deployment will brighten dark spots on mezzanines, platforms, and stairwells. He stated that the LEDs will help keep employees safe and will be a force multiplier for the effectiveness of our 15,000 station cameras. President Davey stated that by 2026, all 472 stations will have new, brighter lights, which last three times longer than the fluorescent bulbs thereby saving $5.9 million dollars per year.
[p003_c0008] President Davey reported that installation of platform barriers continues and spoke about expansion of the pilot program to other stations. He explained that these barriers represent a cost-effective solution to tangibly improve customer’s perceived and real safety on platform.

### [p004_c0007] 6. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0008] Chief Michael Kemper delivered the safety and security report.
[p004_c0009] Chief Kemper reported that the subway system experienced a 45.4% increase in index crimes in January 2024, from 152 in 2023 to 221 in 2024. He stated that more than 1,000 additional uniformed officers have been deployed in the subway system. Chief Kemper reported that crime in the subway system decreased by 17.7% during the first three weeks of February. He reported that all categories of enforcement are up including the issuance of summons and, when necessary, arrests. Chief Kemper acknowledged the role video surveillance in the subway system has played in deterring crime and as an investigative tool.
