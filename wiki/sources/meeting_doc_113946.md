### [p003_c0002] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0003] President Davey delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0004] He stated that May marks his one-year anniversary as President of New York City Transit. He noted that customer service continues to be his North Star. He discussed multiple successful initiatives that have been underway, including the “Faster, Cleaner, Safer” plan, new Customer Service Centers, and a reimagined Station Agent role out of the booth.
[p003_c0005] Noting vast improvements in customer satisfaction metrics, President Davey thanked all the employees, and in particular, Group Station Manager Germain Jackson, Group Station Superintendent Renuka Bhupathi, Mariam Standing, and Ken Lau. He also discussed recent improvements to OMNY.
[p003_c0006] In addition, President Davey discussed contributions of other employees in other areas of the system, including Sam Wong, Frank Farrell, Mike Lubanski, Lou Lanfair, and Michael Moore. He also discussed progress with subway safety initiatives, and thanked Governor Hochul, Mayor Adams, and Chief Kemper.
[p003_c0007] Regarding Subways, President Davey discussed ridership milestones and improvements to weekend service.
[p003_c0008] Regarding Buses, President Davey discussed improvements in speed, safety, reliability, and cleanliness. He thanked Frank Annicaro, Tyrone Lampkin, Bus Operator Cleveland, and David Crespo. He also noted progress being made with the “Open Stroller” pilot program, the ABLE camera program, and a legislative proposal for five fare-free bus routes.
[p003_c0009] Regarding Paratransit, President Davey discussed significant improvements in customer satisfaction and ridership, and thanked Chris Pangilinan and his team. He also discussed additional initiatives underway to continue to improve customer satisfaction.
[p003_c0010] He concluded by stating that Transit is what makes New York City possible, and expressed confidence that even cleaner, faster, and safer service will be achieved by this time next year.
[p003_c0011] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0012] 6. ACCESSIBILITY
[p003_c0013] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p003_c0014] Arroyo discussed the 2-gate Wide Aisle Gate pilot, and stated he was encouraged by the early results. He noted on fare evasion, initial figures were positive. He also discussed expansion plans focusing on Sutphin-Archer JFK Airport Station.
