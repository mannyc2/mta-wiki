## Page 6

### [p006_c0001] Congested Corridor Findings
[p006_c0002] Woodhaven Blvd is one of the most dangerous corridors in the city for drivers and pedestrians.
### [p006_c0003] Issues
- [p006_c0004] • High traffic speeds causing unsafe conditions for drivers and pedestrians • Long distances for pedestrian crossing • Heavy congestion at bottlenecks (overpasses) • Medians that make turns difficult for through traffic, forcing buses to use service roads • Slow bus service in need of improvement

## Page 7

### [p007_c0001] Congested Corridor Findings
[p007_c0002] Woodhaven / Cross Bay Blvds are wide thoroughfares unlike others in the city that present a unique opportunity for innovative service and design.
### [p007_c0003] Strengths and Opportunities
- [p007_c0004] • Woodhaven / Cross Bay Blvds create critical connections both locally and to the Rockaways • Safety and traffic flow issues point to desirability of reconfiguring roadway at key points • Transit improvements have potential to be integral contributors to resiliency building efforts

## Page 19

### [p019_c0001] SBS and Traffic Flow
[p019_c0002] Most of Woodhaven and Cross Bay Blvs have 4-5 lanes per direction, with the highest traffic volume at Union Turnpike, where just 3 lanes go over the bridge.
### [p019_c0003] Proposed
- [p019_c0004] • Design for unique Woodhaven Boulevard/Cross Bay Boulevard context • Continue to provide 3 continuous lanes for general traffic • Reconfigure roadway to better use “extra” space without creating merges (bus lanes, medians, narrowed crossing distances) • Redesign complex intersections (i.e. Park Lane S) • Use service roads in appropriate ways • Study local, limited, and express bus services
