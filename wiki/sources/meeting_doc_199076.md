### [p006_c0005] Discussion:
[p006_c0006] The MTA Long Island Rail Road (“LIRR”), on behalf of the MTA Grand Central Madison Operating Company (“GCMOC”), requests Board approval for a retroactive modification awarded under an Immediate Operating Need (“ION”). The ION was approved by the Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, MTA Procurement to modify the competitively negotiated contract with George S. Hall (“GSH”), for the operation and maintenance of the Grand Central Madison (“GCM”) terminal. This modification is for the purchase of a GCM C01 replacement Substation Traction Power Rectifier Transformer and the purchase of a spare Transformer in the amount of $1,241,739.
[p006_c0007] GCM receives its primary traction power for the southerly section of GCM from two dedicated traction power substations (“TPS”), identified as C01 and C02. On June 10, 2025, an electrical fault occurred to the Rectifier No. 1 transformer within C01 TPS, requiring its replacement.
[p006_c0008] Since the failure of the C01 transformer, GCMOC and GSH have taken immediate steps to address the issue and prepare for the transformer replacement. GSH solicited bids from three major electrical contractors: E-J Electric Installation Co. (“E-J Electric”), Unity Electric, and Five Star Electric. Five Star Electric declined to bid. GSH and GCMOC personnel evaluated proposals from E-J Electric ($1,190,000) and Unity Electric ($1,465,998). GSH awarded the contract to E-J Electric, whose bid was approximately 19 percent less than the second bid and within range of the MTA’s internal estimate, and therefore considered fair and reasonable. The net cost to the MTA of $1,241,739 includes GSH’s overhead and profit per the GSH agreement.
[p006_c0009] There is no Significant Adverse Information (“SAI”) that pertains to GSH at this time. In connection with previous contracts awarded to E-J Electric, the subcontractor to be utilized for this modification, E-J Electric was found to be responsible notwithstanding SAI pursuant to all All-Agency Responsibility Guidelines.
[p006_c0010] E-J Electric has certified that pursuant to EO 16, it is not doing business in Russia.
[p006_c0011] The subcontract awarded to E-J Electric by GSH has been evaluated to determine the necessity and appropriate scope, if any, of cybersecurity requirements, including any requirements under federal, state, and local law and regulations. Any applicable cybersecurity requirements, to the extent required, have been included in the terms and conditions.
### [p006_c0012] Impact on Funding
[p006_c0013] This contract will be funded by operating funds that have been approved in the LIRR Operating budget.
