### [p003_c0001] Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committees Meeting Minutes | 3 June 23, 2025
### [p003_c0002] 5. LIRR PRESIDENT’S REPORT:
[p003_c0003] LIRR President Rob Free (“President Free”) reported that ridership continued in the upward trend in May, with over seven million customers. Year to date (“YTD”) ridership has increased 10% over the same time last year.
[p003_c0004] President Free cited that, consistent with the initial projections for ridership between Penn Station and Grand Central Madison as a 60/40 split, there were two days this month where there was a 58/42 split, proving that riders are embracing Grand Central Madison.
[p003_c0005] President Free stated that on-time performance (“OTP”) was 96.7% for May and YTD OTP was also 96.7%.
[p003_c0006] President Free discussed the Amtrak incident involving a power outage in tunnel three, which is one of the tunnels used by LIRR. President Free emphasized that the efforts of LIRR, MTA, and the Governor holding Amtrak accountable lessened the impact of the incident. President Free stated that he will continue to hold Amtrak to their responsibilities to ensure that LIRR service is not affected during their capital work.
[p003_c0007] President Free noted a 30% reduction in customer injury rates and an 11% reduction in employee injury rate for the month of April this year compared to April last year.
[p003_c0008] President Free provided an update on the progress of LIRR’s strategic priorities, including bundling as many work activities as possible into a planned outage to increase productivity, work efficiently, and minimize customer impact. President Free continued by providing examples of completed projects such as the Webster Avenue Bridge replacement in Manhasset and the rail replacement project east of Ronkonkoma.
[p003_c0009] President Free announced the start of the “Summer Saturdays” program in July and August where monthly ticket riders can travel and bring up to two friends or family for $1.00 each.
[p003_c0010] President Free reported on the smoke condition that occurred on June 10 at Grand Central Madison caused by a transformer fire west of the station during the morning rush hour. The layout of the tunnels and tracks at Grand Central Madison minimized the impact to customers. Service continued on other tracks when one tunnel was shut down. The incident also provided an opportunity to learn from the safety protocols and procedures set in place. The cause was still being investigated by FDNY.
[p003_c0011] President Free concluded his remarks by congratulating Lori Ebbighausen, Vice President Corporate Safety, on her retirement and thanking her for her years of service to the railroad.

### [p004_c0003] 6. METRO-NORTH PRESIDENT'S REPORT:
[p004_c0004] Metro-North President Justin Vonashek ("President Vonashek") introduced Vice President of System Safety Brian Pina and thanked Brian O'Donnell for his contributions during the transition.
[p004_c0005] President Vonashek stated that performance remains strong at above 98% in May. Ridership has seen record-breaking numbers post-pandemic, with highest month in May and highest daily and weekly ridership in May, at 90% pre-pandemic.

Metro-North One consists of six pillars of core priorities.
[p004_c0007] In response to a question from Board Member Zuckerman, President Vonashek advised that Metro-North One builds upon and continues the direction that began under Cathy Rinaldi's leadership and is designed to emphasize that the focus of Metro-North's service is moving people.
[p004_c0008] Board Member Valente commented that he is very supportive of the "Metro-North One" initiative and noted that the Chief Transportation Office and the Vice President of the Capital Program had gone into the field with conductors and mirrored their work, which resulted in some positive changes and a strengthening of the relationship of labor and management.
