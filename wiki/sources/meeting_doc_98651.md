### [p005_c0001] MTA has made several recent advances to promote BPM access

### [p006_c0001] NYCT: Key Findings
[p006_c0002] In New York City, bike infrastructure is strongest in Manhattan's core. But it is expanding to the outer boroughs.
- [p006_c0003] • Bike parking: 90% of subway stations have some form of parking installed within 100 feet of an entrance. • Micromobility share: 68% of subway stations are covered with access to Citi Bike or e-scooter share and a total of 74% will be by the end of 2024. • Bike lanes: Street network coverage and connectivity to NYCT facilities is highest in the core, but overall 2/3 of subway stations have some infrastructure nearby.

### [p008_c0001] Commuter Rail: Key Findings
### [p008_c0002] Improving cycling, pedestrian, and micromobility amenities for the commuter railroad stations requires coordination with a range of stakeholders
- [p008_c0003] • Bike Parking: Of stations outside of NYC, 70 LIRR stations (71%) and 56 MNR stations (79%) have bike racks • Micromobility Share: 12 LIRR stations and 11 MNR stations are serviced by some form of micromobility share • Bike Lanes: Bike lane connectivity to commuter rail stations is limited outside of NYC
