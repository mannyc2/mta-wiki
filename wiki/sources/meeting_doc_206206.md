## Page 2

### [p002_c0001] Pattern proposal
- [p002_c0002] • Both parties agree about the following retroactive general wage increases: June 2023: 3% June 2024: 3% June 2025: 3.5% • MTA has offered a 3% general wage increase for 2026. The five unions have requested a 5% increase. • This would set a new pattern for 2026, ahead of any other union agreement. • Wage increases above budget have implications for the MTA, State, and City budgets.
[p002_c0003] _2_
[p002_c0004] _The MTA logo, consisting of the letters 'MTA' in white inside a blue circle._

## Page 3

### [p003_c0001] We could fund this with reasonable changes to work rules
- [p003_c0002] ▶ Reduce the full extra day's penalty pay that is currently required when engineers operate both electric and diesel trains in the same day ▶ Reduce the full extra day's penalty pay required when engineers operate trains in regular passenger service, and then are directed to move the train to a yard – which may be stacked with other penalty pay ▶ Enhance customer service by having ticket clerks, who are now restricted to selling tickets behind a window, provide customer assistance at station platforms ▶ Restrict employees from working more than 18 continuous hours
[p003_c0003] _3_
[p003_c0004] _The MTA logo, consisting of the letters 'MTA' in white inside a blue circle._

## Page 4

### [p004_c0001] Without work rule changes, who pays?
[p004_c0002] An agreement without work rule changes could need to be funded by:
- [p004_c0003] • Higher fares • Increased taxes with additional burden to New York's taxpayers • Service cuts • Headcount reductions
[p004_c0004] _4_
