### [p001_c0002] Standard Follow-Up Report: Transit Adjudication Bureau, 1 st Quarter 2024
[p001_c0003] The purpose of this quarterly report is to update the Transit Committee on Transit Adjudication Bureau (TAB) activities and outcomes, as reflected by several Key Indicators.
[p001_c0004] TAB is the statutory administrative tribunal that receives, processes, adjudicates and pursues collection of civil penalties arising from the tens of thousands of civil summonses that are issued each year for violations of the Transit Rules of Conduct.
[p001_c0005] This quarterly reporting on TAB activities and outcomes commenced in 1992. The report provides the Committee with metrics, covering the most recently completed quarter, for the following Key Indicators:
- [p001_c0006] • Number of TAB violations received by TAB • Number and dollar amount of payments TAB received • TAB revenues and expenses for the quarter • Number of cases adjudicated

### [p002_c0001] STANDARD FOLLOW-UP REPORTS: TRANSIT ADJUDICATION BUREAU FIRST QUARTER 2024
[p002_c0002] The following is a comparison of the key indicators for the first quarter of 2024 as compared to the same period in 2023:
- [p002_c0003] • Summonses issued in the first quarter increased by 6.1% (from 54,734 in 2023 to 58,048 in 2024). • TAB received 34,566 payments in 2024, a 3.5% increase from the 33,402 received in 2023. Direct payments decreased by 10.3% (29,041 to 26,050) from the first quarter of 2023 and payments received from state tax refunds increased 95.3% from 4,361 to 8,516. • Overall total revenue for the first quarter totaled $3,722,033, a 11% increase from the 2023 first quarter revenue of $3,353,234. Receipts from direct payments decreased by 14% to $2,322,753 in 2024 as compared to $2,702,146 in the first quarter of 2023. The $1,387,997 in first quarter receipts from state tax refunds relating to outstanding judgments from prior years, represented a 100.4% increase from first quarter state tax refunds of $692,465 in 2023. • Expenses increased by 23.5% ($1,343,124 compared to $1,087,738) from the first quarter of 2023. Please note that starting from Q1 2024, the segment of incoming data utilized to generate this section of the report is rounded to the nearest thousand. • TAB revenue exceeded expenses by $2,378,909 compared to $2,265,496 for the first quarter of 2023.
[p002_c0004] For further information, see the Key Indicators Chart on the following page.
