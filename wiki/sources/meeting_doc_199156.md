### [p002_c0002] Long Island Rail Road
### [p002_c0003] Safety Performance
[p002_c0004] Long Island Rail Road's safety performance demonstrated continued improvement in 2025. During the current 12-month reporting period (January 2025 through December 2025), the reportable customer injury rate decreased from 1.79 to 1.31 per one million customers compared with the preceding 12-month period. Likewise, the reportable employee lost-time injury rate declined from 4.10 to 3.48 per 200,000 working hours relative to the prior 12 months. These improvements are attributable, in part, to the systematic and proactive approach implemented to identify, assess, and mitigate risk factors affecting both employees and customers.

### [p005_c0001] Metro-North Railroad
### [p005_c0002] Focus on Safety
[p005_c0003] Metro-North Railroad (MNR) remains firmly committed to prioritizing the safety of both our customers and employees. Since the last Safety Committee meeting, we have advanced several initiatives designed to strengthen our safety culture, reinforce safe work practices, and further support our commitment to maintaining a safe and reliable operation.
### [p005_c0004] Employee Focused
[p005_c0005] Collaborated with Station Operations and the MNR Fire Brigade to implement a proactive, voluntary stretching initiative aimed at reducing overexertion and soft tissue injuries. This simple, low disruption control addresses a common daily risk by encouraging employees to prepare their bodies before beginning work and before engaging in physically demanding tasks. The partnership reflects strong local ownership of risk and a shared commitment to keeping employees healthy, prepared, and working safely.
### [p005_c0006] Customer Focused
[p005_c0007] Supported multiple community events in New York and Connecticut that provided opportunities for MNR to educate riders and the public on safe behaviors when traveling on our trains and navigating railroad grade crossings. Through our Together Railroads and Communities Keeping Safe (TRACKS) program, these engagements strengthen public awareness, reinforce safe practices, and highlight MNR's ongoing commitment to protecting the safety of our riders and the communities we serve.
[p005_c0008] Through consistent public outreach and engagement, we achieved zero reportable grade-crossings incidents for two consecutive years.

### [p008_c0001] New York City Transit
[p008_c0002] Overall NYCT Employee Lost Time Accident (LTA) Rates for the 12 months ending December 2025 decreased by 2.1% compared to the prior 12-month period. This decline was driven by reductions in Employee Assaults (-11.2%) and Overexertions (-22.4%). At the departmental level, both Subways (-5.1%) and Regional Bus (-5.7%) Employee LTA Rates also declined over the same comparative period. Customer Accident Rates for both Subways and NYCT Bus similarly declined when compared to the 12 months ending December 2024. NYCT Bus Customer Accident Rate decreased by 13.2%. While nominal Subway Customer Accidents increased slightly by 2.0% (3,041 to 3,102), ridership grew at a greater rate than accidents. As a result, the 12-month rate decreased by 4.7%. The 12-month average January 2025-December 2025 bus collisions per million miles is 59.30, the trend is holding about steady with a slight decline comparing 2025 to 2024. The Department of Buses is addressing the causative and contributing factors of collisions through multifaceted employee education/outreach and updated planning:
### [p008_c0003] Updating Bus Collision and Customer Accident Reduction Plan
[p008_c0004] To reduce collisions and customer accidents by implementing proactive safety measures, strengthening employee training, enhancing infrastructure, and increasing public awareness.
### [p008_c0005] Slim Jim” Outreach for B/Os regarding hot spot locations
[p008_c0006] Quick reference materials and “Hot Spots” for all Boros with the number of accidents at each location were issued to all Operators. Road Operations also uses these locations to conduct their respective BASICS.
### [p008_c0007] Roll out of Vision Zero 7 starting in July (geared more for Operators)
[p008_c0008] Vision Zero provides training on defensive driving practices, including minimizing distractions, maintaining situational awareness, adjusting speed to conditions, anticipating signal changes, navigating areas with limited visibility, and remaining alert to vulnerable road users. The program places particular emphasis on safe driving and turning near cyclists and micromobility users, who may move unpredictably. Training is provided to the Operators once every two years and rotated with DMV Article 19A training (DMV Certifies that B/Os meet Special Requirements for Bus Drivers). The update is in concept phase with updated topics and directed at Bus Operators.
### [p008_c0009] Four New Simulators for Operators training and retraining
[p008_c0010] There will be four simulators available; one at Spring Creek Depot and three at Zerega. DOB is piloting the OWARE system and may expand it in the future. This system is installed on all 242 buses at Casey Stengel Depot. This data logger tool will be used to monitor bus operation and coach operators on parameters such as time, high-rate braking and accelerations, speeding, and B/O Pass Number.
### [p008_c0011] Expansion of the OWARE (Operator behavior tool) initiative
[p008_c0012] DOB is piloting the OWARE system and may expand it in the future. This system is installed on all 242 buses at Casey Stengel Depot. This data logger tool will be used to monitor bus operation and coach operators on parameters such as time, high rate braking and accelerations, speeding, and B/O Pass Number.
### [p008_c0013] Developing Public Safety Campaign partnering with MTA Corporate Communications
[p008_c0014] DOB is working with Corporate Communications, Technology Group and the CTO office to develop Public Service Announcements. One of the messages that was being announced is for customers to secure themselves when boarding/alighting to mitigate customer injuries/incidents.

### [p011_c0001] MTA Bridges and Tunnels
### [p011_c0002] MTA Bridges and Tunnels
### [p011_c0003] Data Review
[p011_c0004] In December 2025, MTA Bridges and Tunnels recorded a total collision rate of 2.49 per million vehicles—a 12.4% decrease from the rate of 2.84 per million vehicles recorded in December 2024. The collisions-with-injury per million vehicles also declined to 0.66 per million vehicles, a 16.7% reduction from last year’s rate of 0.79. The lost-time injury rate for December 2025 was 3.3, which constitutes a 33.8% decrease relative to December 2024, when the rate was 4.9.
### [p011_c0005] Moving Forward
[p011_c0006] MTA Bridges and Tunnels staff and our joint partners continue to focus on engineering, education, and enforcement to further reduce collisions. Facility-specific initiatives including the development of safe work practices, high-hazard safety reviews, and expanded task-based training has been implemented to support improved lost-time injury performance.
[p011_c0007] In addition, monthly and quarterly facility-specific safety reports identifying injury causes and trends, compliance findings, and site-specific safety initiatives are issued and reviewed. Over the last quarter, Environmental, Health, and Safety staff have instituted weekly safety walks and monthly safety committee meetings with each facility, at which injury trend data, accident root causes, and compliance inspection results are discussed and shared with key facility staff.

[p013_c0007] C&D Safety is partnering with OCIP to analyze inspection data and identify recurring hazards. ### [p013_c0012] OSHA and Site Safety Training (SST) Development
[p013_c0013] C&D Safety is piloting OSHA and SST training courses tailored to the unique hazards of transit construction. These courses are being tested to ensure they equip employees with practical skills for hazard prevention and emergency response. ### [p029_c0002] MTA Police Department
### [p029_c0003] Fourth Quarter 2025 Summary
[p029_c0004] The Metropolitan Transportation Authority Police Department (MTA PD), a force of over 1,300 sworn officers serving the LIRR, MNR, SIR, and key MTA bridges, continued to advance its mission of safeguarding riders, employees, and critical infrastructure. This was achieved through visible patrols, data-driven deployment, and strategic collaboration with regional partners.
### [p029_c0005] Key Highlights
- [p029_c0006] • Crime Reduction: Major (index) crime dropped by more than 24% for the year, with significant decreases in Burglary, Robbery and Grand Larceny. Quarter over quarter, major crimes fell nearly 16% from October to December. • Arrests and Summonses: Arrests rose 3% from October to December with notable increases on the LIRR and SIR, though offset by a decline on MNR. Officers issued several thousand criminal and fare evasion summonses this quarter, underscoring a continued focus on quality-of-life enforcement. • Fare Evasion Response: MTA PD responded to hundreds of fare evasion calls in 2025 and continued coordinated enforcement efforts with LIRR and MNR teams. Additionally, a large number of fare evasion summonses were issued within NYC Transit. • Operational Support: Supported MTA Bridges and Tunnels maintenance operations during a major snowstorm by providing traffic safety and security to facilitate uninterrupted snow plowing on bridge spans. • Infrastructure Coverage: Enforcement expanded on the East River bridges, with thousands of summonses issued in September on the Throgsneck and Whitestone spans. Upcoming new deployments on smaller bridges will mark further progress in infrastructure protection and inter-agency coordination. • Community Engagement: The SCOUT Co-Response Initiative continued to assist individuals in crisis by connecting them with shelter and mental health services. In the third quarter, the program made hundreds of contacts, resulting in multiple referrals to care facilities by embedded clinicians.
### [p029_c0007] Summary
[p029_c0008] The fourth quarter of 2025 marked ongoing progress in reducing crime, enhancing enforcement, and strengthening operational readiness. Through focused deployment, expanded infrastructure coverage, and strong regional partnerships, the MTA PD remains committed to ensuring the safety and confidence of all who use the region's transportation systems.
