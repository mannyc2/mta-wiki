### [p001_c0001] Transit & Truck Priority
[p001_c0002] On October 3, 2019, 14th Street between 9th Avenue and 3rd Avenue will become a Transit & Truck Priority corridor as part of an 18-month pilot project designed to:
- [p001_c0003] ■ Increase speeds and reliability for M14 A/D Select Bus Service ■ Improve safety along a Vision Zero Priority corridor
### [p001_c0004] New 14 th Street Regulations
### [p001_c0005] 6 am – 10 pm
[p001_c0006] Only buses and trucks may make through trips between 9th Avenue and 3rd Avenue.
[p001_c0007] All other vehicles may make local trips to access the curb and garages, but must turn at the next available right.
[p001_c0008] Commercial vehicles may load and unload in short-term metered loading zones.
[p001_c0009] Passenger vehicles may drop-off and pickup all along the corridor.
### [p001_c0010] 10 pm – 6 am
[p001_c0011] All vehicles may make through trips along the corridor.
[p001_c0012] No Parking regulations allow expeditious loading and unloading along the corridor.
### [p001_c0013] All Times
[p001_c0014] All vehicles are restricted from making left turns off of 14th Street (except MTA buses at signed locations).
[p001_c0015] Learn more at nyc.gov/betterbuses Contact: 14StTTP@dot.nyc.gov 212.839.6210
### [p001_c0016] Frequently Asked Questions
### [p001_c0017] Where can I be dropped off or picked up?
[p001_c0018] Pickups and drop-offs are allowed throughout the corridor; drivers are responsible for following traffic rules. For some, it may be faster to arrange for pickup or drop-off at a corner.
### [p001_c0019] Why are left turns banned?
[p001_c0020] Left turn bans keep buses from getting stuck behind turning vehicles and increase pedestrian safety; left turns are associated with high rates of pedestrian injuries.
### [p001_c0021] Why are trucks allowed?
[p001_c0022] 14th Street is a designated truck route. Allowing trucks helps to limit truck traffic on side streets.
### [p001_c0023] Which vehicles are considered trucks?
[p001_c0024] Any vehicle that has more than two axles OR six or more wheels is considered a truck.
### [p001_c0025] Where is commercial loading allowed?
[p001_c0026] Short-term loading zones are provided along the corridor every day from 6 am to 10 pm; meters are in effect Monday to Saturday.
### [p001_c0027] How is Transit & Truck Priority enforced?
[p001_c0028] Restrictions are enforced through automated cameras along 14th Street. NYPD traffic agents may also issue summonses.
### [p001_c0029] How are curb regulations enforced?
[p001_c0030] Curb regulations are enforced by NYPD traffic agents; automated cameras may only be used for bus lane enforcement.
### [p001_c0031] How will this project be monitored and evaluated?
[p001_c0032] Data on bus performance, safety, parking, traffic, trucks, and pedestrians will be collected and publicly reported regularly. Community engagement will enable public input.
[p001_c0033] Begins October 3, 2019
