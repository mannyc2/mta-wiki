### [p004_c0001] Why Victory Boulevard?
[p004_c0002] NYC DOT and MTA seek to improve bus service on Victory Boulevard because:
- [p004_c0003] • There are 25,789 average daily riders on 16 routes that facilitate connections to the Staten Island Ferry. • 619 injuries on the corridor in the past 5 years, including 60 who were severely injured. Victory Boulevard is a Vision Zero priority corridor. • Existing bus priority treatments are due for an upgrade. • According to a recent MTA survey, Staten Island bus riders would like MTA to prioritize reducing wait times, providing more reliable service, and minimizing delays.*

[p004_c0005] Source: MTA Survey, Fall 2024*

### [p005_c0001] Existing Bus Lanes on Victory Boulevard
- [p005_c0002] • There have been unpainted, curbside, peak-period bus lanes on segments of Victory Boulevard since 1963 • NYC DOT implemented pedestrian and bus improvements on Victory Boulevard in 2016 near the Staten Island Ferry Terminal • Red paint was installed on the block between Bay St and St Marks Pl in 2020
