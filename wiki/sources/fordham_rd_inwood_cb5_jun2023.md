### [p004_c0001] Why Fordham Road?
- [p004_c0002] • In 2008, MTA and DOT launched the first Select Bus Service (SBS) route on the Bx12 • Bx12 is the busiest bus route in the Bronx, and second busiest in NYC after the M15 • Critical crosstown transportation corridor and serves as a major Bronx-Manhattan connection • 85,000 average daily bus riders on 5 routes (Bx12-SBS + Local, Bx9, Bx17, Bx22) • This area of the city is located within a Tier 1 Priority Investment Area in the NYC Streets Plan • Bus riders and pedestrians form the majority of roadway users on Fordham Road • 62% of households on Bx12 corridor have no access to a private vehicle. 71% commute to work via public transit, walking, or biking
> [p004_c0003] A photograph of a yellow and blue MTA bus stopped at a bus stop. Several people are waiting to board the bus. The bus has a 'CAUTION WIDE ROADWAY TRUCKS' sign and a 'This Bus Uses Clean-Air Technology' sign. The bus is at Fordham Rd at University Ave.
[p004_c0004] _Bx12 SBS, Fordham Rd at University Ave_
- [p004_c0005] • Fordham Rd is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with 4 VZ priority intersections at Sedgwick, University, Grand and Jerome Aves • 83 persons killed or significantly injured between 2014-2018 between Deegan and Boston Road
[p004_c0006] _4_

### [p005_c0001] Existing Bus Priority
- [p005_c0002] • Currently only curbside bus lanes exist on Fordham Rd • Along most of the corridor, bus lanes are only in effect in peak periods, Monday-Friday, 7AM to 7 PM, with a two-hour midday window for deliveries • Westbound 7 AM to Noon, and 2 PM to 7 PM • Eastbound 7-10 AM, and Noon to 7 PM • There is a segment of curbside bus lanes in western section of Fordham, from Sedgwick Ave to University Ave, that are in effect 7am-7pm Monday-Friday, without the two-hour midday break

### [p006_c0001] Curb Demand Challenges
- [p006_c0002] • Curbside bus lanes are a less efficient means of balancing bus priority and curb access • Deliveries are only permitted during a 2-hour window in the midday when the bus lanes are not in effect • Expeditious pick-ups and drop-offs by passenger cars are allowed at any time, regardless of bus lane hours, which causes further delays for buses needing to weave in and out of traffic • Observations and data show that existing curbside bus lanes are frequently blocked throughout the day

### [p010_c0001] Proposed Alternatives Summary
[p010_c0002] Three alternatives, initially proposed at CAB #3 in March 2022 have been under discussion/study:
[p010_c0003] Will continue to study
### [p010_c0004] Alternative A: Offset Bus Lanes in Both Directions:
[p010_c0005] Convert existing curbside bus lanes to offset lanes and extend offset lanes. In effect at all times (24/7).
### [p010_c0006] Alternative B: Eastbound Busway from Morris Av to Webster Av:
[p010_c0007] Eastbound through traffic restrictions on Fordham Rd, except for buses and trucks, with local access on all blocks. Offset lane in westbound direction and offset bus lanes as proposed in Alternative A outside the Busway section. In effect at all times (24/7).
### [p010_c0008] Alternative C: Two-Way Busway between Jerome & Webster Aves:
[p010_c0009] Both eastbound and westbound through traffic restrictions on Fordham Rd, except for buses and trucks, with local access on all blocks; also includes offset bus lanes proposed in Alternative A outside the Busway section. In effect at all times (24/7).

### [p047_c0001] How Does ABLE Work?
- [p047_c0002] • ABLE was implemented on all Bx12-SBS buses on November 18, 2022 with a 60 day warning period, with summonses issued starting January 17, 2023 • Enforces against vehicles parking or standing in a bus lane during the bus lane hours • Two buses must capture the same vehicle 5 minutes apart to issue a violation • ABLE cameras capture license plate information photos and videos, with location information • Human review by DOT process ensures violations are captured and issued according to program rules • When ABLE cameras are installed on new routes, DOT will issue warnings to motorists for the first 60 days • ABLE can enforce anywhere along bus lane, not just in fixed locations
