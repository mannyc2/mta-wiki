### [p003_c0004] 5. METRO-NORTH PRESIDENT’S REPORT:
[p003_c0005] Metro-North President Justin Vonashek (“President Vonashek”) reported that both September and year-to-date on-time performance (“OTP”) through September remained strong at 97.9%.
[p003_c0006] President Vonashek stated that the laser train has been operating daily with great success. OTP for October is 98.7%.
[p003_c0007] President Vonashek stated that Metro-North served 6.3 million customers in September, which is an 8.2% increase from 2024, and a new record of 87.5% of pre-pandemic ridership. The weekday ridership increased 7% compared to 2024. More people are purchasing commutation tickets, at 40% of average daily ridership. The Leaf Peeper trains have attracted thousands of riders on the weekends. A new record was set last weekend with nearly 141,000 customers each day on the Hudson Line. Due to increased demand, an extra round-trip train was added this past weekend.
[p003_c0008] President Vonashek provided an update on the status of the Siemens Charger locomotives: four have been accepted and are in revenue service and three are undergoing acceptance testing on property. For the holidays, Metro-North will be operating Shopper Specials on the New Haven Line. From Saturday November 29 to Saturday January 3, there will be four extra inbound trains during the morning and early afternoon, and four extra outbound trains later in the afternoon. On Sundays from November 30 to January 4, there will be two extra inbound trains during the morning to early afternoon, and two extra outbound trains in the afternoon.
[p003_c0009] President Vonashek reported that for the 12-month period ending in August 2025, Metro-North’s safety performance remained strong. The reportable customer injury rate was flat from the prior year. The reportable employee lost time injury rate improved by over 10% versus previous year. This was achievable through collaboration with our labor partners, addressing root causes, and developing corrective action plans.
[p003_c0010] President Vonashek announced that at Governor Hochul’s request, Metro-North will be operating a roundtrip train between Grand Central Terminal and Albany beginning early next Spring. This is a result of the loss of capacity as part of Amtrak’s East River Tunnel project. The service will be an extension of an existing Hudson Line Poughkeepsie train.
[p003_c0011] President Vonashek stated that Metro-North recently completed Phase One of the Park Avenue Viaduct Rehabilitation project. The Park Avenue Viaduct is over 100 years old and carries every train serving Grand Central Terminal, which is 98% of all Metro-North mainline service. Phase One of the project was completed ahead of schedule and under budget. President Vonashek

### [p007_c0006] 7. SPECIAL EVENTS RIDERSHIP PRESENTATION:
[p007_c0007] LIRR Senior Vice President of Operations Paul Dietlin ("SVP Dietlin") provided a presentation of ridership for special events and the efforts taken to minimize impacts to the operation. SVP Dietlin stated that the LIRR is not just a commuter railroad. Non-commutation ridership has increased 33% since 2023. LIRR serves five major venues: Citi Field, Barclays Center, Madison Square Garden, Forest Hill Stadium, and USB Arena. LIRR also serves other events at locations throughout the island when needed. In total, LIRR provides a primary mode of transportation for over 600 major events each year, many occurring during the PM peak. Potential impacts to the operation include increased load and unload times, especially since the peak hours means there is already a tight schedule. LIRR works closely with organizers of events to gain insight into each event, including information on tickets sales and zip codes to help plan the required service. Through September, the LIRR has provided service to 448 events for this year,

[p008_c0003] SVP Dietlin stated that special events add a level of complexity to LIRR operation. LIRR likes to maintain a level of consistency with train schedules, crew sizes, and the number of train cars. The operation is successful because it is consistent. During an event, LIRR needs to collect tickets and control passenger flow, which can consist of 1200 riders all trying to board a train at the same time after an event. LIRR has developed an approach to manage these events while delivering safe and reliable operation as well as great customer experience. One approach is gating, where crews are stationed at platform entrances and exits to collect tickets before boarding. LIRR has gated 810,000 passengers to date. Another approach is adding station stops to existing routes and runs relief or extra trains to accommodate high passenger volume. Crews, with the assistance of MTAPD, work to keep operation moving and passenger flow controlled. These steps enable the trains to run as close as possible to schedule.
[p008_c0004] In response to Board Member Miller's question regarding other venues besides the main five venues discussed, SVP Dietlin stated that other events include the cricket tournament this year, the Ryder Cup, and an Oyster Festival in Oyster Bay.
