### [p003_c0001] 5. PRESIDENT’S REPORT
[p003_c0002] President Richard Davey delivered the President’s Report.
[p003_c0003] President Davey stated that he challenged the team to push forward with faster, cleaner, and safer service in 2023, and discussed numerous achievements the past year. He discussed improvements in operating efficiency, routes, and bus lane enforcement. He also discussed improvements in cleaning services and facilities.
[p003_c0004] Emphasizing the importance of customer safety, he noted that crime is down and ridership is up. He discussed successes with subway car cameras, expanded security, tactile warning edges, protecting frontline employees, and quality of life issues.
[p003_c0005] President Davey also discussed weekend service enhancements. He noted progress with customer communication, including expansion of wifi in the tunnels and better accessibility signage on subways and buses.
[p003_c0006] He thanked the employees at New York City Transit and emphasized that they are highly valued. He also discussed successes in securing financial stability. He stated in conclusion that the 2024 North Star will be 70% customer satisfaction and $300 million in operating savings at New York City Transit.
[p003_c0007] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0008] 6. ACCESSIBILITY REPORT
[p003_c0009] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, MTA, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p003_c0010] Mr. Arroyo discussed recent accessibility enhancements including eight newly accessible stations, improved technology with Navilens wayfinding, language translation, and service information, and the improved Access-A-Ride app. He noted the addition of audio/visual Digital Information System (DIS) on buses. He also noted expansion of the Open Stroller Program, and the implementation of more high contrast floor decals. He thanked Senator Hoylman-Sigal for help with the decals, and he thanked the Infrastructure Capital Construction Team (ICC) for their work with tactile warning strips.
[p003_c0011] With respect to fare control, Mr. Arroyo highlighted eight new fare gates that have improved accessibility for customers with mobility devices and those traveling with strollers, bikes and luggage. He stated that the wide aisle gates will be expanded in 2024.
[p003_c0012] He thanked the Capital Construction Team for improving elevator service, and he thanked the Board members for approving congestion pricing.

### [p004_c0006] 8. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0007] Michael Kemper, Chief of Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0008] Chief Kemper stated that subway crime was on the rise in November. He noted that this was driven mainly by an increase in grand larcenies. He noted, however, that overall crime for the year was down.
[p004_c0009] He reported a large increase in summonses for quality-of-life infractions. He reported there was also a large increase in arrests this year. He assured that the NYPD was committed to a safe end of the year and wished everyone a happy holiday.

### [p004_c0011] 9. PROCUREMENT
[p004_c0012] Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, Operations, MTA, presented the procurement package.
[p004_c0013] Mr. Montanti stated the procurement package included one action this month. The item was an award of a purchase contract (B40715) to New Flyer America, Inc. for the procurement of 224 low-floor 60-foot diesel buses with an option for 21 buses, as well as related items such as spare parts, special tools and equipment, diagnostic testing, technical documentation, and training, in a total amount of $291.7 Million.
[p004_c0014] There was no vote regarding the procurement.
