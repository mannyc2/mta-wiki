### [p003_c0003] Project Phasing
[p003_c0004] In order to bring improvements to the Webster Avenue corridor as quickly as possible, the project has been split into two phases. In 2013, NYCDOT and NYCT will focus on implementing priority improvements, including offset bus lanes and off-board fare collection. A capital project to build more complicated concrete improvements, including SBS bus bulbs, will follow in 2014-2015.
### [p003_c0005] 2013 Improvements
- [p003_c0006] 1 Resurfacing the majority of Webster Avenue between E 165th Street and E 204th Street 2 Red painted bus lanes on Webster Avenue between E 165th Street and E Gun Hill Road 3 Pedestrian safety islands at key locations 4 Off-board fare collection
- [p003_c0007] 5 Traffic improvements at key intersections 6 Key bus stop and curb regulation changes 7 Transit Signal Priority
### [p003_c0008] 2014-15 Capital Project
- [p003_c0009] 1 Build bus bulbs at SBS stations on Webster Avenue 2 Additional pedestrian safety improvements, including neckdowns and extended medians
> [p003_c0010] An aerial photograph of a city street, Webster Avenue, showing various traffic lanes and surrounding buildings. ### [p003_c0011] Community Outreach
[p003_c0012] The Webster Avenue corridor received the highest overall ranking of potential new SBS routes at the Bronx BRT Phase II Community Workshop in 2009. In 2011, NYCDOT and NYCT began work on the Webster Avenue SBS Project, meeting with the community many times over the next two years, including:
- [p003_c0013] 1. Community Advisory Committee (CAC) Meetings 2. Public Open Houses 3. Door-to-door surveys of local businesses and institutions 4. Numerous stakeholder meetings and presentations
