### [p011_c0001] Door-to-Door Business Outreach
[p011_c0002] May 17, 2022
[p011_c0003] DOT, WHBID, and staff from CM De La Rosa's office went door-to-door to businesses on 181st St to hear concerns
### [p011_c0004] Key concerns raised included:
- [p011_c0005] • Customers confused about Busway regulations, afraid of getting tickets • Safety and quality of life concerns in the evening • Parking availability, including impacts of ConEd construction staging • Commercial loading hours do not match the when deliveries are made • Street vending

## Page 15

### [p015_c0001] Bus Rider Survey: Top Benefits
[p015_c0002] According to survey respondents, the top benefits of the Busway have been:
[p015_c0003]

```text
Quality of bus service 49% Reduced vehicular traffic on 181st St 41% Additional pedestrian space for safer street crossings 59% Improved environmental quality (noise, vehicle exhaust) 25% There have been no benefits to the 181st St Busway 14%
```
[p015_c0004] *Respondents could choose multiple benefits
### [p015_c0005] Comments included:
- [p015_c0006] • Number of buses have increased, and service has gotten better • Pedestrians see fewer conflict with vehicles, and feels safer crossing 181 st St • Reduced congestion along Busway limits
[p015_c0007] _15_

## Page 16

### [p016_c0001] Bus Rider Survey: Top Challenges
[p016_c0002] According to survey respondents, the top challenges of the Busway have been:
[p016_c0003]

```text
There have been no challenges to the 181st St Busway 45% Quality of bus service 30% Pedestrian safety 21% Access to businesses 14% Other 9% Access to taxi, Uber, Lyft, Via 5%
```
[p016_c0004] *Respondents could choose multiple challenges
### [p016_c0005] Comments included:
- [p016_c0006] • Increased traffic and congestion near the Washington Bridge, where the Busway ends • Bus service is still slow and inadequate along the following lines: Bx3, Bx11, Bx13 • Confusing signage • Bus stop crowding is still a concern

### [p018_c0001] Next Steps
### [p018_c0002] 181st St Busway:
- [p018_c0003] • Reduce Busway hours based on community feedback • New regulations: 6 AM – 8 PM, 7-days/week • Move to make Busway pilot permanent
### [p018_c0004] Ongoing DOT work to address community concerns:
- [p018_c0005] • Adjust curb regulation on southwest corner of 181 st St/Audubon Ave • Adjust commercial loading hours to afternoon and allow morning parking • Work with ConEd to explore alternatives to on-street storage of construction materials to free up parking spaces • Continue to monitor traffic on side streets
