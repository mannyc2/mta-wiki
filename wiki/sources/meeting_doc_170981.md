### [p003_c0004] 4. APPROVAL OF WORK PLAN
[p003_c0005] President Crichlow stated that there were no changes to the work plan.
[p003_c0006] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0007] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0008] President Crichlow delivered the President's Report.
[p003_c0009] He emphasized elevator and escalator availability is a priority, and noted 98.7% of NYCT elevators are currently available. With respect to privately owned elevators, he noted 93.2% are currently available.
[p003_c0010] President Crichlow reported last week was Employee Appreciation Day, and discussed the day's celebrations. He encouraged everyone to thank their transit workers. He congratulated William Amarosa on his appointment to Senior Vice President, Subways, Rachel Cohen on her appointment to Vice President, Paratransit, and Bernard Jackson on his appointment to Chief Operating Officer.
[p003_c0011] Chief Operating Officer Bernard Jackson thanked President Crichlow for the introduction and stated he looks forward to working with everyone.
[p003_c0012] President Crichlow discussed Congestion Relief and noted significant improvements to bus performance including better travel time and speed. He also discussed progress with the Rockaways Rehab Project and thanked the US Coast Guard, Schiavone Construction, MTA C&D, and the many divisions within the Department of Subways for their efforts.
[p003_c0013] William Amarosa, Senior Vice President, Subways, provided an update on the R211 fleet and on Subways performance.
[p003_c0014] Rachel Cohen, Vice President, Paratransit, provided an update on Paratransit performance.
[p003_c0015] Frank Annicaro, Senior Vice President, NYCT Department of Buses and MTA Bus, provided an update on Buses performance.
[p003_c0016] Christopher Pangilinan, Chief, Operations Planning, NYCT, provided an update on Ridership.

[p004_c0004] Joseph Gulotta, Chief, Transit, NYPD delivered the Safety and Security Report.

### [p004_c0007] 7. PROCUREMENT
[p004_c0008] Louis Montanti, Deputy Chief Procurement Officer, Operations, MTA provided a report on procurement, which included two items. He stated the first is a request to award a competitively negotiated contract for the purchase, installation, and maintenance of four Bus Operator Simulator (“BOS”) systems to Etech Simulation Corporation (“Etech”). He stated this will be a six-year contract totaling $1.4 million.
[p004_c0009] He stated the second item is the approval to exercise an option to extend the term of a miscellaneous service contract awarded to Trillium CNG for the operation and maintenance of compressed natural gas (“CNG”) fueling facilities for the Department of Buses (“DOB”) and MTA Bus Company for a period of three years in the total estimated amount of $10.1 million.
[p004_c0010] Upon motion duly made and seconded, the Committee approved the procurement.
[p004_c0011] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
