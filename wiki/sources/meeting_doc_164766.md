### [p003_c0001] 3. APPROVAL OF MINUTES
[p003_c0002] Upon motion duly made and seconded, the Committee approved the Minutes of the Committee meeting held on December 16, 2024.
[p003_c0003] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0004] 4. APPROVAL OF WORK PLAN
[p003_c0005] Frank Annicaro, Senior Vice President (SVP), NYCT Department of Buses and MTA Bus, presented the 2025 Work Plan for approval. He noted that the Transit Adjudication Bureau Report will no longer be a NYCT Work Plan item and will instead be reported during the Finance Committee. He stated that the Work Plan approval will be moved to December instead of January the following year. SVP Annicaro also advised that the Work Plan included a staff summary of the Queens Bus Network Redesign for review and approval.
[p003_c0006] Upon motion duly made and second, the Committee approved the 2025 Work Plan.
[p003_c0007] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p003_c0008] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0009] A recorded video was presented wherein Demetrius Crichlow President, NYCT, delivered the President's Report.
[p003_c0010] President Crichlow emphasized NYCT's core missions of safety and service for 2025 and outlined four specific goals: (i) modernize service delivery; (ii) support and protect employees; (iii) harden the system and increase fare compliance; and (iv) improve customer service. He spoke in detail about the various efforts underway to reach these goals such as leveraging data to enhance performance analytics, implementing employee training and succession planning, installing security cameras on buses and making service information more user-friendly. He discussed NYCT's support of the launch of the Central Business Tolling District Tolling Program (CBDTP).
[p003_c0011] SVP Annicaro delivered the Buses Report and discussed performance metrics. He spoke about initiatives Buses will undertake in 2025 to help achieve NYCT's four goals noted above. He reported that preliminary data shows CBDTP tolling has improved traffic on bus routes.
[p003_c0012] William Amorosa, Acting Senior Vice President, Subways, NYCT, delivered the Subways Report and discussed performance metrics. He discussed Subways' goals for 2025 including improving safety and operational performance, delivering a customer focused environment and improving fiscal responsibility.
[p003_c0013] Rachel Cohen, Acting Vice President, Paratransit, NYCT, delivered the Paratransit Report and discussed performance metrics and trends from 2024. She discussed Paratransit's 2025 goals

### [p004_c0004] 6. SAFETY AND SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0005] Jerry Sullivan, Chief, Transit, NYPD delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0006] Chief Sullivan reported that crime in the month of December decreased from December of the previous year. He attributed the decrease to fewer robberies and grand larcenies. He reported significant increases in arrests and summonses in 2024. He commended the hard work of the officers deployed throughout the system and emphasized their continued commitment to combat crime in the system including quality of life crimes and fare evasion.
[p004_c0007] Refer to the video recording of the meeting, produced by the MTA and maintained in MTA records, for the details.
### [p004_c0008] 7. ACCESSIBILITY REPORT
[p004_c0009] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, MTA, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p004_c0010] Mr. Arroyo spoke about the new accessible stations in Westchester Square and Queens, noting that the Queens station was the first to have a direct to platform elevator. He discussed the outreach Paratransit conducted related to the 2025-2029 Capital Plan including launching an online survey for customers concerning which stations to prioritize. Mr. Arroyo noted that the survey can be accessed directly from the MTA Accessibility website.
