### [p003_c0007] 5. PRESIDENT'S REPORT
[p003_c0008] President Richard Davey delivered the President's Report.
[p003_c0009] President Davey noted that March 18 th was Transit Employee Appreciation Day, and he spoke of his visits to over a dozen employee facilities that day and his experience shadowing a conductor. He reported that other members of the leadership team, including Janno Lieber, Demetrius Crichlow and Frank Annicaro, had also visited employees at various work sites.
[p003_c0010] President Davey's remarks about Transit Employee Appreciate Day were followed by a video celebrating transit workers at transit agencies across the United States.
[p003_c0011] President Davey then stressed the importance of employee safety, noting assaults are down, but also noting that even one incident is too many.
[p003_c0012] He reported that February and March saw strong ridership growth, especially with respect to Paratransit and Subways.
[p003_c0013] President Davey discussed two New York Times articles, one that featured Jose LaSalle, the Department of Subway's weekend czar, and another that featured two subway overhaul shops.
[p003_c0014] He commended Chief Mechanical Officer, Subway Car Equipment Siu Ling Ko for her excellent work as the first female to hold that position, and for being featured on NY1. President Davey noted that March was Women's History Month, and emphasized the important roles currently performed by women at the MTA, including two agency presidents.
[p003_c0015] President Davey acknowledged that customers and employees need to feel safe in the system. He discussed Governor Hochul and Chair Lieber's five point plan to improve safety, which includes focusing on mental health outreach, ensuring justice is delivered to perpetrators, adding subway

### [p004_c0008] 7. SAFETY & SECURITY REPORT
[p004_c0009] Michael Kemper, Chief of Transit, NYPD, delivered the Safety and Security Report.
[p004_c0010] Chief Kemper stated that deployment of uniformed officers increased in February and has resulted in a decrease in overall crime. He noted that arrests and summonses are up. He reiterated the commitment to keeping everyone safe. He stated that criminal activity by recidivists continues to be a problem.

[p004_c0013] Quemuel Arroyo, Chief Accessibility Officer, MTA, delivered the Accessibility Report.
[p004_c0014] Mr. Arroyo discussed the recent installation of tactile warning strips in numerous subway stations. He noted the importance of this feature to assist blind and low vision customers. He thanked the ICC team for their success with this project.
