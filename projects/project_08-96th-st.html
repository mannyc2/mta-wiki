Summary

The 2019 Better Buses Action Plan listed this project as "08 96th St, Riverside Dr to FDR Dr," with a 2-mile corridor serving the M96 and M106 and carrying 16,000 daily riders. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p031_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p031_c0004]

The plan described the M96 crosstown route as high-ridership and slow, and it noted that local stakeholders had requested an SBS upgrade. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p031_c0003]

The 2019 potential improvements were to repurpose one travel lane to an offset bus lane where feasible, update curb regulations to match loading needs, update signal timing for bus operations, and consolidate bus stops. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p031_c0006]

By a later MTA performance document, NYC DOT had announced completion of a major Manhattan 96th Street redesign intended to improve bus speed, reliability, and commutes for 15,500 weekday riders on the M96 and M106. [meeting_doc_160446#p010_c0012]

The completed project was described as adding 1.7 miles of bus lanes on the Upper East and Upper West sides between Second Avenue and West End Avenue. [meeting_doc_160446#p010_c0012] [meeting_doc_160446#p011_c0008]

The completed design included offset and curbside bus lanes in both directions to prevent vehicle blockages, plus pedestrian safety improvements, left-turn bays, neighborhood loading zones, and treatments to reduce driver turning speeds. [meeting_doc_160446#p011_c0008]

A later MTA employee-recognition source described the 96th Street bus lane project in Manhattan as recently implemented and credited Bus Service Planner Robert Thompson with overseeing it. [meeting_doc_167246#p010_c0006]

That same source said early indications for the M96 bus lanes were showing improved speeds, but it did not provide a numeric speed-change value for M96. [meeting_doc_167246#p010_c0008] [meeting_doc_167246#p010_c0009]
