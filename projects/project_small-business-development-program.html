MTA Small Business Development Program (SBDP)

project_small-business-development-program

Overview

The MTA Small Business Development Program (SBDP) is described as a program to develop emerging contractors through classes, on-the-job training, and technical assistance on MTA prime contracts, building a pool of qualified contractors able to compete for and complete MTA construction projects safely, on time, and within budget [meeting_doc_102781#p040_c0003]. April 2024 Diversity Committee materials place the SBDP update under the Department of Diversity and Civil Rights business-development agenda [meeting_doc_138186#p002_c0015] [meeting_doc_138186#p041_c0001] [meeting_doc_138186#p041_c0002].

Program supports include prime contract bid opportunities up to $3 million, business management, leadership and technical training, access to working capital and surety bonding, consulting services, MTA project experience, and 10-business-day fast-track payments [meeting_doc_138186#p044_c0002].

Reported Progress

In December 2022, the source reported 536 SBDP contracts totaling $570 million, 140 loans totaling $19.2 million, $560 million in surety bonds, and more than 1,070 firms participating in training; the same section marked the program as achieving its goals [meeting_doc_102781#p020_c0004] [meeting_doc_102781#p021_c0003].

By August 18, 2023, SBDP materials reported 600 contracts totaling $633 million, an estimated 15,168 jobs created, 151 loans totaling $20.2 million, $588 million in aggregate surety bonds, and over 1,072 training-program participants; 217 firms were actively enrolled as of June 30, 2023 [meeting_doc_121026#p043_c0002] [meeting_doc_121026#p044_c0004].

April and June 2024 materials show continued growth: April reported 651 contracts totaling $695 million, 154 loans totaling $20.6 million, over $500 million in surety bonds, and an estimated 16,865 jobs created [meeting_doc_138186#p020_c0002]. June reported 652 contracts totaling $698 million, 158 loans totaling $21.2 million, over $600 million in surety bonds, and more than 1,183 firms participating in SBDP training [meeting_doc_143146#p016_c0004].

Recent Updates

For 2023, DDCR SBDP goals focused on increasing contractor recruitment and improving participation in the loan program [meeting_doc_121026#p042_c0003]. September 2023 materials said first-half 2023 performance exceeded expectations and that the BOC Capital loan-program contract funded six of six loan applicants, compared with three approvals in the prior year [meeting_doc_121026#p042_c0004] [meeting_doc_121026#p042_c0006].

April 2024 materials reported that SBDP exceeded a 10% contractor-recruitment goal with a 17% increase, adding 70 new firms compared with 60 the prior year; the same block said BOC approved ten of ten 2023 loan applicants, compared with three the prior year [meeting_doc_138186#p045_c0005]. September 2024 minutes again reported a 17% increase in new-firm recruitment, three first-quarter outreach sessions, three contracts awarded, four loans approved, and a $2 million bond secured in the first quarter of 2024 [meeting_doc_151596#p005_c0001].

A June 2024 procurement item sought Board approval to extend TDX Construction Corporation's SBDP management contract by up to six months, through March 31, 2025, and add $5 million in funding. The same item explains SBDP as identifying and qualifying firms for enrollment into the MTA Small Business Mentorship Program, where qualified organizations are trained and assisted in competing for larger MTA construction-management projects [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0007]. The extension was intended to keep the mentoring program operating during an RFP process expected to conclude in Q3/Q4 2024 and to allow transition time if a new vendor was selected [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0009].

By December 2024, listed scope changes included rebranding SBDP to MTA EDGE, adding contract-compliance staff, adding flexibility in training courses, developing an alumni program, establishing business-assistance partnerships, and creating specific quantified success metrics [meeting_doc_160081#p020_c0006].

Caveats

The page's sources use both SBDP and SBMP language. The June 2024 procurement item says SBDP identifies and qualifies firms for SBMP, while committee updates report SBDP performance metrics directly [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0007].

Cumulative totals vary slightly by source date: April 2024 reports 651 contracts/$695 million and 154 loans/$20.6 million, while June 2024 reports 652 contracts/$698 million and 158 loans/$21.2 million [meeting_doc_138186#p020_c0002] [meeting_doc_143146#p016_c0004].

The cited June 2024 procurement source expected the replacement-contract RFP to conclude in Q3/Q4 2024, and the December 2024 source lists a rebrand to MTA EDGE, but these cited page sources do not by themselves identify the final vendor outcome [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0009] [meeting_doc_160081#p020_c0006].
