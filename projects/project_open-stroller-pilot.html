Overview

The Open Stroller Pilot created dedicated stroller space so riders with young children could board without folding strollers first; depending on bus model, the space could be an open area near the rear door or a space made by flipping up two side-by-side seats. [meeting_doc_151641#p011_c0007]

The pilot was presented as an accessibility improvement that preserved on-bus accessibility for customers with disabilities and later as dedicated stroller space with no impact to existing priority seating. [meeting_doc_151641#p011_c0007] [meeting_doc_128921#p028_c0003]

Pilot Results And Expansion

When the six-month pilot was just over halfway through, MTA reported persistent use across seven pilot routes, with the highest usage on the Bx23 and S53 and no reported conflicts or incidents. [meeting_doc_104791#p018_c0010] [meeting_doc_104791#p019_c0002]

Ridership counts and customer feedback suggested that riders wanted new stroller policies and were interested in seeing the pilot expanded. [meeting_doc_104791#p019_c0002]

The Bus Open Stroller Pilot later expanded to over 1000 buses on 57 routes, and a December 2023 accessibility update described it as more than 1,000 buses serving 57 routes in all five boroughs. [meeting_doc_121046#p018_c0008] [meeting_doc_128921#p027_c0007]

Program Scale

The pilot created dedicated stroller space on fifty-seven local and select routes operating out of six depots, and those routes served a source-stated combined "250,000 daily weekly riders" in 2021. [meeting_doc_151641#p011_c0006]

The Bus Open Stroller Pilot retrofitted all local and select buses operating out of those six depots with designated stroller spaces. [meeting_doc_151641#p011_c0006]

Customer Appeal

A board-update slide labeled the Open Stroller Pilot under "Increase Appeal for Customers" and "Innovation: Open Stroller Pilot." [meeting_doc_131981#p016_c0002] [meeting_doc_131981#p016_c0003]
