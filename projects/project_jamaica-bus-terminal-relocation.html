Summary

The relocation record covers both the move out of the old Jamaica Bus Terminal and the real-estate actions needed to keep bus operations running. Lease materials describe the old JBT at 89th Avenue and Merrick Boulevard, used by NYCT, MTA Bus, and Nassau County/NICE Bus, with the property sold for anticipated mixed-use development and an alternate location needed to avoid interrupting operations [meeting_doc_104796#p070_c0010]. NYCT later reported that bus operations officially moved to the new interim 168th Street Bus Terminal in Jamaica on June 1, 2025, temporarily replacing the 165th Street Bus Terminal while that site closed for redevelopment [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p010_c0011].

Operations And Lease Context

Early lease material identified a commercial parking lot at 90-01 168th Street as the alternate site, with GJDC as lessor, NYCT/MTA Bus as lessees, and Nassau County/NICE Bus expected to use the site as a subtenant paying a pro-rata rent share based on bus bays [meeting_doc_104796#p070_c0008] [meeting_doc_104796#p070_c0011]. The same action also included a proposed NYPD sublease for about 18-20 vehicles within the lot because existing NYPD parking along 168th Street would interfere with bus operations [meeting_doc_104796#p071_c0005].

A related support-space lease with Gail Lloyd Enterprises covered 2,051 square feet at 168-25 Jamaica Avenue for bus-operation support tied to the relocated terminal [meeting_doc_129081#p044_c0008]. Another staff summary says GJDC's parking lot had been identified as the suitable replacement site, the GJDC lease approved in January 2023 was being finalized, and a Nassau County/NICE Bus sublease was being negotiated [meeting_doc_129081#p044_c0010].

Current Status

The later amendment source says the old terminal lease had expired in September 2023 and was extended through December 2024, while the property sale and expected mixed-use redevelopment still drove the need for an alternate site [meeting_doc_146996#p013_c0010]. It also states that the 90-01 168th Street lease became temporary after the alternative-site use changed, with a new bus terminal planned within the mixed-use development on the present site [meeting_doc_146996#p013_c0011].

NYCT framed the relocation as aligned with the Queens Bus Network Redesign and said the terminal was moving from Merrick Boulevard to a new facility on 90th Avenue; the same passage describes the old terminal's final week as an operational and safety problem, with buses backing into traffic and onto sidewalks [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p005_c0005]. The interim terminal was described as one block east of the former site, serving 10 MTA bus routes, five NICE routes, and nearly 10,000 daily riders [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p010_c0011]. A later page in the same packet says the interim terminal serves the same MTA and NICE routes, is close to the 169th St F station, and is expected to be replaced by a new terminal back at 165th Street after construction is complete [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p025_c0003].

Lease Terms

The January 2023 GJDC lease action listed a five-year term with ten one-year options, $50,000 per month in construction-period rent for about nine months, and $1.6 million in annual base rent inclusive of real estate taxes [meeting_doc_104796#p070_c0008]. The July 2024 amendment listed construction-period rent of $39,001.35 per month for six to nine months, annual base rent of $1,468,016.20 with 2.5% annual increases, and an additional $200,000 toward broker commission [meeting_doc_147016#p001_c0003].

Caveats

The sources distinguish the interim 168th Street/90th Avenue move from the eventual new terminal at 165th Street, so the 168th Street facility should not be described as the permanent endpoint [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p010_c0011] [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p025_c0003].

The parcel and lease terms shift across sources: January 2023 material lists Block 9800, Lot 1 and a five-year term with ten one-year options, while later amendment material lists Block 9800, Lot 5 and a five-year term with five one-year options [meeting_doc_104796#p070_c0008] [meeting_doc_146996#p013_c0008].

A related canonical ACE relation appears unsupported for this page: its evidence block discusses ACE expansion to five bus routes, not the Jamaica Bus Terminal relocation [nyct_key_performance_metrics_june2025#p005_c0003].
