Overview

Amtrak's East River Tunnel rehabilitation addresses Superstorm Sandy damage. LIRR described the work in October 2024 as requiring one tunnel to be removed from service at a time for about 30 months; an April 2025 MTA Board resolution separately described two East River tunnel tubes used predominantly by Amtrak trains as damaged by the 2012 storm and said Amtrak was planning a large-scale rehabilitation using a full shutdown. [meeting_doc_157646#p003_c0007] [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0002] [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0003]

MTA/LIRR Actions And Service Planning

In September 2024 finance materials, MTA Real Estate sought authorization for permanent and temporary easements to the National Railroad Passenger Corporation for the East River Tunnel 1 and 2 Rehab project at Sunnyside Yard in Queens. The action listed $4.79 million in total compensation, split between $2.93 million for permanent easements and $1.86 million for temporary easements, for work including signal houses, traction and signal power duct banks and manholes, a cable bridge, and a construction work area. [meeting_doc_151746#p001_c0001] A related staff summary identifies LIRR as the agency and Amtrak as the licensee, gives the Sunnyside Yard location, lists three permanent easements and two temporary easements, and states that the temporary easements last until the earlier of 41 months, a longer LIRR-approved duration, or completion of the work. [meeting_doc_151756#p009_c0008]

The November 2024 LIRR timetable was designed around the outage. LIRR materials said Amtrak's project would shut down one of the four tubes and require notable Long Beach Branch schedule changes, while also stating there would be no Long Beach Branch service reduction and Manhattan travel times would be unchanged. [meeting_doc_155021#p001_c0009] At the October 28, 2024 Joint LIRR and Metro-North Committee meeting, President Free said the November 11 timetable followed months of dialogue among the three rail carriers using Penn Station; he reported that Grand Central Madison helped limit overall LIRR service impacts to two Penn-to-GCM train diversions and a 27-minute adjustment to another train. [meeting_doc_157646#p003_c0007]

Later LIRR timetable materials continued to list East River Tunnel rehabilitation among projects supported by seasonal timetable and trackwork programs, including the May 19 through September 1, 2025 timetable and a later September timetable focused on state of good repair and reliability work. [meeting_doc_170846#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_179526#p001_c0005]

2025 Board Concerns

On April 30, 2025, an MTA Board resolution sought approval of a statement outlining concerns about Amtrak's means, methods, schedule, resources, and readiness to complete East River Tunnel repairs within a three-year shutdown period while limiting service impacts. [meeting_doc_171436#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_171436#p001_c0007] The resolution says MTA had urged Amtrak to consider a night-and-weekend shutdown approach and that Amtrak instead favored a full shutdown of the two tunnels over three years. [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0004] [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0005] It calls on Amtrak to reconsider a full shutdown strategy and reopen discussions about adjusting scope to support an overnight/weekend approach that would limit risks to Amtrak service and LIRR commuter service. [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0011] [meeting_doc_171436#p002_c0012]

A companion April 30, 2025 board update frames the operational exposure: LIRR, Amtrak, and NJ Transit operate through Penn Station and the East River Tunnels, including 461 daily LIRR trains through the tunnels; with the same number of trains running on fewer tracks, the presentation warned that small delays could cascade. [meeting_doc_171496#p002_c0001] [meeting_doc_171496#p004_c0005] [meeting_doc_171496#p005_c0005] The same update states that two of four tracks in Amtrak's East River Tunnel were flooded during Superstorm Sandy and had needed repair since 2012, with construction initially set to begin in 2019 but pushed back to 2025. [meeting_doc_171496#p003_c0002]
