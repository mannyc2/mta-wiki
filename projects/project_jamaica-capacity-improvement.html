Overview

Jamaica Capacity Improvement (JCI) is framed in MTA Board materials as LIRR's master plan for future railroad operations through Jamaica Station. The same board package says Phase 1 was substantially complete, with a new passenger platform in Jamaica for dedicated Atlantic Terminal service and infrastructure supporting Grand Central Madison service; Phase 2 objectives include raising allowable train speeds, increasing train throughput, adding parallel routing, universal 12-car platforming, and state-of-good-repair work across the Jamaica complex [meeting_doc_121326#p072_c0008].

Trackwork And Service Effects

LIRR staff summaries tied near-Jamaica switch installation work to the project. For the weekend of December 10-11, LIRR planned to take two main tracks out of service between Jamaica and Queens Interlocking for about 48 hours, replacing westbound service at Queens Village and Hollis with buses during that outage [meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0008]. A later staff summary described a similar January 7-8 Jamaica switch installation with the same two-track outage and westbound bus substitution at Queens Village and Hollis [meeting_doc_102751#p002_c0005]. The summaries state that funding for the trackwork projects was in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budget [meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0010] [meeting_doc_102751#p002_c0007].

2023 Work Plan Thread

Multiple LIRR Committee Work Plans carried "Winter Trackwork Programs" as a November 2023 item, describing schedule adjustments for switch installations near Jamaica in support of Jamaica Capacity Improvement. The same item also grouped Valley Stream switch maintenance and Wreck Lead Bridge rail maintenance, so the work-plan entry is broader than JCI alone [meeting_doc_102721#p005_c0011] [meeting_doc_102721#p005_c0012] [meeting_doc_105781#p005_c0020] [meeting_doc_105781#p005_c0021] [meeting_doc_121096#p004_c0009] [meeting_doc_121096#p004_c0010].

Property Acquisition And Phase 2

The September 2023 MTA Board package says LIRR was advancing Jamaica Capacity Improvement Phase II and needed one fee acquisition and six permanent easements adjacent to the LIRR right-of-way; where negotiated agreements could not be reached, EDPL proceedings would be used to acquire the interests [meeting_doc_121326#p070_c0007] [meeting_doc_121326#p070_c0009]. MTA held an EDPL public hearing on July 27, 2023 for proposed JCI property acquisitions, and the board materials say oral and written comments were made part of the record and considered [meeting_doc_121326#p072_c0004]. The same findings say future projects and JCI Phase 2 required air rights and one full-parcel purchase for signal equipment platforms supporting Phase 2 signal infrastructure [meeting_doc_121326#p072_c0009].

Caveats

The sources do not present a single unified chronology: the December 10-11 and January 7-8 blocks describe specific outages, while the November 2023 work-plan blocks describe an agenda item without exact outage dates [meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_102751#p002_c0005] [meeting_doc_121096#p004_c0009] [meeting_doc_121096#p004_c0010].

The page combines project-wide JCI references with Phase II property-acquisition material. The staff summaries only say switch installation outages were "in support of" JCI, while the broad goals and property-acquisition details come from Phase II EDPL findings [meeting_doc_100336#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_121326#p072_c0008].

Valley Stream switch maintenance and Wreck Lead Bridge rail maintenance are grouped in the same Winter Trackwork Programs item, but the cited text does not make them JCI components [meeting_doc_105781#p005_c0021].
