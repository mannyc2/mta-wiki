Overview

The Jamaica Capacity Improvement (JCI) Phase II project is described as an LIRR effort to increase train throughput, reduce travel times through Jamaica, lengthen Jamaica Station platforms, upgrade rail systems, and bring infrastructure within the project limits to a state of good repair. [meeting_doc_101026#p084_c0010]

The project is also described as part of the LIRR's master plan for future railroad operations through Jamaica Station, with Phase 2 objectives including higher allowable train speeds, parallel routing capabilities, universal 12-car platforming, and state-of-good-repair work across the Jamaica complex. [meeting_doc_121201#p151_c0008]

The stated completion benefits are higher train speeds, lower station dwell times, more reliability, and an overall increase in capacity at Jamaica. [meeting_doc_121201#p151_c0010]

Property Acquisition And Easements

The December 2022 Finance Committee book listed acquisition of property interests for Phase 2 of the Jamaica Capacity Improvement Project as an action item. [meeting_doc_102786#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_102786#p012_c0005]

The 2022 Board materials described permanent property acquisitions along the LIRR right-of-way and requested authorization to acquire property interests by negotiated agreement or eminent domain. [meeting_doc_101026#p084_c0008] [meeting_doc_101026#p084_c0011]

The 2022 staff summary said MTA would try to acquire property interests through negotiated agreements, but might need eminent domain if agreements could not be reached, and sought authority to satisfy EDPL requirements to avoid project delays. [meeting_doc_101026#p084_c0012]

In September 2023, the Finance Committee book stated that one fee acquisition and six permanent easements were required adjacent to the LIRR right-of-way for JCI, with negotiated agreements underway and EDPL proceedings available where agreements could not be reached. [meeting_doc_121201#p149_c0009]

The 2023 findings say those acquisitions included certain air rights and one full-parcel purchase for multiple signal equipment platforms, which would support new signal infrastructure and provide safe employee clearing areas. [meeting_doc_121201#p151_c0009]

A February 2024 real-estate item identified a permanent easement from MD Hossain and Suraya Hossain at 93-20 170th Street for an about 308.7 square foot air-rights area, with a permanent term and $55,000 compensation for installation and maintenance of an aerial platform. [meeting_doc_133386#p005_c0008]

The February 2024 Finance Committee minutes also recorded the Hossain easement as a permanent easement to facilitate an aerial platform above property adjacent to the LIRR right-of-way in Queens. [meeting_doc_135356#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_135356#p005_c0005]

The June 2024 Finance Committee minutes listed easements with 166 Archer Avenue Co. LLC and with the State of New York Division of Military and Naval Affairs in connection with or in support of JCI Phase II. [meeting_doc_146976#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_146976#p004_c0015]

Public Process And Board Findings

MTA held an EDPL public hearing on July 27, 2023, to receive comments on proposed property acquisitions for the Jamaica Capacity Improvement Project. [meeting_doc_121201#p151_c0004]

The hearing record included oral statements and written comments about the JCI project and proposed property acquisitions, and written comments were accepted through August 10, 2023. [meeting_doc_121201#p149_c0011]

On September 20, 2023, the MTA Board approved acquisition of property interests by eminent domain or otherwise and made EDPL Determination and Findings for the Jamaica Capacity Improvement Project. [meeting_doc_121201#p151_c0003]

The findings determined that MTA should exercise eminent-domain power to acquire the listed property interests in order to permit the purposes of the JCI Project to be achieved. [meeting_doc_121201#p152_c0010]
