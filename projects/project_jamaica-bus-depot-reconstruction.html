Jamaica Bus Depot Rebuild and Expansion

project_jamaica-bus-depot-reconstruction

Overview

The Jamaica Bus Depot Reconstruction Project concerns the existing depot at 165-18 Tuskegee Airmen Way in Jamaica, Queens, bounded by Tuskegee Airmen Way, Merrick Boulevard, 107th Avenue, and 165th Street. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0010]

The existing depot has operated since 1939 and is described in the board materials as a critical component of the Queens Division bus depot network. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0010]

The project would reconstruct and expand the depot so it can manage operations, maintenance, and on-site storage for up to 272 Standard Bus Equivalents for current bus assignments and future surface-transit demand in southeastern Queens. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0011]

Planned Facility

The project plan includes 15 maintenance bays, one chassis wash station, three fueling lanes, three bus wash lanes, two interior bus wash stations, administrative and storage space, and infrastructure for an entirely zero-emissions bus fleet. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0012]

The plan calls for at least 60 electric buses to be assigned to the depot immediately upon completion. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0012]

The materials describe the new depot as placing bus storage and operations behind sound-barrier walls, improving the Merrick Boulevard streetscape, adding public art, and providing a LEED-certified building with a green roof, architectural lighting, and landscaping. [meeting_doc_101026#p087_c0013]

Property Acquisition

In November 2022, MTA sought approval of Determination of Findings under the Eminent Domain Procedure Law for fee interests and temporary easements needed to facilitate the project. [meeting_doc_100276#p117_c0008]

MTA held an EDPL public hearing on September 12, 2022, to describe the project, proposed property interests, and general environmental impact, and written comments were accepted until September 23, 2022. [meeting_doc_100276#p118_c0007]

The Determination and Findings stated that the Board approved acquisition by eminent domain if necessary, and the attached chart listed permanent full takings on six Merrick Boulevard parcels plus temporary easements on 165th Street parcels. [meeting_doc_100276#p119_c0008] [meeting_doc_100276#p123_c0007]

The same materials said initial construction would demolish existing properties along Merrick Boulevard for the new depot structure, keep the existing depot operating until the new facility is operational, and then demolish the existing depot. [meeting_doc_100276#p120_c0010]

February 2023 Property Step

February 2023 Board materials requested authorization for NYCT and MTA Bus to buy two commercial properties from Bauerschmidt Realty Holding Corp. at 106-04 Merrick Boulevard and 166-15 107th Avenue through negotiated agreement. [meeting_doc_106031#p065_c0008]

Those materials state that six adjacent commercial properties had to be acquired in fee for the depot expansion and reconstruction, and that the MTA Board had authorized negotiated agreement or condemnation in July 2021 subject to further Board approval for negotiated terms. [meeting_doc_106031#p065_c0010]

After independent appraisals and formal offer letters in June 2022, Bauerschmidt Realty Holding Corp. accepted MTA's offer for Lots 90 and 95 in lieu of condemnation. [meeting_doc_106031#p065_c0011]

MTA Real Estate requested contracts of sale valued at $2.9 million for Lot 90 and $430,000 for Lot 95. [meeting_doc_106031#p065_c0012]

The February 2023 Finance minutes also listed a City University of New York lease for temporary relocation of NYCT buses in connection with the Jamaica Bus Depot project. [meeting_doc_105971#p008_c0006]

Status Update

A February 2024 Capital Program Committee update described Jamaica Bus Depot Reconstruction as replacing a 1939 building and designing the depot to accommodate an all-electric fleet. [meeting_doc_133356#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_133356#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0005]

The February 2024 update reported the project at 13% complete and said the IEC concurred with an Estimate at Completion of $654 million. [meeting_doc_133356#p007_c0002]

The update attributed an eight-month delay to redesign and late permitting of a temporary parking lot needed to maintain continuous facility operation during construction, shifting the current substantial-completion forecast from December 2026 to August 2027. [meeting_doc_133356#p007_c0002]

The same update said top risks had been identified, mitigations were being developed, and the IEC saw possible schedule recovery with substantial time remaining. [meeting_doc_133356#p007_c0002]
