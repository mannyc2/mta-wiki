Open Stroller Pilot Program

project_meeting-doc-102806-open-stroller-pilot

Context

The Open Stroller Pilot Program appears here as an accessibility and bus customer-experience initiative, not as a corridor-specific bus-priority street project. In the November 29, 2022 Operations Committee minutes, MTA discussed it alongside OMNY and station accessibility as one of the accessibility initiatives being communicated to the public at more than thirty events. [meeting_doc_102806#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_102806#p004_c0004]

Implementation Signals

A May 2023 performance update described the Open Stroller pilot as successfully launched the prior September and preparing to expand to roughly 1,000 buses on 50 routes by the end of the year. [meeting_doc_111811#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_111811#p006_c0007]

The May 22, 2023 committee minutes also grouped progress on the Open Stroller pilot with bus improvements in speed, safety, reliability, and cleanliness, alongside the ABLE camera program and a proposal for five fare-free bus routes. [meeting_doc_113946#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_113946#p003_c0008]

By the June 26, 2023 committee minutes, the Accessibility Report described continued retrofitting along 57 routes as part of the MTA Bus Open Stroller Pilot Program, and noted that NYCT had shown a new bus model with stroller space at a Bus Festival event. [meeting_doc_115246#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_115246#p004_c0007]

Bus Design

A February 2024 employee-recognition source describes the implementation work as redesigning buses to accommodate the Open Stroller Pilot Program and provide a designated space in buses for open strollers. [meeting_doc_133281#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_133281#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_133281#p003_c0009]
