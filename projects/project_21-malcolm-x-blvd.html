Project Scope

This record covers the Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave Transit Improvements project, identified as item 21 for "Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave, Chauncey St to Atlantic Ave." The Better Buses Action Plan describes a 0.2-mile corridor serving B46 SBS and B46 buses, with total daily ridership of 42,000 and average bus speeds of 5.2 mph in the AM and 3.5 mph in the PM. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0007]

The project is a localized bus-priority follow-up to the broader B46/Utica Avenue SBS corridor, not the original SBS launch project. The Action Plan says the B46 had already received Select Bus Service upgrades in 2016, while the May and June 2019 decks say the B46 was Brooklyn's busiest bus route, had 38,000 daily riders, connected Bed-Stuy to the 3 4 A C J Z and 30 bus routes, and had seen southbound ridership increase while speeds slowed on blocks without bus lanes. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p044_c0006] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p004_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p004_c0002]

Why This Segment Was Prioritized

The 2019 project decks say Malcolm X Blvd / Utica Ave was selected as a priority project because of slow bus speeds, unreliable travel times, high ridership, and high bus volumes. One May/June version quantified the issue as 3.4 mph during peak periods, compared with a 6.7 mph Brooklyn average, with buses about every 2 minutes per direction during peak. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p008_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p007_c0002]

The bottleneck focus was Atlantic Ave. For Fulton St to Atlantic Ave, the February/March 2020 decks describe traffic backing up to Fulton St and beyond, with bus speeds of 4.5 mph in the AM and 2.7 mph in the PM before the bus lane. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p009_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p008_c0002] For Chauncey St to Fulton St, they say half the block is a B46 bus stop, southbound traffic backs up from Fulton St, and traffic and parking block buses from reaching the stop. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p012_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p011_c0002]

Treatments

The May/June 2019 proposal included a southbound curbside bus lane from Fulton St to Atlantic Ave, keeping overnight parking while allowing buses to bypass traffic approaching Atlantic Ave. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p017_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p014_c0002] By the February/March 2020 update, this had become "1 block of southbound bus lane, photo enforced"; the decks report bus speeds improved 54% in the AM peak and 106% in the PM peak, traffic speeds improved 18% in the AM peak and 17% in the PM peak, and overnight parking was preserved. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p010_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p009_c0002]

The May 2019 deck also proposed a queue jump signal at Utica Ave & Atlantic Ave for northbound and southbound buses, described as a 7-second head start for buses and pedestrians crossing Atlantic Ave and as a pedestrian safety improvement on a Vision Zero Priority Corridor. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p018_c0002]

The remaining southbound segment was Chauncey St to Fulton St. In 2019, the deck linked it to the B46 upgrade to longer articulated buses and listed the implementation date as TBD; in February/March 2020, the same segment was described as a curbside bus lane that would let buses bypass traffic and access the stop while keeping overnight parking. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_may2019#p019_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_jun2019#p015_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p015_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p014_c0002]

Timeline And Status In The Sources

The public process began with a CB 3 outreach campaign in 2018. The March 2020 deck describes a 5-month effort from July to December 2018, 27 venues in CB 3, 619 survey participants, and conversations with 800+ members of the public. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p020_c0002] Its survey summary says 83% of respondents regularly rode the bus and B46 was the second most used route. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p021_c0002]

The February/March 2020 timeline lists proposal development in late 2018/early 2019, CB 3 Transportation Committee in May 2019, CB 3 Full Board in June 2019, implementation and evaluation of Fulton St to Atlantic Ave in July-Dec. 2019, CB 3 Transportation Committee in February 2020, CB 3 Full Board in March 2020, and spring 2020 for implementing Chauncey St to Fulton St. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p017_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p016_c0002] The same February/March route overview says B46 SBS was upgraded to articulated buses in January 2020. [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_feb2020#p004_c0002] [malcolm_x_blvd_utica_ave_mar2020#p004_c0002]

Caveats And Open Questions

The staged evidence supports Fulton St to Atlantic Ave as implemented by February/March 2020, but only supports Chauncey St to Fulton St as scheduled for spring 2020; no checked source proves that later segment was actually installed. The queue jump signal is clearly present in the May 2019 proposal, but the checked February/March 2020 blocks do not establish completion. Ridership values differ by source framing: the Action Plan lists 42,000 total daily ridership for the project, while the local project decks describe the B46 as 38,000 daily riders.
