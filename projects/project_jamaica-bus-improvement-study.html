Overview

The Jamaica Bus Improvement Study was a NYCT/DOT effort, begun in 2009, to improve bus movement on Jamaica's busiest corridors and better coordinate pedestrians, buses, and drivers. [jamaica#p001_b0001]

Jamaica Center was framed as a major transit hub, with more than 47 bus routes, E/F/J/Z subway service, a major LIRR station, and the AirTrain to JFK. [jamaica#p001_b0001]

The study goals were to improve bus travel to and from Jamaica, improve connections to subway and rail stations, and balance bus travel with pedestrian and vehicle traffic. [jamaica#p001_b0001][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p001_c0005]

Study Findings

Field observations and outreach described narrow streets with heavy bus and local traffic, misaligned intersections, poor bus lane markings, undesirable bus layovers, missing passenger amenities, bus bunching, long waits, congestion from turns, diversions to side streets, confusing signage and lane markings, and insufficient enforcement. [20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0003][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0004][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0005][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0011][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0013]

May 2011 business outreach with the Jamaica Center Business Improvement District interviewed Jamaica Avenue merchants about potential bus lane changes, including delivery times and delivery-vehicle parking patterns. [20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0015][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0016][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0018][20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p002_c0020]

Preliminary Recommendations

The preliminary package combined bus lane upgrades and extensions, bus stop moves, intersection and operational adjustments, curb access regulations, and education on bus lane rules. [jamaica#p001_b0001]

The May open-house materials described a mix of offset and curbside bus lanes with red lane markings and overhead signage, with bus lane hours still to be determined and midday loading windows to be used where needed. [20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p004_c0004]

The bus lane mileage summary listed 1.6 existing miles, 1.4 miles of additions, 0.1 miles of removals, and 2.9 miles as the new total. [20110517_jamaica_open_house_posters#p004_c0006][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p012_c0003]

Archer Avenue recommendations improved the existing 150th-to-160th Street lanes with signs and terra-cotta markings, retained 24-hour operation, extended the eastbound lane from Sutphin Boulevard to 150th Street and from 160th Street to Merrick Boulevard, and reconfigured 160th Street to Guy R. Brewer Boulevard for two eastbound lanes and one westbound lane. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p015_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p015_c0005][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p015_c0006][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p016_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p017_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p017_c0005]

Jamaica Avenue recommendations used offset bus lanes, expanded hours, and turn restrictions on existing Parsons-to-168th Street lanes, plus a Parsons-to-Sutphin Boulevard west extension that might require curb parking or delivery restrictions. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p020_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p020_c0005][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p022_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p022_c0005]

The Merrick Boulevard proposal called for a high-visibility 24-hour lane from Hillside Avenue to Archer Avenue to improve access to the 165th Street Bus Terminal and allow bus layover north of 89th Avenue. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p024_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p024_c0005][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p024_c0006]

The 165th Street proposal called for a bus lane from Hillside Avenue to 89th Avenue to prioritize bus movements exiting the 165th Street Bus Terminal. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p025_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p025_c0005]

The package also included lengthening, separating, and reversing bus stop locations; changing parking and truck-loading regulations; and realigning the Hillside Avenue/Home Lawn Street/169th Street area to make Home Lawn Street the primary north-south roadway and provide more pedestrian space. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p027_c0004][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p028_c0004][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p028_c0005][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p028_c0006][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p028_c0007][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p030_c0004]

Public Process And Status

The study materials identify Community Advisory Committee Meeting #1 on September 29, 2009, Community Advisory Committee Meeting #2 on March 25, 2011, a May 12, 2011 open house, and Queens Community Board 12 presentations on June 14 and June 15, 2011. [201104_jamaica_cac2_slides#p010_c0014][201104_jamaica_cac2_slides#p001_c0001][201104_jamaica_cac2_slides#p001_c0002][jamaica#p001_b0001]

The June 15 CB12 deck summarized the study recommendations as improved bus lanes, bus stop and layover relocation, intersection realignments, dedicated livery and commuter-van areas, curb regulation revisions, placard parking relocation, potential delivery windows, and bus shelters where possible. [201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0003][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0004][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0005][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0008][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0009][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0010][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0011][201106_jamaica_cb12_slides#p010_c0012]

The source webpage stated an implementation target of spring 2012 for the proposed improvements. [jamaica#p001_b0001]
