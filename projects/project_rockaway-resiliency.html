Summary

The Rockaway Resiliency Bundle was presented in January 2023 as an A-line Queens resiliency package made up of several projects bundled to take advantage of required outages. The same Capital Program Committee discussion tied it to the Local Hiring Goals pilot, where 20% of the New York State labor force would come from project communities. [meeting_doc_104701#p005_c0007]

The award record identifies contract C35327 to Schiavone Construction Co. LLC for design-build rehabilitation and flood-resiliency work along New York City Transit's Rockaway Line in Queens. The January 2023 award amount was $392,685,186, with a 1,339-calendar-day duration. [meeting_doc_104701#p006_c0005]

Scope And Purpose

Related procurement and project-status records describe the work as viaduct repairs at Hammels Wye and along the Rockaway Line branches, flood-mitigation elements, a new signal tower and track crossover at Beach 105th Street, related traction-power, signal, and utility work, and replacement of electrical and mechanical components on the South Channel Bridge. [meeting_doc_102776#p102_c0009]

The broader resiliency rationale is the Rockaway Line's exposure across Jamaica Bay. Later capital materials say Superstorm Sandy caused severe damage, including embankment breaches and signal-cable impacts, and suspended service for seven months. Those materials frame the project as long-term flood mitigation bundled with state-of-good-repair work so service can recover more quickly after future storm surge events. [meeting_doc_131491#p015_c0006] A January 2024 project snapshot described the Rockaway Line Viaduct as about 5 miles of elevated structure from Beach 116 St-Rockaway Park to Far Rockaway-Mott Avenue. [meeting_doc_131491#p016_c0005]

2025 Service Window

By December 2024, related records described the Rockaway Line Rehabilitation & Resiliency Project as a $393 million investment, with a planned January 17, 2025 to May 19, 2025 outage to reconstruct the elevated Hammels Wye and rehabilitate South Channel Bridge systems. [meeting_doc_163201#p003_c0005] During the outage, MTA described shuttle-bus options between Howard Beach-JFK Airport and the Rockaways, discounted Far Rockaway LIRR tickets, extra QM15 service, and a subway shuttle between Rockaway Park and Far Rockaway. [meeting_doc_163121#p024_c0008] [meeting_doc_163121#p025_c0002]

In April 2025, NYCT performance materials said the anticipated end of the Rockaway Resiliency project was the following month, described the project as fortifying the A train and Rockaway Shuttle segment across Jamaica Bay, and credited the shuttle train and bus operation used during the work period. [meeting_doc_170921#p009_c0009]

Completion Signals

May 2025 materials say full subway service to the Rockaway Peninsula resumed on Monday, May 19, after 17 weeks of capital improvements, and describe the project as delivered on time and on budget for A and S train resiliency and reliability. [meeting_doc_174141#p022_c0003] The same source says the work included complete reconstruction of Hammels Wye plus modernized signal and power systems, including new traction-power cable, signal cable, and a track switch. [meeting_doc_174141#p022_c0004] A May 28, 2025 board chair presentation also listed "Rockaway Resiliency" with a check mark. [meeting_doc_174391#p009_c0002]
