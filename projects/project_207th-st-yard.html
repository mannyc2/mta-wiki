207th Street Yard

project_207th-st-yard

Overview

207th Street Yard is described as NYCT's main storage yard for A and C line rolling stock, with the maintenance shop for the A, B, C, and D lines and the overhaul facility for the 1-7 lines. The same January 2025 update says Superstorm Sandy flooded the 43-acre yard in 2012, creating the need for critical rail-operation repairs and added flood protection. [meeting_doc_163236#p010_c0010]

The source set treats the work as a combined yard flood-mitigation, signal-repair, and sewer-replacement program. A later update describes the broader 207th Street Yard project as a resiliency and infrastructure upgrade dating back to 2018, at the second-largest transit facility in New York City after Coney Island Yard. [meeting_doc_163236#p010_c0009] [meeting_doc_164901#p007_c0001]

Scope

The completed scope included 2,100 feet of flood-resilient walls, replacement of 35,000 feet of track and 83 switches, signal and power-system upgrades, a new signal and Rapid Transit Office tower, and a new water line for fire suppression. [meeting_doc_164901#p007_c0001]

A January 2025 component list also identifies five flood gates, two signal buildings, replacement of 80% of yard tracks with a new signal system, an interceptor sewer outside NYCT's 207th Street Yard, traction-power control and third-rail distribution upgrades, and a new water main for Building A and fire suppression. [meeting_doc_163236#p010_c0014]

Status History

In May 2023, the 207th St Yard project was reported 92% complete with a $643 million budget/EAC; the Independent Engineering Consultant expected completion within the latest EAC but flagged the November 2023 substantial-completion target as challenging and said January 2024 was possible. [meeting_doc_111791#p007_c0001]

At the same time, the related 207th St Sewer Replacement project was 63% complete, with a $152 million budget and $163 million EAC; C&D was contesting a contractor move from April 2024 to November 2024 substantial completion and trying to recover schedule through concurrent remaining work. [meeting_doc_111791#p007_c0001]

By February 2024, C&D said 207th Street Yard had aligned five separate projects under one project management team for cost, schedule, risk, and safety accountability, with minimal impact on yard operations. [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0005] The yard project was then 97% complete, while the sewer replacement was 75% complete with a reduced $153 million budget/EAC and a November 2024 substantial-completion forecast. [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0007]

Completion

In January 2025, C&D reported that its Infrastructure Business Unit achieved 48 substantial completions in 2024, including 207th Street Yard, valued at $563 million. The same meeting book says the project reached substantial completion in 2024 and that all protections against future storms were in place. [meeting_doc_163236#p010_c0006] [meeting_doc_163236#p010_c0012]

A January 2025 status table lists Yard Flood Mitigation & Signal Repairs as 100% complete with a $649 million forecast and Sewer Replacement as 100% complete with a $152 million forecast, for a combined $801 million forecast; punch-list work was due to be completed in April 2025. [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0003] [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0004]

An IEC schedule slide says both projects reached substantial completion in Q4 2024, with all major elements completed and in service and punch-list items remaining. It attributes flood-protection and signal-repair extensions to field conditions and design changes, and sewer-replacement extensions to public-utilities work and private-property access issues. [meeting_doc_163236#p021_c0003] [meeting_doc_163236#p021_c0005] [meeting_doc_163236#p021_c0007]

By February 2025, the project was described as completed and executed in close coordination with New York City Transit to minimize service disruptions. The sewer relocation moved the sewer system outside the 207th Street facility, used micro-tunneling to avoid costly utility conflicts and street closures, and was reported completed on schedule and $8 million under budget. [meeting_doc_164901#p007_c0001] [meeting_doc_164901#p007_c0004]
