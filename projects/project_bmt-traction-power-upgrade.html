Project Context

The BMT Traction Power Control System Upgrade, contract C34866/7, is a C&D Systems project focused on the control layer for BMT traction power. Across Capital Program Committee updates, MTA describes the purpose consistently: improve reliability and operation of the existing BMT traction power control system and add redundancy in case of failure. [meeting_doc_111791#p069_c0004] [meeting_doc_138181#p010_c0003] [meeting_doc_173986#p020_c0004]

Scope

The core scope is to replace antiquated Master Terminal Units at the Power Control Center with touchscreen LED monitors and a state-of-the-art video wall, and to build a new Emergency Backup Power Control Center. [meeting_doc_111791#p069_c0004] The work footprint is systemwide within the control network: 13 power substations, 14 communications rooms, and the Power Control Center. [meeting_doc_138181#p010_c0003] By the 2024 update, MTA also noted a separate IRT SCADA upgrade in design, planned for a 2026 award, which appears adjacent to but distinct from this BMT project. [meeting_doc_138181#p010_c0003]

Progress And Cost

The May 2023 status table listed August 2025 as both the original and forecast substantial-completion date, a $55 million original and forecast estimate at completion, and approximately 14% completion. [meeting_doc_111791#p069_c0005]

By April 2024, the schedule and $55 million estimate at completion were still shown unchanged, while reported progress had advanced to approximately 65% complete. [meeting_doc_138181#p010_c0004]

By May 2025, the status table still forecast August 2025 substantial completion, but the cost baseline shown in the table had changed: original estimate at completion was listed as $68.5 million and forecast estimate at completion as $61.9 million. The same table reported the project approximately 98% complete. [meeting_doc_173986#p020_c0006]

Program Context

The project appears in recurring C&D Systems progress updates rather than as a standalone procurement item. The 2023 update frames C&D Systems as the unit delivering mission-critical systems, control centers, advanced technology integration, emergency and customer communications, electronic security, and communications networks. [meeting_doc_111791#p068_c0005] In 2024, the same unit described a broader shift toward integrated systems-of-systems, including command-and-control systems, plant management systems, backbone infrastructure, and data center facilities. [meeting_doc_138181#p009_c0006]
