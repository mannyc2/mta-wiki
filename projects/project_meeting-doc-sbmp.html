Program Role

The Small Business Mentoring Program sits inside MTA's small-business construction pipeline. A June 2024 procurement item says the Small Business Development Program identifies and qualifies firms for enrollment into the MTA Small Business Mentorship Program, and that under SBMP, managed by MTA Construction and Development, qualified organizations are trained and assisted in competing for larger and more complex MTA construction management projects. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0007]

A December 2022 update reported a current-year SBMP goal of 70 projects totaling $75 million. At that point, 15 projects had been awarded totaling $8.9 million, 21 were in bidding totaling $19.3 million, 10 were pending award totaling $7.6 million, and 11 were pending bid opening totaling $11.7 million. [meeting_doc_102781#p004_c0009]

Contract And Procurement Timeline

The June 2024 procurement package listed TDX Construction Corporation as vendor for "Small Business Mentoring Program Management" under a March 1, 2018-September 30, 2024 contract term. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0004] The same package requested a $5,000,000 modification to Contract No. 10200-01, listed against an original amount of $43,058,640 and a current amount of $43,048,640. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0005]

The procurement discussion described the extension as up to six months, through March 31, 2025, and tied it to continuity while a replacement RFP was underway. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0007] The stated purposes were to keep the mentoring program running during the RFP process and to provide transition time for vendors and ongoing projects managed by incumbent TDX if a new vendor was selected. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0009] Finance Committee minutes described the same June 2024 action as an estimated $5 million MTA Headquarters item extending TDX's SBMP management contract through March 31, 2025, with the current contract price structure unchanged. [meeting_doc_146976#p003_c0008]

The procurement package said the replacement RFP was unique because it combined SBMP and SBDP in one solicitation, with proposals under review and an award expected to be recommended to the Board in Q3/Q4 2024. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0010] It also states that the "Small Business Mentoring Project" is funded by MTA's Capital Budget. [meeting_doc_143366#p006_c0015]

Scale And Capital-Plan Context

A March 2025 Diversity Committee table reported SBMP contract awards of 60 contracts totaling $67.8 million in 2023 and 23 contracts totaling $32.2 million in 2024. [meeting_doc_166966#p022_c0002] The March 2025 capital-plan commitments included awarding $400 million for small businesses through SBMP and adding 350 more firms to SBMP over the next five years. [meeting_doc_166966#p035_c0003]

The same commitment slide put SBMP inside a wider 2025-2029 diversity and small-business procurement frame: award $6.5-$7.5 billion to MWBEs, DBEs, and SDVOBs; add local hiring goals to $5 billion of contracts; and certify 300 new DBE firms. [meeting_doc_166966#p035_c0003] The preceding slide said MTA anticipated approximately $7.5 billion in funding available for MWBE/SDVOB/DBE contracting over the next five years. [meeting_doc_166966#p034_c0003]

Related SBDMP Procurement Context

A separate December 2024 procurement package requested Board approval to award McKissack-WSP JV a seven-year, $112,699,501 miscellaneous service contract for management of MTA's Small Business Development and Mentoring Program. [meeting_doc_160291#p001_c0007] [meeting_doc_160291#p002_c0006] That package described SBDMP as a program to increase small-business participation on MTA public works contracts, with guidance on estimating, project scheduling, Quality Assurance/Quality Control plans, and bidding strategies. [meeting_doc_160291#p002_c0007]

The December 2024 package said the contract was the first single RFP covering both the Small Business Development Program and SBDMP, and that McKissack-WSP would work with MTA agency project managers to provide mentoring services through all phases of construction, bank loan repayment administration, payment tracking, and project-meeting attendance. [meeting_doc_160291#p002_c0008] It also said each MTA agency would select projects estimated up to $3 million for which only SBDMP firms could bid, with the awarded firm receiving guidance from McKissack-WSP. [meeting_doc_160291#p002_c0008]
