Penn Station Access

project_annual-2021-penn-station-access

Overview

Penn Station Access extends Metro-North New Haven Line service into Penn Station via Amtrak's Hell Gate Line, the Hell Gate Bridge, and Harold Interlocking. The planned Bronx stations are Hunts Point, Parkchester/Van Nest, Morris Park, and Co-op City, with associated power upgrades, Bronx and Queens substations, and right-of-way work. [meeting_doc_115181#p002_c0007] [meeting_doc_104741#p006_c0004]

Beyond the four accessible stations, C&D project materials describe bridge rehabilitations, 19 miles of new and rehabilitated track, new and reconfigured interlockings, signal/power/communications modernization, and expansion of Metro-North's New Rochelle Yard. The same materials frame Hell Gate Line state-of-good-repair work as a reliability benefit for Amtrak customers and future Northeast Corridor service. [meeting_doc_115231#p010_c0004]

Public Benefit

MTA materials describe the project as adding direct Penn Station service for New Haven Line customers and new rail access for East and South Bronx communities. The documents estimate roughly 500,000 people within one mile of the four planned stations, and a later update says the project will reduce commute times to Midtown by up to 50 minutes. [meeting_doc_104741#p006_c0004] [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0003]

Timeline And Delivery

MTA's 2021 annual report says the agency approved a design-build contract in December 2021, and that the Design Build and Project Management Consultant contracts were awarded on December 29, 2021. Ground was broken on December 9, 2022. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p008_c0003] [annual_report_narrative_2021#p089_c0003] [meeting_doc_104741#p006_c0004]

A project-status block lists original substantial completion as March 2027, a forecast of Q4 2027, a $2.867B budget excluding Metro-North rolling stock procurement, and approximately 20% completion. A budget block separately states that hiring was planned to start in 2025, with service expected to commence in November 2027. [meeting_doc_115231#p010_c0005] [meeting_doc_160111#p001_c0014]

The main documented delivery risk is access to Amtrak right-of-way and Force Account support. One status update says the project was approximately 6-9 months behind schedule; a later update says coordination had improved, but the independent engineering consultant stated that full schedule recovery was unlikely and the budget would need reevaluation after resequencing. [meeting_doc_115231#p011_c0003] [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0004]

Property And Fleet Dependencies

Real estate work is a major dependency. The Amtrak easement package covered four Bronx station properties and two Queens substation sites, with not-to-exceed compensation of $28 million at the 30% design stage and a mechanism for adjustment as design advanced. Later real-estate materials describe additional acquisitions for stations, substations, right-of-way augmentations, and New Rochelle Yard improvements, including Palmer Center easements and reimbursement work tied to a New Rochelle Yard retaining wall. [meeting_doc_115181#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_115181#p002_c0012] [meeting_doc_173856#p001_c0010] [meeting_doc_179566#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_179566#p001_c0010]

Fleet planning is separate from the core civil budget: the $2.867B budget note excludes Metro-North rolling stock, while procurement materials call for coach cars for the new Penn Station Access route and 13 B+AC locomotives for service from Penn Station to the four new Bronx stations via Amtrak territory. [meeting_doc_115231#p010_c0005] [meeting_doc_164941#p005_c0007] [meeting_doc_164941#p007_c0006]

Caveats

Budget scope needs normalization: the 2021 annual report states a $12.867 billion total project budget, while later project-status materials list $2.867B excluding rolling stock. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p089_c0003] [meeting_doc_115231#p010_c0005]

Schedule is not fully settled in the cited record set: one block says service is expected in November 2027, while the independent engineering consultant said full schedule recovery was unlikely pending resequencing. [meeting_doc_160111#p001_c0014] [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0004]

Easement quantities and costs may change because the Amtrak package was based on 30% design and explicitly allows adjustment as design advances. [meeting_doc_115181#p001_c0008] [meeting_doc_115181#p002_c0012]
