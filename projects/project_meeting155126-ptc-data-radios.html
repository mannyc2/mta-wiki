PTC Data Radios - MNR and LIRR

project_meeting155126-ptc-data-radios

Overview

This page covers an October 2024 Metro-North-led procurement for Positive Train Control data radios for Metro-North and LIRR, with GE Grid Solutions, LLC listed as vendor for contract #422937. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0003] The staff summary says Metro-North, on behalf of itself and LIRR, sought Board approval for a noncompetitive estimated-quantity contract after an initially competitive IFB process; the Joint LIRR/Metro-North Committee minutes describe the same item as a competitively solicited and negotiated estimated-quantity contract. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0005] [meeting_doc_157646#p011_c0013]

The business reason given was lifecycle and compliance risk: GE had supplied the original PTC radios for both railroads' rolling stock fleets, the current radios were described as obsolete, and replacement units were said to be required to maintain PTC functionality and federal mandate compliance. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0006] [meeting_doc_157646#p012_c0002]

Procurement Path

The IFB was advertised in the New York State Contract Reporter, the New York Daily News, and on the MTA website, and two bids from GE and Siemens Mobility were received on July 21, 2023. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0007] Both bidders took exceptions to contract terms, including cybersecurity requirements, and both bids were deemed non-responsive; Metro-North then chose to negotiate with GE after concluding Siemens' technical approach and cost were not economically viable. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0008]

As of the October 25 procurement packet, the item was scheduled for Committee consideration on October 28 and Board action on October 30; the October 28 minutes state that the Committee recommended approval of the procurement item. [meeting_doc_155126#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_157646#p012_c0004]

Scope And Cost

The listed contract term runs from November 1, 2024 through November 30, 2031, with an estimated total of $3,797,000 split as $1,146,046 for Metro-North and $2,650,954 for LIRR. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0003] The contract scope includes project engineering, hardware and firmware integration, factory validation, prototype testing or commissioning, and delivery of the first production units over a five-year period. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0010] [meeting_doc_157646#p012_c0002]

GE's work is described as proprietary-firmware engineering to design, build, and test the next-generation PTC Data Radio with backward compatibility for legacy radios; after prototype delivery for environmental integration testing and commissioning, an initial lot of 250 units would be supplied over a five-year delivery schedule. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0009] Each delivered unit would carry a five-year warranty, GE would support existing legacy units through December 2026, and additional radios beyond the initial quantity would have a negotiated firm fixed price of $2,950. [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0009] [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0010] [meeting_doc_155126#p006_c0011]

The procurement materials say the project would be funded by the railroads' operating budgets, and the MTA Department of Diversity and Civil Rights set zero percent MWBE/SDVOB goals because it found a lack of subcontracting opportunities and marketplace firms. [meeting_doc_155126#p007_c0008] [meeting_doc_157646#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_155126#p007_c0006]
