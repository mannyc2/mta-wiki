Overview

Nostrand Ave / Rogers Ave Select Bus Service was planned as a 9.3-mile B44 corridor project from the Williamsburg Bridge to Sheepshead Bay, converting the B44 Limited into SBS while keeping B44 Local service in place. [2010_06_08_brt_nostrand_cb1#p007_c0002] [2010_06_08_brt_nostrand_cb1#p008_c0002] A Fall 2011 project handout described SBS as coming to Nostrand, Rogers, and Bedford Avenues through a NYC DOT and MTA New York City Transit partnership, with the goal of shortening travel time and improving reliability for B44 riders. [201011_nostrand_handout#p001_c0004]

The core service change was directional: B44 SBS would use Nostrand Avenue southbound and Rogers Avenue northbound, while the B44 local would remain on New York Avenue for northbound local service. [201110_nostrand_cb8_slides#p010_c0003] [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p002_c0020] The launch flyer later states that B44 Nostrand/Rogers Avenues Select Bus Service began on November 17, 2013. [2013_11_sbs_nostrand_flyer#p001_c0001]

Project Elements

Planning materials described a package of bus-priority and station treatments: offset bus lanes with bus bulbs for 5 miles, transit signal priority for 3.8 miles from Flatbush Avenue to Sheepshead Bay, new low-floor buses, next-generation fare collection, and station/bus branding. [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0003] [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0004] [2011_03_07_brt_nostrand_cb3#p006_c0006] By late 2011, design materials focused the bus-lane/station work on Flushing Avenue to Eastern Parkway and Empire Boulevard to Farragut Road, with bus lanes at the right curb on Nostrand and Rogers between Eastern Parkway and Empire Boulevard and on Bedford Avenue between DeKalb and Flushing Avenues. [20111205_nostrand_cb3_slides#p007_c0002] [201109_brt_nostrand_cac4#p006_c0004]

The implemented project report says the final SBS package used off-board fare collection, bus lanes, rerouting of the northbound bus from New York Avenue to Rogers Avenue, bus-stop consolidation, traffic improvements, larger buses, bus bulbs, new shelters, and greenery. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0004] It reports 9.6 miles of bus lanes covering just over half of the route, plus fifteen bus bulbs and five neckdowns on the project corridors. [brt_nostrand_progress_report_june2016#p018_c0006] [brt_nostrand_progress_report_june2016#p020_c0003]

Planning And Outcomes

The planning record shows a multi-year public process: early timelines placed CAC meetings and open houses in 2010-2011, final design in 2011, and SBS implementation/construction in 2012. [2010_06_08_brt_nostrand_cb1#p012_c0002] [201109_brt_nostrand_cac4#p009_c0002] A December 2011 CB3 deck stated there had been 19 public meetings in the previous three years about Nostrand & Rogers SBS. [20111205_nostrand_cb3_slides#p009_c0002]

The 2016 progress report found post-implementation B44 SBS travel times improved by 15-31% compared with the former B44 Limited, with northbound route travel times 31% faster in the AM and 20% faster in the PM, and southbound travel times 19% faster in the AM and 15% faster in the PM. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0006] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p024_c0004] It also reported a 10% B44 SBS ridership increase from 2014 to 2015, higher satisfaction among SBS riders than former Limited riders, and safety reductions where bus bulbs/neckdowns or bus lanes were added. [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p027_c0008] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p026_c0003] [b44_sbs_progress_report_2016#p005_c0006]
