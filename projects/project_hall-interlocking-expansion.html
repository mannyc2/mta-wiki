Hall Interlocking Expansion Project

project_hall-interlocking-expansion

Overview

Hall Interlocking Expansion is framed as the first construction piece of Jamaica Capacity Improvements Phase 2. The broader JCI program is described as streamlining train routing, enhancing service, increasing train throughput and speeds, and reducing travel time through Jamaica; Phase 2 also includes state-of-good-repair work, power and signal replacement, platform extensions for 12-car trains, and higher-speed switches [meeting_doc_104746#p006_c0006] [meeting_doc_104746#p006_c0008]. The Hall project itself is described as a new rail bridge through Jamaica's Hall Interlocking to create two parallel train routes east of the station, with the design-build contract awarded and construction expected to finish in fall 2026 [meeting_doc_104746#p006_c0009].

Scope And Status

A later progress item says MTA Construction & Development and LIRR were making progress on Hall Interlocking Expansion as part of the multi-phase JCI Program, and that the expansion would create two new parallel routes east of Jamaica Station [meeting_doc_170831#p014_c0005]. The work described there centers on a new 280-foot, two-track railroad bridge over 150 Street and Atlantic Branch Track 1, assembled from girders delivered to E Yard and launched pier-to-pier using rollers and a jacking system [meeting_doc_170831#p014_c0006] [meeting_doc_170831#p014_c0007]. The same item says retaining walls are being replaced or modified to raise the new bridge and through tracks for Atlantic Branch clearance; future stages would extend the tracks into two routes that eventually become Montauk Branch Track 2 and Main Line Track 2 [meeting_doc_170831#p014_c0008].

A Capital Program Committee source labels the item "Jamaica Capacity Improvements Phase 2, Hall Interlocking Expansion" and says Hall Interlocking Expansion is the first JCI Phase 2 program element for construction [meeting_doc_127556#p011_c0003] [meeting_doc_127556#p021_c0003]. Its project-status table gives October 2026 as both original and forecast substantial completion, lists a $168.6 million Hall Interlocking Expansion budget, says the project was approximately 11% complete and design was 90% complete, and names MLJT as contractor [meeting_doc_127556#p011_c0006]. Another paragraph in the same source says the construction contract was awarded in December 2022 to MLJT for $91.6 million, with overall substantial completion forecast for October 2026 and the project 11% complete [meeting_doc_127556#p022_c0003].

Timetable Support

LIRR timetable-change memos repeatedly list Hall Interlocking Expansion among projects supported by schedule plans. The project appears in the September 5-November 12, 2023 timetable memo and again in the September 2-November 9, 2025 memo [meeting_doc_115156#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_115156#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_179526#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_179526#p001_c0005]. It also appears in intervening 2024 and 2025 timetable memos [meeting_doc_124266#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_133331#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_138226#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_155021#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_164866#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_170846#p001_c0004].

Caveats

Keep the $168.6 million project budget separate from the $91.6 million construction contract amount; both appear in the November 2023 source [meeting_doc_127556#p011_c0006] [meeting_doc_127556#p022_c0003].

The timetable memos list Hall Interlocking Expansion alongside other projects, so they support recurring timetable/schedule-plan context but do not isolate Hall-specific service impacts by themselves [meeting_doc_115156#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_179526#p001_c0005].

Identity review may be needed: earlier procurement/board sources describe the same or very similar Hall bridge/two-track work with MLJT, including a November 2022 award request and December 2022 approval [meeting_doc_100281#p090_c0007] [meeting_doc_100281#p090_c0009] [meeting_doc_102911#p013_c0001].
