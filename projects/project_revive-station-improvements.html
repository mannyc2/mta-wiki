REVIVE Station Improvement Program

project_revive-station-improvements

What The Sources Say

In NYCT's November 2024 performance metrics document, REVIVE is described as a recently rebranded station-improvement program: the stated model is to make meaningful station upgrades while stations are already closed for other planned maintenance and construction work. [meeting_doc_157731#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_157731#p009_c0009]

The same November 2024 President's message places station revivals in a broader post-2020 station/customer-service recovery narrative, saying NYCT had recently celebrated its 100th station revival and that customer feedback from station revivals had been overwhelmingly positive. [meeting_doc_157731#p006_c0002]

In the April 2025 metrics document, NYCT reported that Facilities had completed 130 station revivals since the start of REVIVE and was on pace to complete another 40 in 2025. [meeting_doc_170921#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_170921#p005_c0005]

Station-Environment Context

The April 2025 update groups station revivals with other station-environment improvements: more than 270 LED light conversions, continuing platform-barrier installation, and communications-room upgrades. The source presents these as related station-environment work, but does not define the lighting, barrier, or communications-room items as formal REVIVE components. [meeting_doc_170921#p005_c0005]

A nearby November 2024 note also mentions two new elevators at the 14th Street station complex, while separate Capital Projects and Accessibility sections describe that work as a distinct 14th Street accessibility megaproject. [meeting_doc_157731#p009_c0009] [meeting_doc_157731#p021_c0003] [meeting_doc_157731#p024_c0006]
