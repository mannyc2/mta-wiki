New Randall's Island Ramps and Accessibility Improvements at RFK Bridge

project_rfk-randalls-island-ramps

Project Scope

New Randall's Island Ramps and Accessibility Improvements at the RFK Bridge is a Bridges & Tunnels / MTA Construction & Development Design-Build project for access between the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge and Randall's Island. A November 2022 procurement item identified Contract RK-93 with DeFoe Corporation for design-build reconstruction of Randall's Island ramps, at $108,173,500 and 840 Calendar Days. [meeting_doc_100281#p085_c0008] The contract discussion described reconstruction of two vehicular ramps and three pedestrian ramps of the RFK Bridge on Randall's Island. [meeting_doc_100281#p085_c0010]

The core scope is two new vehicular ramps to Randall's Island for traffic flow and safety, plus replacement of three pedestrian ramps with ADA-compliant ramps to improve accessibility from the Bronx and Manhattan to Randall's Island Park. [meeting_doc_102776#p016_c0014] Later updates describe the non-vehicular elements as three new ADA-compliant bike-ped paths replacing original deteriorated ramps, and then as three replacement shared-use path ramps on Randall's Island. [meeting_doc_129101#p013_c0003] [meeting_doc_160206#p054_c0007]

Accessibility And RFK Context

The project is framed as both access work and state-of-good-repair work. The December 2023 update says the completed project would provide end-to-end ADA connectivity among Randall's Island, Manhattan, and the Bronx, while also facilitating future reconstruction of the Manhattan Plaza structure in a future capital plan. [meeting_doc_129101#p013_c0003] The December 2024 update repeats the Manhattan Plaza connection and says the improvements benefit thousands of people during special events on Randall's Island, plus hundreds of everyday park users. [meeting_doc_160206#p054_c0007]

A separate MTA micromobility update places the RFK Bridge work in a broader set of three projects improving interborough connectivity to Randall's Island: Manhattan Side Connection completed, Randall's to Manhattan and Bronx ongoing with projected completion in 2024, and Randall's to Queens award imminent with projected completion in 2028. [meeting_doc_129371#p006_c0005] [meeting_doc_129371#p006_c0006] [meeting_doc_129371#p006_c0007] This page's Manhattan/Bronx scope appears to correspond to the Randall's-to-Manhattan-and-Bronx item; the Queens item should stay separate unless canonical review merges it.

A real-estate information item also shows the Randall's Island property context: B&T's option agreement with the City, acting through Parks, covered premises on Randall's Island for improvements to the RFK Bridge and its approaches on Randall's Island. [meeting_doc_157716#p002_c0008] On July 30, 2024, B&T executed a Fifth Modification extending the agreement term to January 1, 2028 while it negotiated future property-interest adjustments for ongoing RFK Bridge approach improvements. [meeting_doc_157716#p002_c0010]

Schedule And Budget

As of the December 2022 Bridges and Tunnels update, the project had a forecast award in December 2022, an original budget of $124.7 million, an EAC of $120.8 million, and substantial completion forecasted for April 2025 on target. [meeting_doc_102776#p016_c0015] [meeting_doc_102776#p016_c0016] [meeting_doc_102776#p016_c0017] The 2023 look-ahead was to perform design, pre-construction survey, inspection and testing, and begin construction of the new ramps. [meeting_doc_102776#p016_c0019]

By the December 2023 update, the status table listed original/forecast substantial completion as April 2025 / December 2024, original/forecast budget as $124.7M / $119.4M, status as approximately 40% complete, and contractor as Defoe Corporation; the table footnote says the original budget does not include $7.5M in project reserves. [meeting_doc_129101#p013_c0004] Reported progress included completion of foundations, abutment walls, and the majority of columns for new vehicular and pedestrian ramps, with the project currently ahead of schedule. [meeting_doc_129101#p013_c0006] The 2024 look-ahead was to complete ramp columns, superstructure steel, concrete decks and parapets, install related utilities, and achieve substantial completion. [meeting_doc_129101#p013_c0008]

By the December 2024 update, the project was listed as substantially complete, four months ahead of schedule, with the same $124.7M original / $119.4M forecast budget line and Defoe Corporation as contractor. [meeting_doc_160206#p054_c0008]

Source Notes

Source terminology changes over time: pedestrian ramps, ADA-compliant ramps, bike-ped paths, and shared-use path ramps all appear in the cited project descriptions. This draft treats them as the same three non-vehicular accessibility elements because the sources tie them to the same Randall's Island ramp reconstruction, but that identity should remain reviewable.

Separate RFK records cover adjacent work, including the north pedestrian ramp and Harlem River Lift Span fender upgrades, Manhattan Plaza painting, suspended-span retrofit, and RFK/FDR work. Those should not be merged into this page without additional canonical review.
