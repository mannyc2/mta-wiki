Port Washington Yard Track Extensions

project_port-washington-yard-track-extensions

Summary

Port Washington Yard Track Extensions appears in the November 2022 and November 2023 LIRR ESA Support Projects updates as a project in the Planning phase with a $3.0 million project budget. [meeting_doc_100321#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_100321#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_100321#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0001] [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0002]

The project-specific slide in the November 2022 update says options for yard track extensions were severely limited by extensive development near the station, and that advancement depended on agreement with the Town of North Hempstead for real estate acquisition/swaps. [meeting_doc_100321#p008_c0004] [meeting_doc_100321#p008_c0005]

The November 2023 update keeps the same planning-phase and $3.0 million budget entry, but describes the limiting condition as extensive parking near the station and adds funding availability to the conditions for advancement, alongside a Town of North Hempstead real estate agreement. [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p008_c0004] [meeting_doc_127536#p008_c0005]

Source-Time Reading

The available source evidence supports a source-time planning/contingency note rather than a construction timeline: the tables list the project in Planning, and the project-specific slides describe constraints and dependencies rather than actual or forecast milestone dates. [meeting_doc_100321#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_100321#p008_c0005] [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p008_c0005]

The two updates should preserve their distinct wording: the 2022 source emphasizes "development" and "real estate acquisition/swaps," while the 2023 source emphasizes "parking," "real estate acquisition," and "funding availability." [meeting_doc_100321#p008_c0005] [meeting_doc_127536#p008_c0005]
