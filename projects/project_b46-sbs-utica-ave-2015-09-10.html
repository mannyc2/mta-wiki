Utica Avenue Select Bus Service

project_b46-sbs-utica-ave-2015-09-10

Project Snapshot

Utica Avenue Select Bus Service was a DOT/MTA project to upgrade the B46 Limited to Select Bus Service on the Utica Avenue corridor in Brooklyn, with goals of improving bus service, maintaining traffic flow and curb access, and increasing safety for all users. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0001]

Brooklyn community members identified Utica Avenue in the 2009 BRT Phase II Study as a corridor that could support and benefit from Select Bus Service, and the community ranked the Utica Avenue B46 corridor as one of two corridors most in need of improvements. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0010]

The B46 carried nearly 50,000 passengers every day, was described as the second-busiest bus route, connected to 30 bus routes and the 3, 4, A, and C subway lines, and had the B35-B46 transfer described as the most frequently used bus-to-bus transfer in the five boroughs. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0012]

Existing Conditions And Early Treatments

DOT began corridor improvements in August 2014, including 1.3 miles of offset and curbside bus lanes on Utica Avenue between St. Johns Place and Church Avenue, selected left-turn bays and bans, and new loading zones. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p003_c0002]

In the bus-lane sections, B46 Limited and local bus travel times decreased by 8-15 percent, and vehicle travel times decreased by 20-25 percent during AM and PM peak periods. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p003_c0002]

March 2015 materials described the corridor as having among the highest rates of motor-vehicle-crash injuries in Brooklyn, with 18 fatalities and 250 severe injuries between 2009 and 2013 and 60 percent of vehicles traveling above the then-30 mph speed limit. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0002] [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0003]

Community feedback highlighted crowding, bus bunching and reliability, Williamsburg service levels, lack of real-time information, double parking, congestion, blocked bus lanes, pedestrian safety at complex intersections, dangerous driving, speeding, and lighting at night. [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0002] [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0003] [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0004] [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0005] [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0006] [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p006_c0007]

Proposed SBS Package

The proposed upgrade included off-board fare collection, dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, service changes to improve reliability, and capital improvements at SBS stations. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p007_c0002] [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p007_c0003]

The station package included real-time passenger information, bus bulbs, pedestrian safety enhancements at complex intersections, and new shelters, plantings, and benches. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p007_c0003]

The April 2015 CB8 presentation described the dedicated-bus-lane element as an extension south of Church Avenue. [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p008_c0003]

For off-board fare payment, riders would pay with MetroCard or coins before boarding and receive a ticket, while NYCT would conduct random fare-payment inspections. [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p007_c0003] [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p007_c0005]

Draft Service Plan

Before the SBS proposal, the B46 LTD generally traveled between Kings Plaza and Williamsburg Bridge Plaza, while the B46 Local generally traveled between Kings Plaza and DeKalb Avenue. [2015_03_11_brt_uticaave_cb4_transportation_committee_presentat#p014_c0002]

The draft service plan maintained high-ridership Limited stops and key transfers, had SBS make limited stops south of Avenue H, extended local service to the full corridor 24 hours per day, and set DeKalb Avenue as the SBS northern terminal to increase reliability and better serve the busiest route segment. [2015_03_11_brt_uticaave_cb4_transportation_committee_presentat#p015_c0002]

The DeKalb terminal decision was tied to the claim that only 9 percent of riders traveled between Broadway and the rest of the route, while Broadway was 24 percent of route miles and had the slowest speeds. [2015_03_11_brt_uticaave_cb4_transportation_committee_presentat#p015_c0002]

The proposed SBS station list included DeKalb Av, Gates Av, Halsey St, Fulton St, Eastern Pkwy, Empire Blvd, Winthrop St, Church Av, Ave D, Ave H, Flatlands Av, Ave N, and Ave U (Kings Plaza). [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p016_c0002]

Outreach And Next Steps

The November 2014 workshop included a Select Bus Service presentation, a travel discussion, and a mapping exercise about transit, traffic, and safety concerns at specific intersections. [2014_11_brt_utica_workshopsummary#p002_c0002]

By mid-March 2015, outreach materials listed Community Advisory Committee Meeting #1 in September 2014, Public Workshop #1 in November 2014, a CB8 Transportation Committee presentation in November 2014, Community Advisory Committee Meeting #2 in February 2015, and CB3 and CB4 Transportation Committee presentations in March 2015. [2015_03_17_brt_uticaave_cb1_transportation_committee_presentat#p005_c0005]

By the April 9, 2015 CB8 presentation, the outreach list included a March 2015 open house and March-April 2015 community board meetings. [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p001_c0002] [2015_04_09_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p004_c0005]

Spring 2015 next steps were to finalize the B46 SBS and B46 Local service plan, develop conceptual street design changes south of Church Avenue and north of St. Johns Place if needed, present to other community boards, and hold a public open house. [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p022_c0002] [2015_03_10_brt_uticaave_cb3_transportation_committee_presentat#p022_c0003]

Scope And Identity

This page is about the source-time B46 Select Bus Service project on Utica Avenue, centered on the September 10, 2015 Community Board 8 update and the 2016 follow-up materials. The 2015 deck presents a "B46 Select Bus Service Update" for Community Board 8 and names New York City Transit and NYC DOT on the cover, so this record should stay focused on that Utica Avenue SBS implementation package rather than the broader all-source B46 SBS route identity. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p001_c0001] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p001_c0002] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p001_c0004] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p001_c0006]

The project's corridor context was the Utica Avenue B46 corridor, which the staged decks tie to the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study. The 2015 deck also describes the corridor as Brooklyn's busiest bus route, a connection to 3 4 A C trains and 30 bus routes, and a Vision Zero Priority Corridor. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p002_c0002] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p002_c0003] By the 2016 service plan, B46 SBS was described as running between Avenue U (Kings Plaza) and DeKalb Avenue, while the B46 Local would run between Avenue U (Kings Plaza) and Williamsburg Bridge Plaza. [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p008_c0003] The Winter/Spring 2016 newsletter says the B46 Local would be extended to Williamsburg Bridge Plaza 24 hours per day, continue local stops from DeKalb Av to Kings Plaza, and that B46 SBS would make SBS stops from DeKalb Av to Kings Plaza and would not make local stops south of Avenue H. [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0004] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0005] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0006] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p004_c0007]

Timeline And Elements

At the September 2015 source date, the street program was partly implemented and partly in progress. St. Johns Place to Church Avenue is listed as "Implemented Summer 2014"; the associated slide reports added bus lanes, left-turn bays, and turn restrictions, plus 8% to 15% lower B46 Limited and local travel times and 20% to 25% lower peak-direction vehicle travel times. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p005_c0002] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p005_c0003] Church Avenue to Fillmore Avenue was "Implementing August-September 2015" in the CB8 deck, and later 2016 committee materials describe that segment as adding bus lanes, left-turn bays, turn restrictions, pedestrian islands, truck loading zones, curb-regulation updates, and transit signal priority. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p005_c0004] [brt_uticaave_cb17_transportation_committee_2016#p005_c0004] [brt_uticaave_cb17_transportation_committee_2016#p005_c0005]

The same 2015 deck frames Bergen Street to Atlantic Avenue as a narrower northbound issue: significant queuing approaching Atlantic Avenue, B46 Limited buses taking 3-4 minutes on average between Bergen Street and Atlantic Avenue during peak periods, and 6,000-7,000 daily riders slowed from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p006_c0002] The proposed response was three northbound blocks, 7 a.m. to 7 p.m. to preserve overnight parking, with implementation listed as fall 2015 or spring 2016 for a northbound curbside bus lane from Bergen Street to Atlantic Avenue. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p007_c0002] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p009_c0004] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p009_c0005]

By February and April 2016, the source-time status shifts toward launch. The CB17 and CB9 committee decks say "SBS Service Launches Spring 2016." [brt_uticaave_cb17_transportation_committee_2016#p008_c0003] [brt_uticaave_cb9_transportation_committee_2016#p008_c0003] The April 4 public-hearing deck says "SBS Service Launches Late Spring 2016" and lists launch-package elements: resurfacing Utica Avenue from Clarkson Avenue to Kings Highway, bus-stop adjustments, fare machines, Bus Time wayfinding signs, and shelters or benches where feasible. [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p010_c0002] [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p010_c0003] The newsletter uses the public-facing phrasing "B46 Limited to become B46 Select Bus Service (SBS) Spring 2016" and "SERVICE STARTS 2016." [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p001_c0004] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p003_c0015]

The service package combined bus priority, fare collection, information, and capital work. The newsletter separates completed fall 2015 dedicated bus lanes from bus priority at lights, spring 2016 faster fare payment, and 2017-2018 bus bulbs. [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0003] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0004] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0006] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0008] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0013] [brt_uticaave_winter_spring_newsletter_2016#p002_c0014] The CB18 deck gives the fare-payment mechanics: riders pay with MetroCard or coins before boarding and receive a ticket, with MTA NYCT random fare payment inspections. [brt_uticaave_cb18_transportation_committee_2016#p028_c0003] [brt_uticaave_cb18_transportation_committee_2016#p028_c0005] Longer-term committee slides identify bus bulbs at SBS stops, curb extensions, pedestrian islands, high-visibility crosswalks, and resurfacing south of Flatlands Avenue as a 2017-2018 capital phase. [brt_uticaave_cb17_transportation_committee_2016#p009_c0002] [brt_uticaave_cb17_transportation_committee_2016#p009_c0003]

This record sits between earlier Utica bus-priority work and the broader B46 SBS route page. The staged evidence here connects the 2014 and 2015 bus-lane/street-marking work to a spring or late-spring 2016 SBS launch package and later planned capital improvements; it should not be read as the complete later route history.

Caveats And Open Questions

The six-source target set supports spring, late spring, and "SERVICE STARTS 2016" launch language, but not an actual launch date or current service status. Ridership background varies by source: the 2015 CB8 deck says nearly 50,000 passengers per day, while the April 2016 public-hearing deck says nearly 46,000. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p002_c0003] [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p003_c0003]

The Bergen Street to Atlantic Avenue curbside bus lane is listed as fall 2015 or spring 2016 in the CB8 deck; this target source set does not independently confirm completion. [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p009_c0004] [2015_09_10_brt_uticaave_cb8_presentation#p009_c0005] The April 2016 public-hearing deck says bus lane hours and left-turn restrictions were reduced from 24-hours to 7am-7pm only, so any treatment summary should preserve that qualification. [brt_cb17_uticaave_apr2016#p013_c0007] The 2017-2018 bus bulbs and related capital work are planned/source-time capital phases in these materials unless later staged source evidence is cited.
