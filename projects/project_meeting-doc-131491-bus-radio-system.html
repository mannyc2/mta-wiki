Purpose And Scope

The Bus Radio System Replacement replaced the NYCT and MTA Bus analog bus radio system with a fully integrated digital system for voice and data communications for operators and field supervisors. [meeting_doc_131491#p029_c0003]

The January 2024 project update described the legacy Department of Buses radio communications system as more than 30 years old and serving more than 6,000 buses and non-revenue vehicles. [meeting_doc_131491#p014_c0005]

The system connected the fleet, the Bus Command Center, and base radio sites across the Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens, Staten Island, New Jersey, and Yonkers for MTA bus service-area coverage. [meeting_doc_131491#p014_c0005]

The IEC scope listed a Design-Build contract awarded to Parsons Transportation Group for $202 million, with notice to proceed in March 2016 and an original substantial-completion date of January 2021. [meeting_doc_131491#p029_c0003]

The same scope included base stations, one mobile base station at Yukon depot, approximately 6,000 buses, non-revenue vehicles, 1,250 portable radios, Bus Command Center dispatch consoles, radio hardware/software, and integration and testing of the BRS network. [meeting_doc_131491#p029_c0003]

Progress And Risks

In January 2024, the project status table forecast August 2024 substantial completion, increased the forecast budget from $330 million to $355 million, and described the project as approximately 75% complete. [meeting_doc_131491#p014_c0007]

At that same update, the Bus Command Center was operating successfully, the radio system was stable and in full operation, and more than half of the existing bus fleet had the new radio system installed: 3,250 buses at about 350 buses per month. [meeting_doc_131491#p014_c0009]

The January 2024 IEC schedule page said the contractor forecast August 2024 substantial completion, while the IEC forecast no earlier than December 2024 because of full system load and stress testing, maintenance tickets, and PowerTrunk Radio interference with AT&T Band 14 transmission. [meeting_doc_131491#p032_c0003]

The January 2024 IEC budget page said the current total budget was $315 million, the project EAC was $355 million, and the IEC forecasted a $360 million EAC due to potential time-impact delays and remaining-work risk. [meeting_doc_131491#p032_c0005]

The January 2024 risk page identified bus radio coverage and interference issues plus required full system load and stress testing as schedule risks, while noting mitigation through a mobile base station at Yukon depot, PowerTrunk/AT&T interference fixes, and ongoing monitoring as buses were added to the system. [meeting_doc_131491#p033_c0003]

By February 2024, the C&D update said the project had experienced several challenges but was over 75% complete and still forecasting substantial completion for August 2024. [meeting_doc_133356#p006_c0005]

The February 2024 IEC text said the project team forecast remained August 2024, but the IEC forecast substantial completion no earlier than December 2024 because outstanding technical issues still had to be resolved and full system load and stress testing remained a risk. [meeting_doc_133356#p007_c0001]

Completion And Operating Value

In January 2025, the update said the new bus radio system was declared substantially complete on December 31, 2024 and provided approximately 6,000 buses and non-revenue vehicles with a new radio communication system replacing one that was more than 30 years old. [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0006]

The January 2025 completion update said the system provided reliable real-time voice and data communications, enhancing data collection and overall bus performance. [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0006]

The January 2025 update listed 35 base radio stations and a Bus Command Center outfitted with hardware and software for computer-aided dispatch of the bus fleet and non-revenue bus support vehicles. [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0007]

The January 2025 status table showed the project more than 96% complete, with a previous budget of $330 million and a forecast budget of $356 million. [meeting_doc_163236#p011_c0009]

A February 2025 update said the long-awaited upgrade was delivered at the end of 2024 after contractor performance challenges, equipping nearly 6,000 buses with modern voice and digital communication systems integrated with the MTA's Bus Command Center. [meeting_doc_164901#p006_c0009]

The February 2025 update said the upgrade included new hardware and software for computerized dispatch and 35 base stations across New York City, parts of New Jersey, and Yonkers for bus-operations coverage. [meeting_doc_164901#p006_c0009]

Related People And Operations

A Department of Buses profile said Carl Persuad was involved in upgrading the Bus Radio System alongside prognostic maintenance analytics and zero-emissions fleet work. [meeting_doc_133281#p007_c0006]
