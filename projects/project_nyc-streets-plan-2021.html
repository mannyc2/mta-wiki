Role In Bus Priority

NYC DOT describes the NYC Streets Plan as a five-year transportation plan to improve the safety, accessibility, and quality of city streets, and the same source says Local Law 195 of 2019 directs DOT to issue and implement a transportation master plan every five years with annual progress updates. [streets_plan#p001_b0001]

IBO frames the 2021 Streets Plan as DOT's post-pandemic bus-speed strategy and says it was developed to comply with Local Law 195 targets for protected bus lanes, transit signal priority intersections, protected bike lanes, and other street outcomes. [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p007_c0007]

The Comptroller report says the Streets Plan mandated 20 miles of protected bus lanes in 2022, 30 miles each year through 2026, and TSP at thousands of intersections by 2026. [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0003]

Bus Lane Mandate And Progress

IBO says Local Law 195 required DOT to build at least 150 miles of protected bus lanes in the first five-year plan, defining those lanes as physically protected by barriers or enforced with cameras. [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p010_c0009]

IBO says DOT built 9.6 miles of protected bus lanes in the first two calendar years of the plan and would have needed 46.8 miles in each of 2024, 2025, and 2026 to meet the 150-mile goal. [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p010_c0009] [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p010_c0010] [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p011_c0010]

The Comptroller report separately says NYC DOT built only 23.1 miles of protected bus lanes since 2022 against an 80-mile target through 2024, and it evaluates dedicated bus lanes as failing the Streets Plan target. [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0006]

As of 2024, the Comptroller report counted about 163 total bus-lane miles citywide, including 144 miles of curbside or offset lanes, 6.8 miles of center-running lanes, and 5.7 miles of busways. [behind_schedule_2025#p025_c0006]

Transit Signal Priority

IBO says the Streets Plan requires DOT to implement TSP at 750 intersections in the first year and 1,000 intersections per year afterward where feasible, and its table records 781 completed intersections in 2022 and 754 in 2023. [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p011_c0004] [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p011_c0006]

The Comptroller report says DOT had installed TSP at over 2,000 intersections by the end of 2023 and was on track for the Streets Plan TSP goal. [behind_schedule_2025#p024_c0001] [behind_schedule_2025#p027_c0007] [behind_schedule_2025#p028_c0001]

The same report says TSP can reduce bus delay at traffic lights by lengthening green signals or reducing red signals as buses approach, and it cites a 2017 evaluation finding an average 18% travel-time reduction on five SBS routes where TSP was active. [behind_schedule_2025#p027_c0006]

Evaluation Context

The Comptroller report credits SBS, Fast Forward, Better Buses, and NYC Streets Plan initiatives with tangible improvements including four dedicated busways, widespread TSP installation, and bus-network redesign starts. [behind_schedule_2025#p004_c0005]

The same report says those tools were not reaching their full potential because cancelled and downscaled projects missed targets, bus performance had stagnated, and rider improvements remained undelivered. [behind_schedule_2025#p004_c0006]

IBO similarly says citywide bus speeds in December 2024 stayed largely consistent with January 2019 despite several speed-focused initiatives, and it identifies traffic congestion, lack of dedicated bus lanes, and insufficient traffic enforcement as possible explanations. [speeding_up_slowly_2025#p003_c0008]

The Comptroller report says DOT's bus goals include protected bus-lane delivery and TSP installation, but it also says public reporting is missing project impacts on bus performance and tangible bus-performance standards. [behind_schedule_2025#p037_c0002]
