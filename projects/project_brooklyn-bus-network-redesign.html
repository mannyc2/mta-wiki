Overview

The Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign is NYCT's borough-scale effort to take a fresh look at Brooklyn's legacy bus network after decades of growth, changed travel patterns, falling ridership, slower buses, and declining reliability. The Draft Plan frames the redesign around five customer priorities: frequent service, faster travel, reliable service, better connections, and an easy ride. It describes the December 1, 2022 Draft Plan as a proposed network of routes, stops, and generalized frequencies reimagined for current and future Brooklyn bus customers [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p001_c0003] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p005_c0002] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p005_c0003].

The project timeline began with a public launch in October 2019, including surveys, ten open houses, and on-street engagement. MTA released an Existing Conditions Report in January 2020, paused the redesign in March 2020 because of COVID-19, announced the restart of bus network redesigns in August 2021, and then moved toward the December 2022 Draft Plan and winter/spring 2023 outreach [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p006_c0004] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p006_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p006_c0007] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p006_c0008] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p005_c0006].

After the Draft Plan release, MTA planned 18 virtual public workshops, one for each Brooklyn community district; later meeting materials record 15 in-person public events in spring 2023 and say that comments from hundreds of New Yorkers at the January-March 2023 workshops would inform the Proposed Final Plan. A January 2024 update says Operations Planning was preparing that Proposed Final Plan based on nearly 8,000 comments received in late 2022 and 2023; later 2025 corridor materials expected the Proposed Final Plan in late 2025, while an IBO report described Brooklyn as still in planning and said MTA would unveil a final plan in the second half of 2025 [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p007_c0003] [meeting_doc_111811#p020_c0003] [meeting_doc_111811#p020_c0006] [meeting_doc_131541#p015_c0011] [linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_jan2025#p007_c0005] [behind_schedule_2025#p028_c0004] [behind_schedule_2025#p029_c0002].

Proposed Network

The Draft Plan proposed a redesigned local bus network with 69 routes: 54 Local, 3 Rush, 7 Limited, and 5 Crosstown/SBS routes. It also described extensive route change types, including 9 new routes, 15 minor routing changes, 7 major routing changes, 7 route extensions, 8 shortened routes, 3 routes both extended and shortened, 4 branch changes, 14 routes with stop changes only, and 2 routes with no routing or stop changes. NYCT board materials summarized the plan as 69 local routes plus 19 express routes, for 88 routes total [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0003] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0004] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0006] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0007] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0008] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0009] [meeting_doc_102836#p005_c0004].

The redesign's simplification strategy was not only route renaming. The Draft Plan says frequency improvements were focused on key corridors and historically disadvantaged neighborhoods, especially areas far from the subway; it also describes reinvesting resources from removed redundancies, speeding up buses, and consolidating nearby parallel routes onto one street. The proposed all-day frequent network would rise to 31 routes, with access within a quarter-mile increasing from 77 percent to 87 percent of Brooklyn residents, although the same block notes that certain streets would no longer have all-day frequent service because of Draft Plan routing changes [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p017_c0008] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p045_c0002].

Speed And Reliability

The Draft Plan names three main travel-time strategies: expanding bus priority with NYC DOT, balancing bus stop spacing, and creating faster service patterns such as Rush and Crosstown/SBS routes. It connects stop spacing directly to bus speeds, saying Brooklyn local routes averaged about 830 feet between stops and that removing one stop saves about 20 seconds per trip, with potentially larger savings in peak periods [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p018_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p010_c0002] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p010_c0004].

The plan also proposed broader service-span changes: nine routes with span increases, two with span reductions, and six additional overnight routes compared with the existing network. Those changes included new overnight service on B13, B49, B57, B83, B40 Rush, B53, B55 Crosstown, and B66, alongside discontinuation of overnight service on B48 and B68 [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p049_c0002] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p050_c0002].

Bus Priority Context

The bus-priority relationship is source-backed but should be read as coordination, not automatic implementation. The Draft Plan says MTA was working with NYC DOT to expand bus priority improvements and that the NYC Streets Plan identified Brooklyn corridors where treatments such as bus lanes, busways, queue jumps, transit signal priority, pedestrian safety elements, and bus stop accessibility upgrades could support all-day bus service. It says 49 corridors were studied, including 17 top-ranked corridors selected using demand, performance, feasibility, network role, demographics, and equity metrics [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0003] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0004] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0002] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0008].

Specific bus-priority examples tied to the redesign include 2023 DOT work on Flatbush Avenue, the B82 SBS South Brooklyn corridor, and Livingston Street. The Draft Plan describes potential Flatbush treatments including bus lanes, a busway, transit-signal priority, queue jumps, and bus stop improvements; B82 SBS capital work including bus bulbs, bus pads, median bus stops, raised crosswalks, and related pedestrian improvements; and Livingston Street options such as physically separated bus lanes, bus boarding islands, or busways. Later Flatbush Avenue materials say MTA and DOT were evaluating routing in conjunction with the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign to improve travel times and reduce congestion and bus delays [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p022_c0003] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p022_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p022_c0007] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p022_c0009] [flatbush_ave_cab3_jul2025#p021_c0002].

Caveats

The checked repo evidence does not confirm a released or implemented Brooklyn Proposed Final Plan; sources place it in preparation, expected in 2025, or still in planning [meeting_doc_131541#p015_c0011] [linden_blvd_fountain_ave_conduit_ave_bkcb5_jan2025#p007_c0005] [behind_schedule_2025#p028_c0004]. The 69-route count has scope nuance: the draft says it includes five Q routes in Brooklyn Division and excludes Q58, while showing some Queens-redesign routes for context [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0010] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p038_c0011]. Bus-priority links are strongest as coordination and candidate/project context; not every listed priority corridor is evidenced here as built or funded through the redesign [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p019_c0005] [brooklyn_bus_network_draft_plan_without_route_profiles#p020_c0004].
