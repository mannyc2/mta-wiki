Second Avenue Subway Phase 2

project_annual-2021-second-ave-subway-phase2

Overview

Second Avenue Subway Phase 2 is the northward extension of Q service in Manhattan to 125th Street and Lexington Avenue. The cited 2023 EDPL record says MTA needed property interests for Contracts 2, 3, and 4 to construct and operate the new 106th, 116th, and 125th Street stations, entrances, and ancillary facilities. [meeting_doc_112036#p064_c0007] [meeting_doc_112036#p061_c0010]

Contract 2 is the tunnel-and-station package from about 115th Street and Second Avenue north along Second Avenue and west along 125th Street, including conversion of an existing tunnel into the 116th Street station, twin bored tunnels, mined caverns/tunnels for 125th Street, and off-street work sites along the route. [meeting_doc_112036#p064_c0008]

Funding And Schedule

In the 2021 annual narrative, MTA reported $221 million committed as of December 2021, initial funding of $1.735 billion, and $4.555 billion added through the 2020-2024 capital program, bringing the Phase 2 budget to $6.29 billion. The next source block says FTA approved the project to enter New Starts Engineering on January 6, 2022. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p087_c0007] [annual_report_narrative_2021#p088_c0001]

A 2024 project deck put anticipated cost at $6.968B, with $3.405B as the FTA committed grant amount and $3.563B as MTA local share. The same deck says the FFGA was signed in November 2023, with an anticipated September 2032 revenue service date, and that Contract 1 for utility relocation/building remediation was awarded to CAC Industries in December 2023. [meeting_doc_146956#p025_c0003] [meeting_doc_146956#p024_c0003]

Status Notes

Mid-2024 materials described a funding risk from the CBDTP pause: MTA said it could not use $15B in CBDTP proceeds, was reprioritizing currently available capital funding, and would continue mission-critical design and real estate acquisition. [meeting_doc_146956#p014_c0006] Later materials said SAS Phase 2 would move forward, listing $3B in MTA local funding and $2B in federal support in the 2020-2024 Capital Plan. [meeting_doc_157976#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_157976#p003_c0007]

Project updates state that Phase 2 was expected to save riders up to 20 minutes; one update said it would serve 100,000 riders per day and had saved $1.3B from Phase 1 lessons, while a later Contract 2 award deck listed 110,000 daily riders. [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0005] [meeting_doc_182066#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_182066#p003_c0003]

Recent procurement/schedule blocks show the project moving into contracting: two Phase 2 contracts were advancing to the Board, with program-management incentives/penalties and a design contract revised to design-bid-build for final fit-out/systems; the Contract 2 schedule block lists revenue service in September 2032, Contract 2 tunnel/structure work from September 2025 to August 2029, and Contract 4 systems/finishes through September 2032. [meeting_doc_170976#p005_c0011] [meeting_doc_182066#p011_c0001] [meeting_doc_182066#p011_c0002]

A later property action sought authorization to acquire 124-130 W 125th Street/124 West 125th Street for SAS2 construction. The associated blocks explain that an adjacent lot had already been acquired for Ancillary A, a vertical shaft containing ventilation, riser space, and an emergency stair, and that design progress made Lot 46 necessary for the same infrastructure. [meeting_doc_171381#p043_c0007] [meeting_doc_171381#p043_c0008] [meeting_doc_171381#p043_c0009] [meeting_doc_171381#p043_c0010]

Caveats

Status is document-time sensitive: mid-2024 materials describe CBDTP-pause funding risk, while later materials describe the project moving forward and an on-schedule September 2032 revenue-service target. [meeting_doc_146956#p014_c0006] [meeting_doc_157976#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_182066#p011_c0001]

The generated frontmatter includes completion_date: "$5 billion", but the cited source treats $5 billion as an impact/funding figure, not a completion date. [meeting_doc_157976#p003_c0004] [meeting_doc_157976#p003_c0007]

Ridership figures differ by source date: 100,000 riders per day in one update, and 110,000 daily riders in the Contract 2 award deck. [meeting_doc_151861#p007_c0005] [meeting_doc_182066#p003_c0002]
