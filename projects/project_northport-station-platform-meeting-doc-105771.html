Northport Station Platform Replacement

project_northport-station-platform-meeting-doc-105771

Overview

The Northport Station Platform Replacement is described as an LIRR project on the Port Jefferson Branch to replace the high-level platform and make related station improvements, with the February 2023 source placing Northport Station 40 miles east of Penn Station and the June 2023 source placing it in East Northport, Suffolk County. [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0005] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0004]

The sources describe Northport as an 1873 station that serves about 2,300 weekday customers or passenger trips, and the June source says its single platform was over 30 years old and beyond its useful life. [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0005] [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0006] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0005]

Scope

The February source says crews were replacing the deteriorating platform with a new 12-car high-level concrete platform, ADA ramp, aluminum railings, platform shelters, boiler house, and Automatic Snow and Ice Melting System. [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0007]

The June source adds that the work was intended to bolster ADA accessibility, improve customer experience, repair station and platform elements, and provide a heated platform, automated boiler-room controls, updated ADA ramp, shelters, lighting, signage, a new plaza area, and other site improvements. [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0004] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0006]

Timeline And Status

In the February 21, 2023 performance metrics document, construction is described as having begun in spring 2022 with an $18.8 million budget, 95% of west-end slabs installed, boiler-house walls complete, the project approximately 25% complete, and substantial completion expected in the third quarter of 2023. [meeting_doc_105771#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0008]

In the June 26, 2023 performance metrics document, Phase 1 is described as substantially complete and open for customer use, while Phase 2 on the east half had commenced in May 2023 with existing slabs demolished and about 25% of new Phase 2 slabs installed. [meeting_doc_113886#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0003] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0005] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0006]

The two sources frame the work as a phased platform replacement that maintained customer boarding during construction rather than closing the full platform. [meeting_doc_105771#p016_c0007] [meeting_doc_113886#p016_c0005]
