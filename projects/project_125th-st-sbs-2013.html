M60 Select Bus Service on 125th Street

project_125th-st-sbs-2013

Context

The 2013 materials present this as Select Bus Service for the M60 on 125th Street, with June updates prepared for Manhattan Community Boards 9, 10, and 11. The corridor already carried Bx15, M60, M100, and M101 service; a 2011 boarding table lists 32,630 weekday bus boardings on 125th Street, with the M60 the largest single route at 9,682 boardings and described as the busiest route [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p003_c0005] [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p003_c0007] [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p003_c0008].

The M60 case was not framed only as an airport-access project. The June materials say half of M60 ridership was on 125th Street and only 11% was to the airport, with later slides showing 49% of M60 boardings and 51% of alightings on 125th Street and stating that most riders were traveling within Manhattan rather than to or from LaGuardia Airport [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p002_c0012] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p003_c0005] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p003_c0007] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p003_c0009].

Proposed Package

The proposed SBS package combined limited stops, off-board fare payment, offset lanes for buses and right turns, and updated parking regulations [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0003] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0004] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0006] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0007] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0008]. Earlier March materials also list transit signal priority and pedestrian safety improvements at key intersections [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p006_c0011] [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p006_c0012].

For stops, the CB10 update says M60 SBS would stop at every subway or rail station on 125th Street plus Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue, while Bx15, M100, and M101 stops would remain unchanged and some bus stops would be lengthened to help buses reach the curb [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p008_c0004] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p008_c0005] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p008_c0006].

The street-design materials show bus lanes from east of Lenox Avenue to Second Avenue, including offset bus lanes, a curbside eastbound bus lane east of Park Avenue, and commercial loading zones [2013_06_05_sbs_125th_cb9#p011_c0005] [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p011_c0005]. The CB10 update also lists AM commercial loading zones and new left-turn restrictions from 125th Street at Frederick Douglass Boulevard and Adam Clayton Powell Boulevard [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p012_c0006] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p012_c0007].

Outreach And Status

The outreach record in the June updates includes summer 2012 consultation with CB9, CB10, and CB11; a September 2012 public workshop; three community advisory committee meetings; public and merchant open houses; spring 2013 community meetings; walking tours; an M15 SBS tour; and a May 2013 town hall [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0006] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0007] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0008] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0009] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0010] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0011] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0012] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0013] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p007_c0014].

As presented in March 2013, the timeline still pointed toward implementation in 2013, with a Community Advisory Committee meeting scheduled for March 18 and an M60 SBS open house in early April [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p024_c0008] [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p024_c0010] [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p024_c0012].

Caveats

The June updates still list "Finalize Parking Regulations" and "Finalize Bus Stop Locations," and the CB11 update says to "Move toward implementation," so this page treats these materials as pre-implementation planning documents rather than proof of completed implementation [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p017_c0005] [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p017_c0006] [2013_06_04_sbs_125th_cb11#p017_c0007].

The CB10 update says changes were made in response to community input, including bus lanes beginning east of Lenox Avenue, commercial-loading-zone hours/locations, and keeping the left turn from 125th Street to St. Nicholas Avenue [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p016_c0003] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p016_c0004] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p016_c0005] [2013_06_12_sbs_125th_cb10#p016_c0006].

Planning Context

This page represents the pre-implementation 2013 planning record for Select Bus Service on 125th Street, as presented to Manhattan Community Board 11 on March 5, 2013. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p001_c0002] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p001_c0003] The corridor was framed as a multi-route bus street served by Bx15, M60, M100, and M101; the 2011 weekday boarding table lists 32,630 total boardings on 125th Street, with M60 the largest single route at 9,682 boardings. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0006] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0009] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p003_c0010]

The M60 case was not presented as airport-only. In the CB11 deck, 49% of M60 boardings and 51% of alightings were on 125th Street, while the airport accounted for 11% of boardings and 10% of alightings; the deck states that most riders were not traveling to or from LaGuardia Airport. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p004_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p004_c0007] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p004_c0010]

Problem Definition

The operating problem was slow, interrupted bus movement. The April workshop source says buses were stopped over 60% of the time, gives eastbound PM peak bus speed as 2.7 mph, and compares that with a 7.7 mph average bus speed in NYC. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0015] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0016] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p001_c0017]

Outreach comments shaped the design constraints: preserve curb access for shoppers and merchants, address double parking by cars and trucks, place SBS stops at major subway and rail connections, continue merchant/vendor outreach, and address issues at 125th Street and Lexington Avenue. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p009_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p009_c0006] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p009_c0007] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p009_c0008] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p009_c0009]

The survey evidence complicated a simple parking-loss narrative. The April workshop source says 752 sidewalk-survey respondents were interviewed, 90% walked or used transit to reach 125th Street, and only 10% used a car or taxi/livery cab. It also says 200 businesses and institutions were interviewed between Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue; 50% did not consider parking a major business concern, most customers parked for less than 30 minutes, most deliveries were completed in less than 30 minutes, and parking rules could be revised to better match those patterns. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0009] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0010] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0017] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0020] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0023] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0026] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p004_c0027]

Proposed Package

The proposed package combined SBS operations with street and curb changes. The SBS feature set included offset bus lanes, off-board fare collection, bus lane enforcement, and transit signal priority; the typical design described offset bus lanes replacing one general travel lane in each direction while maintaining curb access, with curb regulation updates, left-turn bans, and right-turn bays. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0006] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0008] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p002_c0014] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p007_c0002]

The parking proposal aimed for more consistent regulations along 125th Street: unmetered parking would become one-hour metered parking, and the commercial loading zone between Third Avenue and Second Avenue would be replaced by a curbside bus lane. A separate slide listed potential evening one-hour metered parking and morning commercial loading zones under consideration. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p010_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p011_c0002] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p011_c0003]

Stop Pattern And Local Service

The proposed stop pattern kept M60 SBS limited while preserving local service. The April workshop source says M60 SBS would stop at every subway or rail station on 125th Street plus Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue, that local service would be unchanged on Bx15, M100, and M101, and that those routes would continue making local stops along 125th Street. It also says many bus stops would be lengthened to help buses reach the curb. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0003] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0004] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0005] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0006] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0007]

Related Design Items

The traffic analysis did not frame the proposed design as a corridor-wide auto-delay collapse: existing delay was shown as less than 45 seconds at all intersections, and proposed average delay was described as remaining the same along 125th Street except eastbound at Morningside Avenue and westbound at Third Avenue. [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p012_c0005] [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p014_c0002]

Two CB11 items should be treated as related but not identical to the core M60 SBS stop pattern. The same March deck proposed rerouting the M100 in East Harlem to eliminate an unsafe left turn from 125th Street onto Third Avenue and increase service on 125th Street between Third Avenue and First Avenue. It also showed proposed bus bulbs at Lexington Avenue, with the stated purpose of reducing pedestrian congestion and crossing distances. [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p024_c0004] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p024_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p024_c0006] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p025_c0005] [2013_03_sbs_125_st_cb11_slides#p025_c0006]

Status And Caveats

The April workshop source still listed revise design based on community input, finalize curb regulations, finalize bus stop locations, implementation in 2013, continued investigation of pedestrian safety improvements at Lexington Avenue and Amsterdam Avenue, and a late-spring 2013 CAC #4 meeting. This page should therefore be read as a proposed 2013 planning package, not as proof that the later M60 SBS launch or post-implementation outcomes had already occurred.
