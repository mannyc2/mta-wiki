Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service

project_flushing-jamaica-sbs-2014

Overview

Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service was presented on October 7, 2014 as a corridor planning effort with goals to improve bus speed and reliability, connect Downtown Flushing and Jamaica, improve safety for all street users, and maintain community character. [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p001_c0001] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p001_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p003_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p003_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p003_c0004] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p003_c0005]

The 2014 corridor overview split the study geography between Main Street routes Q20A/B and Q44, and Parsons/Kissena routes Q25 and Q34. [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p014_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p014_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p014_c0004] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p014_c0005]

By the May 2015 open house, the project had narrowed into "The Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica SBS," with the Q44 from Bronx Zoo to Jamaica via Main Street proposed for upgrade from Q44 Limited to Q44 Select Bus Service while Q20A/B local service would continue as-is. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p007_c0001] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p007_c0002] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p007_c0003]

Why It Mattered

The 2014 presentation reported weekday ridership of 13,609 on Q20 and 28,689 on Q44 on Main Street, plus 19,324 on Q25 and 7,054 on Q34 on Kissena/Parsons, for total corridor ridership over 68,000 daily riders. [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p016_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p016_c0005] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p016_c0008]

Delay and speed data pointed to a slow corridor: the 2014 presentation said the Q44 LTD spent 29% of observed time in traffic and at red lights and 18% at bus stops, and it summarized overall bus speeds as under 10 mph. [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p018_c0001] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p018_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p018_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p020_c0006]

The 2015 open house repeated that the Q44 operated at less than 10 mph and identified slow Q44 speeds during both morning and evening commute times, particularly on Sutphin Boulevard. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p008_c0003] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p008_c0006]

Safety was also part of the project rationale, with the 2015 open house identifying Main Street from Northern Boulevard to Queens Boulevard as one of Queens' ten most dangerous roadways and reporting 32 pedestrians killed or severely injured from 2008 to 2012. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0001] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0003] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0004]

Proposed Design Pieces

The 2015 proposal included bus lane areas under development, while noting that in areas without bus lanes the Q44 SBS would operate in mixed traffic along the same route as the Q44 Limited. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p012_c0001] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p012_c0002] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p012_c0003]

In Flushing, the open house showed offset bus lanes at Main Street and Maple Avenue with existing curbside parking/loading, one general traffic lane in each direction, curbside bus stops, and banned turns. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0001] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0002] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0003] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0004] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0005] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0006] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p013_c0007]

In Jamaica, the open house showed peak-period curbside bus lanes with bus stops at the curb and general traffic lanes in each direction. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p015_c0001] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p015_c0004] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p015_c0005] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p015_c0006]

The 2015 safety package listed priority intersections on the Q44 SBS route and paired them with proposed Vision Zero treatments including left-turn restrictions, pedestrian safety islands, and bus bulbs. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0005] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0006] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p011_c0007]

Timeline And Follow-Through

The 2014 timeline put data collection and analysis in summer/fall 2014, corridor selection and concept design in winter 2014/2015, corridor plan development in 2015, and final design, implementation planning, construction phasing, and SBS launch in a later final-design step. [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p025_c0006] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p025_c0007] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p026_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p026_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p027_c0002] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p027_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p028_c0003] [2014_10_07_brt_flushingjamaica_pw1_presentation#p028_c0004]

The 2015 outreach timeline lists public workshop dates in September/October 2014, a second public workshop on January 22, 2015, a Flushing open house on May 27, 2015, and a Jamaica open house on May 28, 2015. [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0011] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0012] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0013] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0014] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0017] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0018] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0019] [2015_05_brt_flushing_jamaica_presentation_openhouse#p004_c0020]

A later SBS route index states that Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service began on November 29, 2015 on the Q44 in the Bronx and Queens and was expected to benefit 42,000 daily riders of the Q44 corridor. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001]
