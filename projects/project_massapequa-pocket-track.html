Massapequa Pocket Track

project_massapequa-pocket-track

Summary

Massapequa Pocket Track appears in MTA Construction & Development's November 2022 and November 2023 LIRR ESA Support Projects updates as a Massapequa Pocket Track project in "Beneficial Use" with a $19.6 million project budget. [meeting_doc_100321#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_100321#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0002]

Both updates give the project its own slide labeled "MASSAPEQUA POCKET TRACK" and "Beneficial Use Achieved." [meeting_doc_100321#p005_c0001] [meeting_doc_100321#p005_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p005_c0001] [meeting_doc_127536#p005_c0002]

The milestone sequence is stable across the two updates: signal system cutover in March 2020, pocket track in-service commissioning in June 2021, and closeout in November 2021, all marked actual. [meeting_doc_100321#p005_c0003] [meeting_doc_127536#p005_c0003]

The MTA 2021 annual report describes the project as a completed new 12-car stub-ended electrified and interlocked pocket track east of Massapequa Station, and identifies it as an East Side Access Readiness Project. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p078_c0004] [annual_report_narrative_2021#p079_c0001]

The annual report says the new pocket track included three interlocked turnouts and switches providing crossover access to each main line track, about 1,700 feet of new track, and a new 75-foot bridge over Unqua Creek sharing the existing Unqua Creek Bridge abutments. [annual_report_narrative_2021#p079_c0001]

The source deck image captioning shows the Massapequa Pocket Track as a curved railway track with overhead power lines and a wooded hillside, but the substantive project facts in these pages are the budget, beneficial-use status, and milestone dates. [meeting_doc_100321#p005_c0004] [meeting_doc_127536#p005_c0004] [meeting_doc_100321#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_100321#p005_c0003] [meeting_doc_127536#p003_c0002] [meeting_doc_127536#p005_c0003]
