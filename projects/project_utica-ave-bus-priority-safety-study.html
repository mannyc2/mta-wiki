Utica Avenue Bus Priority and Safety Study

project_utica-ave-bus-priority-safety-study

Scope

The Utica Avenue Bus Priority and Safety Study focused on Utica Avenue from St. Johns Place to Church Avenue, with key intersections at Eastern Parkway, Empire/Lefferts, and Church Avenue, and with goals to improve pedestrian and driver safety, improve bus travel times, and maintain parking and curb access. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p002_c0004]

Earlier materials framed the work around parking and loading requests, B46 bus issues, safety issues, a proposed loading and bus-lane improvement, and a broader Utica Avenue corridor study. [2012_01_24_sbs_utica_merchants#p002_c0003]

Needs

The transit case centered on limited subway access, the B46 as the second-busiest bus route in NYC, more than 49,000 bus passengers per weekday, an average speed of 6.8 mph below the Brooklyn average, and congestion-related slow buses and bunching. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p003_c0002]

The safety case for Carroll Street to Church Avenue cited 63% of vehicles exceeding the 30 mph speed limit, 766 crashes, 41 severe injuries, 2 fatalities, and one of Brooklyn's highest motor-vehicle injury rates. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p004_c0003]

Community feedback emphasized the B46's local importance, pedestrian safety at Eastern Parkway, East New York Avenue, and Church Avenue, risky driving, parking supply for local businesses, enforcement, coordinated signal timing, and avoiding traffic-flow impacts. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p006_c0003]

Proposed Changes

Near Eastern Parkway, the proposed plan relocated the bus lane to the southbound approach, provided more parking between President and Carroll Streets, reduced bus delays on the southbound approach to Eastern Parkway, and created a net gain of 19 peak-period parking spaces. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p009_c0004]

For the Carroll-to-Church segment, proposed solutions converted one travel lane to an offset bus lane, added left-turn bays at some intersections, prohibited left turns elsewhere, implemented Transit Signal Priority, and optimized corridor signal timings. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p011_c0005]

At Church Avenue, the deck described the location as the city's busiest single-route bus-to-bus transfer and a very high crash location, with a proposal for offset bus lanes, bus bulbs, left-turn prohibition, and high traffic volumes. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p012_c0006]

Signal changes included optimizing offsets, increasing the signal cycle from 60 seconds to 90-120 seconds, increasing green time for Utica Avenue while maintaining safe pedestrian crossing times, matching cycles at Church, Linden, and Eastern Parkway, and implementing Transit Signal Priority from Kings Highway to Broadway with NYCT. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p017_c0003]

Analysis And Timing

Traffic analysis measured AM, midday, and PM peak conditions at all intersections, built a Synchro network, included traffic-lane and signal-timing changes, and found that in the PM peak 10 intersections improved, 7 were neutral, and 1 declined slightly from LOS B to LOS C. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p018_c0003]

Projected benefits included faster and more reliable B46 service, groundwork for potential future Select Bus Service, reduced off-peak speeding from bus lanes, pedestrian-safety improvements from turn restrictions and bays, acceptable traffic level of service, 19 added spaces near Eastern Parkway, and 22 removed spaces along the southern corridor. [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p019_c0004] [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p019_c0006] [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p019_c0008] [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p019_c0010]

Outreach cited in the 2012 update included a stakeholder group meeting on September 15, 2011, a merchants and Council Member Mealy staff meeting on January 24, 2012, and a Council Member Mealy Community Advisory Committee meeting on February 4, 2012. [2012_04_26_sbs_utica#p006_c0003]

The study was presented to Brooklyn CB 8 on September 12, 2013, Brooklyn CB 17 on September 19, 2013, and Brooklyn CB 9 on September 24, 2013. [2013_09_12_sbs_utica_cb8#p001_c0004] [2013_09_19_sbs_utica_cb17#p001_c0004] [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p001_c0004]

The September 12 deck listed next steps as presenting proposed changes to community boards, finalizing an implementation plan and schedule potentially for Fall 2013, and developing a bus-bulb capital project if desired. [2013_09_12_sbs_utica_cb8#p017_c0003]

The September 19 and September 24 decks updated next steps to presenting proposed changes for feedback and finalizing an implementation plan and schedule proposed for Spring 2014. [2013_09_19_sbs_utica_cb17#p020_c0003] [2013_09_24_sbs_utica_cb9#p020_c0003]
