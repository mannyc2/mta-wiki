ABLE is the MTA/NYCT bus-lane camera enforcement program: the source material describes it as automating violation notices for vehicles observed obstructing bus lanes, and frames that enforcement as part of making bus lanes and Select Bus Service work. The April 2024 procurement record is unusually concrete for a meeting-doc source: it sought Board ratification of contracts with Hayden AI and Seon USA Corp for purchase, installation, operation, and maintenance of up to 1,023 Hayden systems and up to 1,000 Seon systems. [meeting_doc_138336#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_138336#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_140411#p004_c0011]

The same procurement record also documents the transition from ABLE toward the broader Automated Camera Enforcement, or ACE, scope. After legislative approval to ticket vehicles obstructing bus stops and double-parked vehicles, the Department of Buses updated the scope and renamed the project ACE, with contract modifications still expected to return for later ratification. A later financial plan flags a practical caveat: lower violation-penalty receipts were attributed to slower than expected installation of exterior bus cameras. [meeting_doc_138336#p002_c0006] [meeting_doc_157946#p017_c0004]
