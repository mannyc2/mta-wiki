Service Shape

The South Fork Commuter Connection is described as a coordinated weekday train-and-shuttle service between Speonk and Montauk. In the morning, customers could park west of the Shinnecock Canal, ride east to Southampton, Bridgehampton, or East Hampton, then transfer to free shuttle buses serving nearby employment centers; the fare cited in the minutes was $3.25. [meeting_doc_113891#p006_c0003]

2023 Expansion

The May 22, 2023 committee minutes frame the issue as restoring summer-Friday service: Rob Free and local leaders announced that South Fork Commuter Connection summer-Friday service would run for the first time since the service's 2019 debut. The same paragraph says Friday trains that previously would have been suspended from Memorial Day through Labor Day would instead operate year-round beginning "this Friday." [meeting_doc_113891#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_113891#p006_c0003]

The source also makes the service constraint explicit: LIRR pointed to finite equipment and single-track operations between Patchogue and Montauk as limits on how much service could be added east of Speonk. [meeting_doc_113891#p006_c0003]

Ridership Signal

In the 2024 Annual Ridership Report, LIRR said South Fork Commuter Connection ridership on the Montauk Branch was up about 20% from 2023, and described 2023 ridership as more than 50% higher than pre-COVID ridership. [meeting_doc_170826#p004_c0001] The same report lists Montauk Branch annual ridership at 1,904,464 customers in 2024, up 8.0% from 1,762,578 in 2023. [meeting_doc_170826#p008_c0004]
