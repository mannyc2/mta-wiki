Scope Note

This page treats M60 Select Bus Service as the implementation project: the M60 SBS service package connecting Manhattan and Queens, with 125th Street as a key Manhattan segment and Astoria Boulevard/LaGuardia as the Queens side. It should stay distinct from the route identity page route_125th-laguardia-sbs and from the broader 125th Street planning page project_m60-sbs-125th-street-2013. The June 2013 CB1 source called the project "Select Bus Service on the M60" and scoped it to "Manhattan and Queens"; its route map described the line starting at 125th Street, crossing the RFK Bridge into Queens, continuing along Astoria Boulevard, and serving LaGuardia terminals. [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0003] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0004] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0004]

Planning And Launch

At the June 6, 2013 Community Board 1 briefing, the project was still pre-implementation: the listed next steps were to "Finalize Bus Stop Locations" and "Move toward implementation." [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p001_c0002] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p001_c0003] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p008_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p008_c0007] The proposed package included limited stops, off-board fare payment marked "Manhattan only," offset lanes for buses and right turns, and updated parking regulations. [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0007] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0008] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0009] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0010]

The service design linked the 125th Street stop pattern to the full airport route: M60 SBS stops were described at major intersections in Astoria and East Elmhurst, at every subway/rail station on 125 th St plus Amsterdam Av and Second Av, with some bus stops lengthened to help buses reach the curb. [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0006] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0007]

A 2014 flyer moved the project into launch: "Sunday, May 25, 2014 M60 Select Bus Service is coming!" and street work was to begin in early May with bus lane painting, parking regulation updates, and fare-machine installation. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0002] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0002] The flyer kept the local-service distinction clear: Bx15, M100, and M101 would make all local stops on 125th St, and Q19 would make all local stops on Astoria Blvd. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0004]

2014 Implementation

By January 2015, CB11 materials labeled the work "2014 Implementation" and stated that service launched on May 25, 2014. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p002_c0002] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p008_c0002] Customer Ambassadors staffed SBS stops for two weeks during the launch period to explain fare payment and stop changes. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p008_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p008_c0004]

Reported installed elements included 39 fare machines, 6 new bus shelters, a Bus Time wayfinding panel at Madison Av, metered parking between Fifth and Third Avenues, 200 new parking spaces for residential/retail use on 124 th and 126 th Streets, 9 new AM commercial loading zones, and dedicated lanes for buses and right turns between Lenox Av and Second Av. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p003_c0004] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p004_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0004] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p006_c0005] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p007_c0002] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p007_c0003]

Reported Results

The June 2013 briefing framed the pre-launch problem in terms of delay: its table showed M60 total travel times of 48.7 minutes westbound and 56 minutes eastbound, and it summarized that buses were stopped 45% of the time. [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p004_c0004] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p004_c0005] It also linked heavy double parking in Manhattan to bus bunching/delays in Queens. [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p004_c0006]

The January 2015 update reported SBS trips 11-14% faster end to end, with October 2013 to October 2014 table values of 59 to 52 minutes, 59 to 51 minutes, and 65 to 58 minutes across the listed time periods. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p009_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p009_c0004] In the Lenox Av to Second Av section, it reported SBS trips 32-34% faster, with table values of 14.8 to 10.2 minutes, 13.5 to 9.2 minutes, and 17.8 to 11.8 minutes. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p010_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p010_c0004]

The local-bus and traffic results were more qualified. Local bus trips between Lenox Av and Second Av were reported 7-20% faster, but the April 2015 update said local bus service had "only improved on the east side of 125 th Street, where bus lanes exist." [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p011_c0004] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p016_c0003] Taxi speeds were mixed: the January table showed eastbound speeds of 11.0 to 12.2 MPH, 9.0 to 9.0 MPH, 9.5 to 7.8 MPH, and 8.5 to 9.2 MPH, while the text summarized taxi trips as generally faster eastbound and unchanged or slightly slower westbound. [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p012_c0003] [2015_01_06_brt_125th_cb11#p012_c0004]

Source-Time Caveats

The 2015 updates did not close every thread. The January CB10 update called for additional traffic and transit monitoring, including parking observations, traffic volumes, and additional traffic and transit data collection, and also said to "Explore further improvements to M60 SBS." [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p013_c0002] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p013_c0003] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p013_c0004] The April 2015 update introduced a "125 th Street: 2015 Plan" to extend bus lanes west from Lenox Av to Morningside Av, add four blocks of new offset bus lanes and right-turn bays, and explore Transit Signal Priority at potential intersections between Amsterdam and Second Avenues. [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p019_c0001] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p019_c0002] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p020_c0003] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p020_c0005] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p029_c0002] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p029_c0003] [2015_04_14_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p029_c0004]

Treat status as source-time: planned in the June 2013 source, launched/implemented in the 2014-2015 sources. Most performance evidence is an October 2013 vs October 2014 comparison, not a long-term trend. April 2015 follow-on items are plans/proposals in the cited source, not proof of completion. The February 10, 2015 source is an 86th Street SBS presentation with an M60 SBS update section; use it as corroboration, not the primary 125th Street source. The target page merges several closely related M60/125th records, so future cleanup may need to separate route identity, 125th Street corridor planning, and M60 SBS implementation more cleanly.
