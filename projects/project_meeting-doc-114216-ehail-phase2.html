Summary

E-Hail Phase 2 was presented in June 2023 as an expansion of Access-A-Ride's on-demand E-Hail pilot. The service model let ADA paratransit customers book taxi or for-hire-vehicle trips directly with a vendor by app, using sedans or wheelchair-accessible vehicles, with the stated purpose of adding flexibility and spontaneity while keeping paratransit part of an integrated accessible transit system. [meeting_doc_114216#p001_c0001] [meeting_doc_114216#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_114216#p006_c0004] [meeting_doc_114216#p006_c0006]

Phase 2 was framed as a response to Phase 1 lessons. Phase 1 had run since 2017 for 1,200 Access-A-Ride customers, with unlimited monthly trips, unlimited subsidy, no defined plan for data collection or expansion, and a reported $12M-$16M annual budget. [meeting_doc_114216#p007_c0002] [meeting_doc_114216#p007_c0003] The Phase 2 goal was to make E-Hail more financially sustainable and better aligned with AAR strategy: traditional Access-A-Ride for everyday trips, E-Hail as an additional flexible option, and pilot data to support recommendations within one year. [meeting_doc_114216#p008_c0003] [meeting_doc_114216#p008_c0004]

Phase 2 Design

The June presentation described an expansion to 3,600 total E-Hail customers by adding 2,400 randomly selected customers representative of AAR, with the expanded service beginning August 1. [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0002] [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0003] [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0006] It also listed added on-demand providers: Arro, Corporate Transportation Group (CTG), The Drivers Cooperative, and Uber. [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0004] [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0005]

The Phase 2 structure introduced a $4 co-pay and monthly trip/subsidy limits: a Distance program with 25 trips per month up to $44 each, and a High Volume program with 40 trips per month up to $29 each, while retaining unlimited traditional AAR access at the base fare. [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0007] MTA characterized this as aligned with more mature on-demand paratransit programs because it used co-pays, monthly trip allocations, and trip subsidy limits. [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0008] [meeting_doc_114216#p009_c0009]

Service Context And Early Status

The expansion sat within a broader AAR improvement push. The June presentation reported record ridership of over 2.4 million rides in the first four months of 2023, installation of more than 600 iPad GPS units into a 1,200-vehicle fleet, and taxi/FHV drivers providing 70% of AAR trips. [meeting_doc_114216#p004_c0003] [meeting_doc_114216#p004_c0004] [meeting_doc_114216#p004_c0006] Other ongoing initiatives included new scheduling software, a tighter 20-minute on-time window, improved online booking, and E-Hail expansion. [meeting_doc_114216#p005_c0011]

A July 2023 NYCT performance report described AAR demand as growing, with roughly 25,900 weekday trips and 16,200 weekend trips in May, and said AAR was improving for-hire-vehicle performance while adding driver incentives and expanding E-Hail in August. [meeting_doc_115256#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_115256#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_115256#p007_c0004] [meeting_doc_115256#p007_c0005] The same report stated that the E-Hail Phase 2 expansion would launch in August 2023, add 2,400 customers, and run from August 1, 2023 through January 31, 2024. [meeting_doc_115256#p017_c0007]

Data Notes

The July report provides operating context rather than a post-launch evaluation. June paratransit ridership and provider-type data were marked not yet available, and the E-Hail paragraph was still forward-looking. [meeting_doc_115256#p010_c0010] [meeting_doc_115256#p016_c0004] [meeting_doc_115256#p017_c0007] It did report that June AAR satisfaction was 69%, up one point from May, with perceived improvements in no-show frequency and on-time pickup. [meeting_doc_115256#p009_c0009]

Summary

E-Hail Phase 2 was the Board-approved expansion of Access-A-Ride's on-demand E-hail program, authorized to begin on or about August 1, 2023. [meeting_doc_115331#p014_c0003] The expansion moved the program beyond the 1,200 Phase 1 customers: MTA materials described Phase 2 as tripling enrollment to 3,600 customers, with compliant existing E-hail customers continuing and 2,400 additional AAR customers added. [meeting_doc_114166#p147_c0014] By September 2023, NYCT reported that Phase 2 had launched in August, the 1,200 Phase 1 customers had transitioned, and onboarding of more than 2,400 new customers would begin in September. [meeting_doc_121046#p017_c0007]

The service model was an on-demand taxi or for-hire-vehicle option that customers booked directly with a provider by app or phone, with MTA subsidizing a set number of trips per month. [meeting_doc_140671#p004_c0003] In the May 2024 Phase 3 presentation, MTA described the current Phase 2 pilot as having five taxi/FHV providers, a $4 initial customer charge, two benefit tiers, and 10,000 invited customers with 1,600 registered. [meeting_doc_140671#p004_c0005] The two Phase 2 tiers were a Distance program with 25 monthly trips and a $40 MTA subsidy per trip, and a High Volume program with 40 monthly trips and a $25 MTA subsidy. [meeting_doc_140671#p004_c0005]

MTA's later assessment was favorable but bounded: it reported 81% overall satisfaction among E-Hail participants, about 15 E-Hail trips per month among active pilot customers, and continued use of traditional AAR for some trips. [meeting_doc_140671#p005_c0002] The same source says monthly trip and subsidy allocations reduced E-Hail costs by more than 50% and enabled MTA to expand benefits to more customers. [meeting_doc_140671#p005_c0002] For Phase 2, MTA listed annual cost as $4.5 million per year at a $21 average cost per trip. [meeting_doc_140671#p006_c0002]
