MTA New Bus Program

project_mta-new-bus-program

Overview

The MTA New Bus Program is a bus-procurement and fleet-modernization program run through NYCT's Department of Buses for NYCT and MTA Bus service, with procurement configurations guided by the MTA capital program and the Zero Emissions Transition plan. [meeting_doc_176356#p026_c0003]

In the 2023 program update, MTA described its bus fleet as the largest in the country, with 5,809 buses and an average fleet age of 7.38 years based on the Spring 2023 bus assignment. [meeting_doc_111791#p045_c0002] [meeting_doc_111791#p045_c0006] [meeting_doc_111791#p045_c0009]

MTA framed new bus replacement as a way to improve reliability, add customer amenities, and advance the zero-emissions bus goal. [meeting_doc_111791#p045_c0004] [meeting_doc_111791#p045_c0005]

Program Status

The 2025 update describes the 2020-2024 capital-program bus portfolio as 2,161 buses, with 1,000 delivered, 944 in production or expected to be in production by the end of 2025, 207 in procurement, and 10 future orders. [meeting_doc_176356#p019_c0002] [meeting_doc_176356#p019_c0003]

The 2025 IEC review says it was monitoring 15 bus contracts, including 8 contracts totaling 489 buses that had completed deliveries since the June 2024 report and 7 active contracts totaling 944 buses valued at $1.225 billion. [meeting_doc_176356#p026_c0005]

The 2025 contract table lists completed deliveries since the prior report for 15 New Flyer BEBs, 45 New Flyer BEBs, 25 Nova SDBs, 25 Nova SDBs, 85 Nova SDBs, 5 Nova BEBs, 173 Nova SDBs, and 116 New Flyer SDBs. [meeting_doc_176356#p028_c0003]

The 2025 contract table lists active contracts for 224 New Flyer articulated diesel buses, 250 Prevost express buses, 162 New Flyer BEBs, 25 New Flyer BEBs, 18 New Flyer articulated electric buses, 193 New Flyer BEBs, and 72 New Flyer articulated electric buses. [meeting_doc_176356#p028_c0003]

Zero-Emissions Transition

The 2023 update identified the Zero-Emissions Bus Program as MTA's commitment to reduce operating emissions by 85% by 2040. [meeting_doc_111791#p050_c0002]

The 2025 IEC review says battery-electric bus purchases are mandated by MTA's Zero Emissions Transition Plan. [meeting_doc_176356#p027_c0003]

By the 2025 review, the 65 BEBs noted in the prior year's report had all been delivered by early 2025, 60 New Flyer buses had entered revenue service from 4 depots, and 5 Nova buses had been received for testing and evaluation after NYCT acceptance. [meeting_doc_176356#p027_c0004]

The 2025 review says 470 BEBs, including 90 articulated electric buses, were in procurement with deliveries expected to begin in October 2025 and completion scheduled for May 2029. [meeting_doc_176356#p027_c0004]

Reliability, Amenities, And Bus-Priority Technology

The 2025 update reports that, for the 12 months ending May 2025, newest standard and articulated buses had MDBF of 7,625 compared with 3,269 for buses to be replaced, and newest coaches had MDBF of 21,107 compared with 5,986 for coaches to be replaced. [meeting_doc_176356#p022_c0003] [meeting_doc_176356#p022_c0004] [meeting_doc_176356#p022_c0005]

The 2025 update says 1,753 buses were 12 years old or older and would be replaced in the capital programs, and it states that the newest buses delivered since 2021 were 2 to 3 times more reliable than older fleets. [meeting_doc_176356#p022_c0006]

The 2023 update ties the fleet program to service-reliability tools, including Automatic Bus Lane Enforcement on 423 buses across 16 routes and a plan to expand to 1,000 buses by the end of that year. [meeting_doc_111791#p059_c0002] [meeting_doc_111791#p059_c0003]

The 2023 update also reports Transit Signal Priority on more than 4,400 buses across 122 routes, with all new buses TSP-ready and more than 3,000 intersections systemwide. [meeting_doc_111791#p059_c0004] [meeting_doc_111791#p059_c0005]

The 2025 update says new buses include accessibility and operator-safety features such as continuous flip-up seats, a wider 32-inch ramp, and new full driver enclosures. [meeting_doc_176356#p024_c0004] [meeting_doc_176356#p024_c0006] [meeting_doc_176356#p024_c0008]

Risks And Watch Points

The 2023 IEC review said completed bus contracts had experienced delays from COVID, technical, and supply-chain issues, while active non-BEB contracts were on schedule and on budget. [meeting_doc_111791#p065_c0003]

The 2023 IEC review said the 60-bus BEB contracts were delayed to September 2024, a 10-month slip, and that EAC had increased by $6.5 million because of Early Warning Detection System development. [meeting_doc_111791#p065_c0005]

The 2025 IEC review says the 60 New Flyer BEBs in revenue service had performance problems, with life-to-date MDBF of 2,111 versus 13,000 for equivalent New Flyer standard diesel buses and daily full-service availability of about 60% versus 91% for equivalent New Flyer SDBs. [meeting_doc_176356#p029_c0003]

The 2025 IEC review identifies BEB primary failures as charging issues, energy-storage-system string failures, battery overheating, and failure to achieve required state of charge. [meeting_doc_176356#p029_c0003]

The 2025 IEC review says battery life for the 60 New Flyer BEBs was a concern, that 5 Nova BEBs began testing and evaluation in May 2025, and that BEB issues could affect acceptance into service for 205 upcoming BEBs projected to begin delivery in October 2025. [meeting_doc_176356#p030_c0003]

The 2025 IEC review lists broader concerns that bus procurement volumes were inconsistent with a twelve-year fleet-replacement goal, that delayed acceptance could keep older buses in service longer, and that Nova's withdrawal from the U.S. bus market further constrained short-term acquisition opportunities. [meeting_doc_176356#p031_c0003]
