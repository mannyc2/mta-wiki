Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements

project_grand-st-grand-av-safety-bus-priority

Overview

Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements is a late-2024 planning and outreach project for Grand Street/Grand Avenue between Metropolitan Avenue and Queens Boulevard. The same deck family was presented to Brooklyn Community Board 1 on November 12, 2024, Queens Community Board 4 Transportation Committee on November 26, 2024, and Queens Community Board 5 Transportation Committee on December 17, 2024. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p001_c0001] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p001_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p001_c0003] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p001_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p001_c0003] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p001_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p001_c0003]

The corridor is framed as a bus-priority candidate because Q54, Q58, and Q59 together carry over 51,000 daily bus passengers, with Q58 described as the highest-ridership route in Queens. The same slide identifies the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot and Central Maintenance Facility as serving 10 routes. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p004_c0002] The project is also shown in the Queens bus-priority corridor context and tied to MTA/NYC DOT coordination around the Queens Bus Network Redesign. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p006_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p006_c0003]

Existing Needs

The depot is a central operating issue: the presentation says 1,032 buses pull in and out every weekday, including more than 80 movements during the busiest hour, and that truck traffic and Grand St Bridge congestion create pull-in and pull-out difficulties that translate into longer waits and less reliable service. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p007_c0005] Bus speeds are reported as low as 4 mph in some sections, with slow speeds concentrated near Grand St L, the Grand Av Depot, and the Long Island Expressway; the deck attributes slowdowns to traffic congestion, double parking, and high volumes of commercial vehicles. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p008_c0002]

The corridor also has a safety case. For 2019-2023, the December deck reports 359 crash injuries, 25 severe injuries, 3 fatalities, and a total of 28 people killed or severely injured; it also identifies Grand Av as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with a Vision Zero Priority Intersection at Grand Av and Queens Blvd, and places it in the top third of dangerous corridors in Queens. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0006]

The surrounding census tracts are presented as transit-dependent: 38,000 residents, about one-third of households without private-vehicle access, more than half of commuters using transit, walking, or biking, and an average commute of 43 minutes. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p009_c0003] The deck's table gives commute mode shares of 50% transit, 37% car, 5% walking, and 35% of households with no vehicle. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p009_c0006] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p009_c0008]

Design Direction

The stated vision is to make bus service fast, reliable, and on-time, improve bus operations around the Grand Av Bus Depot, make Grand Av safer to walk, bike, and drive, and preserve access for businesses and loading. The stated challenges are traffic congestion, double parking and business access needs, and limited space for bikes and buses. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p014_c0003] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p014_c0005]

The physical design problem is constrained by a roughly 50-foot street width for the corridor, with 25-foot combined moving and parking space in each direction between Metropolitan Av and the Long Island Expressway. The deck says painted bike lanes exist only between 74th Street and Queens Boulevard, with no bike facilities or sharrows elsewhere. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p012_c0002]

The toolkit under consideration includes signal timing, Transit Signal Priority, bus lane camera enforcement through DOT stationary cameras and MTA on-bus ACE cameras, turn bays, and streetscape improvements. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p020_c0002] The bus-lane option slides show several 50-foot cross sections: single offset lane, single curbside lane, two curbside lanes, and one curbside plus one offset lane. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p024_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p024_c0003] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p025_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p025_c0003] The deck's open-discussion slide asks which tools and bus-lane options sound useful, what tradeoffs are acceptable, and what a successful project would look like, so these should be read as options under discussion rather than a selected final design. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p026_c0002]

Timeline

As of the late-2024 presentations, DOT/MTA described Fall 2024 work as discussing existing conditions, needs, and wants with community boards, elected officials, and stakeholders, plus a merchant survey, site visits, and data collection. Winter/Spring 2025 next steps were continued outreach, developing and sharing draft plans, refining the proposal based on feedback, and sharing a refined plan. Implementation was projected for late 2025 or 2026, with a note that the schedule may vary depending on feedback and analysis. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p022_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p022_c0003]
