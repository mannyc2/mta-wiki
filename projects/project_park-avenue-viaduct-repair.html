Park Avenue Viaduct Repair

project_park-avenue-viaduct-repair

Overview

The Park Avenue Viaduct is a critical Metro-North state-of-good-repair project: a 2022 railroad performance report says it carries all Metro-North trains serving Grand Central Terminal, representing 98% of Metro-North service, and calls it a single point of failure for service into and out of Manhattan [meeting_doc_100291#p015_c0006]. April 2024 capital-program materials describe the viaduct as an elevated steel structure over Park Avenue between East 110th Street and the Harlem River Lift Bridge, carrying four MNR tracks used by the Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven lines to reach Harlem-125th Street and Grand Central Terminal; the same item says 750 east-of-Hudson passenger trains per day either traverse it or connect with a train that does [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0009].

Scope

The replacement program covers Phase 1 from East 115th Street to East 123rd Street and Phase 2 from East 127th Street to the midblock between East 131st and East 132nd Streets [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0008]. The December 2024 project description says the work replaces the foundations, structure, tracks, power, communications, and signals of an over-130-year-old viaduct that had reached the end of its useful life [meeting_doc_160206#p018_c0014]. In April 2024, C&D sought to add Phase 2 to the existing Phase 1 design-build contract with Halmar International because the Phase 2 work was nearly identical to Phase 1 and because adding it to the same contract was expected to improve cost and schedule outcomes [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0011].

Timeline And Status

Key preconstruction milestones were already in place by the 2022 year-end report: New York City legal agreements were executed in July 2022, inspection and flag reports had been completed and submitted, and the design-build construction contract was awarded in December 2022 [meeting_doc_111726#p030_c0004]. The April 2024 board materials list the Phase 2 modification as a Capital Program Committee action on April 29, 2024 and Board action on April 30, 2024 [meeting_doc_138616#p016_c0002]. For Phase 2 early work, the design-builder was directed on April 8, 2024 to proceed up to $2.8 million for mobilization, survey, design, and exploratory work [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0013]. The negotiated modification price was $240,967,621, with a 545-day extension that kept Phase 1 completion at April 3, 2026 and set overall substantial completion at September 30, 2027 [meeting_doc_138181#p037_c0003].

Delivery Rationale

The board item says adding Phase 2 to the existing contract would let about two-thirds of Phase 1 and Phase 2 proceed simultaneously, saving about 18 months and about $70 million compared with a separate procurement, while reducing weekend service outages by 60%, or roughly 24 weekend outages [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0012]. A December 2024 status table listed the combined project budget as $833.6 million, including Phase 2, and reported the project approximately 48% complete [meeting_doc_160206#p019_c0004]. The same December status packet says the DB contract was awarded to Halmar in December 2022, the base notice to proceed was January 2023, Phase 1 milestone completion was April 2026, contractual substantial completion was September 2027, and the IEC-reviewed schedule was on schedule [meeting_doc_160206#p032_c0005].

Operating Context

Metro-North reported in April 2024 that Phase 1 work from 115th to 123rd Streets had already replaced 2,200 feet of steel third rail with aluminum third rail, relocated signal power feeder and communications cables, and erected one of two gantry systems [meeting_doc_140311#p004_c0007] [meeting_doc_140311#p005_c0002]. The October 2024 schedule summary says the October 6 schedules continued to support major C&D, CDOT, and Maintenance of Way projects requiring extensive track outages on all three lines, including continuation of Park Avenue Viaduct Span renewal [meeting_doc_151786#p001_c0008]. A November 2024 capital-program update later described the viaduct work as rebuilding a 130-year-old structure with no impact to service and 21 months ahead of schedule [meeting_doc_157976#p016_c0009].

Caveats

The sources mix service-impact language: the Phase 2 bundling rationale says weekend outages would be reduced by 60% [meeting_doc_138181#p036_c0012], an October 2024 schedule summary says schedules supported extensive track outages including Park Avenue Viaduct Span renewal [meeting_doc_151786#p001_c0008], and a November 2024 presentation says "no impact to service" [meeting_doc_157976#p016_c0009].

The 2027 endpoint is source-stated as both "in service in 2027" [meeting_doc_100291#p015_c0006] and contractual substantial completion in September 2027/on schedule [meeting_doc_160206#p032_c0005]; do not infer a more specific public opening date from these sources alone.
