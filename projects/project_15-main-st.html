Q44 Select Bus Service on Main Street

project_15-main-st

Context

Q44 Select Bus Service on Main Street appears in the 2015 CB7 deck as a proposal to replace Q44 LTD with Q44 SBS, following the same route from Merrick Blvd in Jamaica to the Bronx Zoo, alongside a Main Street bus-lane proposal. [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p001_c0001] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p009_c0002] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p009_c0003] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p012_c0001]

The corridor context was a Flushing-to-Jamaica bus-priority effort that the 2015 deck says had been identified in earlier BRT studies and characterized by "long and slow bus trips." [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p005_c0002] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p005_c0003]

The 2015 corridor overview counted 42,000 daily riders on Main Street, with Q20A/B running Jamaica to College Point and Q44 running Jamaica to Bronx Zoo. [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p008_c0001] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p008_c0003] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p008_c0004]

Design Thread

The 2015 bus-lane proposal described offset bus lanes northbound from 60 Av to Downtown Flushing and southbound from Downtown Flushing to 60 Av, while noting that Flushing bus lanes would terminate at 40 Rd in 2015 because of a forthcoming pedestrian-safety capital project. [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p012_c0003] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p012_c0004] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p012_c0007]

The same 2015 deck treated Main St from Northern Blvd to Queens Blvd as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor and reported 3 fatalities and 32 killed-or-severely-injured cases from 2009 to 2013. [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p007_c0001] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p007_c0002] [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p007_c0003]

By the 2019 Better Buses Action Plan, the page-bearing project had narrowed to "Main St, Northern Blvd to 40th Rd," a 0.2-mile Flushing segment. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0007]

The 2019 plan says that segment had been converted to southbound Bus and Truck Only in March 2017 and that the conversion produced a 23% bus-speed improvement. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0006]

The 2019 plan framed the next step as clarifying and reinforcing the Bus and Truck Only regulation. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0006]

Potential 2019 improvements included pavement markings at 37th Ave, extending the northbound curbside bus lane to Northern Blvd, signal-timing adjustments around the Bus and Truck Only corridor, and additional signage for Bus and Truck Only and redirected traffic. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0011]

Service Scope

The 2019 action-plan entry listed Q17, Q19, Q20A, Q20B, Q25, Q27, Q34, Q44 SBS, Q50, Q65, and Q66 as routes served by the 0.2-mile segment. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0007]

The 2019 action-plan entry listed total daily ridership of 150,000 for the segment's served routes. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0007]

Read together, the 2015 and 2019 sources make this record a bridge between the full Q44 SBS/Main Street corridor proposal and a later short-segment reinforcement project in Downtown Flushing. [2015_07_07_brt_flushing_jamaica_cb7_presentation#p012_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p038_c0006]
