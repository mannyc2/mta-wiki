Scope

CT Bridge Projects is the Connecticut-only bridge-timber work shown in Metro-North Railroad Maintenance of Way Division updates. The source decks identify the program context as Track, Structures, Stations and Facilities for Metro-North Railroad, with source dates of December 31, 2022, June 2023, and September 2023. [meeting_doc_104771#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_104771#p001_c0003] [meeting_doc_104771#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_115146#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_115146#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_124216#p001_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p001_c0004]

The project scope is stated as "CT Bridge Projects" and "Bridge Timbers (CT only)." [meeting_doc_104771#p010_c0002] [meeting_doc_104771#p010_c0003] The listed locations are primarily New Haven Line bridge-timber sites; the 2023 lists also include New Canaan / Noroton River. Bridge Program Context

The same quarterly decks place CT Bridge Projects among several Metro-North bridge work pages. Immediately nearby are the Overhead Bridge Program (NY), which covers bridge flag repairs on Harlem, Hudson, New Haven-NY, and Port Jervis lines and lists typical repairs such as deteriorated-member replacement, bridge-seat rebuilding, abutment-wall repair, load-bearing-support repair, and timber deck replacements. [meeting_doc_124216#p007_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p007_c0003]

The CT timber program is separate from the NY-only undergrade bridge timber and NY undergrade bridge repair pages. Those pages report NY-only timber quantities and Hudson/Harlem/Port Jervis repair locations, while CT Bridge Projects reports Connecticut bridge-timber locations and totals. [meeting_doc_124216#p008_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p008_c0003] [meeting_doc_124216#p009_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p009_c0003]

It is also separate from the S-Program (CTDOT Bridge Program). That adjacent CTDOT bridge page was described as deferred to 2023 due to lack of funding in the December 2022 source, pending funding from CDOT in the June 2023 source, and funded in September 2023 with work to commence in October 2023 and carry over to 2024. [meeting_doc_104771#p011_c0002] [meeting_doc_115146#p011_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p011_c0002]

Individual Connecticut bridge jobs appear on a separate adjacent page: Cos Cob Bridge NH 29.90 in Greenwich and Saga NH 44.32 in Westport are listed with their own steel repair or bascule-support-framing progress, not as the CT Bridge Projects timber total. [meeting_doc_104771#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_115146#p012_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p012_c0002]

Caveats And Open Questions

The inspected CT Bridge Projects blocks do not state a dollar budget or contract value; they provide planned timber counts, site-level percent-complete notes, and total percent complete. [meeting_doc_104771#p010_c0004] [meeting_doc_115146#p010_c0004] [meeting_doc_124216#p010_c0003]

The September 2023 source has an internal scope mismatch: the heading says "460 Timbers Planned," while the table total says "570 Timbers Planned" and includes added Main Street and Campbell Avenue locations. Preserve both literals until reviewed against the source PDF image. [meeting_doc_124216#p010_c0002] [meeting_doc_124216#p010_c0003]

Each CT Bridge Projects slide includes the note "All subject to change due to current events," so these should be treated as document-time status snapshots, not final project outcomes. [meeting_doc_104771#p010_c0005] [meeting_doc_115146#p010_c0005] [meeting_doc_124216#p010_c0004]
