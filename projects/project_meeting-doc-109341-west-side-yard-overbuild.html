West Side Yard Overbuild

project_meeting-doc-109341-west-side-yard-overbuild

Summary

The source-backed core of this record is narrow: "West Side Yard Overbuild" is a named project that LIRR timetable planning repeatedly had to support, not a detailed scope memo. The May 22, 2023 through September 5, 2023 timetable package lists it among "Continuing projects" alongside East Side Access, Main Line Expansion, and Mid-Suffolk Yard Construction. [meeting_doc_109341#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_109341#p001_c0005]

A same-name September 5, 2023 through November 12, 2023 record, plus later November 13, 2023 through March 3, 2024, March 4, 2024 through May 19, 2024, and March 3, 2025 through May 19, 2025 timetable packages, continue to list "the West Side Yard Overbuild" among projects supported during the timetable period. [meeting_doc_115156#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_115156#p001_c0006] [meeting_doc_124266#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_124266#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_133331#p001_c0004] [meeting_doc_133331#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_164866#p001_c0005] [meeting_doc_164866#p001_c0006]

The useful status reading is therefore cautious: at document time, it was at least an active or continuing timetable-supported project in 2023, and it remained in recurring supported-project lists into 2025. The cited timetable sources do not assign a specific customer-facing service adjustment to the overbuild itself; they list it together with other projects and trackwork.

Funding And Schedule

The schedule evidence is the sequence of timetable windows above, not a construction schedule for the overbuild. The funding evidence is also program-level: the LIRR staff summaries say funding for "these projects" is contained in the Long Island Rail Road Operating and MTA Capital Program budget, but they do not provide an itemized West Side Yard Overbuild budget. [meeting_doc_109341#p002_c0006] [meeting_doc_115156#p003_c0003] [meeting_doc_124266#p002_c0006] [meeting_doc_133331#p003_c0004] [meeting_doc_164866#p002_c0010]

West Side Yard / Hudson Yards Context

A broader West Side Yard Development source gives the likely physical and institutional context for the word "overbuild." A 2008 MTA real estate action describes development at the John D. Caemmerer West Side Yard, including the Western Rail Yard and Eastern Rail Yard, as a mixed-use development with 99-year ground leases over approximately 26.17 acres. [meeting_doc_129081#p015_c0006]

The same source says MTA had issued RFPs for sale or long-term lease of air space and related real property interests for development at the Eastern and Western Rail Yard sections. [meeting_doc_129081#p016_c0004] It also states two primary objectives: maximizing revenue for MTA's capital plan and assuring safe, uninterrupted LIRR service at West Side Yard, described as a 24/7 active storage, maintenance, and cleaning facility. [meeting_doc_129081#p016_c0005]

That context helps explain why an overbuild project would matter to rail operations: the real estate source discusses substantial platform and infrastructure costs over existing railroad tracks and facilities, and separately describes property interests in "space above a limiting plane" where a roof would be required above LIRR tracks and facilities. [meeting_doc_129081#p016_c0007] [meeting_doc_129081#p016_c0012] The timetable sources, however, do not identify the exact overbuild parcel, developer, work package, roof segment, or construction milestone.
