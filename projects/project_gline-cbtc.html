Overview

The G Line CBTC Design-Build project covers a wayside CBTC signal system on the NYCT G Line from Court Square in Queens to Church Avenue in Brooklyn. [meeting_doc_102776#p099_c0021]

The December 2022 award package sought Board approval for a $368.697 million, 57-month design-build contract with Crosstown Partners and a separate $36.951697 million, 25-year maintenance contract with Thales. [meeting_doc_102776#p099_c0021]

Scope

The base contract replaces the existing fixed-block, relay-based signal system, adds CBTC that can operate with CBTC-equipped trains and NYCT's B Division ATS system, and includes track replacement plus train-control-room construction or fit-out. [meeting_doc_102776#p100_c0006]

The contract options covered 5G and Wi-Fi wayside data communication systems, CBTC test-facility engineering support, and wayside trip stops. [meeting_doc_102776#p100_c0008]

By June 2023, the project-review scope also listed ATS-B expansion, required software systems, track work, facilities work, wayside 5G DCS, an Enhanced Integrated Test Facility, wayside train stops, a 25-year Thales maintenance contract, and WSP project-management support. [meeting_doc_113971#p046_c0003]

Delivery Status

The design-build contractor received notice to proceed in January 2023, with June 2025 concept-design validation, July 2027 substantial completion, and September 2027 final completion shown in the June 2023 milestone table. [meeting_doc_113971#p047_c0003] [meeting_doc_113971#p047_c0004]

In June 2023, the project budget and EAC were reported as $620 million, with the project 9% complete by expenditures and schedule time elapsed. [meeting_doc_113971#p048_c0003]

In March 2024, the IEC described Crosstown as the first CBTC Centric project and first Design-Build project for the Signals & Train Control Business Unit, and flagged it as software-intensive with significant software risks. [meeting_doc_135341#p051_c0003]

In March 2024, the budget was reported as $633 million, the project was 11% complete with 25% time expended, and the IEC forecast a $638 million EAC. [meeting_doc_135341#p053_c0003]

In March 2024, the IEC forecast Crosstown substantial completion slipping to Q4 2027. [meeting_doc_135341#p022_c0003] [meeting_doc_135341#p022_c0004]

In July 2025, the project table reported a $624 million budget, the TC Electric/Hitachi/Crosstown Partners/AECOM design-build team, about 53% completion, north in-service in 2027, south in-service in 2028, and substantial completion in Q2 2029. [meeting_doc_179731#p016_c0006]

By July 2025, a substantial amount of wayside work and track work had been completed during the Summer 2024 shutdown, overall construction progress remained as planned, and 5G radio wayside operation had been demonstrated for FCC requirements. [meeting_doc_179731#p016_c0007]

The July 2025 report attributed in-service delay to procurement, design, installation, and commissioning of a 5G radio retrofit for the R211 and R179 fleets. [meeting_doc_179731#p016_c0008] [meeting_doc_179731#p017_c0002]

The July 2025 report also said the contractor was late obtaining approval of the signal wayside block design, and that the forecast EAC had increased by $24 million for potential additional support costs excluding potential contractor claims. [meeting_doc_179731#p017_c0003]
