The 2023 ABLE expansion was presented as both a bus-speed intervention and an enforcement-capacity buildout. In the February 2023 performance report, MTA said it would expand buses with onboard enforcement cameras from 423 to 1,023 by the end of 2023, reaching up to 80% of existing bus lanes. The same update tied well-enforced bus priority to faster bus movement and described a planned ABLE expansion bill for bus-stop obstructions and double parking, including violations outside designated bus lanes. [meeting_doc_105921#p029_c0011]

By the July 17, 2023 Committee on Operations minutes, President Davey reported ABLE activation on twelve additional routes, bringing the total to nineteen routes, and said those routes were seeing faster service, fewer collisions, and increased customer satisfaction. For places where ABLE was not yet active, staff separately described an NYPD enforcement initiative targeting cars parked illegally in right-of-way lanes and bus lanes, beginning in Queens, expanding to the Bronx, and planned for Brooklyn in August. [meeting_doc_121271#p004_c0001] [meeting_doc_121271#p004_c0002]

The financial-plan materials fold ABLE into NYCT operating efficiencies rather than treating it only as a street-operations project. The November Financial Plan describes revenues and schedule savings from installing cameras on additional buses and expanding enforcement, permitted through the State 2023-24 Enacted Budget, as part of an $80 million operating-efficiency package. Its initiative table lists ABLE revenue as net revenue from vehicles fined in bus lanes, with $22.1 million shown for 2024. [meeting_doc_127706#p022_c0003] [meeting_doc_127706#p023_c0002]

The December 2023 NYCT budget staff summary adds implementation cost texture: the expanded ABLE program required equipment plus event-processing fees, fines-processing fees, software, and contract-management costs, with fine revenues expected to offset or exceed operating expenses. The same summary again links ABLE to bus lane violation revenues and unscheduled bus-operation overtime savings from cameras on additional buses and expanded enforcement. [meeting_doc_128961#p002_c0004] [meeting_doc_128961#p001_c0011]
