Context

The Grand St, Metropolitan Av, Morgan Av Street Improvement Project is listed in the Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements decks as a DOT project to address blocked bike lanes, bus stops, and sidewalks; poor visibility for pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers; and slow bus speeds. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb4_nov2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p013_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_nov2024#p013_c0002]

The project appears within a broader Grand St/Grand Av study area from Metropolitan Av to Queens Blvd, where the decks report more than 51,000 daily bus passengers on the Q54, Q58, and Q59 and identify the Q58 as the highest-ridership route in Queens. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p004_c0002]

The same corridor materials say the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot and Central Maintenance Facility serve 10 routes, and a depot-focused slide reports 1,032 weekday bus pull-ins and pull-outs, including more than 80 movements during the busiest hour. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p004_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p007_c0005]

Problems The Project Sits Beside

The decks describe operational difficulties around the depot as tied to truck traffic and congestion from the Grand St Bridge, with effects on wait times and service reliability for riders. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p007_c0005]

They also report bus speeds as low as 4 mph in some sections, with slow speeds concentrated near Grand St L, the Grand Av Depot, and the Long Island Expressway. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p008_c0002]

The safety context is severe: from 2019 to 2023, the decks report 359 injuries, 25 severe injuries, 3 fatalities, and 28 total people killed or severely injured on Grand Av between Metropolitan Av and Queens Blvd. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0006]

The decks also identify Grand Av as a Vision Zero Priority Corridor, identify Grand Av and Queens Blvd as a Vision Zero Priority Intersection, and place the corridor in the top third of dangerous corridors in Queens. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p010_c0002]

Design Relationship

The broader Grand St/Grand Av effort states a vision to make bus service fast, reliable, and on-time; improve bus operations around the Grand Av Bus Depot; make Grand Av safer to walk, bike, and drive; and ensure access to businesses and loading activity. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p014_c0003]

The same slide names traffic congestion, double parking and business access needs, and space for bikes and buses as the main challenges. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p014_c0005]

The deck frames the street geometry as constrained, with an approximately 50-foot street width along the corridor and 25-foot combined moving and parking space in each direction between Metropolitan Av and the Long Island Expressway. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p012_c0002]

It also says painted bike lanes exist between 74th Street and Queens Boulevard, with no bike facilities or sharrows in other sections. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p012_c0002]

Status And Adjacent Work

The Grand St, Metropolitan Av, Morgan Av Street Improvement Project is presented on the same DOT Projects slide as the Queens Boulevard Capital Project and the Grand St Bridge Reconstruction. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p013_c0002]

That slide says the Queens Boulevard Capital Project would include pedestrian, bike, and bus improvements with construction starting in Fall 2026, and says the Grand St Bridge Reconstruction was expected to begin formal environmental review in 2025. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p013_c0002]

The broader outreach schedule for the Grand St/Grand Av work called for Fall 2024 discussions with community boards, elected officials, and stakeholders, plus a merchant survey, site visits, and data collection. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p022_c0002]

The same schedule called for Winter/Spring 2025 outreach, draft plans, proposal refinement, a refined plan, and implementation in late 2025 or 2026, with timing dependent on feedback and analysis. [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p022_c0002] [grand_ave_metropolitan_ave_queens_blvd_cb5_dec2024#p022_c0003]
