Project Context

Coney Island Yard Flood Mitigation & Sandy Repairs is a Brooklyn NYCT resiliency project for the Coney Island Yard Complex. The January 2024 Capital Program Committee update says Superstorm Sandy flooded the yard with millions of gallons of water, damaging tracks and signals and corroding the electrical system; the project provides flood mitigation and drainage improvements intended to protect the yard from future storm events while minimizing impacts to shop and yard operations. [meeting_doc_131491#p011_c0011]

The yard context matters: the same update describes Coney Island Yard as the largest yard in MTA's transit system, approximately 100 acres, able to house more than 800 subway cars, and serving the West End, Brighton, Culver, Crosstown, and Sea Beach lines. [meeting_doc_131491#p011_c0012]

Scope And Delivered Work

The contract scope includes a perimeter flood wall around the Coney Island Yard Complex, extending approximately 30 feet below grade and approximately 12-15 feet above grade. [meeting_doc_104701#p071_c0007] By the January 2024 update, the project had installed more than two miles of perimeter flood protection, about 12,000 linear feet, using steel sheeting, a jet grout-concrete wall system, deployable barriers, and nine flood doors. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0006]

The project also addressed water removal and utility resilience. The January 2024 update says two new pump stations, additional drainage and outfall systems, and nearly 20,000 linear feet of drainage pipe and structures were installed, with the new east outfall and rehabilitated west outfalls in service. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0008] Power work included about 4,250 feet of new power cable bridges, more than 400,000 linear feet of pulled power and communications cables, and completed cutover to new cable feeds. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0010] [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0011] Rehabilitation of four Circuit Breaker Houses was added by change order and was complete by that update. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0013]

Schedule, Budget, And Modifications

The January 2024 status table listed a $524 million budget, a December 2023 substantial-completion forecast, and the project as more than 99% complete. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0002] The narrative says the project reached substantial completion on December 29, 2023; it also explains that the original April 2023 substantial-completion date was extended by eight months because of previously reported signal-cable damage during construction and because required track outages were not available until October. [meeting_doc_131491#p012_c0003]

The signal-cable issue appears in earlier procurement materials. In January 2023, C&D requested Board ratification of a $1,309,700 modification to Tully Construction's C-34836 contract to replace the functionality of 11 damaged signal cables using a new Programmable Logic Controller system; that modification included 104 excusable calendar days and moved substantial completion to April 14, 2023. [meeting_doc_104701#p071_c0006] February 2023 Board minutes record approval of the same C-34836.138 ratification item. [meeting_doc_106031#p012_c0011]

The January 2023 staff summary says the cable damage occurred on February 17, 2022, when 40-foot steel sheet piles were being installed along Shore Road near Gate 4 and the contractor severed a bundle of underground signal cables. The summary notes that there were no signal monuments or other indications nearby and that the cables were not shown in the as-built drawings provided to the contractor. [meeting_doc_104701#p071_c0007] C&D and NYCT Signals chose to use the PLC system rather than replace the damaged cables in kind, describing the PLC approach as more reliable and cost-efficient than running eleven new 100-pair cables roughly 2,000 linear feet from the yard junction box to the Stillwell Terminal Signal Relay Room. [meeting_doc_104701#p071_c0008]

A later April 2024 ratification covered additional signal wiring for the PLC system. The staff summary says the contract had initially provided for replacement-in-kind of copper signal cables damaged by Superstorm Sandy between the Stillwell Relay Room and Coney Island Yard Tower B, but a fiber-optic PLC system was installed instead because of reliability and state-of-good-repair concerns. [meeting_doc_138181#p038_c0009] When internal signal-rack components were dismantled, C&D found insufficient slack in several hundred conductors to directly connect the PLC system to existing relays and train-location boards; the resulting modification purchased and installed the additional wiring needed at the Stillwell Relay Room and Tower B. [meeting_doc_138181#p038_c0010] The negotiated price was $2,359,600, with 259 excusable and non-compensable calendar days extending substantial completion from April 14, 2023 to the declared December 29, 2023 date. [meeting_doc_138181#p038_c0011]
