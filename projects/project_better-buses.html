Better Buses Program

project_better-buses

Program Role

Better Buses is NYC DOT's umbrella for citywide bus-priority work with MTA and NYPD. The program is framed around making bus service dependable, improving citywide bus speeds by 25%, reversing declining ridership, and combining DOT street-design projects with camera and NYPD bus-lane enforcement, MTA service management, and bus network redesigns. [better_buses#p001_b0001]

DOT's 2024 camera-enforcement report places Better Buses in the lineage of Select Bus Service: SBS paired camera-enforced bus lanes with corridor-specific treatments and reported route-dependent speed increases of 15% to 31%, while Better Buses was initiated in 2019 to extend SBS goals to a broader set of corridors. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p005_c0002] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p005_c0003]

Treatment Portfolio

The Action Plan goals are broad: improve 5 miles of existing bus lanes each year, install 10-15 miles of new bus lanes each year, pilot up to 2 miles of physically separated bus lanes, deliver street-design projects benefiting 600,000 daily riders, add 300 Transit Signal Priority intersections per year, expand bus lane camera enforcement, increase NYPD bus-lane enforcement with seven dedicated tow truck teams, evaluate bus stops, support MTA bus-network redesigns, and press for all-door boarding and restarting SBS. [better_buses#p001_b0001]

The DOT toolkit is correspondingly mixed: new and upgraded bus lanes, potential protected bus lanes, bus boarders, queue-jump signals, traffic-flow changes, curb management, pedestrian-safety treatments, bus-stop accessibility work, turn restrictions, and busways. DOT's Better Buses page lists source-time projects ranging from corridor studies and proposed bus-priority lanes to capital follow-ups and busway-style projects, so this page should read as a program portfolio rather than a single implementation project. [better_buses#p001_b0001]

Timeline And Implementation

A February 2021 Fordham Road CAB deck ties the program to the Mayor's 2019 State of the City goal to improve bus speeds by 25%, the April 2019 Better Buses Action Plan release, 16.3 miles of bus lanes installed in 2020, and eight additional Bronx corridors identified for priority treatments, including Fordham Road. [fordham_rd_major_deegan_expwy_boston_rd_cab_feb2021#p004_c0003]

DOT later reported that in 2022 and 2023 it installed 23.1 miles of new and upgraded bus lanes under Better Buses, including University Avenue and Gun Hill Road in the Bronx, 1st Avenue, 3rd Avenue, Avenues A and D in Manhattan, 21st Street and Northern Boulevard in Queens, and Livingston Street in Brooklyn. The same report says DOT prioritizes bus-mounted and stationary cameras as soon as feasible after new bus-priority projects are implemented. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p005_c0003]

Better Buses Restart is a related 2021 initiative under the program. The Comptroller report says it aimed to install nine new major bus lanes and busways, extended and made permanent the 14th Street busway, and created plans for five additional busways, four operational as of 2025. [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0003] [behind_schedule_2025#p025_c0005]

Outcomes And Evaluation

The strongest official output metric in the checked sources is lane delivery: DOT says it installed 23.1 miles of new and upgraded bus lanes in 2022-2023, while the broader bus-lane network exceeded 180 miles through 2023. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p002_c0002] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p005_c0003]

The evaluation record is more mixed. The Comptroller report credits SBS, Fast Forward, Better Buses, and NYC Streets Plan initiatives with tangible improvements such as four dedicated busways, widespread TSP installation, and bus network redesign starts, but says the tools were not reaching their full potential because projects were cancelled, downscaled, or missed targets and because bus speeds had stagnated. [behind_schedule_2025#p004_c0005] [behind_schedule_2025#p004_c0006]

For bus-priority tools specifically, the Comptroller report says Better Buses Action Plan goals included 25% citywide speed improvement, 10-15 miles of new bus lanes per year, five upgraded miles per year, TSP, busways, and automated enforcement. It then evaluates dedicated bus lanes as falling short of Streets Plan targets and automated enforcement as underway but needing improvement. [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0002] [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0006] [behind_schedule_2025#p024_c0001]

Caveats

The 2024 DOT camera report explicitly says the newer requirements for bus speed, reliability, and ridership reporting were not part of the 2022-2023 report because the data was not collected for that purpose, and that the next two-year report would incorporate those measures. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p003_c0004] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p003_c0005]

Mileage figures need scope labels. DOT's 23.1 miles refers to "new and upgraded bus lanes" in 2022 and 2023, while the Comptroller report uses related but not identical frames such as protected-lane targets, Streets Plan mandates, and 2024 shortfalls. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p005_c0003] [behind_schedule_2025#p023_c0006] [behind_schedule_2025#p025_c0007]
