Overview

The Lexington Avenue bus-lane evidence covers two adjacent Manhattan segments. The 2019 Better Buses Action Plan listed "01 Lexington Ave, 96th St to 60th St" as a 1.8-mile corridor where the existing 7-10 AM curbside bus lane was often blocked by curb demands, serving M98, M101, M102, and M103 with 44,000 daily riders and average bus speeds of 5.0 mph in the AM and 3.9 mph in the PM. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p024_c0001] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p024_c0007] [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p024_c0008]

For that 96th-to-60th segment, the action-plan package was to repurpose one travel lane as an offset bus lane, extend metered parking and truck-loading hours on the west curb, add turn bays at major intersections, and install bus boarders at the busiest stops. [better_buses_action_plan_2019#p024_c0013] The June 2019 CB8 presentation made the offset-lane proposal more specific: shift the west-curb bus lane to the offset position from 96th Street to 60th Street, keep it in effect at all times, and enable commercial metered parking during the morning peak. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p001_c0002] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p011_c0002]

Treatments And Operations

The 2019 design used turn bays and curb management to preserve traffic and curb access: southbound right-turn bays were proposed at heavy right-turn locations, additional turn bays were proposed at 68th Street and 60th Street, and commercial-meter changes were intended to improve curb access and reduce double parking. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p012_c0002] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p012_c0003] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p013_c0002] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p013_c0003] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p014_c0002] The same deck proposed bus boarding platforms at M101 Limited stops, with 79th Street and 72nd Street listed as potential 2019/2020 locations and 96th, 86th, and 68th Streets as potential future locations. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p017_c0002] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p017_c0003] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p017_c0009]

The 2025 board-material cluster covers the adjacent 60th-to-52nd Street segment. It says eight MTA bus routes use Lexington Avenue there - BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM11, SIM22, SIM26, and SIM6 - with 71,000 daily riders depending on those routes, average speeds of 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours, an existing curbside lane often blocked by loading and passenger pickup/drop-off, and 88 injuries in the project area over the past five years, six of them severe. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002] The same source says close to 6,000 riders travel directly on the 8-block segment on an average weekday, with about 1,900 boardings and 1,800 alightings there. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p006_c0002]

2025 Upgrade Proposal

The 2025 proposal applies the 96th-to-60th offset-lane precedent to 60th-to-52nd Street: it states that a similar design was implemented on Lexington Avenue from 96th Street to 60th Street in 2019, after which bus speeds increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35%, and says consistency across segments is preferred for bus operations. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p007_c0002] The proposed 60th-to-52nd design would convert the existing curbside bus lane to offset bus lanes for improved speed and reliability, add a curbside lane for quick pickup/drop-off, use bus-mounted camera enforcement, observe the bus lane and curb access needs after implementation, and refine curb regulations in 2026. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p009_c0003] [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p010_c0007] [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p012_c0003]

Timeline evidence differs by segment: the 2019 CB8 deck listed Summer 2019 implementation and Fall/Winter 2019 post-implementation monitoring, while the October 2025 deck listed Fall 2025/Spring 2026 implementation for the 60th-to-52nd upgrade. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p020_c0002] [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p012_c0003]
