OMNY fare payment system

project_omny

Overview

OMNY is MTA's New Fare Payment System for replacing MetroCard fare collection with an account-based contactless system. It accepts contactless bank cards, digital wallets, and MTA-issued closed-loop cards, and was publicly branded "One Metro New York" in April 2019. [meeting_doc_170976#p010_c0006]

The original NFPS scope covered NYCT, other non-MTA agencies, and legacy fare systems for Metro-North and LIRR. In 2024, MTA changed the Cubic Contract A-34024 scope by removing LIRR/MNR incorporation, adding OMNY B2B Portal and mobile-app enhancements, and extending substantial completion by 29 months. [meeting_doc_140456#p009_c0003] [meeting_doc_140621#p021_c0007] After the railroad work was removed, MTA said OMNY substantial completion could be achieved by December 2025 instead of full implementation not being achievable before 2027; railroad fare modernization is now described separately as RRNFPS. [meeting_doc_140621#p022_c0007] [meeting_doc_170976#p010_c0003] [meeting_doc_170976#p012_c0004]

Rollout Status

By 2024, OMNY had separate Delivery and Operations & Services teams: Delivery focused on closed-loop features and CVM installation, while Operations managed steady-state operations, maintenance, and customer experience. [meeting_doc_140456#p010_c0002]

The 2025 update reported more than one million Student OMNY cards issued to NYC Public Schools, more than one million Reduced Fare OMNY cards mailed, Paratransit and Fair Fares conversion expected before the end of 2025, more than 4 million taps on a given day, and more than 67% of NYCT customers using OMNY across ridership classes. [meeting_doc_170976#p010_c0007] CVM rollout had grown from 70 machines at roughly 37 subway stations to more than 670 at more than 320 NYCT stations, with all 980 CVMs expected in the third quarter of 2025. [meeting_doc_170976#p010_c0008]

A May 2025 committee update said expanding OMNY access to all customer segments had been achieved, tap-and-go had become the dominant payment form across subway and bus systems, and two-thirds of NYC Transit riders used OMNY. [meeting_doc_174221#p011_c0006] The same update focused transition strategy on the remaining one-third of customers still using MetroCard before MetroCard sales cease on December 31, 2025. [meeting_doc_174221#p011_c0007]

Caveats / Open Questions

Commuter rail remains a scope boundary: MTA decided to descope commuter railroad elements from Cubic's OMNY contract and continue using Masabi for mobile ticketing and Scheidt & Bachmann for vending machines, while keeping the OMNY back office scalable for future commuter-rail accommodation. [meeting_doc_140456#p014_c0003]

The May 2025 IEC review said the current budget was adequate and December 2025 substantial completion was achievable, but also identified a risk of delay to Q2 2026 because final acceptance requires 90 consecutive days of backend validation for all OMNY taps and because of Cubic staffing challenges, software defects, and time needed to resolve validation issues. [meeting_doc_174221#p014_c0002]

A 2024 IEC concern also noted a CVM coin recirculation issue, specifically around issuing dimes as change, with Cubic actively engaged to resolve it. [meeting_doc_140456#p020_c0003]
