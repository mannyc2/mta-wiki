The LIRR Concourse renovation at Penn Station was a customer-space project for the main 33 St Concourse, described in related capital-project material as the primary corridor linking LIRR riders with subway lines at 7 Av and 8 Av; before reconstruction, the corridor was 30 feet wide and its temperature and fresh-air circulation were not to modern standards. [meeting_doc_104701#p013_c0004] By September 2022, LIRR had reopened a more modern and spacious concourse, widening it to 57 feet, nearly doubling walking space, raising illuminated ceilings to 18 feet, and describing the reopening as on time and on budget. [meeting_doc_104741#p005_c0003]

The December 2022 minutes still called the space a work in progress, with retail and dining expected later, but framed the reopened concourse as a step toward transforming Penn Station into a modern, spacious, world-class, single-level terminal. [meeting_doc_104741#p005_c0003] A related January 2023 capital-project source said the investment added wayfinding, fresh air, and passenger information systems, integrated the LIRR Concourse with East End Gateway, and showed the project 90% complete with substantial completion forecast for March 2023. [meeting_doc_104701#p013_c0005] [meeting_doc_104701#p013_c0007]

Later board presentation material continued to identify the project by name: a May 2023 Chair's Presentation listed "Penn Station LIRR Concourse," a January 2024 Chair's Board Update paired it with "Increase Appeal for Customers" and showed the modernized concourse, and a July 2024 Chair's Presentation placed it with "Increase Appeal for Customers" and "Knocking out Capital Projects."
