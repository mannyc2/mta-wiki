Home MTA projects Queens Bus Network Redesign Queens Bus Network Redesign service changes Queens Bus Network Redesign service changes Changes were made to Queens bus service in summer 2025. We've introduced a new Queens bus network. We've expanded the all-day frequent network, added new overnight service, removed some stops, and added new connections to major destinations and transfer points. Changes to the Queens bus network went into effect in two phases: Phase 1: June 29, 2025 Phase 2: August 31, 2025 Routes without Sunday service changed on either June 30 or September 2. Search for your bus route to see changes and when they happened. What changed We've taken a fresh look at the map to bring you faster, more reliable service with better connections. To do this, most bus routes have changed, which includes: New bus routes and route numbers. New Rush routes that get you to the subway faster. Changes to bus stops, including stops being relocated or removed. Routing changes to reduce turns. Starting June 29, 2025, Queens bus riders who tap to pay using the same device or card will get a second free bus transfer on every trip. The first transfer must be within two hours of the first tap and the second transfer must be within three hours. The MTA will pilot this additional free transfer for approximately six months while evaluating the new Queens bus network. Why we made changes It's been several decades since we made major changes to Queens bus routes, during which time growth and travel patterns across the borough have changed. To create the new Queens bus network, we took feedback from five years of public engagement to develop and refine proposals that meet riders' needs, modernize service, and improve operations for our workforce. The redesign focuses on four customer priorities: reliable service, faster service, better connections, and simplified service. Learn more about the Queens Bus Network Redesign. Stay connected For live customer support, chat with us in the MTA app or call 511. To send us feedback about the changes, use our Bus Network Redesign contact form .
