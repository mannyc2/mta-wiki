Proposed B103 LTD

route_b103-ltd-proposed-draft

The Proposed B103 LTD is a limited-stop route proposed as part of the Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan Proposed B103 LTD Route Improvement Summary Table. The route would serve Downtown Brooklyn, Park Slope, Kensington, Flatbush, and Canarsie, carrying approximately 13,000 daily riders Church Ave project update. It is described as a secondary route on Flatbush Avenue, operating along that corridor for parts of its alignment Flatbush Ave kickoff meeting.

The Draft Plan proposes weekday service hours from 5:00 AM to 1:30 AM with a frequency of 3 minutes in the AM Peak (6:00-8:59 AM), 7 minutes Midday, 4 minutes in the PM Peak, 5 minutes in Early Evening, and 12 minutes in Late Evening Frequency table. The route improvement summary shows that the B103 LTD would benefit from more direct routing, new connections, improved stop spacing, improved frequency, and designation as a priority corridor Route Improvement Summary Table.

In the MTA Transit All-Stars program, dispatcher Peter Watts of Queens South Road Operations was recognized for helping improve service along the B103 route Transit All-Stars recognition. Superintendent Kelly Geoghegan was also noted for her role in Queens South Road Operations that contributed to improvements on routes including the B103 Transit All-Stars.

The route record carries a "split_candidate" scope with a "merged_service_variant_conflict" reason, reflecting that different source observations classify its service type as both "limited" and "local" rather than reaching a single normalized value Route Improvement Summary Table.
