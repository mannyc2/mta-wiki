The M79 Select Bus Service runs crosstown on 79th Street in Manhattan between Riverside Drive and FDR Drive, covering just over two miles route description. The M79 bus corridor was first identified as a potential Select Bus Service candidate in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study phase II study, and planning accelerated in 2016 following community board presentations and stakeholder outreach community board consultations. The route was modeled on the success of the earlier M86 Select Bus Service modeled after M86.

M79 SBS launched on May 21, 2017, replacing M79 Local service launch date. The redesign brought dedicated bus lanes, signal timing improvements, off-board fare payment, and real-time passenger information to approximately 12,500 daily riders improvements and ridership. The route provides a crucial connection to the 1, 6, B, and C subway lines and 16 bus routes, including the M15 SBS connections.

Within two years of launch, the route recorded an average 8% travel time reduction compared to pre-SBS M79 Local service, with ridership up 9% in the first year alone against a Manhattan-wide average increase of 0.5% performance results. Car travel time on the corridor decreased by 4% on average across all time periods Car Travel Time Decrease Entire Route 4 percent, and 96% of M79 SBS riders reported satisfaction with the service satisfaction rating. Capital street-design treatments added later included curb extensions, bus pads, and pedestrian islands capital improvements. The route continues to be listed under MTA's automated camera enforcement program ACE listing.
