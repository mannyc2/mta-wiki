The IRT Flushing Line (7) is an A Division subway route serving Manhattan and Queens across an east-west corridor Mets-Willets Point Station on IRT Flushing Line 4 and 7 Line Schedule Changes. Two source documents at the MTA Capital Program Committee level document distinct activities on the 7 line.

At Mets-Willets Point Station on the 7 line, the MTA Capital Program Committee on May 20, 2026 considered a Staff Summary requesting approval of a $1,810,621 Design Agreement with Queens Future, LLC for State of Good Repair Inspection and Design Services Staff Summary location and amount. Queens Future, a joint venture of Hard Rock Casino and Steve Cohen awarded a downstate gaming license in December 2025, has committed to making the station fully ADA-accessible and performing a major mezzanine renovation, platform/canopy painting, and windscreen and lighting replacement as part of the development Queens Future commitments to station improvements. MTA had previously retained STV, Inc. in 2023 to produce a state of good repair inspection report for the station 2023 STV inspection.

The second source document, a July 8, 2022 NYCT Staff Summary, describes weekday schedule adjustments on the 4 and 7 lines implemented in December 2022 to match post-COVID shifts in peak-period rider demand Schedule Changes heading Purpose: match ridership demand shifts. There was no net change in total daily trips on the 7 line, but one fewer crew was required for 7 service No net trip change, one fewer crew.
