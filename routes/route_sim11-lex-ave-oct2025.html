SIM11

route_sim11-lex-ave-oct2025

SIM11 is one of eight MTA bus routes that travel on Lexington Avenue between 60th Street and 52nd Street in Manhattan, listed alongside BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM22, SIM26, and SIM6 8 MTA bus routes on Lexington Ave between 60th and 52nd. These eight routes carry approximately 71,000 riders daily on this segment, where the average bus speed is 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours, and the existing curbside bus lane is often blocked by loading and passenger pick-up/drop-off activity 71,000 riders, 6 mph avg speed, blocked bus lane.

In the past five years, 88 injuries occurred in the project area, including 6 severe injuries 6 88 injuries, 6 severe. On an average weekday, close to 6,000 daily riders travel on this slow 8-block segment, with about 1,900 riders boarding 1900 and 1,800 riders alighting 1800 6,000 daily riders, 1,900 boarding, 1,800 alighting. NYC DOT has proposed upgrading the existing curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane, following a design similar to the offset lane implemented on Lexington Avenue between 96th and 60th Streets in 2019, which resulted in bus speed increases of up to 26% and a 35% reduction in pedestrian injuries offset bus lane proposal with 96th-60th reference. The project is a joint effort between NYC DOT and MTA, with enforcement through bus-mounted cameras under the MTA Automated Camera Enforcement Program offset bus lane enforcement by camera.
