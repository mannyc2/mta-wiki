Route Context

South Bronx Select Bus Service is the SBS variant of the Bx6, connecting the South Bronx and Upper Manhattan. NYC DOT's SBS route index says South Bronx SBS began service on September 3, 2017 on the Bx6 route, serving nearly 25,000 daily riders and connecting to multiple subway lines, Metro-North, and 20 other bus routes. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001] A 2022 project-update deck likewise identifies the route as Bx6 Select Bus Service, launched September 3, 2017, with a map running from Riverside Dr W Terminal in Washington Heights toward the Food Center Dr Terminal in Hunts Point. [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p004_c0001] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p004_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p004_c0003]

Pre-launch planning treated the Bx6 South Bronx corridor as a Select Bus Service candidate from the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study, and described the corridor as serving nearly 25,000 daily riders, with many transit connections and high no-vehicle household share nearby. [brt_bx6_cb2_feb2017#p003_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb2_feb2017#p003_c0003] The February 2017 project goals were to improve bus speeds, address boarding and vehicle-organization issues near the courts, improve pedestrian conditions, and better organize traffic flow. [brt_bx6_cb2_feb2017#p007_c0002] Outreach evidence in that same deck records a November 2015 kickoff, on-street outreach from November 2015 through June 2016, 578 individual comments, more than 1,300 information cards, and 59 location-specific online comments. [brt_bx6_cb2_feb2017#p008_c0002]

The core service plan split the existing Bx6 into Bx6 SBS and Bx6 local: SBS would stop only at SBS stops, while the local would continue stopping at every stop. [brt_bx6_cb2_feb2017#p009_c0002] A Spring 2017 newsletter framed the Bx6 as an interborough route serving the South Bronx and Upper Manhattan, and described planned bus lanes and boarding islands as safety and mobility improvements for bus riders, pedestrians, and drivers. [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0015] The same newsletter explains off-board fare payment as a way to let riders pay before boarding and board through any door, shortening dwell time at stops. [bx6_spring_newsletter_2017#p001_c0004]

Implemented Improvements And Results

By the 2022 project updates, the implemented Bx6 SBS package included bus lanes, fare machines, boarding islands, and an eastbound 161st Street bus-only tunnel. [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p005_c0001] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p005_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p005_c0003] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p005_c0004] [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p005_c0005] The same update summarizes full-route travel-time results as 11% to 16% faster end-to-end after SBS launch. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p007_c0002] The underlying tables show eastbound travel times improving from October 2016 local service to October 2017 SBS service in all three listed periods, with some 2018 values still below the 2016 local baseline. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p007_c0004] Westbound results show the same broad pattern: October 2017 and October 2018 SBS travel times are lower than October 2016 local travel times across the listed periods. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p007_c0006]

Reliability evidence is also directionally positive but mixed over time. A 2022 update says reliability improved, with fewer late buses and less bunching. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0002] The late-bus table shows eastbound late buses falling from 22.0% in 2016 local service to 14.1% in 2017 SBS service, then rising to 17.1% in 2018 and 16.2% in 2019; westbound late buses fell from 19.9% to 13.8%, then rose to 17.8% in both 2018 and 2019. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0004] Bunching fell more sharply in the table, with both directions and peak periods below the 2016 local baseline through 2019. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p010_c0006] Ridership evidence should be read separately from speed and reliability: the 2022 update says both Bronx bus ridership and Bx6 corridor ridership were decreasing, while Bx6 declined more slowly than the borough average. [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p011_c0002] [brt_bx6_cb4_may2022#p011_c0004]

Capital And Redesign Threads

The 2022 capital schedule planned additional community-board presentations in Spring 2022, design finalization in Winter/Spring 2023, procurement in Summer/Fall 2023, and construction start in Winter/Spring 2024. [brt_bx6_cb12_may2022#p021_c0002] A later 2026 city source says a major project had started near Yankee Stadium to improve Bronx crosstown bus service and street safety, including westbound bus-only lanes, conversion of the 161st Street underpass to buses only, pedestrian safety upgrades, bus shelters, benches, leaning bars, pedestrian refuges, and other boarding-accessibility improvements. [161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001] That source also describes reconstruction/redesign on East 161st Street, portions of East 163rd Street, and segments of the Bx6-SBS route in Manhattan, with construction expected to continue through 2028. [161st_bx6_capital_project_2026#p001_b0001]

A separate Bronx Bus Network Redesign thread affects how future Bx6 SBS references should be read. The 2019 final plan proposed Bx6 SBS service along Bruckner Boulevard, Bronx River Avenue, and Story Avenue as part of a Soundview connectivity change. [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_2019#p019_c0002] The 2021 addendum then said Bx6 SBS implementation/realignment, related Bx5 and Bx6 Local schedule changes, and all-door boarding timing were postponed until 2023 because of the COVID-19 pause and OMNY/MetroCard transition timing. [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p003_c0003] [bronx_bus_network_final_plan_addendum_2021#p003_c0004]
