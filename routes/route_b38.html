Proposed B38 Local

route_b38

The Proposed B38 Local is a bus route on Dekalb and Lafayette Avenues in Brooklyn, serving as a primary connection between Downtown Brooklyn, Fort Greene, Clinton Hill, Bed-Stuy, Bushwick, and Ridgewood CB 2 Existing Conditions Meeting Dec 2024. The route carries over 18,000 average daily riders and offers both Local and Limited-stop service, with Limited service operating weekdays from 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM B38 service description. During peak hours, buses arrive every 5 to 10 minutes, and the route provides transfers to every subway line in Brooklyn B38 peak headway and subway connections. Speeds on the Dekalb-Lafayette corridor are slow, averaging around 4 to 8 MPH corridor speed noted.

Under the MTA's Brooklyn Bus Network Redesign Draft Plan, the proposed B38 Local would be a 24-hour route with more direct routing, improved stop spacing, and improved frequency Draft Plan route improvements table. Proposed frequencies are 3 minutes during the AM Peak, 4 minutes Midday, 4 minutes during the PM Peak, 5 minutes in the Early Evening, and 12 minutes in the Late Evening Draft Plan frequency table. NYC DOT and MTA are pursuing bus and safety improvements along the Dekalb-Lafayette corridor, where the B38 is the primary bus route DOT and MTA seeking B38 improvements. The B38 also operates from the Grand Av Bus Depot, which serves ten bus routes including the B38 Grand Ave Bus Depot routes noted.
