B44 (Nostrand Avenue)

route_b44-brt-phase2

B44 (Nostrand Avenue) is a Phase I Select Bus Service (SBS) route in Brooklyn, classified as serving an "Underserved Area" due to limited subway access on the Nostrand Avenue corridor Nostrand Avenue SBS Phase I route table. The existing B44 local bus service carried 44,000 trips per day at the time of the BRT Phase II report, and new SBS service was planned to begin in 2011 Nostrand Avenue SBS Phase I route table.

The B44 was one of five Phase I BRT routes developed jointly by MTA New York City Transit and the New York City Department of Transportation, alongside Fordham Road/Bx12, First/Second Avenues, 34th Street, and Hylan Boulevard Phase I BRT route list. The route operates along Nostrand Avenue and adjacent corridors from Williamsburg to Sheepshead Bay, connecting residential neighborhoods, commercial districts, and institutions such as Brooklyn College and Kings County Medical Center B44 corridor description. The B44 also runs along Rogers Avenue and Nostrand Avenue, both designated Vision Zero priority corridors due to high rates of pedestrian injuries and fatalities Vision Zero priority corridor designation.

Average bus speeds in Brooklyn were approximately 7 miles per hour at the time of the Phase II report, highlighting the need for bus priority improvements on high-ridership corridors like the B44 Average bus speeds by borough table.
