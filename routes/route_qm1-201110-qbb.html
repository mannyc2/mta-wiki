QM1 Express Bus Route

route_qm1-201110-qbb

QM1 Express Bus Route is an express bus route in Queens documented as part of the 2010 Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study by NYC DOT NYC DOT presentation presentation to community boards. The route carried 750 daily riders according to the study's data, contributing to Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB 7920 riders per day of 7,920 across all express routes using the bridge QM1 daily ridership and express total.

The QM1 operates with a deadhead route shared with QM1A, QM2, QM2A, QM10, QM11, and QM12 buses QM1 deadhead route. The QBB corridor carried approximately 16,000 bus trips per day including about 8,000 local riders, with 110 buses during PM rush hour, and over 75% of express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delayed their bus QBB corridor statistics.

Of Express Bus Rider Survey Respondents 511 respondents (511 express riders surveyed), 88% were traveling to or from work, 81% rode the bus daily, and 80% perceived traffic congestion sometimes, often, or always during PM hours at locations including the Queensboro Bridge express rider survey data. Among express riders who perceived delay on the bridge, 62% reported an average delay of 5-15 minutes express rider delay perception.

The study's Queens-side focus area includes Queens Plaza, ramp connections to 21st St/Thomson Ave, Thomson Ave, Queens Blvd, and Northern Blvd study area Queens side.
