Q25 bus route

route_q25-queens

The Q25 bus route runs from Jamaica to College Point in Queens via the Kissena Blvd/Parsons Blvd corridor Q25 route description and connects Linden Hill, Flushing, Kissena Park, and Pomonok Q25 neighborhood connections. The route carries 19,324 weekday riders with an 8-minute peak frequency Q25 ridership and frequency, part of a corridor that serves a combined 26,000 daily riders on the Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd segment Parsons/Kissena daily riders — a figure captured by the 26,000 daily riders on Parsons Blvd/Kissena Blvd 26000 riders metric. By comparison, the parallel Main Street corridor carries 42,000 daily riders, as reflected in the 42,000 daily riders on Main Street 42000 riders metric Main Street daily riders.

The Q25 LTD and the Q34 were identified as paired routes on the Kissena/Parsons Boulevards corridor during the Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service study Q25 LTD and Q34 on Kissena/Parsons. The corridor itself was flagged in the 2006 NYC BRT study and 2009 BRT Phase II report as characterized by "long and slow bus trips" long and slow bus trips, with the Q25 LTD delayed during AM and PM peak periods nearly 50% of its travel time Q25LTD peak delay.

The Q25 was identified as a candidate for SBS upgrade alongside the Q44 Two SBS Candidates: Q44 & Q25. Under the Queens bus network redesign, the proposed Q25 would absorb existing Q34 trips and provide improved service along Kissena and Parsons Boulevards, serving one of the Queens routes with the highest number of residents below the poverty line Q25 absorbing Q34. The route was also included in NYC DOT's Transit Signal Priority program along Kissena Boulevard TSP on Q25 on Kissena Boulevard.
