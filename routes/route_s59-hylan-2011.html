Hylan Boulevard Corridor Transportation Improvement Study - S79 Select Bus Service Begins September 2, 2012Hylan Boulevard Corridor Transportation Improvement Study - S79 on Hylan Boulevard +selectbus service Progress Report

The S59 local bus is a Staten Island local route serving Richmond Avenue, and it was a key component of the service plan when the S79 was converted to Select Bus Service on September 2, 2012 S59 along Richmond Avenue. Under the SBS restructure, the S79 became the limited-stop S79 SBS, while the S59 and S78 continued to serve all local stops with added service S59 and S78 continue local stops. At community meetings, the plan was presented as the S59 and S78 providing local service alongside the streamlined S79 SBS S59, S78 provide Local service. Stakeholders had expressed concern that local routes would become overcrowded after the S79 reduced its stops from 80 to 18; to address this, frequency and service span were increased on the S59 and S78 increased frequency and service span. A free "3rd leg" transfer was also offered to customers who started on a local bus, transferred to the S79 SBS, and then connected to a bus or subway in Brooklyn free 3rd leg transfer. Between 2011 and 2013, combined ridership on the S59, S78, and S79 grew by 6.6%, compared to only 1.2% growth on other Staten Island local routes 6.6% combined ridership increase. Customer satisfaction surveys conducted before (June 2012) and after (April 2013) SBS implementation showed that S78 riders reported average wait times 8.4 minutes decreasing from 8.4 to 5.9 minutes S78 wait time decrease and average travel times 27.9 minutes decreasing from 27.9 to 23.7 minutes S78 travel time decrease.
