S Shuttle (Rockaway Park Shuttle)

route_meeting-doc-160271-s-shuttle

The S Shuttle (Rockaway Park Shuttle) operates between Broad Channel and Rockaway Park – Beach 116 Street in Queens, serving as a vital link on the Rockaway Line along with the A Train, which together carry over 9,000 average weekday riders Rockaway Line weekday ridership. In 2012, the line suffered extensive damage from Superstorm Sandy, and A Train service was suspended for seven months; while emergency repairs restored service, the MTA determined that significant permanent work was still needed Sandy damage description.

The Rockaway Line Rehabilitation & Resiliency Project was described as a $393 million investment to reconstruct the elevated Hammels Wye and rehabilitate electrical and mechanical systems on the South Channel Bridge $393M project details. C&D President Torres-Springer reported that fully reconstructing the 65-year-old Hammels Y was necessary to continue providing service to the Rockaway Peninsula for the next 50 years Hammels Y reconstruction context. The cited material planned a 17-week service outage beginning January 2025 and ending May 2025 to complete the work safely and cost-efficiently 17-week outage timeline.

During this outage, the S Shuttle service between Broad Channel and Rockaway Park - Beach 116 Street was to be suspended S Shuttle suspension. It was to be replaced by a special fare-free S Shuttle train serving all stations on the peninsula between Far Rockaway - Mott Avenue and Rockaway Park - Beach 116 Street fare-free replacement shuttle. Separately, the MTA said LIRR would discount the peak Far Rockaway Ticket 7 USD from $7 and the off-peak Far Rockaway Ticket 5 USD from $5 to a discounted Far Rockaway Ticket 2.75 USD price of $2.75 for trips to and from the Far Rockaway LIRR Station, allowing A Train customers to use LIRR service as an alternative LIRR Far Rockaway discounted ticket.
