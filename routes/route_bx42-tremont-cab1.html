Tremont Av Bus Priority Improvements - Community Advisory Bo

Bx42 is a Bronx bus route that operates on the Tremont Avenue Corridor. As part of the Bx40/Bx42 service group, the route was restructured under the MTA Bronx Bus Network Redesign implemented in June 2022, which gave Bx36 and Bx40/Bx42 straight, direct routing so that Bx40/Bx42 serves Tremont Avenue east of Rosedale Avenue and the two route pairs no longer switch streets at Webster Avenue Bronx Bus Network Redesign routing description. The redesign also consolidated bus stops to improve travel time and reliability Bus stop consolidation.

The Tremont Avenue Corridor is a 6.8-mile major east-west transit corridor in the Bronx from Sedgwick Avenue to Boston Road serving six routes including Bx42 with a combined daily ridership of approximately 69,000 2019 Bronx Bus Network Plan corridor profile. The study area for the Tremont Avenue Bus Priority Project extends from University Avenue to Boston Road, where the Bx36 Limited and local carry nearly 29,000 daily bus passengers (Bx36 daily bus passengers 29000 passengers) as the 4th busiest bus route in the Bronx Study area description.

The corridor's census tracts total approximately 118,000 residents (Residents in Tremont Av census tracts 118000 residents), with 72% of households lacking access to a private vehicle (Households without vehicle access 72 percent, above the city average of 55%) and 78% commuting via public transit, walking, or biking (Commute via public transit/walking/biking 78 percent, above the city average of 71%) Demographic summary. The median household income 31000 USD is $31,000 (below the city average of $67,000) and average travel time to work 45 minutes is 45 minutes Income and commute data.

From 2016 to 2020, over 1,100 people were injured in crashes on Tremont Avenue (Total injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020 (from table) 1107 people), with 43 severe injuries (Severe crash injuries on Tremont Av 2016-2020 43 people) and 2 fatalities (Crash fatalities on Tremont Av 2016-2020 2 people) Safety summary. The corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor with three designated Vision Zero Priority Intersections at Grand Concourse, Anthony Avenue, and Boston Road Vision Zero designation.
