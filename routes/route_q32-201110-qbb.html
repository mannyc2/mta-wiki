The Q32 Local Bus Route is a local bus operating between Queens and Manhattan via the Queensboro Bridge (QBB). During the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, the Q32 carried approximately 4,340 average weekday riders, with 2,290 eastbound and 2,050 westbound, making it the highest-ridership local route among the three QBB local bus routes surveyed Q32 weekday ridership table. The study found that over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delays their bus, and of those who perceived delay on the bridge, 58% said the delay was 5-15 minutes QBB congestion and rider survey.

The Q32 is one of four local bus routes (alongside M1, M2, M3, and M4) that serve the Madison Avenue corridor study area between East 23rd and East 42nd Street, an area carrying 92,000 daily bus riders on 34 total bus routes Madison Avenue study area description. Bus speeds on this corridor are as low as 4.5 miles per hour, compared to a Manhattan average of 6.2 mph and a New York City average of 8.1 mph Madison Avenue bus speeds. As of October 2024, weekday AM peak (6-10 AM) speeds on the corridor were 5.7 mph for local buses and 7.5 mph for express buses October 2024 speed data.

Madison Avenue was identified as a Bus Priority Corridor in the 2023 Streets Plan Update Streets Plan designation. The proposed Madison Avenue Bus Improvement Project would extend existing 24/7 northbound double bus lanes south from East 42nd Street to East 23rd Street, paired with existing southbound double bus lanes on 5th Avenue from 60th to 34th Street Double bus lane proposal Existing southbound lanes. The project was presented to Manhattan Community Board 5 on May 19, 2025 and to Manhattan Community Board 6 on June 2, 2025 CB5 presentation date CB6 presentation date, with design refinement planned for Spring 2025, implementation for Summer 2025, and performance monitoring for Fall-Winter 2025 Project timeline.
