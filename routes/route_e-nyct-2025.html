The E Subway Line is one of four subway lines — along with the F, M, and R — that operate along the Queens Boulevard corridor in Queens and Manhattan Queens Boulevard lines identified. A major multi-year signal modernization project along Queens Boulevard was nearing completion as of 2021, promising more frequent and reliable service on the E, F, M, and R trains signal modernization project status.

In December 2025, the MTA implemented the F/M route swap, the first permanent change to the subway network since 2017, permanently swapping the routes the F and M take between Queens and Manhattan during the week F/M swap launch details. Although the swap directly changes F and M routing, it is expected to benefit all four Queens Boulevard lines — including the E — by reducing delays, increasing reliability, and easing crowding for approximately 1.2 million daily riders on the E, F, M, and R lines combined benefit to all four lines 1.2 million riders estimate.
