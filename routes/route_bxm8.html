BxM8 Express Bus

route_bxm8

The BxM8 Express Bus is a Bronx-Manhattan express bus route that traverses the 116th Street, Manhattan Avenue, Morningside Avenue, and Pleasant Avenue corridor, listed among ten bus routes in the study area for NYC DOT's 116th Street Bus & Pedestrian Priority Project CB10 Feb 2025 presentation lists BxM8 among study area routes. The study corridor carries over 65,000 daily bus passengers across routes including the BxM8 CB11 Mar 2025 presentation lists BxM8 among 10 routes with 65,000+ daily passengers.

The BxM8 also appears in later presentations with slightly narrower study-area definitions; a CB10 June 2025 presentation lists the route among ten routes serving over 64,000 daily passengers on a 1.1-mile corridor segment CB10 Jun 2025 presentation lists BxM8 among 10 routes, and a CB11 May 2025 presentation lists it among seven routes serving over 36,000 daily passengers along Pleasant Avenue CB11 May 2025 presentation lists BxM8 among 7 routes.

The project is part of the NYC Streets Plan transit goals to increase sustainable travel modes, expand access to job opportunities through faster transportation, and allow barrier-free travel CB9 Feb 2025 presentation: Streets Plan transit goals. Community vision for the corridor includes making bus service fast, reliable, and on-time, improving safety, and adding commercial vehicle loading; identified challenges include traffic congestion, double parking, and circulation needs Vision statement from CB9 Feb 2025 Challenges from CB9 Feb 2025.

The route's on-bus passenger data was included in leave-loads analysis for the study area, with only routes traveling within the study area counted CB9 Feb 2025 describes leave-loads methodology for BxM8 and other routes. Proposed treatments for the corridor include offset bus lanes, curbside bus lanes, left-turn bays, queue jump signals, pedestrian islands, curb extensions, and mid-block crossings that would affect bus operations for the BxM8 and other routes in the corridor.
