Route Role

The M34/M34A SBS is framed in 2025 materials as critical crosstown service in a 34th Street study area from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive, with connections to MTA Subway, PATH, LIRR, NJ Transit, Amtrak, and local and express bus routes. [34th_st_enhanced_bus_priority_cb6_jun2025#p004_c0002]

The 2012 Project Analysis Report described the project as enhanced bus service along 34th Street from the Hudson River to the East River, and said the new bus lanes and stations would be served by M34 and M34A Select Bus Service and express buses. [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p007_c0003] [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p014_c0003]

SBS Launch And Early Results

The 2011 launch material set Sunday, November 13, 2011 as the start date, renamed M34 to M34 SBS and M16 to M34A SBS, introduced pre-payment at all M34 and M34A stops, and kept service levels and route alignments the same. [201110_brt_34th_open_house_slides#p028_c0002]

The fare process shifted to paying before boarding at sidewalk machines, keeping a proof-of-payment receipt, and boarding through the front or rear door without showing the receipt to the driver. [201110_brt_34th_open_house_slides#p030_c0002]

Inspector teams were described as conducting random checks, with a $100 fine for passengers without receipts. [201110_brt_34th_open_house_slides#p033_c0002]

A follow-up presentation reported that travel times were reduced by 10%, that ridership of 20,200 was recorded on 3/15, that frequency increases added 24 weekday trips, 24 Saturday trips, and 12 Sunday trips, and that MTA Bus Time was implemented on M34 with web, text, and smartphone information. [120418_brt_cb4_34th_street#p004_c0002] [120418_brt_cb4_34th_street#p005_c0002]

Street Design And Busway Rules

Early Transitway planning selected the 34th Street Transitway as the BRT alternative and described the SBS-style package as high-quality bus lanes, prepayment fare collection, transit signal priority, and other service enhancements. [100503_brt_cb5_34th_street_transitway#p011_c0003] [100503_brt_cb5_34th_street_transitway#p012_c0002]

The 2012 Project Analysis Report described a streetbed/sidewalk SBS design with offset and curbside bus lanes, loading and unloading zones on every block, right-turn bays, 24 SBS stations, 14 sidewalk extensions at 18 stations, seven express bus stops, signs, transit signal priority, and signal-timing changes. [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p013_c0006] [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p013_c0007]

The same report said the project would maintain two-way traffic and make 63% of exclusive bus-lane miles offset from the curb to reduce blockages compared with curbside lanes. [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p028_c0006] [201201_brt_34th_project_analysis_report#p028_c0007]

NYC DOT busway material defines a 1.1-mile busway between Ninth and Third avenues with regulations every day from 6am to 10pm. [busway_34thstreet#p001_b0001]

The brochure limits through travel between Third and Ninth avenues to buses, trucks with six or more wheels, Access-A-Ride vans, and emergency vehicles, while allowing passenger and for-hire vehicles to enter for block access if they make the next available right turn. [34th_st_busway#p002_c0008] [34th_st_busway#p002_c0013]

The busway brochure lists increasing bus speeds and reliability for more than 28,000 daily M34/M34A SBS riders and 22 express bus routes, while improving truck movement and local deliveries along 34th Street. [34th_st_busway#p002_c0005]

2025 Priority Work And Enforcement

June 2025 materials said M34/M34A SBS speeds improved after SBS but the route remained one of NYC's slowest buses, with average weekday peak-hour speeds of 5.0 mph, speeds as low as 3 mph, and over 750 average weekday passenger-delay hours from roadway congestion. [34th_st_enhanced_bus_priority_cb6_jun2025#p006_c0003]

The June 2025 proposal centered on a busway between Third and Ninth avenues in both directions, retained existing bus lanes elsewhere, added painted pedestrian spaces, hardened daylighting, concrete median islands, and prioritized curb management for loading and delivery needs. [34th_st_enhanced_bus_priority_cb6_jun2025#p010_c0002]

The same proposal allowed buses, trucks, emergency vehicles, and Access-a-Ride along the entire corridor, allowed local access for cars, taxis, and for-hire vehicles from side streets, required those vehicles to leave at the next required turn using posted signage, and connected those rules to faster and more dependable bus service plus loading, deliveries, and customer access. [34th_st_enhanced_bus_priority_cb6_jun2025#p011_c0002]

The 2024 bus lane camera report says ABLE mobile cameras operated through 2023 on 21 routes including M34-SBS, and MTA's Automated Camera Enforcement page updated May 15, 2026 lists M34-SBS on 34 St as an ACE route. [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0003] [bus_lane_camera_report_2024#p008_c0004] [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001]
