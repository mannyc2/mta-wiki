Evidence Clusters

This page combines several M101 evidence clusters. Read them as scoped references: 125th Street/M60 SBS local-stop context, Third Avenue and Lexington Avenue bus-priority work, a 42nd Street transfer mention, and Automated Camera Enforcement coverage.

125th Street And M60 SBS

In the 125th Street sources, M101 appears with Bx15, M60, and M100 as part of the bus service on the corridor. [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0004] The 2011 weekday boarding table gives M101 7,198 boardings and a 32,630 total across Bx15, M60, M100, and M101. [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0006] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0007] Workshop comments separately say that "M100 and M101 buses operate rather infrequently" while discussing bus operations and reliability on 125 th Street. [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p004_c0009]

The SBS treatment evidence should not be read as converting M101 itself into the limited-stop service. A 2013 presentation says "M60 SBS makes limited stops in Manhattan and Queens" while "Bx15, M100 and M101 make local stops along 125 th Street." [2013_03_07_sbs_125th_cb9#p013_c0005] Later 2013 materials say "Local service is unchanged on Bx15, M100, M101" and "Bus stops are unchanged on Bx15, M100, M101." [2013_04_09_sbs_125th_workshop_3#p008_c0004] [2013_06_05_sbs_125th_cb9#p009_c0006] The 2014 M60 launch flyer repeats that the Bx15, M100 and M101 make all local stops on 125th St. [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003] A 2015 post-implementation update keeps the same split: M60 SBS stops focus on major destinations and transit connections, while Bx15, M100 and M101 continue to stop on every block on 125 th Street. [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p005_c0004] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p005_c0005]

Third Avenue And 57th Street

On Third Avenue, M101 is part of bus-priority evidence directly. A May 2014 presentation describes an existing curbside bus lane implemented in 1982 from 36 th St to 56 th St, in effect 7am-7pm, with 59,000 daily riders on M101, M102, M103 and 12 express routes. [2014_05_05_brt_thirdave_cb6_presentation#p002_c0002] A December 2014 presentation says that in fall 2014 the curbside bus lane was converted to an "offset" bus lane from 36 th Street to 56 th Street, improving bus speeds and reliability for M101, M102, M103, and express buses. [2014_12_01_brt_thirdave_cb6_presentation#p002_c0002]

The same December 2014 source scopes a separate 57 th Street issue: no bus stops between 55 th Street and 61 st Street because buses could not access the curb from 57 th St to 59 th St due to right-turn lanes to Queensboro Bridge. [2014_12_01_brt_thirdave_cb6_presentation#p004_c0003] Its proposed bus boarding island would allow M101, M102, and M103 to stop at 57 th Street and provide pedestrian refuge at two crosswalks. [2014_12_01_brt_thirdave_cb6_presentation#p011_c0004]

Lexington Avenue

The 2019 Lexington Ave Bus Lane Enhancement evidence covers Lexington Ave from 96 th St to 60 th St. It lists service by the M98, M101, M102, and M103, total daily ridership of 44,000, buses scheduled every 3 minutes in AM and PM peaks, and average bus speeds of 5.0 mph AM / 3.9 mph PM. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p008_c0002] The proposal was to shift the bus lane from the west curb to the "offset" position from 96 th St to 60 th St, in effect at all times. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p011_c0002] A related treatment proposed bus boarding platforms at M101 Limited stops, naming 79 th St and 72 nd St as potential for 2019/2020 and 96 th St, 86 th St, and 68 th St as potential future locations. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p017_c0002] [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_june2019#p017_c0003] The May 2019 version gives the stated platform benefits: speeds boarding, ensures the bus aligns with the curb, and adds pedestrian space. [bus_forward_lex_ave_96th_60th_st_may2019#p016_c0007]

The 2025 Lexington Avenue Bus Lane Upgrades evidence is a separate 60 th Street to 52 nd Street cluster. It says 8 MTA bus routes travel on Lexington Avenue between 60 th St and 52 nd St, including M101, and that 71,000 riders depend on those routes daily; it also gives average speed as 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours, and says the existing curbside bus lane is often blocked by loading and passenger pick up / drop off. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p004_c0002] That source proposes upgrading the existing curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane and links the rationale to the existing 96 th St - 60 th St offset segment, where it says bus speeds increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35%. [lexington_ave_60_st_52_st_oct2025#p007_c0002]

Other M101 References

In the 42nd Street material, M101 appears as transfer context rather than as the target route of the corridor project: the source says daily transfers are infrequent between M42 and M101/102/103 buses, while most M42 transfers are to subway, including 529 daily to GCT. [42nd_st_cb6_sep042019#p017_c0003]

For ACE, the MTA route list directly includes "M101, 3 Av / Lexington Av" under Manhattan ACE bus routes. [mta_automated_camera_enforcement#p001_b0001] A separate meeting document says that the M101 "has sped up by 25% on 3rd Avenue" as one example of larger route gains. [meeting_doc_167241#p028_c0009]

Caveats And Open Questions

The route aliases are noisy. 125th Street/M60 SBS, Third Avenue, Lexington Avenue, 42nd Street, and ACE should remain separate evidence clusters. Several ridership/speed values are route-group or corridor values, not M101-only values: 59,000 for M101/M102/M103, 44,000 for M98/M101/M102/M103, and 71,000 for eight Lexington Avenue routes. The 125th Street sources consistently frame M101 as local-stop service beside M60 SBS, not as the limited-stop SBS route. Some canonical M101 mentions from other sources, such as M86 or Bx6 transfer references, were not used because they are outside the target page's source-id cluster. Document-time status varies by cluster: 2013 proposals, 2014/2015 post-implementation statements, 2019 proposals, 2025 proposals, and ACE evidence should not be collapsed into one current-status statement.
