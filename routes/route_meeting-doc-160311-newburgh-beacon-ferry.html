Newburgh-Beacon Ferry Route

route_meeting-doc-160311-newburgh-beacon-ferry

The Newburgh-Beacon Ferry Route began service in October 2005 after a successful demonstration project and MTA Board approval, following the similar Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry service which launched in September 2000 Newburgh-Beacon Ferry Service Start. Both routes provide regularly scheduled commuter ferry service Monday through Friday during morning and evening peak times, and NY Waterway provides contingency bus services in the event of ferry disruptions Ferry Service Schedule and Contingency. Ancillary services include parking facility maintenance, trash and snow removal, and dock maintenance such as light fixture and stair or ramp tread replacement Ancillary Services.

The ferry services on this route are covered under competitively solicited contract MN241812 with Port Imperial Ferry Corp., doing business as NY Waterway NY Waterway Contract Listing. Metro-North Railroad issued a request for proposals to 16 firms on September 13, 2024, and received a single proposal from NY Waterway on October 25, 2024 RFP Issuance Date Single Proposal Received Date. The Selection Committee unanimously selected NY Waterway for negotiations after evaluating its technical proposal Selection Committee Unanimous Recommendation.

The total contract value is $44,293,662, comprising a four-year base amount of $16,781,539 and three two-year options totaling $27,511,723 Contract Total Amount Base and Options Amounts. Through negotiations, MNR secured a reduced hourly operating rate of $834 (down from the initial $846) and a reduced parking rate of $36 per hour (down from $42), contributing to the Total Cost Savings 916412 USD of approximately $916,412, or 2.03 percent, across the base and option years Negotiated Rate Reductions and Savings. The Operating Rate Reduction 1.4 percent was 1.4 percent and the parking rate reduction was 14.2 percent Rate Reduction Percentages. Funding for the ferry service comes from MNR's Operating Budget at 23 percent, federal funding at 39 percent, and the NYS Department of Transportation at 38 percent Funding Source Breakdown. The Metro-North Committee reviewed the procurement on December 16, 2024, and the MTA Board approved it on December 18, 2024 Committee and Board Meeting Dates.
