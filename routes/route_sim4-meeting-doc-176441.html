SIM4X and SIM8X Discontinuation and Conversion

The SIM4 is a weekday peak period, peak direction express bus route operating between Staten Island's South Shore along Richmond Avenue and Downtown Manhattan SIM4 route description. The SIM4 and its variant SIM4X serve Downtown Manhattan, while the related SIM8 and SIM8X serve Midtown SIM4/SIM4X serve Downtown, SIM8/SIM8X serve Midtown.

The SIM4X variant was introduced as part of the Staten Island Express Bus Redesign in August 2018, making only two stops on Staten Island (at the Staten Island Mall Park-and-Ride and one other stop) but serving all the same Manhattan stops as the full SIM4 route SIM4X established in 2018 redesign. In January 2019, one additional stop per direction was added on Staten Island January 2019 stop addition. The June 2025 staff summary lists the SIM4X as operating 15 trips per weekday, with 7 morning trips to Manhattan and 8 afternoon/evening trips to Staten Island SIM4X 15 trips/weekday (SIM4X 15 trips per weekday 15 trips per weekday, SIM4X 7 morning trips to Manhattan 7 trips, SIM4X 8 afternoon/evening trips to Staten Island 8 trips).

The SIM4X trips are underutilized, running at an average load factor of approximately 15% SIM4X 15% load factor (SIM4X average load factor 15% 15 percent). A June 2025 staff summary recommends discontinuing both the SIM4X and SIM8X variants and reinvesting resources into 7 additional full-route SIM4 trips (3 to Manhattan and 4 to Staten Island) and 2 additional full-route SIM8 trips, with implementation planned for Fall 2025 proposal for 7 SIM4 and 2 SIM8 trips Fall 2025 implementation date (Proposed 7 additional SIM4 trips 7 trips, Proposed 3 SIM4 trips to Manhattan 3 trips, Proposed 4 SIM4 trips to Staten Island 4 trips, Proposed 2 additional SIM8 trips 2 trips). The net decrease in operating costs from this change is estimated at $2.5 million per year $2.5M net decrease (Net decrease in operating costs $2.5M/year 2500000 USD per year).
