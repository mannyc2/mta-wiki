X63 Express Bus Route

route_x63-201110-qbb

The X63 Express Bus Route is a Queens-based express bus service that crosses the Queensboro Bridge (QBB). This route was documented in 2011 as part of the NYC DOT Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, which identified cost-effective ways to improve bus speed and reliability for local and express buses using the bridge Study Goal. The study recorded the X63 with an estimated daily ridership of 380, marked with an asterisk indicating estimated volume Express Route Ridership Table. The X63 appeared alongside other express routes such as the X64, X68, X51, and numerous QM-series routes in the study's express bus inventory Express Route Ridership Table. The total daily express bus ridership across all QBB routes was 7,920 Express Route Ridership Table.

At the time of the study, the QBB carried approximately 16,000 bus trips daily, including about 8,000 local riders and 110 buses during PM rush hour QBB Corridor Description. Over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delayed their bus Congestion Impact. An express rider survey conducted as part of the study found that of 511 express respondents, 88% were traveling to or from work, 81% rode the bus daily, and 52% sometimes used a subway alternative Express Rider Survey. Among express riders who perceived delay on the bridge, 62% reported delays of 5-15 minutes Express Rider Survey. The most cited improvement suggestions from express riders were bus lanes, enforcement of bus lanes, and routing changes Express Rider Survey.

The Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB 7920 riders per day metric captures the aggregate express rider volume across all QBB express routes Express Ridership Table.
