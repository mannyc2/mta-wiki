M125 - new route

route_m125-dec2021

The M125 was introduced as part of the Bronx Bus Network Redesign alongside the Bx25, with the route proposed in late 2021 and implementation targeted for summer 2022 Proposed new M125 route description. The route was created from a split of the Bx15: the Bx15 was shortened to terminate at The Hub in the Bronx, while the new M125 operates from The Hub to 125th Street in Manhattan, terminating in Manhattanville M125 route proposal details. The M125 serves the southern segment of the former Bx15 alignment via 125th Street and shares the corridor with modified M100 service, whose southern terminal was moved to Amsterdam Avenue and West 125th Street; the discontinued M100 segment is covered by the M60 SBS, M101, and the M125 M125 and M100 modification details. The route was planned to operate at a weekday frequency of 8 minutes or better all-day with the same 24-hour service span as the Bx15 M125 frequency and service span. By September 2022, NYCT reported implementing a new permanent routing of the southbound M125 traveling from the Bronx to Manhattan; with building construction finished near the terminal, the first M125 southbound stop was moved to be shared with the last southbound Bx15 stop on 3rd Avenue M125 permanent routing implementation. The M125 is one of two new local bus routes introduced by the redesign, the other being the Bx25 serving northern Co-op City Bx25 and M125 as new local routes. A service equity analysis conducted for the Bronx redesign in accordance with Title VI found that the proposal would not result in a disproportionate impact on minority or below-poverty populations Bronx redesign equity analysis finding.
