SIM30

route_sim30-42nd-st

SIM30 is a Staten Island Express Bus Route that operates along 42nd Street as part of the 42nd Street Transit Improvements project corridor Staten Island Express Bus Routes on 42nd St. It is one of six Staten Island express bus routes (along with SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, and SIM26) that use 42nd Street, identified in 2017 as a Bus Forward priority corridor due to slow bus speeds 42nd St Bus Forward priority. The corridor runs 2.0 miles from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive and carried approximately 16,000 daily MTA bus passengers across all eight routes that use it corridor length and ridership. Average bus speeds on the corridor were 4.2 mph in the AM peak and 2.9 mph in the PM peak corridor bus speeds.

The 42nd Street Transit Improvements project, part of the Better Buses Action Plan, proposed offset bus lanes in effect 24/7, curbside bus lanes in effect 7 AM–7 PM, and commercial metered loading from 7 PM–7 AM proposed curb regulations. The project also proposed bus stop consolidation at Lexington Avenue and 3rd Avenue bus stop consolidation, bus boarders at select stops bus boarders, and 12-foot-wide bus lanes with investigation of physical protection in certain segments wider bus lanes.

In a June 2019 presentation to Community Board 6, the route was shown on a map of Staten Island running east-west in the center of the borough, with a red line indicating the 42nd St Project Corridor along the M42 Manhattan Route SIM30 on Staten Island map. A separate presentation to Community Board 4 included the same map, labeling SIM30 as running east-west in the center of Staten Island SIM30 map label on CB4 slide.
