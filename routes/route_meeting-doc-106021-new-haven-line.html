New Haven Line

route_meeting-doc-106021-new-haven-line

The New Haven Line is a Metro-North Railroad commuter rail line serving stations in New York (the Bronx and Manhattan) and Connecticut, operated by MTA Metro-North Railroad ridership table shows New Haven Line alongside Harlem and Hudson Lines. In 2022 the line achieved 96.8% on-time performance, just below the railroad-wide goal of 94.0% New Haven Line performed at 96.8%. The line includes branches to New Canaan, Danbury, and Waterbury New Canaan, Danbury and Waterbury Branch ridership to/from Manhattan increased.

Annual ridership on the New Haven Line was approximately 22.89 million in 2022 and rose to approximately 28.70 million in 2023, an increase of 25.4% from the prior year New Haven Line 28.70 vs 22.89, 25.4% change. In 2024, New Haven Line ridership increased approximately 11.8% over 2023, with New Canaan Branch ridership to/from Manhattan rising 40.6%, partly reflecting recovery from a summer 2023 maintenance service outage New Haven line ridership increased 11.8% impact of summer maintenance outage May 30 to September 2.

The Penn Station Access project (PSA) will create a new Metro-North link to Penn Station, providing one-seat passenger rail service for New Haven Line customers, running through the Bronx via Amtrak's Hell Gate Line, through Queens, and connecting to the LIRR Mainline at Harold Interlocking PSA will provide one-seat passenger rail service for MNR's New Haven Line customers. In January 2026 the MTA Board authorized easement acquisitions for PSA power infrastructure, including a permanent subsurface easement at 1140 Sackett Avenue, Bronx, for Easement compensation $110,000 110000 and easements from Consolidated Edison for the new Oak AC substation totaling 1,089 square feet at a cost not to exceed $210,000 COMPENSATION: $110,000 Total: 1,089 ± square feet, COMPENSATION: Not to exceed $210,000.

Connecticut Department of Transportation (CTDOT) proposed two 5% fare increases for the New Haven Main Line, effective September 1, 2025 and July 1, 2026, which the MTA Board approved on July 30, 2025 after public hearings in Stamford, New Haven, and virtually 5% increase effective September 1, 2025, and a 5% increase effective July 1, 2026 July 30, 2025 New York, New York. Schedule changes affecting the New Haven Line took effect October 5, 2025 and are planned for March 29, 2026 Hudson, Harlem and New Haven Lines – Effective Sunday, October 5, 2025 Hudson, Harlem and New Haven Lines – Effective Sunday, March 29, 2026.
