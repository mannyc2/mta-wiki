B12 is a Brooklyn local bus route running along Clarkson Avenue, Empire Boulevard, and East New York Avenue, and is part of the MTA's Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program ACE bus routes list. The B12's 4.3-mile route runs from Prospect Lefferts Gardens to East New York and carries approximately 5,500 average weekday customers B12 route details. The route operates on Flatbush Avenue for part of its alignment; under the Flatbush Ave Bus Priority project, a bus-only signal (queue jump) at Parkside Avenue was planned for Summer 2024 installation, serving both the B41 and B12 in northbound, southbound, and eastbound directions bus only signal planned at Parkside Ave.

The B12 was identified as a historically low-performing route and is part of the NYCT Department of Buses' Route Improvement Initiative B12 part of Route Improvement Initiative. Since the initiative began in January 2024, service delivery on the B12 improved by 4.1%, customers experienced a 10% improvement in journey time, and waiting time improved by 4.7% B12 performance metrics. The Flatbush Avenue corridor where the B12 operates serves a transit-dependent population: 59% of households have no access to a private vehicle and 76% of residents commute via public transit, walking, or biking corridor demographics.

Staff recognized for improvements on the B12 include Surface Line Dispatcher Robert Averill, the B12's AM supervisor stationed at the Fulton Street terminal, and Transportation Superintendent Sharon Watson, who oversees the B12's line dispatchers Robert Averill role Sharon Watson role.
