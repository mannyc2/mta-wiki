South Brooklyn Crosstown Kick-Off Boards

The B64 is one of 14 east-west bus routes serving the South Brooklyn Crosstown corridor, listed alongside the B1, B2, B3, B4, B6, B7, B8, B9, B11, B36, B82, and B100 on NYC DOT kick-off presentation boards from June 2015 east-west route list. These east-west routes carried a combined ridership of 178,000 per day (South Brooklyn east-west corridor combined ridership 178000 riders per day) combined daily ridership.

The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need BRT Phase II priority. Key issues raised at 2009 public workshops included long, slow bus trips and the fact that an east-west bus trip across south Brooklyn could take up to 2 hours 2009 workshop issues.

The South Brooklyn Crosstown kick-off meeting presented SBS improvements that on existing routes had yielded bus speed improvements of 15-23% (SBS bus service speed improvement 15-23% faster bus service percent), ridership increases averaging 110% (SBS ridership increase 110 percent), 95% rider satisfaction (SBS rider satisfaction 95 percent), and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors (SBS crash reduction Up to 20% reduction percent) speed metric ridership metric satisfaction metric crash metric. Proposed project strategies included dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare collection, all-door boarding, transit signal priority, improved station amenities, real-time passenger information, and pedestrian safety improvements improvement strategies real-time info pedestrian safety.

The source presentation boards were published by NYC DOT, with community outreach coordinated by Matt Kroneberger of the Transit Development Group contact information. The B64 is recorded as a true bus route in Brooklyn, identified from the east-west route list in the June 2015 kick-off materials B64 route listing.
