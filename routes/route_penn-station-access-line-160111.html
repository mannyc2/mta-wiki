Penn Station Access Line

route_penn-station-access-line-160111

The Penn Station Access Line is a planned commuter rail route that extends Metro-North Railroad's New Haven Line into Penn Station New York, adding four new accessible stations in the Bronx PSA project description. The 2025 Final Proposed Budget (November 2024) projected first ridership of 0.429 million annual riders in 2028, with zero ridership in all prior years PSA ridership projection in 2025 budget. Service was expected to commence in November 2027, with hiring for train crews, operations managers, and maintenance staff planned to start in 2025 PSA service commencement and hiring timeline.

The 2025 Final Proposed Budget authorized 9 Penn Station Access positions in 2025, growing to 80 in 2026, 153 in 2027, and 231 in 2028 PSA positions in November 2024 plan. By the February 2026 Financial Plan (reflecting the 2026 Adopted Budget), a "Re-Timing of Penn Station Access" adjustment had been applied, reducing the 2026 position count to 45 per month and shifting costs and positions into later years PSA positions in 2026 Adopted Budget monthly detail Re-timing of Penn Station Access listed as MTA Plan adjustment Re-Timing of Penn Station Access dollar and position adjustments. The re-timing was listed alongside MTA Operating Efficiencies as an MTA Plan Adjustment adopted by the Board in December 2025 MTA Board adoption December 2025 MTA Operating Efficiencies and re-timing. The Penn Station Access Line is classified as a true commuter-rail route and is an extension of the New Haven Line PSA as New Haven Line extension.
