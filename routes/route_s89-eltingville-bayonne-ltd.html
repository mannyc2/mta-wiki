The S89 Eltingville-Bayonne LTD is a limited-stop bus route in Staten Island that ran at 16.1 miles per hour in December 2024, notably faster than the Staten Island borough-wide average of 13.9 miles per hour for the same period page 9, S89 speed of 16.1 mphpage 8, Staten Island December 2024 average speed 13.9 mph.

The S89's December 2024 speed was higher than its borough's January 2019 average of 14.3 miles per hour, though Staten Island overall saw essentially flat speeds between those dates page 8, Staten Island January 2019 vs December 2024 speeds. At the citywide level, average peak-hour bus speeds across all 316 routes stood at 7.6 miles per hour in December 2024, little changed from 7.8 miles per hour in January 2019 page 7, citywide peak-hour speeds unchanged.

The S89 is classified as a Limited (limited-stop) service variant operating in Staten Island, according to data from the February 2025 IBO report "Speeding Up Slowly: A Review of Initiatives to Improve Bus Speeds in New York City" page 9, route label and type in source textpage 1, report titlepage 2, February 2025 publication date.

The route's speed of 16.1 mph was the highest among individual routes called out in the IBO report's discussion of wide variation across bus routes; for comparison, the M50 crosstown in Manhattan ran at just 4.2 miles per hour in the same period page 9, S89 vs M50 comparison. This performance gap reflects broader borough-level patterns: Staten Island had the highest average bus speeds citywide while Manhattan had the lowest page 8, Staten Island and Manhattan borough speed description.
