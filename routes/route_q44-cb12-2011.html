Q44 local bus route

route_q44-cb12-2011

This page documents the Q44 local/limited bus route as referenced in early planning materials for the Flushing-to-Jamaica Select Bus Service (SBS) project, prior to its SBS conversion. The Q44 local bus route served the Hillside Av and Sutphin Blvd bus stop in Jamaica alongside the Q20A and Q20B routes as early as 2011, as part of a Jamaica Bus Improvement Study bus stop reconfiguration Q20A/Q20B/Q44 bus stop label. The Q44 Limited (LTD) ran from Jamaica to the Bronx Zoo via Main Street, serving as a vital inter-borough link between Queens and the Bronx alongside the Q50 Q44: Jamaica to Bronx Zoo Q44 is vital link between Queens and the Bronx. The Flushing-to-Jamaica corridor had been identified in the initial NYC BRT study (2006) and the BRT Phase II report (2009) and was characterized by "long and slow bus trips" long and slow bus trips.

The Q44 LTD carried approximately 28,689 daily riders, representing 42% of the roughly 68,000 daily riders across four bus lines on the Main Street and Kissena/Parsons corridors Q44 28,689 42%. During AM and PM peak periods, the Q44 LTD and Q25 LTD were delayed nearly 50% of their travel time, with the majority of delays (29%) caused by buses stuck in traffic or at red lights; overall bus speeds averaged less than 10 mph delayed during AM and PM peak periods nearly 50%.

By 2015, the Q44 LTD was planned for upgrade to Q44 Select Bus Service, with the Q20A/B local service continuing unchanged Q44 Limited will be upgraded to Q44 Select Bus Service Q44 LTD will be replaced by Q44 SBS. Proposed SBS features included offset bus lanes in Flushing at Main St and Maple Ave, peak-period curbside bus lanes in Jamaica, transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, and Vision Zero safety improvements at priority intersections FLUSHING: OFFSET BUS LANES JAMAICA: PEAK PERIOD CURBSIDE BUS LANES Signal Priority for Buses. The SBS would make fewer stops than the Q44 LTD: 8 fewer in the Bronx and 5 fewer in Queens 8 fewer stops in The Bronx, 5 fewer stops in Queens. Curb regulation changes, bus lane installation, and fare machine deployment were scheduled for September/October 2015 September/October 2015: Bus Lanes, Fare Machines, Curb Regulation Changes.
