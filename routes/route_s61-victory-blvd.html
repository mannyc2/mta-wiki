The S61 is a Staten Island local bus route listed among five local routes serving Victory Boulevard, alongside the S46, S48, S62, and S66 S61 listed as local route Local heading. In Winter 2025, NYC DOT and MTA conducted community outreach for the Victory Boulevard Transit and Safety Improvements project, which targets a corridor carrying 25,789 average daily riders across 16 routes that facilitate connections to the Staten Island Ferry Winter 2025 Outreach 25,789 daily riders on 16 routes.

Existing bus lane treatments on Victory Boulevard include unpainted, curbside, peak-period lanes in place since 1963, with red paint installed between Bay Street and St Marks Place in 2020 existing bus lane history. Bus speeds on the corridor drop below 8 mph during the morning peak and below 7 mph in the afternoon and evening, based on MTA stop-to-stop speed data from October 2024 AM speeds below 8 mph afternoon speeds below 7 mph evening speeds below 7 mph.

Between 2019 and 2023, the Victory Boulevard corridor recorded 619 total injuries, including 60 severe injuries, with zero fatalities; pedestrians accounted for 106 injuries and 14 severe injuries 619 injuries, 60 severe, pedestrian breakdown. The corridor ranks in the top 10% most dangerous corridors in Staten Island top 10% most dangerous. NYC DOT is studying potential treatments including upgraded bus lanes with red paint, extended bus lane hours, and extended lanes south of Forest Avenue, with the goals of reducing transit commute times, increasing safety, and creating faster connections to the Staten Island Ferry potential treatments.
