B60 Brooklyn

route_b60

The B60 Brooklyn is a local bus route in Brooklyn that passes through Williamsburg and Canarsie, serving the Marcy Avenue corridor between Borinquen Place and Broadway route map showing B60. The route has an average daily ridership of 12,226 (2024) and is one of three Williamsburg Bridge Plaza-bound bus routes that experience delays and slower speeds along this tail-end stretch, alongside the B24 and Q54 B60 ridership and delay context. The BQE North and South (2024) study recommended providing a bus priority lane to alleviate these delays BQE study recommendation. The B60 also operates from the Grand Av Bus Depot, which serves ten bus routes including the B38, B47, B57, B60, B62, and Q59 Grand Av Depot route mentions.

The B60 was selected as Brooklyn's fare-free bus pilot route under the 2023 New York State budget mandate pilot map showing B60. The pilot ran from September 24, 2023 through August 31, 2024 pilot dates. During school months (September 2023 to May 2024), weekday ridership on the B60 rose 34% from a pre-pilot baseline of 10,081 to 13,545, and weekend ridership rose 38% from 4,794 to 6,627 school-month weekday table school-month weekend table. This weekend increase is reflected in the School Weekend Overall +38% 38 metric. The total cost attributed to the B60 route was $4,787,000, contributing to the overall pilot cost of Total Pilot Cost $16,475,000 16475000 across all five routes cost breakdown table.

The B60 was added to the MTA's Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program on December 8, entering a 60-day warning phase in which vehicles blocking bus lanes on the route were subject to camera enforcement ACE expansion to B60. The route's major streets for ACE enforcement are Wilson Avenue and Rockaway Avenue B60 ACE route listing.
