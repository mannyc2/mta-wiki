X27 (NYCT)

route_meeting-doc-160441-x27

X27 is an NYCT-operated express bus route connecting Bay Ridge, Brooklyn to Midtown and Downtown Manhattan X27 listed among express routes with frequency increases. As part of a systemwide bus service enhancement package supporting Congestion Pricing (the Central Business District Tolling Program), the X27 received one additional peak-period, peak-direction trip in each the AM and PM peaks X27 gets 1 trip per peak direction in AM and PM peaks. The X27 was one of eight express bus routes selected for weekday frequency increases based on high current utilization eight routes with high utilization selected.

These express bus route enhancements were scheduled for implementation in Spring 2025 Express bus routes: Spring 2025. Six of the eight express routes receiving frequency increases had previously seen service added in summer 2024 in anticipation of Congestion Pricing, only to have those trips removed in fall 2024 when the program was paused; the Spring 2025 additions utilize coach buses from the current fleet six express routes restored using current fleet coaches.

The overall service revision package carries an annualized operating cost increase — Annualized operating cost increase - total 8000000 USD — of which Annual operating cost - express bus routes 1380000 USD is attributable to express bus routes $8M total, $1.38M for express routes. Of the total, Annual operating cost - NYCT bus routes portion 5100000 USD funds NYCT bus routes and Annual operating cost - MTA Bus Company portion 2900000 USD funds MTA Bus Company routes $5.1M NYCT, $2.9M MTA Bus Co.. The staff summary recommending these changes was dated December 10, 2024, with Christopher Pangilinan as Chief of Operations Planning and Robert Lai as Project Manager staff summary details document date.
