The M86 SBS Route is a Select Bus Service (SBS) conversion of the M86 crosstown bus route, operating along 86th Street in Manhattan between W 87th Street and West End Avenue on the west and E 92nd Street and York Avenue on the east route limits in progress report. SBS is a joint program between MTA New York City Transit (NYCT) and the New York City Department of Transportation (NYC DOT), applying bus rapid transit principles through dedicated bus lanes, off-board fare payment, transit signal priority, and enhanced station designs SBS program description.

The M86 SBS launched on Monday, July 13, 2015, following several weeks of station construction and roadway marking installation launch date and description. Prior to SBS conversion, the M86 carried over 24,000 daily passengers, making it the second-busiest crosstown bus route in Manhattan (behind the M14A/D) and the route with the highest per-mile ridership in the city ridership ranking prior to SBS.

The M86 SBS route serves as a connection to the 1, 4, 5, 6, B, and C subway trains as well as twelve MTA NYCT bus routes including the M15 SBS connection listing. In October 2016 presentations, the route was described as carrying over 25,000 passengers per day with the most passengers per mile of any bus route in New York City ridership per mile claim.

Early performance metrics showed significant improvements. metric_eastbound_travel_time_reduction_11pct 11 percent: comparing February 2015 (pre-SBS) run times to average February 2016 run times, eastbound travel time was reduced 11% from 23.7 minutes to 21.1 minutes eastbound travel time reduction. metric_westbound_travel_time_reduction_8pct 8 percent: westbound travel time dropped 8% from 22.0 minutes to 20.3 minutes over the same period westbound travel time comparison. A July 2016 customer survey found metric_customer_satisfaction_96pct 96 percent for overall service customer satisfaction survey results.

The M86 SBS featured several infrastructure and service treatments. It was the first SBS route to have real-time passenger information and wayfinding totems installed at multiple locations during the initial launch, combining static wayfinding maps with LED displays of Bus Time GPS-based arrival estimates real-time signage description. Off-board fare payment was provided at all stations except a few end-of-route stops where drop-offs predominate off-board fare exceptions. Street design improvements included bus queue jump lanes, lane realignments, and upgraded crosswalks street design elements. As part of capital construction, six pedestrian-safety neckdowns and four bus bulbs (at Lexington and Third Avenues in both directions) were added neckdowns and bus bulbs count bus bulb locations.

The Second Avenue Subway Q service began operation on December 31, 2016, with a stop at 86th Street. The April 2017 progress report noted that MTA NYCT expected the Q to affect M86 SBS ridership and planned to monitor changes Q service effect monitoring. The route was also listed among routes with automated camera enforcement on 86th Street ACE route listing on mta.info bus lane camera report route listing.

The success of the M86 SBS informed planning for the parallel M79 corridor, which was identified as a potential SBS candidate in the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study M79 SBS background. Community board presentations in late 2016 noted that the M79 analysis would investigate similar treatments used for the M86 SBS, with goals of increasing bus speeds and reliability and improving pedestrian safety M79 planning approach.
