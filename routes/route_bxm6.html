BxM6 Express Bus

route_bxm6

The BxM6 Express Bus is a Bronx-Manhattan express bus route that appears as one of several express routes listed in NYC DOT's 116th Street Bus Priority Review materials from 2025. It is included among ten bus routes (M3, M7, M102, M116, BxM1, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, BxM11) serving a 2.2-mile study area along 116th Street, Manhattan Avenue, Pleasant Avenue, and Morningside Avenue that carries over 65,000 daily bus passengers 116th St study area: 10 routes, 65,000+ daily passengers. In the CB10 portion of the corridor the study area was later refined to 1.1 miles, still carrying over 64,000 daily passengers across the same ten routes CB10 study area: 1.1 miles, 64,000+ passengers. In the CB11 portion the route set narrows to seven routes (M102, M116, BxM6, BxM7, BxM8, BxM9, BxM11) serving over 36,000 daily bus passengers CB11 study area: 7 routes, 36,000+ passengers. The BxM6 is also flagged in on-bus leave-loads ridership data used for the study BxM6 included in on-bus ridership data, and appears in Better Buses program materials alongside other Bronx-Manhattan express routes Better Buses listing BxM6 among express routes. The route's mention in these materials reflects its role as an express bus serving Manhattan's East Side and the Bronx, though the 116th Street study materials focus on corridor-level conditions rather than route-specific performance metrics for the BxM6 itself BxM6 as part of corridor study.
