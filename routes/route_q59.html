The Q59 bus is a local bus route serving the Grand Street/Grand Avenue corridor in Queens and Brooklyn, one of three routes (alongside the Q54 and Q58) included in NYC DOT's Grand St/Grand Av Safety & Bus Priority Improvements study area between Metropolitan Avenue and Queens Boulevard Study area and daily passengers. The Q59 operates out of the MTA Grand Av Bus Depot, which serves ten bus routes including the B38, B47, B57, B60, B62, and Q59 Depot operates 10 routes including Q59. The depot sees 1,032 buses pulling in and out every weekday, with over 80 movements during the busiest hour, and faces significant operational difficulties due to truck traffic and congestion from the Grand Street Bridge 1,032 bus movements per weekday. The area lacks direct subway service, making buses a critical mobility option for residents Area lacking direct subway service.

Bus speeds along the corridor are as low as 4 MPH in some sections, with slowdowns concentrated near the Grand Street L train station, the Grand Ave Depot, and the Long Island Expressway, caused by traffic congestion, double parking, and high volumes of commercial vehicles Bus speeds as low as 4 MPH.

The Grand Avenue corridor is a Vision Zero Priority Corridor; from 2019 to 2023, 359 people were injured in crashes between Metropolitan Avenue and Queens Boulevard, including 25 severe injuries and 3 fatalities, placing it in the top third most dangerous corridors in Queens Safety data and Vision Zero designation 359 injuries 2019-2023.
