S92 along Victory Boulevard, Staten Island (TSP in development)

route_s92-tsp-2017

The S92 along Victory Boulevard, Staten Island was identified as a planned Transit Signal Priority corridor in NYC DOT's July 2017 TSP report Victory Boulevard extension listed as planned TSP corridor. TSP was first deployed in New York City in 2006 on Victory Boulevard using infrared line-of-sight technology, though that early-generation system is no longer operational TSP first introduced on Victory Boulevard in 2006. By the end of 2017, NYC DOT reported that it was ready to implement a new generation of TSP on the Victory Boulevard corridor serving the S62 and S92, contingent on MTA's planned procurement of updated bus technology DOT ready to implement TSP on S62/S92 by end of 2017. A July 2017 DOT press release similarly cited the S62 and S92 along Victory Boulevard as routes where TSP was being pursued S62 and S92 mentioned in DOT press release. As of the 2017 report, this was among eleven planned corridors for TSP expansion in the city, alongside routes such as the Q44 SBS on Main Street, the M60 SBS along LaGuardia/125th Street, and others full list of planned TSP corridors. The report noted that TSP requires the MTA to install GPS-based technology on buses, while NYC DOT performs traffic analysis to adjust signal timing without adding new hardware at intersections TSP system uses NYCWiN and existing signal controllers.
