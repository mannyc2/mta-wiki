The Q44-SBS (Bronx-Flushing-Jamaica Select Bus Service) began service on November 29, 2015, replacing the Q44 Limited along its route from Merrick Boulevard in Jamaica to the Bronx Zoo via Main Street service start date Q44 from Bronx Zoo to Jamaica via Main Street Q44 LTD replaced by Q44 SBS. The Q20A/B local service continued unchanged alongside the new SBS service Q20A/B local continues as is.

The route was prioritized for the SBS upgrade based on daily ridership of over 29,000 and its role as a key inter-borough connection between Queens and the Bronx Q44 prioritized based on ridership vital Bronx-Queens inter-borough link. Planned treatments included offset bus lanes in Flushing, peak-period curbside bus lanes in Jamaica, improved fare collection, transit signal priority, and Vision Zero safety improvements at priority intersections such as Main Street/Roosevelt Avenue and Sutphin Boulevard/Archer Avenue Flushing offset bus lanes Jamaica peak-period curbside bus lanes bus lanes, fare machines, curb changes improved fare collection priority intersection list.

The Q44 SBS was one of the initial routes equipped with Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE), also known as Automated Bus Lane Enforcement (ABLE), using bus-mounted cameras to enforce bus lane and bus stop rules Q44-SBS listed among ABLE routes ACE route on Main St / Cross Bronx Service Rd first of 300 additional equipped buses on Q44 SBS starting September. The route also received transit signal priority (TSP) technology TSP on Q44 SBS. In 2023, the MTA installed front-of-bus bike racks on the Q44 SBS as part of an interborough bike access initiative Q44 SBS bike racks.
