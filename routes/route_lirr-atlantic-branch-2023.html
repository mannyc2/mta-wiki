Atlantic Branch

route_lirr-atlantic-branch-2023

The Atlantic Branch, operated by the MTA Long Island Rail Road, recorded an overall on-time performance of 94.0% for 2023 in the LIRR's Annual Operating Report, down from 98.1% in 2022 Performance Summary 2023. No annual goal is listed for the branch Atlantic Branch annual goal not stated. Its sub-period on-time performance for 2023 was higher than the overall figure: weekend service achieved 99.0%, AM Peak 98.1%, PM Peak 97.8%, Total Peak 97.9%, and Off Peak Weekday 97.8% Atlantic Branch sub-period OTP 2023.

In a February 2025 timetable change covering March 3 through May 19, 2025, the Atlantic Branch was listed as the site of Rail Replacement and Maintenance work March 3 Timetable project list. The same schedule also supported Atlantic Tunnel Lighting improvements Atlantic Tunnel Lighting. Funding for these projects was contained in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budget Funding source.

System-level metrics from the 2023 Annual Operating Report provide broader context: the LIRR reported 18,365 total delay minutes systemwide 18365 minutes, with Train Operations (29.9%) 5483 minutes and External Factors (19.9%) 3646 minutes as the largest categories System Categories of Delay 2023. Fleet-wide Mean Distance Between Failures (MDBF) 176622 miles reached 176,622 miles for December 2023, above the 170,000-mile goal Fleet MDBF table.
