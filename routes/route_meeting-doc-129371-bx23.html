Bx23 (MTA Bus)

route_meeting-doc-129371-bx23

The Bx23 is a local bus route operated by MTA Bus Company (MTABC) connecting Co-op City with the Pelham Bay Park Station in the Bronx Bx23 service description. In a 2023 update on the Extending Transit's Reach Strategic Action Plan, MTA reported that the Bx23 was one of four local bus routes equipped with front-of-bus bike racks, joining the S53, S93, and Q50 front-of-bus bike racks on local routes. A separate October 2022 presentation on bicycle, pedestrian, and micromobility access confirmed that permanent bike racks had been installed on the same four bus routes crossing MTA bridges permanent bike racks installed.

As part of a congestion pricing-related service enhancement proposal, the Bx23 was identified for running time adjustments among a package covering eight express bus routes and sixteen local bus routes/route combinations service enhancement recommendation Bx23 running time adjustments. The package's annual operating cost 8000000 USD was estimated at approximately $8 million, of which the MTA Bus Company annual cost allocation 2900000 USD was roughly $2.9 million per year MTA Bus Company cost allocation. A first-year local bus rehabilitation cost 5000000 USD of $5 million would also be needed to rehabilitate 29 local buses first-year bus rehabilitation cost. Local bus route adjustments were scheduled for implementation in Summer 2025 local bus implementation date.
