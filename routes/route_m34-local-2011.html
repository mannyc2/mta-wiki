The M34 Bus Route (Local) was a local crosstown bus operating the length of 34th Street in Manhattan between the Javits Center and the FDR Drive M34 route description. By 2009 its performance was poor: the average one-way travel time was 28 minutes for the 2-mile route, yielding an average speed of only 4.3 mph, marginally faster than walking 28-minute travel time 4.3 mph speed. Of the total trip time, only 40% was spent in motion, while dwell time consumed 38% and waiting at traffic signals consumed 22% time breakdown. These metrics — M34 one-way travel time 28 minutes 28 minutes, M34 average speed 4.3 mph 4.3 miles per hour, M34 time in motion 40% of trip 40 percent, M34 dwell time 38% of trip 38.2 percent, and M34 signal delay 22% of trip 21.9 percent — captured the baseline conditions before major improvements.

In 2011, NYCDOT and NYCT planned near-term improvements for the M34 including the introduction of off-board fare collection and additional bus lane camera enforcement 2011 planned improvements. NYCDOT Project Manager Will Carry presented these changes at the March 2011 Community Advisory Committee meeting, explaining that they would apply to both the M34 and M16 routes Will Carry presentation. Earlier CAC discussions in January 2011 had addressed stop consolidation along the corridor, including proposals to consolidate stops at Madison and Fifth Avenues to meet NYCT stop-spacing guidelines and create expanded loading zones stop consolidation.

Future development in the study area was projected to generate an estimated 5,000 to 6,000 new bus riders, requiring 34 additional AM-peak and 31 additional PM-peak bus trips future ridership growth — captured as Total future new bus riders 5,000-6,000 upwards of 5,000 to 6,000 new bus riders riders. The M34 formed one of two bus routes (along with the M16) serving the 34th Street corridor during this period, operating as a local service with plans to evolve toward Select Bus Service features.
