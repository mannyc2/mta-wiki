Hylan Boulevard/Richmond Avenue Select Bus Service (proposed)

route_hylan-richmond-av-sbs-proposed

The Hylan Boulevard/Richmond Avenue Select Bus Service (proposed) was a proposed Select Bus Service route on Staten Island studied as part of the Hylan Boulevard Transportation Improvement Study, a joint effort of MTA New York City Transit and the New York City Department of Transportation MTA NYCT NYC DOT. A map showed the proposed SBS route connecting Richmond Avenue in the south to Bay Ridge in the north, with proposed stations at locations including the Staten Island Mall, Eltingville Transit Center, and Staten Island Railway stations Hylan/Richmond SBS map. The study corridor ran along Hylan Boulevard and Richmond Avenue on the south shore of Staten Island Study Corridor Map.

The study identified corridor issues including traffic congestion on Hylan Boulevard, which carries 28,000 vehicles on a typical weekday, and long transit commute times, with 72% of transit commuters having trips longer than 60 minutes Corridor Issues Transit Commute Times. Bus passenger delays were attributed to traffic congestion, frequent express bus stops, narrow lanes, and fare payment on board Bus delay causes. Project goals included improving traffic flow, reducing bus travel time and improving reliability, improving transit access to Manhattan and Brooklyn, and improving safety Project Goals.

A toolbox of potential improvements was considered, encompassing traffic and safety treatments (left-turn lane projects, signal optimization, pedestrian islands, sidewalks, wider medians), bus service changes (curb or offset bus lanes, simplified service patterns, longer stop spacing), and technology (transit signal priority, real-time bus information, advanced payment) Traffic and Safety Bus Service Technology. Specific focus-area treatments evaluated on Hylan Boulevard included peak-period bus lanes, transit signal priority, advance signals, bus lay-bys, and widened landscaped medians Hylan focus area Old Town to Benton Hylan Tysens lay-by map Hylan Cleveland to Wiman.

The study timeline anticipated a draft concept plan in summer 2011, a preferred plan in fall 2011, and implementation targeted for 2012 or 2013 Draft Concept Plan Summer 2011 Preferred Plan Fall 2011 Target 2012 or 2013. A public meeting was held on June 8, 2011, and further CAC and public meetings were scheduled for late 2011 Public Meeting June 8, 2011 Timeline.
