Queens Bus Network Redesign May 2022 Update

The Q84 appears in the Queens Bus Network Redesign as a case study for the "Better bus stop spacing" strategy within a system of 115 bus routes in Queens 115 routes serving 800,000 weekday riders in Queens 800000 riders Case Study: Q84 115 bus routes that serve 800,000 customers. The route is proposed as a new "Rush" type service connecting Cambria Heights and Downtown Jamaica Creating a new Rush route to connect Cambria Heights and Downtown Jamaica.

As defined in the redesign, "Rush" Routes are designed to connect outer borough neighborhoods quickly to the subway Rush Routes connect outer borough neighborhoods quickly to the subway. The Q84 stop spacing reflects this logic: stops are closer together in residential Cambria Heights and farther apart near the Jamaica LIRR/Subway hub Stops are closer together in residential Cambria Heights and farther apart near the Jamaica LIRR/Subway hub. The redesign's proposed stop spacing for Rush Routes is 1,250 feet Rush Routes NA → 1,250 feet, a change from the Local Route target of Local route stop spacing 800-1,200 feet 800 feet → 1,200 feet feet also shown in the same table.

The Q84 was presented during the public outreach phase following the New Draft Plan release in March 2022 Released New Draft Plan (Mar 2022), as part of the MTA Queens Bus Network Redesign process that began with open houses in April 2019 Launched project with open houses and surveys (Apr 2019). Because the Q84 is a proposed redesign route rather than a current operating route, its final routing, stop locations, and service frequency depend on community feedback and adoption of the redesign plan.
