S79 local bus

route_s79-hylan-2010

The S79 local bus was a local bus route operating along Hylan Boulevard on Staten Island, serving as the predecessor to the S79 Select Bus Service (SBS) Replaces local S79 service. The route carried significant ridership, with approximately 16,000 daily trips across the S78 and S79 local buses combined 16,000 trips/day on S78 and S79 local buses.

The S79 local bus faced chronic delays from traffic congestion on Hylan Boulevard, the only continuous north-south route on the south shore, carrying about 28,000 vehicles on a typical weekday Traffic Congestion on Hylan Blvd. Congestion, frequent express bus stops, narrow lanes, and onboard fare payment all contributed to bus passenger delays Causes of bus passenger delays.

Under the Hylan Boulevard Transportation Improvement Study, the S79 local bus was planned for replacement by a streamlined S79 Select Bus Service with limited-stop spacing, a more direct route near the Staten Island Mall and Eltingville Transit Center, and off-board fare collection Introduce Limited stop spacing on S79. Proposed improvements included peak-period bus lanes on Hylan Boulevard, transit signal priority (TSP), advance signals at key intersections, and optimized signal timing Streamlined S79 bus service would operate with fewer stops. The S79 SBS improvements were announced as coming in 2012 S79 SELECT BUS SERVICE IMPROVEMENTS COMING IN 2012.
