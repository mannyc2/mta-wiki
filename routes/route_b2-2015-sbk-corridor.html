The B2 route is listed among 14 east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn that were identified as part of the South Brooklyn Crosstown Select Bus Service study corridor 14 bus routes provide East-West service. The east-west bus routes in this corridor carried a combined ridership of 178,000 per day 178,000 per day. The Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study (2009) identified the south Brooklyn east-west corridor as a priority service need priority service need. At the time of the June 2015 kick-off meeting, seven Select Bus Service routes were operating citywide seven SBS routes operating in NYC, with documented performance outcomes including 15–23% faster bus service SBS bus service speed improvement 15-23% faster bus service percent 15-23%, ridership increases averaging 110% SBS ridership increase 110 percent ↑110%, 95% bus rider satisfaction SBS rider satisfaction 95 percent 95%, and up to a 20% reduction in crashes along SBS corridors SBS crash reduction Up to 20% reduction percent ↓20%. The project was led by NYC DOT, with Matt Kroneberger serving as Community Outreach Coordinator Contact Matt Kroneberger.
