Q97

route_163121-q97-shuttle

The Q97 is a temporary shuttle bus route established to provide alternate service during the Rockaway Line rehabilitation project, which commenced on January 17, 2025 January 17 commencement. The route runs nonstop between Howard Beach-JFK Airport and Far Rockaway-Mott Av, offering one of two shuttle bus options for Rockaways residents during the four-month subway shutdown Q97 nonstop service description. The Q97 operates alongside the Q109, which follows a local routing with stops at Broad Channel and Beach 90 St Q109 local route.

The shuttle bus service is free to riders, with 53 AM and 55 PM weekday peak buses deployed across both routes free shuttle service and bus counts. Additional express service was added on the QM15, QM16, and QM17 to provide more one-seat rides between the Rockaways and Manhattan, and the Far Rockaway Ticket on the Long Island Rail Road was discounted to $2.75 LIRR discount QM15 extra service.

The shuttle service is part of a $393 million capital project to harden and modernize the Rockaway Line, which suffered extensive damage from Superstorm Sandy in 2012 project cost and scope. The project is led by Hugo Zamora, serving as the dedicated New York City Transit project lead overseeing the customer experience Zamora project lead. Work is expected to be completed in May 2025, at which point the Q97 shuttle service will end completion timeline.
