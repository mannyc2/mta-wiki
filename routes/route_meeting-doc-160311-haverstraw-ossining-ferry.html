Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry Route

route_meeting-doc-160311-haverstraw-ossining-ferry

Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry Route is a commuter ferry service connecting Haverstraw, Rockland County, with Ossining, Westchester County, across the Hudson River. After a successful demonstration project and MTA Board approval, service began in September 2000 Haverstraw-Ossining Ferry Service Start. The route operates Monday through Friday during morning and evening peak hours on regularly scheduled service Ferry Service Schedule.

The route is operated by Port Imperial Ferry Corp., doing business as NY Waterway, under MNR contract MN241812 Contract Listing. Service covers both the Haverstraw-Ossining and Newburgh-Beacon routes under the same contract MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount. Ancillary services include daily parking facility maintenance, trash removal, snow removal, dock maintenance such as light fixture and stair tread replacement, and contingency bus services during ferry disruptions Ancillary Services Description.

A prior competitively solicited contract (No. 1000044225) was awarded in 2014 for four years with three two-year renewal options Prior Contract Background. In September 2022, MNR sought board approval for a modification exercising the final two-year renewal option covering January 2023 through December 2024 2022 Modification Listing.

The current contract MN241812 was approved for award at the December 2024 MTA Board meeting Board Meeting Date. It is a four-year competitively solicited contract with three two-year options MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount. The base amount is MN241812 Base 4-Year Amount 16781539 USD and the total not-to-exceed value including options is MN241812 Total Contract Value 44293662 USD MN241812 Total Contract ValueMN241812 Base 4-Year Amount.

Through negotiations, MNR reduced NY Waterway's initial proposed rates from $846 per hour for operating services to MN241812 Negotiated Hourly Operating Rate 834 USD/hour (a 1.4 percent reduction) and from $42 per hour for parking services to MN241812 Negotiated Parking Rate 36 USD/hour (a 14.2 percent reduction) Negotiated Rate Reductions. This produced total savings of approximately MN241812 Total Cost Savings 916412 USD (2.03 percent) over the base and option years Total Cost Savings.

The service is funded by MNR's Operating Budget (23 percent) 23 percent, federal funding (39 percent) 39 percent, and NYSDOT (38 percent) 38 percent Funding Split. The MTA Department of Diversity and Civil Rights established a 22.5 percent DBE goal for the contract DBE Goal. The source states there are no alternatives for these ferry services No Alternatives.
