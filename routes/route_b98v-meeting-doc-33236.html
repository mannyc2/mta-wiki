Transit and Bus Committee Meeting March 2021

B98V was a new temporary bus route launched on March 1, 2021, running from Pink Houses in Brooklyn to the state COVID-19 vaccination site at Medgar Evers College B98V route described in Transit and Bus Committee Meeting. The route operated as a pilot program with buses running every 30 minutes from 6:30 a.m. to 9 p.m. B98V pilot program details.

The route served NYCHA buildings and community centers in Brooklyn, providing enhanced transit access to a mass vaccination site in a traditionally underserved community of color B98V vaccination equity context. A companion route, Q98V, operated simultaneously from Edgemere in Queens to the York College vaccination site Q98V companion route. The two vaccination sites together had the capacity to vaccinate 3,000 New Yorkers each day vaccination site capacity.

The bus report section of the March 2021 Transit and Bus Committee Meeting — where B98V was presented — was introduced by Craig Cipriano, who served as President of MTA Bus Company and Senior Vice President of the NYCT Department of Buses Craig Cipriano role.

This was a time-limited pilot vaccination shuttle, not a permanent route addition to the NYCT or MTA Bus network B98V described as pilot program. No data on ridership, cost, or service duration for B98V was found in the source document beyond the launch and operating schedule.
