The B8 is one of 13 east-west bus routes in south Brooklyn identified as part of the South Brooklyn east-west corridor during the NYC DOT's South Brooklyn Crosstown kick-off meeting in June 2015 B8 listed among east-west bus routes. These 13 routes together carry approximately 178,000 riders per day, with 9 subway routes in the area running mainly north-south 178,000 combined daily ridership9 subway routes serve south Brooklyn. The corridor was first identified as a priority service need by the Bus Rapid Transit Phase II Study in 2009 BRT Phase II Study identified priority. Key issues raised at 2009 public workshops included that bus trips were long and slow, many parts of south Brooklyn were underserved by transit, and a bus trip across south Brooklyn could take up to 2 hours key issues from 2009 workshops.
