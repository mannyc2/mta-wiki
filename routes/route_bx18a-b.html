Bx18A/B

route_bx18a-b

The Bx18A/B is a local bus route in the Bronx, serving the Mt. Eden, Highbridge, and Morris Heights neighborhoods Fare-Free Routes Map Bronx Bx18A/B. It was selected as the Bronx representative in MTA's Fare-Free Bus Pilot, one of five routes chosen based on equity criteria, existing capacity, limited overlap with other services, and fare evasion levels Selection Criteria Pilot Dates.

The pilot ran from September 24, 2023 through August 31, 2024 Pilot Dates Pilot Dates. During the school-year evaluation period (September–May), weekday ridership on the Bx18A/B rose from 4,703 to 6,072, a 29% increase Weekday Ridership Table. Weekend ridership over the same period increased from 2,072 to 2,860, also climbing 38% Weekend Ridership Table.

Operator assaults on the Bx18A/B fell from 7 in the pre-pilot period to 2 during the pilot, a 71.4% decline — the largest drop of any pilot route against a fare-free average of 31.9% and a systemwide decline of 15.4% Bus Operator Assaults Table. The total cost attributed to the Bx18 route was about $1.27 million, including $722,000 in foregone farebox revenue, $148,000 in lost revenue on nearby services, $182,000 in incremental Access-A-Ride costs, and $217,000 in one-time and upfront costs Cost Breakdown Table.

The evaluation found that across all pilot routes, ridership increased but most growth came from existing riders using the service more often rather than from new riders Key Findings 12% New Riders. Service performance showed mixed results, with pilot journey times improving while dwell times increased Key Findings. The total 12-month cost of the fare-free pilot across all five routes was $16,475,000 16475000 Total Cost Table.
