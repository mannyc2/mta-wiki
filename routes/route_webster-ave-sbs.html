Overview

Webster Avenue Select Bus Service is the Bx41 SBS route in the Bronx. It launched on June 30, 2013, replacing the Bx41 Limited, and was described as a joint NYCT/NYCDOT project along Webster Avenue. [brt_route_index#p001_b0001] [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p005_c0005] The route is tied to the Melrose Avenue, Webster Avenue, and East Gun Hill Road corridor, connecting The Hub, Fordham Plaza, and the Gun Hill Road Intermodal Terminal. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p005_c0003] [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p007_c0005]

Service And Street Changes

The SBS package combined off-board fare collection, eight miles of offset bus lanes, intersection traffic improvements, improved scheduling, enhanced stations, and better customer information. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p005_c0005] The dedicated bus lanes were installed on Webster Avenue from E 165 Street to E Gun Hill Road, four miles in each direction, with red paint and overhead/curbside signs. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p014_c0004]

The service change also expanded the old Limited pattern. The progress report says the Bx41 Limited had operated only on weekdays during peak hours with 12-minute headways, while Bx41 SBS operated about 6am to 9pm, seven days a week, with 8-to-12-minute headways. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p011_c0003] NYCT also used the launch to restructure overlapping Webster/Third Avenue service, including the Bx15 and Bx55 context. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p011_c0004]

Early Results

Early post-launch materials report faster Bx41 service. A March 2014 newsletter says Bx41 SBS operated 19 to 23 percent faster than the Bx41 Limited service it replaced, with northbound PM peak one-way travel time reduced from 52 to 40 minutes. [2014_03_01_brt_webster_sbs_newsletter#p003_c0005] An April 2014 presentation table shows before/after peak-period travel-time reductions in both directions, comparing Bx41 Limited in November 2012 with Bx41 SBS in November 2013. [2014_04_10_brt_webster_cb7#p016_c0002]

The August 2014 progress report frames the improvement as less time stopped and more time in motion: an average Bx41 Limited trip was in motion 51 percent of the time, compared with 61 percent for Bx41 SBS, translating to 8.5 minutes saved per trip. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p019_c0004] Its table gives total average trip time as 44.7 minutes for Bx41 Limited and 36.2 minutes for Bx41 SBS. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p019_c0007] Off-board fare collection also contributed to faster boarding, with dwell time reduced by 28 percent according to the same report. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p015_c0004]

Ridership evidence is positive but should be read as early post-launch data. A 2014 newsletter chart shows average weekday ridership higher in the July-December 2013 "after" period than the matching 2012 "before" period, including roughly 20,000 to 24,000 riders in October. [2014_03_01_brt_webster_sbs_newsletter#p003_c0011] The April 2014 CB7 deck reports a similar before/after ridership table, splitting the 2013 total between Bx41 SBS and Bx41 Local. [2014_04_10_brt_webster_cb7#p017_c0002]

Follow-Up Items In The Sources

The August 2014 report still treated some improvements as future work. It expected active transit signal priority on the Bx41 SBS corridor in 2015. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p024_c0004] It also described a possible future extension to LaGuardia Airport via the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge as under consideration but unfunded in the MTA budget. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p024_c0006] Planned 2015-2016 capital work included resurfacing, bus bulbs at SBS stations, street trees/medians where possible, pedestrian safety improvements, neckdowns, new pedestrian-only areas, and sidewalk widening between E 193 Street and E 197 Street. [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p024_c0008] [2014_08_25_brt_websteravenue_progressreport#p024_c0009]
