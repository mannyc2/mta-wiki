SIM6

route_sim6-lex-ave-oct2025

The SIM6 express bus is one of eight MTA bus routes traveling on Lexington Avenue between 60th Street and 52nd Street, a corridor where roughly 71,000 riders depend on these routes daily and where average bus speed is just 6 mph overall (5 mph during daytime hours) 8 MTA bus routes, 71,000 riders, 6 mph avg speed. On an average weekday close to 6,000 daily riders use this slow 8-block segment, with about 1,900 riders boarding 1900 and 1,800 riders alighting 1800 here 6,000 daily riders, 1,900 boarding, 1,800 alighting. In the past five years there have been 88 injuries in the project area, 6 of them severe 6 88 injuries, 6 severe.

The existing curbside bus lane is frequently blocked by loading and passenger pick-up/drop-off activity curbside bus lane often blocked. A NYC DOT proposal (presented to Community Board 6 on October 6, 2025, and to Community Board 8 on October 8, 2025) would upgrade the curbside bus lane to a 24/7 offset bus lane CB6 presentation CB8 presentation, a design similar to the offset lane implemented on Lexington Avenue from 96th to 60th Street in 2019, where bus speeds have since increased up to 26% and pedestrian injuries decreased by 35% offset lane design and 2019 results.

The proposed offset lane is intended to improve bus speed and reliability by letting buses travel without being blocked by standing vehicles, and would include a curbside lane for quick pick-up/drop-off proposed lane configuration: curbside parking, two through lanes, offset bus lane, quick PUDO lane. Enforcement would use the MTA Automated Camera Enforcement Program bus-mounted camera enforcement. Implementation is planned for Fall 2025 or Spring 2026, with curb regulation refinements to follow in 2026 next steps and timeline.
