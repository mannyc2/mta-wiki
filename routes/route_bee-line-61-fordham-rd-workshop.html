Bee-Line 61

route_bee-line-61-fordham-rd-workshop

The Bee-Line 61 is one of the Bee-Line routes (60–62) that travel on Fordham Road between the Bronx and Westchester County Bee-Line 60-62 buses on Fordham Rd. The June 2, 2021 public workshop was held by NYC DOT and MTA to gather community input on transportation issues along the corridor Public Workshop – June 2, 2021. Fordham Road carried 93,700 average daily bus riders on 9 routes 93700 riders per day at the time, including Bee-Line 61 alongside Bx9, Bx12 SBS/Local, Bx17, Bx22, Bx34, and Bee-Line 60 and 62 93,700 average daily riders on 9 routes. These bus routes provide connections to A, B/D, 1, 2/5, 4, and 6 subway lines and the Metro-North Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines subway and Metro North connections. The surrounding community served by Fordham Road routes had 910,000 residents 910000 people, with 62% of households lacking vehicle access 62 percent and 71% commuting via transit, walking, or biking 71 percent demographics of Fordham Road area. The workshop divided the corridor into five sections from Major Deegan Expressway to Boston Road and solicited feedback on transit, pedestrian safety, traffic, and parking issues, with follow-up workshops planned for Fall 2021 workshop discussion format future workshops Fall 2021.
