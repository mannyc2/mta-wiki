Queens Bus Network Redesign Proposed Final Plan Addendum Appmta_automated_camera_enforcement

The Q10 bus route operates in Southwest Queens, traversing several Tier 1 and Tier 2 equity consideration areas under the Queens Bus Network Redesign equity framework Q10 route description in equity evaluation. The existing Q10 has multiple service patterns all sharing the same route number: a direct Lefferts Boulevard route to JFK Airport, a South Ozone Park routing before continuing to the airport, and a third branch ending in South Ozone Park without reaching the airport, creating passenger confusion Q10 service patterns described.

As part of the Redesign's "Simplify Variants" strategy, the Q80 has been introduced as a new route label for the Lefferts Boulevard direct-to-airport branch, serving a population that is over 80% non-white Q80 introduced for Lefferts Blvd. The Q80 offers more frequent and new overnight service along Lefferts Boulevard for airport workers with non-traditional schedules Q80 frequency and overnight service. The Q10 maintains its pattern between Kew Gardens and JFK Airport, continuing to serve residents along Rockaway Boulevard and 130th Street, and is restructured as a "Rush" route providing faster service for South Ozone Park residents traveling to Kew Gardens while maintaining the airport connection Q10 restructured as Rush route. The Q10 also appears in the route improvements summary table under the "Simplify Variants" strategy Q10 route improvements table.

Additionally, the Q10 is listed in the MTA's Automated Camera Enforcement (ACE) program along Lefferts Boulevard, with the source noting a 60-day warning period before fines begin ACE listing for Q10/Q80 on Lefferts Blvd.
