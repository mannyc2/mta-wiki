NEW YORK CITY TRANSIT KEY PERFORMANCE METRICS; December 2024

The M106 Bus Route is a Manhattan bus route serving 96th Street, operated by MTA NYCT's Department of Buses. In December 2024, NYC DOT announced the completion of a major redesign of Manhattan's 96th Street, which added 1.7 miles of bus lanes on the Upper East and Upper West sides from Second Avenue to West End Avenue, aimed at enhancing bus service speed and reliability for the approximately 15,500 weekday riders on the M96 and M106 routes combined 96th Street Redesign Completion. The project includes offset and curbside bus lanes in both directions to prevent vehicle blockages, pedestrian safety improvements, left-turn bays to streamline traffic flow, neighborhood loading zones, and treatments to reduce turning speeds for drivers 96th Street Project Details. The MTA described this work as part of its broader push to improve existing bus lanes and install new ones Bus Priority Push. This information comes from the New York City Transit Key Performance Metrics report prepared for the December 2024 meeting of the NYCT & Bus Committee December 2024 Committee Meeting. No route-level performance metrics specific to the M106 are provided in this source; the systemwide November 2024 bus metrics reported in the same document show average bus speeds of 7.9 mph and Service Delivered of 95.3% November 2024 Bus Metrics, but these are not disaggregated to the M106.
