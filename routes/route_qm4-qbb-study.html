Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study: Summary of RecommendatQueensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study - Approach Summary

The QM4 Express Bus Route is an express bus service between Manhattan and Queens documented in the Queensboro Bridge Bus Priority Study, a 2010-2011 NYC DOT study of local and express buses that use the Queensboro Bridge (QBB) QBB Study Title. The QM4 had 350 daily riders at the time of the study QM4 Daily Ridership 350. Over 75% of QBB express and local bus riders reported that traffic congestion regularly delays their bus Over 75% Report Congestion Delay. The Express Bus Total Daily Ridership on QBB 7920 riders per day metric captures the combined express bus volume, which totaled about 7,920 daily riders across all express routes using the bridge Express Bus Daily Ridership Table. The QM4 was one of several QM express routes sharing a Third Avenue and 57th Street bus stop in Manhattan, where eastbound buses performed a weave maneuver from bus-only lanes to turn onto 57th Street Bus Weave at 3rd Ave & 57th St. As part of the study, a Leading Pedestrian Interval was identified as an opportunity for eastbound bus signal priority at this intersection under Recommendation #2 Leading Pedestrian Interval Opportunity. The short-term improvements from Recommendations #1-4 were presented to local Community Boards in spring 2011 and implemented in fall 2011 Recs 1-4 Implemented Fall 2011. Recommendation #5 concerning additional analysis of express bus priority options on the Thomson Avenue ramps was noted as pending further work by DOT Rec 5 Additional Analysis Pending.
