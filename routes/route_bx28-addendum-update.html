The Bx28 bus route is an NYCT-operated local route in the Bronx serving East Gun Hill Road and Bartow Avenue between Co-Op City and Fordham Center Bx28 routing description. Under the Draft and proposed Final Plans of the Bronx Bus Network Redesign, the route was proposed for streamlining to avoid circuitous segments along Moshulu Parkway and Paul Avenue, but strong community opposition led to the route retaining its original alignment Bx28 retains existing routing. The proposed frequency improvements for the Bx28 are still planned and were not affected by the routing decision frequency improvements still planned.

In a subsequent service enhancement package (Summer 2025 implementation), Bx28 bus route along with the Bx38 was proposed for running time adjustments as part of a broader package covering 16 local bus routes/route combinations Bx28 running time adjustments. The annualized operating cost increase 8000000 USD across all recommended service changes was approximately $8 million, with about $5.1 million per year for NYCT routes 5100000 USD (which includes the Bx28) and $2.9 million per year for MTA Bus Company routes 2900000 USD cost allocation. A first-year cost of approximately $5 million 5000000 USD was budgeted to rehabilitate and extend the life of 29 local buses first-year bus rehabilitation cost.

The Bx28 bus route also benefits from infrastructure investments on Gun Hill Road. In October 2023, NYC DOT completed 3.1 miles of bus lanes on Gun Hill Road, improving travel for approximately 40,000 daily customers on the Bx28, Bx38, Bx41, and Bx41-SBS routes Gun Hill Road bus lanes completion. The Bx28 has also been covered by automated bus lane enforcement (ABLE/ACE cameras) on its E Gun Hill Road / Bartow Avenue alignment as part of a broader camera enforcement program ACE route listing.
