Q26

route_q26-lga-2012

The Q26 appears in the record of the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis (LGA AA), a study that identified Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) as the locally preferred mode for improving airport access BRT selected as locally preferred mode. During the second public meeting, held on November 2, 2011 in workshop format with 23 workshop participants 23 participants, attendees were asked to identify future BRT-based access routes to LaGuardia Airport 23 workshop participants. One participant-identified route listed "Q48 (or Q26) from North Shore Towers to LGA," indicating that the Q26 was discussed as a potential corridor for enhanced service to the airport, alongside the existing Q48 route Q48 (or Q26) from North Shore Towers to LGA. The Q26 is a Queens bus route; the route record established here captures this single 2011-era workshop mention and should be considered a narrow-scope reference tied specifically to the LGA AA process Q26 as participant-identified route. At the workshop's conclusion, 18 surveys returned 18 surveys showed that a majority of participants either supported BRT or supported it with concerns Majority supported BRT or supported with concerns. Workshop participants expressed a range of rider preferences during the voting exercise, most notably Rider Preference: 60 Min, 1 Seat Ride 16 votes, Rider Preference: Express Bus 16 votes, Rider Preference: 35 minute ride in dedicated lane 16 votes, and Rider Preference: Highway 15 votes Rider Preferences table. The primary source for this page is a meeting summary published by consultant URS for NYCDOT project manager Aaron Sugiura, dated January 17, 2012 Client: NYCDOT URS.
