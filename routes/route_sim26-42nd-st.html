SIM26

route_sim26-42nd-st

SIM26 is a Staten Island Express Bus Route that operates along 42nd Street in Manhattan as one of six Staten Island express routes using the corridor 6 Staten Island Express Bus Routes use 42nd St. The 42nd Street Transit Improvements project, a Better Buses Action Plan priority identified in 2017 due to slow bus speeds, serves SIM26 along with M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM25, and SIM30 across a 2.0-mile corridor from 12th Avenue to FDR Drive Routes served on 42nd St corridor. Across all routes using 42nd Street, about 16,000 daily bus passengers travel the corridor and buses carry 79% of people while using only 33% of street space Buses carry 79% of people on 42nd St.

Average bus speeds along the 42nd Street corridor were 4.2 mph in the AM peak and 2.9 mph in the PM peak Average bus speeds 4.2 mph AM / 2.9 mph PM. The M42 local route, which shares the corridor, shows that eastbound weekday running times range from 18.8 minutes overnight (12-6am) up to 34.4 minutes during the midday (10am-3pm), while westbound weekday running times reach 35.2 minutes in the PM peak (3-7pm) M42 running time table.

SIM26 also travels on Lexington Avenue between 60th Street and 52nd Street, one of eight MTA bus routes on that segment (alongside BxM1, M101, M102, M103, SIM11, SIM22, and SIM6) 8 MTA bus routes on Lexington Ave. On average, 71,000 riders depend on these Lexington Avenue routes daily, where the average bus speed is 6 mph overall and 5 mph during daytime hours Lexington Ave bus speeds and ridership.

The proposed 42nd Street design changes include offset bus lanes (24/7), curb space reallocation for commercial loading during overnight hours, bus stop consolidation, and turn restrictions aimed at improving bus speeds and reliability Offset bus lane and loading hour proposals Street design changes. On Lexington Avenue, a similar 24/7 offset bus lane upgrade is planned between 60th and 52nd Streets, converting existing curbside bus lanes to improve speed and reliability, with implementation targeted for fall 2025 or spring 2026 Lexington Ave offset bus lane next steps.

The corridor serves as a major east-west commuter route connecting Port Authority Bus Terminal, Times Square, and Grand Central Terminal, and bus delays on 42nd Street can affect reliability city-wide Up to 210 buses per hour on 42nd St, delays impact city-wide reliability.
