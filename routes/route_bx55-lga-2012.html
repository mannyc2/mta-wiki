Bx55

route_bx55-lga-2012

Bx55 was identified by workshop participants as a potential route for Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) service to LaGuardia Airport during the second public meeting of the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis (LGA AA) on November 2, 2011 Public Meeting #2 held November 2, 2011 with 23 participants. The study had already selected BRT as the locally preferred mode for improving airport access BRT selected as locally preferred mode. Participants proposed a route described as "Bx41/Bx55 from northern Bronx to LGA via Robert F. Kennedy Bridge" alongside many other potential corridors Participant-identified route: Bx41/Bx55 from northern Bronx to LGA via RFK Bridge.

The workshop was held in a small-group format with 23 workshop participants 23 participants and 18 surveys returned 18 surveys afterward 18 surveys returned at workshop completion. In rider-preference voting exercises at the same event, participants most strongly favored a 60-minute, one-seat ride and express bus service (16 votes each), a 35-minute ride in a dedicated lane (16 votes), and highway routing (15 votes) Rider preference voting results table.

The route record for Bx55 captures only this single source-documented mention in the LGA AA study; the route's operational history and current service status are not covered by this evidence Bx55 route mention in LGA AA workshop summary.
