Bx9

route_bx9

The Bx9 is a Bronx local bus route serving the Fordham Road corridor, operating on Broadway, West Kingsbridge Road, East Fordham Road, and Southern Boulevard ACE route listing. As of June 2023, daily bus ridership on the Bx9 was approximately 22,800 Fordham Road bus route ridership data. The Bx9 is one of five bus routes on the Fordham Road corridor, which together carry about 85,000 average daily riders Five-route corridor ridership.

Bus speeds along Fordham Road have been slow and declining. Data from December 2019 shows average bus speeds of 6.8 mph during the AM peak and 5.9 mph during the PM peak for routes including the Bx9, with key issues including high curb demand and frequently blocked bus lanes Fordham Road bus speed data. The Bx9 also serves the Tremont Avenue corridor, a 6.8-mile corridor with a combined daily route ridership of 69,000 Tremont corridor data, and is listed as part of a Future Plan corridor for Pelham Parkway, Fordham Road, and West 207 Street in the Bronx Bus Network Redesign Final Plan and its 2021 Addendum Future Plan corridor listing.

The route has been included in Fordham Road bus priority planning, where alternatives such as offset bus lanes (Alternative A, covering the full corridor 24/7), an eastbound busway (Alternative B, from Morris Avenue to Webster Avenue), and a two-way busway (Alternative C, between Jerome and Webster Avenues) were evaluated Alternative A descriptionAlternative B descriptionAlternative C description. Nearly 16,000 automated bus lane enforcement (ABLE) tickets were issued on Fordham Road between Sedgwick Avenue and Southern Boulevard from November 2022 to April 2023 ABLE enforcement data. In April 2023, buses on the corridor traveled 4.7% faster, as measured for the Bx12 SBS Speed Change +4.7% in April 2023 after ABLE 4.7 percent ABLE speed change table.
