Route Scope

The M60 SBS is the 125th Street-LaGuardia Airport Select Bus Service upgrade of the former M60 route to LaGuardia Airport via 125th Street and Astoria Boulevard. The DOT route index describes the implemented SBS package as including dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, off-board fare payment, limited stops, and low-floor three-door articulated buses, and says service launched on Memorial Day, May 25, 2014. The same route index says M60 SBS connected to twelve subway lines and Metro-North Railroad [brt_route_index#p001_b0001].

A 2014 implementation map gives the route alignment more specifically: from Amsterdam/125th Street in Manhattan, south to 116th Street, east to 106th Street, north to 125th Street, across the RFK Bridge into Queens, then along Astoria Boulevard and Astoria Boulevard/Steinway Street to LaGuardia Airport. A 2013 route map similarly shows the proposed M60 SBS starting on 125th Street, crossing the RFK Bridge, continuing along Astoria Boulevard, and serving LaGuardia terminals [m60_sbs_125th_street#p003_c0003] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0004].

The route should not be read as airport-only. In 2013 planning material, M60 boardings by area were shown as 49% on 125th Street, 21% in Morningside Heights, 19% in Queens, and 11% at the airport; alightings were shown as 51% on 125th Street, 22% in Queens, 17% in Morningside Heights, and 10% at the airport. A separate CB9 briefing stated directly that most riders were not traveling to or from LaGuardia Airport [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p002_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p002_c0007] [2013_06_05_sbs_125th_cb9#p003_c0009].

Planning Context

Early 125th Street workshop materials placed the M60 alongside the Bx15, M100, and M101 as crosstown bus routes on the corridor. A 2011 weekday boardings table showed 9,682 M60 boardings on 125th Street out of 32,630 total bus boardings across those four routes, and the slide text called M60 the busiest route [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0004] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0007] [2012_09_sbs_125th_workshop_1#p002_c0008].

Public workshop comments identified route-specific operating problems before SBS: bus bunching occurred often on the M60 during the AM peak, M60 service was described as slow and unpredictable, passengers with airport luggage made boarding difficult, and many riders used the M60 for local service. The same workshop summary listed delay sources affecting 125th Street buses, including red lights, congestion near Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Boulevard/St. Nicholas Avenue, rush-hour traffic at the Robert F. Kennedy Bridge, turning vehicles, long dwell times, and double parking [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p004_c0009] [2012_09_19_125th_st_sbs_public_workshop_summary#p005_c0004].

SBS Design And Launch

By 2013, the M60 SBS proposal was framed as Select Bus Service on the M60 in Manhattan and Queens, with limited stops, off-board fare payment, offset lanes for buses and right turns, and updated parking regulations. A CB10 presentation also grouped limited stops, off-board fare collection, offset bus lanes, updated curb regulations, transit signal priority, and pedestrian safety improvements as potential improvements for the M60/125th Street SBS project [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0003] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0004] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0007] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0009] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p005_c0010] [2013_03_13_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0005] [2013_03_13_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0007] [2013_03_13_sbs_125th_cb10#p006_c0012].

The launch flyer announced that M60 Select Bus Service was coming on Sunday, May 25, 2014. It distinguished M60 SBS from local service by noting that the Bx15, M100, and M101 would make all local stops on 125th Street, while the Q19 would make all local stops on Astoria Boulevard. The flyer also described dedicated bus lanes between Lenox and Second Avenues, bus-lane camera enforcement, curbside parking/loading/bus stops remaining at the curb, new left-turn restrictions at Fifth and Lexington Avenues, and off-board fare payment at sidewalk SBS stations before boarding [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0002] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p001_c0003] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0004] [2014_m60_selectbusservice_launchflyer#p002_c0006].

Stop Pattern And Results

The proposed stop pattern kept the M60 SBS limited while preserving local coverage by other routes: 2013 materials said M60 SBS would stop at every subway/rail station on 125th Street plus Amsterdam Avenue and Second Avenue, and at major intersections in Astoria and East Elmhurst. A 2015 post-implementation update described M60 SBS stops as focused on major destinations and transit connections, while Bx15, M100, and M101 continued stopping on every block on 125th Street [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0005] [2013_06_06_sbs_125th_cb1#p006_c0006] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p005_c0004] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p005_c0005].

Post-launch materials report faster M60 SBS trips but tie the strongest evidence to the 125th Street corridor, not the full airport route. A January 2015 update said SBS trips were 11-14% faster end to end and 32-34% faster between Lenox Avenue and Second Avenue. A June 2015 CB10 update said SBS upgrades had improved M60 SBS service across the corridor, with 32-34% faster service east of Lenox Avenue and 27-36% faster service west of Lenox Avenue; the same update proposed extending bus lanes west from Lenox Avenue to Morningside Avenue, with summer 2015 implementation [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p009_c0004] [2015_01_13_brt_125th_cb10_post_implementation_update#p010_c0004] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p003_c0002] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p003_c0003] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p004_c0002] [2015_06_09_brt_125th_cb10#p018_c0003].
