The M Subway Line appears here through service-change and infrastructure sources, including the December 2025 F/M route swap announcement F/M swap announcement. On Monday, December 8, 2025, the MTA implemented the first permanent change to the subway network since 2017 by swapping the routes the F and M take between Queens and Manhattan during the week, impacting eight stations in Queens and Manhattan and affecting approximately 1.2 million riders on the E, F, M, and R lines F/M swap announcement. The re-routing is expected to reduce delays, increase reliability, and ease crowding F/M swap expected benefits.

The swap was proposed in an MTA Staff Summary document dated September 15, 2025, which described the plan to reroute F trains onto the 53rd Street line between Queens Plaza and 5 Av/53 St and M trains onto the 63rd Street line between 21 St-Queensbridge and 57 St, with an anticipated implementation in or about December 2025 F M Swap proposal description. Earlier service improvements for the M line include increased weekend service announced on June 29, 2023 at Hoyt-Schermerhorn Sts Station alongside a second batch of R211 subway cars entering service increased weekend service announcement, and the 14 St F/M/L Upgrades project 14 St F/M/L Upgrades.

The M line also benefits from infrastructure modernization along Queens Boulevard; as of November 2021, a four-year signal modernization project along that corridor was wrapping up, promising more frequent and reliable service on the E, F, M, and R trains Queens Boulevard signal modernization.
