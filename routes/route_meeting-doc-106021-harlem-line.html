The Harlem Line is a Metro-North Railroad commuter rail line serving stations in Manhattan and the Bronx.Metro-North Railroad In 2022 the line recorded 97.3% on-time performance.2022 OTP data Total ridership was approximately 14.79 million in 2022 (final estimate) and rose to 18.32 million in 2023, a year-over-year increase of 22.6%.2022-2026 ridership table2023 annual ridership table In 2024, Harlem Line ridership increased 11.6% compared to 2023.2024 ridership increase

A design-build contract valued at $118,000,000 (Design-Build Contract Amount 118000000 USD) was awarded to MLJ Contracting Corp. for ADA station improvements at three Harlem Line stations in the Bronx: Botanical Garden, Williams Bridge, and Woodlawn.contract summarythree stations identified The contract term is 930 calendar days (Contract Duration 930 calendar days) with $300,000 in total stipend payments 300000 USD.contract term and stipend Work includes new elevators, staircases, ramps, platform replacements, and upgraded customer information systems at all three stations.scope of work

A timetable change affecting the Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines took effect on October 5, 2025.October 2025 schedule change A subsequent schedule change was adopted effective March 29, 2026.March 2026 schedule change During a potential NJ Transit strike in 2025, the Harlem and Hudson Lines were identified as alternatives for West of Hudson riders, with a bus connection to White Plains Station on the Harlem Line offering 28 morning rush trains and a 36-minute ride to Grand Central.Harlem Line as alternative for West of Hudson ridersbus to White Plains Station on Harlem Line
