route_s66-victory-blvd

The S66 is one of five local bus routes on Victory Boulevard in Staten Island, alongside the S46, S48, S61, and S62 S66 listed among local routes on Victory Boulevard. Victory Boulevard carries 16 total bus routes (local, limited, and express) serving approximately 25,789 average daily riders and facilitating connections to the Staten Island Ferry corridor-wide ridership and route count. The same set of local routes, including the S66, is consistently referenced across MTA and NYC DOT outreach presentations to Staten Island Community Boards 1 and 2 Winter 2025 CB 1 presentation June 2025 CB 2 presentation.

The corridor faces significant bus speed and safety challenges. During morning peak hours (6-10 AM), bus speeds on Victory Boulevard drop below 8 mph, and during afternoon and evening hours, speeds fall below 7 mph AM bus speeds PM bus speeds. From 2019 to 2023, Victory Boulevard recorded 619 injuries, including 60 severe injuries, ranking it in the top 10% most dangerous corridors in Staten Island safety ranking and injury data. North of Jersey Street, 48% of vehicular road users on Victory Boulevard are bus riders bus rider share.

Existing bus priority treatments on Victory Boulevard date back to 1963, with unpainted curbside peak-period bus lanes that lack enforcement existing lane conditions. NYC DOT and MTA are studying upgrades including red paint, extended hours, and new curbside bus lane segments as part of the Victory Boulevard Transit and Safety Improvements project curbside concept treatments design summary for CB 1 section. Implementation for the CB 1 section (Bay Street to Little Clove Road) was expected in Summer 2025, while the CB 2 section (Wild Avenue to Willowbrook Avenue) has a Spring 2026 target Summer 2025 implementation Spring 2026 implementation for CB 2.
