B35

route_b35-ace

The B35 is a local bus route in Brooklyn operating primarily along Church Avenue and 39 Street. According to NYC DOT presentations from 2019, the B35 carries approximately 29,000 daily riders, making it Brooklyn's third-busiest bus route, and connects to the D, N, R, F, G, B, Q, 2, 5, and L subway lines B35: 29,000 daily riders, Brooklyn's 3rd busiest. The transfer between the B35 and the B46 is the most frequently used bus-to-bus transfer in all five boroughs of New York City B35-B46 most frequent bus-to-bus transfer.

Bus speeds along Church Avenue are persistently low. During the Better Buses Action Plan priority project identification, Church Avenue between Flatbush Avenue and Ocean Parkway was selected as a 2019 priority project due to peak bus speeds averaging just 4.25 mph, compared to a Brooklyn peak average of 6.7 mph, and total corridor ridership of 45,000 daily Church Ave 4.25 mph peak, 45,000 daily riders. The B35 itself is consistently slow at 3 to 5 mph in both directions from 7 AM to 7 PM B35 consistently slow 3-5 mph 7 AM-7PM.

Along Church Avenue between Coney Island Avenue and East 8 Street, bus passengers make up 72% of travelers during the AM peak 72 percent and 69% during the PM peak 69 percent, based on a December 2017 count Bus passengers 72% AM peak, 69% PM peak. Separately, along the Utica Avenue corridor, 61% of residents commute on public transit 61 percent 61% transit mode share Utica corridor.

In 2019, the Church Avenue Transit & Traffic Improvements project implemented curbside bus lanes operating from 7 AM to 7 PM, Monday through Saturday, between Ocean Parkway and East 16 Street, along with new metered parking on side streets and loading zone adjustments Curbside bus lanes Ocean Pkwy to E 16 St. The bus lanes were activated on October 23, 2019 Bus lane activation October 23, 2019.
