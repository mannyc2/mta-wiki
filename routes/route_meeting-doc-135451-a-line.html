A subway line

route_meeting-doc-135451-a-line

The A subway line is a New York City Transit subway route serving the Rockaway Peninsula in Queens, running between Inwood-207 St in Manhattan and Lefferts Blvd and Far Rockaway via Rockaway Blvd A line midday service description. The Rockaway Line segment, also served by the S Shuttle, carries over 9,000 average weekday riders and is a vital link between the Rockaway Peninsula and the rest of New York City Rockaway Line discussion.

In 2012, the Rockaway Line suffered extensive damage from Superstorm Sandy, and A Train service was suspended for seven months Sandy damage. The Rockaway Line Rehabilitation & Resiliency Project invests $393 million to safeguard bridges and viaducts on the line, guaranteeing reliability for the next 50+ years by reconstructing the elevated Hammels Wye and rehabilitating electrical and mechanical systems on the South Channel Bridge, requiring a 17-week outage from January 2025 through May 2025 $393M resiliency project. C&D must fully reconstruct the 65-year-old Hammels Y to continue providing service to the Rockaway Peninsula for the next 50 years Hammels Y reconstruction.

As of the February 2024 Operations Committee meeting, the R211 open gangway cars, first introduced in the United States on the C line, had also rolled out on the A line with a total of 9 trains in service R211 on A line.

At the September 2024 MTA Board meeting, a presentation titled "Replicating G Train Success on the A Line" was delivered, accompanied by a map showing A line route from 207th Street through Manhattan and Brooklyn to the Rockaways, with a highlighted project area near Lefferts Boulevard Replicating G Train Success on the A Line A line project area map.

NYCT's Fall 2025 Proposed Subway Schedule Changes recommend keeping additional trainsets in service through the midday by adding one roundtrip each to Lefferts Blvd and Far Rockaway, extending the span of midday 8-minute average headways between Inwood-207 St and Rockaway Blvd, with no change in the number of crews required for A service A line midday headway extension. The proposed changes would be implemented in November 2025, with weekday schedule adjustments carrying an estimated annual cost of $600,000 budget impact November 2025 implementation.
