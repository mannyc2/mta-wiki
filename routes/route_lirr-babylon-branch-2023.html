The Babylon Branch is a commuter rail branch operated by the MTA Long Island Rail Road, serving Suffolk County via the 2023 Annual Operating Report. According to the LIRR 2023 Annual Operating Report, the branch recorded an overall on-time performance of 93.9% for the year, falling short of the 96.8% annual goal Babylon Branch OTP table. Performance varied by period: AM Peak reached 90.1%, PM Peak 90.7%, and Off-Peak Weekday 94.1%, while Weekend OTP was 94.8% Babylon OTP breakdown. This compares to a 93.2% overall OTP in 2022 2022 comparison.

During 2023, the branch experienced multiple service adjustments due to track work on adjacent lines. On December 6-8, Montauk Branch switch maintenance between Valley Stream and Rockville Centre required eastbound and westbound schedule adjustments on the Babylon Branch Winter Trackwork Programs 2022. On April 8, 2023, Babylon Branch trains operated on adjusted schedules during Montauk Branch track maintenance between Valley Stream and Jamaica Spring Trackwork Programs 2023. During the Van Wyck Bridge Replacement weekends of April 15-16 and April 22-23, some Babylon Branch trains also ran on adjusted schedules Van Wyck Bridge replacement.

In a separate service event on March 17, 2025, the LIRR operated two additional westbound trains from the Babylon Branch for customers traveling to the New York City St. Patrick's Day Parade St. Patrick's Day Parade service.

Funding for the track work programs that affected Babylon Branch service was contained in the LIRR Operating and MTA Capital Program budgets funding source funding note.

The LIRR systemwide recorded 18,365 delay minutes in 2023, with Infrastructure Maintenance 2957 minutes contributing 16.1% (2,957 minutes), Fleet 2523 minutes 13.7% (2,523 minutes), Capital Projects 402 minutes 2.2% (402 minutes), External Factors 3646 minutes 19.9% (3,646 minutes), and Customer Assistance 1167 minutes 6.4% (1,167 minutes) System Categories of Delay 2023.
