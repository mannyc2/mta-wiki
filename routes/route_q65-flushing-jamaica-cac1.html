Q65 on 164 St

route_q65-flushing-jamaica-cac1

The Q65 is a local bus route in Queens, operating along 164 Street as part of the Flushing-Jamaica corridor Q65 on 164 St. The route is one of several bus routes serving this corridor, alongside the Q20A/B and Q44 on Main Street and the Q25 and Q34 on Kissena Boulevard and Parsons Boulevard Flushing-Jamaica Corridor served by. The Flushing-Jamaica corridor was identified for potential bus rapid transit improvements in a 2006 NYC BRT study and the 2009 Bus Rapid Transit Phase II report, which characterized the corridor by "long and slow bus trips" Flushing to Jamaica corridor identified long and slow bus trips. The route Q65 on 164 St runs through an area with over 90,000 daily bus riders Over 90,000 daily bus riders. In total, 13 MTA bus routes use Main Street near Roosevelt Avenue, generating more than 35,000 bus-to-subway transfers to the 7 train -- the largest volume of bus-to-subway transfers at any station in the city 13 MTA bus routes use Main Street. This route record was established from a 2014 Community Advisory Committee presentation and an October 2016 NYC DOT traffic management deck, reflecting the early planning phase for potential Select Bus Service improvements along the Flushing-Jamaica corridor.
