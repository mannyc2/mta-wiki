M15 Limited

route_m15-limited-2010-06-09

The M15 Limited was a limited-stop bus service operating on First and Second Avenues in Manhattan, serving the same corridor as the M15 Local but with fewer stops. It was replaced by M15 Select Bus Service (SBS) on October 10, 2010 M15 SBS replaces M15 Limited M15 Select Bus Service begins: October 10, 2010. Before its replacement, the M15 Limited carried approximately 26,000 average daily riders in March 2010, roughly equal to the M15 Local's ridership of about 26,000 Avg. Daily Ridership chart showing M15 Limited 26,000.

A pre-implementation study found that the M15 Limited spent only 54% of its running time in motion; the remainder was consumed by stops at bus stops (22%), red lights (21%), and other delays (4%) Sources of Delay on M15 Limited pie chart. The M15 Limited also lacked key features that SBS would introduce, including off-board fare payment, improved bus lanes, low-floor articulated buses, bus bulbs, and transit signal priority Features comparison table.

After the transition to SBS, the replacement service was 12 minutes (15%) faster than the M15 Limited and recorded a 30% increase in ridership over the prior Limited service, adding about 4,000 new daily M15 trips SBS 12 minutes (15%) faster than Limited 30% increase from Limited to SBS 4,000 new M15 trips per day. SBS also spent 36% less time at stops than the Limited had SBS spent 36% less time at stops than Limited. By November 2011, 62% of all M15 riders chose SBS over the Local service M15 Local and Limited services had roughly equal ridership totals, but now 62% of all M15 riders choose the SBS.
