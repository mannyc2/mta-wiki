Q72

route_q72-serves-lga-2011

Q72 was one of five bus routes serving LaGuardia Airport as of June 2011, along with the M60, Q33, Q47, and Q48 LGA bus routes listed in June 2011 presentation. The route carried local traffic and airport travelers through Queens neighborhoods including Junction Boulevard, a corridor participants described as heavily congested Q72 commuter congestion report.

At the second public meeting of the LaGuardia Airport Access Alternatives Analysis, held November 2, 2011, a Q72 commuter reported that the trip from Queens Boulevard to the airport took about 25 minutes in the morning, but the return trip in the afternoon could take 45 minutes due to congestion on Junction Boulevard Q72 travel time claim. The same participant noted that bus bunching in the afternoon sometimes resulted in waits as long as 45 minutes at the terminal Q72 afternoon bus bunching claim.

Workshop participants raised additional concerns about the Q72: bus drivers sometimes did not stop to pick up passengers even when space was available, the route was hindered by double-parked vehicles in commercial areas, and the Q23 route was suggested as a potential alternative offering better subway access at 71st Street and Continental Boulevard Q72 route complaints and Q23 alternative suggestion. Participants also noted the Q72 was popular with flight attendants Q72 flight attendant popularity.

Among the route ideas participants identified during the workshop was a limited-stop service on the Q72 bus route, reflecting interest in faster airport access on the corridor limited stops on Q72 proposed.

During the study period, passenger origin data showed that 47% of LaGuardia air passengers whose trip began in New York City came from Manhattan, 20% from other boroughs, and 33% from outside the city LGA passenger origin table. Only 10% of LGA passengers arrived by transit, while 63% used taxi or livery and 23% used personal cars LGA mode share table.
