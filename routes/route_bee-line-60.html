Bee-Line 60

route_bee-line-60

The Bee-Line 60 is a Westchester County Bee-Line bus route serving the Fordham Road corridor in the Bronx, connecting the borough to destinations in Westchester County Better Buses Fordham Road project page. On Fordham Road between the Major Deegan Expressway and Boston Road, the Bee-Line 60 is one of nine bus routes that also include the Bx9, Bx12 SBS/Local, Bx17, Bx22, Bx34, and Bee-Line 61-62 Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes. These nine routes together carry 93,700 average daily riders, of which 40,300 use the Bx12 SBS and Local Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes. The Fordham Road corridor provides connections to the A, B/D, 1, 2/5, 4, and 6 subway lines and the Metro North Harlem, Hudson, and New Haven Lines Fordham Road workshop slide listing corridor routes. The corridor is also a Vision Zero priority corridor, with four priority intersections at Sedgwick Avenue, University Avenue, Grand Avenue, and Jerome Avenue Fordham Road Vision Zero designation. The surrounding neighborhoods served by Fordham Road routes are characterized by high transit dependency: 62% of households have no access to a private vehicle, and 71% commute to work via public transit, walking, or biking 71 percent Fordham Road demographic data. In June 2021, NYC DOT held a public workshop on potential transit improvements along Fordham Road, reviewing the corridor in five sections from the Major Deegan Expressway to Boston Road Public Workshop June 2, 2021.
