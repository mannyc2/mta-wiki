M116 Manhattan is a critical crosstown local bus route operating along 116th Street in Harlem, Manhattan, serving a study corridor that covers 116th Street, Manhattan Avenue, Pleasant Avenue, and Morningside Avenue between 114th and 113th Streets, totaling 2.2 miles study area description. The corridor carries over 65,000 daily bus passengers across ten routes and provides connections to the 2, 3, 6, B, and C subway lines study area description. The route is listed on the MTA's automated camera enforcement page under major streets 116 St / Manhattan Av ACE route listing.

M116 was selected as one of five fare-free bus pilot routes evaluated by NYC DOT and the MTA, shown on a map of fare-free routes in each borough Fare-Free Routes map. The pilot evaluation found that 12% of riders on fare-free routes were new to the route, while existing riders used the route more frequently 12% new riders.

A community visioning process identified challenges including traffic congestion, double parking, and vehicle access needs corridor challenges.

Proposed treatments under the 116th Street Bus & Pedestrian Priority Project include an offset bus lane on 116th Street from Manhattan Avenue to 5th Avenue spanning four blocks while maintaining parking throughout proposed offset bus lane, a daytime curbside bus lane in effect from 7 AM to 7 PM on the north side between 5th Avenue and Madison Avenue curbside bus lane description, seven left turn lanes to maintain traffic flow seven proposed left turn lanes, and 33 painted pedestrian spaces including nine islands and 24 curb extensions providing hardened daylighting proposed painted pedestrian spaces.
