SIM25

route_sim25-42nd-st

SIM25 is a Staten Island Express Bus Route using 42nd Street, one of six Staten Island express routes that travel along this corridor. Eight MTA bus routes across three boroughs use 42nd Street, carrying about 16,000 daily passengers with up to 210 buses per hour. A map shows SIM25 running north-south in southern Staten Island, with the 42nd St Project Corridor along the M42 route in Manhattan.

The 42nd Street Transit Improvements project serves SIM25 along with the M42, SIM8, SIM8X, SIM22, SIM26, and SIM30 routes. The 2.0-mile corridor has average bus speeds of 4.2 mph in the AM and 2.9 mph in the PM. On 42nd Street, buses carry 79% of people using only 33% of street space 79 percent as shown in a street-space comparison chart.

M42 running time data illustrates the congestion that SIM25 and other express routes encounter on 42nd Street. The M42 eastbound weekday running time from 10am-3pm is 34.4 minutes (M42 EB Weekday 10am-3pm running time 34.4 min 34.4 minutes), and the westbound 3-7pm running time is 35.2 minutes (M42 WB Weekday 3-7pm running time 35.2 min 35.2 minutes). Bus travel times are nearly double overnight times throughout the day.

Better Buses identified 42nd Street as a priority corridor in 2017 due to slow bus speeds. The project investigates upgrades to existing curbside bus lanes and safety improvements. Proposed changes include offset bus lanes (24/7), curbside bus lanes (7 AM-7 PM), overnight commercial loading allocations, and bus stop consolidation. Offset bus lanes are proposed to operate 24/7 with curbside bus lanes in effect 7 AM to 7 PM all days.
