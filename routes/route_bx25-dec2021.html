The Bx25 is a new local bus route created as part of the Bronx Local Bus Redesign 2700000 USD Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021, one of two new local routes proposed alongside the M125. It provides service from northern Co-op City to Allerton Avenue and Bedford Park, and shares the Allerton Avenue corridor with the Bx26 while maintaining the corridor's same frequency levels in the December 2021 proposal Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021.

The route was implemented on June 26, 2022 as part of the Bronx redesign rollout NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022. It connects northern Co-op City and the Bay Plaza shopping center with Allerton Avenue and Bedford Park Boulevard, and was intended to provide faster, more direct trips for Co-op City residents as well as residents from elsewhere in the Bronx who want to shop or work at Bay Plaza NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022. The New York City Department of Transportation installed new bus panels and destination signs at stops including a location in Co-op City in preparation for the route's launch NYCT and Bus Committee Meeting March 2022.

The redesign that created the Bx25 was presented for Board approval at the December 15, 2021 MTA Board meeting MTA Board Meeting Dec 2021. As a whole, the redesign increased the net annual operating cost of Bronx bus service by approximately $2.7 million (net annual cost increase 2700000 USD), comprising $1.4 million in NYCT operating costs and $738,000 in MTA Bus Company operating costs implemented in summer 2022, plus an additional $568,000 in NYCT annual operating costs deferred to 2023 Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021. One-time costs included $1.6 million for staff training on the new routes (one-time training cost 1600000 USD) and $255,000 for bus stop changes Bronx Redesign Board Meeting Dec 2021.
