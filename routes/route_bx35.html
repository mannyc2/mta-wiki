The BX35 - ABLE route is a Bronx crosstown bus route that serves the E 167th St / W 181st St corridor lists Bx35 as ACE route with major streets. In the ACE routes dataset it is identified by the route id "BX35" item BX35.

In May 2023 the MTA rolled out the Bx35 as the first ABLE (Automated Bus Lane Enforcement) route of the year first ABLE route of 2023. At that time it carried approximately 19,000 weekday riders with connections to 22 bus routes and the 1, A, 4, B, D subway lines carrying 19,000 weekday riders. On the E 167th St / E 168th St corridor, the Bx35 carried 22,000 weekday riders with crosstown connections to 8 bus routes and the 4, B/D trains, and had average bus speeds of 4.7 mph in the AM peak and 4.3 mph in the PM peak Bx35 corridor data.

The Bx35 was included in the Webster Avenue Select Bus Service planning process and appeared in proposed SBS design maps with existing and relocated stops along Webster Avenue at E 167th St and E 168th St existing WB Bx35 stop proposed EB Bx35 stop. A later CB3 presentation proposed relocating the WB Bx35 stop to E 167th St relocate WB Bx35 stop. The project was split into two phases, with Phase 2 (2015-16) including planted medians and bus bulbs along the corridor that serves the Bx35 project split into two phases Bx35 running on E 167 St.

The Bx35 also appears on the Washington Bridge corridor (Amsterdam Ave to University Ave) as one of five routes carrying a combined 111,400 weekday riders Bx35 on Washington Bridge corridor, and on the E.L. Grant Highway corridor with the Bx11 and Bx13 serving a combined 36,000 weekday riders Bx35 on E.L. Grant Highway corridor. A 2020 NYC DOT presentation for Community Board 3 proposed bus priority and safety improvements for the Bx35 on the E 167th St / E 168th St corridor, including queue jump signals to address slow speeds and double parking Bx35 Bus Priority and Safety Improvements queue jump signal goals.
