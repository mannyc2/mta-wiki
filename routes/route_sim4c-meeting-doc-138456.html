Service Changes: Express Bus Service Additions Summer 2024Service Changes: Bus Service Enhancements

SIM4C is an NYCT-operated express bus route serving Staten Island between the Huguenot-Richmond Avenue corridor and Midtown/Downtown Manhattan SIM4C route description. In anticipation of the congestion pricing launch planned for June 2024, the MTA identified SIM4C as one of the express bus routes with the highest utilization during any single hour of an average weekday and proposed adding 1 Staten Island-bound trip in the weekday afternoon 1 trips SIM4C included in trip additions SIM4C trip details congestion pricing context. The entire package of trip additions across six express routes carried an annualized operating cost increase of approximately $883,000, of which $629,000 per year would be on NYCT routes (including $496,000 for the SIM23 and SIM24 funded by the City of New York) and $254,000 per year on MTA Bus routes, with funding from the Outer Borough Transportation Account cost and funding breakdown. Implementation was planned for June 2024 with a commitment to monitor ridership levels after the planned tolling start to assess whether the additional service is justified June 2024 implementation ridership monitoring plan.

A subsequent service enhancement proposal documented in a later meeting document again included the same SIM4C trip addition alongside a broader package affecting eight express bus routes and 16 local bus routes/route combinations SIM4C in larger service package package scope. That larger package had a total annualized operating cost increase of approximately $8 million, including approximately $1.38 million for express bus routes, with express bus route changes planned for Spring 2025 $8 million total cost Spring 2025 express implementation.

The added trip would be evaluated as part of regular reviews of bus ridership levels to determine whether it should continue operating regular evaluation of added trips. The project manager for both proposals was Robert Lai of the Operations Planning Department Robert Lai, project manager Robert Lai, project manager.
