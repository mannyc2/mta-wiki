The Hudson Line is a Metro-North Railroad commuter rail line serving Manhattan, the Bronx, and Hudson Valley counties along the Hudson River corridor. MTA Metro-North Railroad operates the line, as documented across multiple MTA board reports and financial plans February Financial Plan Metro-North Railroad.

The Hudson Line carried approximately 12.11 million riders in 2023, rising 21.2% from 2022 with about 1.0 million more rides 2023 ridership summary. In 2024, Hudson Line ridership rose a further 13.8% from 2023, adding approximately 1.7 million more rides 2024 ridership summary. Budget forecasts for 2026 projected total Hudson Line ridership of 14.150 million, with 4.929 million commutation and 9.221 million non-commutation trips ridership table.

The Hudson Line achieved 97.0% on-time performance in 2022, matching the railroad's 94.0% goal 2022 OTP summary. By 2025 the overall on-time performance reached 98.1%, exceeding the 94.0% goal across all measured periods 2025 performance table.

A schedule change effective October 5, 2025 introduced new "Super-Express" trains making the trip to and from Poughkeepsie Station in under 90 minutes, along with PM Peak and weekend adjustments to support Park Avenue Viaduct track work Super-Express trains. A subsequent schedule change effective March 29, 2026 made minor adjustments to off-peak weekday outbound trains to Poughkeepsie March 2026 adjustments.

In the event of an NJ Transit strike, the Hudson Line served as an alternative for West of Hudson customers, with additional train cars added, cross-honored fares and parking, and free bus and ferry connections strike contingency plan. A drive-to-Beacon option offered riders 12 morning AM rush trains with a 78-minute one-seat ride to Grand Central at 99.1% on-time performance Beacon alternative.
